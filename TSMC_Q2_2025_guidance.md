# TSMC Q2 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 17, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2025

---

## Q2 2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $30.1B (+17.8% QoQ in USD; +11.3% QoQ in NT)
- **Gross Margin:** 58.6% (-20 bps QoQ)
- **Operating Margin:** 49.6% (+110 bps QoQ)
- **EPS:** TWD 15.36 (+60.7% YoY)
- **ROE:** 34.8%
- **FX actual:** 1 USD = 31.05 TWD (vs. Q1 rate of 32.88 TWD; vs. Q2 guidance of 32.5 TWD)

### Q2 Technology Node Breakdown
- **3nm:** 24% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **5nm:** 36% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 14% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 74% of Q2 wafer revenue

### Q2 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 60% of Q2 revenue (+14% QoQ)
- **Smartphone:** 27% of Q2 revenue (+7% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 5% of Q2 revenue (+14% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 5% of Q2 revenue (flat QoQ)
- **DCE:** 1% of Q2 revenue (+30% QoQ)

### Q2 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 2.6 trillion (USD 90 billion)
- **Current liabilities:** decreased TWD 22B QoQ (TWD 38B decrease in accrued liabilities due to income tax payment)
- **AR turnover days:** 23 days (-5 days QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 76 days (-7 days QoQ)

### Q2 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 497 billion
- **CapEx:** TWD 297 billion (USD 9.6 billion)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 117 billion (Q3 2024 dividend)
- **Cash balance:** decreased TWD 30.3B to TWD 2.36 trillion at end of Q2

---

## Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2025)
- **Revenue:** $31.8B–$33.0B (+8% sequential increase or +38% YoY increase at midpoint)
- **Gross Margin:** 55.5%–57.5% (midpoint 56.5%; -210 bps QoQ at midpoint)
- **Operating Margin:** 45.5%–47.5%
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 29.0 TWD

## Gross Margin Bridge
- Q2 GM of 58.6% → Q3 guided midpoint 56.5% (-210 bps)
- FX headwind in Q2: ~220 bps (NT dollar appreciation from 32.88 to 31.05)
- FX headwind guidance vs actual Q2: ~180 bps (NT appreciated 4.4% sequentially vs. 32.5 guidance)
- Q3 FX headwind: ~260 bps (NT to appreciate another 6.6% sequentially at $1 = TWD 29)
- FX sensitivity: every 1% appreciation of NT vs USD reduces NT revenue by 1% and gross margin by ~40 bps
- ~75% of COGS is in NT dollars
- Overseas fab dilution in Q2: slightly more than 100 bps (Arizona fab margin dilution kicking in)
- Overseas fab dilution guidance: 2%–3% every year in early stages (starting 2025), widening to 3%–4% in later stages over next 5 years

## Full Year 2025 Guidance
- **Revenue growth:** Around 30% in USD terms (raised from mid-20s%)
- **CapEx:** $38B–$42B (maintained)
- **Full year GM:** Mid- to high 50s% (maintained)
- **AI accelerator revenue:** Expected to double YoY (maintained)
- **AI revenue CAGR:** Mid-40s% over next 5 years (raised from "close to 40%")

## Long-Term Targets
- **Revenue CAGR:** Close to 20% over next 5 years
- **Gross Margin:** 53% and higher
- **Overall long-term CAGR:** 20% for next several years

## N2 Technology
- **Volume production:** Ramping well in H2 2025
- **Yield:** On track, comparable to N3 at same stage
- **Revenue 2025:** Meaningful contribution starting H2
- Performance specs: 10%–15% speed improvement at same power, or 20%–30% power improvement at same speed, and >15% chip density increase vs. N3E
- N2 tape-outs in first 2 years expected higher than both N3 and N5 in their first 2 years
- N2 profitability structurally better than N3; expected to return to corporate average faster than N3 did

## N2P
- Volume production scheduled for H2 2026

## A16
- **Production:** H2 2026 (maintained)
- Performance specs: 8%–10% speed improvement at same power vs. N2P, 15%–20% power improvement at same speed, 7%–10% chip density gain
- Power efficiency improvement close to 20%
- Strong customer engagement

## A14 (New Technology Announced)
- Features second-generation nanosheet transistor
- Performance vs. N2: 10%–15% speed improvement at same power, or 20%–30% power improvement at same speed, ~20% chip density gain
- Volume production scheduled for 2028
- Super Power Rail offering planned for 2029

## Overseas Fabs
- **Total U.S. investment:** USD 165 billion
- **U.S. expansion:** 6 advanced wafer manufacturing fabs, 2 advanced packaging fabs, and a major R&D center
- **Arizona Fab 1:** Entered high-volume production Q4 2024 using N4; yield comparable to Taiwan
- **Arizona Fab 2:** Construction already complete (N3 technology); speeding up volume production by several quarters
- **Arizona Fab 3:** Construction begun (N2 and A16 technologies)
- After completion, ~30% of 2nm and more advanced capacity in Arizona
- **Japan Kumamoto Fab 1:** Volume production started late 2024 with very good yield
- **Japan Fab 2:** Construction scheduled to start later in 2025
- **Taiwan:** Plans to build 11 wafer manufacturing fabs and 4 advanced packaging facilities over next several years
- **U.S. ITC:** Increased from 25% to 35%
- **Overseas dilution:** 2%–3% in early stages, widening to 3%–4% in later stages

## CoWoS / Advanced Packaging
- Capacity expanding; still tight
- Revenue from packaging growing significantly
- Enabling AI chip production at scale

## Manufacturing Efficiency
- 85%–90% common/shared tools between N7, N5, N3, and N2 nodes
- N3 cycle time: approximately 4 months
- 1% productivity gain equals approximately USD 1 billion for TSMC

## Edge AI / On-Device AI
- On-device AI die size increase: about 5%–10%

## Foundry 2.0 / Industry
- Semiconductor industry healthy
- Foundry 2.0 growth strong
- Non-AI demand recovery continuing

## Dividend
- Sustainable and steadily increasing

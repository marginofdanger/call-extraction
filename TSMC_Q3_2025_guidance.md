# TSMC Q3 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 16, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2025

---

## Q3 2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $33.1B (+10.1% QoQ in USD; +6% QoQ in NT)
- **Gross Margin:** 59.5% (+90 bps QoQ; exceeded guidance high end by 200 bps)
- **Operating Margin:** 50.6% (+100 bps QoQ)
- **EPS:** TWD 17.44 (+39% YoY)
- **ROE:** 37.8%
- **FX actual:** 1 USD = 29.91 TWD (vs. guidance of 29.0 TWD)

### Q3 Technology Node Breakdown
- **3nm:** 23% of Q3 wafer revenue
- **5nm:** 37% of Q3 wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 14% of Q3 wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 74% of Q3 wafer revenue

### Q3 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 57% of Q3 revenue (flat QoQ)
- **Smartphone:** 30% of Q3 revenue (+19% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 5% of Q3 revenue (+20% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 5% of Q3 revenue (+18% QoQ)
- **DCE:** 1% of Q3 revenue (-20% QoQ)

### Q3 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 2.8 trillion (USD 90 billion)
- **Current liabilities:** decreased TWD 101B QoQ (TWD 112B decrease in accrued liabilities; paid TWD 136B in 2025 provisional tax)
- **AR turnover days:** 25 days (+2 days QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 74 days (-2 days QoQ)

### Q3 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 427 billion
- **CapEx:** TWD 287 billion (USD 9.7 billion)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 117 billion (Q4 2024 dividend)
- **Cash balance:** increased TWD 106B to TWD 2.5 trillion at end of Q3

## Quarterly Guidance (Q4 2025)
- **Revenue:** $32.2B–$33.4B (~1% sequential decrease or ~22% YoY increase at midpoint)
- **Gross Margin:** 59%–61% (midpoint 60%; +50 bps QoQ at midpoint)
- **Operating Margin:** 49%–51%
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 30.6 TWD

## Full Year 2025 Guidance
- **Revenue growth:** Close to mid-30s percent in USD terms (raised from "close to 30%")
- **CapEx:** $40B–$42B (narrowed from $38B–$42B)
- **Full year GM:** Mid- to high 50s% (maintained)
- **AI accelerator revenue CAGR:** Mid-40s% over next 5 years (maintained)
- **AI accelerator revenue 2025:** Doubling YoY (maintained)

### CapEx Allocation
- ~70% for advanced process technologies
- ~10%–20% for specialty technologies
- ~10%–20% for advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others

## 2026 Outlook (Preliminary)
- Expect another healthy growth year
- AI demand continuing strong
- N2 ramping to larger volume

## Long-Term Targets
- **Revenue CAGR:** Close to 20% over next 5 years
- **Gross Margin:** 53% and higher

## N2 Technology
- Volume production starting Q4 2025 (on track)
- Yield comparable to N3 at same stage
- Strong customer pull
- Faster N2 ramp expected in 2026, fueled by both smartphone and HPC AI applications

## N2P
- Volume production scheduled for H2 2026

## A16
- **Production:** H2 2026 (maintained)
- Customer tape-outs progressing

## Overseas Fabs
- **Arizona Fab 1:** Volume production progressing; yield gap narrowing vs Taiwan
- **Arizona Fab 2:** Construction complete; working on speeding up volume production
- TSMC close to securing a second large piece of land nearby in Arizona
- Two advanced packaging fabs planned in Arizona
- OSAT partner already breaking ground in Arizona (schedule earlier than TSMC's packaging fabs)
- **Japan Fab 1:** Full production; high quality
- **Japan Fab 2:** Construction on track
- **Germany:** Construction progressing
- **Taiwan:** Preparing multiple phases of 2nm fab in both Hsinchu and Kaohsiung Science Parks
- **Overseas dilution:** closer to 2% in H2 2025; widens to 3%–4% in latter stages (beyond 2025)

## Gross Margin Dynamics
- **Full year 2025:** Mid- to high 50s%
- N3 dilution expected to catch up to corporate average sometime in 2026
- N2 structural profitability is better than N3
- Overseas fab dilution closer to 2% in H2 2025
- FX sensitivity: every 1% move of USD against TWD affects gross margin by ~40 bps
- N2 typical dilution pattern: 2%–3% for first 7–8 quarters of mass production
- Value-selling contributing positively
- Electricity and depreciation as headwinds

## AI Revenue / Demand
- Advanced packaging revenue approaching close to 10% of total revenue
- 1 gigawatt of AI data center requires ~$50B investment (per customer)
- Customer engagement lead time now at least 2–3 years in advance
- TSMC has more than 500 different customers across all end market segments

## Dividend
- Sustainable and steadily increasing

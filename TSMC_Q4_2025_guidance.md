# TSMC Q4 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** January 15, 2026 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 2025 (Full Year 2025)

---

## Q4 2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $33.7B (+1.9% QoQ in USD; +5.7% QoQ in NT)
- **Gross Margin:** 62.3% (+280 bps QoQ; exceeded high end of guidance by 130 bps, driven by better-than-expected cost improvement efforts)
- **Operating Margin:** 54.0% (+340 bps QoQ)
- **Operating Expenses:** 8.4% of net revenue (down from 8.9% in Q3 due to operating leverage)
- **EPS:** TWD 19.5
- **ROE:** 38.8%
- **FX actual:** 1 USD = 31.01 TWD (vs. guidance of 30.6 TWD)

### Q4 Technology Node Breakdown
- **3nm:** 28% of Q4 wafer revenue
- **5nm:** 35% of Q4 wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 14% of Q4 wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 77% of Q4 wafer revenue

### Q4 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 55% of Q4 revenue (+4% QoQ)
- **Smartphone:** 32% of Q4 revenue (+11% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 5% of Q4 revenue (+3% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 5% of Q4 revenue (-1% QoQ)
- **DCE:** 1% of Q4 revenue (-22% QoQ)

### Q4 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 3.1 trillion (USD 98 billion)
- **Current liabilities:** increased TWD 182B QoQ (TWD 95B from accrued liabilities, TWD 61B from reclassification of bonds payable to current portion)
- **AR turnover days:** 26 days (+1 day QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 74 days (flat QoQ)

### Q4 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 726 billion
- **CapEx:** TWD 357 billion (USD 11.5 billion)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 130 billion (Q1 2025 dividend)
- **Cash balance:** increased TWD 297B to TWD 2.8 trillion at end of Q4

## Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2026)
- **Revenue:** $34.6B–$35.8B (+4% QoQ; +38% YoY at midpoint)
- **Gross Margin:** 63%–65% (midpoint 64%; +170 bps QoQ, driven by continued cost improvement efforts including productivity gains and higher utilization, partially offset by overseas fab dilution)
- **Operating Margin:** 54%–56% (midpoint 55%)
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 31.6 TWD

## Full Year 2026 Guidance
- **Revenue growth:** Close to 30% in USD terms
- **CapEx:** $52B–$56B (up from $40.9B in 2025)
- **CapEx allocation:** 70%–80% for advanced process technologies; ~10% for specialty technologies; 10%–20% for advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others
- **AI accelerator revenue CAGR:** Mid- to high 50s% over 5 years (2024–2029) (raised from mid-40s%)
- **AI accelerator revenue 2025 actual:** High teens% of total revenue
- **Foundry 2.0 industry:** Expected to grow 14% YoY in 2026
- **Dividend:** At least TWD 23/share in 2026
- **Tax rate:** 17%–18%
- **Depreciation:** Increasing by high teens percentage YoY in 2026, mainly as N2 ramps
- **Advanced packaging revenue:** climbing to just over 10% of total revenue in 2026; expected to grow faster than corporate average over next 5 years

## Long-Term Targets (Raised)
- **Revenue CAGR:** Approach 25% CAGR in USD terms for 2024–2029 period (raised from "close to 20%")
- **Gross Margin:** 56% and higher through the cycle (RAISED from 53% and higher)
- **ROE:** High 20s% through the cycle
- **Dividend:** Sustainable and steadily increasing per share on both annual and quarterly basis

## N2 Technology
- Successfully entered high-volume manufacturing in Q4 2025, at both Hsinchu and Kaohsiung
- Volume production ramping; becoming meaningful revenue contributor in 2026
- N2 will be a bigger node than N3 from the start (in revenue dollar terms)
- Yield on track
- N2P scheduled for volume production in H2 2026
- N2 dilution: 2–3 pts for full year 2026

## A16
- **Production:** Volume production on track for H2 2026
- Backside power delivery (SPR)
- Strong customer engagement; almost all innovators working with TSMC

## N2 Margin Dilution
- N2 initially dilutive: expect 2–3 pts dilution for full year 2026
- N2 should return to historical timelines for reaching corporate average performance
- Structurally better profitability than N3

## Overseas Fabs
- **Arizona Fab 1:** High-volume production started Q4 2024; yield achieved parity with Taiwan
- **Arizona Fab 2:** Construction complete; tool installation in 2026; HVM H2 2027 (moved forward from 2028)
- **Arizona Fab 3:** Construction already started
- **Arizona Fab 4 + advanced packaging fab:** Permits being applied for
- **Second large piece of land purchased** nearby for future expansion
- Independent giga-fab cluster in Arizona for smartphone, AI, HPC applications
- After completion, ~30% of 2nm and more advanced capacity in Arizona
- **Japan Fab 1:** Volume production started late 2024; very good yield
- **Japan Fab 2:** Construction started; technology and ramp based on customer need
- **Germany:** Construction progressing
- **Taiwan:** Preparing multiple N2 fabs in Hsinchu and Kaohsiung Science Parks; plans to build 11 wafer manufacturing fabs and 4 advanced packaging facilities over next several years
- **Overseas fab dilution:** 2–3% in early stages; widening to 3–4% in later stages as more fabs come online

## Pricing
- Strategic value-selling continues
- Technology value increasing
- Earning the right return with increasing value in supply chain
- Blended wafer ASPs up ~20% in 2025 (second consecutive year); pricing benefits roughly covered inflation from tools, equipment, materials, labor

## N3 Margin Trajectory
- N3 gross margin expected to cross over to corporate average sometime in 2026
- Leveraging cross-node capacity optimization (N7, N5, N3) to support profitability
- N3 no longer a material dilution headwind

## CoWoS / Advanced Packaging
- Capacity continuing to expand
- CoWoS-L, SoIC advancing

## Gross Margin 2026 Puts & Takes
- **Tailwinds:** Higher utilization rate (moderately increasing), N3 crossing corporate average, value-selling, productivity/manufacturing excellence, cross-node capacity optimization
- **Headwinds:** Overseas dilution 2–3% early / 3–4% later, N2 ramp (2–3 pts), FX uncertainty, depreciation growth (high teens% YoY)
- Full year GM: 56% and higher long-term target; GM trajectory continues at elevated levels

## Four Growth Platforms
- AI, smartphone, HPC, IoT, automotive
- AI as dominant growth driver; high teens% of 2025 revenue
- All 4 platforms fueling growth in next several years
- Smartphone: AI smartphone contributing
- HPC: 58% of 2025 revenue (+48% YoY)
- Automotive: 34% YoY growth in 2025
- IoT: 15% YoY growth in 2025

- Recent AI market developments "very positive"
- Increasing AI model adoption across consumer, enterprise, sovereign AI
- Customers' customers (CSPs) directly requesting capacity — strong signals
- Engagement lead time now at least 2–3 years in advance
- Conviction in multiyear AI megatrend remains strong
- Demand for semiconductor "very fundamental"

## Power Supply
- Not a constraint to expansion plans

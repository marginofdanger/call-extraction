Delivery Speed

  • Q2 2025: Set another global speed record; delivered to Prime members at fastest speeds ever (per CEO)
  • Same-Day / Next-Day (U.S.): Delivered 30% more items same-day or next-day than Q2 2024 (per CEO)
  • Direct Lanes: Share of orders moving through direct lanes (straight from fulfillment to delivery) increased over 40% YoY (per CEO)
  • Average Distance: Packages traveled 12% less distance (per CEO)
  • Handling Touches: Lowered handling touches per unit by nearly 15% (per CEO)
  • Rural / Small City Expansion: Plans to expand same-day and next-day delivery to tens of millions of U.S. customers in 4,000+ smaller cities, towns and rural communities by year-end; already available in 1,000+ communities (per CEO)

Fulfillment & Outbound Costs

Capital Investments

Q2 2025 Cash CapEx

  • Cash CapEx: $31.4B (per CFO)

AWS Strategic Commentary

AI Business

  • Scale: Multibillion-dollar annual revenue run rate (per CEO)
  • Growth: Triple-digit year-over-year percentage growth (per CEO: "a large fast-growing triple-digit year-over-year percentage multibillion-dollar business")
  • Demand: "More demand than we have supplied for at the moment" (per CEO)

AWS Backlog

  • Q2 2025 Backlog: $195B (+25% YoY; per Dave Fildes) -- up from $189B (+20% YoY) in Q1 2025

Capacity Constraints (CEO Jassy, Q&A)

  • Current State: Demand exceeds capacity (per CEO)
  • Single Biggest Constraint: Power (per CEO)
  • Other Constraints: Chips (supply intermittent), server components (once chips arrive, yields on server builds can disappoint), new chip generations sometimes late (per CEO)
  • Relief Timeline: "I don't believe that we will have fully resolved the amount of capacity we need for the amount of demand that we have in a couple of quarters. I think it will take several quarters. But I do expect that it's going to get better each quarter" (per CEO)
  • Industry-Wide: "It's really true across the industry today" (per CEO)

AWS Acceleration Potential (CEO Jassy, Q&A with Brian Nowak)

  • No Segment Guidance Given: "We don't give guidance by segment" (per CEO)
  • Optimistic Drivers: (1) More enterprises resuming march to modernize infrastructure and move from on-premises to cloud; (2) AI accelerating as more companies deploy AI applications into production at scale; (3) More capacity coming online in coming months and quarters (per CEO)
  • On Competitive Positioning: CEO noted AWS has "meaningfully larger business" than #2 player (estimated at ~65% of AWS size); competitive advantage in operational performance, availability, durability, latency/throughput, security, and end-to-end AI stack functionality (per CEO)
  • 85-90% Still On-Premises: CEO reiterated that 85-90% of worldwide IT spend is still on-premises; expects equation to flip over next 10-15 years, further accelerated by AI (per CEO)

Inference Cost Imperative (CEO Jassy, Q&A)

  • Training vs. Inference Split: "At scale, 80% to 90% of the cost will be an inference because you only train periodically, but you're spinning out predictions and inferences all the time" (per CEO)
  • Trainium2 Price Performance: "About 30% and 40% better price performance than the other GPU providers out there right now" (per CEO)
  • Trainium3 in Development: "We're already working on our third version of Trainium as well" (per CEO)

Custom Silicon Strategy

Trainium2

  • Status: Landing capacity in larger quantities (per CEO)
  • Key Customer: Anthropic's newest Claude models trained on Trainium2; backbone of Amazon Bedrock (per CEO)
  • Price Performance: ~30-40% better than other GPU providers (per CEO)

Trainium3

  • Status: In development; CEO confirmed "already working on our third version of Trainium" (per CEO)

NVIDIA Partnership

  • EC2 Instances: Launched EC2 instances powered by NVIDIA Grace Blackwell Superchips -- "AWS' most powerful NVIDIA GPU accelerated instance" (per CEO)
  • Long-Term Commitment: "We have a very deep partnership with NVIDIA and will for as long as I can foresee" (per CEO)

Graviton (CPU Custom Silicon)

  • Performance: ~40% more price performance than other leading x86 processors (per CEO)

AI Platform & Services

Bedrock

  • Claude 4 (Anthropic): Recently added; "fastest-growing model ever in Bedrock" (per CEO)
  • Amazon Nova: Second most popular foundation model in Bedrock (per CEO); new customization features allow organizations to infuse unique expertise while optimizing cost and speed (per CEO)

Agent Infrastructure

  • Strands (Open Source): Released May 2025; 2,500 GitHub stars; 300,000+ downloads; makes it easier to build agents (per CEO)
  • AgentCore: Just released; industry's first secure serverless runtime for agent deployment; provides synchronous/asynchronous execution, identity and boundaries, memory service, MCP-compatible gateway, code execution tools, web browser tools, observability service (per CEO)

Applications (Top of Stack)

  • AWS Transform: Agent that reduces mainframe modernization from years to months; VMware TC2 conversions up to 80x faster; .NET Windows to .NET Linux migrations reducing licensing costs by up to 40% (per CEO)
  • Kiro (Agentic IDE): New coding agent IDE; several hundred thousand developers using/requesting access in first couple of weeks; 100,000 users in first 5 days of preview (per CEO)
  • Kiro Differentiation: Combines "vibe coding" with structured spec building; event-driven agent hooks for automated test updates, API documentation refresh, security credential scanning (per CEO)

Tariff Impact & Consumer Demand

Current Observations (CEO Jassy)

  • No Demand Diminishment: "Through the first half of the year, we haven't yet seen diminishing demand nor prices meaningfully appreciating" (per CEO)
  • No Broad ASP Increases: No broad-scale ASP increases observed in H1 2025 (per CEO)
  • Uncertainty Remains: "It's impossible to know what will happen. Where will tariffs finally settle especially China? What happens when we deplete the inventory we forward bought" (per CEO)
  • Seller Diversity Advantage: Over 2 million sellers with different strategies on whether to pass on higher costs; customers advantaged by shopping at Amazon because "more likely to find lower prices" (per CEO)
  • CEO: "Much of it [tariff noise] thus far has been wrong and misreported"

Forward Risk Factors

  • Inventory Depletion: Unknown what happens when prebuys and forward-deployed inventory are depleted (per CEO)
  • Cost Absorption: Unclear who will absorb higher costs if tariffs persist (per CEO)
  • Q3 Guide Reflects Tariff Uncertainty: Guidance range incorporates range of assumptions about macro environment including tariffs (per CFO)

Alexa+

Rollout Status

  • Current Access: Millions of U.S. customers have access (per CEO: "millions of customers have access now")
  • U.S. Completion: Rest of U.S. coming in next couple of months (per CEO)
  • International Rollout: Starting more broadly later in the year (per CEO)
  • Customer Reception: Very positive feedback; ratings very high; usage much more expansive than prior Alexa; number of calls meaningfully higher (per CEO)

Monetization Potential (CEO Jassy, Q&A)

  • Pricing: Prime members get Alexa+ for free; non-Prime pay $9.99/month (per CEO; down from $19.99/month cited in Q1 call)
  • Revenue Levers (CEO): 1. Device sales (customers excited about Alexa+-enabled devices) 2. Shopping (delightful shopping experience) 3. Advertising (multiturn conversations create ad opportunities) 4. Subscription revenue beyond current pricing over time

Project Kuiper

Status

  • Third Successful Launch: Recently completed (per CEO)
  • Pre-Commercial Agreements: "Impressive amount of enterprise and government customers who have signed agreements to use Kuiper" even before commercial launch (per CEO)
  • Target Market: 400M-500M households worldwide without broadband connectivity (per CEO)
  • Competitive Landscape: Will be one of only 2 players with modern technology in low Earth orbit satellite (per CEO)
  • Differentiators: Expected advantage in uplink/downlink performance; compelling pricing; seamless AWS integration attractive to enterprises and governments (per CEO)
  • Commercialization Timeline: "Later this year or early next year" (per CEO)
  • Delays: Some delays with rocket providers; Amazon has "most of the available rocket launches over the next couple of years" (per CEO)

Internal AI Adoption (CEO Jassy, Q&A)

  • Company Approach: Fully embracing AI internally -- coding, analytics, research, finance, business process automation, customer service (per CEO)
  • Key Tools: Kiro (coding agent), Connect (AI-powered call center software), various internal agents under development (per CEO)
  • Strategic Posture: CEO published blog post in June outlining intent to have every area of the business adopt AI tools; goal is to allow teammates to "invent for customers much more quickly and expansively" (per CEO)

Key Analyst Q&A Data Points

Tariff Impact & AWS Growth (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan)

  • CEO on Tariffs: No diminished demand or broad ASP increases in H1; uncertain where tariffs will settle, especially China; prebuying has buffered impact so far; 2M+ seller diversity an advantage for customers
  • CEO on AWS vs. Competitors: $123B annual run rate is meaningfully larger than #2 (~65% of AWS size); competitive advantage in operational performance, security (citing recent "adventures" at competitors), and functionality; demand exceeds capacity

AWS Backlog & Capacity Constraints (Mark Mahaney, Evercore)

  • Dave Fildes: Backlog $195B at June 30 (+25% YoY)
  • CEO on Supply Constraints: Single biggest constraint is power; also chip supply, server component yields, chip generation timing; will take "several quarters" to fully resolve but expects improvement each quarter
  • Alexa+ Monetization: CEO outlined device sales, shopping, advertising, and subscription as revenue levers; $9.99/month for non-Prime; "still very early days"

International Segment (Colin Sebastian, Baird)

  • CFO: 4.1% operating margin, +320 bps YoY; ~700 bps improvement over 10 quarters; established countries (UK, Germany, Japan) at U.S.-like margins; 8 emerging countries launched in 5 years, all progressing
  • CEO on Kuiper: 2 players with modern LEO satellite tech; expects performance and pricing advantage; enterprise/government agreements already signed; commercialization later this year or early next year

AWS AI Strategy (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley)

  • CEO on AI Narrative: "It is so early right now in AI"; activity is top-heavy (small number of large frontier models + large-scale apps like ChatGPT and coding agents); vast majority of gen AI applications still in pilot/development; agents barely getting started
  • CEO on Inference Economics: 80-90% of cost at scale will be inference, not training; Trainium2 at 30-40% better price performance than other GPUs; Trainium3 in development
  • CEO on Services Stack: SageMaker AI for model building, Bedrock for leveraging frontier models, Strands for building agents, AgentCore for deploying agents at scale
  • CEO on Acceleration: Won't give segment guidance, but optimistic about (1) enterprise cloud migration resumption, (2) AI applications moving to production at scale, (3) more capacity coming online

Internal AI Adoption (Ron Josey, Citi)

  • CEO: AI will change every area of work -- coding, analytics, research, finance, customer service, business process automation; Amazon is fully embracing it; blog post in June set company direction; tools like Kiro and Connect already in use

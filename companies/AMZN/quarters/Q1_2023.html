AWS Growth & Optimization -- CRITICAL FORWARD COMMENTARY

Customer Optimization Dynamics

  • Customers of all sizes in all industries continue to look for cost savings
  • Optimization efforts continue into Q2; "expected" continuation
  • Customers are "cost optimizing versus cost cutting" -- important distinction per CEO Jassy
  • Customers say they are cost optimizing to "reallocate those resources on new customer experiences"
  • AWS sales teams actively helping customers optimize their spend
  • Projects to rebuild/reallocate take time to build before implementation

AWS Margin Trajectory

  • Q1 2023 operating margin: 24.0% (vs. 35.3% in Q1 2022; vs. 24.3% in Q4 2022)
  • Sequential margin roughly flat; includes $270M AWS severance
  • AWS OI ex-severance: ~$5.4B, or ~25.3% margin

Generative AI / Large Language Models -- KEY STRATEGIC INITIATIVE

Three-Layer Framework (CEO Jassy)

  • All large language models require compute; key is the chips in that compute
  • GPUs currently "expensive and scarce" -- hard to find enough capacity
  • Trainium: AWS custom chip specialized for ML training; second version released
  • Inferentia: AWS custom chip specialized for inference/predictions; second version released
  • Price/performance combination described as "pretty differentiated and very significant"
  • Most current spend on training; as models move to production, "all the spend is going to be in inference"
  • Bedrock: Managed foundational model service (announced ~1 week prior to call)
  • Allows customers to run foundational models from Amazon (Titan) or third parties (AI21, Anthropic, Stability AI)
  • Customers can customize models with proprietary data without leaking IP to the generalized model
  • Runs with same security, privacy, and features as other AWS applications
  • Described as "very compelling for customers"
  • Only "a small number of companies" will invest time and billions to build their own LLMs
  • Applications built on top of LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT as an example)
  • CodeWhisperer: AWS coding assistant; general availability announced; developers input natural language, get code output
  • Every Amazon business building on top of LLMs to "reinvent customer experiences"
  • Expected to transform "virtually every customer experience that exists and many that don't exist"

Alexa / Echo Integration

  • Building a "much larger and much more generalized and capable" LLM underneath Alexa
  • ~200 million endpoints in use across entertainment, shopping, smart home, information
  • Vision: world's best personal assistant; LLMs "really accelerate the possibility"
  • "Significant business model underneath it" per CEO Jassy

Cost Structure & Fulfillment Regionalization

Regionalization Rollout (CEO Jassy)

  • U.S. fulfillment network transitioned from national to regional model
  • 8 interconnected regions created in geographic areas, each with broad relevant selection
  • Each region operates in a "largely self-sufficient way" while still able to ship nationally when necessary
  • Rollout "just recently completed" as of Q1 2023
  • "Quite bullish on the early results" -- shorter travel distances mean lower cost to serve and faster delivery

Delivery Speed

  • Providing more 1-day and same-day deliveries
  • "On track to have our fastest Prime delivery speeds ever in 2023"

Cost to Serve Progress

  • Revenue and unit sales growth outpaced fulfillment expense and outbound shipping cost growth in Q1
  • Inflationary pressures easing: reductions in linehaul shipping rates, lower diesel fuel and electricity costs
  • Continued process and technology improvements in fulfillment productivity
  • Exited Q4 with good balance of labor; leveraged that through Q1

Margin Recovery Path

  • North America operating margin Q1 2023: 1.2%
  • Pre-pandemic target range: 4%-6% (per CFO Olsavsky)
  • CFO: "Making solid progress on recovering our cost structure and getting it back to pre-pandemic levels"
  • CEO: "Pretty optimistic that we have a chance not just to recover to where we were pre pandemic in terms of operating margin, but I think there's additional upside with some of the opportunities we've identified"
  • Described as "a longer road than bouncing back in 1 or 2 quarters"

Grocery Strategy Update

Current State

  • Large existing grocery business in non-temperature-controlled items (canned goods, packaged food, paper products, pet supplies, personal care, health/beauty, consumables)
  • Consumables performing "very, very strong" in current cautious consumer environment

Whole Foods

  • Continues to grow nicely
  • Multiple changes made in last year that "changed the profitability trajectory" positively
  • Still a portion of overall market segment

Amazon Fresh

  • "Wish we were further along at this point" -- CEO Jassy
  • Tried lots of ideas; haven't yet found conviction around format for broader expansion
  • "Set of experiments and ideas and concepts" across dozens of stores
  • "Pretty optimistic that we have something that may very well work"
  • Hopeful to find format "over this next year"

International E-Commerce

Q1 Performance

  • Revenue accelerated to +9% ex-FX (vs. +5% ex-FX in Q4 2022)
  • Europe economy stabilizing; consumer confidence increasing; inflation ticking down
  • Upside versus internal expectations in Q1

Structure

  • Established countries: Already profitable; look similar to North America at earlier development stage; working toward profitability parity; forward-loaded Prime benefits
  • Emerging markets: Added 10+ new countries in last 5 years; similar profitability curve to early North America (9 years to breakeven in US); additional challenges (payment methods, infrastructure); slower adoption but good trajectory
  • Prime Video driving strong adoption, traffic, and new customer acquisition in emerging countries

Health Care Strategy (CEO Jassy)

Amazon Pharmacy

  • Launched 2020; "off to a good start"; continuing to grow

One Medical Acquisition

  • Compelling digital app (chat/video with practitioners)
  • Same-day/next-day appointments at physical clinics
  • Relationships with health specialists in each city
  • Integration with Amazon Pharmacy for automatic medicine delivery
  • Aims to fundamentally change the primary care experience

Kuiper (Low Earth Orbit Satellite)

  • Target market: hundreds of millions of households, businesses, and government entities with limited/no Internet connectivity
  • Use cases: online education, business operations, entertainment, shopping, government coverage
  • Strong customer interest across consumer, business, and government segments
  • Designs released; "pretty exciting" customer reception
  • Believed to have "the chance to be a very large business"

  • Health care & Kuiper as big bets (Justin Post, BofA): CEO used investment framework (big enough, underserved, differentiated, competence) to justify; health care is multitrillion dollar broken market; Kuiper addresses hundreds of millions without connectivity; chose as examples of non-obvious investments similar to how AWS seemed unlikely in 2003

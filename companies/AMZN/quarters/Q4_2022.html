Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2023)

Key Guidance Assumptions & Commentary

  • Q4 actual FX headwind: ~360 bps on revenue (better than ~460 bps guided for Q4)
  • Q1 2023 guided FX headwind: ~210 bps (continued moderation)
  • FX slight favorability on operating income continues ($213M in Q4)
  • Consumers remain cautious about spending; shifting to lower-priced items, value brands, and everyday essentials over discretionary categories
  • Strong holiday season: customers came to Amazon for deals, fast delivery, and widest-ever selection
  • First-ever Prime Early Access Sale in October and traditional Thanksgiving-Cyber Monday holiday weekend both outperformed expectations
  • Third-party sellers comprised record 59% of unit sales in Q4
  • Advertising grew 23% ex-FX despite macro pressures on marketing budgets
  • Customer spending on everyday essentials (consumables, beauty, softlines) continues
  • Discretionary categories (e.g., electronics) seeing trade-down to lower-priced items
  • Q4 2022 reported growth: 20% YoY (down from 28% ex-FX in Q3 2022)
  • January 2023 run rate: "So far in the first month of the year, AWS year-over-year revenue growth is in the mid-teens" -- CFO Olsavsky
  • Forward outlook: "We expect these optimization efforts will continue to be a headwind to AWS growth in at least the next couple of quarters"
  • Deceleration trajectory: 40% (Q4 2021) -> 37% (Q1 2022) -> 33% (Q2 2022) -> 28% (Q3 2022) -> 20% (Q4 2022) -> mid-teens (Jan 2023)
  • New customer pipeline remains healthy and robust; many customers continuing to plan cloud migrations
  • 90-95% of global IT spend remains on-premises; long-term cloud migration tailwind intact
  • Cost optimization by customers: deferring workloads, switching to lower-cost products, running calculations less frequently, changing storage tiers
  • Industries with specific weakness: financial services, mortgage companies (volume-driven), crypto (lower trading), advertising-linked analytics
  • Entered Q4 with labor more appropriately matched to demand vs. Q4 2021
  • Productivity gains in fulfillment centers: in line with plan
  • Transportation network: overperformed expectations; higher Amazon Logistics productivity, improved line haul fill rates
  • Good leverage driven by strong holiday volumes
  • Overall strong effort by operations team
  • Continued focus on reducing cost to serve while maintaining customer experience
  • 2023 outlook: "We have to make and expect to make big improvements in 2023" -- CFO Olsavsky

Long-Term Positioning

  • 90-95% of global IT spend remains on-premises; massive runway for cloud migration
  • AWS has significantly more capability, stronger security, larger partner ecosystem
  • Historical precedent: 2020 downturn led to resurgence and acceleration of cloud journeys post-recovery
  • Question is whether same pattern will repeat with current optimization cycle

Cost Optimization / Restructuring Initiatives

Layoffs & Role Eliminations

  • 18,000+ roles eliminated (announced in Q4, largely executed in January 2023)
  • Primarily impacting: Stores business, Devices business, Human Resources teams
  • Severance cost: $640M (accrued in Q4 2022)
  • Timing: Most terminations occurred in January; Q4 accrual covers post-separation severance/benefits; Q1 2023 will carry ~3 weeks salary for those terminated in January
  • Hardest decision the leadership team has been part of (per CEO Jassy)

Program Closures & Slowdowns

  • Closed: Amazon 4-Star bookstores, Fabric.com, Amazon Care, Amazon Glo, Amazon Explore
  • Slowed: Physical store expansion (Amazon Fresh) -- paused until a differentiated, economically viable format is found; optimistic about finding right equation in 2023
  • Slowed: Some devices (went slower on certain device investments)
  • Delayed/Mothballed: Some fulfillment center builds in early 2022 to align capacity with demand

Physical Store Impairments

  • $720M impairment primarily related to Amazon Fresh and Amazon Go stores
  • Continuously refining store formats to find ones that resonate with customers
  • Exiting certain stores with low growth potential
  • Impairment covers capitalized costs and associated values of leased buildings

Cost to Serve Reduction

  • #1 priority per CEO Jassy: reducing cost to serve in operations network
  • Fulfillment center footprint built over 25 years was doubled in just a couple of years
  • Built last-mile transportation network roughly the size of UPS in a couple of years
  • Network now has many more nodes to optimize; requires figuring out flows, utilization, and efficiency across all links
  • Pleased with Q4 progress; work will extend into 2023

Self-Insurance Reserve

  • $1.3B increase in reserves for general product and automobile self-insurance liabilities
  • Driven by business growth (especially fulfillment and transportation network expansion), industry-wide claim inflation
  • Covers both asserted and unasserted claims

AWS Strategic Commentary

Customer Cost Optimization

  • Enterprise customers of all sizes evaluating ways to optimize cloud spending
  • Optimization efforts started mid-Q3 2022 and continued through Q4; expected to persist for at least the next couple of quarters
  • Key benefit of cloud: ability to handle demand swings and optimize costs quickly during uncertainty
  • Helping customers save money is in Amazon's DNA; focused on long-term relationships, not quarterly optimization
  • Elasticity of cloud: can scale up for demand surges or give back capacity when not needed -- something not possible on-premises

Demand by Industry (Q4 detail)

  • Financial services: Lower demand; mortgage companies seeing lower compute as mortgage volumes declined
  • Crypto: Lower trading volumes reducing compute needs
  • Advertising-linked: Less analytics and compute on advertising spend as ad budgets tightened
  • Broad-based: Most enterprises cautious about infrastructure expense; not limited to specific sectors

Customer Actions to Reduce AWS Bills

  • Switch to lower-cost products (e.g., Graviton instances)
  • Run calculations/analytics less frequently (e.g., 2 weeks of data instead of 90 days)
  • Change storage tiers for data
  • Defer workloads where possible
  • Not necessarily best long-term decisions, but common in uncertain economic environments

New Customer Pipeline

  • Robust and healthy despite optimization headwinds
  • New deals, new workloads continuing to come to cloud
  • Companies during periods of discontinuity often strategically reassess and plan cloud migrations
  • Absolute dollar growth YoY still significantly more than any competitor in the space (per management's best estimation)

AWS Margin Drivers

  • Q4 operating margin: 24.3% (vs. 29.8% in Q4 2021; vs. 26.3% in Q3 2022)
  • Sequential decline driven by continued cost pressures (energy, SBC, wage inflation) and slower revenue growth
  • FY 2022 operating margin: 28.5% (vs. 29.8% in FY 2021)

Grocery Strategy

Overview (CEO Jassy commentary)

  • Grocery is a "really important and strategic area" for Amazon
  • Very large market segment with high purchase frequency
  • Believes grocery will be omnichannel over time: online delivery, pickup, in-store, and hybrid models

Three Components

  • 1. Online Grocery (Amazon.com): Very large business in packaged food, paper products, canned goods, pet supplies, health/personal care, consumables; continuing to grow at rapid clip; lacks significant market share in perishables 2. Whole Foods: Significant-sized business; continuing to grow; CEO "really likes the progress on profitability in the last year"; premium product but good business in grocery space 3. Amazon Fresh (physical stores): A few dozen stores; doing "a fair bit of experimentation" to find differentiated format with attractive economics; paused expansion until equation is right; optimistic about finding right format in 2023; encouraging signs

International E-Commerce Strategy

Established Markets (CEO Jassy commentary)

  • Pre-pandemic CAGR (2019-2021): UK >30%, Germany 26%, Japan 21% (all net of FX)
  • Meaningful market share gains during pandemic period; have not given back that growth
  • Now large enough to be impacted by macro headwinds (European inflation, Ukraine war energy impact)

Emerging Markets

  • India, Brazil, Middle East, Africa, Turkey, Mexico, Australia
  • All on right trajectory following patterns similar to earlier North America and established international market development
  • Fixed investment required when entering new geographies; driving revenue to cover that investment
  • Believes Amazon will have a "large profitable international e-commerce business"

Investment Priorities (CEO Jassy)

Key Strategic Investments Continuing

  • Streaming entertainment and devices
  • Kuiper (low Earth orbit satellite): Remains a priority investment
  • Health care: Continued investment (One Medical acquisition context)
  • Focus on investments that could be "the fourth pillar" for Amazon

Investment Framework (Four Questions)

  • 1. If successful, could it be big enough to move the needle at Amazon? 2. Is the opportunity being well served today? 3. Does Amazon have a differentiated approach? 4. Does Amazon have (or can quickly acquire) the necessary competencies?

Cost vs. Investment Balance

  • Streamlining costs across all businesses (existing and new investments)
  • Thorough review of all investments and spending levels
  • Maintain ability to invest in key long-term strategic investments
  • Not every investment will succeed, but "it only takes one or two of them becoming the fourth pillar" to meaningfully change the company

Prime Membership

Health & Engagement

  • Prime membership remains strong
  • Dollars purchased per Prime member remain healthy (varies by geography)
  • Video properties (Thursday Night Football, Rings of Power) drove record Prime sign-ups
  • Direct link between video engagement and higher purchases of everyday products on Amazon

Value Proposition (CEO Jassy)

  • Prime costs less than many entertainment-only subscription programs ($14-15/month)
  • Includes: Prime Video, fast shipping, Prime Music, Prime Gaming, Prime Photos, Buy with Prime, grocery benefits, RxPass ($5/month unlimited eligible medications)
  • Continuously adding benefits and experimenting with new features
  • Still "early days" in making the service fully featured

Key Analyst Q&A Data Points

  • North America margin recovery path (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley): Pre-pandemic cost structure disrupted by network doubling; working to regain prior efficiency; good headway in 2022 but "have to make and expect to make big improvements in 2023"; network nodes have expanded significantly (more FCs + large transportation network); optimizing flows, utilization, and links across all facilities takes time
  • Grocery strategy (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan): Amazon Fresh paused expansion until finding right differentiated format with good economics; optimistic about finding that in 2023; Whole Foods profitability improved meaningfully in last year; online grocery growing rapidly in non-perishable categories
  • Emerging bets strategic importance (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan): Investment framework based on four criteria (scale potential, underserved opportunity, differentiated approach, competence); enthusiastic about Kuiper, health care, streaming entertainment; not every investment will succeed but need only one or two to become transformative "fourth pillar"
  • International e-commerce outlook (Eric Sheridan, Goldman Sachs): UK CAGR >30%, Germany 26%, Japan 21% (2019-2021, ex-FX); market share gains retained; emerging markets (India, Brazil, Middle East, etc.) on right trajectory; believes in "large profitable international business" long term; macro headwinds in Europe from inflation, Ukraine war, energy costs
  • Q4 severance vs. Q1 operating income (Eric Sheridan, Goldman Sachs): $640M severance charge in Q4 covers post-separation benefits; Q1 carries ~3 weeks salary for January terminations; no additional large severance charge in Q1
  • AWS growth trajectory & mid-teens sustainability (Justin Post, BofA): Mid-teens in January; broad-based optimization across industries not just select sectors; weakness in financial services, crypto, advertising-linked compute; companies cutting analysis windows (90 days to 2 weeks); robust pipeline of new customers and migrations; 90-95% of IT spend still on-premises
  • AWS market share (Justin Post, BofA): To best estimation, absolute dollar growth YoY still significantly more than any competitor; advantage from more capabilities, stronger security, larger partner ecosystem
  • AWS growth recovery timing (Justin Post, BofA): Cannot forecast with certainty; "bit uncharted territory economically"; long-term trends still favorable; quickest way to save money is to get to the cloud; 2020 downturn led to post-recovery acceleration; unclear if pattern repeats
  • AWS margin and revenue reacceleration (Justin Post, BofA): Did not directly address margin recovery timing; long-term value proposition unchanged
  • Prime engagement and wallet share (Ron Josey, Citi): Prime membership strong; spending per member healthy; video properties (TNF, Rings of Power) drove record sign-ups; direct link between engagement and everyday product purchases; third-party selection growth contributing to value; speed of delivery improvements expected to further strengthen engagement
  • AWS mid-teens holdability & enterprise demand deterioration (Mark Mahaney, Evercore): Cannot forecast beyond current quarter with certainty; "uncharted territory economically"; new deals and workloads still coming; value proposition strong; short-term belt tightening may lead to post-downturn cloud acceleration (as in 2020)
  • CEO priority changes vs. prior CEO (Mark Mahaney, Evercore): Each era is different; pandemic/labor shortage/war/inflation required adjustments; pulled back on some devices, physical stores (except grocery), some programs; leaning into health care, Kuiper, streaming; maintaining long-term investment philosophy while streamlining costs; looking for "fourth pillar" opportunities

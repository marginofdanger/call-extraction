AWS Reacceleration -- CRITICAL FORWARD COMMENTARY

Q3 2024 Performance & Trajectory

  • Q3 2024 Growth: 19.1% YoY ex-FX (sustained from 19% in Q2; 4th consecutive quarter of reacceleration)
  • Annualized Revenue Run Rate: $110B (milestone)
  • Operating Income: $10.4B (+$3.5B YoY)
  • Operating Margin: 38.1% (includes ~200 bps benefit from server useful life change)
  • AWS Growth Trajectory: 37% (Q1'22) -> 33% (Q2'22) -> 28% (Q3'22) -> 20% (Q4'22) -> 16% (Q1'23) -> 12% (Q2'23) -> 12% (Q3'23) -> 13% (Q4'23) -> 17% (Q1'24) -> 19% (Q2'24) -> 19.1% (Q3'24)
  • AI Business Run Rate: Multibillion-dollar revenue run rate; growing triple-digit percentages YoY; growing 3x faster at its stage of evolution than AWS itself grew

Three Drivers of AWS Margin (CFO Olsavsky)

  • 1. Accelerating Top Line Demand: Helps with all efficiencies and cost control efforts 2. Cost Control: Measured hiring (office staff down slightly YoY, flat to end of 2023); strong infrastructure efficiency; best at matching supply and demand 3. Server Useful Life Change: Extended server useful life in 2024; added ~200 bps of margin improvement YoY

Capacity Constraints (CEO Jassy)

  • Demand Exceeds Supply: "I believe we have more demand that we could fulfill if we had even more capacity today"
  • Chip Bottleneck: "Pretty much everyone today has less capacity than they have demand for, and it's really primarily chips that are the area where companies could use more supply"
  • Growth Could Accelerate: "The rate of growth there has a chance to improve over time as we have bigger and bigger capacity"

AWS Margin Forward Commentary (CFO Olsavsky)

  • Fluctuation Expected: "Over time, this margin will fluctuate in segment. There's a number of factors to keep in mind, including our level of investment, the amount of innovation and product development we're doing for new products and services, changes in our staffing for sales and support organizations and, of course, capital to support customer growth"
  • On Q3 Performance: "If we do that [match supply and demand well] in this business, it can result in quarters that we just saw"

AI Margins Over Time (CEO Jassy -- Q&A with Ross Sandler)

  • Early Stage: "The AI space is, for sure, earlier stage, more fluid and dynamic than our non-AI part of AWS"
  • Current vs. Future Margins: "The margins are lower than what I think they will be over time. The same was true with AWS" in early years
  • AWS Historical Analogy: Margins around 2010 were "pretty different than they are now"; market maturation drove margin expansion
  • Expectation: "As the market matures over time, there are going to be very healthy margins here in the generative AI space"

Custom Silicon Strategy (CEO Jassy)

Trainium & Inferentia

  • NVIDIA Partnership: Deep partnership; first to offer H200 GPUs through EC2 P5e instances; advantaged networking performance via Elastic Fabric Adapter and Nitro
  • Trainium2: Starting to ramp up "in the next few weeks"; "very compelling for customers on price performance"
  • Manufacturing Demand: "We've gone back to our manufacturing partners multiple times to produce much more than we'd originally planned"
  • Customer Interest: "We're seeing significant interest in these chips"; customers excited about expected price performance
  • Dual Supply Strategy: CEO: customers want both NVIDIA GPUs (deep partnership) and better price performance through custom silicon (Trainium/Inferentia)

Generative AI Strategy -- Three-Layer Framework (CEO Jassy)

Layer 1: Compute / Custom Chips (Bottom Layer -- Model Builders)

  • NVIDIA: First to offer H200 GPUs via EC2 P5e instances; networking innovations (EFA, Nitro) provide advantaged performance
  • Trainium2: Ramping in next few weeks; compelling price performance; gone back to manufacturing partners multiple times for more supply
  • SageMaker HyperPod: Automatically splits training across 1,000+ AI accelerators; saves training time by up to 40%; auto-repairs faulty instances from saved checkpoints

Layer 2: Foundational Models / Bedrock (Middle Layer)

  • Broadest Model Selection: Recently added Anthropic Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Meta Llama 3.2, Mistral Large 2, multiple Stability AI models
  • Key Capabilities: Model evaluation, guardrails, RAG, agents; multi-model orchestration
  • Traction: "Part of what makes Bedrock so appealing to customers and why it has so much traction is that Bedrock makes this much easier"

Layer 3: Applications / Amazon Q (Top Layer)

  • Positioning: "Most capable generative AI-powered assistant for software development and to leverage your own data"
  • Code Acceptance: Highest reported code acceptance rates in the industry for multiline code suggestions
  • Q Transform: Saved Amazon $260M and 4,500 developer years migrating over 30,000 applications to new Java JDK versions

Machine Learning Feature Velocity

  • CEO: "In the last 18 months, AWS has released nearly twice as many machine learning and gen AI features as the other leading cloud providers combined"

Fulfillment & Cost to Serve

Inbound Network Rearchitecture (Key Initiative)

  • CEO: "In the process of significantly changing the way we inbound items into our fulfillment network and subsequently spread them to our regional fulfillment nodes"
  • Progress: Hundreds of changes to U.S. inbound network; opened more than 15 inbound buildings
  • Inventory Placement: Improved ability to spread inventory across fulfillment centers by 25% YoY
  • Expected Benefits: Further improved inventory placement, faster delivery time, saved transportation costs, increased units shipped per box
  • Stage: "Still relatively early in this rearchitecture"

Same-Day Delivery

  • Scale: Over 40 million customers had orders delivered same-day in Q3, +25% YoY
  • CEO: "Not only the fastest way to get products to customers but also one of our lowest-cost ways to deliver"

Robotics & Automation (12th-Generation Fulfillment Center)

  • New Facility: Shreveport, Louisiana -- first facility incorporating newest robotics inventions
  • Benefits: Reduces fulfillment processing time by up to 25%; increases same-day/next-day eligible items; expected 25% improvement in cost to serve during peak
  • AI in Robotics: Recently hired team from strong robotics AI organization; AI expected to be central to future robotics work
  • CEO: "Though we believe we have more expansive automation and robotics than other retail peers, it's still early days in how much automation we expect in our fulfillment network"

Cost to Serve Progress (CFO Olsavsky)

  • CFO: "In the third quarter, we continue to make progress in improving our fulfillment network cost structure, driven in part by improved inventory placement"
  • Results: Better productivity in transportation network; higher shipping efficiency from higher units per box

International Segment

Profitability Progress

  • Q3 2024 Operating Income: $1.3B (+$1.4B YoY; vs. -$95M in Q3 2023)
  • Operating Margin: 3.6% (+390 bps YoY)
  • Year-to-Date Operating Profit: $2.5B (profitable in all 3 quarters of 2024)
  • 7th Consecutive Quarter of YoY Operating Margin Improvement

Geographic Breakdown (CFO Olsavsky)

  • Established Countries (UK, Germany, Japan): Seeing strength; driving efficiencies in on-road productivity in transportation network and better fulfillment center execution
  • Emerging Countries (~10 launched in last 7 years): Growing revenue at a healthy rate; leveraging cost structures; investing strategically in Prime benefits
  • Long-Term Target: CFO: "Our expectations in each of those countries mirror those in North America. And we intend, over the fullness of time, that we're going to aim for North America margins, which are not static themselves"
  • Drivers of Improvement: Lower cost to serve, greater advertising contribution, improved selection, faster delivery speeds driving consumer demand

Consumer Trends & Unit Economics

Unit Growth vs. ASP Dynamics

  • WW Paid Unit Growth: +12% YoY (accelerated from +11% in Q2 2024)
  • Revenue Growth: +11% YoY (unit growth outpacing revenue growth)
  • CFO: "When you look at the split between revenue growth and unit growth, you do see some impact of the lower ASP products that we're selling as well as some of the trade down that consumers are doing"
  • 3P Unit Mix: 60% in Q3 (vs. 61% in Q2; everyday essentials skew more to 1P than 3P)
  • Consumer Behavior: Customers looking for deals, price conscious; Prime events well received; saved billions for Prime members

Everyday Essentials Strategy

  • CFO: "We take that as a real positive, seeing the growth in everyday essential categories, which are really predicated on speed"
  • Stickier Relationship: Everyday essentials lead to higher orders, larger baskets (helps ship economics), stronger repeat orders
  • CFO: "We'll take any short-term degradation in ASP because what we're focused on primarily is free cash flow here"

Lower ASP Strategy (CEO Jassy)

  • CEO: "As we're able to take our cost to serve down, it just opens up the aperture for more items, particularly lower ASP items that we're able to supply in an economic way"
  • Broader Consideration Set: Lower cost to serve + broader selection + faster speed + same-day network expansion = serving more customer shopping missions

Prime Membership

  • Paid Membership: YoY paid membership growth accelerated in Q3 from both U.S. and globally
  • Prime Day Events: Helped customers save over $5 billion across more than 50 million deals
  • Delivery Speed: On track to deliver fastest speeds ever for Prime members globally for the second year in a row
  • New Benefits: Unlimited grocery delivery from Whole Foods, Amazon Fresh, and local 3P grocery partners for $9.99/month; fuel savings of $0.10/gallon at BP, Amico, ampm stations

Retail Competitive Positioning (CEO Jassy)

  • Market Share: Amazon is ~1% of worldwide global retail market share; ~80%-85% of retail market share still in physical stores
  • Secular Shift: Believe equation of online vs. physical will flip in the next 10 to 20 years; significant opportunity for multiple players
  • Key Advantages: 1. Meaningfully broader selection than almost all competitors 2. Low prices with significant deals 3. Very significant advantages in speed of delivery 4. Customer-first orientation and DNA
  • Speed Drives Conversion: "The faster that we're able to promise customers that we can get them their items, the more frequently they buy and the more they actually use Amazon"

Amazon Pharmacy

  • Delivery Speed: Can deliver to 95% of first-time Amazon Pharmacy customers in the U.S. within 2 business days
  • Same-Day Pharmacy: Can deliver to 20% of U.S. Prime members within 24 hours
  • 2025 Expansion: Plan to launch operations in 20 new cities; nearly half the U.S. will have ability to have medications delivered within hours
  • CEO: "We believe making it easier for customers to get their medications will improve medication adherence, which we know can directly improve health outcomes"

Alexa Ecosystem

  • Installed Base: ~500 million Alexa devices; ~200 million active endpoints
  • Rearchitecture: In process of rearchitecting Alexa brains with next-generation foundational models
  • CEO on Future: "If you rearchitect the brains of Alexa with next-generation foundational models...we have an opportunity to be the leader in that space"
  • Next-Gen Assistants: "The next generation of these assistants and the generative AI applications will be better at not just answering questions and summarizing the indexing and aggregating data, but also taking actions"

Kindle

  • Performance: Early sales significantly outperformed expectations; Kindle having an excellent year
  • Reading Engagement: Over 20 billion average monthly pages read on Kindle devices worldwide

  • AWS Margin Sustainability (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan): CFO: 3 drivers -- accelerating demand, cost control (measured hiring, infrastructure efficiency), server useful life change (~200 bps YoY). "Over time, this margin will fluctuate in segment." AWS margin at 38.1% in Q3.

  • Robotics Roadmap (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley): CEO: Shreveport facility is first with full-workflow robotics integration; "very encouraging results." Team invested in foundational building blocks for next-gen capabilities. AI will be central to robotics network. Recently hired from strong robotics AI organization.

Revenue vs. unit gap reflects lower ASP products and consumer trade-down. Everyday essentials skew 1P, create stickier customer relationships, bigger baskets, more repeat orders.

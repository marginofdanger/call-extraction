Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2022)

Key Guidance Assumptions & Commentary

  • Incremental Cost Headwinds Expected in Q2: ~$4B (down from ~$6B in Q1 2022) - Productivity improvements expected to reduce labor-related costs - Fixed cost deleverage to persist but narrow through the year - Inflation expected to persist (fuel, shipping, wages)
  • Stock-Based Compensation (Q2 2022): ~$6B (up from $3.3B in Q1; seasonal step-up from annual RSU grants; reflects wage inflation for engineers/tech workers)
  • Prime Day Timing: Moved to Q3 2022 (July); was in Q2 2021 where it contributed ~400 bps to Q2 2021 YoY revenue growth
  • Q2 2021 Comp Context: Continuation of elevated pandemic-driven net sales growth through roughly first half of Q2 2021; moderated in second half as vaccines became available

Strategic & Operational Updates

Fulfillment Network & Capacity

  • Doubled the size of operations over past 2 years; nearly doubled workforce to 1.6M employees
  • Labor and physical space are no longer bottlenecks (were throughout much of 2020-2021)
  • Currently have excess capacity in fulfillment and transportation network
  • Capacity decisions made 18-24 months in advance; limited ability to adjust midyear
  • Delivery speed performance approaching pre-pandemic levels (early 2020)
  • Widest selection ever available for Prime fast delivery
  • Fixed cost deleverage expected to narrow as year progresses; capacity will be needed for Q3 (Prime Day) and Q4 (holiday)

Prime Membership

  • Prime members meaningfully increased spend since start of pandemic
  • Consistently high member renewal rates
  • Added millions of new Prime members during the quarter
  • Stronger usage of Prime benefits over past 2 years; greater reliance on Amazon for shopping and entertainment
  • No significant difference between Prime and non-Prime member volume trends

Labor & Productivity

  • Started and ended Q1 at 1.6M employees; peaked at 1.7M mid-quarter
  • Omicron variant caused substantial increase in employees out on leave in early Q1
  • Hired additional employees to cover absences; quickly became overstaffed as variant subsided
  • Productivity improvements seen in last few weeks of Q1 and into April
  • Expect to reduce productivity cost headwinds in Q2 (but not full $2B recovery)
  • Not expected to reach pre-pandemic productivity rates in next quarter or two, but "a good bit of the way"

Transportation & Shipping

  • AMZL (Amazon Logistics) network provides competitive shipping rates vs. external carriers
  • Third-party carriers would not have had capacity to handle Amazon's transportation loads
  • 1-day and same-day shipments would not be cost effective with third-party shippers
  • Shipping cost growth (14% YoY) vs. flat unit growth driven by inflation and productivity factors

AWS Momentum

  • Customers include Telefonica, Verizon, Boeing, MongoDB, Amdocs, Bundesliga, Maple Leaf Sports & Entertainment, NHL, THREAD
  • Completed launch of first 16 Local Zones in the United States; 32 more planned across 26 countries
  • Local Zones extend AWS Regions to edge of cloud near population/industry/IT centers; enable single-digit millisecond latency
  • Some volatility in utility pricing for energy costs in operating data centers

Advertising

  • Advertising revenue up 25% YoY ex-FX; characterized as "strong run rate compared to the revenue growth rate"
  • Valued by sellers, vendors, and others who use it to reach customers at point of purchase consideration
  • Continuing to roll out new products for sellers to manage advertising and analyze/calculate payback on marketing investments

Consumer Demand & Macro

  • No signs of consumer softness or weakening in demand
  • March characterized as "very strong" sequentially (difficult YoY comp due to Q1 2021 stimulus payments)
  • Revenue was 61% higher in Q1 2022 vs. Q1 2020 on a 2-year stack
  • Cognizant of household budget pressures from inflation; historically Amazon benefits as consumers seek value (great prices, selection, convenience)
  • Pandemic-driven elevated growth period: mid-May 2020 to mid-May 2021 (revenue jumped from 20% to 40% growth almost overnight)
  • Units jumped to mid-40% to 50% range during pandemic due to product mix (gloves, cleaning agents, etc.)

1-Day & Same-Day Delivery

  • Approaching pre-pandemic service levels
  • Trust builds over time as consumers see more items in stock and available for fast delivery
  • Amazon becomes "first stop" before considering a store visit
  • Expecting similar elasticity benefits to pre-pandemic period; takes weeks/months to fully materialize

Key Analyst Q&A Data Points

  • Q2 Revenue sequential growth context (Mark Mahaney, Evercore): Guidance implies ~3% sequential growth; typical non-COVID years saw 4%-6% sequential growth in Q2; management attributes difference to Prime Day timing shift and difficult H1 2021 comps, not consumer softness
  • Q2 Operating income bridge (Mark Mahaney): ~$4B incremental costs in Q2 (down from $6B in Q1) but similar ~500 bps YoY margin decline explained by $6B SBC step-up from $3.3B in Q1
  • Inflation pass-through (Justin Post, BofA): Pricing philosophy unchanged -- aim for low competitive pricing in 1P business; 3P sellers set their own prices; first-ever FBA fuel & inflation surcharge implemented
  • Shipping costs vs. units (Justin Post, BofA): Shipping costs +14% YoY vs. 0% unit growth; AMZL network savings real vs. external carriers but inflation/productivity headwinds persist
  • 1-Day/Same-Day elasticity (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley): Approaching pre-pandemic service levels; trust-building takes weeks/months; expect similar pre-pandemic elasticity benefits
  • Technology and content drivers (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley): T&C up 19% YoY, partially offset by ~$1B server useful life change; driven by AWS infrastructure, headcount for AWS build-out, consumer tech teams, Alexa/Echo
  • Project Kuiper (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley): Referenced as an item moving through T&C line item (no quantitative detail provided)
  • Productivity timeline (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan): Adjusting headcount from 1.7M peak to 1.6M by end of Q1; working on employee-level productivity and network harmonization; good strides in last month; expect meaningful but not complete recovery to pre-pandemic rates over next couple of quarters
  • $4B incremental cost composition (Eric Sheridan, Goldman Sachs): Q4 2021 had $4B in cost penalties ($1B fixed cost deleverage carried into 2022); Q1 2022 productivity issue different from H2 2021 labor shortage (now overstaffing vs. prior understaffing); Omicron delayed productivity recovery by ~2 months; inflation amplified by Ukraine conflict/fuel costs since last call
  • H2 2022 capacity cadence (Eric Sheridan, Goldman Sachs): Fixed cost deleverage expected to narrow through the year; will be "glad to have capacity" in Q3 (Prime Day) and Q4 (holidays); YoY revenue comps improve in H2 as pandemic growth period (May 2020-May 2021) is lapped
  • Ukraine impact on demand (Ross Sandler, Barclays): No meaningful difference between Prime vs. non-Prime; March was "very strong" sequentially; no macro weakness indicators in demand; impact primarily on cost side (fuel, inflation)
  • AWS backlog (Jason Helfstein, Oppenheimer): $88.9B, +68% YoY, 3.8-year weighted average remaining life
  • Advertising demand vs. supply chain (Jason Helfstein, Oppenheimer): Advertising up 25% YoY; management pleased with performance; continuing product innovation for sellers

YoY Comp Context

  • Q1 2022 Revenue 2-Year Stack: +61% vs. Q1 2020 (pandemic growth went up and stayed up)
  • Pandemic Growth Window: Revenue jumped from 20% to 40% growth from mid-May 2020, held for one year through mid-May 2021
  • Lapping Timeline: Difficult YoY comps essentially ending by mid-May 2022
  • Q2 2021 Prime Day: Contributed ~400 bps to Q2 2021 YoY revenue growth; moved to Q3 in 2022

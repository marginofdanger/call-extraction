Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2025)

Key Guidance Assumptions & Commentary

  • Servers/Network Equipment Life Decrease (6 years to 5 years): Beginning January 2025; decreases full year 2025 operating income by ~$700M; primarily impacts AWS segment
  • Early-Retired Servers/Network Equipment Continued Charges: Decreases full year 2025 operating income by ~$600M; primarily impacts AWS segment
  • Fulfillment Center Heavy Equipment Life Increase (10 years to 13 years): Beginning January 2025; increases full year 2025 operating income by ~$900M
  • Net Aggregate Impact of Useful Life Changes on Full Year 2025 Operating Income: ~-$400M (for assets on balance sheet as of December 31, 2024; per CFO: "on an aggregate basis, we estimate this will decrease full year 2025 operating income by approximately $400 million")
  • Q4 2024 Actual FX Impact: ~$900M headwind (~$700M worse than guided)
  • Q1 2025 Guided FX Impact: ~$2.1B headwind (~150 bps unfavorable)
  • CFO: "A reminder that we are comping the impact of last year's leap year. The extra day contributed approximately $1.5 billion of additional net sales across our businesses in Q1 2024 or about 120 basis points to the year-over-year growth rate, which impacted all segments"

Capital Investment Outlook (2025)

2025 CapEx Guidance

  • Q4 2024 Run Rate as Proxy: $26.3B quarterly; CEO: "I think that is reasonably representative of what you would expect in annualized CapEx rate in 2025" (implies ~$105B full year)

AWS Strategic Commentary -- CRITICAL FORWARD TOPICS

Capacity Constraints & Growth Trajectory (CEO Jassy, Q&A)

  • Current State: "It is true that we could be growing faster, if not for some of the constraints on capacity"
  • Constraint Sources: 1. Third-Party Chips: "Chips from our third-party partners, come a little bit slower than before with a lot of midstream changes that take a little bit of time to get the hardware actually yielding the percentage-healthy and high-quality servers we expect" 2. Trainium2 Ramp: Just went to general availability at re:Invent; "the majority of the volume is coming in really over the next couple of quarters, the next few months" 3. Power Constraints: "The world is still constrained on power from where I think we all believe we could serve customers if we were unconstrained" 4. Component Shortages: Motherboards and other components "a little bit short in supply for various types of servers"
  • Relief Timeline: "I predict those constraints really start to relax in the second half of '25"
  • Growth Outlook: "I think we can be growing faster even though we're growing at a pretty good clip today"

AWS Margin Commentary (CFO Olsavsky, Q&A with Doug Anmuth)

  • Fluctuation Expected: "We have seen a lot of fluctuation in operating margin in AWS. And we've said historically that they will be lumpy"
  • AI Margin Headwind: "AI is still early stage. It does come originally with lower margins and a heavy investment load...in the short term, over time, that should be a headwind on margins"
  • Long-Term View: "Over the long term, we feel the margins will be comparable in non-AI business as well"
  • Q4 2024 Margin: ~36.8% (down from 38.1% in Q3 2024; includes ~200 bps useful life benefit and ~$920M accelerated depreciation charge)

DeepSeek / Inference Cost Commentary (CEO Jassy, Q&A with Eric Sheridan)

  • Assessment of DeepSeek: "We were impressed with what DeepSeek has done" -- particularly the sequencing of reinforcement learning before supervised fine-tuning, and inference optimizations
  • Industry Dynamics: "There is a lot of innovation to come...you have seen and will continue to see a lot of leapfrogging between us"
  • Quick Response: Made DeepSeek R1 models available in Bedrock and SageMaker "faster than you saw from others"
  • Cost Curve Historical Analogy (Jevons Paradox): CEO drew parallel to AWS launch in 2006 -- S3 at $0.15/GB, compute at $0.10/hour -- "people thought that people would spend a lot less money on infrastructure technology. And what happens is companies will spend a lot less per unit of infrastructure...but then they get excited about what else they could build that they always thought was cost prohibitive before, and they usually end up spending a lot more in total on technology"
  • AI Inference Cost Outlook: "I believe the cost of inference will meaningfully come down. I think it will make it much easier for companies to be able to infuse all their applications with inference and with generative AI. And I think if you run a business like we do...the cost of inference coming down is going to be very positive for customers and for our business"

Custom Silicon Strategy (CEO Jassy)

Trainium2

  • Launch: General availability at re:Invent in December 2024
  • Price Performance: EC2 instances with Trainium2 chips are "typically 30% to 40% more price performant than other current GPU-powered instances available"
  • Early Adopters: Adobe, Databricks, poolside, Qualcomm have seen "impressive results in early testing"
  • Anthropic Partnership: Project Rainier -- cluster of Trainium2 UltraServers containing hundreds of thousands of Trainium2 chips; "5x the number of exaflops as the cluster that Anthropic used to train their current leading set of cloud models"
  • Volume Ramp: Majority of Trainium2 volume coming "over the next couple of quarters, the next few months"

Trainium3 & Trainium4

  • Trainium3: Expected to preview "late in '25" (per CEO)
  • Trainium4: "Defining Trainium4 thereafter" (per CEO)

NVIDIA Partnership

  • Status: "We obviously have a deep partnership with NVIDIA and will for as long as we can see into the future"
  • Dual Strategy: Customers want both NVIDIA GPUs and better price performance through custom silicon

Generative AI Strategy -- Three-Layer Framework (CEO Jassy)

Layer 1: Compute / Custom Chips (Bottom Layer -- Model Builders)

  • Trainium2: 30-40% more price performant than current GPU-powered instances; just launched
  • SageMaker HyperPod: Splits training workloads across many AI accelerators; saves training time by up to 40%; auto-repairs faulty instances from last saved checkpoint
  • New SageMaker Capabilities: Ability to manage costs at a cluster level; prioritize which workloads should receive capacity when budgets are reached

Layer 2: Foundational Models / Bedrock (Middle Layer)

  • Broadest Choice: Recently added Luma AI, poolside, and over 100 other popular emerging models at re:Invent
  • DeepSeek R1: Added to Bedrock and SageMaker
  • New Features at re:Invent: Prompt caching, intelligent prompt routing, model distillation -- "all of which help customers achieve lower cost and latency in their inference"
  • Amazon Nova Models: Amazon's own family of frontier models; "compare favorably in intelligence against the leading models in the world"; ~75% lower price than other models in Bedrock; integrated with fine-tuning, model distillation, knowledge bases/RAG, agentic capabilities
  • Nova Customers: Palantir, Deloitte, SAP, Dentsu, Fortinet, Trellix, Robinhood

Layer 3: Applications / Amazon Q (Top Layer)

  • Positioning: "Most capable generative AI-powered assistant for software development and to leverage your own data"
  • Q Transform Savings at Amazon: Saved $260M and 4,500 developer years migrating 30,000+ applications to new Java JDK versions (referenced again from prior call)
  • New Q Transformations: Windows .NET to Linux, VMware to EC2, mainframe migration acceleration
  • Mainframe Migration: "Q can turn what was going to be a multiyear effort to do a mainframe migration into a multi-quarter effort, cutting by more than 50% the time to migrate mainframes"

Fulfillment & Cost to Serve

Inbound Network Rearchitecture

  • Status: "Recently rolled out our redesigned U.S. inbound network" (per CEO); "still in its early stages"
  • Improvement: Percentage of order units available in the ideal building improved by over 40% YoY ahead of Black Friday
  • CEO (Q&A): "We've made a pretty significant architectural change in our inbound network...you tend to get some low-hanging fruit efficiencies early, but then there's all sorts of tuning and refinement you have to do once you actually see it working live across the really vast network"

Same-Day Delivery

  • Network Expansion: Same-day delivery sites expanded by over 60% in 2024; now serve 140+ metro areas
  • Impact on Everyday Essentials: Big impact on everyday essentials and pharmacy business; customers using Amazon "much more frequently"

Robotics & Automation

  • Shreveport Facility: Next-generation robotics initiatives put together for the first time; "very, very encouraged by what we're seeing there, by both the speed improvements...the productivity improvements, the cost to serve improvements"
  • Expansion Plans: Plans to expand robotics to other facilities -- both new builds and retrofits of existing facilities
  • AI in Robotics: Generative AI powers the brains of robotic systems (telling robotic claws what's in a bin, how to pick/place items)
  • Scale of AI Usage: ~1,000 different generative AI applications either built or in process of building across Amazon
  • CEO: "This will be a many-year effort as we continue to tune different parts of our fulfillment network where we can use robotics"

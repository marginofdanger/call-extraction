Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2022)

Key Guidance Assumptions & Cost Bridge (Q2 to Q3)

  • Fulfillment Network Ops Improvement: ~$1.5B expected QoQ sequential cost improvement
  • Offsets to Ops Improvement: - AWS Investments: Higher infrastructure investments to support strong customer growth; larger depreciation on growing fixed asset base; increased energy costs from utility price volatility for data centers - Digital Content for Prime Members: Thursday Night Football (starting September 15); The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power (premiering September 2)
  • Net Effect: ~$1.5B ops improvement expected to be "largely offset" by AWS investment increases and digital content costs
  • Energy Costs: New cost pressure from ramp-up in natural gas prices affecting AWS data center electricity rates
  • Inflationary Pressures: Higher fuel, trucking, air and ocean shipping rates expected to continue into Q3
  • Fixed Cost Leverage: Expected to improve in H2 2022 as Amazon grows into capacity; challenging YoY comp expected to have ended in Q2; slowed future network capacity additions

Strategic & Operational Commentary

Fulfillment Network Optimization

  • Slowed 2022 and 2023 operations expansion plans to better align with expected customer demand
  • QoQ improvements in delivery speed and inventory in-stock levels
  • Moved quickly to adjust staffing levels and improve efficiency of significantly expanded operations network
  • Expect opportunity to further improve in second half of 2022
  • Fixed cost leverage expected to improve in H2 as company grows into capacity

Consumer Demand & Macro Environment

  • Strong growth in sales through Q2; no direct macro weakness hitting businesses
  • Not seeing discounting pressures that other retailers are experiencing
  • Macroeconomic issues principally on inflation side (cost, not demand)
  • Management cognizant things could change quickly; conservatism built into forward guidance
  • Prime Day (July 12-13) described as very strong for sellers

AWS Strategy & Macro Resilience

  • Believe still in early stages of enterprise and public sector adoption of cloud
  • Historical precedent from 2008 downturn: cloud computing shows its value when companies face choice of building own data center vs. using cloud
  • Prepared to help customers optimize costs in potential economic slowdown (as in 2020)
  • Continuing to sign new customers in new industries including government agencies
  • Investment in sales force and sales support showing benefits
  • New customer agreements: Delta Air Lines, Riot Games, British Telecom, Jefferies Investment Bank

International Segment Strategy

  • Emerging markets (India, Brazil, Middle East) require healthy investment for expansion
  • Established markets (U.K., Germany, Japan) showing improved profitability over time
  • Investments in: Prime Video (local language content), payment methods, third-party transportation services, Internet/telecom infrastructure
  • Strong competition across emerging markets

Seller Fees & Inflation Pass-Through

  • FBA fuel surcharge added in May to compensate for inflationary pressures
  • Neither Prime fee increase nor seller fee increase came close to covering Amazon's costs
  • Not passing through inflationary costs at 100% to external groups
  • Seller business remains strong; 57% unit mix is highest ever

Buy with Prime Program

  • Currently invitation-only for U.S.-based merchants using FBA
  • Allows Prime members to shop directly from merchant online stores with Amazon-like experience (fast free delivery, seamless checkout, free returns)
  • Will expand to more merchants throughout 2022
  • Opportunity to support merchants who may or may not be FBA sellers

Prime Video Content Pipeline (Q3 2022)

  • The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power -- premiering September 2
  • NFL Thursday Night Football -- exclusive access starting September 15

Key Analyst Q&A Data Points

  • Q2-to-Q3 EBIT bridge (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley): $1.5B ops improvement offset by AWS depreciation growth, AWS headcount additions (product engineers, sales, customer support), and energy cost increases; new information this quarter is pressure from electricity/natural gas prices in data centers; wage inflation concentrated in SBC (150 bps QoQ step-up as % of revenue)
  • CapEx pre-spend dynamics (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley): ~40% of 2022 capital spend supports warehouses/transportation capacity effective in 2023+; adjustments to time horizon have less current-year impact than expected; capital coming down in fulfillment/transportation areas
  • AWS margins sequential decline (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan): Q1 ~35% to Q2 ~29%; margin rate will fluctuate based on new investment (sales force, new regions, infrastructure) offset by infrastructure efficiency gains; longer customer commitments with credits to help cloud conversion create margin variability; lot of strength in business currently
  • AWS booking trends / macro (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan): Drawing lessons from 2008 downturn where cloud showed value vs. building own data centers; prepared to help customers optimize costs as in 2020; continuing to sign new customers in new industries/government
  • AWS backlog (Dave Fildes): Up 65% YoY, ~13% QoQ; weighted average remaining life ~3.9 years (growing); longer commitments are a focus
  • Advertising geographic split (Eric Sheridan, Goldman Sachs): Majority in North America; making great strides in international; expanding product array from consumer websites to video (Twitch, Freevee, Amazon Music ad-supported tier, Thursday Night Football, streaming TV ads)
  • Advertising macro resilience (Brian Olsavsky): Still seeing strong growth; highly efficient advertising at point of purchase; highly measurable; Amazon products compete well when companies look to optimize ad spend
  • 3P seller mix dynamics (Jason Helfstein, Oppenheimer): 57% is highest ever; Amazon "relatively indifferent" between 1P and 3P; 3P helps with selection; FBA makes 3P Prime-eligible; sellers are also large advertising customers
  • International FX impact on operating income (Jason Helfstein, Oppenheimer): $231M unfavorable FX impact included in $1.7B International segment loss
  • International profitability trajectory (Dave Fildes): Established markets (U.K., Germany, Japan) improving profitability over time; emerging markets (India, Brazil, Middle East) require continued investment; investments in local language video content, payment methods, transportation, telecom infrastructure
  • Prime fee increase impact (Youssef Squali, Truist): Results above expectations positively; membership and retention still strong; in-stock never higher; delivery speed increasing; neither Prime fee nor seller fee increases came close to covering inflationary costs
  • Buy with Prime roadmap (Youssef Squali, Truist): Currently invitation-only for FBA merchants; will expand throughout 2022; opportunity to support merchants beyond FBA with delivery and checkout tools
  • Headcount trajectory (Stephen Ju, Credit Suisse): QoQ decline by design; Q1 added 14,000 workers (vs. Q1 2021 reduced by 27,000); Omicron overstaffing resolved by end of April / early May through hiring adjustments and natural attrition; still up 188,000 YoY; nearly double pre-pandemic levels; workforce now stabilized; good hiring rates; expect adjustments for holiday demand
  • SBC below guide (Stephen Ju, Credit Suisse): Came in at ~$5B+ vs. ~$6B guided; primary driver was fewer employee stock awards vesting in Q2 than expected; Q1-to-Q2 step-up pattern is normal (annual RSU grants) but magnitude larger than usual due to wage inflation for technical employees

YoY Comp Context

  • Pandemic CAGR: 25% compound annual growth since start of pandemic (higher than pre-pandemic)
  • Pandemic Lapping: Elevated growth period (May 2020-May 2021) now fully lapped as of May 2022; growth rates going forward "will no longer require this historical explanation"
  • Q2 2021 Prime Day: Contributed ~400 bps to Q2 2021 YoY revenue growth; Q2 2022 had no Prime Day (moved to July 12-13 in Q3)
  • Q2 2021 Context: Vaccines became more available (especially in U.S.); more normal shopping patterns began emerging
  • H2 2022 Fixed Cost Comp: Challenging YoY comp vs. high holiday-level utilization in H1 2021 expected to have ended in Q2 2022

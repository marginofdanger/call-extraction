Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2024)

Key Guidance Assumptions & Commentary

  • 1. Prime Day: 10th Prime Day held in mid-July 2024; largest ever; historically creates a headwind to Q3 operating margin from deals and surrounding marketing spend; Prime members globally saved billions of dollars 2. Holiday Capacity Ramp: Q3 is when Amazon begins ramping capacity to handle Q4 holiday volumes in fulfillment network 3. NFL Thursday Night Football: Return of TNF in Q3 drives quarter-over-quarter increase in digital content costs
  • CFO: "A lot of the same consumer trends that we have been talking about for the last year"; consumers being careful with spend, trading down, looking for lower ASP products, looking for deals
  • CFO: "That continued into Q2, and we expect it to continue into Q3. We're seeing signs of it continuing in Q3"
  • CEO: Lower average selling prices (ASPs) driven by consumer trade-down behavior; more discretionary higher-ticket items (computers, electronics, TVs) growing faster than industry but slower than in a robust economy
  • Unit Strength vs. ASP Pressure: Revenue growth deceleration from Q1 to Q2 was tied to ASP compression, not unit weakness; units actually slightly accelerated QoQ when adjusted for leap year
  • Q2 2024 Actual FX Impact: ~$1.0B headwind (~$300M worse than the ~$700M anticipated in Q2 guidance)
  • Q3 2024 Guided FX Impact: ~90 bps unfavorable headwind (wider than Q2 actual)
  • CEO: "Our seller fees are a little lower than expected given the behavior changes we've seen from our latest fee changes"
  • Context: Lower apparel fees spurred substantial unit growth in apparel; incentive for sellers to inbound to multiple Amazon facilities getting more traction than hoped; some short-term revenue compression but positive long-term unit trends

Capital Investment Outlook (H2 2024)

Long-Term Stores Margin Expansion (CEO Jassy -- Q&A with Brian Nowak)

  • CEO: "I continue and the team continues to believe that we have the opportunity to expand the margin in our Stores business"
  • CEO: "It's not going to happen in 1 quarter. It's not going to happen in one fell swoop. It's going to take work over a long period of time"
  • Opportunity Areas: 1. U.S. regionalization still not fully optimized; continuing to bear lower cost to serve 2. Inbound network regionalization (next phase) 3. More units per box 4. Automation and robotics 5. Same-day facility expansion
  • Not Binary: CEO: investments in Kuiper, etc. use different teams than Stores operations; can pursue margin expansion and new business investment simultaneously

AWS Reacceleration -- CRITICAL FORWARD COMMENTARY

Q2 2024 Performance & Trajectory

  • Q2 2024 Growth: 18.8% YoY reported (19% on as-reported currency basis); accelerated from 17.2% in Q1
  • Annualized Run Rate: $105B+ (milestone)
  • Operating Income: $9.3B (+$4.0B YoY)
  • Operating Margin: 35.5% (includes ~200 bps benefit from server useful life change)
  • AWS Growth Trajectory: 37% (Q1'22) -> 33% (Q2'22) -> 28% (Q3'22) -> 20% (Q4'22) -> 16% (Q1'23) -> 12% (Q2'23) -> 12% (Q3'23) -> 13% (Q4'23) -> 17.2% (Q1'24) -> 18.8% (Q2'24)

Three Macro Trends Driving AWS Growth (CEO Jassy)

  • 1. Cost Optimization Largely Complete: "Companies have completed the significant majority of their cost optimization efforts and are focused again on new efforts"; CEO: "we have seen the lion's share of the cost optimization happen" 2. Infrastructure Modernization / Cloud Migration Resuming: Companies returning to pre-pandemic march of on-premises to cloud migration; CEO: "about 90% of the global IT spend is still on-premises"; described as "low-hanging fruit" 3. Generative AI Momentum: "Our AI business continues to grow dramatically with a multibillion-dollar revenue run rate despite it being such early days"; significant demand; "all going to be built from the get-go in the cloud"

Forward Growth Commentary (CEO Jassy -- Q&A with Mark Mahaney)

  • On Sustainability of Acceleration: "All 3 of those things have a chance and will likely continue over time"; "relatively large business at $105B revenue run rate at this point" but 90% of global IT still on-premises
  • Generative AI as Incremental TAM: "It's going to be really large...it is not something that we originally factored when we were thinking about how large AWS could be"; unlike non-AI cloud (migration from on-prem), gen AI "is going to get big fast, and it's largely all going to be built from the get-go in the cloud"

AWS Backlog

  • Q2 2024: $156.6B (+19% YoY)

AWS Margin Forward Commentary

  • CFO: "As we've long said, we expect AWS operating margins to fluctuate over time, driven in part by the level of investments we're making at any point in time"
  • CFO: AWS margins in mid-30s% range in Q2, up from mid-20s% range last year; driven by cost reductions, efficiencies, and ~200 bps from server useful life change
  • Assessment: Increasing CapEx in H2 2024 for AWS infrastructure will drive higher depreciation, which will likely pressure margins going forward

AI Capital Allocation Philosophy (CEO Jassy -- Q&A with Eric Sheridan)

  • Capacity Management Track Record: "We've learned over time to manage this reasonably well"; built algorithmic and sophisticated models over 18 years to land the right amount of capacity
  • Current Demand Exceeds Supply: "While we're investing a significant amount in the AI space and in infrastructure, we would like to have more capacity than we already have today. We have a lot of demand right now, and I think it's going to be a very, very large business for us"
  • Signal Quality: "It's not like a company shows up to do a training cluster asking for a few hundred thousand chips the same day. You have a very significant advanced signal when you have customers that want to take down a lot of capacity"
  • Risk of Overinvestment: CEO acknowledged AI capacity models are "more fluid" than traditional cloud models but emphasized strong customer demand signals mitigate risk

Custom Silicon Strategy (CEO Jassy -- Q&A with Eric Sheridan)

Trainium & Inferentia

  • Trainium2: Coming later this year; "very compelling on price performance"; significant demand
  • Inferentia: Second-generation inference chip
  • Graviton Analogy: Custom CPU (Graviton) saves customers up to 30%-40% price performance vs. leading x86 processors; been "very successful for customers and for our AWS business"
  • GPU Supply Constraints: "In a world where it's hard to get GPUs today, the supply is scarce and all the schedules continue to move over time, customers are quite excited and demanding at a high clip our custom silicon"
  • Return Profile: CEO expects custom AI chips will have "very good return profile just like Graviton has" and be "another differentiating feature around AWS relative to others"

Generative AI Strategy -- Three-Layer Framework (CEO Jassy)

Layer 1: Compute / Custom Chips

  • NVIDIA Partnership: Deep partnership; broadest selection of NVIDIA instances available
  • Trainium (Training): Custom chip; Trainium2 coming later this year with compelling price performance
  • Inferentia (Inference): Custom chip; second version available
  • SageMaker HyperPod: Recently launched feature that "changes the game and networking performance for large models"; model builders increasingly standardizing on SageMaker

Layer 2: Foundational Models / Bedrock

  • Adoption: "Tens of thousands of companies using it already"
  • Model Selection: Largest selection; recently added Anthropic Claude 3.5, Meta Llama 3.1, Mistral Large 2
  • Key Capabilities: Model evaluation, guardrails, RAG, agenting, easy model switching between types and sizes
  • Differentiation: CEO: "best generative AI capabilities in critical areas"

Layer 3: Applications (Amazon Q)

  • Positioning: "Most capable generative AI-powered assistant for software development and to leverage your own data"
  • Code Suggestions: "Highest known score and acceptance rate for code suggestions"
  • Security: Outperforms all other publicly benchmarkable competitors on catching security vulnerabilities
  • Code Transformation: Amazon migrated over 30,000 Java JDK applications in a few months using Q; saved the company $260 million and 4,500 developer years vs. traditional approach
  • CEO: "That's a game changer"

Machine Learning Feature Velocity

  • CEO: "During the past 18 months, AWS has launched more than twice as many machine learning and generative AI features into general availability than all the other major cloud providers combined"

Fulfillment & Cost to Serve

Cost to Serve Improvements

  • Inventory Regionalization: More regional placement, closer to customers; resulted in more consolidated shipments with higher units per box shipped; packages traveling shorter distances; better on-road productivity
  • Inbound Network Regionalization: Next major initiative; will lower cost to serve and get items closer to end users
  • Units Per Box: Continued focus on combining more units per box (lowers costs, better for environment)
  • Automation & Robotics: Expanding use in fulfillment centers
  • Same-Day Facility Network: Continuing to build out network
  • CEO on Timeline: "These cost improvements won't happen in 1 quarter or 1 fell swoop. They take technology and process innovation with a lot of outstanding execution, but we see a path to continuing to lower our cost to serve"
  • CEO: "We see a path to continuing to lower our cost to serve, which has very meaningful value for customers in our business"

Seller Inbound Incentive

  • CEO: Incentive for sellers to send items to multiple Amazon inbound facilities is "getting more traction than we'd even hoped"; allows sellers to save money where they save Amazon effort and money

Lower ASP Strategy

  • CEO: As cost to serve decreases, Amazon can afford more low-ASP selection that can be supported economically; coupled with fast delivery, puts Amazon in consideration set for more shopping needs

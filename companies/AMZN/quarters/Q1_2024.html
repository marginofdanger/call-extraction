AWS Reacceleration -- CRITICAL FORWARD COMMENTARY

Q1 2024 Performance & Trajectory

  • Q1 2024 Growth: 17.2% YoY reported (~16% ex-leap year); significant reacceleration from 13% in Q4 2023
  • Annualized Run Rate: $100B+ (milestone reached)
  • Operating Income: $9.4B (+$4.3B YoY)
  • Operating Margin: 37.6% (+800 bps QoQ)
  • AWS Growth Trajectory: 40% (Q4'21) -> 37% (Q1'22) -> 33% (Q2'22) -> 28% (Q3'22) -> 20% (Q4'22) -> 16% (Q1'23) -> 12% (Q2'23) -> 12% (Q3'23) -> 13% (Q4'23) -> 17.2% (Q1'24)

Three Macro Trends Driving AWS Performance (CEO Jassy)

  • 1. Cost Optimization Cycle Ending: "The lion's share of cost optimization is behind us"; customers learned from the experience but the major wave has passed; CFO: "we think the majority of the recent cycle is behind us, and we're likely closer to a steady state" 2. Infrastructure Modernization Resuming: Companies returning to pre-pandemic march of moving from on-premises to cloud; described as "low-hanging fruit" for customers; "very significant growth" in this space 3. Generative AI Momentum: "Multibillion-dollar revenue run rate" already in AI; driving both training and inference workloads; still "relatively early days"

Market Opportunity

  • CEO: "We're at $100 billion-plus annualized revenue run rate, yet 85% or more of the global IT spend remains on-premises"
  • CEO: "This is before you even calculate gen AI, most of which will be created over the next 10 to 20 years from scratch and on the cloud"

AWS Forward CapEx Signals

  • CFO: "$14 billion of CapEx" in Q1 2024; "we expect that to be the low quarter for the year"
  • CFO: "We are seeing strong demand signals from our customers and longer deals and larger commitments, many with generative AI components"
  • CEO: "The more demand AWS has, the more we have to procure new data centers, power and hardware"
  • CFO: "It will increase depreciation, definitely in that segment"
  • CEO/CFO consensus: Confident in capital returns from CapEx spend based on 18 years of investing capital upfront and seeing revenue/OI/FCF follow

AWS Margin Trajectory

  • Q1 2024 Operating Margin: 37.6%
  • Margin Trajectory: 35.3% (Q1'22) -> 29.0% (Q2'22) -> 26.3% (Q3'22) -> 24.3% (Q4'22) -> 24.0% (Q1'23) -> 24.2% (Q2'23) -> 30.3% (Q3'23) -> 29.6% (Q4'23) -> 37.6% (Q1'24)
  • Note: Includes ~$900M/quarter benefit from server useful life extension (5 to 6 years) starting Q1 2024
  • Forward View: CFO warned margins will "fluctuate, driven in part by the level of investments"; higher CapEx/depreciation expected to pressure margins over time

Generative AI / Large Language Models -- KEY STRATEGIC INITIATIVE

AI Revenue Scale

  • Current Revenue Run Rate: "Multibillion-dollar revenue run rate already" in AI (per CEO)
  • Status: "Still relatively early days"
  • Comparison: "Given our much larger infrastructure cloud computing base, at this growth rate, we see more absolute dollar growth again quarter-over-quarter in AWS than we can see elsewhere" -- CEO

Three-Layer Framework (CEO Jassy)

  • NVIDIA: "Broadest selection of NVIDIA compute instances around"
  • Trainium: AWS custom chip for ML training; high demand given "favorable price performance benefits relative to available alternatives"
  • Inferentia: AWS custom chip for inference
  • Trainium2: "Larger quantities of our latest generation Trainium2 is coming in the second half of 2024 and early 2025"
  • Anthropic Training on Trainium: "They're training their future models on our custom silicon on Trainium" -- CEO
  • SageMaker Performance: Perplexity.ai trains models 40% faster on SageMaker; Workday reduces inference latency by 80%; NatWest reduces AI time to value from 12-18 months to under 7 months
  • Adoption: "Tens of thousands of customers" already on Bedrock (including adidas, New York Stock Exchange, Pfizer, Ryanair, Toyota)
  • Model Selection: Added Anthropic Claude 3, Meta Llama 3, Mistral, Cohere, and new Amazon Titan models in recent months
  • Key Features: Model evaluation, RAG (retrieval augmented generation), guardrails, agents (multistep tasks), fine-tuning
  • Custom Model Import (NEW): Allows importing models built in SageMaker or elsewhere into Bedrock; described as "a sneaky big launch" -- CEO; "satisfies a customer request we've heard frequently and that nobody has yet met"
  • SageMaker + Bedrock Integration: Customers building models in SageMaker can now deploy through Bedrock's production features; described as "quite appealing"
  • Amazon Q General Availability: Announced day of the earnings call; described as "the most capable generative AI-powered assistant for software development and leveraging company's internal data"
  • Software Development Capabilities: Generates, tests, and debugs code; transforms code (e.g., old Java to new, .NET from Windows to Linux); Agents capability for autonomous feature implementation
  • Enterprise Data Capabilities: Q Apps lets employees build apps from internal data using natural language; searches across wikis, Salesforce, S3, and other data stores
  • Performance Claims: "Highest-known score and acceptance rate for code suggestions"; outperforms competitors on security vulnerability detection; leads on connecting multiple steps and automatic actions
  • Named Q Customers: Brightcove, British Telecom, Datadog, GitLab, GoDaddy, National Australia Bank, NCS, Netsmart, Slam, Smartsheet, Sun Life, Tata Consultancy Services, Toyota, Wiz

Training vs. Inference Dynamic (CEO Jassy)

  • Training: Significant current spend; models being trained on AWS including Anthropic's future models on Trainium; largest foundational model builders building new models every 12-18 months
  • Inference (future driver): "Once you get those models into production...you spend much more in inference than you do in training because you train only periodically, but you're spinning out predictions and inferences all the time"
  • Both on AWS: "We see both training and inference being really big drivers on top of AWS"

Security Differentiation

  • CEO: "If you've been paying attention to what's been happening in the last year or so, you can see there are big differences between providers on these dimensions. AWS has a meaningful edge"
  • Impact: Companies moving their AI focus to AWS due to security and operational performance

Advertising

Q1 Performance

  • Advertising Services Revenue Growth: +24% YoY ex-FX
  • Primary Driver: Sponsored products, supported by continued improvements in relevancy and measurement capabilities

Prime Video Ads

  • Status: "Very early days, just launched a few months ago"; "off to a really good start"
  • Value Proposition: Ability to link impact of streaming TV advertising to business outcomes like product sales or subscription sign-ups, whether brands sell on Amazon or not
  • Advertiser Interest: Brands excited to expand advertising with Amazon in video beyond Twitch and Freevee to Prime Video shows and movies
  • CEO: "It's very early for streaming TV ads but we're encouraged by the early response"

Growth Drivers

  • Significant opportunity ahead in sponsored products
  • Prime Video ads still getting started
  • Advertising remains "important contributor to profitability in North America and international segments" -- CFO

Fulfillment & Cost Structure

Regionalization & Inbound Network

  • Regionalization: Continued benefits; "we're not done there" -- CEO; still refining and extracting value from regionalization work
  • U.S. Inbound Overhaul: Revamping inbound fulfillment architecture for better inventory placement closer to customers; "iterative process throughout the year"
  • International Application: Lessons from U.S. regionalization being applied to international operations
  • Seller Incentives: Lowered outbound fees; added incentive for sellers to inbound to more cost-efficient locations; "seeing very optimistic signs"

Units Per Box & Consolidation

  • CEO: Seeing increase in units delivered per box; "important driver for reducing our cost"
  • Benefits: Fewer boxes/deliveries, better customer experience, lower cost to serve, lower carbon impact
  • CEO: "Still early with respect to how we can continue to optimize the number of units per box"

Same-Day Facilities

  • CEO: "Our same-day facilities are our least expensive facilities in the network"
  • Expansion: "We still have a fraction of the number of those that we will have in the U.S." and internationally
  • Economics: Faster speeds do not necessarily mean higher cost if infrastructure built with the right building blocks
  • Impact: Will continue to change cost structure while increasing speed

Robotics & Automation

  • CFO: Investment focus areas include "adding robotics and automation" within fulfillment network
  • CFO: "These improvement opportunities will take time. However, we have a solid plan in place and we like the path we're on"

Pre-Pandemic Margin Comparison

  • CFO: "We look back to before the pandemic, and we say, first, we can achieve those operating margins even without the impact of advertising. And we're not quite there yet. But we're not limiting ourselves to that"
  • CEO: "Don't believe that we're at the end of what we can do in terms of improving our cost structure on the store side"

Third-Party Logistics (Supply Chain by Amazon)

Service Components (CEO Jassy)

  • 1. Cross-Border / Customs: Service to get items through the border and customs 2. Domestic Transportation: Ship from customs to various facilities 3. Upstream Warehousing: Low-cost storage with automatic replenishment into fulfillment centers 4. Outbound Shipping: Ship to Amazon end customers or seller's own customers 5. Buy with Prime: Enables Prime customers to buy from third-party seller websites; "increases their conversion 25% versus what they would do on their own" 6. Supply Chain by Amazon: End-to-end abstraction layer on top of individual services

Growth & CapEx

  • CEO: "That collective set of businesses is growing very significantly. It's already what I would consider a reasonable sized business"
  • CapEx Impact: "Not something that we anticipate being a giant capital expense driver for us" -- modest increase on top of existing stores infrastructure

Grocery Strategy Update

Three Segments

  • 1. Non-Perishables (Consumables): "Very, very significant" business; continues to grow at a "very rapid rate"; includes canned goods, pet food, health care, beauty products 2. Whole Foods Market: "Pioneer in organic grocery"; continues to grow "very nicely"; profitability trajectory improved over last 18 months; launching new smaller "Whole Foods Market Daily Shops" format in Manhattan in the fall 3. Amazon Fresh (Physical Stores): Launched V2 format primarily in Chicago and Southern California; "early results a lot"; "really meaningfully better in almost every dimension"; need to decide best rollout approach

New Prime Grocery Benefit

  • $9.99/month: All-you-can-eat grocery delivery for Prime members
  • Value: Pays for itself with one Whole Foods order per month or one Amazon Fresh order under $40
  • Reception: "Off to a great start"

International Segment Profitability

Structure (CFO Olsavsky)

  • Established Countries (Europe, Japan, UK): "Profitable in their own right"; following same trajectory as United States; adding selection, features, Prime benefits
  • Emerging Countries (10 in last 7 years): Each on unique trajectory; pattern: good CX -> Prime sign-ups (Prime Video helps) -> cost structure optimization at scale -> add advertising -> breakeven -> positive income/FCF
  • CEO: "I'm again quite bullish on our international stores business...it's going to be a big, profitable business for us"

Cross-Pollination

  • Average Package Travel Distance (International): Down 25 kilometers (per analyst question context)

Project Kuiper

  • Timeline: Production satellite launch and commercial beta approaching

Health Care

Amazon Pharmacy

  • CEO Jassy: "We're in a position to do both"; stores business has cost structure improvement ahead; AWS has massive growth opportunity (85%+ IT spend on-premises); generative AI is "a possibility like this in technology in a really long time, for sure, since the cloud, perhaps since the Internet"
  • CFO Olsavsky: "We're at the stage now where we're doing both at the same time continually"; historically oscillated between investment and margin expansion, now doing both

CapEx Confidence Signals

  • CFO: "We don't spend the capital without very clear signals that we can monetize it"
  • CFO: Capital deployed upfront; revenue, operating income, and FCF follow with "strong returns on invested capital"
  • CEO: Gen AI workloads "will transform every experience...going to be built from scratch on the cloud largely"

The only “miss” vs midpoint (Q3 ’25) was entirely driven by $4.3B in nonrecurring charges.

3 Key Takeaways

1
Amazon never missed the low end of guidance. Across 15 quarters and 2 metrics (30 data points), Amazon never delivered below the low end of its guided range. The guidance floor is a perfectly reliable minimum.
2
OI guidance is systematically sandbagged by ~$2.8B per quarter. The average beat vs midpoint of $2.8B represents a 20–25% upside to the guided midpoint. This is among the most conservative guidance patterns in large-cap tech. From Q2 2023 through Q2 2025, the average beat was $3.6B.
3
Revenue guidance is more precisely calibrated. The average beat of +2.2% is meaningful but moderate. Revenue guidance accuracy tightened over time: the average beat was 2.7% in 2022–2023 vs. 1.7% in 2024–2025, suggesting management is calibrating more precisely as the business stabilizes.
4
The Q1 2024 OI beat was the largest in the dataset. Amazon guided $8.0B–$12.0B and delivered $15.3B — $5.3B above midpoint and $3.3B above the high end. This reflected the compounding impact of cost restructuring, fulfillment regionalization, and the server life extension (~$900M/Q benefit).
5
The only revenue miss was FX-driven. Q3 2022 was the sole quarter where revenue missed the midpoint (−0.3%). FX headwinds were ~460 bps vs. the ~390 bps assumed in guidance — the incremental ~$900M FX headwind accounts for the entire shortfall.
6
OI guidance became dramatically more conservative from 2023 onward. Average OI beat vs midpoint: $1.3B (Q2–Q4 2022) → $3.8B (Q1–Q4 2023) → $3.9B (Q1–Q4 2024) → $2.3B (Q1–Q4 2025, depressed by one-time charges). Management learned during the 2022 uncertainty to guide conservatively and never stopped.
7
Special charges distort the 2025 OI picture. On a reported basis, Q3 and Q4 2025 appear weaker vs guidance. On an adjusted basis ($21.7B and $27.4B respectively), both would have beaten the high end. Investors should track adjusted OI vs guidance for a cleaner signal.
8
Q2 2025 was the largest revenue beat. Revenue of $167.7B exceeded the high end ($164.0B) by $3.7B (+2.3%), the largest absolute and percentage beat vs high end in the dataset. This occurred despite elevated tariff uncertainty, suggesting underlying demand remains robust.

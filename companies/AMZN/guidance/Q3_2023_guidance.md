## AWS Growth Stabilization -- CRITICAL FORWARD COMMENTARY

### Q3 Reported Performance & Trajectory
- **Q3 2023 growth:** 12% YoY (stabilized; same rate as Q2 2023)
- **Annualized run rate:** $92B
- **Sequential dollar add:** $919M (largest absolute QoQ increase among cloud peers per CEO)
- **Context:** Growth rate stabilized after deceleration from 40% (Q4'21) to 12% (Q2'23); Q3 confirmed stabilization at 12%

### Cost Optimization vs. New Workloads
- **Optimization status:** "Still saw elevated cost optimization relative to a year ago" but "continued to attenuate" -- CEO Jassy
- **Nature of optimization:** CEO noted "a very significant portion of the optimization are customers taking advantage of enhanced price performance capabilities in AWS" (e.g., Graviton adoption, savings plans) rather than shutting down workloads
- **Graviton adoption:** 40% better price performance than leading x86 processors; percentage of EC2 instances launched on Graviton "very substantially higher than it was before"
- **Savings Plans:** Growing number of customers moving from hourly on-demand to 1-3 year commitments
- **New deal momentum:** "Pace and volume of closed deals pick up" and "strong last couple of months of new deals signed" -- CEO Jassy
- **September deals disclosure:** "We signed several new deals in September with an effective date in October that won't show up in any GAAP reported number for Q3, but the collection of which is higher than our total reported deal volume for all of Q3" -- CEO Jassy (IMPORTANT forward signal)
- **Forward view:** CEO expects optimization to "continue over time" to attenuate; "very optimistic about AWS in the medium to long term"

### CEO Forward Commentary
- "I remain very optimistic about AWS in the medium to long term"
- "$92 billion revenue run rate business where 90% of the global IT spend still resides on premises. And if you believe like we do, that equation is going to flip. There's a lot more there for us"

## Generative AI / Large Language Models

### Three-Layer Strategic Framework (CEO Jassy)

#### Layer 1: Compute / Custom Chips
- **Trainium:** Custom AI training chip; Anthropic chose to train future LLMs on Trainium
- **Inferentia:** Custom AI inference chip; Anthropic will use for deployment
- **Anthropic Partnership:** "Leading LLM maker Anthropic chose AWS as its primary cloud provider and will use Trainium and Inferentia to build, train and deploy its future LLMs"
- **Perplexity.ai:** Chose to go "all in with AWS," including running future models on Trainium and Inferentia
- **Supply dynamics:** "There's a real shortage right now in the industry in chips. It's really hard to get the amount of GPUs that everybody wants" -- CEO
- **Chip advantage:** Better price performance + availability vs. GPU shortage; AWS has ordered "meaningfully in advance"

#### Layer 2: Large Language Models as a Service (Bedrock)
- **Bedrock:** Achieved general availability in Q3; managed service providing access to multiple LLM providers
- **Model Partners:** Anthropic, Stability AI, Coherent, AI21, Meta's Llama 2 (first time available through a fully managed service), plus Amazon's own Titan models
- **Key Features:** Model customization with proprietary data, enterprise-grade security, agents capability, ability to move between model types and sizes
- **Anthropic Integration:** Customers get exclusive early access to future Anthropic models through Bedrock with unique features, model customization, and fine-tuning
- **Named Customers:** adidas, Booking.com, Bridgewater, Clariant, GoDaddy, LexisNexis, Merck, Royal Philips, United Airlines, Perplexity.ai
- **CEO Assessment:** "Bedrock is the easiest way to build and scale enterprise-ready generative AI applications and a real game changer for developers"

#### Layer 3: Applications
- **CodeWhisperer:** AI coding companion; new customization capability allowing it to understand proprietary code bases; "another first of its kind out there in its current form"
- **Stores Business:** Generative AI for product discovery, inventory forecasting, last-mile route optimization, 3P seller product page creation
- **Advertising:** Generative AI image generation tool for brands to create lifestyle images from product photos
- **Alexa:** New LLM-powered conversational AI capabilities; multiple requests at once; more natural conversational requests

### Gen AI Revenue Timing & Scale
- "Our generative AI business is growing very, very quickly" -- CEO Jassy
- "Almost by any measure, it's a pretty significant business for us already" -- CEO Jassy
- "Companies are still in the relatively early stages" and "we're very early in generative AI"
- "I believe [generative AI] will be tens of billions of dollars of revenue for AWS over the next several years"
- Companies still experimenting with model types and sizes to find right cost/latency characteristics
- Large models often "more expensive than what they anticipated" leading to experimentation with smaller models

### Gen AI Impact on CapEx
- FY 2023 capital investment of ~$50B includes "increased infrastructure CapEx to support growth of our AWS business, including additional investments related to generative AI and large language model efforts"
- AWS re:Invent conference (November 27) expected to showcase many new gen AI capabilities

# Amazon.com, Inc. Q3 2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 27, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2022

---

## Q3 2022 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated
- **Total Net Sales:** $127.1B (+15% YoY reported; +19% YoY ex-FX)
- **Net Sales vs. Guidance:** Within $125.0B-$130.0B guidance range (near midpoint)
- **FX Impact on Revenue:** ~$5.0B unfavorable (~460 bps); **worse than guided ~390 bps** (incremental ~$900M headwind vs. guidance assumption)
- **Operating Income:** $2.53B (-48% YoY reported; -55% YoY ex-FX)
- **Operating Income vs. Guidance:** Within $0-$3.5B guidance range
- **Operating Margin:** 2.0% (vs. 4.4% in Q3 2021)
- **FX Impact on Operating Income:** $357M favorable (investments denominated in foreign currencies create slight favorability)
- **Net Income:** $2.87B (vs. $3.16B in Q3 2021; -9% YoY)
- **Rivian Pretax Valuation Gain:** $1.1B (included in nonoperating income from common stock investment in Rivian Automotive)
- **EPS (Basic):** $0.28 (vs. $0.31 in Q3 2021)
- **EPS (Diluted):** $0.28 (vs. $0.31 in Q3 2021; vs. consensus $0.20; **40% beat**)
- **Net Product Sales:** $59.3B (+8% YoY)
- **Net Service Sales:** $67.8B (+21% YoY)
- **Stock-Based Compensation:** $5.56B (+75% YoY; vs. $3.18B in Q3 2021; up from $5.21B in Q2 2022)
- **SBC Increase Driver:** Primarily driven by reduction in estimated forfeiture rate on certain unvested stock awards
- **Income Tax Expense:** $69M (vs. $1.16B in Q3 2021)
- **Depreciation & Amortization:** $10.2B (vs. $8.9B in Q3 2021; +14% YoY)

### Revenue by Category
- **Online Stores:** $53.5B (+7% YoY reported; +13% ex-FX)
- **Physical Stores:** $4.7B (+10% YoY reported; +10% ex-FX)
- **Third-Party Seller Services:** $28.7B (+18% YoY reported; +23% ex-FX)
- **Subscription Services:** $8.9B (+9% YoY reported; +14% ex-FX)
- **Advertising Services:** $9.5B (+25% YoY reported; **+30% ex-FX**; CFO noted "sales grew 30% year-over-year, excluding the impact of foreign exchange")
- **AWS:** $20.5B (+27% YoY reported; **+28% ex-FX**)
- **Other:** $1.3B (+163% YoY reported; +168% ex-FX)

### Segment Results

#### North America
- **Net Sales:** $78.8B (+20% YoY reported; +20% ex-FX)
- **Operating Income (Loss):** -$412M (vs. +$880M in Q3 2021; -147% YoY)
- **Operating Margin:** -0.5% (vs. +1.3% in Q3 2021)
- **Net Sales Mix:** 62% of consolidated

#### International
- **Net Sales:** $27.7B (-5% YoY reported; +12% ex-FX)
- **Operating Income (Loss):** -$2.47B (vs. -$911M in Q3 2021; loss widened 171% YoY)
- **FX Impact on International Operating Income:** -$216M unfavorable
- **Operating Margin:** -8.9% (vs. -3.1% in Q3 2021)
- **Net Sales Mix:** 22% of consolidated

#### AWS
- **Net Sales:** $20.5B (+27% YoY reported; +28% ex-FX)
- **Annualized Sales Run Rate:** $82B
- **Operating Income:** $5.4B (+11% YoY reported; +1% ex-FX)
- **FX Impact on AWS Operating Income:** $478M favorable
- **Operating Margin:** 26.3% (vs. 30.3% in Q3 2021; vs. 29.0% in Q2 2022; sequential decline)
- **Absolute Dollar Growth:** Added $4.5B to the $16.1B revenue base in Q3 2021
- **AWS Backlog:** $104.3B at end of Q3 (up ~57% YoY; up from prior quarter)
- **AWS Growth Trajectory:** Q3 reported 28% ex-FX; **back end of Q3 exited at mid-20% growth rate** (deceleration within the quarter)

### AWS Margin Drivers (Q3 Detail)
- **Wage Inflation:** Higher wages in 2022, concentrated in tech employees (heavily in AWS)
- **Energy Costs:** Materially higher than pre-pandemic; electricity and natural gas prices up more than 2x over last couple years; **contributes ~200 bps margin degradation vs. 2 years ago**
- **Stock-Based Compensation:** Step-up in SBC from annual grant cycle; wage inflation for technical employees concentrated in AWS

### Third-Party Sellers
- **3P Seller Unit Mix:** 58% of total paid units in Q3 (highest percentage ever; up from 56% in Q3 2021)

### Operational Efficiency / Cost Improvements
- **Q3 Operations Cost Improvement:** Over $1B in operations cost improvements driven by higher leverage of fixed cost base and continued productivity improvements in fulfillment and transportation networks
- **Target vs. Actual:** Aimed for ~$1.5B sequential improvement vs. Q2; **came up ~$0.5B short** (achieved ~$1.0B)
- **Shortfall Driver:** Productivity gains lower than planned due to running very high inventory levels in warehouses; Prime Day and Prime Early Access Sale preparations created space constraints and blockages to productivity improvement
- **Management Outlook:** Encouraged by progress but "still a lot of opportunity to continue to improve productivity and drive cost efficiencies"

### Prime Day & Events
- **Prime Day (July 2022):** 8th annual event; contributed approximately **400 bps to Q3 YoY sales growth rate**
- **Prime Day Volume:** More than 300 million items purchased worldwide; biggest Prime Day net sales event in Amazon history
- **Prime Day Timing Note:** Prime Day occurred in Q2 2021; shifted to Q3 in 2022
- **Prime Early Access Sale (October 2022):** First-ever event; tens of millions of Prime members shopped; ordered more than 100 million items from selling partners

### Prime Video Highlights
- **The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power:** Attracted more than 25 million global viewers on first day; drove more Prime sign-ups globally than any other Amazon Original
- **NFL Thursday Night Football:** Premiered in September; averaged more than 15 million viewers during first broadcast; drove the 3 biggest hours of U.S. Prime sign-ups in the history of Amazon
- **Cost Impact:** Step-up in Prime Video content and marketing costs in Q3 from Rings of Power global premiere and NFL Thursday Night Football launch

### Nonrecurring Charges
- **Business Closures:** Nonrecurring charges related to closure of Amazon Care, Fabric.com, and Amazon Explore

### Employees & Headcount
- **Headcount (End of Q3 2022):** 1,544,000 (full-time and part-time; excludes contractors & temporary personnel)
- **YoY Change:** +5% (vs. 1,468,000 in Q3 2021)
- **Hiring Pause:** Pausing hiring in certain businesses as part of belt-tightening measures
- **Winding Down Products/Services:** Shutting down products and services where resources better spent elsewhere

### Capital Investments (Full Year 2022 Outlook)
- **2022 Full Year Expectation:** ~$60B in capital investments (CapEx + equipment finance leases); broadly in line with 2021
- **Capital Mix Shift for 2022:**
  - **Fulfillment & Transportation:** ~$10B reduction vs. 2021 (moderating build expectations to align with demand)
  - **Technology Infrastructure (primarily AWS):** ~$10B increase vs. 2021 (supporting rapid growth, innovation, continued expansion of AWS footprint)
- **CapEx Cut from Original 2022 Plan:** Cut about 1/3 of original 2022 budget while focusing capital on AWS and customer demand
- **Purchases of Property & Equipment (Q3 2022):** $16.4B (vs. $15.7B in Q3 2021)
- **TTM Purchases of P&E (net):** $59.4B (+14% YoY)

### Cash Flow & Balance Sheet
- **Operating Cash Flow (Q3 2022):** $11.4B (vs. $7.3B in Q3 2021; +56%)
- **Operating Cash Flow (TTM):** $39.7B (-27% YoY vs. $54.7B)
- **Free Cash Flow (TTM):** -$19.7B (vs. +$2.6B TTM through Q3 2021)
- **FCF Less Principal Repayments of Finance Leases & Financing Obligations (TTM):** -$28.5B
- **Cash & Cash Equivalents:** $34.9B (vs. $36.2B at Dec 31, 2021)
- **Total Debt (Long-Term):** $58.9B (vs. $48.7B at Dec 31, 2021)
- **Inventory:** $36.6B (vs. $32.6B at Dec 31, 2021; elevated due to supply chain issues from Asia; more weeks of cover than normal)
- **Inventory Issue:** Working capital impact from accounts payable days being less than inventory days has been negative; expected to normalize into 2023
- **Shares Outstanding:** 10,198M (vs. 10,175M at Dec 31, 2021)
- **Stock-Based Awards Outstanding:** 399M shares (3.9% of common shares; up from 311M / 3.1% in Q3 2021)

### Worldwide Shipping & Units
- **WW Shipping Costs (Q3 2022):** $19.9B (+10% YoY; vs. $18.1B in Q3 2021)
- **WW Paid Units Growth:** +11% YoY (vs. +8% in Q3 2021)
- **WW Seller Unit Mix:** 58% (vs. 56% in Q3 2021)

## Quarterly Guidance (Q4 2022)

### Net Sales
- **Q4 2022 Net Sales Guidance:** $140.0B to $148.0B
- **Implied YoY Growth:** +2% to +8% (vs. Q4 2021 net sales of $137.4B)
- **FX Impact (unfavorable):** ~460 bps estimated unfavorable impact from foreign exchange rates
- **Implied YoY Growth ex-FX:** ~6.6% to ~12.6%
- **Consensus (pre-call):** $146.1B
- **Assessment:** Guidance range midpoint ($144B) below consensus; **below-seasonal ramp** reflecting macro caution

### Operating Income
- **Q4 2022 Operating Income Guidance:** $0 to $4.0B
- **Prior Year Comp:** $3.5B in Q4 2021
- **Implied Operating Margin (at midpoint):** ~1.4% (at $2.0B on ~$144B sales)
- **Assessment:** Wide range reflecting macro uncertainty; midpoint implies YoY decline

### Key Guidance Assumptions & Commentary

#### FX Impact
- **Biggest individual factor in Q4 guidance** per CFO; 460 bps unfavorable impact on revenue growth in dollars
- FX has slight favorability on operating income due to international investments denominated in foreign currencies
- FX headwind was larger than expected in Q3 (~460 bps actual vs. ~390 bps guided); same level carried into Q4

#### Consumer / Stores Business
- Growth rates started to slow as Q3 progressed, particularly in international segment
- International weakness tied to tougher recessionary environment in Europe (Ukraine war, energy crisis)
- North America was strong but also started to slow a bit
- Expect moderating sales growth impacts to persist throughout Q4
- Ready for a range of holiday spending outcomes; optimistic but realistic
- In-stock levels are really high; delivery speeds getting very close to target
- Best selection ever offered
- Advertisers looking for effective advertising; Amazon ads at point of purchase positions well

#### AWS Trajectory
- **Q3 reported 28% ex-FX growth for the full quarter**
- **Back end of Q3 was more in the mid-20% growth rate** (deceleration within quarter)
- **Mid-20% run rate carried as assumption into Q4 guidance**
- Customers focused on controlling costs in macro uncertainty; proactively helping customers cost optimize
- Industries with lower demand showing up in volumes: financial services, mortgage business, cryptocurrencies
- Customers managing workloads, switching to lower-cost products, adopting Graviton chips for better cost-performance ratios
- re:Invent conference coming in late November; expected 40,000+ in-person attendees

#### Cost Structure & Operations
- $1.5B sequential improvement target for Q3; achieved ~$1.0B (missed by ~$0.5B)
- Hard to improve productivity much in Q4 due to maximum stress on operations during holiday peak
- Goal: leave operations in strong condition for fast start on initiatives in Q1 2023
- Forward CapEx plan tightened; feel good about arc of demand vs. supply in fulfillment and transportation
- Inflation remains a wildcard; working to minimize (fully utilized trucks, preventing long-zone shipments)
- Belt-tightening measures: pausing hiring in certain businesses, winding down select products/services
- Work on cost structure continues through end of year and into first half of 2023

## Cost Optimization / Belt-Tightening Initiatives

### Three Buckets of Operations Improvement
1. **Productivity:** Made good strides but still a lot of work; hard to make progress in Q4 peak season; fast start planned for Q1 2023
2. **Fixed Cost Leverage:** Altered forward plan, reduced CapEx; feel good about demand-supply arc in fulfillment/transportation
3. **Inflation:** Wildcard; working to minimize fuel, trucking, shipping costs through operational efficiency (full truck utilization, avoiding long-zone shipments)

### Hiring & Restructuring
- Pausing hiring in certain businesses
- Winding down products and services where resources better spent elsewhere (Amazon Care, Fabric.com, Amazon Explore)
- Aim to strike balance between long-term customer investment and operational efficiency

### Network Context
- Fulfillment network doubled over last 2.5 years
- Cost structure not yet back to pre-pandemic levels in operations
- 2023 will be key year for continued improvement

## AWS Strategic Commentary

### Customer Cost Optimization
- Uptick in customers focused on controlling costs
- Proactively helping customers cost optimize (as in prior periods of economic uncertainty)
- Options: move storage to lower-priced tiers, shift workloads to Graviton chips
- **Graviton3 processors:** 40% better price performance than comparable x86-based instances
- Cloud computing benefit: turning fixed cost into variable expense for customers

### Demand by Industry
- Some industries have lower demand: financial services, mortgage business, cryptocurrencies
- Comparison to 2020: In pandemic, some industries went down while others surged; that offsetting dynamic not in place now
- Everyone is cautious and watching spend

### Infrastructure Expansion
- AWS Middle East region launched in August 2022
- AWS Asia Pacific region in Thailand announced
- Continuing to expand breadth and depth of service offerings
- Recent launches: new EC2 machine learning training instances, AWS IoT FleetWise

## Key Analyst Q&A Data Points

- **AWS exit rate & Q4 trajectory (Eric Sheridan, Goldman Sachs):** Q3 reported 28% ex-FX growth; back end of quarter was mid-20% growth rate; that rate carried as assumption into Q4 guidance; not sure how it will play out; international consumer business saw biggest deceleration, tied to European recession/energy crisis; North America strong but also slowing a bit; strong July driven by Prime Day, then moderating through quarter
- **Free cash flow path (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan):** Multiple factors: (1) drop-off in trailing 12-month income vs. prior year from operations cost headwinds; (2) inventory elevated with more weeks of cover due to supply chain issues from Asia; AP-to-inventory working capital cycle has been flipping unfavorably, expected to normalize in 2023; (3) high CapEx from network doubling, now being reduced; intent to recover income, normalize inventory/AP cycle, and drive CapEx efficiency to restore FCF
- **AWS margins (Mark Mahaney, Evercore):** Sequential decline in AWS op margin from Q2 to Q3; driven by: (1) stock-based comp and wage inflation (concentrated in tech employees/AWS); (2) energy costs materially higher than pre-pandemic (electricity/natural gas prices up more than 2x over last couple of years; ~200 bps margin degradation vs. 2 years ago); margins will fluctuate as balance investments vs. customer pricing renegotiations; working to optimize operations and use less energy; will outrun energy cost growth trajectory as AWS scales
- **International losses (Mark Mahaney, Evercore):** Mix of profitability in established markets (Europe, Japan) offset by emerging country investments and Prime benefits investment; QoQ increase in losses driven by higher fuel costs in Europe and margin dilution from Prime Day (devices sold at/below cost); working hard to ensure current profitability is not the new normal
- **AWS customer optimization detail (Brent Thill, Jefferies):** Some industries have lower demand (financial services, mortgage, crypto); customers looking to save vs. committed spend; options include managing workloads, switching to lower-cost products, adopting Graviton chips; cloud computing turns fixed to variable; good for long-term adoption but short-run offset from budget-conscious companies
- **AWS backlog (Dave Fildes):** $104.3B at end of Q3; ~57% up YoY; new customer pipeline healthy; backlog can fluctuate quarter-to-quarter based on commitments signed in period
- **Operations cost improvement target (Ross Sandler, Barclays):** Targeted $1.5B sequential improvement from Q2 to Q3; achieved ~$1.0B (came up ~$0.5B short); shortfall mostly in productivity due to very high inventory levels in warehouses from Prime Day and Prime Early Access Sale preparations; space constraints created blockages to productivity improvement
- **Path to historical North America retail margins (Ross Sandler, Barclays):** Cannot forecast into 2023 yet; message is that work needed in 2023 is identified and being worked on now; Q4 is not a good quarter for productivity improvement due to peak season stress; fast start on initiatives planned for Q1 2023
- **Holiday season outlook (John Blackledge, Cowen):** Ready with best selection ever, high in-stock levels, delivery speeds near target; optimistic about holiday but realistic that various factors weighing on consumer wallets; ready for a range of outcomes; Prime Early Access Sale created great value and allowed holiday purchasing head start

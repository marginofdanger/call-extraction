## Generative AI / Large Language Models

### Three-Layer Strategic Framework (CEO Jassy)

#### Layer 1: Compute / Custom Chips
- **Graviton:** General purpose CPU chip; used by 50,000+ customers including 98 of top 100 EC2 customers; ~40% better price performance than other leading x86 processors
- **Trainium:** Custom AI training chip; on second version; described as "very appealing price performance option"
- **Inferentia:** Custom AI inference chip; on second version
- **EC2 P5 Instances:** Powered by NVIDIA H100 GPUs for training large models; customers excited but supply has been scarce
- **Custom chip thesis:** Market currently has only one viable option (NVIDIA) and supply is scarce; AWS aims to provide differentiated alternative

#### Layer 2: Large Language Models as a Service (Bedrock)
- **Bedrock:** Managed service providing access to multiple LLM providers
- **Model Partners:** Anthropic (Claude 2), Stability AI (Stable Diffusion XL 1.0), AI21 Labs, Cohere, plus Amazon's own Titan models
- **Capabilities:** Model customization with proprietary data, enterprise-grade security, privacy features, managed service wrapper
- **New Feature:** Agents for Amazon Bedrock (allows customers to create conversational agents with personalized, up-to-date answers)
- **Named Customers:** Bridgewater Associates, Coda, Lonely Planet, Omnicom, 3M, Ryanair, Showpad, Travelers

#### Layer 3: Applications
- **CodeWhisperer:** AI coding companion; "off to a very strong start"
- Every Amazon team building generative AI applications to enhance customer experiences
- Most gen AI applications expected to be built by other companies, with the largest number expected on AWS
- "The core of AI is data. People want to bring generative AI models to the data, not the other way around" -- CEO Jassy

### Gen AI Revenue Timing
- Machine learning/AI already contributing to AWS revenue for several years (compute for training and inference, 20+ ML services)
- Generative AI specifically: "Very early stages... a few steps into a marathon" -- CEO Jassy
- "I expect that will be very large, but it will be in the future"
- Every Amazon business has multiple generative AI initiatives underway (stores, AWS, advertising, devices/Alexa, entertainment)

### Gen AI Impact on CapEx
- FY 2023 capital investment guidance of "slightly more than $50 billion" includes "a pretty significant amount of capital expense in the AWS business for large language models and for generative AI"
- "Quite a bit of demand right now"
- CEO: "I would like to have the challenge of having to spend a lot more in capital in generative AI because it will mean that customers are having success"

## Fulfillment Regionalization & Cost to Serve

### Regionalization Results
- Transitioned from 1 national U.S. network to 8 separate regions
- **20% reduction** in number of touches for a delivered package
- **19% reduction** in miles traveled to deliver packages
- **1,000+ bps increase** in deliveries fulfilled within region (now at **76%**)
- Lower miles + fewer touches = lower cost and faster delivery simultaneously

### Same-Day Facilities
- Located in largest U.S. metro areas
- Handle top-moving 100,000 SKUs; cover millions of other SKUs via injection from nearby FCs
- Order to delivery-ready in as little as 11 minutes
- **Planning to double the number of same-day facilities**
- Described as "one of our most cost-effective fulfillment mechanisms" and "one of our least expensive"
- Smaller facilities with less conveyance, streamlined pick-to-pack-to-dock

### Future Cost Opportunity
- "While we are pleased with the progress we have made, we see more opportunity to drive improved cost efficiencies going forward" -- CFO Olsavsky
- "We still see several ways in which we can be more efficient in this structure" -- CEO Jassy
- Reevaluated virtually every part of fulfillment network; identified additional structural changes for future upside
- "We believe that we are far from the law of diminishing returns" -- CEO Jassy

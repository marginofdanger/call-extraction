## Generative AI / Large Language Models -- KEY STRATEGIC INITIATIVE

### Three-Layer Framework (CEO Jassy)

#### Layer 1: Compute / Chips
- All large language models require compute; key is the chips in that compute
- GPUs currently "expensive and scarce" -- hard to find enough capacity
- **Trainium:** AWS custom chip specialized for ML training; second version released
- **Inferentia:** AWS custom chip specialized for inference/predictions; second version released
- Price/performance combination described as "pretty differentiated and very significant"
- Most current spend on training; as models move to production, "all the spend is going to be in inference"

#### Layer 2: Foundational Models / Bedrock
- **Bedrock:** Managed foundational model service (announced ~1 week prior to call)
- Allows customers to run foundational models from Amazon (Titan) or third parties (AI21, Anthropic, Stability AI)
- Customers can customize models with proprietary data without leaking IP to the generalized model
- Runs with same security, privacy, and features as other AWS applications
- Described as "very compelling for customers"
- Only "a small number of companies" will invest time and billions to build their own LLMs

#### Layer 3: Applications
- Applications built on top of LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT as an example)
- **CodeWhisperer:** AWS coding assistant; general availability announced; developers input natural language, get code output
- Every Amazon business building on top of LLMs to "reinvent customer experiences"
- Expected to transform "virtually every customer experience that exists and many that don't exist"

### Alexa / Echo Integration
- Building a "much larger and much more generalized and capable" LLM underneath Alexa
- ~200 million endpoints in use across entertainment, shopping, smart home, information
- Vision: world's best personal assistant; LLMs "really accelerate the possibility"
- "Significant business model underneath it" per CEO Jassy

## Cost Structure & Fulfillment Regionalization

### Regionalization Rollout (CEO Jassy)
- U.S. fulfillment network transitioned from national to regional model
- **8 interconnected regions** created in geographic areas, each with broad relevant selection
- Each region operates in a "largely self-sufficient way" while still able to ship nationally when necessary
- Rollout "just recently completed" as of Q1 2023
- "Quite bullish on the early results" -- shorter travel distances mean lower cost to serve and faster delivery

### Delivery Speed
- Providing more 1-day and same-day deliveries
- "On track to have our fastest Prime delivery speeds ever in 2023"

### Cost to Serve Progress
- Revenue and unit sales growth outpaced fulfillment expense and outbound shipping cost growth in Q1
- Inflationary pressures easing: reductions in linehaul shipping rates, lower diesel fuel and electricity costs
- Continued process and technology improvements in fulfillment productivity
- Exited Q4 with good balance of labor; leveraged that through Q1

### Margin Recovery Path
- **North America operating margin Q1 2023:** 1.2%
- **Pre-pandemic target range:** 4%-6% (per CFO Olsavsky)
- CFO: "Making solid progress on recovering our cost structure and getting it back to pre-pandemic levels"
- CEO: "Pretty optimistic that we have a chance not just to recover to where we were pre pandemic in terms of operating margin, but I think there's additional upside with some of the opportunities we've identified"
- Described as "a longer road than bouncing back in 1 or 2 quarters"

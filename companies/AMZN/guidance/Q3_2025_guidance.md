# Amazon.com, Inc. Q3 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 30, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2025

## Q3 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated
- **Total Net Sales:** $180.2B (+13% YoY reported; +12% YoY ex-FX; per CEO and press release)
- **FX Impact on Revenue:** ~130 bps favorable impact (~$1.6B; per supplement: FX favorable impact $129M operating income; revenue FX derived from 13% reported vs. 12% ex-FX)
- **Operating Income (Reported):** $17.4B (flat YoY reported vs. $17.4B in Q3 2024)
- **Operating Income (Adjusted, ex-special charges):** $21.7B -- **Beat -- $1.2B above the high end** of $15.5B-$20.5B Q3 guidance range (per CFO: "Excluding these 2 charges, worldwide operating income would have been $21.7 billion or $1.2 billion above the high end of our guidance range")
- **Special Charges (Q3 2025):** $4.3B total reduction to operating income:
  1. **$2.5B FTC legal settlement** -- impacts North America segment; recorded in Other Operating Expense line
  2. **$1.8B severance costs** for role eliminations -- impacts all 3 segments; recorded primarily in Technology & Infrastructure, Sales & Marketing, and G&A expense lines
- **Operating Margin (Reported):** 9.7% (vs. 11.0% in Q3 2024)
- **Net Income:** $21.2B (+38% YoY vs. $15.3B in Q3 2024; includes $9.5B pretax gain on Anthropic investment)
- **EPS (Diluted):** $1.95 (vs. $1.43 in Q3 2024)
- **Trailing 12-Month Free Cash Flow:** $14.8B (down from $47.7B TTM in Q3 2024; -69% YoY driven by $50.9B YoY increase in net CapEx)
- **Trailing 12-Month Operating Cash Flow:** $130.7B (+16% YoY vs. $112.7B)
- **WW Paid Units Growth:** +11% YoY (per CFO and supplement)
- **Employees:** 1,578,000 (+2% YoY vs. 1,551,000 in Q3 2024)

- **Q3 2025 Backlog:** $200B (per CEO: "Backlog grew to $200 billion by Q3 quarter end")

### AI Business
- **Trainium2 Revenue:** Multibillion-dollar business; grew 150% quarter-over-quarter (per CEO)
- **Trainium2 Status:** Fully subscribed (per CEO)
- **Trainium2 Price Performance:** ~30-40% better than other GPU options (per CEO: "Trainium is 30% to 40% more price performant than other options out there")

- **Bedrock Token Usage:** Majority of token usage in Amazon Bedrock is already running on Trainium (per CEO)
- **Bedrock Long-Term Vision:** "In the long run, believe Bedrock could be as big a business for AWS as EC2" (per CEO)

- **Preview:** Expected at the end of 2025 (per CEO: "Trainium3 should preview at the end of this year")
- **Volume Production:** "Much fuller volumes coming in the beginning of '26" (per CEO)
- **Performance:** Expected to be ~40% better than Trainium2 (per CEO: "I expect Trainium3 will be about 40% better than Trainium2")
- **Demand:** "A lot of customers, both very large, and medium-sized who're quite interested in Trainium3" (per CEO)
- **Customer Base Expansion:** "Today, with Trainium2, we have a small number of very large customers. But...we expect to accommodate more customers starting with Trainium3" (per CEO)

### NVIDIA Partnership
- **Relationship:** "Very deep relationship with NVIDIA...We buy a lot of NVIDIA. We are not constrained in any way in buying NVIDIA" (per CEO)
- **Forward Outlook:** "I expect that we'll continue to buy more NVIDIA both next year and in the future" (per CEO)
- **Multi-Chip Strategy:** "We're always going to have multiple chip options for our customers" (per CEO)

### Graviton (CPU Custom Silicon)
- **Price Performance:** ~40% better than other x86 processors (per CEO)

### AWS Growth Drivers (CEO, Q&A)
- **AI Growth:** Seeing growth in inference, training, custom silicon (Trainium), Bedrock, SageMaker (per CEO)
- **Enterprise Migration:** Large number of enterprises returning to moving from on-premises to cloud; AWS continues to earn "the lion's share of those transformations" (per CEO)
- **CEO on Sustainability:** "I look at the momentum we have right now, and I believe that we can continue to grow at a clip like this for a while" (per CEO)

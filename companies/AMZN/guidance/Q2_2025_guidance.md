# Amazon.com, Inc. Q2 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 31, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2025

---

## Q2 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated
- **Total Net Sales:** $167.7B (+12% YoY ex-FX; per CEO)
- **FX Impact on Revenue:** $1.5B favorable impact in Q2 (per CFO: "Foreign exchange had a $1.5 billion favorable impact to revenue in the quarter as foreign currencies generally strengthened versus the U.S. dollar")
- **FX vs. Q2 Guidance:** Q2 guidance had anticipated ~10 bps (~$100M) unfavorable FX impact; actual was $1.5B favorable -- **significant FX tailwind vs. expectations**
- **Operating Income:** $19.2B (+31% YoY; per CEO)
- **Operating Income vs. Guidance:** **Beat -- $1.7B above the high end** of $13.0B-$17.5B guidance range (per CFO: "which was $1.7 billion above the high end of our guidance range")
- **Trailing 12-Month Free Cash Flow:** $18.2B (per CEO)
- **WW Paid Units Growth:** +12% YoY (per CFO)

### Revenue by Category
- **Advertising Services:** $15.7B (+22% YoY; per CEO: "$15.7 billion of revenue in the quarter, growing 22% year-over-year")
- **AWS:** $30.9B (+17.5% YoY; per CFO)
- **AWS Annualized Revenue Run Rate:** >$123B (per CEO and CFO)
- **Online Stores / Physical Stores / 3P Seller Services / Subscriptions / Other:** Not individually broken out on call

### Segment Results

#### North America
- **Net Sales:** $100.1B (+11% YoY; per CFO)
- **Operating Income:** $7.5B (+$2.5B YoY; per CFO)
- **Operating Margin:** 7.5% (+190 bps YoY; per CFO)

#### International
- **Net Sales:** $36.8B (+11% YoY ex-FX; per CFO)
- **Operating Income:** $1.5B (+$1.2B YoY; per CFO)
- **Operating Margin:** 4.1% (+320 bps YoY; per CFO)
- **Margin Progression:** Close to 700 bps improvement over the last 10 quarters (per CFO: "we've seen an increase of close to 700 basis points during that time period")
- **Established Countries (UK, Germany, Japan):** Already at similar margin profiles to the U.S.; strong transportation network productivity, higher units per package, faster delivery at lower cost (per CFO)
- **Emerging Countries:** 8 countries launched in the last 5 years; all at varying stages of growth; all making "very nice improvements quarter-over-quarter" (per CFO)

#### AWS
- **Net Sales:** $30.9B (+17.5% YoY; per CFO)
- **Annualized Revenue Run Rate:** >$123B (per CFO)
- **Operating Income:** $10.2B (per CFO)
- **Operating Margin:** 32.9% (per CFO; down from record 39.5% in Q1 2025)
- **AWS Growth Trajectory:** 17% (Q1'24) -> 19% (Q2'24) -> 19.1% (Q3'24) -> 19% (Q4'24) -> 17% (Q1'25) -> **17.5% (Q2'25; slight sequential acceleration)**
- **Q1-to-Q2 Margin Decline Drivers (CFO):**
  1. **Seasonal SBC step-up (~half of QoQ decline):** "The largest quarter-over-quarter driver of the decrease or about half is due to the seasonal step-up in stock-based compensation expense, driven by the timing of our annual compensation cycle"
  2. **Higher depreciation expense** from growing CapEx investments
  3. **Unfavorable FX impact** on a YoY basis
- **Margin Commentary (CFO):** "We expect AWS operating margins to fluctuate over time driven in part by the level of investments we are making at any point in time. We will continue to invest more capital in chips, data centers and power to pursue this unusually large opportunity that we have in generative AI"

### 3P Seller Mix
- **WW Third-Party Seller Unit Mix:** 62% in Q2 2025 -- **highest ever** (+100 bps YoY vs. Q2 2024; per CFO)

### Delivery Speed
- **Q2 2025:** Set another global speed record; delivered to Prime members at fastest speeds ever (per CEO)
- **Same-Day / Next-Day (U.S.):** Delivered 30% more items same-day or next-day than Q2 2024 (per CEO)
- **Direct Lanes:** Share of orders moving through direct lanes (straight from fulfillment to delivery) increased over 40% YoY (per CEO)
- **Average Distance:** Packages traveled 12% less distance (per CEO)
- **Handling Touches:** Lowered handling touches per unit by nearly 15% (per CEO)
- **Rural / Small City Expansion:** Plans to expand same-day and next-day delivery to tens of millions of U.S. customers in 4,000+ smaller cities, towns and rural communities by year-end; already available in 1,000+ communities (per CEO)

### Fulfillment & Outbound Costs
- **Outbound Shipping Costs:** +6% YoY (per CFO; meaningfully slower pace than +12% unit growth)
- **Cost Drivers:** Improved inventory placement, strong leverage on high unit volumes, higher in-demand inventory from 1P and 3P partners (per CFO)
- **Robotics Milestone:** Deployed 1 millionth robot across global fulfillment network (per CEO)
- **DeepFleet:** AI-driven robot travel efficiency improvement of 10%; acts as traffic management system for robot coordination (per CEO)

### Prime Day (July 2025)
- **Performance:** Biggest Prime Day ever -- record sales, record number of items sold, record number of Prime sign-ups in 3 weeks leading up to the event (per CEO)
- **Sellers:** Independent sellers (mostly small and medium-sized businesses) saw best sales performance of any Prime Day event (per CEO)

### Everyday Essentials
- **Growth:** Outpaced the rest of the business globally (per CEO)
- **Share:** Representing 1 out of every 3 units sold (per CEO)

### Amazon Pharmacy
- **Growth:** Up 50% YoY year-to-date on an already significant size base (per CEO)

## AWS Strategic Commentary

### AI Business
- **Scale:** Multibillion-dollar annual revenue run rate (per CEO)
- **Growth:** Triple-digit year-over-year percentage growth (per CEO: "a large fast-growing triple-digit year-over-year percentage multibillion-dollar business")
- **Demand:** "More demand than we have supplied for at the moment" (per CEO)

### AWS Backlog
- **Q2 2025 Backlog:** $195B (+25% YoY; per Dave Fildes) -- **up from $189B (+20% YoY) in Q1 2025**

### Capacity Constraints (CEO Jassy, Q&A)
- **Current State:** Demand exceeds capacity (per CEO)
- **Single Biggest Constraint:** Power (per CEO)
- **Other Constraints:** Chips (supply intermittent), server components (once chips arrive, yields on server builds can disappoint), new chip generations sometimes late (per CEO)
- **Relief Timeline:** "I don't believe that we will have fully resolved the amount of capacity we need for the amount of demand that we have in a couple of quarters. I think it will take several quarters. But I do expect that it's going to get better each quarter" (per CEO)
- **Industry-Wide:** "It's really true across the industry today" (per CEO)

### AWS Acceleration Potential (CEO Jassy, Q&A with Brian Nowak)
- **No Segment Guidance Given:** "We don't give guidance by segment" (per CEO)
- **Optimistic Drivers:** (1) More enterprises resuming march to modernize infrastructure and move from on-premises to cloud; (2) AI accelerating as more companies deploy AI applications into production at scale; (3) More capacity coming online in coming months and quarters (per CEO)
- **On Competitive Positioning:** CEO noted AWS has "meaningfully larger business" than #2 player (estimated at ~65% of AWS size); competitive advantage in operational performance, availability, durability, latency/throughput, security, and end-to-end AI stack functionality (per CEO)
- **85-90% Still On-Premises:** CEO reiterated that 85-90% of worldwide IT spend is still on-premises; expects equation to flip over next 10-15 years, further accelerated by AI (per CEO)

### Inference Cost Imperative (CEO Jassy, Q&A)
- **Training vs. Inference Split:** "At scale, 80% to 90% of the cost will be an inference because you only train periodically, but you're spinning out predictions and inferences all the time" (per CEO)
- **Trainium2 Price Performance:** "About 30% and 40% better price performance than the other GPU providers out there right now" (per CEO)
- **Trainium3 in Development:** "We're already working on our third version of Trainium as well" (per CEO)

## Tariff Impact & Consumer Demand

### Current Observations (CEO Jassy)
- **No Demand Diminishment:** "Through the first half of the year, we haven't yet seen diminishing demand nor prices meaningfully appreciating" (per CEO)
- **No Broad ASP Increases:** No broad-scale ASP increases observed in H1 2025 (per CEO)
- **Uncertainty Remains:** "It's impossible to know what will happen. Where will tariffs finally settle especially China? What happens when we deplete the inventory we forward bought" (per CEO)
- **Seller Diversity Advantage:** Over 2 million sellers with different strategies on whether to pass on higher costs; customers advantaged by shopping at Amazon because "more likely to find lower prices" (per CEO)
- **CEO:** "Much of it [tariff noise] thus far has been wrong and misreported"

### Forward Risk Factors
- **Inventory Depletion:** Unknown what happens when prebuys and forward-deployed inventory are depleted (per CEO)
- **Cost Absorption:** Unclear who will absorb higher costs if tariffs persist (per CEO)
- **Q3 Guide Reflects Tariff Uncertainty:** Guidance range incorporates range of assumptions about macro environment including tariffs (per CFO)

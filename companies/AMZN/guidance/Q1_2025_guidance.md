# Amazon.com, Inc. Q1 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** May 1, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2025

---

## Q1 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated
- **Total Net Sales:** $155.7B (+10% YoY ex-FX; per CEO)
- **FX Impact on Revenue:** $1.4B headwind YoY (per CFO: "a $1.4 billion headwind from foreign exchange year-over-year in the quarter")
- **Operating Income:** $18.4B (+20% YoY; per CEO)
- **Operating Income vs. Guidance:** **Beat -- ~$400M above the high end** of $14.0B-$18.0B guidance range (per CFO: "approximately $400 million above the high end of our guidance range")
- **Net Income:** $17.1B (includes pretax gain of $3.3B in nonoperating income from Anthropic investment -- conversion of convertible notes to nonvoting preferred stock; per CFO)
- **Trailing 12-Month Free Cash Flow:** $25.9B (per CEO)
- **WW Paid Units Growth:** +8% YoY (per CFO)

### Revenue by Category
- **Advertising Services:** $13.9B (+19% YoY; per CEO: "$13.9 billion of revenue in the quarter and growing 19% year-over-year")
- **AWS:** $29.3B (+17% YoY; per CFO)
- **AWS Annualized Revenue Run Rate:** $117B (per CEO and CFO)
- **Online Stores / Physical Stores / 3P Seller Services / Subscriptions / Other:** Not individually broken out on call

### Segment Results

#### North America
- **Net Sales:** $92.9B (+8% YoY; per CFO)
- **Operating Income:** $5.8B (per CFO)
- **Operating Margin:** 6.3% (per CFO)
- **One-Time Charges Impact:** Without one-time charges (historical customer returns resolution + costs to receive inventory pulled forward ahead of tariffs), North America operating margin would have been ~7.2% (~90 bps higher; per CFO)

#### International
- **Net Sales:** $33.5B (+8% YoY ex-FX; per CFO)
- **Operating Income:** $1.0B (per CFO)
- **Operating Margin:** 3.0% (per CFO)
- **One-Time Charges Impact:** Without one-time charges, International operating margin would have been ~3.7% (~70 bps higher; per CFO)

#### AWS
- **Net Sales:** $29.3B (+17% YoY; per CFO)
- **Annualized Revenue Run Rate:** >$117B (per CFO)
- **Operating Income:** $11.5B (per CFO)
- **Operating Margin:** ~39.2% (calculated: $11.5B / $29.3B)
- **AWS Growth Trajectory:** 17% (Q1'24) -> 19% (Q2'24) -> 19.1% (Q3'24) -> 19% (Q4'24) -> **17% (Q1'25; deceleration)**
- **Margin Commentary (CFO):** "We expect AWS operating margins to fluctuate over time, driven in part by the level of investments we're making at any point in time. We plan to bring on an increasing amount of capacity in the back half of the year."

### One-Time Charges (Q1 2025)
- **Historical Customer Returns Charge:** Recorded during the quarter; not yet resolved (per CFO)
- **Tariff Inventory Pull-Forward Costs:** Costs to receive inventory that was pulled forward into Q1 ahead of anticipated tariffs (per CFO)
- **Combined Impact:** ~90 bps on North America operating margin; ~70 bps on International operating margin (per CFO: "Without these charges, North America and International operating margins would have been approximately 90 basis points and 70 basis points higher")

### Anthropic Investment Gain
- **Pretax Gain:** $3.3B included in nonoperating income (per CFO: "pretax gain of $3.3 billion included in nonoperating income and it relates to our investment in Anthropic. This activity is not related to Amazon's ongoing operations, but rather the result of the conversion of a portion of our convertible notes to nonvoting preferred stock")

### 3P Seller Mix
- **WW Third-Party Selling Unit Mix:** 61% in Q1 2025 (consistent with Q1 2024; per CFO)

### Delivery Speed
- **Q1 2025:** Set new delivery speed records; fastest delivery ever for Prime members worldwide (per CEO)
- **Same-Day / Next-Day:** Delivered more items same-day or next-day in Q1 than any other quarter in history (per CEO)

### Everyday Essentials & Grocery
- **Everyday Essentials Growth:** Grew more than 2x as fast as the rest of the business in Q1 (per CEO)
- **Everyday Essentials Share:** Represented 1 out of every 3 units sold in the U.S. on Amazon (per CEO)
- **Grocery Scale:** Over $100B in gross sales last year (excluding Whole Foods Market and Amazon Fresh); Amazon is one of the largest grocers in the U.S. (per CEO)

### Customer Savings Events
- **Q1 Deal Events:** Big Spring Sale (U.S. and Canada), Spring Deal Days (Europe), Ramadan/Eid Sale events (Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Turkiye, UAE) -- customers saved over $500M (per CEO)

## Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2025)

### Net Sales
- **Q2 2025 Net Sales Guidance:** $159.0B to $164.0B
- **Implied YoY Growth:** ~+7.6% to +11.0% reported (vs. Q2 2024 net sales of $147.977B)
- **FX Impact:** ~10 bps headwind (per CFO: "approximately 10 basis points in the quarter")
- **Midpoint:** $161.5B (implies ~9.1% YoY reported growth)

### Operating Income
- **Q2 2025 Operating Income Guidance:** $13.0B to $17.5B
- **Prior Year Comp:** Q2 2024 operating income was $14.7B
- **Midpoint:** $15.25B (implies ~3.7% YoY growth at midpoint)
- **Implied Operating Margin Range:** ~8.0% to ~10.7% (at midpoint revenue of $161.5B)
- **Range Width:** $4.5B (wider than typical; per CFO: "with the uncertainty, we've added a bit to the range that we've given you")

### Key Guidance Assumptions & Commentary

#### Stock-Based Compensation Step-Up
- **CFO:** "The stock-based comp always steps up generally in Q2 versus Q1 and then reset the rate that carries through for the next 4 quarters. You can look at historic trends to get an idea of that"
- **Impact:** Seasonal increase in Q2 driven by annual compensation cycle; impacts Q2 operating income guidance (per CFO)

#### Kuiper Launch Costs
- **CFO:** "We do have some additional Kuiper launch costs in Q2. You saw a launch happened this week. And a reminder that we expense those launch costs until the point of commercialization, which the plan is to have that later in this year"

#### Tariff Costs in Q2
- **CFO:** "We do have tariffs that we'll be paying on retail purchases based on current tariffs. It's not large in Q2. We had done a lot of prebuying of inventory in Q1"

#### Wider Guidance Range Due to Uncertainty
- **CFO:** "Just generally, I think with the uncertainty, we've added a bit to the range that we've given you. We generally have a wide range. But just the general uncertainty that we're seeing and uncertainty of consumer demand and all everything else is causing us to increase the ranges a bit"

#### April Demand Trends
- **CFO:** "We saw actually some strength in April based on what could end up being some prebuys of a number of things...and advertising has been strong. So we think there's a lot of positive trends, but certainly, uncertainty right now for the quarter"

## AWS Strategic Commentary

### AI Business
- **Scale:** Multibillion-dollar annual revenue run rate (per CEO)
- **Growth:** Growing triple-digit percentages year-over-year (per CEO)
- **Demand:** "As fast as we actually put the capacity in, it's being consumed" (per CEO)
- **CEO:** "Our AI business right now is a multibillion-dollar annual run rate business that's growing triple-digit percentages year-over-year"

### Capacity Constraints & Growth Trajectory (CEO Jassy, Q&A)
- **Current State:** Demand exceeds supply; CEO believes could be driving more revenue with more capacity
- **Constraint Sources:**
  1. **NVIDIA GPU Instances (P5):** Bringing on a lot of P5 instances as fast as possible
  2. **Trainium2 Instances:** Landing more and more instances; more coming "in the coming months"
  3. **Supply Chain Bottlenecks:** "Motherboards and some other componentry" are jammed up due to overall demand
- **Relief Timeline:** CEO: "I do believe that the supply chain issues and the capacity issues will continue to get better as the year proceeds"
- **Back Half Capacity Ramp:** CFO: "We plan to bring on an increasing amount of capacity in the back half of the year"

### AWS Revenue Lumpiness (CEO Jassy, Q&A with Justin Post)
- **Enterprise Sales Cycles:** Migrations are unpredictable; some companies move fast, others slow; tranches can expand as customers get excited about cloud benefits
- **Startup Unpredictability:** Hard to predict when startups find product-market fit and scale
- **AI Growth Volatility:** CEO cited coding agents (Cursor, Vercel on AWS) as example of explosive, unpredictable growth in recent months
- **AI Adoption Stage:** "Even though we have a lot of combined inference in those areas, I would say we're not even at the second strike of the first batter in the first inning. It is so early right now"
- **Growth Context:** 17% YoY growth on a $117B revenue run rate is "still pretty significant growth" (per CEO)

### AWS Backlog
- **Q1 2025 Backlog:** $189B (+20% YoY; per Dave Fildes)
- **Weighted Average Remaining Life:** 4.1 years (per Dave Fildes)

### AWS Margin Drivers (CFO Olsavsky, Q&A with Doug Anmuth)
- **Strong Margin Performance:** Attributed to strong growth + continued investment in innovation and technology
- **Efficiency Drivers:**
  1. Software and process improvements optimizing server capacity -> lower infrastructure cost
  2. More efficient custom networking gear -> lower network costs
  3. Maximizing power usage in existing data centers -> lowers costs and reclaims power for newer workloads
  4. Advancing custom silicon like Graviton -> better price performance for Amazon and customers
- **Margin Headwinds Going Forward:** Level of investment, competitive pricing, mix of gen AI services ramping up, significant infrastructure investment planned for H2 2025
- **CFO:** "We do have a lot of investment in infrastructure going on and planned for the second half of the year. So that will -- we'll start to see the impact of that"

### Enterprise Cloud Migration Resurgence (CEO Jassy, Q&A with Brent Thill)
- **Pre-Pandemic:** Aggressive, methodical ("almost metronomic") migration cadence
- **Pandemic:** Cost optimization focus slowed migrations
- **Post-Pandemic:** Gen AI explosion diverted attention
- **Current Trend (Last 16-18 months):** Enterprises realize they need to do both AI and cloud migration; "increased resurgence and understanding for enterprises that they are dropping the low-hanging fruit if they don't move their infrastructure to the cloud"
- **CEO:** "We're having meaningful success in those conversations and in companies choosing to transform their infrastructure on top of AWS"

## Tariff Impact & Consumer Demand

### Current Observations (CEO Jassy)
- **No Demand Attenuation:** "We haven't seen any attenuation of demand yet" (per CEO)
- **Potential Stocking Up:** "We've seen some heightened buying in certain categories that may indicate stocking up in advance of any potential tariff impact" (per CEO)
- **No ASP Increase Yet:** "We have not seen the average selling price of retail items appreciably go up yet" (per CEO)
- **Reasons for Stable ASPs:** (1) Forward buying by Amazon as 1P seller; (2) Advanced inbounding by 3P sellers; (3) Most sellers haven't changed pricing yet (per CEO)

### Competitive Positioning on Tariffs (CEO Jassy)
- **Not Uniquely Susceptible:** Retailers buying from companies who themselves buy from China pay more than Chinese sellers direct to U.S. consumers on Amazon marketplace (per CEO)
- **Broad Selection Advantage:** Hundreds of millions of unique SKUs; better positioned to weather disruption (per CEO)
- **Seller Diversity:** 2M+ global sellers won't all pursue the same strategy; some will absorb tariffs to capture share (per CEO)
- **Historical Pattern:** Amazon has emerged from uncertain eras with more relative market share (per CEO)
- **Supply Chain Diversification:** Over last ~6 years, significantly diversified manufacturing away from China for AWS components and devices (per CEO)

### Inventory Management in Tariff Environment (CEO Jassy, Q&A with Brian Nowak)
- **Balancing Act:** Must be thoughtful about how much inventory enters fulfillment nodes; too much inventory slows productivity and ability to deliver quickly at target cost structure (per CEO)
- **3P Sellers:** Some sellers wanting to get as much inventory in as early as possible to beat potential deadlines (per CEO)

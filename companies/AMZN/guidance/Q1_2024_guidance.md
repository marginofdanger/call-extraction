# Amazon.com, Inc. Q1 2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** April 30, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2024

---

## Q1 2024 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated
- **Total Net Sales:** $143.3B (+13% YoY ex-FX; near the top end of guidance range)
- **Net Sales vs. Guidance:** **Near top end** of $138.0B-$143.5B guidance range; would have **exceeded top end** absent $700M unfavorable FX headwind vs. plan
- **FX Impact on Revenue:** $700M or ~50 bps unfavorable headwind to revenue relative to what was guided (more than planned)
- **Leap Year Impact on Revenue:** Added approximately 120 bps to YoY quarterly revenue growth rate
- **Operating Income:** $15.3B (+221% YoY; +$10.5B YoY vs. $4.8B in Q1 2023)
- **Operating Income vs. Guidance:** **Beat -- $3.3B above the high end** of $8.0B-$12.0B guidance range
- **Highest Quarterly Operating Income Ever:** $15.3B (surpassed Q4 2023 record of $13.2B)
- **FX Impact on Operating Income:** Negligible (per CFO)
- **Operating Margin:** ~10.7% (at $15.3B on $143.3B sales)
- **Trailing 12-Month FCF Adjusted for Equipment Finance Leases:** $48.8B (+$53.2B YoY; per CEO)

### Revenue Categories (from CEO/CFO commentary)
- **Advertising Services:** +24% YoY ex-FX (per CEO: "strength in advertising was primarily driven by sponsored products, supported by continued improvements in relevancy and measurement capabilities for advertisers")
- **Third-Party Seller Services:** +16% YoY ex-FX worldwide (per CFO)
- **AWS:** $25.0B (+17.2% YoY reported; ~16% YoY ex-leap year impact; per CFO)

### Segment Results

#### North America
- **Net Sales:** $86.3B (+12% YoY)
- **Operating Income:** $5.0B (+$4.1B YoY improvement; vs. $0.9B in Q1 2023)
- **Operating Margin:** 5.8% (+460 bps YoY vs. 1.2% in Q1 2023)
- **Operating Margin Trajectory:** -2.3% (Q1'22) -> -0.8% (Q2'22) -> -0.5% (Q3'22) -> -0.3% (Q4'22) -> 1.2% (Q1'23) -> 3.9% (Q2'23) -> 4.9% (Q3'23) -> 6.1% (Q4'23) -> **5.8% (Q1'24)**
- **Margin Drivers:** Improvements in cost to serve (regionalization, consolidated customer shipments), improved leverage from strong unit growth, lower transportation rates
- **Context:** CFO noted they can achieve pre-pandemic operating margins even without advertising benefit; "not quite there yet" but "not limiting ourselves to that"

#### International
- **Net Sales:** $31.9B (+11% YoY ex-FX)
- **Operating Income:** $903M (+$2.2B YoY improvement; vs. -$1.3B loss in Q1 2023)
- **Operating Margin:** 2.8% (+710 bps YoY vs. -4.3% in Q1 2023)
- **Operating Income Trajectory:** CFO: "steady progression in operating income in our international segment"
- **Established Countries (Europe, Japan, UK):** Profitable in their own right; following similar trajectory as the United States; adding selection, new features like grocery, Prime benefits
- **Emerging Countries (10 launched in last 7 years):** Each has its own particular trajectory on profitability; approach: (1) good customer experience, (2) Prime sign-ups (often driven by Prime Video), (3) cost structure optimization at scale, (4) add advertising, (5) breakeven then positive income/FCF
- **Consumer Spending Note:** CFO flagged "Europe where it appears to be a bit weaker relative to the U.S."

#### AWS
- **Net Sales:** $25.0B (+17.2% YoY reported; ~16% YoY ex-leap year; per CFO)
- **Annualized Revenue Run Rate:** $100B+ (per CEO: "$100 billion-plus annualized revenue run rate")
- **Operating Income:** $9.4B (+$4.3B YoY improvement; vs. $5.1B in Q1 2023)
- **Operating Margin:** 37.6% (+800 bps QoQ vs. ~29.6% in Q4 2023; per CFO: "AWS margins increased 800 basis points sequentially off Q4")
- **AWS Growth Trajectory:** 40% (Q4'21) -> 37% (Q1'22) -> 33% (Q2'22) -> 28% (Q3'22) -> 20% (Q4'22) -> 16% (Q1'23) -> 12% (Q2'23) -> 12% (Q3'23) -> 13% (Q4'23) -> **17.2% (Q1'24; significant reacceleration)**
- **Server Useful Life Change:** Increase from 5 years to 6 years effective January 2024; ~$900M/quarter benefit to operating income; primarily benefits AWS segment (per CFO in Q4 2023 call; confirmed included in these results)
- **Margin Drivers:** Cost controls, expanding revenue on top line, lower cost structure, server useful life change benefit
- **Workload Mix:** Growth in both generative AI and non-generative AI workloads across diverse group of customers and industries
- **Cost Optimization Status:** "While there will always be a level of ongoing optimization, we think the majority of the recent cycle is behind us, and we're likely closer to a steady state of these optimization efforts" -- CFO
- **Forward Margin Commentary:** "Over time, we expect the AWS operating margins to fluctuate, driven in part by the level of investments we are making in the business" -- CFO

### Third-Party Sellers
- **3P Seller Unit Mix:** 61% of total paid units (+200 bps YoY vs. 59% in Q1 2023; same as Q4 2023)
- **3P Seller Services Revenue Growth:** +16% YoY ex-FX
- **Drivers:** Strong 3P unit growth plus increased adoption of optional services such as fulfillment and global logistics

### Capital Investments
- **Q1 2024 CapEx:** $14.0B (per CFO in Q&A: "In Q1, we had $14 billion of CapEx")
- **Q1 2024 as Baseline:** CFO: "We expect that to be the low quarter for the year"
- **FY 2023 Total Capital Investments (CapEx + Equipment Finance Leases):** $48.4B (for comparison)
- **FY 2024 CapEx Outlook:** "Anticipate our overall capital expenditures to meaningfully increase year-over-year in 2024, primarily driven by higher infrastructure CapEx to support growth in AWS, including generative AI" -- CFO
- **Mix:** Majority of CapEx increase will be to support AWS infrastructure and generative AI; stores CapEx is modest capacity increases plus same-day fulfillment network and Amazon Logistics fleet upgrades
- **Demand Signals:** CFO: "Seeing strong demand signals from our customers and longer deals and larger commitments, many with generative AI components"
- **Capital Deployment Philosophy:** "We spend most of the capital upfront. But as you've seen over the last several years, we make that up in operating margin and free cash flow down the road as demand steadies out" -- CEO

### Delivery Speed
- **Q1 2024:** Delivered to Prime members at fastest speeds ever
- **March 2024, Top 60 U.S. Metros:** Nearly 60% of Prime member orders arrived same or next day
- **Global Cities (Toronto, London, Tokyo):** About 3 out of 4 items delivered same or next day
- **Impact:** Faster delivery drives increased purchase frequency, growth in Everyday Essentials, and higher total spend per Prime member

### Cost to Serve
- **Improvement Areas:** Regionalization benefits continuing; increased consolidation of units into fewer boxes; improved inbound fulfillment architecture for better inventory placement
- **Inbound Network:** Revamping U.S. inbound fulfillment architecture for better inventory placement closer to customers; "iterative process throughout the year"
- **Seller Fee Structure:** Lowered outbound fees in a meaningful way; added incentive for sellers to inbound into locations that allow more cost-efficient fulfillment
- **Same-Day Facilities:** "Our least expensive facilities in the network"; still have a fraction of the number they will have in U.S. and internationally
- **CEO Forward View:** "I don't think we're at the limits of what we can do. It's not going to all happen in 1 year. We're going to be working hard at this and inventing at this for several years, but I think we have a lot of upside in front of us"

### Cash Flow & Capital Returns
- **Trailing 12-Month FCF Adjusted for Equipment Finance Leases:** $48.8B (+$53.2B YoY)
- **Debt Repayment:** Paying down over $25B of debt from high watermark at end of Q1 2023 through 2024
- **Capital Returns Commentary:** "As far as dividends or buybacks or any other capital structure moves, we don't have anything to share with you on that today" -- CFO
- **Priority:** First priority remains investing in growth opportunities (especially generative AI); second priority is repaying debt taken on during negative FCF period (2021-2022)

## AWS Reacceleration -- CRITICAL FORWARD COMMENTARY

### Q1 2024 Performance & Trajectory
- **Q1 2024 Growth:** 17.2% YoY reported (~16% ex-leap year); significant reacceleration from 13% in Q4 2023
- **Annualized Run Rate:** $100B+ (milestone reached)
- **Operating Income:** $9.4B (+$4.3B YoY)
- **Operating Margin:** 37.6% (+800 bps QoQ)
- **AWS Growth Trajectory:** 40% (Q4'21) -> 37% (Q1'22) -> 33% (Q2'22) -> 28% (Q3'22) -> 20% (Q4'22) -> 16% (Q1'23) -> 12% (Q2'23) -> 12% (Q3'23) -> 13% (Q4'23) -> **17.2% (Q1'24)**

### Three Macro Trends Driving AWS Performance (CEO Jassy)
1. **Cost Optimization Cycle Ending:** "The lion's share of cost optimization is behind us"; customers learned from the experience but the major wave has passed; CFO: "we think the majority of the recent cycle is behind us, and we're likely closer to a steady state"
2. **Infrastructure Modernization Resuming:** Companies returning to pre-pandemic march of moving from on-premises to cloud; described as "low-hanging fruit" for customers; "very significant growth" in this space
3. **Generative AI Momentum:** "Multibillion-dollar revenue run rate" already in AI; driving both training and inference workloads; still "relatively early days"

### Market Opportunity
- **CEO:** "We're at $100 billion-plus annualized revenue run rate, yet 85% or more of the global IT spend remains on-premises"
- **CEO:** "This is before you even calculate gen AI, most of which will be created over the next 10 to 20 years from scratch and on the cloud"

### AWS Forward CapEx Signals
- **CFO:** "$14 billion of CapEx" in Q1 2024; "we expect that to be the low quarter for the year"
- **CFO:** "We are seeing strong demand signals from our customers and longer deals and larger commitments, many with generative AI components"
- **CEO:** "The more demand AWS has, the more we have to procure new data centers, power and hardware"
- **CFO:** "It will increase depreciation, definitely in that segment"
- **CEO/CFO consensus:** Confident in capital returns from CapEx spend based on 18 years of investing capital upfront and seeing revenue/OI/FCF follow

### AWS Margin Trajectory
- **Q1 2024 Operating Margin:** 37.6%
- **Margin Trajectory:** 35.3% (Q1'22) -> 29.0% (Q2'22) -> 26.3% (Q3'22) -> 24.3% (Q4'22) -> 24.0% (Q1'23) -> 24.2% (Q2'23) -> 30.3% (Q3'23) -> 29.6% (Q4'23) -> **37.6% (Q1'24)**
- **Note:** Includes ~$900M/quarter benefit from server useful life extension (5 to 6 years) starting Q1 2024
- **Forward View:** CFO warned margins will "fluctuate, driven in part by the level of investments"; higher CapEx/depreciation expected to pressure margins over time

## Generative AI / Large Language Models -- KEY STRATEGIC INITIATIVE

### AI Revenue Scale
- **Current Revenue Run Rate:** "Multibillion-dollar revenue run rate already" in AI (per CEO)
- **Status:** "Still relatively early days"
- **Comparison:** "Given our much larger infrastructure cloud computing base, at this growth rate, we see more absolute dollar growth again quarter-over-quarter in AWS than we can see elsewhere" -- CEO

### Three-Layer Framework (CEO Jassy)

#### Layer 1: Compute / Custom Chips
- **NVIDIA:** "Broadest selection of NVIDIA compute instances around"
- **Trainium:** AWS custom chip for ML training; high demand given "favorable price performance benefits relative to available alternatives"
- **Inferentia:** AWS custom chip for inference
- **Trainium2:** "Larger quantities of our latest generation Trainium2 is coming in the second half of 2024 and early 2025"
- **Anthropic Training on Trainium:** "They're training their future models on our custom silicon on Trainium" -- CEO
- **SageMaker Performance:** Perplexity.ai trains models 40% faster on SageMaker; Workday reduces inference latency by 80%; NatWest reduces AI time to value from 12-18 months to under 7 months

#### Layer 2: Foundational Models / Bedrock
- **Adoption:** "Tens of thousands of customers" already on Bedrock (including adidas, New York Stock Exchange, Pfizer, Ryanair, Toyota)
- **Model Selection:** Added Anthropic Claude 3, Meta Llama 3, Mistral, Cohere, and new Amazon Titan models in recent months
- **Key Features:** Model evaluation, RAG (retrieval augmented generation), guardrails, agents (multistep tasks), fine-tuning
- **Custom Model Import (NEW):** Allows importing models built in SageMaker or elsewhere into Bedrock; described as "a sneaky big launch" -- CEO; "satisfies a customer request we've heard frequently and that nobody has yet met"
- **SageMaker + Bedrock Integration:** Customers building models in SageMaker can now deploy through Bedrock's production features; described as "quite appealing"

#### Layer 3: Applications (Amazon Q)
- **Amazon Q General Availability:** Announced day of the earnings call; described as "the most capable generative AI-powered assistant for software development and leveraging company's internal data"
- **Software Development Capabilities:** Generates, tests, and debugs code; transforms code (e.g., old Java to new, .NET from Windows to Linux); Agents capability for autonomous feature implementation
- **Enterprise Data Capabilities:** Q Apps lets employees build apps from internal data using natural language; searches across wikis, Salesforce, S3, and other data stores
- **Performance Claims:** "Highest-known score and acceptance rate for code suggestions"; outperforms competitors on security vulnerability detection; leads on connecting multiple steps and automatic actions
- **Named Q Customers:** Brightcove, British Telecom, Datadog, GitLab, GoDaddy, National Australia Bank, NCS, Netsmart, Slam, Smartsheet, Sun Life, Tata Consultancy Services, Toyota, Wiz

### Training vs. Inference Dynamic (CEO Jassy)
- **Training:** Significant current spend; models being trained on AWS including Anthropic's future models on Trainium; largest foundational model builders building new models every 12-18 months
- **Inference (future driver):** "Once you get those models into production...you spend much more in inference than you do in training because you train only periodically, but you're spinning out predictions and inferences all the time"
- **Both on AWS:** "We see both training and inference being really big drivers on top of AWS"

### Security Differentiation
- **CEO:** "If you've been paying attention to what's been happening in the last year or so, you can see there are big differences between providers on these dimensions. AWS has a meaningful edge"
- **Impact:** Companies moving their AI focus to AWS due to security and operational performance

## Fulfillment & Cost Structure

### Regionalization & Inbound Network
- **Regionalization:** Continued benefits; "we're not done there" -- CEO; still refining and extracting value from regionalization work
- **U.S. Inbound Overhaul:** Revamping inbound fulfillment architecture for better inventory placement closer to customers; "iterative process throughout the year"
- **International Application:** Lessons from U.S. regionalization being applied to international operations
- **Seller Incentives:** Lowered outbound fees; added incentive for sellers to inbound to more cost-efficient locations; "seeing very optimistic signs"

### Units Per Box & Consolidation
- **CEO:** Seeing increase in units delivered per box; "important driver for reducing our cost"
- **Benefits:** Fewer boxes/deliveries, better customer experience, lower cost to serve, lower carbon impact
- **CEO:** "Still early with respect to how we can continue to optimize the number of units per box"

### Same-Day Facilities
- **CEO:** "Our same-day facilities are our least expensive facilities in the network"
- **Expansion:** "We still have a fraction of the number of those that we will have in the U.S." and internationally
- **Economics:** Faster speeds do not necessarily mean higher cost if infrastructure built with the right building blocks
- **Impact:** Will continue to change cost structure while increasing speed

### Robotics & Automation
- **CFO:** Investment focus areas include "adding robotics and automation" within fulfillment network
- **CFO:** "These improvement opportunities will take time. However, we have a solid plan in place and we like the path we're on"

### Pre-Pandemic Margin Comparison

- **CEO:** "Don't believe that we're at the end of what we can do in terms of improving our cost structure on the store side"

## Third-Party Logistics (Supply Chain by Amazon)

### Service Components (CEO Jassy)
1. **Cross-Border / Customs:** Service to get items through the border and customs
2. **Domestic Transportation:** Ship from customs to various facilities
3. **Upstream Warehousing:** Low-cost storage with automatic replenishment into fulfillment centers
4. **Outbound Shipping:** Ship to Amazon end customers or seller's own customers
5. **Buy with Prime:** Enables Prime customers to buy from third-party seller websites; "increases their conversion 25% versus what they would do on their own"
6. **Supply Chain by Amazon:** End-to-end abstraction layer on top of individual services

### Growth & CapEx
- **CEO:** "That collective set of businesses is growing very significantly. It's already what I would consider a reasonable sized business"
- **CapEx Impact:** "Not something that we anticipate being a giant capital expense driver for us" -- modest increase on top of existing stores infrastructure

# Amazon.com, Inc. Q4 2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** February 1, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 2023 (Full Year 2023)

---

## Q4 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated
- **Total Net Sales:** $170.0B (+14% YoY ex-FX; +13% YoY reported)
- **Net Sales vs. Guidance:** **Beat -- ~$3B above top end** of $160.0B-$167.0B guidance range (per CFO: "approximately $3 billion above the top end of our guidance range")
- **Operating Income:** $13.2B (+383% YoY; +$10.5B YoY vs. $2.7B in Q4 2022)
- **Operating Income vs. Guidance:** **Beat -- $2.2B above top end** of $7.0B-$11.0B guidance range (per CFO: "$2.2 billion above the high end of our guidance range")
- **Operating Margin:** ~7.8% (at $13.2B on $170.0B sales)
- **Highest Quarterly Operating Income Ever:** $13.2B (surpassed Q3 2023 record of $11.2B)
- **Trailing 12-Month FCF Adjusted for Equipment Finance Leases:** $35.5B (+$48.3B YoY; per CEO in opening remarks)

### Segment Results

#### North America
- **Net Sales:** $105.5B (+13% YoY; acceleration of 200 bps vs. Q3 2023)
- **Operating Income:** $6.5B (+$6.7B YoY improvement; vs. -$0.2B loss in Q4 2022)
- **Operating Margin:** 6.1% (+120 bps QoQ vs. 4.9% in Q3 2023)
- **Operating Margin Trajectory:** -2.3% (Q1'22) -> -0.8% (Q2'22) -> -0.5% (Q3'22) -> -0.3% (Q4'22) -> 1.2% (Q1'23) -> 3.9% (Q2'23) -> 4.9% (Q3'23) -> **6.1% (Q4'23)**
- **Consecutive Quarters of Improvement:** 7 quarters since Q1 2022 trough
- **Cumulative Improvement:** +840 bps over 7 consecutive quarters (per CFO: "800 basis points over these past 7 quarters" from Q1 2022 low)

#### International
- **Net Sales:** $40.2B (+13% YoY ex-FX; acceleration of 200 bps vs. Q3 2023)
- **Operating Loss:** -$419M (improved $1.8B YoY vs. -$2.2B loss in Q4 2022)
- **Operating Margin:** approximately -1.0% (vs. ~-5.5% in Q4 2022; continued improvement but still negative)
- **Commentary:** CFO attributed improvement to lower cost to serve (increased units per box, lower transportation rates, leverage across fixed costs); established countries (UK, Germany, Japan) drove improvement; emerging countries "on their own trajectory of journey to profitability"
- **Digital Content Spend:** Higher spend in Q4 on marketing and content for live sports (English Premier League, Champions League in Germany and Italy)

#### AWS
- **Net Sales:** $24.2B (+13% YoY; acceleration from 12% in Q3 2023)
- **Annualized Revenue Run Rate:** Approaching $100B (per CEO: "now approaching an annualized revenue run rate of $100 billion")
- **Incremental QoQ Revenue:** >$1.1B (per CEO: "more than $1.1 billion of incremental quarter-over-quarter revenue"; "on an FX-neutral basis, is more than any other cloud provider")
- **Operating Income:** $7.2B (+$2.0B YoY; vs. $5.2B in Q4 2022)
- **Operating Margin:** 29.6% (+500 bps YoY; effectively flat QoQ vs. 30.3% in Q3 2023)
- **AWS Growth Trajectory:** 40% (Q4'21) -> 37% (Q1'22) -> 33% (Q2'22) -> 28% (Q3'22) -> 20% (Q4'22) -> 16% (Q1'23) -> 12% (Q2'23) -> 12% (Q3'23) -> **13% (Q4'23; reaccelerated)**
- **Margin Drivers:** Head count reductions from earlier in 2023; slowdown in pace of hiring (per CFO)
- **Cost Optimization Status:** "Continued to attenuate" per CEO; "lion's share of cost optimization has happened"
- **Backlog:** $155.7B as of 12/31/2023 (+$45B YoY; +$20B QoQ; per Dave Fildes)

### Revenue Categories (from CEO/CFO commentary)
- **Advertising Services:** +26% YoY ex-FX (per CEO: "advertising growth remained strong, up 26% year-over-year, which is primarily driven by our sponsored ads")
- **Third-Party Seller Services:** +19% YoY ex-FX worldwide (per CFO)
- **Third-Party Seller Unit Mix:** 61% of total WW paid units (highest level ever; per CFO)

### Cost to Serve
- **First Decline Since 2018:** In 2023, Amazon reduced cost to serve on a per unit basis globally for the first time since 2018
- **U.S. Cost to Serve Reduction:** Down >$0.45 per unit vs. prior year (per CEO)
- **Drivers:** Regionalization, improved inventory placement, lower transportation distances, expansion of same-day facilities

### Delivery Speed
- **2023 Full Year:** More than 7 billion items arriving same or next day to Prime members globally
  - U.S.: >4 billion items same/next day
  - Europe: >2 billion items same/next day
- **Q4 Same-Day/Overnight (U.S.):** Increased >65% YoY
- **Record:** Fastest delivery speeds ever for Prime members in 2023
- **Impact:** Increased purchase frequency across all major geographies; strengthened demand for Everyday Essentials

### Holiday Season Performance
- **Prime Big Deal Days (October):** Kicked off holiday season for Prime members
- **Black Friday / Cyber Monday:** Largest event ever; open to all customers
- **Customer Savings in Q4:** Nearly $10 billion across millions of deals and coupons (~70% more than last year)

## Generative AI / Large Language Models

### Three-Layer Strategic Framework (CEO Jassy)

#### Layer 1: Compute / Custom Chips
- **Trainium2 (announced at re:Invent):** 4x faster training performance and 3x more memory capacity vs. first-generation Trainium; "enabling advantageous price performance versus alternatives"
- **Graviton:** 40% more price-performant than other x86 alternatives (custom generalized CPU chips)
- **NVIDIA Partnership:** "We offer the most expansive collection of compute instances with NVIDIA chips"
- **Named AI Chip Customers:** Anthropic, Airbnb, Hugging Face, Qualtrics, Rico, Snap

#### Layer 2: Large Language Models as a Service (Bedrock)
- **Adoption:** "Many thousands of customers using the service after just a few months" since launch
- **Status:** "Off to a very strong start"
- **New Capabilities:** Guardrails, knowledge bases (RAG), agents (multistep tasks), fine-tuning
- **Model Partners:** Anthropic, Cohere, Meta (Llama 2), Stability AI, Amazon Titan family

#### Layer 3: Applications
- **Amazon Q (launched at re:Invent):** Expert on AWS, writes/debugs/tests code, does translations (e.g., old Java to new), queries data repositories via 40+ connectors (Salesforce, S3, ServiceNow, Slack, Atlassian, Zendesk); "most capable work assistant"
- **Rufus (launched day of call):** AI shopping assistant trained on product catalog, community Q&A, customer reviews, and broader Web; enables discovery via natural language questions; "significant customer experience improvement for discovery"

### Gen AI Revenue Scale & Timing
- **Current State:** "In absolute numbers, it's a pretty big number. But in the scheme of a $100 billion annual revenue run rate business, it's still relatively small" (CEO)
- **Forward Outlook:** "We really believe we're going to drive tens of billions of dollars of revenue over the next several years" (CEO)
- **Growth:** "Encouraging how fast it's growing and our offering is really resonating with customers" (CEO)
- **CFO:** "Revenues are accelerating rapidly across all three layers"
- **Iteration Reality:** "There's meaningful iteration required in building a production gen AI application with the requisite enterprise quality at the cost and latency needed" (CEO)
- **Model Choice:** "Customers don't want only one model. They want different models for different types of applications and different-sized models for different applications" (CEO)

### Gen AI Across Amazon Consumer Businesses
- **Product Review Summaries:** Generative AI application for summarizing customer reviews
- **Apparel Fit Prediction:** Gen AI for predicting fit for different apparel items
- **Inventory Forecasting:** Gen AI in fulfillment centers for demand forecasting
- **Advertising:** Ad copy generation from images (and vice versa)
- **Alexa:** Building "very expansive large language model" to make Alexa more productive
- **Every Business Unit:** "Every business that we have has multiple generative AI applications that they're building" (CEO)
- **CEO Assessment:** "Gen AI is and will continue to be an area of pervasive focus and investment across Amazon primarily because there are a few initiatives, if any, that give us the chance to reinvent so many of our customer experiences and processes, and we believe it will ultimately drive tens of billions of dollars of revenue for Amazon over the next several years"

### Developer Productivity
- **Amazon Q Coding Companion:** "If you can get 30%, 40% better productivity for your developers, which, in many cases, for companies is their most scarce resource, it's a game changer" (CEO)
- **Differentiation vs. Other Coding Companions:** Not just code generation -- also debugging, testing, transformations, troubleshooting, data querying across 40+ connectors

## Fulfillment Regionalization & Cost to Serve

### 2023 Results
- **First Per-Unit Cost to Serve Decline Since 2018:** Confirmed for full year 2023 globally
- **U.S. Savings:** Cost to serve down >$0.45 per unit vs. prior year
- **Drivers:** Regionalization (storing items closer to customers), same-day facility expansion, lower transportation distances

### 2024 Outlook
- **CEO:** "We're not done lowering our cost to serve"
- **Key Focus Areas:** "Our inbound fulfillment architecture and resulting inventory placement are areas of focus in 2024, and we have optimism there's more upside for us"
- **CEO:** "We've challenged every closely held belief in our fulfillment network and reevaluated every part of it and found several areas where we believe we can lower costs while also delivering faster for customers"
- **No Ceiling:** "I don't believe that we believe that 2018 is the north star in cost to serve. I think we believe we can keep evolving it and being better than that" (CEO)

### Broader Cost Controls (CFO)
- **Fixed Cost Controls:** "We've gotten a lot better at fixed cost controls as we scale. And I think you're seeing that as part of our ability to lower cost per serve. It's not only in operations, it's actually throughout the company"
- **Inflationary Relief:** "Seeing a reduction in some of the inflationary factors that hit us especially hard in 2021 and 2022, things like transportation services, fuel and others. So not totally out of the woods there but coming down"

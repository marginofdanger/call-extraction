## Cost Optimization / Restructuring Initiatives

### Layoffs & Role Eliminations
- **18,000+ roles eliminated** (announced in Q4, largely executed in January 2023)
- **Primarily impacting:** Stores business, Devices business, Human Resources teams
- **Severance cost:** $640M (accrued in Q4 2022)
- **Timing:** Most terminations occurred in January; Q4 accrual covers post-separation severance/benefits; Q1 2023 will carry ~3 weeks salary for those terminated in January
- Hardest decision the leadership team has been part of (per CEO Jassy)

### Program Closures & Slowdowns
- **Closed:** Amazon 4-Star bookstores, Fabric.com, Amazon Care, Amazon Glo, Amazon Explore
- **Slowed:** Physical store expansion (Amazon Fresh) -- paused until a differentiated, economically viable format is found; optimistic about finding right equation in 2023
- **Slowed:** Some devices (went slower on certain device investments)
- **Delayed/Mothballed:** Some fulfillment center builds in early 2022 to align capacity with demand

### Physical Store Impairments
- $720M impairment primarily related to Amazon Fresh and Amazon Go stores
- Continuously refining store formats to find ones that resonate with customers
- Exiting certain stores with low growth potential
- Impairment covers capitalized costs and associated values of leased buildings

### Cost to Serve Reduction
- #1 priority per CEO Jassy: reducing cost to serve in operations network
- Fulfillment center footprint built over 25 years was doubled in just a couple of years
- Built last-mile transportation network roughly the size of UPS in a couple of years
- Network now has many more nodes to optimize; requires figuring out flows, utilization, and efficiency across all links
- Pleased with Q4 progress; work will extend into 2023

### Self-Insurance Reserve
- $1.3B increase in reserves for general product and automobile self-insurance liabilities
- Driven by business growth (especially fulfillment and transportation network expansion), industry-wide claim inflation
- Covers both asserted and unasserted claims

## AWS Strategic Commentary

### Customer Cost Optimization
- Enterprise customers of all sizes evaluating ways to optimize cloud spending
- Optimization efforts started mid-Q3 2022 and continued through Q4; expected to persist for at least the next couple of quarters
- Key benefit of cloud: ability to handle demand swings and optimize costs quickly during uncertainty
- Helping customers save money is in Amazon's DNA; focused on long-term relationships, not quarterly optimization
- Elasticity of cloud: can scale up for demand surges or give back capacity when not needed -- something not possible on-premises

### Demand by Industry (Q4 detail)
- **Financial services:** Lower demand; mortgage companies seeing lower compute as mortgage volumes declined
- **Crypto:** Lower trading volumes reducing compute needs
- **Advertising-linked:** Less analytics and compute on advertising spend as ad budgets tightened
- **Broad-based:** Most enterprises cautious about infrastructure expense; not limited to specific sectors

### Customer Actions to Reduce AWS Bills
- Switch to lower-cost products (e.g., Graviton instances)
- Run calculations/analytics less frequently (e.g., 2 weeks of data instead of 90 days)
- Change storage tiers for data
- Defer workloads where possible
- Not necessarily best long-term decisions, but common in uncertain economic environments

### New Customer Pipeline
- Robust and healthy despite optimization headwinds
- New deals, new workloads continuing to come to cloud
- Companies during periods of discontinuity often strategically reassess and plan cloud migrations
- Absolute dollar growth YoY still significantly more than any competitor in the space (per management's best estimation)

### Long-Term Positioning
- 90-95% of global IT spend remains on-premises; massive runway for cloud migration
- AWS has significantly more capability, stronger security, larger partner ecosystem
- Historical precedent: 2020 downturn led to resurgence and acceleration of cloud journeys post-recovery
- Question is whether same pattern will repeat with current optimization cycle

### AWS Margin Drivers
- **Q4 operating margin:** 24.3% (vs. 29.8% in Q4 2021; vs. 26.3% in Q3 2022)
- Sequential decline driven by continued cost pressures (energy, SBC, wage inflation) and slower revenue growth
- FY 2022 operating margin: 28.5% (vs. 29.8% in FY 2021)

## Grocery Strategy

### Overview (CEO Jassy commentary)
- Grocery is a "really important and strategic area" for Amazon
- Very large market segment with high purchase frequency
- Believes grocery will be omnichannel over time: online delivery, pickup, in-store, and hybrid models

### Three Components
1. **Online Grocery (Amazon.com):** Very large business in packaged food, paper products, canned goods, pet supplies, health/personal care, consumables; continuing to grow at rapid clip; lacks significant market share in perishables
2. **Whole Foods:** Significant-sized business; continuing to grow; CEO "really likes the progress on profitability in the last year"; premium product but good business in grocery space
3. **Amazon Fresh (physical stores):** A few dozen stores; doing "a fair bit of experimentation" to find differentiated format with attractive economics; paused expansion until equation is right; optimistic about finding right format in 2023; encouraging signs

## International E-Commerce Strategy

### Established Markets (CEO Jassy commentary)
- **Pre-pandemic CAGR (2019-2021):** UK >30%, Germany 26%, Japan 21% (all net of FX)
- Meaningful market share gains during pandemic period; have not given back that growth
- Now large enough to be impacted by macro headwinds (European inflation, Ukraine war energy impact)

### Emerging Markets
- India, Brazil, Middle East, Africa, Turkey, Mexico, Australia
- All on right trajectory following patterns similar to earlier North America and established international market development
- Fixed investment required when entering new geographies; driving revenue to cover that investment
- Believes Amazon will have a "large profitable international e-commerce business"

## Investment Priorities (CEO Jassy)

### Key Strategic Investments Continuing
- Streaming entertainment and devices
- **Kuiper (low Earth orbit satellite):** Remains a priority investment
- **Health care:** Continued investment (One Medical acquisition context)
- Focus on investments that could be "the fourth pillar" for Amazon

### Investment Framework (Four Questions)
1. If successful, could it be big enough to move the needle at Amazon?
2. Is the opportunity being well served today?
3. Does Amazon have a differentiated approach?
4. Does Amazon have (or can quickly acquire) the necessary competencies?

### Cost vs. Investment Balance
- Streamlining costs across all businesses (existing and new investments)
- Thorough review of all investments and spending levels
- Maintain ability to invest in key long-term strategic investments
- Not every investment will succeed, but "it only takes one or two of them becoming the fourth pillar" to meaningfully change the company

19 Key Takeaways

1
The greatest margin recovery in large-cap tech. Consolidated operating margin went from ~2% (2022, with negative N.A. and International) to 11–12% (2025). N.A. margin improved 1,130 bps (−2.3% → 9.0%), International improved 1,300 bps (−8.9% → 4.1%), and OI guidance midpoints expanded 23x ($1.0B → $23.5B).
2
AWS traced a textbook deceleration-trough-reacceleration arc. Growth fell from +37% (Q1 ’22) to a +12% trough (Q2–Q3 ’23) driven by customer optimization, then reaccelerated to +24% (Q4 ’25) as AI demand exploded. Revenue nearly doubled from $18.4B to $35.6B quarterly.
3
AI went from undiscussed to >$10B ARR in 3 years. Generative AI was not mentioned in 2022 earnings calls. By Q4 ’25, Graviton + Trainium combined exceeded $10B ARR with triple-digit growth, Bedrock was multibillion ARR, and Amazon was spending $200B in 2026 CapEx predominantly for AI infrastructure.
4
CapEx trajectory is unprecedented in corporate history. From $48B (FY ’23, efficiency year) to $125B (FY ’25) to ~$200B guided (FY ’26) — a 4x increase in 3 years. Power capacity is the binding constraint: 3.9 GW added in 2025, targeting ~7.8 GW by 2027.
5
Fulfillment regionalization was transformational. The 2023 transition from 1 national network to 8 (now 10) regions delivered 3 consecutive years of per-unit cost-to-serve reduction while accelerating delivery speed to 8B+ same/next-day items. This flywheel — faster + cheaper simultaneously — powers the N.A. margin story.
6
Advertising became an $85B run-rate business. From $32B (Q1 ’22) to $85B (Q4 ’25), advertising nearly tripled while maintaining 18–30% growth. The 2024 launch of Prime Video ads (315M viewers, 16 countries) added a new high-margin revenue surface that reaccelerated growth from +18% to +24%.
7
AWS backlog signals multi-year demand visibility. Backlog nearly tripled from $88.9B to $244B, with growth reaccelerating to +40% YoY (Q4 ’25). The weighted average remaining life of 4.1 years provides exceptional revenue visibility. Backlog growth outpacing revenue growth (+40% vs. +24%) is bullish for sustained acceleration.
8
Amazon systematically sandbagged guidance. Revenue beat the top end of guidance in 14 of 16 quarters. OI beat the top end in 13 of 16 quarters, often by $2B–$3B. This consistent pattern makes the guided ranges effectively a conservative floor, not a midpoint estimate.
9
FCF peaked then deliberately compressed. TTM FCF swung from −$19.7B (Q3 ’22) to +$51.4B (Q2 ’24), then declined to $11.2B (Q4 ’25) as CapEx exploded. This is not deterioration — OCF hit a record $139.5B. Amazon is choosing to reinvest cash flow at historically aggressive levels.
10
Custom silicon is Amazon’s hidden moat. Trainium3 just started shipping with nearly all supply committed by mid-2026. Trainium4 is in development. The Anthropic partnership (Project Rainier: 500K–1M chips) validates the technology. Over 90% of AWS’s top 1,000 customers use Graviton.
11
Grocery crossed the credibility threshold. From “wish we were further along” (Q1 ’23) to >$150B gross sales and “go-to grocer for 150M+ Americans” (Q4 ’25). The plan to open 100+ new Whole Foods stores signals a durable physical retail commitment. Perishable shoppers shop 2x+ more often, creating a powerful retention flywheel.
12
Kuiper is approaching revenue inflection. From a line item (2022) to 180 satellites in orbit with commercial launch expected 2026. The ~$1B annual cost increase and 50+ launches planned in 2026–2027 show serious commitment. The Leo Ultra terminal (1 Gbps / 400 Mbps) targeting 400M–500M underserved households could become Amazon’s next platform-scale business.

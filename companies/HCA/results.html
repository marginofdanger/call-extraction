6 Key Takeaways

1
Systematic guide-and-raise: In all 3 completed fiscal years, HCA set initial guidance conservatively and raised it at least once during the year. The average initial EPS midpoint beat of +10.9% confirms management builds in substantial buffer.
2
Revenue growth re-acceleration: Revenue CAGR of 7.9% (FY 2022–FY 2025) was driven by a triple tailwind: post-COVID volume rebound, healthcare exchange enrollment surge, and mid-single-digit managed care rate escalators.
3
EPS compounding engine: EPS grew at a 14.0% CAGR — nearly double the revenue growth rate. The gap was driven by EBITDA margin expansion (from ~20.0% to ~20.6%), contract labor normalization, and ~5.5% annual share count reduction via buybacks.
4
EBITDA conservatism strongest of all metrics: Initial EBITDA guidance averaged +5.4% below actual. This was consistently the widest initial guidance miss, as management could not pre-quantify supplemental payment program outcomes at the start of each year.
5
Final guidance is highly accurate: Once raised, final guidance narrowed to within 1–2% of actuals across all metrics. The average absolute deviation between final midpoint and actual was 0.6% for revenue, 1.3% for EBITDA, and 2.8% for EPS.
6
FY 2023 was the standout year: The only year where actuals exceeded the final high end of every guided metric. This coincided with the Q1 2023 volume surge (+7.5% same-facility equivalent admissions), the strongest quarter in the 16-quarter dataset.
7
Cash flow trajectory is impressive: CFO nearly doubled from $8.5B to $12.6B over 3 years. Free cash flow of $7.7B in FY 2025 means HCA generates its entire CapEx budget in about 7 months of operating cash flow.
8
Hurricane risk is real but manageable: FY 2024 was the only year where all metrics came in at or below the final midpoint, directly attributable to ~$200M in Q4 hurricane impact. Management guided to “lower half” after Q3 and actuals confirmed.
9
Supplemental payments are the wild card: The single largest driver of EBITDA guidance uncertainty across all 3 years. FY 2024 saw a $400M+ swing from expected headwind to actual tailwind. FY 2026 guidance embeds $250M–$450M headwind as TN reverts and TX ATLIS pauses.
10
FY 2026 is the most uncertain year: The $3.5B revenue guidance range (widest ever) and $0.9B EBITDA range reflect the compounding uncertainty of EPTC expiration, Medicaid administrative reforms, and supplemental payment resets. If HCA’s guide-and-raise pattern holds, the initial $30.30 EPS midpoint may prove conservative.

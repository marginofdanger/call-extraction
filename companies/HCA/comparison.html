20 Key Takeaways

1
Revenue guidance midpoint grew 29% in 4 years. From $60.5B (FY 2022) to $78.25B (FY 2026 initial), HCA compounded revenue growth at ~7% annually, consistently above the 4–6% long-term target.
2
EPS nearly doubled from $17 to $30+ through EBITDA growth and buybacks. Diluted EPS midpoint grew from $17.00 (FY 2022) to $30.30 (FY 2026 initial). Approximately half came from EBITDA growth, half from the 24% reduction in share count via $26B+ in repurchases.
3
Contract labor normalization was the defining margin story. From ~11.6% of nursing hours at the Q1 2022 peak to 4.2% of SWB by late 2025, contract labor normalization delivered hundreds of millions in annual savings. With levels near pre-pandemic floors, this tailwind has largely played out.
4
Healthcare exchanges grew from immaterial to 10% of revenue. Exchange volume surged from single-digit percentages to 8% of equivalent admissions and ~10% of revenue by FY 2025, driven by Medicaid redetermination migration and enhanced subsidies. This creates a significant vulnerability if EPTCs expire.
5
Supplemental payment programs became a swing factor. From a $100–$200M expected headwind to a $400M actual tailwind (FY 2024), and +$420M in FY 2025, supplemental programs have been HCA’s most volatile and consequential earnings variable. The FY 2026 reversal ($250–$450M headwind) is the largest identified drag.
6
The guide-and-raise pattern held across every complete fiscal year. HCA raised intra-year EBITDA guidance in FY 2023, FY 2024, and FY 2025 — each time starting conservative and ending above initial estimates. The only mid-year cut was Valesco-driven (Q3 2023 top end lowered by $200M).
7
Managed care pricing reset from 2.5–4% to mid-single-digits proved durable. First flagged on the Q2 2022 call, elevated rate escalators persisted through 3+ years of contracting cycles, counterbalancing the mix shift toward lower-reimbursement exchange patients.
8
19 consecutive quarters of volume growth through FY 2025. From Q1 2022 through Q4 2025, HCA posted positive same-facility volume growth every quarter, even as growth decelerated from +7.5% (Q1 2023) to +2.4% (FY 2025 full year).
9
The 2024 hurricanes cost $250M but triggered no operational crisis. Hurricanes Helene and Milton caused $250M in damage ($0.73/share), including the closure of Largo Hospital for 3 months. HCA recovered ~$125M by FY 2025, with ~$150M in residual headwinds carrying into 2026.
10
FY 2026 is HCA’s most challenging year-over-year setup. The combination of EPTC expiration ($600–$900M headwind), supplemental payment decline ($250–$450M), and non-repeat of 2025 one-time items creates a formidable earnings bridge. Management is countering with $400M in resiliency savings.
11
Operating cash flow doubled from $8.5B to $12.6B in 3 years. Cash flow from operations grew at a ~14% CAGR from FY 2022 to FY 2025, outpacing EBITDA growth due to improved working capital management and revenue cycle efficiency. This funded $5B annual CapEx and $10B in FY 2025 buybacks simultaneously.
12
The resiliency program accelerated from efficiency initiative to strategic imperative. Originally a $600–$800M target over 5 years (announced late 2023), the program was dramatically accelerated in response to EPTC expiration risk. The $400M in incremental 2026 savings alone approaches the original multi-year target, leveraging benchmarking, AI/automation, and shared service expansion.

What Changed vs Q3 2025

  • New FY 2026 guidance introduced: Revenue $76.5B–$80.0B, EBITDA $15.55B–$16.45B, EPS $29.10–$31.50
  • New EPTC/exchange headwind quantified: $600M–$900M EBITDA impact in 2026
  • New Resiliency savings target: ~$400M incremental in 2026
  • Changed FY 2025 supplemental payments: +$420M vs 2024 (prior expectation: ~$300M)
  • Changed FY 2026 CapEx raised to $5.0B–$5.5B (above prior expectations)
  • Raised Share repurchase authorization: new $10B (Board approved Jan ’26)
  • Raised Quarterly dividend: $0.72 → $0.78 per share

Full Year 2026 Guidance

  • Revenue: $76.5 billion to $80.0 billion
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $15.55 billion to $16.45 billion
  • Net income attributable to HCA Healthcare: $6.5 billion to $7.0 billion
  • Diluted EPS: $29.10 to $31.50
  • Cash flow from operations: $12 billion to $13 billion
  • Capital expenditures: $5.0 billion to $5.5 billion (RAISED from prior expectations; ~$7B approved pipeline coming online ’26/’27/early ’28)
  • Equivalent admissions growth: 2% to 3% (consistent with long-term range)
  • Adjusted EBITDA margins: expected slightly above 20%, consistent with 2025; “pretty stable margins in ’26 versus ’25”

Bad Debt / Uncompensated Care (2026 Outlook)

  • Uninsured volume: expected increase as exchange enrollees lose coverage
  • Silver-to-bronze metal tier shift: potential for slightly more patient-due balances
  • Collection rate difference (bronze vs. silver): bronze enrollees have lower collection rate than silver
  • Management assessment: impact to patient balance collections from metal tier shifts appears "relatively immaterial"
  • Bigger impact: growth in uninsured population rather than uncollectibility of patient balances

Long-Term Framework

  • Long-term volume growth range: 2% to 3%
  • Long-term adjusted EBITDA growth range (referenced): 4% to 6%
  • Core growth (ex-headwinds) for 2026: analysts calculated ~3% to 12% range, ~7% at midpoint, above long-term 4%-6% guidance; management agreed with characterization
  • Margin sustainability: management comfortable that long-term plan will be maintained over time, citing demand, resiliency programs, and digital transformation
  • Resiliency program: described as multiyear, cultural, not episodic; expected to continue generating savings through back half of decade

Health Insurance Exchange (HICS) Headwind

  • Adverse impact on adjusted EBITDA from exchanges: $600 million to $900 million - Includes impact from: (1) administrative reforms enacted in 2025, (2) the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, (3) expiration of enhanced premium tax credits (EPTCs)
  • Contemplated decline in HICS volumes: 15% to 20% in 2026
  • Migration assumptions for lost HICS volumes: - ~15% to 20% of lost HICS volume moves to employee-sponsored insurance (carries a benefit) - Remaining migrates to uninsured (includes decline in utilization)
  • Utilization decline for those becoming uninsured: ~30% decline vs. utilization when they had exchange coverage
  • Exchange population characteristic: heavier ER utilization than traditional managed care/commercial population

Resiliency Program (Offset to Exchange Headwind)

  • Resiliency savings target (incremental '26 vs. '25): ~$400 million
  • Net exchange headwind after resiliency offset: $200 million to $500 million (i.e., $600M-$900M headwind minus $400M resiliency)
  • 4 key areas of resiliency focus: revenue integrity, variable cost efficiencies, fixed cost efficiencies, capacity management
  • 3 primary capabilities leveraged: (1) internal/external benchmarking + advanced analytics, (2) digital transformation with AI and automation, (3) expanding/leveraging shared service platforms
  • Described as a multiyear program, not episodic

Supplemental Payment Programs

  • Anticipated decline in supplemental payments net benefit: $250 million to $450 million
  • Three drivers, each ~1/3 of the decline:
  • 1. Tennessee program: reverting to 4 quarters of net benefit vs. 6 quarters in 2025
  • 2. Texas ATLIS program pause: one specific program paused (termination notice issued by outgoing commissioner; new commissioner reviewing; potential reinstatement)
  • 3. Virginia retro payment: one-time retro payment received in Q4 2025 that will not recur
  • Guidance does NOT include: any potential impact from additional approvals of grandfathered CMS applications (potential upside)

Hurricane-Impacted Markets

  • No significant growth to adjusted EBITDA expected from hurricane-impacted markets over prior year

Share Repurchases & Dividends

  • New share repurchase authorization: $10 billion (Board approved)
  • Anticipated completion of existing authorization: majority in 2026, subject to market conditions
  • Quarterly dividend increase: from $0.72 to $0.78 per share

Cost Assumptions

  • SWB / labor costs: mostly stable trends expected, consistent with last couple of years
  • Contract labor as % of SWB: ~4.2% run rate entering 2026
  • Physician cost pressures: expected high single digit growth in '26 vs. '25
  • Operating costs overall: mostly stable trends expected

Rural Health Transformation Program

  • Status: all 50 states allocated program funding under One Big Beautiful Bill Act
  • Timing/structure/size of state-level awards: not yet known
  • NOT reflected in 2026 guidance given remaining uncertainty
  • HCA rural exposure: ~15% of HCA hospitals are rural hospitals
  • Viewed as potential opportunity (upside to guidance)

Special Topics

Outpatient Strategy & Network Development

  • Current outpatient facilities: ~2,700
  • Target ratio: 18-20 outpatient facilities per hospital by end of decade
  • 2025 additions: ~100 outpatient business units added
  • Pipeline: better pipeline for outpatient acquisition opportunities than in a few years
  • M&A focus: primarily in-market outpatient transactions complementary to networks
  • Hospital M&A: no significant tax-exempt hospital opportunities observed that make financial sense

Technology & AI Agenda

  • EHR transition: accelerating transition from one system to another to standardize datasets across all hospitals
  • Three AI/digital domains:
  • 1. Administrative: revenue cycle, HR, IT, supply chain — implementing AI for efficiency; value expected as part of 2026 resiliency
  • 2. Operational: throughput, asset productivity, scheduling, staffing, OR management
  • 3. Clinical ("Holy Grail"): physician decision support using HCA's proprietary database; nursing support tools for shift change, safety, efficiency
  • Investment level: continued significant investment planned in technology and digital innovation

Payer Digital Integration

  • Series of engagements launched with major payers focused on digital integration, electronic data exchange, administrative simplification, dispute resolution
  • Results: nice reduction in net days in AR, especially in Q4; claims paid more timely; potential mitigation of denials and disputes

Weather / Winter Storm Impact

  • Nashville area: experiencing significant winter storm at time of call
  • Assessment: too early to quantify; management expects to have better sense as January closes
  • Historical precedent: managed through Texas storm a few years ago
  • Expectation: not viewed as significant in most markets beyond Nashville

Q1 2026 Considerations (from Q&A)

  • Supplemental payments: Virginia retro payment in Q4 2025 was a one-time item (tough Q1 comp consideration)
  • Exchange impact timing: watching enrollment figures from CMS; whether enrollees can sustain higher premiums; metal tier shifts; will know more by end of Q1/into Q2
  • Winter storm in Nashville: potential operational disruption in January

What Changed vs Q3 2024

  • New FY 2025 Revenue guidance introduced: $72.8B – $75.8B (Q3 had FY24 guidance: $69.75B – $71.75B)
  • New FY 2025 Adj. EBITDA guidance introduced: $14.3B – $15.1B (Q3 had FY24 guidance: $13.75B – $14.25B)
  • New FY 2025 Diluted EPS guidance introduced: $24.05 – $25.85 (Q3 had FY24 guidance: $21.60 – $22.80)
  • New FY 2025 CapEx guidance introduced: ~$5.0B – $5.2B (Q3 had FY24 guidance: ~$5.0B)
  • New Leverage ratio target lowered to 2.75x – 3.75x (from 3.0x – 4.0x)
  • New $10 billion new share repurchase authorization; dividend raised to $0.72/share (from $0.66)

Hurricane Impact Assumptions (2025 Guidance)

  • Largo Hospital: year-over-year increase in adjusted EBITDA expected from reopening
  • North Carolina division: year-over-year decline expected; lingering effects of Hurricane Helene throughout much of 2025
  • Net effect: Largo increase and North Carolina decline expected to offset each other -- NOT expected to produce a tailwind in 2025

Medicaid Supplemental Payment Programs (2025 Guidance)

  • Net effect range: flat to 2024 to a $250 million headwind
  • Headwind driver: onetime payments received in a few states in 2024
  • Tennessee program (new, calendar year 2025): included in the range; has NOT been approved yet (new administration will address) - "That is inclusive of a pretty wide range of estimation related to the new Tennessee program"

Revenue & Top Line

  • Revenue growth: ~6% year-over-year
  • Same-facility net revenue per equivalent admission: +2.9% over prior year, in line with expectations
  • ASC same-store net revenue growth: ~5% to 6% over prior year

Volume Metrics (Same-Facility Basis)

  • Inpatient admissions growth: +3% year-over-year
  • Equivalent admissions growth: +3% year-over-year
  • Emergency room visits growth: +2.4% year-over-year
  • Inpatient surgeries growth: +2.8% year-over-year
  • Outpatient surgery cases: -1.3% (driven by Medicaid declines; revenue growth still solid due to strong payer/service mix)
  • ASC case volumes: -1% in the quarter
  • Outpatient surgery -- Medicaid volumes: -10%
  • Outpatient surgery -- Commercial and exchange volumes: +slightly over 1%
  • Outpatient surgery -- Self-pay: down

Volume Context & Headwinds

  • Depressed respiratory season impact on admissions: ~1-point drag on same-facility admission growth vs. prior year
  • Depressed respiratory season impact on ER visits: ~2-point drag on same-facility ER visit growth vs. prior year
  • Hurricane impact on volume: estimated 20 to 40 basis points of drag on volume in the quarter
  • Comparison note: Q4 2023 was a strong quarter, creating a tougher year-over-year comparison

Payer Mix

  • Same-facility managed care admissions: +9.2% vs. prior year quarter
  • Payer mix: remained strong in Q4 2024, consistent with trends all year

Profitability

  • Adjusted EBITDA growth: +2.6% vs. prior year quarter (reflects hurricane impact)
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin: declined 60 basis points vs. prior year quarter - Hurricane impact on EBITDA margin: 100-basis point unfavorable impact (Largo Hospital in Tampa + North Carolina division)
  • Diluted EPS (as adjusted) growth: +5.4% vs. prior year quarter

Hurricane Impact (Q4 2024)

  • Estimated financial impact (increased costs + lost revenue): ~$200 million or ~$0.60 per diluted share, in line with expectations
  • Insurance recoveries: not included in estimates; may be received in future
  • Other operating expenses increase: driven by repair costs for Largo Hospital
  • Supply cost increase: hurricanes drove approximately half of the supply increase

Medicaid Supplemental Payment Programs (Q4 2024)

  • Q4 2024 incremental net benefit: lowest of the 4 quarters in the year
  • Tennessee program: partial-year approval covering July 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024; NOT recognized in Q4 2024 (will be a 2025 event)

Contract Labor / Labor

  • Contract labor: -8% year-over-year in the quarter
  • Contract labor as % of SWB: 4.5% to 4.6% in the quarter
  • Wage inflation: stable in Q4

Capital Allocation (Q4 2024)

  • Cash flow from operations: $2.6 billion in the quarter
  • Capital expenditures: $1.29 billion in the quarter
  • Share repurchases: $1.7 billion in the quarter
  • Dividends paid: $165 million in the quarter

Revenue & Top Line

  • Revenue (top line) growth: +8.7% year-over-year
  • Net revenue per equivalent admission growth: +3.2% year-over-year
  • Equivalent admissions growth: +4.5% year-over-year

Profitability

  • Adjusted EBITDA growth: +9% over prior year
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin: +10 basis points improvement year-over-year
  • Diluted EPS (as adjusted) growth: +15.5% over prior year

Volume Context

  • Equivalent admissions growth (through September YTD): ~5%
  • Healthcare exchange volume growth (FY 2024): +44% to 45%
  • Healthcare exchange enrollment growth (FY 2024): ~30%
  • Healthcare exchanges as % of equivalent admissions (FY 2024): 7.5%
  • Healthcare exchanges as % of revenues (FY 2024): ~9%
  • Medicare Advantage Two-Midnight Rule impact: ~50 basis points of overall admission growth for FY 2024, consistent across all 4 quarters

Balance Sheet

  • Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage: at the low end of stated guidance range
  • Leverage ratio target (PRIOR): 3.0x to 4.0x
  • Leverage ratio target (NEW -- LOWERED): 2.75x to 3.75x - Management rationale: "this new range fits our profile and our anticipated use of leverage as a company, assuming no significant transactions or extraordinary events"

Facilities

  • Ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs): 124 (per Q&A; also referenced as ~125)
  • GI centers (not included in ASC count): ~20 to 25
  • Outpatient facilities per hospital (average): ~14 (including ASCs, clinics, urgent care, etc.)

Managed Care Contracting

  • Contracted for 2025: 80%
  • Contracted for 2026: 60%
  • Contracted for 2027: 20%
  • Pricing: still on top of range estimates / targets

Denials & Underpayments

  • Denials growth: not a material impact for the company in Q4 or FY 2024
  • Medicare Advantage observation rate vs. traditional Medicare: MA observation as a % of total is ~20% higher than traditional Medicare

Revenue

  • Revenue range: $72.8 billion to $75.8 billion

Net Income

  • Net income attributable to HCA Healthcare: $5.85 billion to $6.29 billion

Adjusted EBITDA

  • Adjusted EBITDA range: $14.3 billion to $15.1 billion

Earnings Per Share

  • Diluted EPS range: $24.05 to $25.85

Capital Expenditures

  • CapEx range: ~$5.0 billion to $5.2 billion
  • Maintenance vs. growth CapEx split: ~50/50 (per management confirmation in Q&A)

Cash Flow

  • Cash flow from operations range: $10.75 billion to $11.25 billion

Volume Assumptions

  • Equivalent admissions growth: +3% to 4%
  • Net revenue per equivalent admission growth: +2% to 3%
  • Context: 3% to 4% growth is still above long-term guide of 2% to 3%

Volume Bridge (FY 2024 to FY 2025)

  • Healthcare exchange enrollment growth in HCA states (2025): ~13% to 15% (vs. ~30% in 2024)
  • Exchange volume growth: expected to be less than the 44%-45% seen in 2024
  • Medicare Advantage Two-Midnight Rule admission benefit: not expected to repeat in 2025
  • Medicaid redetermination: was a headwind in 2024; expected to flatten in 2025

Margins

  • Full year margins: expected to be consistent with 2024

Professional Fees / Physician Costs

  • Professional fees as % of other operating expenses: ~24%
  • 2025 outlook: cost pressures related to physician costs expected to moderate further but still higher than normal inflationary cost trends
  • Radiology: pressure seen in 2024, expected to continue into 2025, but not expected to be a material impact (radiology is a smaller portion of hospital-based physician subsidies)
  • ER and hospital medicine physician groups: "moved more fully through the business challenges" especially with Valesco acquisition/integration

Labor Assumptions

  • Wage inflation outlook: stable and rational heading into 2025
  • Margin guidance contemplates: steady operating environment including stable overall wage inflation

Supply Chain / Tariffs

  • Supply spend contracted with firm pricing for 2025: ~70%
  • Tariff impact: insufficient detail to produce estimates; monitoring closely
  • HealthTrust Purchasing: working on tariff mitigation strategies (fixed price contracting, supply chain mapping, risk assessment, sourcing)

Shareholder Returns

  • New share repurchase authorization: $10 billion (Board-authorized) - "We anticipate completing a significant portion in 2025, subject to market conditions and other factors"
  • Quarterly dividend increase: from $0.66 to $0.72 per share

Healthcare Exchanges / ACA Subsidies

  • Exchange enrollment (2025): ~25 million nationally, up 12% to 15% over 2024
  • Enhanced subsidies expiration: due to expire at end of 2026
  • HCA position: actively working with Trump administration and coalition partners to find pathway forward; too early to call outcome
  • Exchanges as % of HCA equivalent admissions (2024): 7.5%
  • Exchanges as % of HCA revenues (2024): ~9%

Site-Neutral Payments

  • HCA position: against Medicare hospital outpatient reimbursement cuts; argues 24/7 hospital operations should not be paid same as 8-to-4 ASC operations
  • Sizing potential impact: have not seen a bill with enough detail to estimate - Low-impact end: proposals around hospital-based physician clinic visits and outpatient infusion -- HCA would NOT be materially impacted given how they structure physician clinics - Higher-impact end: proposals around certain outpatient surgical procedures -- would have "a bit more notable impact to HCA"

California Wildfires

  • Impact on HCA facilities: no impact at Southern California hospitals
  • Ventura County: one facility (Thousand Oaks area) was on notice during the Kenneth Fire; Palisades Fire did not reach into Ventura County
  • Riverside, California: some smoke issues in the community, no issue on the facility

M&A Activity

  • Pipeline: primary growth through organic capital spending (network development)
  • Pending acquisition: new hospital in Manchester, New Hampshire, expected to close Q1 2025
  • Outlook: not anticipating any material inorganic transactions

Network Development

  • More facilities at end of 2024 than prior year (via capital investment + incremental acquisitions)
  • Market share data: "really encouraging" -- growing market share
  • Key operational initiatives for 2025: - Emergency room revitalization / optimization program - Operating room optimization initiative (turnaround time, staffing, OR efficiency) - Case management / inpatient throughput (post-acute care placement & discharge process improvements, especially with Medicare Advantage payers) - Length of stay management: solid performance in 2024, forecasting another good year in 2025 - Workforce development: adding Galen colleges of nursing, increasing enrollment, academic medicine partnerships - AI initiatives: early signs of success in administrative, operational, and clinical applications - Employee engagement at all-time high, especially in nursing; reduced turnover

Immigration / Labor Policy

  • HCA does not hire undocumented workers
  • Potential impact: would be indirect, through supply and demand for labor in certain skill mixes
  • No special insight or quantification provided

Key Flags & Observations

  • 1. Leverage ratio LOWERED: From 3.0x-4.0x to 2.75x-3.75x — signals confidence in balance sheet and intent to maintain lower leverage
  • 2. Medicaid supplemental net benefit significantly exceeded expectations in 2024: $400M actual vs. $100M-$200M prior expectation — driven by onetime items NOT expected to recur
  • 3. 2025 supplemental payment headwind: flat to -$250M vs. 2024 — partially offsets the 2024 outperformance
  • 4. 2025 guidance aligns with preliminary outlook provided on prior (Q3) call — no surprise raise or cut
  • 5. Volume deceleration expected: 3%-4% EA growth in 2025 vs. 4.5% in 2024; driven by slower exchange enrollment growth, non-repeat of Two-Midnight Rule benefit, and Medicaid redetermination stabilization
  • 6. Hurricane headwinds expected to be neutral in 2025 (Largo recovery offset by NC lingering weakness)
  • 7. $10 billion new buyback authorization — significant shareholder return capacity
  • 8. Dividend raised ~9% ($0.66 to $0.72 per quarter)
  • 9. Tennessee Medicaid supplemental program: 2024 partial-year approved but not recognized in Q4 2024; new 2025 calendar-year program not yet approved
  • 10. Tariff exposure limited: 70% of supply spend contracted at firm pricing; monitoring but cannot size impact yet

Hurricane Impact (Full Year 2024)

  • Lost revenue + additional expenses from hurricanes: ~$250 million or $0.73 per diluted share (adversely impacted FY 2024)
  • Prior year comparison item: $145 million payer settlement in FY 2023

Medicaid Supplemental Payment Programs (Full Year 2024)

  • Full year incremental net benefit: ~$400 million
  • Prior guidance/expectation: $100 million to $200 million incremental net benefit
  • Variance driver: onetime payments and higher-than-expected program payments in a few states
  • Q2 2024 (highest quarter): $125 million incremental net benefit
  • Q4 2024 (lowest quarter): lowest of the 4 quarters (not specifically quantified)
  • Q1 and Q3 2024: "a little bit higher" than Q2's $125 million (per management commentary)

Capital Allocation (Full Year 2024)

  • Cash flow from operations: $10.5 billion (+11% over prior year)
  • Capital expenditures: $4.9 billion
  • Share repurchases: $6 billion
  • Dividends paid: $690 million

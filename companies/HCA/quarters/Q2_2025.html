Full Year 2025 Guidance (UPDATED / RAISED)

Volume Guidance

  • Equivalent admissions growth (full year 2025): 2% to 3%
  • Status: LOWERED from original 3% to 4% guidance
  • Drivers of volume shortfall vs. original guidance: approximately half from Medicaid (down 1.2% YTD vs. expected flat) and self-pay (up only 1.5% vs. expected to grow at overall rate); approximately half from Medicare (growing 3% but slightly below original expectation)

Long-Term Guidance Framework

  • Revenue growth (long-term): 4% to 6%
  • EBITDA growth (long-term): 4% to 6%
  • EBITDA margin (historical pattern): approximately 19% to 20%
  • Management declined to reaffirm or update margin targets pending EPTC outcome clarity

Headline Results

  • Diluted EPS (as adjusted): $6.84 (up 24% YoY)
  • Revenue growth: 6.4% (slightly above the top end of the long-term 4%-6% guidance range)
  • Adjusted EBITDA growth: 8.4% over prior year quarter
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin: improved 30 basis points vs. prior year quarter
  • Cash flow from operations: $4.2 billion in the quarter

Volume Metrics (Same-Facility)

  • Equivalent admissions growth (Q2): 1.7%
  • Equivalent admissions growth (YTD through June): 2.3%
  • Managed care + HIC equivalent admissions (YTD through June): up 4% (in line with expectations)
  • Medicare equivalent admissions (YTD through June): up 3% (slightly below expectation)
  • Medicaid equivalent admissions (YTD through June): down 1.2% (below expectation; originally expected flat to slight growth)
  • Self-pay/charity volumes (YTD through June): up 1.5% (below expectation; originally expected to grow at overall volume growth rate)
  • Self-pay volumes in 2024 vs. 2023: up almost 7% (prior year context)
  • Divisions with admission growth: 14 out of 15 domestic divisions grew admissions; 14 out of 15 grew adjusted admissions
  • Consecutive quarters of volume growth: 16 quarters
  • Inpatient occupancy (YTD): north of 70%, approximately 73%-74%

Service Line Volumes (Q2)

  • Cardiac procedure volume: up 5%
  • Obstetrics volumes: up 3%
  • Neonatal volumes: up 13%
  • Behavioral health admissions: down (company deliberately shrunk supply in certain facilities)
  • Outpatient surgery cases (pure case count): down 0.6% (better than recent trends; driven almost entirely by Medicaid, self-pay, and lower acuity cases)
  • Outpatient surgery revenue growth: approximately 7.5%-8%
  • Total outpatient revenue growth: almost 8%
  • Inpatient surgeries (same-facility): relatively flat (Medicaid payer mix driven)

Payer Mix & Exchange Data

  • Health care exchange equivalent admissions (YTD through June): up 15.8% (a little better than original expectation)
  • Commercial managed care equivalent admissions ex-exchanges (YTD through June): up just short of 1% (maybe a little below original guidance)
  • Exchange equivalent admissions as % of total: approximately 8%
  • Exchange net revenues as % of total: just over 10%
  • Medicare Advantage as % of total Medicare admissions: 58%
  • Exchange equivalent admissions sequential growth Q1 to Q2 2025: up about 3%
  • Exchange equivalent admissions sequential growth Q1 to Q2 2024: up 14% (prior year context)
  • Exchange volume growth in 2024: over 40% (prior year context)

Labor & Cost Metrics

  • Salary and benefits (SWB): improved as a percentage of revenue vs. prior year
  • Other operating expenses: improved as a percentage of revenue vs. prior year
  • Same-facility contract labor: improved 1% from prior year quarter
  • Contract labor as % of total labor costs (Q2 2025): 4.3% (vs. 4.6% in Q2 2024; pre-pandemic level was approximately 4.1%-4.2%)
  • Supply expense: increased slightly as a percentage of revenue (primarily cardiac-related devices)
  • Same-facility professional fees: increased approximately 10% over prior year (about as expected; physician cost pressures remain elevated above inflationary levels)

Medicaid Supplemental Payment Programs

  • Net benefit increase (Q2 2025 vs. Q2 2024): approximately $100 million increase (due to prior period reconciliation payments and program accrual timing)
  • Net benefit increase (H1 2025 vs. H1 2024): $180 million
  • New Tennessee directed payment program: approved in late June; no benefit accrued in Q2 2025 (will record as cash is received)

Capital Allocation (Q2 2025)

  • Capital expenditures: $1.2 billion
  • Share repurchases: $2.5 billion
  • Dividends: $171 million
  • Tax payment deferral: approximately $850 million deferred to Q4 (IRS relief for Tennessee taxpayers after severe weather in early April)
  • Debt to adjusted EBITDA leverage: remains in the lower half of stated guidance range

Market Share

  • Overall market share (ex-behavioral health): above 28%
  • Market share trends: broad-based growth across service lines and many markets
  • Number of U.S. markets tracked: 43
  • Total facilities: close to 2,700
  • Capital in pipeline (approved, under construction): approximately $5.5 billion

Revenue

  • New range: $74 billion to $76 billion
  • Status: RAISED (guidance increase reflects strong H1 performance)

Net Income Attributable to HCA Healthcare

  • New range: $6.11 billion to $6.48 billion

Adjusted EBITDA

  • New range: $14.7 billion to $15.3 billion
  • Midpoint increase: approximately $300 million vs. prior guidance
  • Composition of $300M increase: approximately half (~$150M) from state supplemental payment programs; approximately half (~$150M) from portfolio operational performance improvement
  • Portfolio improvement breakdown: approximately $100 million better in hurricane-related markets (originally guided flat YoY); offset by approximately $50 million underperformance in 2 other markets; rest of portfolio performing better than anticipated

Diluted Earnings Per Share

  • New range: $25.50 to $27.00

Capital Spending

  • Approximately $5 billion (unchanged)

Quarterly Cadence Commentary

  • Q3 2025: earnings growth expected to be "a little bit lower" relative to guidance midpoint growth rate
  • Q4 2025: earnings growth expected to be "a little bit higher" relative to guidance midpoint growth rate
  • Q4 drivers: hurricane market recovery largely/entirely in Q4; supplemental payment decline concentrated in Q4; one-time payments received in Q4 2024 that do not repeat
  • H2 growth rate (underlying): roughly comparable to H1 after adjusting for hurricane market improvement and supplemental payment timing

Managed Care Contracting Status

  • 2025: largely done
  • 2026: approximately 80% contracted (achieving targeted rate increases)
  • 2027: approximately one-third contracted (achieving targeted rate increases)

Hurricane Market Recovery

  • Original guidance assumption: flat year-over-year for combined hurricane markets (Largo, FL and North Carolina)
  • Updated assumption: approximately $100 million better year-over-year (full year 2025 vs. 2024)
  • Original 2024 impact: approximately $250 million total (approximately 60% additional expenses, 40% lost revenue)
  • Largo hospital: reopened December 1, 2024; year-over-year growth in earnings expected (impact was primarily repairs & maintenance)
  • North Carolina (Mission Hospital / Western NC): - Demand stronger than anticipated - Labor market tightened; required more contract labor - Q1 2025: hurricane markets relatively flat YoY - Q2 2025: slightly negative YoY (not material at company level) - Q3 2025: expected to be slightly negative YoY - Q4 2025: recovery expected (positive YoY)

One Big Beautiful Bill Act / Policy Environment

Medicaid Provisions

  • Management view: adverse impacts over next few years are "manageable"
  • Key mitigants: - Grandfathering provisions for supplemental programs (including previously submitted applications for state-directed payments) - Time lines for phasing in work requirements and supplemental payment program changes - Bifurcation between expansion and non-expansion states lessens impact to HCA
  • HCA Medicaid mix: approximately 60% of Medicaid volumes and revenue are in non-expansion states

Exchange Provisions

  • Management view: some people will lose insurance coverage over next few years; financial resiliency program should offset these effects in the near term
  • 2-Midnight Rule: fully implemented in 2024; no additional movement from observation to inpatient being observed

Tax Provisions

  • 100% bonus depreciation: made permanent and effective back to inauguration day (positive for HCA given capital investment program)
  • No materially increased tax liabilities from the act

Enhanced Premium Tax Credits (EPTCs)

  • Status: scheduled to expire at end of 2025; outcome unknown
  • Management position: actively advocating for extension; developing resiliency programs to offset adverse impact if they expire
  • Current exchange exposure: approximately 8% of equivalent admissions, just over 10% of net revenues
  • More detail expected: Q4 2025 earnings call (when 2026 guidance is issued)

Financial Resiliency Program

Investor Day Target (End of 2023)

  • $600 million to $800 million in targeted savings over 5 years
  • Progress: approximately 1.5 years into 5-year outlook; efforts have been both "accelerated and enhanced" in light of potential policy challenges

Three Main Categories

  • 1. Benchmarking: corporate and shared service functions benchmarked against Fortune 100; field-based facility benchmarking on operational and cost metrics; leverage best practices 2. Automation & Digital Transformation: focused on administrative and operational platforms; includes case management, length of stay improvements 3. Shared Service Platform Expansion: adding additional functions over time; driving scale, standardization, and best practice benefits

Additional Areas

  • Labor-related resiliency plans: "very developed and mature"
  • Supply-related resiliency plans: "very developed and mature"
  • Management confidence on Act provisions: "in the longer term...we believe HCA will be able to generally manage these impacts with our resiliency efforts without material impact to our long-term guidance" (long-term guidance = 4%-6% EBITDA growth)
  • Comprehensive update timing: Q4 2025 earnings call with 2026 guidance

Q&A Quantitative Data Points (Incremental)

Leap Year Impact

  • H1 2025 vs. H1 2024 volume impact from leap year: approximately 50 basis points

Patient Collections & Self-Pay

  • Patient balance growth trend (commercial, annual): historically mid-single-digit increases; recent quarters slightly higher (influenced by exchange patients who have higher patient responsibilities)
  • Exchange patient collection rate: lower than traditional commercial
  • Traditional commercial collection rate: historical levels maintained
  • Truly uninsured collections: very little cash collected; most uninsured patients qualify for charity programs or significantly reduced amounts under patient protection policy

Denial Activity (Medicare Advantage)

  • No significant impact on results from denial activities (reflects investment in revenue cycle management over past couple of years)
  • Partnership activities with key managed care payer partners: digital integration, administrative simplification, better management of disputes

Underperforming Markets

  • Number of underperforming divisions: 2 (out of 15 domestic)
  • Estimated impact: approximately $50 million combined
  • Drivers: competitive dynamics in one division; service mix change (not structural) in the other
  • Expectation: recovery anticipated in H2 2025

Medicaid Mix Context

  • Approximately 60% of HCA Medicaid volumes and revenue are in non-expansion states

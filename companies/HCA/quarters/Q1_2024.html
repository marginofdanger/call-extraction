  • Changed Revenue Per Admission: +3.5% year-over-year (prior: +2.7%)

Full Year 2024 Guidance (Reaffirmed)

  • > Management stated guidance ranges are reaffirmed with no changes from the ranges provided at the Q4 2023 / FY2023 earnings call. Management explicitly noted they do not plan to adjust guidance quarter-by-quarter unless there are material circumstances warranting a change. Ranges were set wide enough to accommodate a range of outcomes.

Guidance Philosophy Change

  • Management explicitly stated they are moving away from resetting guidance every quarter; will only adjust for material circumstances on either side
  • Guidance ranges intentionally set wide enough to accommodate a range of outcomes
  • CFO Bill Rutherford: "We want to get out of the trend, if you will, of trying to reset guidance every quarter-by-quarter"

Volume Outlook / Seasonality Commentary

  • Q1 volume comps: strongest of the year; H2 2024 comps get more difficult as H2 2023 saw positive volume trends
  • March 2024: softer outpatient and elective volumes due to fewer business days and Easter timing; inpatient admissions still grew in March
  • Full year expectation: volume growth may moderate from Q1 levels but demand backdrop remains strong; management noted they may end up on the high side of 3%-4% equivalent admission guidance
  • Leap year: Q1 2024 had 1 extra day, which contributed to ER visit growth

Income Statement & Profitability

  • Diluted EPS (as adjusted): $5.36 (increased almost 9% year-over-year)
  • Revenue growth (consolidated): 11.2% year-over-year
  • Same-facility revenue growth: ~9% year-over-year
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $3.35 billion (5.7% increase over prior year)
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin: 19.3%
  • Prior year Q1 comparison note: Q1 2023 included a $145 million favorable settlement (not repeated in Q1 2024)

Same-Facility Volume Metrics (Year-over-Year)

  • Inpatient admissions: +6%
  • Inpatient surgeries: up almost 2%
  • Equivalent admissions: +5%
  • Emergency room visits: +7% (against a +10% comp in prior year)
  • Outpatient surgery cases: -2% (attributed to calendar effects and Medicaid redetermination declines)
  • Outpatient surgery revenue: increased year-over-year (driven by favorable payer mix despite case decline)
  • Births (obstetrics): grew year-over-year (had previously been declining)
  • Cardiac procedures and rehab admissions: solid growth
  • Portfolio performance: 56 hospitals grew admissions >10% (~1/3 of portfolio); another ~1/4 grew >5%

Payer Mix & Acuity

  • Commercial volumes as % of equivalent admissions: ~36% (vs. 34% prior year)
  • Overall managed care volume growth: +12% to 13%
  • Health insurance exchange (HICS) volume: up close to 50% year-over-year
  • Exchange enrollment in HCA markets: up a little north of 30%
  • HICS as % of total volume: roughly 6%
  • Commercial ER visits: +20% year-over-year
  • Medicaid ER visits: -10% year-over-year
  • Self-pay ER visits: up slightly year-over-year
  • Inpatient case mix index: increased slightly, continuing upward trend of past few years
  • Medicaid redeterminations: ~20% of previously covered patients lost coverage; large portion landing in HICS or employer-sponsored coverage

Revenue per Equivalent Admission

  • Same-facility revenue per equivalent admission: +3.5% year-over-year

Labor & Operating Costs

  • As-reported labor cost as % of revenue: improved 100 basis points year-over-year
  • Contract labor trend: -21.7% year-over-year (down 20%+ referenced multiple times)
  • Contract labor as % of total labor cost: 5.1%
  • Supply cost as % of revenue: improved 10 basis points year-over-year
  • Other operating costs as % of revenue: grew year-over-year but remained relatively consistent for past 3 quarters
  • SWB per adjusted patient day growth: ~3%
  • Wage inflation: running at 2.5% to 3% level (in line with expectations)
  • Professional fee expense: sequential growth continued to moderate, in line with expectations
  • Valesco operations: performed better than expectations in the quarter

Capital Allocation & Balance Sheet

  • Cash flow from operations: just under $2.5 billion
  • Capital expenditures: $1.1 billion (flattish year-over-year; timing-related)
  • Share repurchases: just under $1.2 billion
  • Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage: remains near the low end of stated guidance range
  • M&A spending in quarter: less than $100 million

Capacity & Network

  • Total facilities/sites of care: increased ~5% to ~2,600
  • Inpatient bed capacity added: ~2% year-over-year
  • Non-same-store revenue: ~$500 million (driven by Valesco operations and Wise Health System acquisition)

Guided Metrics (Reaffirmed, Unchanged)

  • Same-facility equivalent admission growth: 3% to 4% (for full year; Q1 came in above 5%, management acknowledged they may end up on the high side)
  • Capital expenditures: ~$5.2 billion
  • Wage inflation assumption: 2.5% to 3% (Note: Specific revenue, EBITDA, and EPS guidance ranges were not restated on this call; management referred to the ranges as previously provided at year-end and noted they are reaffirmed.)

Medicaid Directed Payment Programs (DPP) / DSH

  • FY 2023 gross DPP/DSH amount reported: $3.9 billion
  • FY 2024 outlook: modest headwind in DPP revenue vs. 2023, largely due to settlements realized in 2023 that are not expected to reoccur
  • Number of states with DPP programs: 18 or 19
  • Accrual methodology: for established programs, on an accrual basis; for new programs, recognition deferred until established history and fund flows begin
  • Florida DPP: previously recognized on an annual lump sum basis; started accruing in 2024
  • Expense ratios: operating expenses associated with DPP revenue have remained relatively consistent
  • CMS Final Rule (released week of call): generally viewed as positive; removes potential for some cap add-on payments; potential for changes in how providers receive payments; will take time for states to implement

Valesco Operations

  • Q1 2024 performance: better than expectations
  • Full year 2024 expectation: same total dollar amount as FY 2023, but FY 2023 only had 3 quarters of Valesco vs. 4 quarters in FY 2024 (implies sequential quarterly improvement)
  • Reporting: included in non-same-store results

Outpatient Surgery Dynamics

  • Total outpatient surgery cases: -2% same-facility
  • Commercial outpatient surgery: generally flat (performed better than aggregate)
  • Medicaid outpatient surgery: significant decline (attributed to redeterminations)
  • Self-pay outpatient surgery: decline
  • Working thesis: patients redetermined off Medicaid into HICS/employer plans may defer elective procedures due to new co-pays and deductibles; potential rebound later in year
  • Revenue impact: outpatient surgery revenue was up year-over-year despite case decline, driven by payer mix

Galen College of Nursing (Strategic Update)

  • Target: ~30 campuses by end of 2027
  • Target enrollment: ~30,000 students across campuses
  • Annual graduation target: 7,000 to 8,000 per year
  • Strategy: integrate campuses into HCA system for clinical integration; retain graduates within HCA
  • Status: early stages; seeing incremental improvement in retention of graduates within HCA

Capacity Expansion Pipeline

  • New hospital: San Antonio, Texas (west side) -- opening later in 2024
  • Bed capacity growth expected for FY 2024: ~2% (similar to the ~2% added in Q1 year-over-year)
  • ER capacity: also growing through new units and expansion of existing units
  • Capital pipeline: significant pipeline to deliver more inpatient capacity over next 2 to 2.5 years
  • Investment areas: inpatient beds, ER (on-campus and off-campus), technology for nurses, surgical equipment, outpatient surgery platform

Two-Midnight Rule

  • Status: still early
  • Impact: modest benefit observed; seeing some two-midnight inpatient volume grow due to statusing in accordance with new rules
  • Caveat: not all claims have completed adjudication; too early for definitive quantification

Bad Debt / Collections

  • Bad debt impact from redeterminations: no material change observed in collectibility or bad debts at this stage
  • Self-pay growth note: some self-pay growth includes patients in "pending Medicaid" status as eligibility efforts continue

Labor Pipeline & Hiring

  • Nurse hiring: up
  • Turnover: stabilized
  • Contract labor: down 20%+ year-over-year; management believes there is still more room to improve
  • No specific constraints called out in any labor category

Key Guidance Assumptions

  • Same-facility equivalent admissions growth: approximately 2% to 3%
  • Inpatient admission growth: "somewhere around that 2% number"
  • Outpatient revenue growth: "continue to grow in that mid-single-digit level"
  • COVID admissions as % of total admissions (2023E): "somewhere between 3% and 4%" (down from 5.2% in 2022)
  • Surgical volume growth: expected to "reflect longer-term trends, which has historically been somewhere around that 2-point growth"
  • Revenue per equivalent admission growth: approximately 2% (as reported)
  • Revenue per equivalent admission growth (adjusted for lost non-recurring items): "closer to 3%," which is "in line with our long-term guidance"
  • Context: 2% as-reported reflects loss of COVID support revenue, Texas DPP, and 340B impacts; adjusting for those brings growth closer to 3%, within historical 2% to 3% range
  • Loss of COVID support revenue: ~$280 million (recognized in 2022; "we expect very little revenue from these programs in 2023")
  • Loss of Texas directed payment program: $244 million revenue / $90 million expense recognized in 2022 (non-recurring in 2023)
  • 340B related payment reductions: estimated $50 million to $100 million impact
  • Adjusted for these items: midpoint of 2023 adjusted EBITDA guidance "would be in the middle of our historical 4% to 6% growth expectations"
  • Labor cost as a percentage of revenue: expected to remain "flat with where we ran for the full year" of 2022 (as-reported basis)
  • Contract labor: continued improvement in utilization and cost expected through 2023
  • Supply cost as a percent of revenue: expected to remain "flat" with full-year 2022 levels
  • Supply cost per unit growth: "somewhere around that 2% level, plus or minus a little bit"
  • Other operating expenses: expected to see "higher single-digit year-over-year growth," potentially "at a little bit of pace above our revenue" (driven by ER physician subsidies, anesthesiologist subsidies, utilities, insurance, professional fees)
  • Incremental long-term investment spending (technology, clinical transformation, nursing schools): approximately $150 million incremental in 2023 vs. 2022
  • Depreciation: estimated approximately $3.1 billion
  • Interest expense: projected approximately $1.975 billion (impacted by higher rates and anticipated draws under revolving credit facilities)
  • Fully diluted shares: expected approximately 278 million for the full year
  • Upside (high end): "achievable if our volume and labor agenda happens, maybe a little bit better than what we anticipate"
  • Downside (low end): "greater inflationary pressures and maybe some more challenges in a tenuous labor market"
  • Range: approximately 2.5% on either side of the midpoint

Long-Term Investment Plan (4 Pillars)

  • 1. Advancing clinical systems and digital capabilities -- alpha pilot underway on clinical system upgrades; beta pilot expected later in 2023; cloud-based standardized information for actionable insights 2. Transforming care models with innovative solutions -- care transformation team led by physician and clinical team with industrial engineers and data analysts; major obstetrics initiative rolling out in 2023 3. Expanding workforce development programs -- seventh Galen College of Nursing School opened in 2022 4. Investing capital in networks to expand offerings -- freestanding ER pipeline, greenfield hospitals, ASC de novo development

Volume Metrics (Same-Facility, Year-over-Year)

  • Same-facility admissions: +2.9% YoY
  • Non-COVID same-facility admissions: +5.4% YoY
  • Same-facility equivalent admissions: +5.4% YoY
  • Same-facility emergency room visits: +11.4% YoY (Sam Hazen: "impressive growth of 11% in the emergency room")
  • Same-facility outpatient surgeries: up slightly YoY; +5.6% sequentially vs. Q3 2022
  • Same-facility inpatient surgeries: "basically flat" YoY
  • Note on surgical volumes: One less business day / surgical day in Q4 2022 vs. prior year, accounting for approximately 1.5 to 2 percentage points of impact

Revenue / Pricing Metrics

  • Same-facility revenue per equivalent admission: -2.6% YoY (influenced by drop in COVID activity)
  • Non-COVID revenue per equivalent admission: +3.7% sequentially vs. Q3 2022
  • Revenue growth: +3% YoY in the quarter (described as "against a difficult comparison")

Acuity / Payer Mix

  • Case mix: increased "just under 2%" sequentially from Q3 2022
  • Payer mix: "remained stable"
  • Payer mix and acuity levels: "remained at favorable levels"

Margin / Cost Metrics

  • Consolidated adjusted EBITDA margin: 20.5% in Q4 2022
  • Total labor cost as a percentage of revenue: improved both sequentially and YoY
  • Contract labor: down approximately 16% YoY from Q4 2021
  • Contract labor as % of total SWB: approximately 7.8%
  • Other operating expenses: "higher single-digit cost growth" in areas such as utilities, insurance, and professional fees; "as a percentage of revenue, staying relatively flat and pretty consistent throughout the year"

Labor / Workforce Metrics

  • Registered nurse turnover: down 26% in Q4 2022 compared to the previous 4-quarter average
  • Current RN turnover rate: approximately 18% to 18.5% (run rate)
  • Pre-pandemic RN turnover rate: approximately 13% to 14%
  • Industry average RN turnover (estimated): mid- to upper-20s%
  • Employee engagement scores: "recovered to around prepandemic levels" and "above the industry average"
  • Hiring: increased 6% YoY in 2022 ("a huge number of new hires")

Capacity Constraints

  • Missed admissions via transfer centers: approximately 2% of total admissions in Q4 2022 (elevated vs. Q3 2022, where it was 1% to 1.5%)
  • Context: "We were busier in the fourth quarter than we were in the third quarter"

Service Line Highlights (Q4 2022)

  • Total joint (orthopedics): +6% in the quarter
  • Urgent care centers: 260 locations (expected to push through 300 in 2023)

Volume Metrics (Same-Facility)

  • Same-facility admissions: +0.5% YoY
  • Excluding COVID admissions (same-facility): +3.4% YoY
  • COVID admissions as % of total admissions: 5.2% (vs. 7.8% in the prior year)
  • Same-facility emergency room visits: +7.6% YoY

Margin Metrics

  • Consolidated adjusted EBITDA margin: "right at 20%" for the full year

Cash Flow & Capital Allocation

  • Cash flow from operations: $8.5 billion
  • Capital spending: just under $4.4 billion (slightly higher than initial expectation of $4.2 billion due to year-end real estate and IT purchases)
  • Dividends paid: approximately $650 million
  • Share repurchases: $7 billion
  • Debt to adjusted EBITDA leverage ratio: "near the low end" of stated 3x to 4x range
  • Proceeds from sales of facilities and healthcare entities: approximately $1.2 billion

Non-Recurring Revenue Items Recognized in 2022

  • COVID support (DRG add-ons, sequestration removal, HRSA reimbursement for uninsured COVID patients): approximately $280 million
  • Texas directed payment program (related to last 4 months of 2021, recognized in Q1 2022): $244 million in revenues and $90 million in expenses (net ~$154 million)

Revenue Per Equivalent Admission

  • Full year non-COVID revenue per equivalent admission: "roughly flat" on YoY comparisons

Capital Allocation & Shareholder Returns

  • New share repurchase authorization: $3 billion (Board approved)
  • Remaining authorization from previous program: approximately $1.5 billion at year-end 2022
  • Total share repurchase authorization available: approximately $4.5 billion
  • Repurchase timing: "plan on completing the majority of it sometime in this calendar year with a little bit of roll forward afterwards"
  • Quarterly dividend increase: from $0.56 to $0.60 per share

Payer Mix

  • Overall: expected to "mostly remain stable"
  • Health insurance exchange enrollment: "encouraged with what we are seeing"; enrollment in HCA's states "probably a little bit higher than what we see as a nation"
  • Medicaid redeterminations: contemplated within guidance range
  • Commercial contract status: approximately 70% of 2023 contracts already contracted (as of call date); remaining ~30% to be negotiated in early 2023
  • 2024 contract status: approximately 40% contracted
  • Commercial rate escalators: contracts closed in last 3 quarters of 2022 were "in line with expectations, which was around mid-single-digit inflators"

Strategic / Operational Commentary

Portfolio / M&A Activity

  • Sarah Cannon Research Institute JV with McKesson: closed in Q4 2022 (combining cancer research entities)
  • HealthTrust (core trust purchasing organization) JV with Blackstone: closed in Q4 2022
  • Envision JV call option exercised: moving to acquire a larger percentage of the co-venture; expected to close "sometime in the spring" of 2023; full assimilation through rest of 2023; execution benefits expected in 2024
  • Hospital acquisition pipeline: announced LOI on a hospital just outside of Dallas-Fort Worth market
  • Greenfield hospital pipeline: 1 hospital under construction in San Antonio; "7 or 8 parcels of land, maybe 10" designed for future hospital development
  • Ambulatory network: more de novo ASC development than historically; urgent care centers expected to exceed 300 in 2023
  • Portfolio: approximately 180 hospitals and 2,400 outpatient facilities

Key Macro / Demand Themes

  • Demand environment: "strong demand for health care services" with "aging baby boomers, population growth, chronic conditions" driving growth
  • Emergency room resilience: "resiliency of the emergency room for communities is even greater than we thought"
  • Orthopedic site-of-care shift: "large majority of that is behind us" (inpatient to outpatient shift over past 3 years)
  • Physician clinic patient volumes: anecdotal reports that "clinic patient profiles were better in the fourth quarter than they were at any point in time in 2022"

2023 Organizational Priorities

  • 1. Overcoming labor and capacity challenges 2. Countering inflationary pressures 3. Accelerating growth with winning plays -- capital investments in outpatient facilities, clinical equipment, service line expansion

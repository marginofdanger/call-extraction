What Changed vs Q2 2024

  • Maintained Revenue guidance reaffirmed at $69.75B – $71.75B (but lower half expected due to hurricanes)
  • Maintained Adj. EBITDA guidance reaffirmed at $13.75B – $14.25B (lower half expected)
  • Maintained Diluted EPS guidance reaffirmed at $21.60 – $22.80 (lower half expected)
  • Lowered CapEx guidance lowered to ~$5.0B (from $5.1B – $5.3B) due to timing of capital projects
  • Raised Medicaid supplemental payment tailwind now expected closer to $200M (was $100M – $200M range)
  • New Hurricane impact: $200M – $300M estimated Q4 additional expenses/lost revenue ($0.60 – $0.90/share)
  • New Preliminary 2025 outlook: EBITDA/EPS growth near or above upper end of long-term targets (4%–6%)

Full Year 2024 Guidance (Reaffirmed — Lower Half Expected)

  • Revenue: $69.75 billion – $71.75 billion (reaffirmed; lower half expected due to hurricane impact)
  • Net Income: $5.675 billion – $5.975 billion (reaffirmed; lower half expected)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $13.75 billion – $14.25 billion (reaffirmed; lower half expected)
  • Diluted EPS: $21.60 – $22.80 (reaffirmed; lower half expected)
  • Capital Expenditures: ~$5.0 billion (lowered from $5.1B – $5.3B, due to timing of capital projects)

Q3 2024 Actuals (Reported)

  • Diluted EPS (as adjusted): $4.90, up 25% YoY
  • Hurricane Helene impact on Q3 EPS: ~$0.15/share (~$50M pre-tax)
  • Adjusted EBITDA growth: Over 13% YoY
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin improvement: +90 bps YoY
  • Revenue growth: 7.9% YoY; same-facilities revenue growth 7.1%
  • Cash flow from operations: $3.5B
  • Capital expenditures: $1.19B
  • Share repurchases: $1.79B
  • Dividends paid: $169M

Volume (Same-Facility Basis, Q3 2024)

  • Inpatient admissions: +4.5% YoY
  • Adjusted admissions: +4.5% YoY
  • Emergency room visits: +4.6% YoY
  • Inpatient surgeries: +1.6% YoY
  • Outpatient surgery: -2% YoY (but outpatient surgery revenue +5% due to acuity/payer mix)
  • Outpatient surgery revenue per surgical case: +7%
  • Sequential same-facility admissions (Q2 to Q3): +1.4% (in line with trend)
  • Cardiac procedures, inpatient rehab, obstetric volumes: solid growth (no specific figures)

Volume by Payer (Same-Facility Equivalent Admissions, Q3 2024 vs. Prior Year)

  • Medicare equivalent admissions: +5%; total Medicare same-facility +5.3%
  • Traditional Medicare admissions: basically flat
  • Medicare Advantage admissions: +11% (~2pp attributed to two-midnight rule, ~50 bps of overall admission growth)
  • Medicaid equivalent admissions: -8.5%
  • Managed care (excl. exchanges) equivalent admissions: up just short of 4%
  • Exchange volume equivalent admissions: +43% YoY
  • Uninsured equivalent admissions: +7.2%

Payer Mix

  • Medicare Advantage as % of total Medicare volume: 58%
  • Payer mix improvement noted across service lines in the quarter
  • Acuity improvement within inpatient services in the quarter

Labor & Operating Costs

  • Labor costs as % of revenue: improved 160 bps YoY
  • Contract labor: declined 18% YoY; represented 4.6% of total labor cost
  • Supply cost as % of revenue: increased 30 bps YoY; relatively consistent sequentially
  • Other operating costs as % of revenue: increased 40 bps YoY
  • Professional fee cost growth: +10% YoY; flat sequentially vs. Q2 (Q1: +20%, Q2: +13%, Q3: +10%)
  • Wage inflation: stable in the 2.5%–3.5% range
  • Length of stay reduction (YTD via resiliency program): ~1.5%

Medicaid Supplemental Payment Programs

  • Net effect in Q3 2024: modest benefit vs. prior year
  • Components: reduction from Florida program (change to accruing basis), offset by North Carolina benefit (Q3 vs. Q4 2023), plus new Nevada program
  • Full year 2024 tailwind vs. prior year: $100M–$200M; management leans to higher end (~$200M)

Medicare Advantage / Two-Midnight Rule

  • Two-midnight rule contribution to MA admission growth: ~2pp of the 11% MA admission growth (~50 bps of overall)
  • Prior authorization denial trends: modest reduction for accounts with two-midnights or greater vs. prior year
  • MA observation-to-inpatient mix: still significantly higher than traditional Medicare (9 months post implementation)
  • MA CMI-adjusted length-of-stay ratio: ~10% higher than traditional Medicare
  • Denial write-offs: moderated growth; denials not a material impact in Q3 2024

Network Development & Capital Allocation

  • By year-end 2024: ~600 inpatient beds and ~100 new outpatient facilities added
  • Total sites of care: expected to exceed 2,600 by year-end 2024
  • Projects under development: ~$6B currently
  • Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage: at the low end of stated guidance range

Denials & Revenue Cycle

  • Denials not a material impact in Q3 2024
  • Dispute resolution timeline for two-midnight denials: 1–2 years to complete
  • AI/technology embedded in revenue cycle to manage receivables and mitigate denial pressures

Valesco (Physician Services)

  • Integration of ~5,500 physicians completed this year
  • Financial performance on plan, slightly ahead but not material to the company

Hurricane Impact Estimates

  • Q3 impact (Helene): ~$50M pre-tax / $0.15/share; 29 hospitals in path
  • Mission Hospital (Asheville, NC): continued operations; significant water infrastructure damage; water supply costs at least $13M through October
  • Q4 estimated additional impact (Helene + Milton): $200M–$300M / $0.60–$0.90/share (does NOT include insurance recoveries)
  • 34+ hospitals in path of Milton
  • HCA Florida Largo Hospital: flooded, closed; anticipated reopening late December 2024
  • Business interruption coverage exists; timing/amount of recovery not estimable yet

Preliminary 2025 Outlook (Early Look — Formal Guidance in January)

  • Volume growth: 3%–4% (elevated vs. long-term 2%–3% demand assumption)
  • Diluted EPS growth: expected near or slightly above the upper end of long-term target ranges
  • Adjusted EBITDA growth: expected near or slightly above the upper end of long-term targets (4%–6%)
  • Cash net revenue per adjusted admission growth: 2%–3% (early look)
  • Wage inflation: stable at 2.5%–3.5% range, consistent with 2024
  • Exchange enrollment growth: 8%–10% (vs. 30%+ in HCA states in 2024)
  • Medicaid volumes: expected to flatten as redetermination sunsets; generally stable payer mix vs. 2024
  • Hurricane impact: manageable, primarily in North Carolina; Tampa/Largo facility expected operational by end of 2024
  • Resiliency program target savings: $600M–$800M (from Investor Day); near-term to fund tech/innovation
  • Leverage: at lower end of target; financial policy evaluation ongoing, changes to be announced on Q1 call
  • Payer contracting: 2025 contracts 80% complete; 2026 contracts 54% complete; 2027 contracts ~20% complete; mid-single-digit rate updates
  • Professional fees: implied stable cost environment

Special Topics

  • Hurricane recovery precedents: Port Charlotte (Ian), Panama City (Michael), and Houston (Harvey) all performing above pre-storm levels after repair/hardening
  • Outpatient surgery trends: volume declines focused in Medicaid/uninsured; revenue per case +7% on acuity; no significant changes anticipated for 2025
  • AI / Digital agenda: 5–7 year strategy presented; scheduling and revenue cycle showing promising results; not material yet but growing
  • New contracts: Kaiser (Denver) and Blue Cross TN/Chattanooga signed in 2024
  • Bad debt / uninsured: cost trends pretty stable; no significant changes expected into 2025
  • Quality metrics: outcomes better than 2019; quality-based kickers not material to contract escalators

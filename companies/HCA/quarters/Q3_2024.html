Preliminary 2025 Outlook (Early Look -- Formal Guidance in January)

Earnings

  • Diluted EPS (as adjusted): $4.90, up 25% YoY
  • Hurricane Helene impact on Q3 EPS: ~$0.15 per diluted share (~$50 million pre-tax)
  • Adjusted EBITDA growth: Over 13% YoY
  • Adjusted EBITDA margin improvement: +90 basis points YoY

Revenue

  • Revenue growth: 7.9% YoY
  • Same-facilities revenue growth: 7.1%

Volume (Same-Facility Basis)

  • Inpatient admissions: +4.5% YoY
  • Adjusted admissions: +4.5% YoY
  • Emergency room visits: +4.6% YoY
  • Inpatient surgeries: +1.6% YoY
  • Outpatient surgery: -2% YoY (however, outpatient surgery revenue +5% due to acuity/payer mix)
  • Outpatient surgery revenue per surgical case: +7%
  • Sequential same-facility admissions increase (Q2 to Q3 2024): +1.4% (described as "in line with past sequential trend")
  • Cardiac procedures, inpatient rehab admissions, obstetric volumes: "solid growth" (no specific figures provided)

Volume by Payer (Same-Facility Equivalent Admissions, Q3 2024 vs. Prior Year)

  • Medicare equivalent admissions: +5%
  • Total Medicare admissions (same-facility): +5.3% - Traditional Medicare admissions: "basically flat" - Medicare Advantage admissions: +11% (approximately 2% of that 11% attributed to two-midnight rule impact, equivalent to ~50 bps of overall admission growth)
  • Medicaid equivalent admissions: -8.5%
  • Managed care (excluding exchanges) equivalent admissions: up just short of 4%
  • Exchange volume equivalent admissions: +43% YoY
  • Uninsured equivalent admissions: +7.2%

Payer Mix

  • Medicare Advantage as % of total Medicare volume: 58%
  • Payer mix improvement noted across service lines in the quarter
  • Acuity improvement within inpatient services in the quarter

Labor & Operating Costs

  • Labor costs as % of revenue: improved 160 basis points YoY
  • Contract labor: improved 18% YoY (declined 18%); represented 4.6% of total labor cost
  • Supply cost as % of revenue: increased 30 basis points YoY; "relatively consistent sequentially"
  • Other operating costs as % of revenue: increased 40 basis points YoY
  • Professional fee cost growth: +10% YoY; "flat sequentially to the second quarter" - Context: Q1 2024 pro fee growth was +20% YoY; Q2 2024 was +13% YoY; Q3 2024 was +10% YoY
  • Wage inflation: generally stable in the 2.5% to 3.5% range
  • Length of stay reduction (year-to-date via resiliency program): ~1.5% reduction

Medicaid Supplemental Payment Programs

  • Net effect in Q3 2024: "modest benefit" vs. prior year
  • Components: reduction from Florida program (change to accruing basis), offset by North Carolina benefit received in Q3 2024 vs. Q4 2023, plus new Nevada program kicking in this year
  • Full year 2024 Medicaid supplemental payment program tailwind vs. prior year: $100 million to $200 million; management "would now lean to the higher end of that range" (i.e., closer to $200 million)

Medicare Advantage / Two-Midnight Rule

  • Two-midnight rule contribution to MA admission growth: ~2 percentage points of the 11% MA admission growth
  • Equivalent to ~50 bps of overall admission growth; "remained consistent over the year"
  • Prior authorization denial trends: "modest reduction" for accounts with two-midnights or greater vs. prior year in 2024
  • Medicare Advantage observation-to-inpatient mix: "still significantly higher than traditional Medicare" even 9 months post two-midnight rule implementation
  • Medicare Advantage CMI-adjusted length-of-stay ratio: ~10% higher than traditional Medicare, "driven by payer-driven discharge delays"
  • Denial write-offs: moderated rate of growth in 2024 vs. prior year; "denials were not a material impact to HCA in third quarter of 2024"

Capital Allocation

  • Cash flow from operations: $3.5 billion in Q3 2024
  • Capital expenditures: $1.19 billion in Q3 2024
  • Share repurchases: $1.79 billion in Q3 2024
  • Dividends paid: $169 million in Q3 2024
  • Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage: "at the low end of our stated guidance range"

Network Development

  • By end of 2024: expect to have added approximately 600 inpatient beds and 100 new outpatient facilities
  • Total sites of care: expected to exceed 2,600 by year-end 2024
  • Projects under development: ~$6 billion currently

Denials & Revenue Cycle

  • Denials: "not a material impact to HCA in third quarter of 2024"
  • Dispute resolution timeline for two-midnight denials: "can take a year to 2 years to fully complete"
  • AI/technology embedded in revenue cycle to manage receivables and mitigate denial pressures

Valesco (Physician Services)

  • Integration of ~5,500 physicians completed this year
  • Financial performance: "on our plan, maybe slightly ahead of it but it's not material to the company as a whole"

Hurricane Impact Estimates

  • Estimated impact: ~$50 million pre-tax / $0.15 per diluted share
  • 29 hospitals in path of Helene
  • Mission Hospital (Asheville, NC): continued operations but significant city infrastructure damage (primarily water)
  • Water supply costs at Mission Hospital: at least $13 million through October
  • City potable water supply not projected to be reestablished at Mission Hospital "for several more weeks"
  • Estimated additional expenses and lost revenue: $200 million to $300 million / $0.60 to $0.90 per diluted share
  • Impact relates "primarily to our hospitals in North Carolina and our Largo Hospital in Florida"
  • These estimates do NOT include any insurance recoveries
  • 34+ hospitals in path of Milton
  • HCA Florida Largo Hospital: flooded, currently closed; anticipated reopening late December 2024
  • Company has business interruption coverage as part of property insurance program
  • "Not in a position to provide estimates related to either the timing or the potential recovery"
  • "We do believe that there will be a claim for sure, given the size of these storms"

Volume

  • Volume growth assumption for 2025: 3% to 4% (elevated vs. long-term demand assumptions of 2% to 3%)
  • Management stated: "we assume volume growth will continue at elevated levels in the range of 3% to 4% for the year"

Earnings Growth

  • Diluted EPS growth: expected "near or slightly above the upper end of our long-term target ranges"
  • Adjusted EBITDA growth: expected "near or slightly above the upper end of our long-term target ranges"
  • Long-term EBITDA growth target range referenced by analysts: 4% to 6%
  • Hurricane impact in 2025 "taken into account" in these preliminary comments; described as "manageable" and "primarily in North Carolina"

Revenue / Pricing

  • Cash net revenue per adjusted admission growth: planning for 2% to 3% growth in 2025 (early look)

Payer Contracting

  • 2025 commercial contracts: 80% complete
  • 2026 commercial contracts: 54% complete
  • 2027 commercial contracts: just short of 20% complete
  • Contracting rates: "still tracking in that mid-single-digit update"

Labor & Cost Assumptions

  • Wage inflation: anticipated to be "fairly stable" and "generally consistent with what we've seen...for 2024" (i.e., 2.5% to 3.5% range)
  • Overall cost environment: "reasonable assumptions around the rest of our cost to be somewhat stable and in line with 2024"
  • Professional fees: implied stable ("part of Sam's comment for next year of a stable operating environment")

Exchange Enrollment

  • Exchange enrollment growth assumption for 2025: 8% to 10% growth (vs. 30%+ growth in HCA states in 2024)

Medicaid

  • Medicaid volumes: expected to "flatten out a bit in '25 versus '24" as redetermination process sunsets
  • Payer mix impact: net effect of exchange moderation + Medicaid flattening expected to produce "generally stable payer mix" from 2024 to 2025

Hurricane Impact in 2025

  • Ongoing effects: "manageable," "primarily in our North Carolina market"
  • Tampa/Largo facility: expected to be "operational by the end of 2024"
  • Hurricane lingering effects factored into the preliminary outlook of being "near or slightly above the upper end" of long-term growth ranges

Resiliency Program

  • Target savings: $600 million to $800 million (referenced from prior Investor Day)
  • Next 2-3 years: program to "pay for tech and innovation investments"
  • Deeper into the decade: to "support our margins and deal with the challenges that may come"
  • Management "pleased with the current status and the trajectory"

Leverage / Capital Allocation

  • Currently at the "bottom" / "lower end" of leverage ratio target
  • Evaluation of financial policies ongoing as part of annual planning/budgeting process; any changes to be announced on Q1 call

Special Topics

Hurricane Recovery -- Historical Precedents (Management Commentary)

  • HCA Faucette Hospital (Port Charlotte) -- Hurricane Ian: repaired, hardened, now "performing at a higher level than pre-Hurricane Ian"
  • Panama City, FL -- Hurricane Michael: repaired, hardened, "performing at a higher level than pre-storm"
  • Houston system -- Hurricane Harvey: recovered, "exceeding pre-Harvey performance"
  • Management belief: "we can produce similar results with these 2 hospitals in time"

Outpatient Surgery Trends

  • Volume declines "focused almost entirely in Medicaid and uninsured"
  • Revenue per surgical case up 7% due to acuity growth and payer mix improvement
  • "Profitability of our outpatient surgery service is better as a result"
  • No significant changes anticipated for 2025
  • Advancing number of surgery centers through greenfield development and targeted acquisitions

AI / Digital Agenda

  • Presented full plan on digital agenda and AI strategy for next 5 to 7 years
  • Areas showing promising results: scheduling capabilities (demand forecasting, workforce allocation), revenue cycle management (denial mitigation)
  • AI initiatives "not material yet" but growing
  • Expect "incremental improvements in '25 and '26 and on into the rest of this decade"
  • Management view: "at an inflection point"

Network / New Contracts

  • 2 major commercial contract signings in 2024: Kaiser in Denver and Blue Cross of Tennessee/Chattanooga
  • "Less than a handful" of commercial contracts across the country where HCA does not participate
  • Medicaid adjusted admissions represent 17%-18% of total HCA adjusted admissions

Bad Debt / Uninsured

  • Uninsured cost trends described as "pretty stable"
  • No significant changes anticipated going into 2025

Quality Metrics

  • Clinical outcomes and quality performance "better than it was in 2019" (pre-pandemic)
  • Quality-based kickers in commercial contracts described as "not material to the overall escalators"

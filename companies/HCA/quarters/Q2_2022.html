Full Year 2022 Guidance (Unchanged from Q1 2022 Update)

  • Guidance Status: UNCHANGED from prior quarter
  • Capital Expenditures: ~$4.2 billion (per analyst reference; management did not dispute)
  • Cash Flow from Operations (as % of Adjusted EBITDA): Revised expectation ~8.0%-8.5% (original guide was 9.0%-9.5%; CFO Bill Rutherford noted: "it's probably going to be in around the 8%, maybe 8.5% level") > Note: Bill Rutherford stated "our full year 2022 guidance remains unchanged from what we highlighted last quarter" in prepared remarks. The cash flow from ops percentage refinement came in Q&A.

Adjusted EBITDA Margin Outlook

  • Near-Term Margin Planning Range: 19%-20% (reiterated by CFO Rutherford; "there'll be periods we're north of that, some periods we're within that")
  • Q1 2022 Margin: ~19.7% (referenced as "[97]" likely 19.7%)
  • Q2 2022 Margin: 20.5%

Long-Term Growth Framework

  • Long-Term EBITDA Growth Target: Mid-single digits, often toward the high end of a 4%-6% range (reiterated; no change from prior commentary)
  • Inpatient Demand Growth (in HCA markets): 1%-2%
  • Outpatient Demand Growth (in HCA markets): "A little bit more than that" (i.e., slightly above 1%-2%)
  • Management View: "Not seeing anything structural that would suggest that overall demand is reduced or necessarily increase beyond what its normal trends were"

Volume Outlook (Back Half 2022)

  • Inpatient Admissions: Expected to grow at a "more modest level than previously indicated in our guidance"
  • Outpatient Volume: Expected "in line" growth
  • Seasonal Trends: Management expects volumes to return to "pre-pandemic seasonal trends"
  • Management Commentary: "Normal course of business has stabilized... we think we're going to see patterns return to kind of their normal seasonal trend"

Earnings & Profitability

  • Diluted EPS: $4.21 (after excluding $0.11 loss on sale of facilities and $0.20 from retirement of debt)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $3.04 billion
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin: 20.5% (vs. ~19.7% in Q1 2022)

Revenue

  • Total Revenue: $14.82 billion (vs. consensus $14.73B)
  • Same-Facility Revenue Growth: +4% YoY

Volume Metrics (Same-Facility Basis)

  • Same-Facility Inpatient Admissions: -1.2% YoY
  • Same-Facility Adjusted Admissions: +0.5% YoY
  • Non-COVID Admissions: -0.6% YoY
  • Non-COVID Managed Care Admissions: +0.7% YoY (favorable payer mix)
  • Total Medicare Admissions: +0.6% YoY
  • COVID Admissions: -18% YoY; represented ~3% of total admissions (consistent with prior year mix)
  • COVID Admissions (Sequential): -70% vs. Q1 2022
  • Emergency Room Visits (Same-Facility): +7.3% YoY (above pre-pandemic 2019 levels)
  • Occupancy Rate: ~72% in Q2 2022

Revenue Intensity

  • Same-Facility Inpatient Revenue per Admission: +3.3% YoY
  • Non-COVID Case Mix: Up slightly YoY, maintaining prior gains

Labor & Staffing

  • Cost per FTE (YoY Growth): ~4.5% in Q2 (improved from ~7%+ in Q1 2022)
  • Contract Labor as % of SWB: Just above 8% at end of June (month of June); was 9.0%-9.2% in Q1 2022
  • Contract Labor Expense Trend: Down 22% in June vs. April (within Q2); sequential dollar decline vs. Q1
  • Nurse Wage Increases: Mid-single digits
  • Turnover: Down over 20% in Q2 vs. Q1 2022
  • Recruitment: Up 18% in Q2 vs. Q1 2022

Cash Flow & Balance Sheet

  • Cash Flow from Operations: $1.63 billion in Q2 ($3.0 billion first-half 2022)
  • Capital Spending: $1.08 billion in Q2 (vs. $842 million in Q2 2021; ~$2.0 billion year-to-date)
  • Share Repurchases: ~$2.7 billion in Q2
  • Debt-to-Adjusted EBITDA: Just above the low end of stated leverage range
  • Available Liquidity: Just over $2.7 billion at end of Q2

COVID Support / Government Funding

  • COVID Support Revenue Recognized (Year-to-Date): ~$200 million
  • COVID Support Revenue (Q1 2022): ~$20-$25 million
  • Management Commentary: Most COVID support programs are "pretty much concluded"

Commercial / Managed Care Pricing

  • Historical Commercial Pricing Escalation: 2.5%-4% range
  • Expected Future Commercial Pricing Escalation: Mid-single digits (Sam Hazen: "I do anticipate it being somewhere around the mid-single digits")
  • Timing: 2022 already fully contracted; some uplift expected in 2023 from renegotiated contracts; full benefit anticipated in 2024
  • Payer Recognition: Management seeing "early success recognition by the payers" of inflationary pressures on providers
  • Contracts with Renegotiated Terms: Reflecting "more escalation in pricing than what we had seen in our past trends"

Capital Allocation & Deployment

  • CapEx Year-to-Date: ~$2.0 billion (tracking toward ~$4.2 billion full year guidance)
  • Share Repurchases (Q2): ~$2.7 billion
  • Capital Allocation Strategy: No material change expected; diversified approach across technology, outpatient development, and targeted hospital investments
  • FTC/Utah Hospital Acquisition: FTC challenged; management says this "doesn't change our overall outlook" for M&A strategy
  • Ambulatory/Outpatient: Acquired another urgent care company in Virginia division
  • Galen Nursing Colleges: 3 opened in Q2; 2 more scheduled for later in 2022; pipeline of 6-8 over the following 12-18 months; now at 13 total schools (8 added since acquisition)

Accounts Receivable

  • AR Days: Increased during 2022; at a "high mark" as of Q2
  • Drivers: Payers reinstating claim edits that were paused during COVID; some delays in payment processing cycle
  • Outlook: Management expects improvement through the rest of the year

Network & Capacity

  • Outpatient Facilities: ~2,500 outpatient facilities as part of hospital network ecosystem (~12:1 outpatient-to-hospital ratio)
  • Market Share: Grew from 3% in 2011 to 28% currently (in HCA's markets)
  • Capacity Constraints: Episodic staffing constraints limited transfer center throughput at certain facilities at certain times (not structural)
  • Behavioral Health & Rehab: Operating below full capacity due to staffing constraints; improving sequentially

Health Insurance Exchange / ACA

  • Exchange Volume Growth in 2021: ~20%-25% growth in a given period, "primarily attributable to the increase in enrollment... attributable to the enhanced subsidies"
  • Risk Factor: Enhanced premium tax credit expiration could impact enrollment and volume
  • Management Position: "Hopeful that they find ways to continue those" subsidies

Recession Preparedness

  • Key Differentiator vs. Past Cycles: ACA exchanges and Medicaid expansion provide a safety net that did not exist in prior recessions (previously, job loss meant joining uninsured ranks)
  • Management Confidence: "HCA Healthcare has performed well in past recessionary cycles, I'm confident that we can perform reasonably well in future recessionary cycles"
  • Labor Benefit: Recessions historically provide labor relief

Management Tone & Qualitative Signals

  • Overall Tone: Cautiously optimistic; pleased with Q2 results despite softer-than-expected demand
  • Labor: Clear sequential improvement; management sees continued momentum in recruitment, retention, and contract labor reduction
  • Volumes: More cautious on inpatient admission growth for the back half vs. original guidance; expect return to normal seasonality
  • Pricing: Constructive on managed care pricing discussions; expect mid-single-digit escalators going forward
  • Cost Management: Resiliency programs (benchmarking, shared services, care models) driving improvement
  • Capital Returns: Aggressive share repurchase activity ($2.7B in single quarter)
  • Recession: Well-positioned due to ACA safety net and operational adaptability; confident in ability to manage through downturn

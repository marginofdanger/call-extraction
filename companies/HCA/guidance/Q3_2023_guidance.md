# HCA Healthcare Q3 2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 24, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2023
**Speakers:** Samuel N. Hazen (CEO), William B. Rutherford (CFO), Frank George Morgan (VP IR)

---

## Q3 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Earnings & Revenue
- **Diluted EPS:** $3.91
- **Consolidated Net Revenue:** $16.21 billion (up 8.3% YoY from $14.97 billion prior year)
- **Same-Facility Revenue Growth:** +7.9% YoY

### Volume Metrics (Same-Facility Basis)
- **Same-Facility Admissions Growth:** +3.4% YoY
- **Same-Facility Commercial Admissions Growth:** +7% YoY
- **Same-Facility Equivalent Admissions Growth:** +4.1%
- **Consolidated Equivalent Admissions Growth:** +4.5%
- **Emergency Room Visits Growth:** +3.5% (same-facility)
- **Outpatient Surgeries Growth:** ~+1% YoY (same-facility)
- **Outpatient Cardiology Procedures Growth:** ~+5% YoY (same-facility)

### Revenue Per Unit
- **Revenue Per Equivalent Admission Growth:** +3.6% YoY

### Margin & Operating Metrics
- **Same-Facility Operating Margins:** over 20%
- **Valesco Impact on Adjusted EBITDA Margins:** approximately -80 basis points in Q3; approximately -40 basis points on YTD basis
- **Valesco Impact on Adjusted EBITDA:** approximately -$100 million in Q3; approximately -$100 million on a YTD basis (inclusive of Q2 revenue revisions)
- **Valesco Margin Impact Breakdown (Other Operating line):**
  - Valesco: ~50 basis points of margin pressure
  - Professional fee growth (ex-Valesco): ~40 basis points of margin pressure
  - Balance: supplemental expense increases related to Florida DPP and other programs
  - Excluding Valesco, other operating was off ~120 basis points; 40-50 bps from pro fees, balance from supplemental expenses

### Contract Labor & Workforce
- **Contract Labor Costs:** down 12.5% YoY vs. Q3 2022; down 11% sequentially (Q3 vs. Q2)
- **Available Bed Capacity Constraints (patient divert rate):** 0.9% of total admissions (consistent with Q2 2023)
- **Nurse Hiring:** strongest it has been all year
- **Turnover:** stable in the quarter

### Cash Flow & Capital Allocation (Q3 2023)
- **Cash Flow from Operations:** $2.48 billion
- **Capital Spending:** $1.15 billion
- **Dividends Paid:** ~$160 million
- **Share Repurchases:** $1.14 billion
- **Debt-to-Adjusted EBITDA Leverage Ratio:** near the low end of stated 3x-4x range

### Valesco Venture Details
- **Valesco Revenue (YTD):** ~$380 million (just under $400 million)
- **Valesco Scale:** ~5,000 physicians across ~200 different programs
- **Valesco Forward Run Rate Loss:** approximately $50 million per quarter
- **Valesco Revenue/Cost Model (go-forward quarterly):** ~$200 million revenue, ~$250 million cost (per analyst/management confirmation)
- **Professional Fees as % of Other Operating Expense:** just under 24% in Q3 (similar to Q2; up from 22% in Q1)

### Supplemental Program Notes
- **Florida DPP (Directed Payment Program):** slightly above expectations in Q3, but other supplemental programs were below expectations; net impact roughly offsetting

## Full Year 2023 Guidance (Updated Q3 2023)

> **GUIDANCE REVISION:** Top end of guidance lowered primarily due to Valesco venture losses. Management stated: "we have lowered the top side of our earnings guidance for the year to reflect the effects of these losses."

| Metric | Updated FY 2023 Guidance (Q3 Call) |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $63.5 billion - $64.5 billion |
| **Net Income Attributable to HCA Healthcare** | $4.94 billion - $5.13 billion |
| **Adjusted EBITDA** | $12.3 billion - $12.6 billion |
| **Diluted EPS** | $17.80 - $18.50 |
| **Capital Spending** | approximately $4.7 billion |

### Notable Items Affecting 2023 Adjusted EBITDA (Context for Year-Over-Year Comparisons)
- **2022 Items Not Recurring in 2023:** COVID support payments, out-of-period Texas waiver payment, and 340B impact -- all totaled approximately $500 million (discussed at January year-end call)
- **2023 Q1 Favorable Item:** $145 million payer settlement recorded in Q1 2023
- **Valesco Venture Impact:** ~$100 million negative impact on adjusted EBITDA YTD (through Q3); expected ~$50 million per quarter going forward, meaning less pressure in Q4 than Q3

## Forward Outlook & Qualitative Commentary

### Valesco Venture -- Forward Expectations
- **Expected Quarterly Loss Run Rate:** ~$50 million per quarter going forward
- **Q4 2023 Expectation:** less pressure than Q3 (management: "including less pressure for the company in the fourth quarter")
- **2024 Implication:** 3 quarters of Valesco losses in 2023 vs. 4 quarters in 2024; management confirmed analyst framing that this creates an incremental ~$50 million headwind in 2024
- **Primary Issue:** revenue shortfalls vs. original model -- claims clearing at lower rates than anticipated
- **Mitigation Strategies:** program adjustments, cost structure reductions, payer reimbursement negotiations, revenue cycle management (contracting, coding, billing, collections now fully managed internally), staffing allocation optimization, overhead reduction
- **Revenue Cycle:** HCA now manages the full revenue cycle for Valesco from contracting to coding to billing and collections

### Professional Fees (Ex-Valesco)
- **Growth Rate:** expected to slow going forward vs. ~20% YTD growth
- **Subsidy Requests:** continuing to increase from contracted hospital-based providers
- **Anesthesia:** noted as a particular pressure point

### Labor Environment
- **Cost Per Hour:** in line with full-year expectations; stabilized
- **California Minimum Wage Impact:** "very de minimis" impact on HCA; most compensation already in line
- **Unionization:** HCA has been "successful in pushing through those issues organizationally"
- **New Graduate Training Programs:** investment creating a "little bit of a headwind" in Q3 and throughout the year; expected to benefit Q4 and into 2024
- **Galen College of Nursing:** encouraging enrollment pipeline of new nursing students

### Long-Term Margin Target
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin Range:** 19% to 20% described as "fairly sustainable" (management confirmed confidence in maintaining this)

### Demand Environment
- **Healthcare Demand:** remains strong; expected to remain strong driven by population trends, aging baby boomers, chronic conditions
- **GLP-1 Impact:** "way too early" to judge impact on business; bariatric surgeries less than 0.5% of overall revenue; diversified revenue mix cited as mitigating factor
- **Volume Breadth:** every division had admission growth and adjusted admission growth; every service category had growth except obstetrics (births down slightly), pediatrics (down slightly), behavioral (down due to capacity reallocation, not demand)
- **Calendar Impact:** 1 fewer surgical day in Q3 vs. prior year (headwind to surgery and cardiology procedure volumes)

### ER Revitalization Program
- **Patient Satisfaction:** up 4-5 points since program began
- **ER Throughput:** seeing ER patient within 9-10 minutes with a clinician upon presentation
- **Discharge Throughput:** improved
- **Admission Throughput:** more efficient floor transfers for admitted patients

### Upcoming Events
- **Investor Day:** November 9, 2023 -- will provide early perspectives on 2024, longer-term growth targets, and more detail on company strategy

### 2024 Preview Items (Flagged on Call)
- **Known Headwind:** $145 million Q1 2023 payer settlement (non-recurring in 2024)
- **Known Headwind:** 4 quarters of Valesco losses in 2024 vs. 3 quarters in 2023 (incremental ~$50 million)
- **Nevada UPL Program:** new program being introduced; "still too early" for HCA to size the incremental benefit; awaiting approval level
- **Professional Fee Growth:** expected to decelerate but not yet quantified
- **Volume Trends:** management expressed confidence in durability of current demand and competitive positioning trends

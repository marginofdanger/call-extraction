# HCA Healthcare Q2 2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 23, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2024
**Speakers:** Samuel N. Hazen (CEO), Michael A. Marks (CFO), Frank George Morgan (VP IR)

---

## Q2 2024 Actuals (Reported)

### Earnings
- **Diluted EPS (as adjusted):** $5.50, up **28%** year-over-year

### Same-Facility Volume Metrics (Q2 2024 vs. Prior Year)
- **Inpatient admissions:** +5.8%
- **Equivalent admissions:** +5.2%
- **Emergency room visits:** +5.5%
- **Inpatient surgeries:** +2.6%
- **Outpatient surgery cases:** -2.0% (declines "mostly explained by lower volumes in Medicaid and self-pay categories")
- **Commercial volumes as % of equivalent admissions:** 36.2% (improved year-over-year)
- **Case mix index:** increased slightly vs. prior year
- **Same-facility revenue growth:** +10%

### Volume by Payer (Q2 2024 vs. Prior Year, Same-Facility Equivalent Admissions)
- **Managed care (including exchange volumes):** +12.5%
- **Managed care (excluding exchanges):** up just short of 5%
- **Healthcare exchange volumes:** +46%
- **Medicare volumes:** +6.5%
- **Medicaid volumes:** -10% on equivalent admissions ("mostly related to Medicaid redeterminations")
- **Exchange admissions as % of total admissions:** ~7%
- **Exchange ER visits as % of total ER visits:** ~7%
- **Exchange revenue as % of total revenues:** ~8%-9%

### Outpatient Surgery Detail (Q2 2024 vs. Prior Year)
- **Hospital outpatient same-store:** down a little over 2%
- **ASC same-store:** down a little over 1%
- **Weighted total:** approximately -2%
- Declines "exclusively in Medicaid and uninsured" categories
- **Same-store ASC revenue growth:** ~8%

### Operating Costs & Margins (Q2 2024)
- **Margin improvement:** +100 basis points vs. prior year AND sequentially vs. Q1 2024
- **Labor cost as % of revenue:** improved 200 basis points from prior year
- **Contract labor:** declined 25.7% from prior year
- **Contract labor as % of total labor costs (SWB):** 4.8% (vs. 6.8% in Q2 2023, vs. ~10% at COVID peak early 2022)
- **Supply costs as % of revenue:** improved 50 basis points from prior year
- **Other operating costs as % of revenue:** grew vs. prior year but "remained relatively consistent for the past 4 quarters"
- **Professional fee cost growth (same-facility, YoY):** ~13% in Q2 (vs. 20% increase in Q1 2024)
- **Wage inflation:** stable, running in the 2.5% to 3% range as expected
- **Length of stay:** down 2% year-over-year (year-to-date)

### Adjusted EBITDA
- **Q2 2024 Adjusted EBITDA:** $3.55 billion, +16% vs. prior year
- Included "a modest benefit from Medicaid supplemental payments"

### Medicaid Supplemental Payments
- **Year-over-year earnings increase from Medicaid supplemental programs in Q2:** ~$125 million
- Driven primarily by the **new program in Nevada** and the **accrual of the Florida program** (began Q4 2023)
- **Tennessee program:** with CMS for review; no financial impact anticipated in 2024

### Capital Allocation (Q2 2024)
- **Cash flow from operations:** just under $2 billion (decline of $500 million vs. prior year, driven by increased tax payments and timing of Medicaid supplemental program accruals/cash receipts)
- **Capital expenditures:** $1.28 billion
- **Share repurchases:** $1.37 billion
- **Dividends paid:** ~$170 million
- **Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage:** "remains near the low end of our stated guidance range"

### Capacity & Network
- **Inpatient bed capacity:** up 2% year-over-year
- **Inpatient occupancy:** "low to mid-70s" in Q2
- **Outpatient facilities & clinics:** ~2,600 total, up 5% from prior year
- **Inpatient occupancy levels:** up 2% year-over-year

### M&A / New Stores (Q2 2024)
- **New store revenue in Q2:** ~$400 million
  - ~$250 million from **Valesco**
  - Remainder from acquisitions in Texas (Wise Healthcare System and others)
- **M&A impact on EBITDA:** ~1% dilutive in Q2
- Valesco moves into same-store in 2025

### ER Revitalization Metrics
- **Commercial ER volume growth:** ~18% year-over-year
- **Time to see a patient:** down 2 minutes (from 11 to 9 minutes)
- **Length of stay for discharged ER patients:** down ~15%-20%, to ~160 minutes
- **ER hold time for admitted patients:** "improved markedly on a year-over-year basis"
- **Patient satisfaction:** "over 8 out of 10 patients would highly recommend or recommend an HCA emergency room"

### Exchange Payer Characterization
- Exchange is "second best payer" -- reimbursement below commercial, above Medicare
- Exchange margins: less than typical commercial margins, but better than Medicare

## Full Year 2024 Guidance -- Key Assumptions & Outlook

### Volume
- **Expected volume growth for the year:** 4% to 6% range (equivalent admissions)

### Labor & Operating Costs (Back Half 2024)
- **SWB, supplies, and other operating expenses as % of revenue:** expected to "run mostly where we did June year-to-date"
- **Contract labor as % of SWB (back half 2024):** projected "roughly in the mid-4% range"
- **Professional fee expense growth (YoY):** expected to "moderate a bit more in the back half of 2024"
- **Wage inflation:** stable at 2.5% to 3% range (unchanged from original expectation)

### Medicaid Supplemental Payment Programs -- GUIDANCE REVERSAL
- **Prior guidance (start of year):** anticipated a **headwind of $100 million to $200 million** from Medicaid supplemental programs
- **Updated guidance:** now anticipates an approximate **$100 million to $200 million tailwind** from Medicaid supplemental programs
- **Net swing:** $200 million to $400 million favorable change
- "Most of which occurred in the first half of 2024"
- **Back half of 2024 Medicaid supplemental payments** expected to "look pretty similar to what we had in the back half of 2023"
- **Tennessee Medicaid supplemental program:** no financial impact expected in 2024

### M&A Impact (Full Year Expectation)
- M&A dilution expected to improve in back half; "hopeful that by the end of the fourth quarter, it's not dilutive"

## Special Topics

### Healthcare Exchange / ACA Subsidies
- Enhanced ACA subsidies scheduled to expire at end of 2025
- Management stated it is "a little premature for us to forecast what's going to happen politically"
- Company does not have "a great line of sight on which participants in the exchange have what level of subsidies"
- Planning to study further in 2025; committed to transparency once information is available

### Medicaid Redeterminations
- ~1 year into the redetermination process in most HCA states
- Redeterminations "really started gaining speed towards the end of last year"
- Full anniversary of Medicaid year-over-year comparison not expected "until you get closer to the end of the year"
- Thesis (unproven): patients who migrated from Medicaid to exchanges may have "different seasonality" for accessing services; if true, outpatient surgery should be "better in the second half of the year"

### Medicaid Supplemental Program Sustainability
- Management sees "good sustainability around the Medicaid supplemental payment programs"
- "Well supported historically, both in red states and blue states"
- Two biggest programs are in **Texas and Florida**
- New CMS rule on sustainable Medicaid supplemental programs found to be "positive and supportive and actually good for the provider industry"
- Even with supplemental payments, historical base Medicaid reimbursement plus supplemental payments are "still pretty well short of the cost of caring for those patients"

### Long-Term Demand Outlook
- Long-term demand expected to be in the **2% to 3% range** (per Investor Day, November 2023)
- Coverage expansion supports demand: "when people are covered...they tend to purchase services"
- "Unchartered territories for growth in demand in a normal environment"

### Capital Strategy & Network Development
- ~50% of CapEx goes toward maintenance
- Technology component of CapEx "continues to grow"
- Company "built to be bigger" with balance sheet to support M&A, but "very selective"
- Focus on in-market tuck-in acquisitions rather than large national expansion
- Open to entering new markets but "those opportunities haven't necessarily presented themselves"

### Operational Performance Characterization
- CEO: First 6 months represent "core operations" with no "unusual items benefiting or dragging the business in any material way"
- Performance described as "really quite clean" compared to prior years with COVID choppiness, supplemental payment timing issues, and Valesco integration challenges

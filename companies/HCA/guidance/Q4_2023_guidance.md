# HCA Healthcare Q4_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** January 30, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 2023 (Full Year 2023)
**Source:** HCA Healthcare, Inc. FQ4 2023 Earnings Call Transcript
**Presenters:** Samuel N. Hazen (CEO), William B. Rutherford (CFO)

---

## Q4 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Earnings
- **Diluted EPS (excl. gains on sales):** $5.90, representing a **27% increase** over prior year (Q4 2022)

### Same-Facility Volume Metrics (Q4 2023 vs. Q4 2022)
- **Admissions growth:** +3% year-over-year
- **Equivalent admissions growth:** +4% year-over-year
- **Emergency room visits growth:** +2% year-over-year
- **Inpatient and outpatient surgery volumes:** increased approximately +1%
- **Cardiac procedures:** solid growth (mid-single digits for FY -- see below)
- **Rehab admissions:** solid growth metrics
- **All domestic divisions** had equivalent admissions growth in the quarter

### Revenue (Q4 2023)
- **Same-facility revenue growth:** +11% year-over-year
- **Payer mix:** improved year-over-year
- **Acuity levels:** improved year-over-year

### Adjusted EBITDA (Q4 2023)
- **Adjusted EBITDA growth:** just under +14% year-over-year
- **Supplemental payment programs:** year-over-year adjusted EBITDA increase of approximately **$250 million** related to supplemental payment programs (includes new North Carolina program and favorable adjustments within the Texas program)
- **Florida program:** began accruing quarterly (previously recognized on annual lump sum basis)
- **New/divested facilities impact:** recently acquired entities and divested facilities resulted in about **$90 million less** adjusted EBITDA in the quarter, with roughly **half (~$45 million)** of this decline from the Valesco joint venture

### Operating Margins (Q4 2023)
- **Operating margins improved** in the quarter vs. prior year
- **Sequential improvement** from Q3 to Q4 was described as "the best I've seen" (CEO) -- strongest operational performance of the year

### Contract Labor (Q4 2023)
- **Contract labor as % of SWB:** 5.3% in Q4 2023

### Medicare / Medicare Advantage (Q4 2023)
- **Medicare Advantage admissions:** roughly up **+10%** in the quarter, consistent with trends throughout the year
- **Revenue per admit (Medicare Advantage vs. traditional Medicare):** very consistent through the year, no material step change in Q4
- **COVID admissions:** roughly **2% of total admissions**, very stable throughout the year

### Valesco Joint Venture (Q4 2023)
- **Operating loss:** reduced in Q4, "more in line with expectations"
- **Quarterly run rate:** just under **$50 million** per quarter (loss)

### Patient Acceptance / Capacity (Q4 2023 / Year-End)
- **Patient acceptance rate:** ~90% at year-end (i.e., ~10% of referred patients could not be accepted)
- Improved throughout the year from mid-to-high 80s% in H1 to slightly better in H2
- Still below 2019 levels, but more patients coming through transfer centers than in 2019

### Occupancy
- **Inpatient hospital occupancy:** low 70%, described as "pretty high" -- up over pre-pandemic levels even with additional beds added

## Full Year 2023 Actuals

### Same-Facility Volume Metrics (FY 2023 vs. FY 2022)
- **Admissions growth:** +3.3% year-over-year
- **Equivalent admissions growth:** +4.8% year-over-year
- **Emergency room visits growth:** +4.7% year-over-year
- **Total surgical cases growth:** +2.3% year-over-year
- **Inpatient surgeries:** +2% for the year
- **Outpatient surgeries:** +2.5% for the year (slight migration to outpatient setting)
- **Cardiac volumes:** growing in the **mid-single digits**
- **Case mix index:** increasing (strong acuity trends maintained)

### Payer Mix (FY 2023)
- **Managed care and other admissions:** +6% year-over-year on a same-facility basis

### Revenue (FY 2023)
- **Revenue per equivalent admission growth (same-facility):** +2.7%
- **Same-facility revenue growth:** +7.6%
- **Consolidated revenue growth:** +7.9%

### Diluted EPS (FY 2023)
- **Diluted EPS (excl. gains/losses on sales and debt retirement):** grew almost **+13%** vs. 2022

### Adjusted EBITDA (FY 2023)
- **Adjusted EBITDA growth (as-reported):** +5.5% year-over-year
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin (consolidated):** **19.6%**, in line with range of expectations

### Cost Management (FY 2023)
- **SWB as % of revenue:** improved **60 basis points** on a consolidated basis vs. prior year
- **Contract labor:** declined **-20%** for the year
- **Supply costs as % of revenue:** improved **40 basis points** vs. prior year
- **Professional fees growth:** ~15% to 20% for FY 2023 (see Q&A -- guidance for 2024 targets 8%-10%)

### Valesco Joint Venture (FY 2023)
- **Total negative EBITDA:** approximately **$150 million** for FY 2023 (9 months of ownership, acquired April 2023)
- Q4 quarterly run rate: just under $50 million loss

### Capital Allocation (FY 2023)
- **Cash flow from operations:** **$9.4 billion** (vs. $8.5 billion in 2022, ~11% growth)
- **Capital expenditures:** just above **$4.7 billion** (in line with expectations)
- **Share repurchases:** approximately **$3.8 billion**
- **Dividends paid:** approximately **$660 million**
- **Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage:** near the **low end** of stated 3x to 4x range

### Medicare Admissions Historical Trend (Same-Facility, Composite Medicare + MA)
- **2019:** +2.6%
- **2021:** +2.1%
- **2022:** +3.4%
- **2023:** +4.0%

### Adjusted Admissions Historical Trend (Same Payer Combination)
- **2019:** +3.7%
- **2021:** +5.3%
- **2022:** +4.7%
- **2023:** +5.7%

### Supplemental Payment Programs
- **Number of states with supplemental payment programs:** 17 or 18
- **New programs recognized in 2023:** North Carolina program, Florida accrual began in Q4
- **Texas program:** certain favorable settlements recognized

### Wage Inflation
- **Expected wage inflation:** generally **2.5% to 3%** going forward (return to normal trends)

- **Professional fees growth target (2024):** **8% to 10%** (vs. 15%-20% in FY 2023) -- a targeted step change improvement

### Contract Labor
- **Continued improvement expected** in contract labor through 2024 (specific target not quantified)

### Capital Return / Shareholder Actions
- **New share repurchase authorization:** **$6 billion** (in addition to ~$300 million remaining on prior authorization; total ~$6.3 billion available)
- **Share repurchase plan for 2024:** around **$5 billion** (per CEO prepared remarks)
- **Quarterly dividend increase:** from **$0.60 to $0.66 per share** (10% increase)

### Two Midnight Rule (2024 Impact)
- Rule effective January 2024
- **Not material amount factored into 2024 guidance**
- Expected to be a **moderate positive** over time; could provide **modest benefit** as implementation ramps through 2024
- Range of guidance accommodates various outcomes

### Medicaid Redeterminations (2024 Impact)
- **Modest benefit** seen, **not material** yet
- **~30% to 35%** of individuals who lost Medicaid coverage showing up with either HICS (Health Insurance Exchange) or employer-sponsored coverage
- "Decently large number" being reapplied to Medicaid (redetermined off for technical reasons)
- Range of guidance allows for various outcomes; potential for continued benefit into 2024-2025

### COVID Impact (2024)
- **COVID admissions:** ~2% of total admissions as of Q4 2023, very stable, not a material factor

## Special Topics

### CFO Transition
- **William B. Rutherford** retiring after 34 years with HCA (10 years as CFO)
- **Mike Marks** named as next CFO; 28 years with HCA, former SVP of Financial Operations, previously led National Group for 10 years

### Strategic Investment Priorities (2024)
1. **Network expansion:** facilities, services, workforce to meet demand growth and increase market share; over $2 billion new capital projects scheduled; newly acquired hospitals and outpatient facilities to be integrated
2. **Digital capabilities / technology:** advancing digital tools, care transformation and innovation program, financial resiliency program, revenue cycle integration, case management improvements, automation and benchmarking
3. **Balance sheet flexibility:** capital spending over $5 billion; share repurchases around $5 billion; strong cash flow supports dual investment in business and shareholder returns

### Key Operational Initiatives
- **Case management initiative:** length of stay was down in 2023, case mix modestly up -- described as "early-stage success"
- **Internal benchmarking initiative:** comparing deeply across organization on variable costs and fixed costs under Mike Marks' leadership
- **Financial resiliency program:** multiple components including revenue cycle integration, process improvement, technology and automation
- **Galen College of Nursing / Centers for Clinical Advancement:** continued expansion as part of workforce development investment

### Health Insurance Exchange (HIX/HICS) Enrollment
- Strong enrollment in health insurance exchanges across HCA states noted as a favorable development for 2024

### Capital Returns on CapEx
- **Returns on capital projects:** upper teens percentage returns
- Outpatient facilities: short-cycle, efficient capital allocation with quick returns
- Hospital investments: long-lived assets with longer return cycle
- Disciplined look-back process to validate assumptions

### Surgical Growth Outlook (Q&A)
- Q4 surgery slightly slower than year-to-date (calendar effect in December affecting outpatient)
- No structural change expected; investment continuing in both outpatient and inpatient programs
- Ambulatory surgery centers: several opened or opening in 2024; some acquisitions made

### Supplemental Payment Program Detail (Q&A)
- Programs are complex with multiple activities affecting timing and amounts
- **Key Q4 2023 items:** new North Carolina program recognized; Texas settlement; Florida accrual began
- **2024 headwind ($100M-$200M):** driven by non-recurring 2023 settlements and Florida accrual timing change
- **New 2024 program:** Nevada

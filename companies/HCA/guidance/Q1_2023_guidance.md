# HCA Healthcare Q1 2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** April 21, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2023 (FQ1 2023)
**Speakers:** Samuel N. Hazen (CEO), William B. Rutherford (CFO), Frank George Morgan (VP IR)

---

## Q1 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Earnings & Profitability
- **Diluted EPS (excl. losses on sales of facilities):** $4.93 (grew ~20% year-over-year)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $3.17 billion (vs. $2.94 billion prior year; grew ~8% year-over-year)
- **Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 20.3%
- **EBITDA Clearance Rate:** 36% (described by CEO as "a really good metric")

### Revenue & Payer Items
- **Same-Facility Revenue Growth:** 5% year-over-year
- **Commercial Payer Settlement (one-time):** $145 million increase in revenues related to resolving disputed claims with a commercial payer covering a 6-year period
- **Prior Year Texas-Directed Payment Program (Q1 2022 comparison item):** $244 million of revenues and $90 million of expenses recognized in Q1 2022
- **COVID-Related Support Payments:** ~$30 million in Q1 2023 vs. ~$190 million in Q1 2022

### Volume Metrics (Same-Facility Basis)
- **Admissions Growth:** +4.4% year-over-year
- **Non-COVID Admissions Growth:** +12% year-over-year
- **Equivalent Admissions Growth:** +7.5% year-over-year
- **Emergency Room Visits Growth:** +10% year-over-year
- **Inpatient Surgeries Growth:** +3.6% year-over-year
- **Outpatient Surgeries Growth:** +5% year-over-year
- **Outpatient Cardiology Procedures Growth:** +7% year-over-year
- **COVID Admissions as % of Total:** ~3% in Q1 2023 vs. 9.7% in Q1 2022

### Payer Mix & Revenue Per Unit
- **Same-Facility Managed Care and Other Admissions Growth:** +4.2% year-over-year
- **Non-COVID Managed Care Admissions Growth:** +11.3% year-over-year
- **Non-COVID Case Mix Improvement:** just under 1% vs. prior year and sequentially from Q4 2022
- **Non-COVID Inpatient Revenue Per Admission Growth:** +2.2% year-over-year
- **Exchange Volume Growth:** ~19% year-over-year
- **Florida Exchange Enrollment Growth:** +18%
- **Texas Exchange Enrollment Growth:** ~30%

### Labor & Staffing
- **Contract Labor Cost Reduction:** ~20-21% year-over-year
- **Contract Labor as % of SWB:** 7.1% in Q1 2023 (vs. 9.5% in Q1 2022; vs. mid-7s% in H2 2022)
- **Contract Labor as % of Hours:** 10.3% in Q1 2023 (vs. ~11.5-11.6% in Q1 2022)
- **Nursing Turnover Rate (last 6 months annualized):** ~17% (vs. pre-pandemic levels of ~14.5-15% in 2018-2019)
- **Registered Nurse Hiring Growth:** +~19% vs. previous 4-quarter average
- **Total Head Count Growth:** ~3% year-over-year (after adjusting for divestitures; ~2% unadjusted)
- **Overall Hiring Growth:** ~13% year-over-year; ~19% vs. running average in the quarter (mostly nursing)
- **Labor Costs as % of Revenue:** improved both sequentially and year-over-year
- **Other Operating Expenses as % of Revenue:** increased ~20 basis points year-over-year (inflationary pressures in professional fees and fixed cost items)

### Capacity Metrics
- **Inpatient Occupancy Rate:** ~73-74%
- **Capacity Declination Rate (unable to accept transfer patients):** 1.5% of total admissions in Q1 2023 (down from 2.0% in Q4 2022; declined 25% vs. Q4 2022; still above pre-pandemic levels)
- **San Antonio Market Occupancy:** ~90%

### Cash Flow & Capital Allocation
- **Cash Flow from Operations:** $1.8 billion (up $458 million from $1.35 billion in Q1 2022)
- **Capital Spending:** just under $1.2 billion in Q1
- **Dividends Paid:** $175 million
- **Share Repurchases:** just under $850 million
- **Debt to Adjusted EBITDA Leverage Ratio:** near the low end of stated 3x to 4x range

### Outpatient Procedure Mix (Total Joints)
- **Total Joint Surgeries -- Outpatient Setting:** ~80% of total joints now done outpatient (reversed from pre-pandemic when ~80% were inpatient)
- **Total Joint Volumes:** up year-over-year (and were up prior year vs. year before)

### Network & Facilities
- **Hospital Count:** ~180 hospitals
- **Outpatient Facilities:** ~2,500

## Full Year 2023 Guidance (Updated / Raised)

> **Note:** Guidance was raised from the initial FY2023 guidance provided on the Q4 2022 call. The raise was described as principally reflecting the strong Q1 2023 performance. Below are the UPDATED ranges as provided on this Q1 2023 call.

| Metric | Updated FY 2023 Guidance (Q1 Call) |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $62.5 billion - $64.5 billion |
| **Net Income Attributable to HCA Healthcare** | $4.75 billion - $5.16 billion |
| **Adjusted EBITDA** | $12.1 billion - $12.7 billion |
| **Diluted EPS** | $17.25 - $18.55 |
| **Capital Spending** | ~$4.6 billion |

### Guidance Commentary
- **Guidance Raise Drivers:** Strong Q1 demand trends, favorable volume momentum, continued improvement in operating costs (especially contract labor)
- **Capital Spending Increase Rationale:** (1) Growth investment opportunities including new hospital development and outpatient network expansion; (2) Land acquisitions for future development (Las Vegas, Salt Lake City, Austin TX, Dallas TX, multiple Florida markets); (3) Inflationary increases in construction costs factored in
- **Contract Labor Target (Year-End Exit Rate):** 6.5% to 7% of SWB (vs. 7.1% in Q1 2023; pre-COVID baseline was ~6%)
- **Compensation Per Hour:** trending in line with expectations for 2023

- **Closed:** early April 2023 (increased ownership interest in Valesco JV with Envision)
- **Consolidation Timing:** beginning Q2 2023
- **Expected Annual Revenue Contribution:** ~$1 billion
- **Expected Adjusted EBITDA Impact:** no material impact

## Commercial Contracting Cycle

- **Rate Increase Targets:** mid-single digits (vs. ~3.5% pre-pandemic)
- **2023 Contracting Status:** ~93% contracted
- **2024 Contracting Status:** ~2/3 (approximately 67%) completed (consistent with historical pacing)
- **2025 Contracting Status:** ~1/4 (approximately 25%) completed
- **Rate Environment Commentary:** "payers recognize the pressures in the marketplace for providers and are allowing sort of responsible increases" -- Sam Hazen

---

## Medicaid Redeterminations

- **Impact Observed to Date:** none as of call date (redeterminations just beginning)
- **Company Response:** formalized internal approach; outreach to Medicaid patients to help identify alternative coverage; working with states and community agencies
- **Management View:** third-party studies suggest a relatively high percentage of displaced individuals may qualify for employer-sponsored coverage or enhanced subsidy exchange coverage; "ultimately... we wouldn't really anticipate any material downside, and hopefully there could be some upside benefit" -- Bill Rutherford

---

## Surgical Volume Outlook

- **Historical Normalized Surgical Growth Rate:** typically 1% to 2% per year
- **Outlook Drivers:** (1) return to normal historical volume patterns; (2) continued investment in outpatient footprint; (3) program development on inpatient side; (4) improvement in staffing capacity allowing more OR rooms to open
- **Outpatient vs. HOPD Growth:** hospital outpatient department surgical volumes grew slightly more than ASC volumes in Q1

## Capacity & Network Development

### Land Acquisitions for Future Hospital Development
- **San Antonio, TX:** 2 hospitals under construction (market runs ~90% occupancy)
- **Austin, TX:** land owned for new hospitals
- **Dallas, TX:** land owned for new hospitals
- **Las Vegas, NV:** land purchased in Q1 2023
- **Salt Lake City, UT:** land purchased in Q1 2023
- **Multiple Florida markets:** land owned for new hospitals

### Capacity Constraints Commentary
- Declination rate improving (1.5% in Q1 vs. 2.0% in Q4 2022) but still above pre-pandemic levels
- March was better than January within Q1, suggesting improving intra-quarter trend
- Key variable for further improvement: staffing -- getting sufficient staff into facilities to open beds, surgical capacity, and ER capacity

## Q&A Key Quantitative Details

### On Volume Consistency Through Q1 (Steven Valiquette, Barclays)
- **Management Response:** volume was "pretty consistent" throughout Q1; guidance raise "principally" reflects Q1 performance; good demand continues

### On Surgical Growth Sustainability (Scott Fidel, Stephens)
- **Typical Surgical Growth:** 1-2% historically
- **Expectation:** continued growth in both inpatient and outpatient surgical volume as staffing capacity improves

### On Payer Mix / Exchange Volume (Jason Cassorla, Citigroup)
- **Non-COVID Commercial Volume Growth:** +11% (managed care)
- **Exchange Volume Growth:** ~19% year-over-year
- **Employment Backdrop:** "basically full employment in most of our markets"

### On Contract Labor Reinvestment (Sarah / Cantor Fitzgerald)
- **Strategy:** savings from reduced contract labor being reinvested into employed workforce (wages and hiring)
- **Pre-COVID Contract Labor Baseline:** ~6% of SWB
- **Year-End Target:** 6.5-7% of SWB

### On Commercial Rate Cycle (Stephen Baxter, Wells Fargo)
- **Current Renewal Rate Increases:** mid-single digits
- **Pre-Pandemic Renewal Rate:** ~3.5%
- **2024 Contracting Progress:** ~2/3 complete (consistent with historical norms)

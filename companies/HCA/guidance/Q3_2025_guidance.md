# HCA Healthcare Q3 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 24, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2025
**Participants:** Samuel N. Hazen (CEO), Michael A. Marks (CFO), Frank George Morgan (VP IR)

---

## Q3 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Headline Performance
- **Diluted EPS growth (as adjusted):** 42% year-over-year
- **Revenue growth:** 9.6% year-over-year

### Volume Metrics (Same-Facility, Q3 2025 vs. Prior Year)
- **Same-facility equivalent admissions:** +2.4% YoY
- **Same-facility inpatient surgical volume:** +1.4% YoY
- **Same-facility outpatient surgical volume:** +1.1% YoY
- **Same-facility ER visits:** +1.3% YoY
- **Commercial and Medicare ER visits combined:** +4.1% YoY
- **Medicaid ER visits:** down YoY
- **Self-pay ER visits:** down YoY
- **Respiratory season impact on admissions growth:** estimated 50 basis points drag YoY
- **Respiratory season impact on ER visit growth:** estimated 70 basis points drag YoY

### Payer Mix (Same-Facility Equivalent Admissions, Q3 2025 vs. Prior Year)
- **Total commercial equivalent admissions:** +3.7% YoY
- **Exchange equivalent admissions:** +8.0% YoY
- **Commercial excluding exchanges equivalent admissions:** +2.4% YoY
- **Medicare equivalent admissions:** +3.4% YoY
  - **Medicare Advantage:** +4.8% YoY
  - **Traditional Medicare:** +90 basis points YoY
- **Medicaid equivalent admissions:** +1.4% YoY (turned positive after being down for several quarters)
- **Self-pay equivalent admissions:** -6.0% YoY

### Revenue Per Equivalent Admission
- **Net revenue per equivalent admission growth drivers (Q3 2025 vs. prior year):**
  - Approximately half of the growth was related to state supplemental payment increases in revenue
  - Next largest driver was payer mix improvement
  - Case mix index up approximately 30 basis points YoY (down slightly sequentially from Q2 2025)
  - Dispute resolution activities provided some support
- **Medicare case mix index (Traditional):** up a bit YoY
- **Medicare Advantage case mix index:** pretty flat to prior year

### Medicaid State Supplemental Payment Programs
- **Net benefit to adjusted EBITDA increase (Q3 2025 vs. prior year):** approximately $240 million
- **Key state drivers:**
  - **Tennessee:** largest driver of net benefit in Q3; cash received in Q3 2025; began accruing the program
  - **Texas:** grandfathered application approved; enhancement to existing program; only 1 month of impact in Q3
  - **Kansas:** grandfathered application approved; cash received in Q3 2025; calendar year program, so 9 months of impact recorded in Q3 2025
- **Note:** Multiple other pluses and minuses across portfolio of programs led to the aggregate $240 million net benefit

### Labor and Expense Metrics
- **Contract labor (same-facility):** basically flat Q3 2025 vs. prior year
- **Contract labor as % of total labor costs:** 4.2% in Q3 2025
- **Other operating expenses as % of revenue:** increased YoY, driven primarily by Medicaid state supplemental payment expenses and, to a lesser extent, professional fees
- **Same-facility professional fees:** +11% YoY (Q3 2025 vs. Q3 2024); approximately +1% sequentially from Q2 2025
- **Professional fees as % of total other operating expenses (as-reported):** approximately 24%
- **Professional fee inflation drivers:** more related to anesthesia and radiology this year
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin:** improved YoY, driven primarily by good performance in labor and supplies
- **Length of stay management:** "really good performance" in Q3

### Capital Allocation (Q3 2025)
- **Cash flow from operations:** $4.4 billion in the quarter
- **Capital expenditures:** $1.3 billion in the quarter
- **Share repurchases:** $2.5 billion in the quarter
- **Dividends:** $166 million in the quarter
- **Federal income tax deferral (year-to-date):** approximately $1.3 billion deferred to Q4 (IRS relief for Tennessee taxpayers due to severe weather in early April)
- **Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage:** remained in the lower half of the stated guidance range

### Hurricane-Impacted Markets
- **2024 hurricane EBITDA impact (reference):** approximately $250 million total hit; approximately $50 million of that in Q3 2024
- **2025 hurricane market recovery trajectory:**
  - Q1 2025 vs. prior year: approximately flat
  - Q2 2025 vs. prior year: modestly negative
  - Q3 2025 vs. Q3 2024 combined: approximately flat
  - Year-to-date adjusted EBITDA in hurricane markets: modestly below prior year
- **North Carolina markets:** volumes have recovered, but payer mix has deteriorated and significant premium labor required to staff facilities

### Geographic Division Performance
- **Underperforming divisions (from Q2 call):** 2 of 16 geographic divisions had challenges
  - 1 of 2 has recovered in Q3
  - 1 of 2 still working through challenges; confident of resolution heading into 2026

### Surgical Volume Detail (Q3 2025)
- **Outpatient surgery -- strong areas:** general surgery, urological services
- **Outpatient surgery -- weaker areas:** gynecology (slightly down)
- **Inpatient surgery -- strong areas:** neurosciences, orthopedics, cardiac
- **Inpatient surgery -- weaker areas:** neurosurgery (down modestly)
- **Outpatient surgery payer mix:** Medicaid and self-pay volumes below prior year; commercial and Medicare "really strong"

## Full Year 2025 Guidance (RAISED)

### Revenue
- **New range:** $75.0 billion to $76.5 billion
- *(Prior range not explicitly stated on call; guidance raised as part of overall update)*

### Net Income Attributable to HCA Healthcare
- **New range:** $6.50 billion to $6.72 billion

### Adjusted EBITDA
- **New range:** $15.25 billion to $15.65 billion
- **Midpoint increase:** $450 million above prior guidance midpoint
  - **$250 million** from increased net benefit from state supplemental payment programs
  - **$200 million** from operational performance improvement

### Diluted Earnings Per Share
- **New range:** $27.00 to $28.00

### Capital Spending
- **Approximately $5 billion** (unchanged)

### Medicaid State Supplemental Payment Programs (Full Year 2025 vs. 2024)
- **Net benefit guidance:** $250 million to $350 million favorable (full-year 2025 vs. full-year 2024)
- **Q4 2025 expectation (at midpoint):** $120 million decline in net benefit from Medicaid state supplemental payments vs. Q4 2024, due to onetime payments in the prior year
- **Exclusion:** guidance does NOT include any potential impact from additional approvals of grandfathered applications under the Act
- **Pending grandfathered applications under CMS review:** Florida, Georgia, Virginia (among others)
  - CMS not expected to approve during government shutdown, but reviews are active
  - Several states had approvals coming into the shutdown

### Hurricane-Impacted Markets (Full Year 2025 Guidance)
- **Expected adjusted EBITDA growth vs. 2024:** approximately $100 million (unchanged from prior guidance)
- **All of this $100 million growth expected in Q4 2025**
- **Remaining unrecovered headwind vs. pre-storm run rate:** approximately $150 million (of the original $250 million hit)

## Q4 2025 Implied Guidance

- **Implied EBITDA growth rate (Q4 2025 vs. Q4 2024):** high single digits, "maybe 7% roughly" (after adjusting for hurricane impact and supplemental payment decline)
- **Sequential growth (Q3 to Q4):** "in line with past trends" after adjusting for hurricane and supplemental payment considerations

## Preliminary 2026 Outlook

### Volume
- **Expected volume growth:** within the long-term 2% to 3% growth range
- **18 consecutive quarters of volume growth** referenced as a foundation for confidence
- **Exchange volumes:** acknowledged as a small component of overall diversification; potential for flat-to-down if EPTCs not extended

### Operating Costs
- **Expected cost trends:** "mostly stable trends consistent with the past couple of years"
- **Management caveat:** "still early in next year's planning process, and these preliminary views may change before our fourth quarter earnings call"

### Capital
- **More capital coming online in 2026 than in 2025**, adding physical capacity

### Enhanced Premium Tax Credits (EPTCs)
- **24 million Americans** depend on the program for health insurance coverage
- HCA continues to advocate strongly for extension
- Impact not sized due to fluid federal policy environment
- Open enrollment period opening "in a couple of weeks" (from call date)
- Full sizing and impact to be provided on Q4 2025 earnings call

### Resiliency Program
- **Original target (from 2023 Investor Day):** $600 million to $800 million
- Some of those dollars helped in 2024 and 2025
- Program has been enhanced and accelerated over the last 12 to 18 months
- Additional commentary and quantification to be provided with 2026 full-year guidance on Q4 call

### Valesco (Physician Staffing Acquisition)
- **Historical EBITDA drag:** $40 million to $50 million per quarter (referenced by analyst)
- Management expects "continued financial improvement" carrying forward into 2026
- Budgets not yet finalized; no specific number provided
- Favorable progression in financial performance noted

### Capital Allocation
- No 2026 capital allocation plan disclosed
- "Reasonable assumption" that methodology will be "somewhat consistent" with past approaches

### Cash Flow Conversion
- **Historical trend (analyst reference):** EBITDA-to-free-cash-flow ratio moved from ~30% range to ~40s, on track for nearly 50% in 2025
- Management highlighted strong revenue cycle (Parallon), working capital management (net days in ARR, inventory, prepaids), and EBITDA growth as drivers
- Believed to be sustainable into the future

---

## Special Topics

### AI and Digital Transformation
- **Revenue cycle AI:** focused on responding to growing denial and underpayment activities from payers
- **Ambient AI documentation tools:** both piloting and rolling out; designed to help physicians be more complete, accurate, and timely in clinical documentation
- **Resiliency program AI:** robust series of use cases in flight for AI, machine learning, and automation
- **Case mix index / coding intensity:** "coding practices remain consistent and accurate as verified by multiple layers of audits"; no material changes in CMI at detailed line-of-insurance level

### Tariffs
- HealthTrust team working through "very diligent effort" to manage tariff risk
- Efforts include: sourcing adjustments, contract negotiations with vendor partners, moving products across countries of origin

### Capacity and Workforce
- No significant capacity constraints at this point in time (contrast with a couple of years ago when staffing constraints existed)
- Net headcount has improved
- More capital coming online in 2026 than 2025, adding physical capacity
- Length of stay management creating additional room for volume growth

### Government Shutdown Impact
- CMS not expected to approve additional grandfathered Medicaid supplemental payment applications during shutdown
- Reviews between CMS and states are active and continuing during shutdown
- Several states had approvals coming into the shutdown

## Q3 2022 Actuals (Reported)

### Income Statement / Profitability
- **Revenue:** $14,971 million (per S&P consensus table; implied from context)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $2.902 billion
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 19.4%
- **Diluted EPS:** $3.93, excluding losses on sale of facilities of $0.02

### Volume Metrics (Same-Facility Basis)
- **Same-Facility Admissions:** Down 1.5% year-over-year
- **COVID Admissions:** Down ~60% year-over-year; represented ~5% of admissions in Q3 2022 vs. ~13% in Q3 2021
- **Non-COVID Admissions:** Increased 6.9% year-over-year in the quarter; up 2.7% year-to-date
- **Non-COVID Admissions Year-to-Date:** Up ~3% (per CEO comments)
- **Capacity Constraints Impact:** Declined approximately 1% to 1.5% of total admissions due to capacity constraints (patients turned away)
- **COVID Admissions as % of Total:** ~5% of admissions in Q3 2022; ~3% in Q2 2022
- **Revenue per Equivalent Admission:** Generally comparable between Q2 and Q3 2022
- **Non-COVID Revenue per Adjusted Admission:** Up ~2 points year-over-year (excludes Florida DPP)
- **Outpatient Activity vs. 2019:** Up approximately 5% to 6% over Q3 2019
- **Inpatient Activity vs. 2019:** Down slightly (mostly explained by total joint migration to outpatient)

### Florida Directed Payment Program (DPP)
- **DPP Revenue Recorded:** ~$266 million in Q3 2022
- **DPP Expenses Recorded:** ~$125 million in Q3 2022
- **DPP Net Benefit:** ~$141 million (implied)
- **CMS Approval:** Received in September 2022 for annual period ending September 30, 2022

### Hurricane Ian Impact
- **Estimated Q3 Impact:** ~$35 million (hurricane made landfall September 28)
- **Facilities Affected:** 4 hospitals evacuated in Tampa-St. Pete area; 2 hospitals in Charlotte County hit hard
- **Operational Status:** All facilities except one fully operational; damaged facility expected fully operational by year-end
- **Emergency room and one med-surg floor already reopened at the most damaged facility**

### Labor Metrics
- **Contract Labor as % of SWB:** ~7.2% in Q3 2022 (also approximate exit rate for the quarter)
- **Contract Labor Reduction:** Down 19% sequentially from Q2 2022 (approximately half from reduced hourly rate, half from reduced utilization)
- **Contract Labor as % of SWB (earlier in 2022):** Hovering around 9%
- **SWB Cost Per Hour:** Flat Q3 vs. Q2 (first quarter of stabilization in composite labor cost per hour "in a while")
- **Average Hourly Rate Increases (Employed Workforce):** A little north of 4% Q3 vs. Q2 (annual wage increases given in Q3)
- **Contract Labor vs. 2019:** Still running with significantly more contract nurses than 2019 levels

### Cash Flow and Balance Sheet
- **Cash Flow from Operations:** $3.02 billion in Q3 2022
- **Capital Spending:** $1.13 billion in Q3 2022
- **Share Repurchases (Q3):** ~$700 million
- **Share Repurchases (Year-to-Date through Q3):** Just under $5.5 billion
- **Debt-to-Adjusted EBITDA Ratio:** Just above the low end of stated leverage range
- **Available Liquidity (End of Quarter):** ~$3.9 billion
- **Return on Invested Capital:** High teens (percent)

### Supply Costs
- **Supply Cost Trends:** Stable both sequentially (Q3 vs. Q2) and year-over-year
- **Supply Cost per Adjusted Patient:** Down year-over-year (per analyst commentary confirmed by management)

### Other Operating Expenses
- **Increased sequentially from Q2**, mostly due to Florida DPP expenses and increases in professional fees and utility costs

## Preliminary 2023 Outlook (Early Directional Commentary Only -- Formal Guidance Deferred to January)

### Volume / Demand
- **Demand Growth Expected:** ~1% to 2% in 2023 (vs. historical trend of 2% to 3%)
- **Outpatient Activity:** Expected to be a little stronger than inpatient (consistent with historical patterns)
- **COVID Normalization:** 1% to 2% demand growth is lower than historical 2% to 3% partly because 2022 still has some COVID admission activity not expected to repeat at the same level in 2023
- **Normalizing for COVID:** Growth "in the zone of historical trends"
- **Payer Mix:** Expected to remain stable
- **Acuity:** Expected to remain stable

### Managed Care Contracting
- **2023 Contracted:** ~70% contracted for 2023
- **2024 Contracted:** ~45% contracted for 2024
- **Rate Escalators:** Mid-single digits on new/renegotiated contracts (management: "generally in the target of where we had indicated previously... somewhere in the mid-single digits")
- **Escalator Structure (2024 Contracts):** Most inflators are static; some contracts have corridors/dynamic components tied to inflation
- **Elevated Escalators:** Seeing elevated escalators compared to historical trends on the commercial contract book

### Capital Expenditure
- **2023 CapEx:** Anticipated to stay materially in the range of ~$4.2 billion

### Florida DPP (2023)
- **Not anticipated to be a headwind** for 2023; annual program subject to annual CMS approval; management anticipates program will continue in its material form

### Public Health Emergency / Medicaid Redetermination
- **COVID DRG Add-On:** Not material at current COVID volume levels
- **Medicaid Redeterminations:** Will occur when PHE expires; HCA has evaluated and has planning exercises in place

### Why Full 2023 Guidance Was Deferred
- **Reason:** Uncertainty around inflation, particularly labor cost inflation; management wants 3 more months of performance data (9-10 months of normal run of business) to assess the environment and effectiveness of their cost response
- **Timeline:** Full 2023 guidance expected in early January with Q4 2022 results

## Q4 2022 Expectations (Commentary from Q&A)

### COVID Volumes
- **Q3 2022 COVID Admissions:** ~5% of total admissions
- **Q2 2022 COVID Admissions:** ~3% of total admissions
- **Q4 2022 COVID Admissions (CFO intuition):** "Somewhere between" the Q2 (3%) and Q3 (5%) levels

### Seasonality
- **Normal seasonal patterns beginning to emerge;** typically yields more outpatient surgery activity in Q4 vs. Q3
- **Normal patterns also on surgeries, cardiac procedures, and other aspects of business**

### Labor
- **Contract Labor as % of SWB:** Management believes they can stay in the ~7% range for the balance of 2022
- **Incremental improvement expected in utilization** as recruitment and retention continue to improve

### Flu Season
- **No specific flu projections made;** management paying attention to flu volumes and anticipating a potentially busy flu season
- **Does not necessarily change trajectory of Q4** compared to previous expectations

### Hurricane Ian (Q4 Impact)
- **Ongoing remediation;** one facility expected to be fully operational by year-end
- **No specific Q4 dollar impact quantified on the call**

## Strategic / Qualitative Themes

### Four Key Themes from Q3 (CEO)
1. **Top line metrics are stable;** normal seasonality patterns beginning to emerge
2. **Making progress on human resource agenda;** engagement levels are up
3. **Inflation is real** -- a pressure point, but being managed through resiliency agenda and other initiatives
4. **Long-term growth prospects exist across the portfolio**

### Workforce Initiatives
- **Galen College of Nursing:** Opened one more campus in Q3
- **Investments producing:** Improved retention, more new hires, reduced contract labor
- **Engagement Survey Results:** Very positive ("just came in")
- **Recruitment capabilities:** Significantly invested in; improved recruitment processing, better applicant experience, better management experience

### Length of Stay / Case Mix
- **Length of stay:** Up over 2019 levels
- **Case mix:** Up even more than length of stay over 2019
- **Case mix-adjusted length of stay:** Actually down vs. 2019 (favorable)
- **Case management agenda:** Continuing to make incremental progress

### Recession Resilience
- **Affordable Care Act / Exchanges:** Provide a safety net not available in prior recessionary cycles; viewed as a positive
- **Healthcare demand:** Tends to lag the rest of the economy
- **HCA Markets:** Florida, Texas, Nashville, Las Vegas described as "fairly strong durable economies" potentially stronger than the nation as a whole
- **Balance Sheet:** Strong; should create opportunities to invest and gain market share

### Payer Mix / Acuity
- **Acuity and payer mix:** Generally consistent between Q2 and Q3 2022
- **Non-COVID case mix trends:** Seeing some growth; stable overall
- **COVID case mix:** Runs higher than non-COVID (continues to be a compositional factor)
- **No evidence of low-acuity falloff** from consumer concerns about economy or COVID

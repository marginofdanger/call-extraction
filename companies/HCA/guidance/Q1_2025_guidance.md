# HCA Healthcare Q1 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** April 25, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2025
**Executives:** Samuel N. Hazen (CEO), Michael A. Marks (CFO), Frank George Morgan (VP IR)

---

## Q1 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Earnings
- **Diluted EPS (as adjusted):** $6.45; increased more than 20% year-over-year
- **Adjusted EBITDA growth:** +11.3% over prior year quarter
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin improvement:** +110 basis points year-over-year

### Volume (Same-Facility Basis)
- **Inpatient admissions growth:** +2.6% year-over-year
- **Equivalent admissions growth:** +2.8% year-over-year (as reported); ~3.8% adjusted for leap year effect
- **Emergency room visits growth:** +4% year-over-year
- **Inpatient surgeries:** slightly up year-over-year; up approximately 1% per business day (leap year adjusted)
- **Outpatient surgery cases:** down; same-facility decline of 2.1% as reported, approximately -1% on a per business day basis; decline driven by lower acuity cases and by Medicaid and self-pay
- **Cardiac procedures:** strong growth, both inpatient and outpatient surgeries
- **Rehabilitation admissions:** up over 4% on a same-store basis
- **Behavioral health:** down due to intentional repurposing of behavioral health beds to MedSurg
- **Obstetrics volumes:** up slightly even with business day decline
- **Inpatient surgeries as a percent of overall admissions:** down 50 basis points year-over-year (described as not meaningful)
- **Critical care admissions:** described as "at a really good position as it relates to total admissions"
- **Overall case mix:** modestly up

### Revenue
- **Same-facility revenue growth:** approximately 6%
- **Revenue per equivalent admission growth:** approximately 3% year-over-year (~2.9% per analyst reference)
- **Outpatient revenue growth:** grew at a rate a bit higher than inpatient revenue growth
- **All 4 outpatient revenue categories had growth:** emergency services, outpatient surgery, ambulatory (physician/urgent care clinics), and other hospital-based services (cardiology, diagnostics, observation)

### Payer Mix (Same-Facility Basis)
- **Managed care equivalent admissions:** +5.4% year-over-year (inclusive of exchanges)
  - **Exchange equivalent admissions:** +22.4% year-over-year
  - **Core employee-sponsored insurance (ex-exchange):** up about just short of 0.5% over prior year
- **Medicaid equivalent admissions:** -1.4% year-over-year (flattening as redetermination process sunset)
- **Exchange volume as percent of equivalent admissions:** approximately 8%
- **Exchange volume as percent of revenues:** approximately 10%
- **Medicare Advantage as percent of total Medicare admissions:** approximately 57%
- **Medicare Advantage observation mix:** approximately 15% higher than traditional Medicare observation mix (steady)
- **Medicare Advantage length of stay:** runs a bit higher than traditional Medicare

### Operating Costs & Margins
- **Salaries and benefits (SWB) as a percent of revenue:** improved 80 basis points year-over-year; at 43.6% (per analyst reference -- described as lowest since IPO)
- **Supplies as a percent of revenue:** improved 30 basis points year-over-year
- **Other operating expenses as a percent of revenue:** basically flat year-over-year
- **Contract labor:** improved 9.3% from prior year quarter; represented 4.4% of total labor costs in Q1 2025 vs. 5.1% in Q1 2024
- **Professional fee costs (same-facility):** increased 11% year-over-year; approximately flat sequentially vs. Q4 2024
- **Nursing and non-nursing turnover:** less than prior year
- **Employee engagement:** at a high watermark per most recent surveys

### Medicaid Supplemental Payments (DPP)
- **Net benefit increase in Q1 2025 vs. Q1 2024:** approximately $80 million increase year-over-year
- **Driver:** primarily a reconciliation payment in one state and a program accrual (began accruing in Q4 2024)
- **Tennessee:** nothing recorded in Q1 2025; back half of 2024 interim payment was not received; no CMS approval for 2025 calendar year program
- **CMS approvals:** Arizona and Nevada programs approved in recent weeks
- **Reminder:** after considering Medicaid state supplemental payments and related provider taxes, total Medicaid reimbursement does not cover HCA's cost of caring for Medicaid patients

### Hurricanes Impact
- **Q1 2025 hurricane-impacted market earnings:** flat year-over-year (North Carolina division and West Florida / Largo Medical Center)
- **Offset:** year-over-year earnings changes in the two impacted markets offset each other, producing no tailwind -- consistent with original guidance assumption

### Capital Allocation & Balance Sheet
- **Cash flow from operations:** $1.65 billion (down year-over-year due to working capital timing)
- **Capital expenditures:** $991 million (5.4% of revenue; described as lighter than typical; expect step-up remainder of year)
- **Share repurchases:** $2.5 billion in Q1 2025
- **Dividends:** $180 million
- **Acquisitions:** $227 million (Catholic Medical Center in Manchester, NH and Lehigh Medical Center in Fort Myers, FL area)
- **Proceeds from asset sales:** $161 million (primarily sale of Regional Medical Center of San Jose -- described as accretive portfolio optimization)
- **Debt to adjusted EBITDA leverage:** remains at the lower half of stated target range
- **Share repurchase authorization:** $10 billion; company anticipates completing a significant portion in 2025, subject to market conditions

### Network & Capacity
- **Facilities / sites of care:** increased 3.3% to approximately 2,750
- **Inpatient bed capacity added:** approximately 2%
- **Inpatient occupancy:** 77% in Q1 2025 vs. 75% in Q1 2024
- **Capital in approved construction/development pipeline:** approximately $6.2 billion (coming online in 2025, 2026, or first part of 2027)
- **Inpatient capacity pipeline:** roughly 2.5%+ greater than current levels

### Managed Care Contracting
- **2025 contracted status:** over 90% contracted
- **2026 contracted status:** over 75% contracted
- **2027 contracted status:** approximately 25% contracted
- **Contract rates:** described as "really similar to the last couple of years and in context of our targets"
- **Access to lives:** described as higher than almost ever, both commercial and exchange
- **New contracts:** Denver, CO -- now participating provider broadly with Kaiser Health Plan; Chattanooga, TN -- advanced position with Blue Cross of Tennessee on one product
- **Denials and underpayments:** did not have a material impact on Q1 financial results; company's strengthened response efforts are paying off

### Respiratory / Flu Season
- **Q1 2025 vs. Q4 2024:** respiratory volumes pretty significantly up sequentially
- **Q1 2025 vs. Q1 2024:** respiratory volumes pretty much in line, just a tick to prior year (Q1 2024 had strong respiratory season that started early in Q4 2023 and persisted through end of Q1 2024)

### Patient Receivables / Bad Debt
- **Historical patient balance increases (commercial):** mid-single-digit range annually
- **Recent quarters:** increases a little higher than mid-single digits, but not material; influenced by exchange volume growth (exchange patients have slightly higher patient responsibility than traditional commercial)
- **Collectability:** generally maintaining historical levels of collection on patient balances; exchange collections are a bit lower than employee-sponsored insurance but have not had a material impact on aggregate collectibility

## Full Year 2025 Guidance (REAFFIRMED)

**Status: REAFFIRMED as of Q1 2025 earnings call -- no changes to ranges**

*Note: Specific numerical guidance ranges for revenue, adjusted EBITDA, diluted EPS, and capital expenditures were not restated on this call. The CFO stated: "we did reaffirm guidance for 2025, it's first quarter. And really, at this point in the year, we believe our guidance ranges continue to be appropriate for where we are."*

### Key Guidance Items Discussed
- **Equivalent admissions growth target (full year):** 3% to 4% (referenced by analyst / confirmed by management as the assumption)
- **Capital expenditures:** expected $5 billion, possibly as high as $5.2 billion for the full year (expect step-up from Q1 pace)
- **Share repurchases:** anticipate completing a significant portion of the $10 billion authorization in 2025, subject to market conditions
- **Wage inflation:** guidance around wages for 2025 expected to hold and be somewhere close to what already indicated

### Medicaid Supplemental Payments (DPP) -- UPDATED RANGE
- **Previous assumption (from Q4 2024 call):** flat to down $250 million year-over-year for full year 2025 vs. 2024
- **UPDATED assumption (as of Q1 2025 call):** $50 million improvement to a $200 million decline (i.e., range from +$50M to -$200M year-over-year)
- **Range driver:** largely associated with whether or not Tennessee program gets CMS approval
- **Original expectation:** first half of year would be flat with potential declines primarily in back half; Q1 outcome was a bit better than expected

### Hurricane Impact (Full Year)
- **Assumption:** impacts of the 2024 hurricanes would offset each other in 2025 and not produce a tailwind (unchanged)

## Special Topics

### Tariff Risk / Supply Chain
- **Environment:** described as "extremely fluid" and "dynamic"; company not comfortable providing estimates at this time
- **2025 finished goods supply expense with firm pricing:** approximately 70% contracted with firm pricing for 2025
- **2026 firm pricing:** upwards of 60% for some/all of 2026
- **US/Canada/Mexico or exempt products (e.g., pharmaceuticals):** 75% of supply expense comes from these sources or products with broad tariff exemptions
- **HealthTrust (supply chain organization):** working on fixed price contracting, supply chain mapping, risk assessments, rationalizing suppliers and products, working with key suppliers to dederisk and diversify supply chains away from China
- **Management view:** "tariff risk for 2025 is manageable"

### Federal Policy Environment / Medicaid / Exchange Subsidies
- **Characterization:** "very fluid situation"; no specifics on administration priorities; unclear how efforts might be carried out
- **Company position:** supports reasonable reforms; does not support reforms that harm coverage for families/individuals or compromise hospitals' ability to care for patients
- **Enhanced Premium Tax Credits (EPTC):** unknown whether they will sunset; company not in a position to size impact
  - **Exchange enrollment in HCA states:** grew approximately 12% over prior year in 2025
  - **National exchange enrollment:** up to 24 million lives covered across the US
  - **If EPTC sunset:** management generally agrees some would return to employer-sponsored insurance, some would drop a metal tier and stay on exchanges, and others would become uninsured
- **Executive order on hospital drug acquisition costs (HOPDs):** company aware; waiting for draft rules and public comment period before assessing impact
- **Medicaid:** redetermination process has sunset; Medicaid volumes flattening
- **Recession risk to demand:** management stated "I don't think we are seeing anything currently that suggests that the demand assumptions that we have are wrong"; value-based care, co-pays, and deductibles are not disrupting the demand curve
- **Planning approach:** drawing from COVID-19 pandemic experience; considering adjustments to operations and utilizing flexibility of cash flow and balance sheet; maintaining long-term horizon

### Technology / AI / Digital Transformation
- **New function:** Digital Transformation and Innovation Group established
- **Three focus areas:**
  1. **Administrative:** Parallon services, supply chain, HR -- early tools being developed and implemented
  2. **Operational:** staffing/scheduling, case management, length of stay management, prior authorizations -- tools being deployed
  3. **Clinical:** slowly moving into this space; working on obstetrics (labor and delivery process improvement with a partner); early signs of success but very early stage
- **Investment level:** described as "investing heavily in our tech agenda"

### Competitive Environment
- **Current state:** no substantial changes in competitor behavior observed
- **Potential:** if NIH funding remains challenging for academic medical centers, may influence their behaviors and spending
- **HCA advantage:** scale and diversification across portfolio of markets vs. local competitors in single markets
- **Market share:** regaining growing market share in a large portion of markets

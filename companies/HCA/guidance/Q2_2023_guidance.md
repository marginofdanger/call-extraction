# HCA Healthcare Q2 2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 27, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2023
**Speakers:** Samuel N. Hazen (CEO), William B. Rutherford (CFO), Frank George Morgan (VP IR)

---

## Q2 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Revenue
- **Consolidated net revenue:** $15.86 billion, up **7%** YoY (vs. $14.82 billion in Q2 2022)
- **Same-facility revenue growth:** **+6.3%** YoY
- **Revenue per equivalent admission growth:** **+2.9%** YoY
- **Inpatient pricing (revenue per admission):** up ~**6%** (referenced in Q&A)
- **Revenue per outpatient equivalent:** down ~**1%** in the quarter (referenced in Q&A)
- **COVID support payments comparison:** ~$40 million in Q2 2022; none in Q2 2023

### Volumes (Same-Facility)
- **Admissions growth:** **+2.2%** YoY
- **Equivalent admissions growth:** **+3.7%** YoY; driven by **4%** growth in equivalent admissions (consolidated)
- **Inpatient surgeries growth:** **+1.8%** YoY
- **Emergency room visits growth:** **+3.7%** YoY
- **Outpatient surgeries growth:** **+3.3%** YoY
- **Outpatient cardiology procedures growth:** **+5%** YoY
- **COVID admissions as % of total:** ~**1%** (vs. ~3% in Q2 2022)

### Payer Mix & Managed Care Volumes
- **Commercial adjusted admissions growth:** **+5%** YoY
- **Managed care admissions:** up over **4%** in the quarter
- **Managed care adjusted admissions:** **+5%** in the quarter
- **Emergency visits (managed care):** **+9.8%** in the quarter
- **Medicare adjusted admissions:** **+5%** in the quarter
- **Commercial outpatient growth:** up close to **4%**
- **Outpatient surgeries commercial mix:** ~**50%-55%** commercial

### Acuity
- **Acuity / case mix growth:** **+1.6%** YoY

### Profitability
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin:** **19.3%** in Q2 2023
- **Valesco JV margin drag:** ~**30 basis points** reduction to consolidated margin in the quarter
- **Valesco JV revenue consolidated in quarter:** a little north of **$220 million** (approximately breakeven at EBITDA level)
  - ~**70%-75%** of Valesco revenue is in SWB; remainder in other operating expenses
- **Q1 2023 payer settlement (comparison note):** $145 million recognized in Q1 2023; when adjusting for Valesco and this settlement, margins were consistent between Q1 and Q2
- **Diluted EPS:** **$4.29** in Q2 2023
- **Margins vs. 2019:** Management stated margins have increased compared to 2019 pre-pandemic levels; labor costs as a % of revenue in 2023 are below 2019 levels

### Labor Metrics
- **Contract labor cost decline:** **-20%** YoY (Q2 2023 vs. Q2 2022)
- **Contract labor improved sequentially** Q1 to Q2
- **Contract labor as % of SWB:** **6.8%** in Q2 (under 7%)
- **Nurse turnover (annualized rate):** **17%** (approaching pre-pandemic levels; "a few points above pre-pandemic")
- **Nurse hiring growth:** **+9%** YoY for the year
- **Turnover trends:** Declined for nurses; trending toward pre-pandemic levels

### Capacity
- **Instances where HCA could not accept patient transfers:** **0.8%** of total admissions in Q2, down from **1.5%** in Q1

### Cash Flow & Capital Allocation
- **Cash flow from operations:** **$2.475 billion** in Q2 2023, up **$845 million** YoY (vs. $1.63 billion in Q2 2022)
- **Capital spending:** just over **$1.2 billion** in Q2
- **Dividends paid:** **$164 million** in Q2
- **Share repurchases:** **$915 million** in Q2
- **Debt-to-adjusted EBITDA leverage ratio:** near the **low end** of stated range of **3x to 4x**

### Year-to-Date Metrics (First Half 2023)
- **YTD same-facility admissions growth:** ~**3.3%**
- **YTD adjusted admissions growth:** **5.6%**
- **Divisional breadth:** 13 out of 16 divisions had admission growth and adjusted admission growth
- **Hospital-level breadth:** ~**72%** of hospitals had >2% admission growth YTD (includes some COVID comparison pressure in Q1)
- **Inpatient surgery breadth:** ~**50%** of hospitals had inpatient surgery growth above 2%

## Special Topics

### Commercial Rate Cycle / Managed Care Contracting
- **Commercial rate increases:** "mid-single digit level" (consistent with prior commentary)
- **FY 2024 contracted:** a little north of **70%** (previously ~2/3 as of Q1 call)
- **FY 2025 contracted:** ~**25%** (referenced from Q1 call; "about 1/4 of the way through")
- Relationships with major payers described as "strong"
- Authorization and medical necessity review activity has "picked up again" post-COVID easing
- Management noted physician cost pressures (pro fees) will become a factor in rate negotiation justifications

### Valesco Joint Venture (Envision-Related)
- Completed acquisition of majority stake in **Valesco joint venture** during Q2
- Now consolidating operations of the venture
- Revenue impact: ~**$220 million+** in Q2
- EBITDA impact: approximately **breakeven**
- Margin drag: ~**30 basis points** on consolidated adjusted EBITDA margin
- Revenue split: **70%-75%** in SWB, remainder in other operating expenses
- Strategic rationale: mitigates business risk from Envision bankruptcy; supports alignment between hospital-based physicians and hospital care teams on quality, patient satisfaction, and efficiency
- Management does not expect same level of professional fee pressure in H2

### Medicaid Redeterminations
- **Impact to date:** no material impact observed
- **Guidance assumption:** nothing assumed for Medicaid redetermination impact
- Texas (a major HCA state) started early; described as "a little bit messy"
- Many patients receiving determinations have opportunity to re-enroll or qualify for employer-sponsored plans or marketplace coverage
- HCA teams are actively identifying affected individuals and assisting with coverage
- **Long-term view:** management believes there "could be some positive trends" over the long run

### Data Breach / Cybersecurity Incident
- Announced **July 10, 2023**: discovered a list of certain patient information made available by an unknown/unauthorized party on an online forum
- **Data involved:** does NOT include clinical information, payment information, passwords, drivers license, or social security numbers
- **Source:** appears to be theft from an external storage location used exclusively to format email messages
- Notifying all affected patients in accordance with legal/regulatory obligations
- Named as defendant in multiple class action lawsuits
- **Financial impact:** "has not caused any disruption to our day-to-day operations nor do we believe it will materially impact our business or financial results"

### Capital Investment & Pipeline
- Largest pipeline of capital projects currently in motion in recent years
- Includes outpatient development, inpatient expansion, and other facility investments
- Projects expected to come online in **late 2023, 2024, and early 2025**
- CapEx guide increase reflects growth opportunities in market and land acquisitions for future expansion in fast-growing markets
- Investing in new clinical system to move information to the cloud

### Labor & Workforce Development
- Investing in **Galen College of Nursing** (expanding into new markets each quarter)
- **Centers for Clinical Advancement:** ongoing clinical education for upskilling
- Nurse turnover approaching pre-pandemic levels; annualized Q2 rate suggests even closer
- Hiring metrics up, turnover down; expect continued improvement in H2
- Management characterizes labor progress as being in "middle innings"

### Digital Health & AI
- Growing digital agenda with investment in new clinical systems
- Moving standard data sets to the cloud for big data and analytics
- Coupling digital agenda with "care transformation & innovation" initiative
- Opportunities to improve care processes, reduce variation, improve quality, and create efficiencies
- AI expected to "play a huge part" but early stage; specific impacts not yet known
- Partnering with "sophisticated companies" to accelerate digital/AI agenda

### Competitive Positioning
- Market share has improved compared to pre-pandemic levels
- Margins have increased compared to 2019 despite unprecedented labor and physician cost pressures
- Population growth strong in key markets: Texas, Florida, Utah, Nevada, South Carolina, Tennessee

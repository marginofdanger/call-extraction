## Development Cloud / Vault Platform

- **Top 20 Pharma Win:** In Q1, a top 20 pharma selected twelve Development Cloud products across clinical, quality, and regulatory as their enterprise standard; described as one of the largest deals in the company's history
- **Deal Characteristics:** Executive-level decision; 10- to 20-year horizon thinking by customer; likened to building a huge factory; not affected by macro environment
- **Multi-Product Adoption:** 350+ customers with at least 2 Development Cloud applications
- **eTMF (Electronic Trial Master File):** First customer sold roughly in 2012; now the dominant player with network effect; foundational system of record for clinical trials
- **CTMS & Study Startup:** Getting to be "pretty dominant" products; next furthest along after eTMF; setting up for a big year
- **CDMS (Clinical Data Management):** Very early in its life cycle
- **MyVeeva for Patients / Digital Trials:** Further out in the pipeline; early stage
- **Clinical Opportunity:** Long runway of growth; just getting started at a macro level

---

## CRM & Commercial Solutions

- **CRM Customer Adds:** 12 new customers in Q1; another strong quarter
- **CRM Market Share:** Increased again in the quarter
- **CRM Attrition:** Some churn and attrition observed, in line with expectations; offset from a seat/user perspective by expansions
- **Japan CRM:** Wins in the domestic Japanese market largely offset any churn and attrition
- **Pharma Sales Force Outlook:** Sales force remains one of the most effective channels to market; fundamentally strategic and important; some tweaking on the margins expected through the rest of the year
- **Field Medical Teams:** Role of medical is becoming more important over time; some companies increasing medical field roles
- **Digital Mix Shift:** Industry increasing digital mix into the sales force; becoming more productive and efficient

---

## Veeva Data Cloud / Compass

- **Branding:** Announced Veeva Data Cloud as overall brand for data applications; Compass is the patient, prescriber, and sales data product
- **Data Cloud Components:** OpenData (customer reference data), Link (real-time intelligence), Compass (patient, prescriber, sales data for U.S. market)
- **Common Data Architecture:** All data sets built on a common data architecture for interoperability
- **Compass Adoption Stage:** "Definitely early adopter" stage; data-by-the-record model has been standard for 20+ years; Veeva offering unlimited data by use case delivered through software
- **Competitive Dynamics:** Acknowledged incumbent (implied IQVIA) has set the standard for 20+ years; will take time to prove out value proposition before moving from early adopter to mainstream

## Veeva Link

- **Product Expansion:** Four new data applications for commercial and medical teams
- **Growth:** Strong growth with new customer wins and major enterprise expansions in the quarter
- **Platform Maturity:** Maturing into a powerful foundation to build high-value data applications

## Macro Environment & Industry Dynamics

- **Overall Demand:** Strong; not seeing macro effects in any particular segment
- **Life Sciences Industry:** Not cyclical; propelled forward by science (precision medicine, renewed vaccine focus, RNA platforms)
- **Med Tech:** Science moving things forward; regulatory environment becoming more stringent, especially in clinical area, driving adoption
- **Biotech Funding Concerns:** Not seeing softness despite broader biotech funding slowdown
- **COVID Recovery:** Customers have generally weathered the COVID storm; disruption from 6 months prior (Omicron, COVID fatigue, early inflation worries) is no longer present
- **Implementation Pace:** Not seeing slowdown in pace of implementations for large deals
- **Hiring Environment:** Good for Veeva; hiring has been a bit easier due to downturn in broader tech market (especially speculative startups); flight to quality; no hiring freeze

### Billings Guidance Change
- **Normalized billings trimmed by ~$40M** from prior guidance
  - **~Half ($20M):** Related to services revenue reduction (discussed at Investor Day)
  - **~25% of the residual (~$5M):** Higher mix of quarterly billers vs. annual billers in new business than expected
  - **Remaining balance:** Timing of deals pushed from back half of FY24 to early FY25, plus FX headwinds
  - Per Brent Bowman: "About half of it is related to services... The balance of it is really split into a couple of buckets. One is on the proportion of quarterly billers versus annual billers in our new business... And then the other portion of that is related to timing of deals."

## Long-Term Targets

- **Vault CRM Migration Timeline:** 2025-2030 (moving ~90% of the industry from Veeva CRM on Salesforce to Vault CRM)
  - Early customers going through migration process in CY2024 (early adopter program)
  - Ready to scale in CY2025
  - Most customers expected to migrate starting CY2025, with majority in 2026-2027
  - September 2025 = Salesforce agreement end date (2030 deadline for full migration)
- **CPI-linked pricing:** Capped at 4% annually, applied in arrears with at least 8 months notice
- **Data Cloud opportunity:** "15-year journey" to having most of top 20 using Veeva for most data products for most brands (per Peter Gassner)
- **Clinical data opportunity:** "Can be as large or maybe larger than our commercial one" (Peter Gassner)
- **Management long-term framing:** "We're in the early innings of a large and growing opportunity, and we believe our software, data, and high-value services will enable us to drive growth and profitability through 2030 and beyond" (Brent Bowman)

## Key Q&A Commentary

### Billings & Revenue Dynamics
- **Services not a leading indicator for subscription:** "Services is not a leading indicator and there's a number of reasons. There's the timing of deals. There's product requirements that are different between the type of product you're buying, customer-specific requirements." (Brent Bowman)
- **Deal timing:** Some deals pushed from Q4 FY24 to early FY25; in first half of the year had "favorable linearity" -- it "ebbs and flows" (Brent Bowman)
- **Duration changes:** Some large items were simply co-terms onto a common date; "nothing fundamentally changed" (Brent Bowman)
- **Billings normalization method:** Normalizes for changes in renewal base frequency/duration; new business is not normalized, so higher quarterly billers in new business affected the normalized number

### Vault CRM Migration
- **Bayer and GSK rationale:** Innovation, looking forward; GSK described Pharma CRM as "not a commodity but a solved problem, thanks to Veeva" (Paul Shawah)
- **Migration effort:** Compared to 2012-2017 initial CRM migration (moving ~half the market from Siebel/Cegedim); 2025-2030 period has ~90% of industry to move, but effort is "significantly less than half" of original migration effort (Peter Gassner)
- **6 non-Vault CRM wins:** Will start on Veeva CRM and migrate to Vault CRM before 2030; "transition path becomes pretty clear and very, very clean" (Paul Shawah)
- **No forced timeline:** "We're not forcing our customers to go on any particular timeline" (Paul Shawah)

### Commercial Cloud
- **Commercial subscription segment:** Increased through the course of the year; driven by established products (commercial content) plus newer products (Link, Compass, Crossix)
- **Modular content:** Strong adoption and excitement at EU Commercial Summit
- **Marketing Automation:** Development starts in CY2024; "too early to say what the revenue ramp would be" (Peter Gassner)
- **Crossix recovery:** Solid bookings including brand expansions; some competitors "oversold what they could actually deliver" leading to customer returns

### Data Cloud & IQVIA Competition
- **IQVIA anticompetitive behavior:** Not a new dynamic; Peter Gassner has been aware "for more than 10 years"; IQVIA will not grant third-party agreements for data mixing
- **Smaller companies:** Expected next year to see smaller companies commercializing for the first time "IQVIA free for my whole life" (Peter Gassner)
- **Compass momentum:** Record 13 new brands; both new customers starting/trialing and existing customers expanding (brand-by-brand adoption)
- **Compass expansion:** Prescriber and National products announced for early CY2024

### R&D / Clinical
- **Clinical data software (EDC, CDB, Study Training, ePRO, RTSM):** Customers are "very conservative" in adopting; "starts slowly, but then develops momentum" (Peter Gassner)
- **Integration value:** RTSM + EDC integration for screening process could be "transformational" -- delays in pivotal trials can cost "6 months exclusivity on a blockbuster product"
- **Clinical data products:** OpenData clinical (master data), Site base (deep site/investigator profiles), Clinical Pulse (benchmarking, launching CY2024)
- **Gen AI impact in clinical:** "Honestly, I haven't seen a big impact in clinical" -- more in content creation, safety narratives, protocol evaluation (Peter Gassner)

### Macro / Demand Environment
- **Over last 60 days:** Not getting worse or better, "staying stable" (Peter Gassner)
- **Impact:** Conservatism in decision-making; damper on small biotech innovation/funding
- **Pent-up demand:** "More deferred maintenance building up, especially in top 100 companies, more deferred modernization of systems that's going to have to be taken care of over the next 2, 3, 4 years" (Peter Gassner)
- **IRA impact:** Large pharma adjusting product planning and trial timing; may become "the new normal in a year or two"
- **Competitive positioning strengthened:** "Customers being slightly more conservative, they're spending less on speculative projects. So they tend to go more towards Veeva and core capabilities" (Peter Gassner)

### Hiring & OpEx
- **Q3 hiring:** Lower than recent quarters
- **Q4 FY24:** "Reasonable to expect that lower hiring rate continues" (Brent Bowman)
- **Strategy:** Focused on hiring for growth; areas driving customer success and innovation
- **FY25 detail:** Will provide in ~90 days including operating income and margin targets

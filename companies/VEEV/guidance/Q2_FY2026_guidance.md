## IQVIA Dispute Resolution

### Settlement Details
- ~10-year dispute with IQVIA (formerly IMS Health) resolved
- **Litigation settlement charge:** $30.6M recorded in Q2 FY2026 (excluded from non-GAAP results)
- **No restrictions on Veeva** going forward: "There's no restriction on Veeva. There's no restriction on IQVIA, where the market is open and free"
- Veeva and IQVIA will "compete in some areas, partner in some areas"

### Strategic Impact
- **Two historical barriers in commercial now removed:** (1) Salesforce OEM agreement (resolved with move to Vault CRM), (2) IQVIA data restrictions on Veeva Network and Veeva Nitro
- Products previously "impractical" (Network, Nitro, commercial analytics) because they couldn't incorporate IQVIA industry-leading data -- now resolved
- CEO: "The biggest news in commercial for a long time"
- Customer reaction "overwhelmingly positive" -- "just a sense of relief from customers"
- **Revenue impact framework:** "Maybe 25% of that benefit is related to actually revenue there, business consulting and network and Nitro and 75% of it is related to the network and the network effect that will have on the broader commercial"
- **No material FY2026 revenue impact** from IQVIA resolution

## Vault CRM Migration & Competitive Landscape

### Vault CRM Progress
- **100+ live customers** on Vault CRM (up from 80+ at Q1)
- **2 top 20 pharma companies now live** on Vault CRM in major markets (happened this quarter); continuing to go live globally through end of year and beyond
- **7 top 20s formally committed** to Vault CRM (per prepared remarks)
- **9 top 20s committed** to Vault CRM including verbal commitments (2 additional top 20s added)
- **13 Vault CRM wins** in Q2 (vs. 28 in Q1) -- mix of new customers (mostly first-time CRM) and migrations from Veeva CRM; "relatively lumpy"
- **20+ customers migrated** to date; **~300 more to go**
- Bulk of migrations expected in **calendar 2026 and 2027**; "more than 2/3 are going to be done in '26 and '27"
- 2025 expected to have "some of the lower" migration numbers; ramp expected into 2026-2028

### Competitive Position vs. Salesforce
- **Veeva: 9 top 20 wins** (including verbal) vs. **Salesforce: 3 top 20 wins** (including verbal; 1 new verbal this quarter)
- Salesforce earliest top 20 go-live in a single region: **end of 2026**, possibly not finishing until **2029**
- EVP Shawah: "The chance that all 3 Salesforce customers go live in all regions is actually low"
- "All of those 3 wins that they have, they're not equal to a Veeva win, and they all have a contingency plan, which is Veeva"

### Vault CRM Economics
- **Revenue roughly neutral** vs. legacy Veeva CRM: "Price is similar... revenue-wise to Veeva about similar"
- **Short-term COGS higher** due to transition costs (running both Vault CRM and Veeva CRM during cutover)
- **Long-term COGS lower** once transition completes

## Veeva AI / Agentic AI Strategy

### Platform Architecture
- Veeva AI deeply embedded in Vault platform (platform-first approach, ~7 months of development)
- Two components: (1) Platform for customer-built custom agents, (2) Veeva industry-specific agents
- Leveraging **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** for agent-to-agent interoperability across Vault and external systems (SAP, Workday, Microsoft, etc.)
- Structural advantage: agents built inside Vault application server with full security, transaction control, and workflow integration

### Timeline & Monetization
- Working with **early adopters end of CY2025 and CY2026**
- **No material revenue contribution expected in FY2027 (CY2026) or FY2028 (CY2027)**
- "Significant increase in our market size, and that will play out over many years"
- First AI products focused in commercial suite, broadening to R&D next year

### R&D Use Cases
- **TMF (Trial Master File):** Goal to reduce outsourced labor by 50% in the industry; 20/20 top 20 pharma use Veeva TMF
- **Safety & Clinical:** "Hundreds of millions of dollars of outsourced labor who used to do processing type things. I think agentic AI can maybe remove the need for half of that"
- **Commercial:** Not about reducing headcount, but "enabling them to be more productive and hold more relationships"

### Business Consulting as AI Leading Indicator
- "Every AI project is a business consulting project because you're changing the boundaries of what the humans do and what the agents do"
- One early adopter AI project already underway in commercial content area (started with business consulting project)
- Internal AI use: "We don't expect a meaningful contribution from that in the short term" -- tool for productivity and quality, not headcount reduction

## Commercial Cloud / Crossix

### Crossix Performance
- **Continued strong performance** in Q2, following strong Q1
- Q1 FY2026 had disclosed Crossix growing at **30%+ YoY**; Q2 growth rate not specifically disclosed but "continued growth across the Crossix business"
- **Audiences** (usage-based) remains the "higher growth segment" and "primary growth driver" -- smaller but faster growing
- **Measurement** business (subscription-like) also showing good growth
- Crossix seasonality: "tends to be a little bit heavier in Q1 and over the course of the year"
- Growth drivers: (1) increasing **market share**, (2) expanding **product footprint** (audiences relatively new; expanding into HCP marketing measurement)

### Commercial Product Portfolio Unlocked
- Products previously stalled now freed: Campaign Manager, Service Center, Patient CRM (freed from Salesforce dependency ~3 years ago), Network, Nitro, commercial analytics (freed from IQVIA restrictions)
- CEO: "We have the product footprint we need to really increase our revenue in commercial... the runway is clear"

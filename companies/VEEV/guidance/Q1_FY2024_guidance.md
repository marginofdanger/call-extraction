## Commercial Business Commentary

### Veeva CRM / Vault CRM Migration
- **Vault CRM Development:** on track; compelling demo shown at Commercial Summit (May 2023)
- **First Customers Live on Vault CRM:** expected early CY2024 (likely net new SMB customers)
- **CRM Migrations (Existing Customers):** starting in CY2025; majority in CY2026-2029 timeframe
- **Migration Deadline:** customers have through 2030 to migrate off Salesforce platform
- **CRM Wins in Quarter:** 11 new wins (majority in North America; mostly companies commercializing for first time; a couple of competitive displacements of IQVIA)
- **Vault CRM Features:** same application, same data model; automating data and configuration migration
- **Service Center:** new product for inside/hybrid sales reps; included in Vault CRM license (may drive more user licenses)
- **CRM Bot:** first generative AI application for Vault CRM; will be separately licensed (by user); pricing not yet determined
- **Salesforce Relationship:** operational day-to-day partnership working well post-termination announcement; Salesforce technically has right to compete in pharma CRM; no meaningful competitive threat seen
- **Competitive Environment:** IQVIA seen consistently; regional competitors also present; Veeva wins most of the time

### Pharma Sales Rep Reductions
- Majority of reductions have already played through the system
- Remaining reductions expected through remainder of FY2024
- When complete, expected to be "slightly less than the 10%" initially projected
- "Very slight reductions" seen in Q1, roughly in line with expectations
- Market expected to stabilize at new steady state

### Compass Data Products
- **Compass Wins in Quarter:** 5
- **Current Product:** Compass PatientNow (anonymous patient data, 60 billion rows in U.S.)
- **New Products (January 2024 launch):** Compass Prescriber, Compass Sales -- projected prescriber prescribing data at individual, ZIP, state, and national levels
- **Full Suite Availability:** by January 2024, Veeva will have complete suite to be full IQVIA data replacement
- **Go-to-Market:** focused on patient data first; not yet broadly selling full suite (not yet available)
- **Competitive Positioning vs. IQVIA:** better data, better service, more modern, fewer moving parts; direct replacement strategy similar to original CRM displacement of Siebel/Oracle

### Crossix
- Macro environment unchanged in last 90 days; still facing marketing budget headwinds from H2 FY2023
- Quarter-to-quarter variability like professional services (tied to marketing campaigns)
- Strategic direction: tighter integration with CRM system; converting customers to enterprise license agreements over time to smooth variability

## R&D / Development Cloud Commentary

### Development Cloud Progress
- 400+ customers have selected applications in more than one of the five Development Cloud suites
- Innovation engine described as "strong as ever"

### Clinical Data Management (CDMS / EDC)
- **Top 20 Pharma EDC Go-Live:** one top 20 customer went live in 6 months on core EDC (for all new studies) -- described as "unheard of" fast (typically 12-18 months or even 2 years)
- **Market Position:** 6 of top 20 pharmas have selected Veeva EDC (displacing Medidata or Oracle)
- **Competitive Environment:** "quite benign" -- not moved since Veeva entered
- **Expanding Scope:** early progress in randomization/trial supply management and ePRO (patient-reported outcomes); no company has previously led in all three categories
- **Value Proposition:** customers generally pay "a little bit less" but total cost significantly less due to less manpower needed; faster study build, amendments without downtime
- **Integration:** 2 top 20 customers now have both clinical data management and safety products; only 1 live on both; developing integrated business process over next 12 months

### Quality Suite
- **Quality Wins in Quarter:** 26 wins
- **Product Suite:** QualityDocs + QMS + Training + LIMS (lab information system for manufacturing) + Validation (computer systems validation) + LearnGxP (learning content)
- **Market Position:** 275+ customers using both QualityDocs and QMS, including 10 of top 20 pharmas
- **Sanofi:** adopted quality documentation and Quality Management Suite (standardizing on Vault Quality)
- **No Company Has Offered Full Suite Before:** unified data model and platform -- unique market position

## Macro Environment

- **Overall Assessment:** stable; no change in last 90 days; factored into guidance
- **Emerging Biotech Exposure:** ~4% of total revenue; ~1,000-employee companies with no approved product
- **Funding Pressure:** tighter than 2 years ago for small biotechs; not as easy to get funding at desired valuations
- **Project Scrutiny:** tighter for large companies; extra scrutiny on budgets and ROI timelines, but Veeva's core capability projects tend to pass scrutiny well
- **Experimentation:** less experimentation vs. 2 years ago due to scrutiny; discretionary/one-off projects most affected
- **Long Cycle Business:** large established customers operate on long cycles; not significantly affected by macro ups and downs

## AI Strategy

- **CRM Bot:** first AI application; generative AI for Vault CRM; focused on getting product right before pricing
- **Three-Pronged AI Approach:**
  1. Develop more AI applications over time (CRM Bot is first)
  2. Proprietary data assets (Link, Compass) become more valuable with AI
  3. Make Vault platform integrate well with customers' own proprietary AI applications and third-party AI (open ecosystem)
- **Core System of Record:** always needed even in AI world; critical operational systems (drug safety, clinical trials, regulatory, quality) cannot be replaced by AI
- **Data Assets:** Link and Compass are proprietary assets that AI will leverage, making them more valuable

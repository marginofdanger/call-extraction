## CRM / Vault CRM Migration

### Vault CRM Customer Metrics
- **Customers Live on Vault CRM:** 115 (with 23 new customer additions in Q3)
- **Total CRM Customers:** ~400 customers
- **Top 20 Biopharma CRM Status:** 14 of top 20 expected to migrate to Vault CRM; 6 potentially opting for other solutions (Salesforce-based custom builds)
- **Top 20 CRM Status (Prior):** 18 of top 20 were Veeva CRM customers (2 were mainly IQVIA customers)
- **Top 20 Vault CRM Go-Lives:** Significant top 20 biopharma rollout in Japan in Q3; two top 20 biopharmas live and successful across major regions
- **CRM Win-Back Potential:** "We think 4 of the remaining 6 are ones that we will likely be able to win back over time" (per prepared remarks referenced in Q&A)

### CRM Revenue Impact
- **CRM as % of Total Revenue:** ~20% today (down from ~25% two years ago)
- **No Current Year Impact:** "No impact expected this year and likely nothing material for next year either" from top 20 departures -- Brian Van Wagener
- **CRM Is Not a Growth Business:** "The CRM suite itself and the number of field reps and things, that's not a growing business. That's kind of a stable business" -- Peter Gassner
- **Crossix Could Equal CRM Suite by 2030:** "It's not unforeseen that Crossix can be as big as the whole CRM suite by 2030" -- Peter Gassner

### CRM Competitive Dynamics
- **Salesforce Competition:** Top 20 departures driven by "unique customer-specific factors"; some customers want to "try something new"; custom-built on Salesforce platform
- **Win-Back Mechanism:** Could come from executive turnover, implementation failure, region-by-region rollout issues, or end of custom system lifespan (1-10 years)
- **Win Rate in SMB:** "Our win rate and our conversion rate is very strong and stronger in the smaller market because not -- the smaller customers, they don't have this appetite for a custom build" -- Peter Gassner
- **CRM Suite Expansion:** Customers committing to Vault CRM starting to add additional products (Service Center, Marketing Automation, Patient CRM, Campaign Manager)

### CRM Margin Impact
- **Salesforce Royalties / AWS Hosting Overlap:** "In the next couple of years, as we have other customers going through their migration, there are some customers where we have both the Veeva CRM on Salesforce royalties and the AWS hosting costs" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **Near-Term:** "A modest headwind actually over the next year or 2, but pretty immaterial in the grand scheme of things" on subscription gross margins
- **Subscription Gross Margins:** "Essentially stable, slightly up year-over-year" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **Royalties Roll Off:** "Starts to roll off a few years from now"

## Veeva AI

### AI Strategy & Road Map
- **AI Vision:** Deliver "deep, industry-specific AI agents" that will fundamentally transform productivity and execution for life sciences
- **Early Adopter Projects:** Demonstrating clear value in specialized AI agents running in Veeva applications
- **First CRM/Commercial Content Agents:** On track for release in early December 2025
- **Agent Road Map (Published & Accelerated):**
  - CRM and Commercial Content agents: Available early December 2025
  - Safety and Quality agents: Roughly first quarter (~April 2026)
  - Clinical Operations agents: Through end of calendar 2026
  - Clinical Data Management agents: By end of calendar 2026
- **Coverage:** "We're going to have agents in literally all of our software applications as we get through 2026" -- Paul Shawah

### AI Monetization
- **Broad-Based Across Products:** "Broadly even across the board. So with some areas a bit more than others" -- Peter Gassner
- **Safety:** "Big opportunity to reduce the amount of labor needed"
- **Clinical:** "Certain areas of the clinical" for labor reduction
- **Commercial:** "More about insight generation and market advantage in terms of faster insights"
- **Regulatory:** "About speed"
- **Veeva AI Competitive Advantage:** Integrated product team + selling team + business consulting team to deliver AI value; "more holistic than others"

### AI Impact on Field Sales Reps
- **Sales Rep Reductions:** Predicted ~10% reduction; ended up being a little less than that over past couple of years
- **Outlook:** "Stable, at least for the next couple of years. We're not hearing of any AI-related reductions" -- Paul Shawah
- **Drivers of Change:** More related to specific launch ramps or pipeline challenges, not AI

## Development Cloud / R&D Solutions

### Q3 Wins
- **Three Top 20 Biopharma Enterprise Standard Selections:** One each for Veeva Study Startup, Veeva Study Training, and Veeva Safety
- **Safety Go-Live:** Third top 20 biopharma went live in Safety in the quarter

### Product Penetration (Top 20 Pharma)
- **eTMF:** 20 out of top 20 have selected Veeva as enterprise standard
- **RTSM (Randomization & Trial Supply Management):** No top 20 enterprise standard yet (quite new)
- **eCOA:** No top 20 enterprise standard yet (quite new)
- **Safety:** "Just a few" top 20 -- still in early customer phase
- **LIMS (Laboratory Information Management):** Brand new; first early adopter in top 20 for 2 manufacturing sites

### Development Cloud Opportunity
- **Largest Go-Forward Opportunity:** Development Cloud described as "its largest go-forward opportunity in life sciences"
- **Still Early Days:** "Surprisingly, it's still in the early days of Development Cloud for 2 reasons. One is these are super important systems that take time... The other is we're adding more applications"
- **No Integrated Competitor:** "We don't really have a competitor that's trying to do an overall Development Cloud like we're doing" -- Peter Gassner
- **EDC Win-Back:** One top 20 customer decided to go back to prior provider for EDC; described as "a bit of an aberration"; customer still a broad clinical customer; customer has integrated custom architecture including custom CTMS

### Clinical AI Opportunity
- **Clinical as Largest Single Opportunity:** "It's our largest single opportunity, the clinical business" -- Paul Shawah
- **Transformative Potential:** Agents for eTMF document intake, replacing core human labor; productivity gains across broader clinical area

## Quality Cloud

- **Expanding Opportunity:** Reaching new customer types including CDMOs and highly regulated services industries close to life sciences
- **Three Core Products:** Quality documentation (manufacturing SOPs), Quality Management System (deviations and CAPAs), GxP Training (validated training environment)

- **New Products:** Batch release, computer systems validation, LIMS
- **LIMS (New):** First early adopter customer in a top 20 for 2 manufacturing sites; greenfield opportunity replacing 2 main on-premise solutions that are "not modern"

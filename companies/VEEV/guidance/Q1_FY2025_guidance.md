## CRM Migration & Vault CRM Update

- **Vault CRM GA:** April 1, 2024; solely what Veeva is selling going forward; every new customer after April 1 will be on Vault CRM
- **Q1 CRM Wins:** 8 new Veeva CRM wins (last quarter before cutover to Vault CRM exclusively); migration from Veeva CRM to Vault CRM described as "really a nonissue" and "not that big of a deal"
- **Vault CRM Live Customers:** Multiple customers live, smaller companies, many pre-commercial; "the feedback from customers is that it works"
- **Migration to Vault CRM:** 5 years allowed; first migrations from top 20 pharma expected to start next year (CY2025)
- **3 Top 20 Biopharma Committed to Vault CRM:** Including one that presented at Commercial Summit about their commitment
- **Med Tech Win:** One med tech company committed to Vault CRM (conversion from custom Salesforce); will be one of largest new deals at full deployment scale; this company had wanted Veeva CRM for years but Veeva could not sell to them before Vault
- **Full Functionality Target:** Full functionality for Vault CRM expected later in CY2024
- **Commercial Summit:** Over 2,000 attendees; ~100 customer presentations from ~50 companies
- **New Products Demoed:** Service Center and Campaign Manager demoed at Summit; both will be available in CY2024
- **Vision:** Unifying sales, marketing, and medical into a single Vault -- "hasn't been done before"
- **Competitive Position:** Salesforce licensed IQVIA's OCE; industry reaction was "lukewarm to slightly negative" -- OCE "hasn't gained any traction, hasn't worked well for big customers" over 6-7 years

## Compass / Data Cloud Update

- **Compass Patient:** Revenue and deals currently coming from Compass Patient; described as "as innovative as Vault was when we introduced it in 2011"
- **Compass Prescriber & National Prescriber:** Introduced January 2024; very early
- **Compass Differentiation:**
  - Purchase by brand, unlimited access to 80 billion patient records (vs buying a slice of data)
  - More complete data sourcing strategy stressing patient-level accuracy vs retail pharmacy collection
  - Daily data updates enabling triggering campaigns
  - Can see "blocked products" that competitors cannot
- **Compass Use Cases:** Finding new patients, finding new prescribers, finding patterns in patient behavior
- **Summit Feedback:** "Really specific and good testimonials from customers and partners about the value that it's adding"
- **Link Customers:** Over 100; mix of greenfield and incumbent replacement; data quality "far superior" to competitors; price "a bit higher"

## Vault Basics

- **Concept:** For companies under 200 employees; no implementation costs; run on industry-standard Veeva processes
- **Go-Live:** Customers went live in May 2024; idea started in second half of CY2023 (less than 12 months ago)
- **Addressable Market:** ~$100M segment; previously not addressable by Veeva
- **Graduation Path:** Can "flip the switch" and graduate to full Vault (compared to going from QuickBooks to SAP)
- **Future Expansion:** CTMS, QMS products; over time will apply to CRM and commercial products
- **Board-Level Priority:** "Fundamental decision discussed deeply at the Board level last year"
- **Feedback:** Too early for comprehensive feedback but positive signals; customers buying into the concept

## AI Strategy & Impact

- **AI as Enterprise Disruption:** GenAI burst on scene ~1 year ago creating competing priority for large pharma IT departments -- "hey, we had some existing plans. Now this AI, we have to plan for what we're going to do"
- **Impact on Deal Timing:** AI experimentation/planning created distraction from core system upgrades in large enterprises; does not affect SMB customers as much
- **Analogy to COVID:** AI disruption compared to COVID disruption -- diverts attention from core systems temporarily, then demand comes back as "pent-up demand"
- **Core Strategy:** Enable customers and partners to develop AI applications; not build speculative AI products
- **Direct Data API:** Released April 2024; provides concurrent, consistent data ~100x faster than normal API; 2 years in development
- **Trust-Based Approach:** Will be "really targeted" on AI applications; won't overpromise; may step in on use cases where partners can't serve well
- **AI Use Cases:** Field information, pre-call planning, next best action -- "very specific applications"

## Macro Environment

- **Characterization:** "Macro environment remains challenging" per Peter Gassner
- **Revenue Guide Down Drivers:** Macro headwinds (interest rates, global conflicts, IRA navigation), AI disruption as competing priority
- **SMB Impact:** Biotech funding environment still "relatively high" cost of funding; if rates come down and funding environment improves, more small biotechs could start up
- **Biotech Funding:** Peter Gassner: "I wouldn't characterize that as quite strong yet" and always a delay between funding and putting funds to use
- **Guidance Confidence:** Tim Cabral: "This is the best information we have that informs the guidance" but cannot promise if "other macro things continue to deteriorate materially" there won't be further adjustments
- **What Would Help:** Favorable interest rate turn, resolution of global conflicts, successful IRA navigation, political and legislative stability

## Salesforce/IQVIA Partnership

- **Announcement:** IQVIA licensed OCE to Salesforce with intention of using it to build future CRM product
- **Paul Shawah Reaction:** "A little bit surprised" that Salesforce would build on "a product that hasn't worked well in the marketplace"
- **Industry Reaction:** "Lukewarm to slightly negative"; industry well aware of history of IQVIA and OCE
- **Impact Assessment:** "I don't really see any impact from a product or a go-to-market standpoint... potentially even more of a distraction for our competitors"
- **OCE Customer Feedback:** Small companies on OCE are "not happy" because IQVIA is getting out and there's no smooth path to Salesforce

## Key Analyst Q&A Insights

- **Deal Delays Not Lost:** "We're not losing these deals, just getting deferred" -- core systems need to be upgraded/modernized; competitive position "really good" (Peter Gassner)
- **Top 20 R&D Wins:** Customer-specific, not a broad pattern; "there's only 20 top 20. It's not such a large number that you can make general patterns" (Peter Gassner)
- **Cross-Functional Selling:** Decisions in commercial, clinical, safety, and data areas all made by separate functional teams; no broad enterprise-wide Vault platform decisions; "we have to win the business in each area" (Peter Gassner)
- **Billings Growth as Benchmark:** Tim Cabral declined to imply any growth guidance beyond FY2025; noted services revenue growth YoY at low single-digit percent, which impacts billings

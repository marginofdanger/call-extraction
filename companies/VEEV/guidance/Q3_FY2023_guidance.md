## Vault CRM Platform Transition (Strategic Announcement)

### Timeline
- **Salesforce partnership contract:** runs until **2025** (non-compete period)
- **Wind-down period for existing customers:** 2025-2030 (business as usual, customers supported on Veeva CRM until 2030)
- **Early adopters on Vault CRM:** expected in **2024**
- **Majority of customers migrating:** expected starting **2025**

### Financial Impact
- **No material incremental investment required** - leveraging internal resources and Vault platform
- **Pricing & licensing model:** stays the same; all same add-ons available with Vault CRM
- **Salesforce fees:** visible as accrued fees payable in quarterly financials (monthly number); can use as ballpark estimate for post-2025 savings
- **Investment approach:** re-platforming back end, not rewriting; leveraging existing CRM application code (e.g., iPad client not being rewritten)

### Strategic Rationale
- Vault platform is now "very mature, very robust" - safety and CDMS applications running on it
- Bulk of Veeva application revenue is already on Vault platform, percentage increasing
- Better customer experience and better application on unified platform
- Customers can consolidate around a single Veeva Vault center of excellence

## Clinical Business Highlights

### Vault EDC Wins
- **Two top 20 pharma customers** selected Vault EDC in the quarter
- Both were displacements of top 2 market leaders (other than Veeva)
- EDC is one of Veeva's larger applications; these are among their largest deals
- Revenue ramps slowly (study by study by study), slower than most Veeva applications
- Progressing faster with EDC than expected vs. start of 2020

### Vault Safety Milestone
- **First top 20 pharma customer went live** with Vault Safety across main divisions and most countries
- First company to deliver cloud-native drug safety / pharmacovigilance application
- Implementation speed was "about half the time that most customers would have expected"
- Significance: most customers now thinking "I probably will go with Veeva for safety" - question is timing (1-7 years out)

### Clinical Portfolio
- Full suite: EDC, eTMF, CTMS, RTSM (randomization and trial supply management), patient-reported outcomes, CDB
- Clinical operations and clinical data management - "very, very early days"

## Commercial Business Commentary

### Crossix (Advertising/Analytics)
- Growing ~10%, but broader advertising market under pressure
- Moving to more **multiyear agreements and enterprise license agreements** for revenue stability
- Increasing integration with CRM-related products

### Macro / Demand Environment
- Similar to 90 days ago: "not better, not worse"
- Little bit of additional scrutiny, but nothing significantly different
- SMB space: slowdown highlighted in June; not improving significantly, not worsening
- **Deals that slipped from Q2 did close in Q3**
- No pricing pressures from customers; inflation-related license price increases "pretty seamless"
- Large enterprise not susceptible to macroeconomic changes
- Industry is "not recession-proof, but very recession-resistant"

### Customer Data (Compass)
- "Certainly a marathon" - very entrenched competitor with "questionable business practices"
- Happy with product progress; small set of early adopters
- Revenue momentum "not going to be next year. It's a very long-term play"

## Additional Q&A Data Points

### CRM Transition Details
- **Front-end approach:** keeping front-end applications (e.g., iPad) the same; re-platforming back end only
- Migration tools will bring data across including customizations and multichannel email templates
- Limits retraining requirements for customers
- Over time, opportunity to make changes and deliver better application
- **MedTech CRM:** deprioritized; pharma CRM must come first since they share common foundation

### CRO Adoption (CDMS)
- Continued interest and momentum from CROs
- CROs measured in response due to existing policies/procedures
- Momentum driven by sponsors asking about Veeva
- CROs tend to use market-leading technology; Veeva "on its way" to that position

### Consumer Products / Outside Life Sciences
- Consumer products: just passed 100 customers (milestone)
- MedTech R&D growing well
- Main focus remains pharma and biotech (broadest product portfolio, bulk of revenue)

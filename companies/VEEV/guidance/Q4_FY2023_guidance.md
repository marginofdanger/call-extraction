## Vault CRM Migration (from Salesforce to Vault)

- **Status:** Product development ahead of schedule
- **First Demo:** Scheduled for Veeva Summit in May 2023
- **Approach:** Primarily a "lift and shift" -- front-end apps remain the same, back end moves from Salesforce to Vault
- **Customer Impact:** Minimal for end users; "one day pointing to Salesforce, the next day pointing to Vault"
- **Revenue Impact During Migration:** New capabilities and products purchased before migration will be migrated automatically; during migration period may be a "little bit of a distraction"; after migration is an opportunity for more innovation
- **Win Rate:** Unchanged; competitive environment "pretty much the same"
- **CRM Seat Outlook:** Roughly flat for calendar year 2023
  - Previously guided ~10% overall market reduction in pharma sales reps
  - Majority of that reduction has played out; expects to end up "slightly less than the 10%"
  - Market expected to stabilize at this new steady state
  - Some gains in CRM suite offset by slight market reduction

## Veeva Data Cloud / Compass Update

- **Compass:** Dedicated specialized sales team established; very specific product targeting an "entrenched competitor" (IQVIA)
- **Sales & Prescriber Data:** Expected to be generally available later in FY2024
- **Gross Margin Impact from Prescriber Data:** No extra gross margin pressure expected; data is shared/derived from patient data
- **Link and Compass:** Described as "very, very early" but representing a "second leg of commercial"

## Clinical Data Management / Development Cloud

- **EDC (Electronic Data Capture):** 6 of top 20 pharmas committed; 3 signed in Q4 FY2023 alone
- **Revenue Ramp of Large EDC Deals:** 3 to 5 years to full revenue (some may be 2, some may be 6)
- **Clinical Data Management Suite:** Includes EDC, data cleansing/aggregation, ePRO, RTSM, clinical database
- **EDC Positioning:** Management compares EDC to eTMF as the starting point for clinical data management, similar to how eTMF started clinical operations
- **R&D Revenue Growth Areas (ranked):** 1) Clinical (operations + data management), 2) Quality
- **R&D Solutions Revenue Added in FY2023:** ~$220M incremental

---

## Quality Suite / LIMS

- **Vault LIMS (Laboratory Information Management System):** Under development for quality control in pharmaceutical manufacturing
- **Integration:** Will be unified with QMS, QualityDocs, and Training systems within Quality Vault
- **Significance:** Described as "1 of our biggest ticket items in the quality suite" -- not an add-on
- **Use Case:** Testing materials/batches during pharmaceutical manufacturing; managing test specifications, data entry, and release decisions

---

## Crossix Update

- **Headwinds:** Advertising spend reductions in life sciences impacting a la carte/brand-by-brand purchases
- **Enterprise Deals:** Working on more enterprise-type deals; not directly correlated with advertising headwinds
- **Strategy:** Moving customers from a la carte to multiyear enterprise agreements to smooth spend
- **Top 20 Pharma Win:** Signed a top 20 pharma in Q4 FY2023

---

## AI / Generative AI Commentary

- **Internal Cost Savings from AI:** CEO Peter Gassner sees no opportunity to lower internal costs (relationship-based selling, true innovation-based product development)
- **Customer Value:** Will consider over time; will not rush into it
- **Data Assets:** Veeva has significant data in the industry cloud and customer-specific data; potential to answer industry or customer-specific questions
- **View on Generative AI:** "I don't think it's a revolution" -- useful for chat-type interfaces to get relatively low-quality starting-point answers
- **Impact on Drug Development:** Very early; promising things in early-phase drug discovery are more due to ML algorithms than LLMs
- **Impact on Clinical Trials:** No revolution seen yet

## M&A Strategy

- **Approach:** Careful; looking for cultural fit, business fit, and execution capability
- **Activity Level:** Looking more actively than 3 years ago; more effort in M&A area
- **Pipeline:** "Always got a few things in the hopper" but most don't come through
- **Outlook:** Bullish over next 1-2 years that an acquisition could happen as private company valuations become more realistic
- **Track Record:** Proud of acquisition track record

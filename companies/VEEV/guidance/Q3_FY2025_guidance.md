## Long-Term Targets

- **2030 Goals:** Management "excited" looking ahead to 2030 goals; trains are big and they're moving
- **Gross Margin Profile for 2030:** AI will not materially change gross margin outlook; "all factored in" (per Peter Gassner)
- **TAM:** $20 billion total addressable market
- **Horizontal Business Applications:** Platform-specific approach to new markets outside life sciences; no specific timeline for product area or customer segment announcement; "small tiger team" approach, not capital-constrained
- **Capital Allocation:** Revenue from life sciences will stay in life sciences (customer support, success, new products); horizontal investments funded separately
- **Operating Margin Philosophy:** No real change to cost management philosophy; period-by-period expense management; invest in growth as needed

---

## CRM Migration & Vault CRM Update

- **Vault CRM Full Functionality:** Reached parity with Veeva CRM; 2 new applications (Campaign Manager, Service Center) on top
- **Fourth Top 20 Commitment:** Boehringer Ingelheim (existing long-term customer in commercial and R&D); made commitment after thorough evaluation
- **Migration Timeline (Small/Mid):** 4-6 weeks for small customers
- **Migration Timeline (Top 20):** Up to 2 years for largest enterprises
- **Migration Tooling:** Confidence that tooling is in good place; found and fixed issues; making migrations less disruptive
- **Competitive Position:** IQVIA "out of the game" (product sunset); Salesforce is primary competitor but "they don't have a product yet"; Salesforce called out life sciences cloud in 3 of their top 10 deals (per DJ Hynes/Canaccord)
- **Win Rate:** "Very, very high" for new CRM deals; virtually all pre-commercial wins
- **Additional Top 20 Commitments Expected:** Continue to expect additional commitments over the next several months (per Paul Shawah)
- **Gross Margin Impact of Migration:** Secular improvement as customers migrate off Salesforce platform (reducing royalty), but spread out between now and 2030

---

## GenAI Products

- **CRM Bot:** Hooks CRM into customers' own LLM; no additional charge; no compute cost to Veeva
- **MLR Bot:** Veeva hosts and runs LLM (from major tech providers); will be a separate license add-on to PromoMats; monetization details still being finalized; expected early customer rollouts into next year
- **Pricing Philosophy:** Where Veeva incurs significant compute power, they will charge; where they don't, they won't
- **Margin Impact:** "No material impact to gross margin" from AI products (per Peter Gassner)

---

## Development Cloud

- **Broad-Based Adoption:** Strength in all areas of Development Cloud
- **Strategic Partnership Opportunities:** Making progress on several large strategic partnerships in Development Cloud; similar but different to Boehringer Ingelheim/Merck scope -- strategic level, across product lines, but each is unique
- **Suite Maturity:** More mature products now in clinical and quality areas than in prior years; discussions more expansive
- **CDMS (Vault EDC):** Ramps proceeding as expected; multiyear predefined ramps; growth driven by broad base of products
- **Safety Suite:** "Approaching a tipping point" -- depth of customer conversations increasing; legacy systems aging; cloud solutions getting better; Safety Signal and Safety Workbench now available; advanced automation between clinical data management and safety systems
- **Clinical Trial Volume Insulation:** Most Veeva revenue is not on study-by-study basis; long-term enterprise license agreements not affected by ups and downs of study volume; "pretty darn close" to insulated

---

## Commercial Cloud

- **Seat Count Dynamics:** Industry relatively stable; rep count on average stabilized after reductions in recent years; "new steady state"
- **Add-On Attach Rates:** Continuing to increase each quarter; some products have higher attach rates, others still have room to grow
- **Crossix:** Strong performance; execution driving results; audiences and micro audiences getting more fine-tuned; data network growing; competition has "had some stumbles"
- **Compass:** Good quarter; Patient product and Prescriber product described as "pretty disruptive"; flywheel spinning (small but growing)
- **Commercial Content:** Contributing to commercial strength
- **Walgreens Partnership:** Strategic partnership to add data sources; strongest data network in industry (per Peter Gassner)
- **Direct-to-Consumer Advertising Risk:** Even if DTC advertising were eliminated (many ifs), would take many years due to challenges; no near-term impact expected; customers not reacting negatively

---

## Macro Environment & Visibility

- **Demand Environment:** Stabilized; "a bit better" compared to a year ago; customers settled into macro environment
- **Visibility into FY2026:** "Marginally better" visibility compared to a year ago; more mature products, more ramps, less single-product dependency
- **Regulatory/Political Backdrop:** Very early in administration change; customers accustomed to changes; focused on priorities and work; business as usual; no change in customer decision-making observed
- **Overall Sentiment:** Peter Gassner described it as "a good quarter" and said he was "encouraged and energized" by discussions with senior executives

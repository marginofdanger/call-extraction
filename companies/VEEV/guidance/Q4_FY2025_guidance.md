### FY2026 Guidance Context
- **Revenue Growth Composition:** "Subscription growth in FY '25 was 15% [normalized for TFC]. And then excluding FX, it's about 14% this year. So it's a minor deceleration. It's mostly driven by commercial. And within commercial, it's Crossix. And that Crossix deceleration is mostly a function of last year's very strong outperformance, making for a hard year-over-year compare" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **Margin Drivers:** "What you're seeing is that we're always trying to get more efficient... We find that lean teams are more agile. They've got better speed... We're spending about twice on product, what we do on sales and marketing and keeping that focus on product excellence and innovation" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **Margin Leverage Sources:** Broad-based across gross margin improvement (secular increase as R&D grows and more business on Vault platform, better services efficiency) and OpEx efficiency in sales, marketing, and product teams

- **FX Impact:** ~1% revenue headwind; primarily a revenue-side impact with natural hedge on expenses; shakes out on the operating income line
- **No Macro Changes Factored:** "We've not factored in any macro changes into the guide... We just haven't seen any impact yet in our customers' decision-making" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **AI Not Counted On for Margin:** "Not a major contributor to the margin expansion that we saw in Q4 or in the coming year... It's not something we're counting on, though, to deliver on this year's guidance" -- Brian Van Wagener

## Vault CRM Transition Update

### Vault CRM Customer Metrics
- **New Vault CRM Customers in Q4:** 20 new customers (up significantly from prior quarters of 5, 14, and 13)
- **Customer Profile:** "Vast majority of them in the U.S. market, a small number in Europe, and then most of them small, midsized companies, the vast majority selecting their first CRM system" -- Paul Shawah
- **Win Rate:** "We're virtually winning every one of those deals" -- Paul Shawah

### Top 20 Pharma CRM Migration
- **Timeline:** "We expect more announcements in 2025. And I expect the vast majority of decisions, particularly in top 20, will happen by the end of 2026" -- Paul Shawah
- **Red Zone Awareness:** "The customers are starting to get aware of the concept of a red zone... '25, '26 is reasonable for many customers... Maybe the early part of '27 could be there. But after that, you really start getting into the red zone" -- Peter Gassner
- **Change Management Complexity:** "When you're talking about change management with well over 10,000 people and functions and de-customizing and repointing integrations, it's not something you can do in 6 months" -- Peter Gassner
- **Customer Decision Pacing:** "We're not forcing customers and doing anything unnatural... We want to do this in a very customer-friendly way" -- Paul Shawah
- **Migration Automation:** "The data migrator, we put a tremendous investment in that... the actual data migration part... that's a lot of work, which is largely going to be automated in here because we control both the source and the target" -- Peter Gassner
- **Win Expectation:** "We still expect to win the vast majority" -- Paul Shawah

### CRM Innovation Road Map
- **New Products:** Service Center, Campaign Manager, Patient CRM delivering "customer centricity" in one database for the first time in life sciences
- **AI Integration:** CRM bot, CRM voice control planned for this year; "Veeva is the fastest path to AI that you can use in CRM" -- Peter Gassner
- **Customer Centricity:** "We are making really, for the first time, in life sciences, this idea of customer centricity simpler, easier, everything in one database. That's never been done before" -- Paul Shawah

## Development Cloud / Clinical Platform Update

### EDC Progress
- **Top 20 EDC Customers:** 9 of top 20 have committed to Veeva EDC (ninth won in Q4)
- **Revenue Ramp:** "Ramps are steadily contributing to our revenue base, both to billings and to revenue... There's no one product, no one customer, no one year where that really spikes, but it's factored into our guidance for FY '26" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **Competition:** Medidata and Oracle in the EDC area; Veeva has structural advantage through integration between clinical operations and clinical data management

### CTMS Standardization
- **Top 20 CTMS Adoption:** 17 of the top 20 using CTMS (replicating success seen with eTMF)
- **Platform Advantage:** eTMF and CTMS integration on Veeva Vault platform was an innovation that brought together document management and transactional systems for the first time

### Major Q4 Clinical Win
- **All-In Top 20 Customer:** One top 20 biopharma took a full clinical platform approach, adding six major clinical applications all at once; described as one of Veeva's largest subscription orders ever
- **Ramp Timeline:** ~5 years for full ramp ("one of our longer ramps because there are a lot of things in there" -- Peter Gassner)
- **Motivation:** Speed to value and cost elimination ("double your speed of evaluation and implementation when you go all at once... Eliminate these RFPs, these 7 RFPs" -- Peter Gassner)

### Product Pull-Through
- **Products Following eTMF/CTMS:** Study Training, Site Connect, payments module, EDC/CDB, RTSM, eCOA
- **Product Drivers:** Not EDC-attached; driven by (1) customer commitment to modernizing clinical tech, and (2) strategic partnership decision with Veeva

## Safety Cloud Update

- **Second Top 20 Go-Live:** Second top 20 biopharma went live with Veeva Safety in February 2025 and is rolling out the full Safety Suite
- **Cloud Advantage:** "We are the first true cloud integrated set of safety applications. So the core safety processing, safety signaling, the safety workbench reporting, nobody has had that in the cloud before" -- Peter Gassner
- **AI Opportunity:** Safety called out as an area with a lot of opportunity for AI innovation

## Data Cloud / Compass Update

### Compass
- **Compass Brands:** Over 100 brands
- **Patient vs. Prescriber:** Most revenue progress in Compass Patient; Compass Prescriber earlier stage
- **IC-Grade Data:** No customers using Prescriber data for incentive compensation yet; "next year at this time, we will" -- Peter Gassner
- **ELA Trend:** "We haven't seen really a trend to ELAs yet. We're still on the brand by brand. I think that trend to ELAs... we might be a year or so out" -- Peter Gassner
- **Small Company Opportunity:** Peter Gassner "very excited" about bringing Data Cloud value to companies under 50 employees

### Veeva Pulse (New Product)
- **Product Description:** Privacy-safe data generated from CRM activity data showing physician engagement patterns at industry level
- **First Deal:** Signed first deal just before availability; was for a top 20 pharma in the U.S.; notable as "one of our first products where the first deal was actually a 7-figure deal" -- Peter Gassner
- **Geographic Expansion:** Currently available in U.S. and most of Europe; adding Southeast Asian countries, Japan, and potentially 1-2 additional European countries by 2026

### Crossix
- **FY2025 Performance:** "A major driver of the outperformance that we saw in commercial throughout the year" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **Linearity:** "No change that we're seeing to the overall shape of revenue in Crossix" -- Brian Van Wagener
- **FY2026 Outlook:** Expecting continued growth; Crossix deceleration in FY2026 guidance mostly a function of FY2025 strong outperformance creating hard YoY compare

## AI Strategy Update

- **AI Products in Pipeline:** TMF bot (document classification), CRM voice control (coming this year), CRM bot, MLR bot (medical legal regulatory review), plus additional products in plan
- **AI Organization:** Centralized AI solutions group with strong leader to develop core competency
- **AI Timing:** "Our timing is just right because the base of the technology is now stable enough" -- Peter Gassner
- **AI Distraction Largely Behind:** "I think we're largely through that area of AI distraction now" in terms of customer buying behavior -- Peter Gassner
- **LLM Landscape:** Multiple LLMs settling out at platform level; not going to be one or 50; Veeva plays at the application/use case layer on top

## Horizontal Applications / New Markets

- **Platform Investment:** Lean team working on version 2 cloud application platform; Peter Gassner personally reviewing individual engineers
- **Thesis:** "Most of the cloud application platforms now are really version 1. And maybe there's a need for a version 2 type of thing" -- Peter Gassner
- **Application Area:** No announcement yet on which application area Veeva will enter
- **Existing Customer Reaction:** "More curious than anything else" as they think of Veeva primarily in life sciences context
- **AI Integration:** "AI will change the user interfaces on the operating systems over time... A platform that's designed with that in mind" will have advantages -- Peter Gassner

## Macro Environment / Industry Commentary

- **Government Policy Changes:** No impact seen on customer decision-making so far; paying attention to FDA, NIH, price negotiations, but too early to predict impact
- **Tariff Impact:** Very early; could cause lack of focus and delayed contracts if significant disruption occurs; "We haven't seen that yet" -- Peter Gassner
- **Life Sciences Resilience:** "Life sciences is generally more resilient to economic cycles or potential changes... because it's an adaptable industry" -- Paul Shawah
- **Revenue Predictability:** "Our revenue tends to be more predictable... It's core systems. It's annual subscriptions. Projects may get delayed. But... those projects typically come back" -- Paul Shawah
- **Trial Starts:** Recent inflection in trial starts since October 2024 but not a driver of Veeva's clinical confidence; enterprise license agreements are not impacted by clinical trial volumes
- **FSP Trend:** Large pharma moving toward functional outsourcing (FSP) rather than full-service CRO model; Veeva Development Cloud has played a small part in enabling this shift

- **Partner Ecosystem:** Accenture and other partners can flex at scale; strong Veeva expertise across partner network
- **Migration Automation:** Data migrator investment significantly automating migration work ("we control both the source and the target")

7 Key Takeaways

1
Flawless revenue guidance record. 15/15 quarters beat revenue guidance, including beating the high end every time. Average beat of +2.1% ($14.1M) vs midpoint. This is among the most consistent beat records in SaaS — no quarter came within $0.5M of missing.
2
Profitability guidance is far more conservative than revenue guidance. OI beats average +6.9% vs midpoint — more than 3x the revenue beat rate of +2.1%. This suggests management has more visibility into revenue than expenses, or deliberately sets wider OI buffers.
3
Beat magnitudes are accelerating, not stabilizing. Revenue beats grew from +1.3% (FY2023) to +3.2% (FY2026). OI beats grew from +$7M to +$23M per quarter. With FY2026 now complete (4 quarters), the trend is confirmed across a full fiscal year cycle.
4
Full-year guidance has been beaten every year. FY2023: +$11M vs final. FY2024: +$10M vs final. FY2025: +$23M vs final. FY2026: +$28M vs final. Four fiscal years, four beats — the pattern is unmistakable.
5
Q1 FY2024 (TFC trough) saw the most conservative guidance in the dataset. OI was guided at $141–143M; actual was $157M (+10.6% beat). EPS was guided at $0.79–0.80; actual was $0.91 (+14.5% beat). Management over-reserved for TFC uncertainty and then outperformed across every metric.
6
The Q1 FY2026 beat was the largest ever across all metrics. Revenue +$31.5M (+4.3%), OI +$41.9M (+13.6%), EPS +$0.23 (+12.9%) vs guidance midpoints. Driven by Crossix usage-based revenue outperformance and expense timing — management itself flagged this as partially non-recurring.
7
Only one quarter in 15 failed to beat OI midpoint. Q2 FY2023 landed exactly at the midpoint ($202.0M vs $202M guide). Every other quarter beat. No quarter has ever missed the low end of any guided metric — revenue, OI, or EPS.
8
FY2026 cumulative guidance raises were unprecedented. Revenue raised +$148M from initial ($3,048M midpoint → $3,195M actual), OI raised +$134M ($1,300M → $1,434M actual), and EPS raised +$0.78 ($7.32 → $8.10 actual). The final beat vs the already-raised guide confirmed the pattern: the raises themselves were conservative.
9
EPS beat rate of 15/15 with +$0.10 average beat. EPS benefits from both operating outperformance and growing investment income from the $6.6B cash pile. Full-year FY2026 EPS of $8.10 beat the final guide of ~$7.93 by +$0.17 — the largest full-year EPS beat yet.
10
Guidance credibility is a structural competitive advantage. Fifteen quarters of consistent beats builds institutional trust with long-term investors. It also gives management credibility when they provide multi-year targets ($6B by 2030) — the track record suggests these are achievable floors, not aspirational ceilings. FY2027 initial guide of ~$3,610M likely follows the same conservative pattern.

CRM / Vault CRM Migration

Vault CRM Customer Metrics

  • Customers Live on Vault CRM: ~140 (up from 115 at Q3; "the actual number is closer to 140")
  • Top 20 Biopharma CRM Status: 14 of top 20 expected to select Vault CRM; ~5 decisions remaining, expected to play out during calendar 2026
  • Top 20 Commitments: All 10 committed top 20 projects have been started and are underway; "all going to a very stable product that's already live"
  • CRM End of Support: Moved to December 29, 2029 (from September 2030); no customers with projects planned into 2030 timeframe
  • Salesforce Royalties: Will wind down as customers roll off; once all off, payments stop

CRM Competitive Dynamics

  • Vault CRM Product Advantage: "The product is actually better than Veeva CRM and the migrations are going well"
  • Agentic CRM: Now available with Veeva; "our promise was that our customers can innovate, get to CRM, get to Agentic CRM fast"
  • Salesforce Risk: "Greater risk that a customer that decided to do Salesforce and tries to go down the path of a custom build project... sees how difficult it is and comes back to Veeva"
  • Top 20 Custom Build Example: One top 20 has "a large number of development team contracted in India from a third-party system integrator" — "that may work out well or that may not work out well"
  • Win Rates Outside Top 20: Expected to be "even higher than inside of top 20"; SMB companies want trusted partner delivering proven product
  • Cross-Sell Opportunity: Vault CRM migrations creating opportunities for add-on products (Service Center, Campaign Manager, Network, OpenData)

Veeva AI

AI Agent Road Map

  • CRM/Commercial Content Agents: Available (launched early December 2025)
  • Safety and Quality Agents: ~April 2026
  • Clinical Operations Agents: Through end of calendar 2026
  • Clinical Data Management Agents: By end of calendar 2026
  • Full Coverage: "We're going to have agents in literally all of our software applications as we get through 2026"

AI Positioning

  • AI Not Replacing Core Software: "AI is not replacing software. That's not happening." Windows, iOS, Excel, SAP, Workday, Veeva — "these core systems are essential"
  • Core Systems Used by Agents: "These core systems are going to be used by agents as well as human users"
  • Symbiotic with LLM Providers: Anthropic, OpenAI are "engines" — "the new wave of cloud computing, the new AWS"; Veeva builds industry-specific applications on top; "very symbiotic"
  • Anthropic Partnership: Veeva is an enabling and launch partner of Anthropic's Cloud for Life Sciences; "absolutely not" cannibalistic for Veeva
  • AI Timeline: "Early days of AI... It's going to play out over 10 or 20 years"
  • Trust Advantage: "Trust is the #1" factor for customer AI adoption decisions; Veeva's 15 years of delivered promises creates earned trust

AI Monetization

  • Token-Based Pricing: Starting FY2027 with token-based pricing model; "gives us a little bit of predictability around the margin profile"
  • FY2027 Impact: "Not a material impact on FY '27"; "more in the out years and still pretty early"
  • Margin Accretion: "Still quite early" to determine; more focused on product excellence and value creation
  • Commercial Content AI (farthest along): ROI is "very clear" — faster content, lower cost to create; faster drug launches before patent expiration
  • Custom vs Prepackaged Agents: "Bulk of it is with our agents that we're designing"; custom agents around "lighter use cases" expected in H2 FY2027

Development Cloud / R&D Solutions

Key Wins (Q4)

  • Top 20 Safety Win: Won a top 20 biopharma standardization deal; "their existing systems... had lots of deferred maintenance, and that was going to become a critical risk"
  • Top 20 RTSM Enterprise Standard: First top 20 to standardize on Veeva RTSM; "a milestone deal for Veeva and for the industry"
  • First Top 20 Safety Go-Live with Signal: Achieved in Q4

RTSM

  • Product Significance: "You could think of it as significant as the EDC area" — randomizing patients, shipping blinded drug supplies globally
  • Selling Motion: Study-by-study or therapeutic-area basis for top 20; enterprise standardization is new concept
  • Value Proposition: Enterprise solution replacing piecemeal procurement; "you don't have to have an RTSM procurement department in your company"

Study-by-Study Business

  • CRO Channel Opportunity: Beginning to make progress with CROs OEMing Veeva technology to their biotech customers
  • Revenue Potential: "We know that can be a $1 billion business" — EDC, RTSM, eCOA all sold study-by-study; individual studies can be $500K-$1M in technology
  • Go-to-Market Evolution: New focus on CRO channel go-to-market motion

EDC

  • Air Pocket: "Hit a bit of an air pocket there in the EDC" — attributed to "random timings"; progress expected in coming years
  • Competitive Position: "Arguably, we're the leader now. It's us and Medidata" — Medidata better in study-by-study; Veeva on path to leadership
  • eSource Initiative: Announced groundbreaking eSource capability

R&D Growth Dynamics (FY2027)

  • Growth Rate Shift: Mix shift from mature products (eTMF) to large new products (RTSM, EDC, Safety, LIMS) — "still early and getting to scale"
  • Billings vs Subs Gap: Mix shift from mature to new products driving delta between 13% subscription growth and 11% billings growth

Safety

  • Safety Surge: Internal term for current momentum; another top 20 win in Q4
  • AI + Safety: "Pretty clear" ROI — replacing human labor in case intake and case narrative generation; "expensive to hire those people, and it's not easy"
  • Veeva Position: "If you want a great core safety system that has safety AI on top of it and is connected to your other systems, Veeva is the only place you're going to do that"
  • Risk Aversion Factor: Customers reluctant to change core safety database because "they have to report to health authorities all around the world"; product failure means "they have to pull their products off the market"

Crossix / Commercial Cloud

  • FY2026 Performance: "Outstanding year... certainly exceeded our expectations"
  • FY2027 Comps: "The compares do get tougher" — lapping strong Q1 FY2026 outperformance; "a business that's executing really well with a long runway for growth"
  • FY2026 was Major Beat Driver: "Crossix... was really a major driver of the beat over the course of the year" in FY2026

Customer Growth

  • Customer Count Growth: Accelerated to 5% — driven by "really strong execution" and progress in Veeva Basics (small/emerging biotechs)
  • Veeva Basics: ~100 customers selected; long-term potential of "1,000 customers on Basics over time"; graduation path to enterprise Veeva

Macro Environment

  • Pharma Clarity: MFN tariff deals with current administration over past 6 months; "cautious optimism"; "certainty is always better than uncertainty"
  • Industry Health: "The industry overall is pretty healthy"
  • Guidance Approach: Based on customer conversations and planning cycles, less influenced by short-term macro; "longer planning cycles"

Services

  • FY2026 Strong Execution: Both business consulting and R&D services growing healthily; CRM migration activity uptick as expected
  • FY2027: Top 20 CRM projects at various phases (wrapping up, well underway, kicking off); all factored into guidance
  • AI Impact on Implementation: Early days; "tech enabling our services... hasn't borne a lot of fruit just yet, but I do expect it to in the next year or 2"; AI helping with EDC study migration

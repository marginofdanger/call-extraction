Q3 Billings Commentary (from Q&A)

  • Billings growth on constant-currency basis: Management guided ~17% billings growth in Q3 on constant-currency basis
  • Billings growth adjusted for FX and billing term changes: Guiding 18% billings growth adjusted for FX and billing term changes
  • Billings reduction in second half: Approximately 1/3 related to FX (strengthening dollar), balance related to macro (predominantly commercial)

FY2023 Subscription Revenue Detail

  • Full year subscription revenue guidance reduction: ~$17M lower (referenced in Q&A)
  • R&D Solutions full year growth (ex-FX): ~32% growth on a constant-currency basis
  • Commercial Solutions: Experiencing macro headwinds, particularly in Crossix and SMB add-on users

Vault Platform & Quality Suite

Vault Quality Milestone

  • 500+ customers now using at least one Vault Quality Suite product
  • 200+ Vault Training customers
  • Record 52 Vault Quality customer wins in Q2 FY2023
  • Significant progress on Vault QMS projects with enterprise customers
  • Vault LIMS and Vault Validation Management -- new product innovations progressing well, will start working with early-adopter customers later this year

Quality Suite Product Portfolio

  • QualityDocs, QMS, Training -- established products
  • LIMS (Laboratory Information Management) -- newly announced
  • Management: "We're really the only company that has that integrated suite of products"
  • Wins span across segments: enterprise, SMB, pre-commercial, and med tech

CDMS (Clinical Data Management System)

  • Management "really happy with the progress in the quarter"
  • Enterprise momentum: Strong customer success with a couple of large enterprise customers; progressing sales cycles with other enterprise customers
  • Goal: To be the leader in CDMS over time
  • Adjacent products: ePRO (patient-reported outcomes), randomization, trial supply management (recently acquired)
  • CEO Gassner: "Most people would say, 'Pete is going to win that race'" -- referring to CDMS market leadership
  • CDMS characterized as "a marathon, but we're ahead of the game"

Veeva CRM & Commercial

CRM Platform

  • Significant Veeva CRM release in Q2 with major innovations to optimize content use across channels
  • Enables efficient and compliant hybrid engagement models
  • Long-term commercial subscription growth target: ~10% over time (unchanged)
  • Continued ability to take share, which reduces the impact of industry rep reductions on Veeva

SMB & Macro Impact on Commercial

  • Pharma represents ~90% of Veeva's overall revenue (balance is med tech and CP&C)
  • Enterprise customers represent ~60% of total pharma revenue (defined as top ~50 largest pharma companies)
  • SMB represents ~40% of total pharma revenue (wide range: 100-person start-ups to $1B revenue companies)
  • Pre-commercial is a small percentage of SMB
  • Macro impact primarily on lower end of SMB: Lower add-on users (CRM users, PromoMats users)
  • Not seeing unusual churn at SMB level -- more a "lack of expansion" than churn
  • Increased deal scrutiny across all deal sizes, impacting both commercial and R&D

Crossix

  • Crossix experienced more pronounced macro impact than broader commercial business
  • Tied to advertising spend, which has tightened in the macro environment
  • Products include measurement and programmatic (patient audience buying)
  • Management "real bullish on Crossix going forward"
  • Establishing new pattern: some customers moving to enterprise agreement / baseline measurement agreement (less variable)
  • Changes "take a while to play out over time"

Veeva Data Cloud

Data Cloud Components

  • 1. OpenData -- Customer reference data; steady grower, around for a while 2. Link -- First product (Link for Key People) has "really great momentum" in enterprise and SMB; 4 additional Link products announced, all in very early stages with early adopters, getting "a lot of excitement" 3. Compass -- Patient data (launched first), then prescriber data; sales data planned over time

Compass Progress

  • 4 customer wins in Q2 for Compass
  • Typical win pattern: start with a brand, a couple of use cases (e.g., defining patient journey, finding doctors based on patients treated), then expand within brand team and across brands
  • Management: "we're accelerating in that space" and "highly focused on it"
  • Compass characterized as "a marathon" and "we're just getting started" -- too early to call the race
  • CEO: "It's too early to call that race for Compass because we're just getting started. So very different Compass and CDMS."

Business Consulting

  • Record revenue quarter for Veeva Business Consulting
  • Published first-ever Veeva Pulse Field Trends Report (industrywide view of global customer engagement based on 130+ million quarterly HCP interactions)
  • ~150 people in Business Consulting team
  • Started 3 years ago, now "quite profitable"
  • Becoming "a strategic resource to help customers realize their digital transformation"
  • Completes the picture for Veeva: strategic discussions with customers on go-to-market motion, tight feedback loop between consulting, product, and sales

Macro Environment & Competitive Positioning

Macro Commentary

  • Headwinds started in late June and continued at a similar pace through July
  • Impact primarily on commercial side: Crossix (advertising budgets), lower SMB add-on users
  • R&D has not been meaningfully impacted -- products priced on enterprise agreements, not per-user basis
  • No geographic-specific weakness called out (including Eurozone)
  • Industry budget planning for calendar 2023: No indication yet that budgets will be impacted
  • Drug pricing legislation (U.S., Medicaid-focused, top X drugs, implemented 2026): "too early to say how it plays out over the long term"; "in the short and medium term, it's steady as she goes"

Competitive Position

  • CEO: "Our competitive landscape and product strategy also have never been stronger"
  • CFO: "The underlying business remains as strong as we thought 90 days ago"
  • Products described as "real foundational" -- not nice-to-haves
  • Growth areas (Data Cloud, Link, Compass, Business Consulting, Safety, CDMS, new Quality products) are "mostly just about capturing new market share" and more tied to competitive environment than macro
  • Mature/high market share products (PromoMats, CRM) are where macro sensitivity shows

Hiring & M&A Environment

  • Hiring environment better now than a year ago
  • Net ~203 new hires in Q1 (referenced from prior quarter)
  • CEO: "Flight to quality with customers and with employees" during macro environment
  • M&A: Valuations coming down makes for "a more attractive M&A environment"
  • "100% track record of success on our acquisitions" -- remain "careful and surgical"
  • Cash position: ~$3B in cash and investments

Regulatory Wins

  • Federal court dismissed Medidata trade secret lawsuit filed against Veeva in 2017
  • Judge ended proceedings midway through jury trial, finding Medidata unable to substantiate claims

Linearity & Pipeline Commentary

  • Quarter linearity: Started out "a little bit more typical" then saw headwinds in "the later part of June"
  • Pipeline headwinds seen in June continued in July at a similar pace
  • Guidance philosophy: "We take all the best information to give you the best view we have of the business today"
  • Factored in: FX at current rates, increased deal scrutiny, lower SMB spending
  • No particular product reprioritization within R&D portfolio due to macro -- "they're all pretty foundational"
  • More about "natural cycles in the industry which run longer than the temporary macro" (e.g., regulatory strength tied to second replacement cycle)

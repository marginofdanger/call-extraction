Key FY2025 Guidance Commentary

  • Non-GAAP Operating Margin: ~39% implied at midpoint (Brent Bowman: "the 39% reflects execution and our operating model")
  • Services Revenue: Lower outlook vs. earlier expectations; project timing and increased Vault CRM migration investments
  • Subscription Revenue Visibility: "Our visibility is at least equally as good, if not better than it was in previous times" -- Brent Bowman
  • Op Margin Cadence: Q1 guided ~38%; full year ~39%; management declined to provide Q2/Q3 slope detail
  • No Incremental Conservatism: "I wouldn't interpret the choice of words of prudent as there's this incremental level of conservatism baked into the guide" -- Brent Bowman
  • Rep Reductions Impact: "The digital portion of those rep reductions, that's largely behind us... I wouldn't think of that as an incremental headwind" for FY2025 -- Brent Bowman

Long-Term Targets

  • TAM: ~$20 billion; less than 15% penetrated currently
  • Revenue Mix (approximate): - Development Cloud (R&D): ~50% of revenue, ~65% of overall opportunity, growing ~20% - CRM Suite (CRM + add-ons): ~25% of revenue, stable (high market share, migration needed before new add-on sales) - Other Commercial (Crossix, Compass, Link, commercial content): ~25% of revenue, growing ~15%
  • Vault CRM Migration Timeline: Vault CRM general availability April 2024; full parity with Veeva CRM by end of calendar 2024; still customers on Veeva CRM in 2028; fully migrated by 2029-2030
  • Cash Flow: Brent Bracelin (analyst) noted FY2025 could see operating cash flow exceed $1 billion for first time; management had $4 billion in total cash + investments
  • Capital Allocation: Focused on M&A; disciplined approach; not limited by size ("if we saw something that would cost $3 billion we would go for it"); would return money to shareholders rather than do failed acquisition
  • Margin Trajectory: "I think you'll see that from us increasing margins over time" -- Peter Gassner

TFC (Termination for Convenience) Impact

  • The customer contracting change standardizing TFC rights went into effect February 1, 2023, reducing revenues, operating income, and non-GAAP operating income in Q4 and FY2024

Vault CRM Transition Update

  • Vault CRM GA: On track for April 2024
  • Full Parity: "Full parity with everything in Veeva CRM will be in Vault CRM by the end of this year" (end of calendar 2024) -- Paul Shawah
  • Top 20 Pharma Commitments: 3 top 20 global pharmas committed to migrate to Vault CRM (third announced this quarter)
  • Q4 CRM Wins: 9 total CRM wins in Q4 (5 Vault CRM, 4 Veeva CRM)
  • Services Investment: Increased investment in Vault CRM-related services; "it wasn't anything our customers asked for... we really want to do the right thing for those customers" -- Peter Gassner
  • Cross-sell Opportunity: New add-on products (marketing automation, service center) require migration to Vault CRM first; cross-sell opportunity opens up "within 2 years or so" but will "gradually open up" -- Peter Gassner
  • Replatforming Timeline: "We've got to take this medicine for 3 or 4 years" -- Peter Gassner

Clinical Platform / EDC Update

  • Enterprise EDC Wins: 2 new top 20 enterprise EDC wins in Q4 (total of 8 top enterprise wins; 5 of those 8 in the last 12 months)
  • EDC Revenue Ramp: "Those 8 will continue to contribute more as we look out forward... contributing more in FY25 than they did in FY24" -- Brent Bowman
  • Revenue Ramp Timeline: "A couple of these wins... will roll out and they will get to full revenue 4 and 5 years from now" -- Peter Gassner
  • First Full Vault Clinical Ops Suite Customer: Won in Q4; more than tripled the opportunity vs. eTMF alone
  • Clinical Platform Differentiation: Agile study build, no custom coding, unified clinical operations + clinical data management platform
  • Migration Capability: Ability to migrate active studies from legacy EDC systems to Veeva CDMS; competitive advantage in enterprise sales

Data Cloud / Compass Update

  • Compass Suite Complete: Full Veeva Compass Suite (Patient, Prescriber, National) commercially available as of January 2024
  • Product Differentiation: Compass supports both retail and complex in-office therapies; data projected for 4,000+ brands; multisourced data strategy with industry-leading patient tokenization
  • Adoption Stage: Very early; early adopters excited, but large established brands more reticent to change
  • Crossix Strength: Taking market share; continued track record of customer success; largest data network

AI / Technology Commentary

  • Direct Data API: Major investment over last 2 years in Vault's Direct Data API; ~100x faster data extraction; critical for future AI applications
  • AI Strategy: Not invested heavily in LLMs directly; positioned well through data assets ("the durable thing about AI is the data sources... that's kind of where Veeva plays")
  • Industry AI Use Cases: Drug discovery AI (specialized models), general productivity with LLMs, authoring (protocol and regulatory document creation) -- still mostly experimentation, not broad production use

Macro / End Market Commentary

  • Overall Sentiment: "A little more optimism" in the industry; renewed focus on execution and long-term outlook; science going well
  • Emerging Biotech: "Still a tough slog... record number of companies get acquired and go out of business" and small number reaching escape velocity; funding environment remains difficult
  • Top 50 Pharma: "A bit resigned... there's some global conflicts, there's inflation, there's IRA act, but that hasn't hurt us too bad yet"
  • IRA (Inflation Reduction Act): More acute impact on R&D decisions now (molecule and indication selection); commercial impact doesn't really hit until 2026 and is "somewhat gradual and acute on specific companies"
  • Budget Flush: "We don't really see that too much because ours are more critical and complex things, not so much discretionary" -- Peter Gassner
  • Macro Impact on FY2025: Increased optimism unlikely to materially impact current year revenue/billings; "these are big projects... I just don't think it's stuff that would impact this year" -- Peter Gassner

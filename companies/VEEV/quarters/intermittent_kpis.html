What the Disclosure Patterns Reveal

The Three Phases of Veeva's Transformation

These 14 intermittent KPIs map directly to the three overlapping phases of Veeva's business transformation across Q1 FY2023 through Q4 FY2026:

Phase 1: The Salesforce Divorce (FY2023-FY2024). The Vault CRM platform transition was announced in Q3 FY2023, with the first customer win in Q2 FY2024 and first go-live in Q3 FY2024. The TFC standardization (Q3 FY2023 through FY2025) distorted reported financials for 8 quarters, masking ~15% underlying growth behind ~10% reported growth. Management introduced normalized billings to provide transparency.

Phase 2: The Migration Machine (FY2025-FY2026). Vault CRM went from 1 customer live (Q3 FY2024) to ~140 (Q4 FY2026). Top 20 pharma commitments accelerated from 2 to ~10, with 14 of 20 expected to choose Vault CRM. The CRM end-of-support date was pulled forward to December 2029, signaling confidence. Simultaneously, Crossix emerged from advertising headwinds to 30%+ growth, becoming the single biggest driver of revenue beats in FY2026.

Phase 3: The Platform Expansion (FY2025-FY2026+). With the Salesforce dependency resolved, Veeva is expanding into safety (3 top 20 go-lives), quality (LIMS, Batch Release), data cloud (100+ Compass brands, IQVIA partnership), and AI (agents across all products by end CY2026). EDC reached 9 of top 20, eTMF achieved 20/20 dominance, and Vault Basics reached ~100 customers. The $6B 2030 goal requires ~13% CAGR from the current $3.2B base -- exactly where these expansion vectors are pointing.

Disclosure Patterns as Leading Indicators

Metrics that appeared then disappeared: TFC impact disclosures peaked in FY2024 then faded -- the accounting distortion resolved itself. This pattern of "disclose to explain, stop when normalized" is healthy communication.

Metrics that emerged and accelerated: Vault CRM customers live (1 to 140), top 20 CRM commitments (2 to 10+), and Crossix growth (headwinds to 30%+) all followed exponential disclosure curves, reflecting genuine operational acceleration.

Metrics with persistent patience: Compass (100+ brands but prescriber "slower than expected"), Safety (3 go-lives in 3 years), and EDC (9/20 with an "air pocket") reflect the reality that displacing mission-critical systems in life sciences requires a decade of patient execution. The disclosure frequency correlates with management's emphasis, not the revenue magnitude.

The De-risking Story

The single most important trend across all 14 metrics is the declining concentration risk in CRM (25% to 20% to 10% target). Every other metric -- Crossix growth, EDC expansion, Safety go-lives, Compass adoption, Vault Basics, AI agents -- contributes to a more diversified, more durable, and larger Veeva. The intermittent nature of these disclosures reflects a company in active transformation, where the metrics that matter shift as each initiative matures.

Full Year Guidance Commentary

  • R&D Solutions subscription revenue growth guidance: 19% for FY2026 (Brian Van Wagener: "guidance for the year now at 19%")
  • Billings guidance raised -- 2 major factors: (1) FX tailwind flowing through, (2) Q1 subscription revenue outperformance flowing through to billings
  • Revenue raise driven primarily by Q1 Crossix outperformance pulling through to the balance of the year
  • Services revenue timing in Q1 expected to normalize back out over Q2-Q4 (faster-than-expected services delivery in Q1)
  • Business is "largely as we expected it to be 90 days ago" outside of Crossix outperformance and services timing
  • Macro assumption: guidance assumes "things continue roughly as they are now" -- more uncertainty in the world but customers continue to navigate it effectively; no material change to financials or pipeline observed

Long-Term Targets

  • Calendar 2025 Revenue Run Rate Goal: $3B -- achieved this quarter
  • 2030 Revenue Goal: double the $3B run rate (~$6B)
  • 2030 Goals: "double revenue and the positive impact we can have for the industry"
  • Veeva AI Efficiency Target (3-5 year horizon to 2030): Veeva AI can help increase life sciences industry efficiency by ~15% (Peter Gassner)

Revenue Run Rate Milestone

  • Achieved calendar 2025 revenue run rate goal of $3 billion (Q1 annualized: $759M x 4 = ~$3.04B)

Margin Commentary

  • Q1 was a high watermark for margins in FY2026; expected to return to ~44% non-GAAP operating margin in Q2, roughly what is expected for the balance of the year
  • Revenue outperformance (Crossix usage-based + faster services timing) not expected to be a recurring benefit to margins through the balance of the year

Vault CRM Migration Progress

  • Vault CRM Customers Live: over 80 (vs. "a handful" one year ago)
  • Vault CRM Customers Live Target (by ~May 2026): ~200 customers on track
  • Q1 FY2026 New Vault CRM Customers Added: 28 total (approximately half migrations, half net new customers to Veeva)
  • Top 20 Pharma Progress: - 4 top 20 pharmas have publicly announced decision to move to Vault CRM - 3 top 20 biopharmas going live in calendar 2026 - One top 20 pharma (SVP of Digital confirmed at Summit) going live "in a month or so" with full global rollout (~20,000 people) by end of calendar 2025 - Progress is on track or slightly ahead of expectations from 1-2 years ago
  • Red Zone: starts to kick in during 2027 timeframe; large customers want to make decisions before that, which is why most large companies expected to decide in 2025 or 2026
  • Why Customers Choose Vault CRM: better than Veeva CRM, AI strategy (agents in product by end of year), brings sales/marketing/medical together in single customer database; innovation pace
  • Why Some Don't Choose Veeva: some companies want more of a custom-type product; expected to be a minority

CRM Expansion Products

  • Campaign Manager & Service Center: early days, have some early customers in both areas
  • Requirement: customer must be on Vault CRM first
  • Focus: initially on smaller and midsized customers; over time, expect progress in midsize and large companies
  • Value Proposition: bringing sales, marketing, and medical together in a single customer database -- unprecedented in life sciences

Veeva AI Strategy

  • Veeva AI First Release: planned for December 2025
  • AI Agents Planned for First Release: - CRM Bot (pre-call planning agent) - Voice Control - Compliant Free Text - MLR Bot (medical legal regulatory approval agent) - Safety AI agent (transcribes pretext into a safety case)
  • Approach: deeply embedded in core applications, not generic AI; industry-specific processes with deep industry-specific AI
  • Monetization: will charge an appropriate license fee; want pricing to be reasonable and broadly adopted; packaging not yet worked out; no specific revenue projections at this time
  • Customer Reception: "very positive" -- customers need AI working with core applications, trust Veeva with data, can see tangible use cases
  • Separate AI Team: dedicated team working on Veeva AI
  • Direct Data API & AI Partner Program: part of the overall AI strategy alongside Veeva AI applications

Crossix Performance & Commentary

  • Crossix Growth Rate: growing at more than 30% year-over-year
  • Q1 Outperformance Driver: predominantly usage-based area of the business outperformed expectations
  • Lumpiness: usage-based business can be "a little lumpy from quarter to quarter" -- reflected in full year guide
  • Product Breadth Expansion: historically started with digital consumer measurement and optimization; now strong in HCP (health care provider) measurement and optimization, plus audiences across both segments
  • Deal Size: comprehensive Crossix deal value is "maybe double in the size of what it was 3 years ago"
  • No Evidence of Pull Forward: Peter Gassner stated "I wouldn't say any particular pull forward we're seeing"
  • Strategic Direction: tighter integration with CRM products, optimizing for long-term agreements, getting higher in the account
  • Data Network: supports both Crossix and Compass; covers retail, specialty drugs, specialty pharmacy, hospital-administered drugs, complex therapies; can project sales data for ~4,000 brands across retail and nonretail

Data Cloud / Compass Update

  • Compass Brands Added in Q1: ~10 brands (mostly in patient area)
  • Compass Status: "marathon" -- industry used to doing things a certain way; hard business to get into but making good progress
  • Prescriber & National Compass: still in early adopter phase; few early adopters; pipeline getting bigger; focused on adding to data network and customer success; not a mature/scale business yet
  • CRM Pulse: launched March 2025 -- powerful offering for segmentation and targeting "only Veeva can provide"
  • Data Cloud Complete Suite: reference data, deep data, performance data, and pulse data on common data architecture

Clinical / R&D Commentary

  • eTMF (Electronic Trial Master File): 19 of top 20 pharmas; feel they have "a pretty good path to 20 of the top 20"
  • EDC (Electronic Data Capture): 9 of top 20 using Veeva EDC - Some are very early and are multiyear ramp deals - Path to additional top 20s, but nothing imminent - Revenue ramps steadily contributing to revenue base
  • Clinical Platform Vision: integrate clinical operations to clinical data management, reaching out to research sites
  • R&D Solutions Subscription Revenue Growth: 19% for FY2026 guidance (strong growth engine)

Horizontal Business Applications

  • First Focus Area: horizontal CRM (outside of life sciences)
  • Team: different team from life sciences; different operating model; core team in place, will add incrementally over next 2-4 years
  • Target Market: large enterprises (not SMB/HubSpot segment); many entry points
  • First Customers: "hope to have first customers by the end of the year" (Peter Gassner)
  • Differentiation: technology breakthroughs, PBC orientation, business model, customer success focus
  • M&A: keeping "really wide aperture" -- most likely in horizontal applications area (CRM or other); disciplined approach; looking for Crossix-type acquisitions where they can create a lot of value

Macro Environment Assessment

  • Overall: "more uncertain today compared to 90 days ago" but no material change seen in financials or pipeline
  • Customer Conversations: discussions reflect "general unease" -- multifaceted concerns about: - FDA staffing and drug approval timing (PDUFA dates) - Drug pricing executive order impacts on M&A plans - Funding environment for small biotechs (interest rates, bond prices, next round availability)
  • No Financial Impact Observed: deals progressing as expected, contracts renewing, projects on or ahead of schedule
  • Most Durable Business: long-term subscription contracts (not susceptible to short-term swings)
  • Most Exposed: (1) business consulting/services engagements (quicker to be impacted), (2) small emerging biotechs (funding-dependent)
  • Tariffs / Reshoring: neither a headwind nor tailwind for Veeva; no evidence of impact so far; possible project delays if companies need to prioritize emergency reshoring over long-term capability projects
  • Crossix Insulation: shorter business cycle, quick ROI -- "may not be affected" by macro uncertainty

Commercial Summit Takeaways (May 2025)

  • Attendance: ~2,000 people
  • Customer Sentiment: good; Peter Gassner had 50+ one-on-one conversations of 5-10+ minutes each
  • Vault CRM Vibe: "whole different vibe" -- 80 customers live vs. handful a year ago; couple of big top 20 customers midway in flight
  • Veeva AI Reception: "aha moment" when showing CRM Bot demo; customers showed relief that Veeva has a measured, durable AI approach
  • Data Cloud Awareness: becoming really real; people starting to understand Commercial Cloud + Data Cloud working together
  • Industry Trend: leaning into digital marketing and data/analytics more than prior year; getting more systematic in commercial
  • Veeva CRM as Start: "CRM is the start of our commercial core CRM" -- add-on products (marketing, Service Center), Data Cloud, Crossix, business consulting are the broader commercial vision

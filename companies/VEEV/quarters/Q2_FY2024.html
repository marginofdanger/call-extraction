Macro Assumptions

  • FY2024 Macro Assumption: continuation of current environment; "not better, not worse" vs. H1 FY2024
  • SMB / Emerging Biotech Impact: continued headwinds from tight funding environment; "playing out as we expected"
  • R&D Budget Industry Trend: ~3% growth in R&D spend on average across life sciences industry

Vault CRM Migration

  • First Vault CRM Customer Win: announced during Q2 (first win, less than a year since announcing the migration)
  • First Customer Go-Live: planned for Q4 FY2024 (calendar Q4 2023); "a little bit earlier than we had expected"
  • Vault CRM General Availability (GA): April 2024; "will be the go-forward product for all new customers at that point"
  • New Customers Post-GA: all new customers after April 2024 will be on Vault CRM
  • First Migration: starting in 2025
  • Migration of Most Customers: 2026 through 2028; "expect to move the vast majority of that over to Vault CRM over, let's say, the next 5 years"
  • Veeva CRM + Add-ons as % of Total Revenue: ~25%
  • Migration Cost Estimate: cost of migrating to Vault CRM could be ~20% of the total cost of building something new ("we're building a lot of the tools to make that migration a whole lot easier")
  • New Capabilities: Vault CRM includes all functionality of Veeva CRM plus new omnichannel capabilities, including Vault CRM Service Center
  • Salesforce Partnership: "business as usual"; they continue to support existing customers; Veeva expects them to continue through the end of the agreement
  • Salesforce Competitive Threat: "I don't see this as an attractive market for them" -- Paul Shawah; building a pharma CRM is "really deep life sciences specific" and different across geographies
  • IQVIA Competition: "nothing has really changed"; Veeva continues to "win most of the deals"

Clinical Data Management & EDC

  • EDC Customer Wins (Q2): 8 new wins in the quarter
  • EDC Top-20 Wins: 6 top-20 EDC wins; these are multiyear (3, 4, 5 year) deals that ramp over time
  • EDC Revenue Ramp Pattern: "in the first year, it's not going to be a significant amount. And as you get into years 3, 4 and 5, it will become much more material. Revenue will follow that billings path"
  • Primary Competition: Medidata (leading by volume); also winning from smaller SMB-targeted competitors
  • Competitive Advantages: faster study amendments (no data unload/reload), dramatically faster study definition (4 weeks vs. 8 weeks), less custom programming required, integration with clinical operations suite
  • Clinical Trial Volume Sensitivity: "minimal, a very small amount is going to be variable based on near-term trial volumes" -- most deals are multiyear predefined ramping deals
  • RTSM and ePRO: described as opportunity bigger than EDC; customers expected to be cautious (patient safety, patient-facing); expect trial-by-trial adoption initially, potentially enterprise standards over time
  • Bring Your Own Device (ePRO): Veeva pushing patients to use their own web browser / phone vs. provisioned iPad

Veeva Data Cloud (Compass, OpenData, Link)

Compass

  • Compass Patient Wins: continued success in Q2
  • Compass Revenue Timeline: "Compass is not going to be a $60 million business, even a $50 million business... anytime in the next few years"
  • Compass Product Roadmap: Compass Prescriber and Compass National not yet released; customer success leading indicator expected ~12 months out
  • Strategic Vision: "setting out to have a unique, highly integrated, highly excellent suite of data products from Compass to Link to OpenData and to be the leader in life sciences data"
  • Target: displace IQVIA as life sciences data leader

Link

  • Link Revenue Concentration: "probably more than 90%" from Link for Key People
  • Link Product Breadth: Link for Key Accounts (multiple countries), Link for Scientific Awareness, Link for Medical Insights, Link Workflow, Link TrialBase, Link SiteBase
  • Link as Growth Driver: "1 of the primary drivers" of Q2 commercial revenue growth and balance of year

Crossix

  • Crossix Trends: "ups and downs quarter-to-quarter"; leading measurement solution for digital marketing
  • Strategic Direction: integrating more tightly with CRM towards enterprise license agreements; moving away from stand-alone measurement tool
  • Marketing Spend Environment: "increased scrutiny" on marketing spend vs. a couple of years ago

Capital Allocation

  • Cash Position: ~$3.9B total (cash + short-term investments); on pace to eclipse $4B in H2 FY2024
  • Annual Cash Flow Generation: on pace for ~$1B in annual cash flow
  • Primary Use of Cash: M&A focused; "M&A is a use of cash. If we see the right acquisition, that's what we're going to do"
  • M&A Philosophy: "disciplined about it in our strategy and approach"; track record of successful deals
  • No Buyback / Dividend Program Mentioned

Macro Environment & Industry Commentary

  • Macro Outlook: "continuation of what we have been seeing, so not better, not worse" in H1 FY2024; same expectation for H2
  • SMB / Emerging Biotech: continued headwinds; "when the funding environment is down, they might not be able to fund their clinical trials, get the money they need to expand"
  • Change Expectation: "I don't know when things would change, who knows, right? They may change in the future, but you won't know that until they change"
  • R&D Budgets: "we're seeing growth in R&D budgets on average around 3% growth in R&D spend" across life sciences industry
  • Life Sciences Industry Health: "it's a healthy industry"; commercial and R&D both impacted by macro but "nothing more than what we've been seeing"
  • China Exposure: regulations impacting data transfer outside China, affecting Veeva CRM for multinationals in China; Veeva has China SFA product solution; impact "is not material... either in this year or next year"
  • R&D Summit: ~2,000 attendees expected at Boston R&D customer summit; described as "massive cross-selling event"

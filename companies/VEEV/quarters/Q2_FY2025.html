Guidance Changes vs Prior Quarter (Q1 FY2025 Call)

  • Total Revenue: Raised (driven by commercial subscription outperformance in content and Crossix areas)
  • Non-GAAP Operating Income: Raised
  • Non-GAAP Diluted EPS: Raised
  • Commercial Subscription Revenue: Guide raised for the year (per Tim Cabral; driven by commercial content and Crossix)
  • R&D Subscription Revenue: Modest increase from prior quarter (which had been trimmed); better visibility into second-half pipeline, continued productive conversations with customers on larger deals that had moved to the right
  • Professional Services Revenue: Lowered slightly; some delays in service projects, and some customers contracting directly with third-party SIs instead of through Veeva

Long-Term Targets

  • TAM: $20 billion total addressable market - Commercial and Clinical: ~35% each (largest areas) - Quality: next largest area (LIMS, Batch Release emerging) - Regulatory and Safety: remaining portions
  • Vault Basics Addressable Market: $100M+ (per Peter Gassner); ~80% is net new opportunity not previously available to Veeva
  • Service Center + Campaign Manager + Patient CRM: Combined opportunity roughly the same size as core CRM (per Paul Shawah)
  • Capital Allocation: Focused primarily on dry powder for potential M&A; no change in strategy
  • Operating Margin Trajectory: Non-GAAP operating margin back above 40% (41.4% in Q2); management focused on being "appropriately lean" while investing in large opportunity

Key Operational Metrics

  • Vault CRM Wins in Q2: 14 new customer wins (GA in April 2024)
  • Vault CRM Customers Live: 15 customers
  • Vault Basics Customers Live: 12 (launched ~1 year ago; more signed but not yet live)
  • CRM Suite as % of Commercial: ~50% of commercial business
  • Non-CRM Commercial: ~50% of commercial, growing much faster than CRM suite
  • Commercial Subscription Outperformance Drivers: Commercial content and Crossix
  • R&D Subscription Improvement: Modest increase from better second-half pipeline visibility vs 90 days ago

Q3 Commentary

  • Billings Growth: Q3 implies ~5% YoY growth in billings (per analyst), stepping back up in Q4
  • Billings Shape Drivers: (1) Q3 FY2024 was a relatively stronger billing quarter (harder compare); (2) billing term changes driven by movement of renewal dates also impact normalized billing
  • Services: Some continued lumpiness; attach rates differ across products; a few customers (one quite large) chose to contract directly with third-party SIs rather than through Veeva

CRM Migration & Vault CRM Update

  • Vault CRM GA: April 2024; exclusively what Veeva is now selling into market
  • Q2 CRM Wins: 14 wins; "winning virtually every CRM deal"; no deals lost to competitors
  • Next Release (Q4 FY2025): Full functionality parity with Veeva CRM plus additional unique features (better content integration, opportunity management built-in)
  • Migration Timeline: First migrations with small customers expected by end of Q4 FY2025; larger migrations starting in CY2025
  • Top 20 Pharma Migration: One top 20 customer expected live in 50+ countries by end of CY2025
  • Enterprise Commitments: Expect additional top 20 commitments over the next 12 months
  • Salesforce Royalty Impact on Gross Margin: Non-CRM revenue growth (no Salesforce royalty) contributing to gross margin improvement
  • Competitive Position: Primary competitor is Salesforce (building on IQVIA's OCE product rights); IQVIA's OCE product "hasn't performed well in the marketplace"; Salesforce plans first release toward end of CY2025
  • Service Center: Generally available; primarily adopted by SMB/pre-commercial customers first; requires Vault CRM as prerequisite
  • Campaign Manager: On track to be available by end of CY2024; will also require Vault CRM

Vault CDMS (Clinical Data Management)

  • Top 20 Pharma Adoption: Ramps going well; value realization "better than expected" in several areas
  • Site Experience: Positive feedback on UI; no negative feedback from sites
  • Sponsor Value: Building studies faster, less custom programming, fewer errors
  • CDB (Clinical Database) Product: Significant cost savings in data cleaning when used with EDC; auto-finding data anomalies, sending queries to sites, auto-closing queries without human intervention
  • Pipeline: "Very healthy" in top 20 and enterprise
  • CRO Progress: Area where Veeva would like to make more progress, particularly in large CROs; "not yet to where we would like it"
  • Structural Advantage: Having both clinical data management (EDC) and clinical operations (CTMS, eTMF) enables Veeva to handle intricate integration that customers don't want to do themselves

Data Cloud / Compass

  • Compass Patient: Good progress with sales and new brand additions
  • Compass Prescriber & National: More education phase; some sales activity; no customers using for compensation yet; expect compensation use to begin in CY2025
  • Compass Differentiation: Projecting not only retail data but specialty data for ~4,000 brands (specialty pharmacy/complex therapies); fundamentally new approach
  • Compensation Decisions: Generally annual decisions; customers won't switch mid-year
  • Long-Term Outlook: "Very bullish" but "big hill to climb" -- will take time

Crossix Performance

  • Q2 Performance: Second consecutive quarter of solid performance
  • Two Business Lines: (1) Measurement and optimization of media campaigns; (2) Digital audiences for marketing campaigns
  • Audience Business: More focus in last year; results paying off
  • Not Cyclical: Strength attributed to increasing market leadership and product quality, not election cycle or seasonal factors
  • Competitive Dynamics: Some competitors' ROI didn't pan out well; customers returning to quality

AI & Direct Data API

  • AI Partner Program: Early traction
  • Commercial AI Use Cases: Data science (next best action, dynamic targeting, pre-call planning)
  • R&D AI Use Cases: Document generation (clinical study reports), specific medical coding
  • Direct Data API: Purchased product (not free); covers compute costs; no implementation required -- "turn it on and it's on"
  • API Philosophy: Enabling partner ecosystem innovation; Veeva won't disrupt partners having customer success; may step in only if major use case not being served by ecosystem

Safety Suite

  • Strategy: Cloud-based Safety Suite with significant innovation
  • Customers: Some live and "happy for a period of time"
  • Legacy Competitors: Mainly 2 legacy providers
  • Potential Catalysts: (1) Legacy provider issues becoming untenable; (2) EDC-to-Safety integration driving significant cost savings; (3) AI capabilities via Vault platform and Direct Data API that legacy systems cannot match
  • Structural Advantage: Veeva knows what it's doing and is moving forward; legacy providers will not be able to keep up "at some point"

Vault Basics

  • Launched: ~1 year ago (mid-2023)
  • Customers Live: 12; more signed up
  • Addressable Market: $100M+ per Peter Gassner
  • Net New Opportunity: ~80% of Vault Basics opportunity was not previously available to Veeva (companies on paper, spreadsheets, shared drives)
  • Strategic Value: Teaching Veeva how to simplify and standardize, which will benefit enterprise/top 20 business over time
  • Expansion Plans: Will bring Vault Basics to more applications over time, including commercial areas (in a slightly different way)

Macro Environment

  • Overall: "Unchanged from 90 days ago" per Peter Gassner
  • No Thawing: Peter specifically pushed back on "thawing" characterization; described it as "flowing a little bit better now" due to no additional negative macro news in last 90 days, plus some pent-up demand releasing
  • Q2 Performance: Attributed to "strong execution" and "maybe some good luck and no bad luck"
  • Deal Cycles: Some elongation persists from macroeconomic headwinds; Veeva executing through it by staying close to customers

Upcoming Events

  • R&D Summit: Coming up (not dated on call); expected to be "more impactful than it ever has been" given company's increased scale
  • Investor Day: November 7, 2024

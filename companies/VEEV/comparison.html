18 Key Takeaways

1
Perfect guidance beat record. Veeva beat next-quarter revenue guidance in all 14 reported quarters (14/14) and non-GAAP operating income in 13 of 14 (with Q2 FY2023 at midpoint). No quarter ever missed the low end. The magnitude of beats has been increasing — FY2026 averages +3.2% revenue vs midpoint.
2
V-shaped margin recovery. Non-GAAP operating margin went from 39.5% (Q1 FY2023) to 29.8% (Q1 FY2024 TFC trough) to 46.1% (Q1 FY2026 peak) — a 1,630bps swing from trough to peak. FY2026 full-year margins are guided at ~44.8%, structurally 500bps+ above pre-TFC levels, driven by subscription mix shift, Salesforce royalty reduction, and lean operations.
3
R&D Solutions is now the majority engine. R&D subscription revenue grew from 43% of total subscription (Q1 FY2023) to 53% (Q3 FY2026), more than doubling from $174.9M to $364.8M quarterly. The Development Cloud — clinical, quality, regulatory, safety — is where the TAM is largest and penetration is lowest.
4
Vault CRM migration is executing ahead. From announcement (Q3 FY2023) to 115 live customers in 3 years, with 9 of top 20 committed vs Salesforce's 3. The "red zone" deadline pressure (2027+) will drive remaining decisions. Revenue is margin-neutral near-term but margin-accretive long-term as Salesforce royalties roll off post-2025.
5
Crossix became a breakout growth driver. From macro headwinds (~10% growth in FY2023) to 30%+ growth (Q1 FY2026), Crossix transformed from a headwind narrative to a major outperformance driver. Deal sizes roughly doubled in 3 years. Management sees Crossix potentially equaling the entire CRM suite (~$600M run-rate) by 2030.
6
IQVIA resolution removed the last major barrier. The Q2 FY2026 settlement ended a 10-year dispute, freeing Veeva Network and Nitro products. Combined with the Salesforce liberation (Vault CRM), two of three historical commercial barriers are now removed. Management frames this as "the biggest news in commercial for a long time."
7
EDC penetration validates the clinical platform. EDC went from 0 to 9 of top 20 pharma in 3 years. eTMF reached 20/20. The Q4 FY2025 "all-in" deal (6 clinical apps from one top 20) signals platform-level purchasing is emerging, which multiplies per-customer revenue by 3x+ vs eTMF alone.
8
AI strategy evolved from skepticism to full commitment. Peter Gassner's stance shifted from "I don't think it's a revolution" (Q4 FY2023) to agents in "literally all applications" by end of 2026. No material revenue expected until FY2028+, but Safety and Clinical areas could see ~50% reduction in outsourced labor — expanding addressable market significantly.
9
$6.6B cash pile with no deployment. Cash more than doubled from $2.8B to $6.6B over 15 quarters with zero acquisitions, buybacks, or dividends. Management consistently signals M&A as the primary use — now focused on horizontal CRM opportunities outside life sciences. Annual OCF crossed $1B in FY2025.
10
Revenue target progression: $2B → $3B → $6B. Crossed $2B run rate (Q1 FY2023), achieved $3B run rate on schedule (Q1 FY2026), now targeting $6B by 2030. FY2026 guidance of ~$3.17B implies ~15% CAGR needed to reach $6B by FY2031 — achievable given current growth trajectory of ~14% with multiple product ramps ahead.
11
Horizontal expansion is the next chapter. First disclosed at Q4 FY2024, horizontal CRM outside life sciences has progressed from concept to "first customers by end of year" (Q1 FY2026). A separate team with a distinct operating model is being built. This represents a new S-curve opportunity but is too early to quantify.
12
Guidance philosophy: consistently conservative. In 15 quarters, management never once over-promised and under-delivered. Full-year guidance was beaten every year. The pattern is clear: set guidance ~2-3% below likely outcomes, raise through the year, then beat the raised number. This predictability is a key part of the Veeva investment thesis.

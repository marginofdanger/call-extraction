5 Key Takeaways

1
Perfect record — never missed guidance in 13 quarters. Across both revenue and Adjusted EBITDA %, Broadcom has never come in below its guided figure. This is an exceptional track record for a company guiding single-point estimates rather than ranges.
2
Conservative guidance philosophy. Broadcom consistently guides conservatively and then delivers at or above the target. Of 13 revenue pairs, 10 were beats and 3 were met exactly — zero misses. This pattern suggests deliberate sandbagging of guidance.
3
Revenue beats accelerated in FY2025. The FQ4 2025 beat of +3.4% was the largest single-quarter revenue beat in the dataset, driven by surging AI demand. FQ1 2025 also beat by +2.1%, well above the historical average.
4
EBITDA guidance also conservative — beat 9 of 13, never missed. The average EBITDA % beat of +78 bps compounds over time. Every beat flows directly to the bottom line, meaning Broadcom consistently delivers more profit than guided.
5
VMware integration year (FY2024) — shifted to annual guidance, still beat by 3.2%. When Broadcom acquired VMware in late 2023, it suspended quarterly guidance due to integration variability. Even with the uncertainty, the initial $50B annual target was exceeded by $1.6B.
6
AI revenue outlook systematically underestimated. FY2024 AI revenue was raised four consecutive times: from an implied ~$7.5B to $12B, with the actual coming in at $12.2B (+63% vs initial). This reflects the difficulty of forecasting exponential AI infrastructure demand.
7
FQ4 2025 was the largest revenue beat (+3.4%), driven by AI upside. At $18.0B actual vs ~$17.4B guided, this quarter demonstrated that as Broadcom’s AI revenue share grows, the magnitude of beats may increase given conservative guidance practices.
8
Broadcom uses single-point revenue estimates (not ranges), yet never misses. Unlike most semiconductor peers who provide guidance ranges, Broadcom commits to a single number. Despite this tighter target, they have a perfect hit-or-beat record.
9
EBITDA % improved from ~63% to 68% over the period — every beat accumulated. Over 13 quarters, Adjusted EBITDA margin expanded by roughly 500 bps. This reflects both operating leverage on AI revenue growth and VMware integration synergies.

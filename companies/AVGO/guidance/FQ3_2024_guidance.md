# Broadcom Inc. FQ3_2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** September 5, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Fiscal Q3 2024 (May - Jul '24)

## FQ3 2024 Actuals (Reported)

- **Gross Margin:** 77.4% of revenue
- **Operating Expenses:** $2.2B; R&D $1.5B (up YoY due to VMware)

  - Semiconductor OpEx: $881M (+11% YoY, increased R&D)
  - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 56%
- **Infrastructure Software:** $5.8B (+200% YoY), 44% of total revenue
  - VMware contribution: $3.8B (up from $2.7B in Q2)

  - Software OpEx: $1.3B
  - Software Operating Margin: 67% (69% ex-transition costs)

### FQ3 2024 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown
- **Networking:** $4.0B, +43% YoY, 55% of semiconductor revenue — custom AI accelerators grew 3.5x YoY; Ethernet switching (TH5/Jericho3-AI) grew 4x YoY; optical lasers/PIN diodes grew 3x; PCIe switches more than doubled; shipping 5nm 400G NICs and 800G DSPs in volume
- **Wireless:** $1.7B, +1% YoY, 23% of semiconductor revenue
- **Server Storage Connectivity:** $861M, +5% QoQ, -25% YoY — recovery from Q2 bottom
- **Broadband:** $557M, -49% YoY, 8% of semiconductor revenue — remains weak; telco/service provider spending paused; recovery expected in 2025
- **Industrial:** $164M, -31% YoY — approaching bottom; Q4 expected to recover sequentially
- **Non-AI Networking (enterprise/traditional):** bottomed in Q2; Q3 up 17% QoQ but still down 41% YoY

### FQ3 2024 AI Revenue

- **Custom AI Accelerator Customers:** now 3 meaningful customers in production (up from 2 in Q1); all 3 have production-volume deployments in AI data centers (not proof of concept)
- **Next-Gen XPU Wins:** awarded next-generation custom AI accelerators for hyperscale customers (confirmed in Q2, reiterated)
- **Supply:** able to meet upside orders; seeing pull-ins and upsides from hyperscalers rushing to deploy AI capacity
- **Upside Demand:** continues to see upside orders; expects more pull-ins in FY2025 (not included in guidance)

## Non-AI Semiconductor Recovery Outlook
- **Bottom Confirmed:** FY2024 was the lowest point; non-AI semi bottomed in Q1-Q2; Q3 showed recovery
- **Non-AI Bookings:** up 20% in Q3 — clear indicator of recovery
- **Enterprise IT Spending:** past the bottom; Q3 was sequential recovery from Q1/Q2 bottom
- **Broadband:** close to bottom but not yet there; bookings improving; recovery expected in FY2025
- **AI-Driven Upgrade Cycle:** as AI permeates enterprises, need to upgrade servers, storage, networking across entire ecosystem; could drive up cycle that meets or surpasses previous peaks
- **Non-VMware Software:** reached stable level (~$2B/quarter); puts and takes from Brocade cycles

## Custom Silicon / XPU Strategic View
- **Hock Tan View Change:** previously believed merchant silicon would win; has now "flipped" view — large hyperscalers will move to custom ASICs/XPUs for AI accelerators
- **Two-Market Framework:**
  1. Hyperscalers: have scale, financial resources, and motivation to create custom silicon; will all eventually build their own custom accelerators (at different rates and timelines); economically rational given GPU supply constraints and power efficiency
  2. Enterprise: will use merchant GPUs; no resources or return on investment to justify custom silicon
- **Transition Timeline:** accelerating but will take years; significant learning process; largest customer has been at it 7+ years; others catching up (won't take 7 years, but step-by-step)
- **Power Efficiency:** ASICs have superior performance-per-watt vs GPUs; as power becomes defining factor for deployment (500 MW, 1 GW data centers), custom silicon becomes more attractive

## Seagate Hard Disk Drive Partnership
- Acquired Seagate's HDD SoC IP assets earlier in year; structured as IP purchase + engineering talent acquisition
- Broadcom believes nearline HDD storage will sustain as meaningful alternative to flash for hyperscalers for 5+ years
- Road map ambition: enable 50TB drives within 5 years (from current 22-24TB)
- Collaboration benefits entire HDD industry (Seagate and beyond)

## M&A Outlook
- Not currently pursuing acquisitions; focused on VMware transformation (1-2 more years to complete)
- Not participating directly in NVIDIA's Blackwell road map; happy to provide base technology (optical components, DSPs) as part of ecosystem

# Broadcom Inc. FQ2_2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** June 12, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Fiscal Q2 2024 (Feb - Apr '24) — first full quarter of VMware contribution

---

## FQ2 2024 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $12.5B (+43% YoY; ex-VMware +12% YoY)
- **Gross Margin:** 76.2% of revenue
- **Operating Expenses:** $2.4B; R&D $1.5B (both up YoY due to VMware consolidation)
- **Operating Income:** $7.1B (+32% YoY), 57% of revenue
- **Operating Income (ex-transition costs):** $7.4B (+36% YoY), 59% of revenue
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $7.4B (60% of revenue; excludes $149M depreciation)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $4.4B (36% of revenue); ex-restructuring/integration spend of $830M → $5.3B (+18% YoY), 42% of revenue
- **FCF % Decline vs FY2023:** higher cash interest expense from VMware acquisition debt + higher cash taxes (higher U.S. income mix + Section 174 delay)
- **CapEx:** $132M

### FQ2 2024 Segment Revenue
- **Semiconductor Solutions:** $7.2B (+6% YoY), 58% of total revenue
  - Semiconductor GM: ~67% (-370 bps YoY, higher mix of custom AI accelerators)
  - Semiconductor OpEx: $868M (+4% YoY, increased R&D investment)
  - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 55%
- **Infrastructure Software:** $5.3B (+170% YoY), 42% of total revenue
  - VMware contribution: $2.7B (up from $2.1B in Q1)
  - Non-VMware (CA/Symantec/Brocade): ~$2.6B (strong Brocade cycle + strong mainframe renewals; Q1 strength not sustainable)
  - Software GM: 88%
  - Software OpEx: $1.5B
  - Software Operating Margin: 60% (64% ex-transition costs)

### FQ2 2024 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown
- **Networking:** $3.8B, +44% YoY, 53% of semiconductor revenue — AI networking + custom accelerators; doubled switch sales YoY (Tomahawk 5, Jericho3 deployed with Arista, Dell, Juniper, Supermicro); doubled PCIe switches and NICs in AI back-end fabric; 800G optical transition driving DSPs, lasers, PIN diodes; developing next-gen 1.6T connectivity
- **Wireless:** $1.6B, +2% YoY, -19% QoQ (seasonal), 22% of semiconductor revenue — North American customer engagement deep, strategic, multiyear
- **Server Storage Connectivity:** $824M, -27% YoY, 11% of semiconductor revenue — Q2 is the bottom; expect modest second-half recovery
- **Broadband:** $730M, -39% YoY, 10% of semiconductor revenue — broadband remains weak on telco/service provider spending pause; bottom in second half; recovery in 2025
- **Industrial:** $234M, -10% YoY
- **Non-AI Networking (enterprise/traditional hyperscaler):** at bottom Q2, same trajectory as server storage — modest recovery expected

### FQ2 2024 AI Revenue
- **AI Revenue:** $3.1B in Q2 (+280% YoY)
- **FY2024 AI Revenue Outlook:** raised to >$11B (prior: >$10B); $11B is floor — flat from Q2 level would give $11.6B; general trajectory is "getting better"
- **AI Revenue Mix Shift:** was 80% accelerators/20% networking; now ~2/3 accelerators, 1/3 networking; heading towards 60/40 by year end (networking proportion increasing as clusters deploy)
- **Next-Gen AI Accelerators:** awarded next-generation custom AI accelerators for hyperscale customers (securing forward pipeline)
- **AI Cluster Market Position:** 7 of 8 largest AI clusters in deployment use Broadcom Ethernet solutions; expect all mega-scale GPU deployments on Ethernet next year
- **Networking-to-GPU Value Ratio:** ~25-30% of GPU value goes to networking infrastructure (including optical interconnects)
- **Custom AI Accelerator Profitability:** operating margin level easily as profitable or more than normal Broadcom margins given scale; logic portion commands corporate GM; HBM dilutes gross margin but not operating margin

- **ABV (Annualized Booking Value):** VMware $1.2B→$1.9B Q1→Q2; total Broadcom software $1.9B→$2.8B Q1→Q2
- **SKU Simplification:** reduced from >8,000 disparate SKUs to 4 core product offerings
- **Channel Conflict:** eliminated huge amount of channel conflict; simplified go-to-market flow
- **VMware Revenue Trajectory:** $2.1B Q1→$2.7B Q2; on track towards $4B/quarter run rate

- **Restructuring/Integration Costs YTD:** ~$2B
- **VMware Operating Margins:** expect to converge to classic Broadcom software levels by FY2025

## Networking Technology Update
- **Tomahawk 5:** strong adoption in AI data centers; deployed with Arista, Dell, Juniper, Supermicro
- **Tomahawk 6:** on track for late 2025 (100-terabit switch; 2-year cadence from TH5 launch in 2023)
- **1.6T Connectivity:** developing next-gen switches, DSPs, and optics for 1.6T to scale larger AI clusters
- **SONiC/SAI:** complete network operating system deployed at many hyperscalers; open platform differentiator
- **Portfolio Flexibility:** supports both switch-scheduled and endpoint-scheduled AI network architectures; unique differentiator among competitors

## VMware Go-to-Market Model
- **Three Consumption Channels:**
  1. Direct: Broadcom/VMware direct sales for top strategic customers
  2. Channel: 15,000 VARs supported by distributors for long tail of 300,000 customers
  3. Managed Service Providers: VMware-as-a-service for enterprises wanting hosted infrastructure
- **VCF Pricing:** priced down to comparable with compute-only virtualization (compelling adoption incentive)
- **Subscription Conversion:** VMware late to subscription vs Microsoft/Salesforce/Oracle; converting aggressively
- **M&A Outlook:** no rush to buy another company given 12% organic growth; but all options always open — long-term model is grow through combination of acquisition and organic improvement

## Competitive Dynamics
- **Custom ASIC vs GPU:** not competing with NVIDIA on merchant GPU; serving hyperscalers who want customized silicon for specific LLMs; complementary positioning
- **Ethernet Networking vs Spectrum-X:** Broadcom has 25+ years of Ethernet expertise; captured multiple market transitions; supplement and complement GPUs for larger clusters

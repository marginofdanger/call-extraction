# Broadcom Inc. FQ1_2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** March 7, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Fiscal Q1 2024 (Nov '23 - Jan '24) — 14-week quarter; includes 10.5 weeks of VMware contribution

## FQ1 2024 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $12.0B (+34% YoY; ex-VMware +11% YoY)
- **Gross Margin:** 75.4% of revenue
- **Operating Expenses:** $2.2B; R&D $1.4B (both up YoY primarily due to VMware)
- **Operating Income:** $6.8B (+26% YoY), 57% of revenue
- **Operating Income (ex-transition costs):** $7.1B (+30% YoY), 59% of revenue; transition costs $226M
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $7.2B (60% of revenue; excludes $139M depreciation)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $4.7B (39% of revenue); ex-restructuring/integration spend of $658M → 45% of revenue
- **CapEx:** $122M

### FQ1 2024 Segment Revenue
- **Semiconductor Solutions:** $7.4B (+4% YoY), 62% of total revenue
  - Semiconductor GM: ~67% (-190 bps YoY, product mix)
  - Semiconductor OpEx: $865M (+8% YoY, reflects 14-week quarter)
  - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 56%
- **Infrastructure Software:** $4.6B (+153% YoY), 38% of total revenue
  - VMware contribution: $2.1B (10.5 weeks)
  - Non-VMware (CA/Symantec/Brocade): ~$2.5B (strong contract renewals, especially mainframe)
  - Software GM: 88%
  - Software OpEx: $1.3B
  - Software Operating Margin: 59% (64% ex-transition costs)

### FQ1 2024 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown
- **Networking:** $3.3B, +46% YoY, 45% of semiconductor revenue — strong demand for custom AI accelerators at 2 hyperscale customers; Tomahawk 5 800G switches, Thor 2 Ethernet NICs, DSPs, optical components strong demand from hyperscalers and large enterprises building AI data centers
- **Wireless:** $2.0B, -1% QoQ, -4% YoY, 27% of semiconductor revenue — engagement deep, strategic, multiyear
- **Server Storage Connectivity:** $887M, -29% YoY, 12% of semiconductor revenue — weaker demand in first half; expect second half recovery
- **Broadband:** $940M, -23% YoY, 13% of semiconductor revenue — cyclical trough in FY2024; telco spending continues to weaken; no improvement expected until late in year
- **Industrial:** $215M, -6% YoY

### FQ1 2024 AI Revenue
- **AI Revenue:** $2.3B in Q1 (quadrupled YoY)
- **FY2024 AI Revenue Outlook:** >$10B, ~35% of semiconductor revenue (raised from >25%/$7.5B prior guidance)
- **AI Revenue Mix:** ~70% AI accelerators (custom ASICs), ~30% networking components
  - Of 30% networking: more than half is switches/routers (~20% of AI total); remainder is DSPs, PAM-4 optical components, retimers, PCIe switches
- **Custom AI Accelerator Customers:** 2 hyperscalers in production; one engaged for 8 years; takes years of iterative work (matching silicon to foundation models, firmware, software stack) to reach production
- **Supply Constraint:** no supply chain constraint on AI accelerators — sufficient lead time from customers
- **Tomahawk 5 Adoption:** going "great guns" — surpassing prior adoption rates; demand driven by AI data center scale-out, not traditional cloud scale-out
- **AI Accelerator Gross Margin:** naturally lower than traditional silicon due to HBM memory content (purchased from memory suppliers, cannot apply logic-level margins to memory); logic portion commands corporate-level gross margins
- **Competitive Position:** deep strategic multiyear relationships with customers; dismisses Asian competitor claims on design wins as "trash talking"

### FQ1 2024 Software/VMware Metrics
- **Software Bookings:** grew from <$600M (pre-VMware) to $1.8B in Q1; expected to grow to >$3B in Q2
- **VMware Revenue Trajectory:** will grow double-digit % sequentially QoQ through rest of FY2024
- **VMware Strategy:** upselling vSphere compute virtualization customers to VMware Cloud Foundation (VCF) — complete stack (compute, storage, networking, orchestration)
- **Strategic Customers:** focus on ~2,000 largest customers for VCF deployment
- **Commercial Customers:** long tail of 300,000 VMware customers; continue selling improved vSphere (not full VCF); adds OpEx but operating leverage through revenue growth
- **EUC Divestiture:** signed definitive agreement in February 2024; classified as discontinued operations; expected to close in 2024
- **Carbon Black:** retained and merged with Symantec → Enterprise Security Group (more shareholder value than divestiture); revenue/profitability impact not significant

## AI Market Framework
- **Segment 1 — Hyperscalers:** very few players with huge consumer subscriber base and massive data; invest heavily in custom AI accelerators (Broadcom ASICs) + networking; clear ROI from AI investment in subscriber experience and advertising
- **Segment 2 — Enterprise:** large companies running AI on-prem with merchant silicon (not Broadcom ASICs); buy Broadcom networking (switches via Arista 7800 etc., routers, and connectivity components)
- **Networking Content in AI vs Traditional:** AI data centers have very different networking-to-compute content ratio vs traditional CPU-based data centers; much higher networking content in AI

## Custom Silicon Business Model
- **Development Timeline:** takes years (one customer engaged 8 years); iterative process matching custom silicon to customer's foundation models and software stack
- **Ecosystem:** must create entire ecosystem — silicon + firmware + software that works with customer's specific LLM architecture; not just silicon quality
- **Gross Margin:** lower than traditional silicon because HBM memory (big cost component) purchased from suppliers; logic portion commands corporate-level margins
- **Competitive Moat:** deep domain expertise + years of engagement; philosophy of "keep doubling down on what you're best at"

## VMware Integration Progress
- **3 Months In:** progressing very well; strong VCF upselling results; Private AI Foundation with NVIDIA driving enterprise demand
- **Go-to-Market:** spending more on go-to-market and support (unlike CA/Symantec); but operating leverage through revenue growth over 3 years
- **Revenue Recognition:** subscription model → ratable recognition → creates accelerating revenue growth trajectory

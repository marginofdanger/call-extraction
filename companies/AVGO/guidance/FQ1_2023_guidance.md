### FQ1 2023 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown
- **Networking:** $2.3B, +20% YoY, 32% of semiconductor revenue — Tomahawk 4 deployment at hyperscale; growing share in AI networks

- **Industrial:** Resales $229M, -4% YoY — China softness, offset by renewable energy and medical strength

## Generative AI and Networking
- Generative AI driving exponential demand for lossless, low-latency, scalable Ethernet networks
- Large language models require thousands of AI engines working in parallel with massive parametric exchange
- Network becomes critical constraint — must be lossless, low latency, and scale to thousands of engines
- Broadcom investing in new generation Ethernet switching designed specifically for AI workloads (different from traditional data center workloads)
- Customer pull-forward of future products (Tomahawk 5, Jericho3, next-gen compute offload ASICs) observed
- Still early innings for generative AI but urgency from hyperscalers unprecedented

## Tomahawk 5 and Silicon Photonics (Bailly)
- **Tomahawk 5:** 51.2 Tbps Ethernet switching; conventional version launch early 2024
- **Bailly:** fully integrated silicon photonics co-packaged optics + Tomahawk 5; launch shortly after conventional TH5
- Performance: 2x Tomahawk 4, at same or lower power
- Industry-first integrated silicon photonics networking solution announced this week

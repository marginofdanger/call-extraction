# Broadcom Inc. FQ4_2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** December 12, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Fiscal Q4 2024 (Aug - Oct '24) & Full Year FY2024

---

## FQ4 2024 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $14.1B (+51% YoY; ex-VMware +11% YoY)
- **Gross Margin:** 76.9% of revenue (+260 bps YoY)
- **Operating Expenses:** $2.0B; R&D $1.4B (up YoY due to VMware)
- **Operating Income:** $8.8B (+53% YoY), 63% of revenue
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $9.1B (65% of revenue; excludes $156M depreciation)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $5.5B (39% of revenue); ex-restructuring/integration $506M → $6.0B (+22% YoY), 43% of revenue
- **CapEx:** $122M

### FQ4 2024 Segment Revenue
- **Semiconductor Solutions:** $8.2B (+12% YoY, +13% QoQ), 59% of total revenue
  - Semiconductor GM: ~67% (-220 bps YoY, higher mix of AI XPUs)
  - Semiconductor OpEx: $914M (+11% YoY, increased R&D)
  - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 56%
- **Infrastructure Software:** $5.8B (+196% YoY, flat QoQ), 41% of total revenue (multiple deals slipped into Q1)
  - Software GM: 91%
  - Software OpEx: $1.1B
  - Software Operating Margin: 72% (73% ex-transition costs)

### FQ4 2024 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown (New AI/Non-AI Segmentation)
- **AI Revenue:** $3.7B (+150% YoY), part of networking segment
  - AI XPU shipments: doubled in Q4
  - AI connectivity revenue: grew 4x (Tomahawk, Jericho3-AI shipments globally)
  - AI networking revenue: 76% of total networking revenue
  - Mix: 2/3 XPUs, 1/3 networking in Q3; similar in Q4; expect networking proportion to increase in 1H FY2025 before 3nm XPUs ramp in 2H FY2025
- **Non-AI Semiconductor Revenue:** $4.5B (-23% YoY, but +10% recovery from bottom 6 months ago)
- **Networking:** $4.5B, +45% YoY — AI networking 76% of networking
- **Wireless:** $2.2B, +7% YoY, +30% QoQ (seasonal launch); engagement with North American customer across RF, WiFi, Bluetooth, sensing, touch
- **Server Storage Connectivity:** $992M, recovered ~20% from bottom 6 months ago
- **Broadband:** $465M, -51% YoY — reached bottom; significant orders from multiple service providers in Q4; recovery beginning in Q1 FY2025
- **Industrial:** $173M, -27% YoY, ~1% of total revenue — recovery expected 2H FY2025

### FQ4 2024 VMware Integration Metrics
- **VCF Customers Signed:** over 4,500 of largest 10,000 customers (up from 3,000 at Q2)
- **VMware ABV:** $2.7B in Q4 (up from $2.5B in Q3); VCF ~70% of total VMware bookings
- **CPU Cores Booked:** 21M in Q4 (vs 19M prior quarter, vs 15M in Q3)
- **VMware Spending:** $1.2B in Q4 (down from $1.3B Q3; was $2.4B pre-acquisition; operating margin <30% pre-acquisition)
- **VMware Operating Margin:** reached 70% exiting FY2024 (vs <30% pre-acquisition)
- **VMware EBITDA Target:** well on path to significantly exceed original $8.5B target; expect to achieve much earlier than original 3-year timeline
- **Integration Status:** largely complete; starting FQ1 FY2025, will no longer break out VMware revenue/costs separately; reported within Infrastructure Software segment
- **Software Segment Composition (going forward):** Brocade Fibre Channel SAN, CA mainframe, Enterprise Security (Symantec + Carbon Black), and VMware

- **Debt Reduction:** replaced $5B floating rate with fixed rate notes; used cash to pay due notes + additional floating rate, reducing debt by $2.5B total; expect to repay ~$495M fixed rate notes due in Q1
- **DSO:** 29 days (vs 31 year-ago)
- **Inventory:** $1.8B (-7% QoQ)
- **Cash Dividends Paid (Q4):** split-adjusted $0.53/share
- **Tax on Vesting:** $1.4B (8.4M shares eliminated, per Q3 data)
- **Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count:** ~4.91B (split-adjusted); Q1 FY2025 expected ~4.9B
- **Dividend Increase (Q1 FY2025):** $0.59/share (+11% QoQ); implies FY2025 annual dividend of $2.36/share (+12% YoY); 14th consecutive increase since FY2011
- **Capital Allocation Priority FY2025:** debt paydown (focus on reducing interest expense; paying down term loans); no near-term M&A plans but always open

## AI Revenue 3-Year Outlook (FY2027 SAM)
- **FY2027 AI SAM:** $60B-$90B for XPUs and AI networking from 3 existing hyperscale customers alone
- **SAM Definition:** serviceable addressable market (not TAM); bottoms-up from specific customer plans, not top-down
- **FY2024 Baseline SAM:** $15B-$20B (these 3 customers); implies 3-4x growth to $60B-$90B by FY2027
- **FY2027 Deployment Plan:** each of 3 customers plans to deploy 1 million XPU clusters on single fabric by 2027
- **AI Networking as % of AI Content:** currently 5-10% of total AI silicon content; expected to reach 15-20% by 500K-1M XPU cluster scale (exponential increase due to scale-out requirements)
- **Non-Linear Ramp:** expect quarterly variability; only 3 customers deploying in large chunks
- **2 Additional Hyperscalers:** selected Broadcom; in advanced development for next-gen AI XPUs; have taped out chips; expect to become revenue-generating customers before FY2027; could expand SAM significantly (at least one has similar $20B-$30B potential)
- **Next-Gen 3nm XPUs:** first-of-kind in 3nm process node; on track for volume shipments in 2H FY2025
- **Architecture:** scale-up within racks + scale-out between racks; customers have road maps from 100K → 1M XPU clusters on similar architecture basis over next 3-4 years

## Gross Margin Dynamics
- **Semiconductor GM Dilution:** AI XPU growth will continue to dilute semiconductor gross margin (HBM memory content)
- **Operating Margin Improvement:** revenue leverage will more than offset gross margin dilution; operating margin expected to improve from current levels
- **Software GM:** ~91%; will remain around 90%+ level
- **Consolidated GM Trajectory:** impacted by revenue mix between software and semiconductors + product mix within semiconductors

## Non-AI Recovery Outlook
- **Non-AI Semiconductor:** FY2024 was cyclical bottom at $17.8B; expect recovery at mid-single-digit historical growth rate
- **AI-Driven Refresh:** AI workloads permeating enterprises will drive need to upgrade servers, storage, and networking across entire ecosystem
- **Broadband:** reached bottom in Q4 FY2024; significant new orders; recovery starting Q1 FY2025
- **Server Storage:** recovered ~20% from bottom; continuing growth
- **Enterprise IT:** past the bottom; bookings up 20% QoQ in Q3

## Sovereign AI / Enterprise AI
- Sovereign data centers and enterprises will use merchant silicon (GPUs); do not have capability to create custom silicon or software stack
- VMware Cloud Foundation (VCF) is the play for Broadcom in sovereign/enterprise AI — enables private cloud with GPU virtualization on-prem

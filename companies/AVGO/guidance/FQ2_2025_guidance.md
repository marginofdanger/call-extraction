# Broadcom Inc. FQ2_2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction

## FQ2 2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $15.0B (+20% YoY, all organic) — record
- **Gross Margin:** 79.4% of revenue (better than guided on product mix)
- **Operating Expenses:** $2.1B; R&D $1.5B
- **Operating Income:** $9.8B (+37% YoY), 65% of revenue
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $10.0B (67% of revenue; above 66% guidance) — +35% YoY; excludes $142M depreciation
- **Free Cash Flow:** $6.4B (43% of revenue); impacted by interest expense from VMware acquisition debt and cash taxes
- **CapEx:** $144M

### FQ2 2025 Segment Revenue
- **Semiconductor Solutions:** $8.4B (+17% YoY, accelerating from 11% in Q1), 56% of total revenue
  - Semiconductor GM: ~69% (+140 bps YoY, product mix)
  - Semiconductor OpEx: $971M (+12% YoY, increased R&D for leading-edge AI)
  - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 57% (+200 bps YoY)
- **Infrastructure Software:** $6.6B (+25% YoY, above $6.5B outlook), 44% of total revenue

  - Software OpEx: $1.1B

### FQ2 2025 AI Revenue
- **AI Revenue:** >$4.4B (+46% YoY) — 9th consecutive quarter of growth
- **AI Revenue Mix:** 60% XPUs, 40% networking (same as Q1; Hock expected networking to drop but it hasn't)
- **Custom AI Accelerators:** grew double digits YoY; on trajectory with no lumpiness or softening
- **AI Networking:** grew >170% YoY; driven by Ethernet scale-out AND scale-up
- **Scale-Up Networking Density:** 5-10x more consumption/density than scale-out (pleasantly surprised Broadcom)
- **3 Hyperscale Customers:** unwavering in investment plans despite economic environment; each deploying towards 1M XPU clusters by 2027
- **4 Engagement Partners (Prospects):** no comment on FY2026 contribution; only discuss customers, not prospects
- **Inference Acceleration:** hyperscalers doubling down on inference to monetize platforms and justify training investment; may see acceleration of XPU demand into back half of 2026 for inference on top of training
- **FY2025 AI Growth Rate (~60% YoY):** expected to sustain into FY2026 based on improved visibility and inference demand layering on top of training
- **FY2027 SAM:** maintained $60B-$90B from 3 existing customers; no update to SAM ("Stop talking about SAM now")

## FY2026 AI Revenue Outlook
- **Growth Trajectory:** FY2025 AI growth rate (~60% YoY) expected to sustain into FY2026
- **Inference Catalyst:** inference demand accelerating on top of training demand; hyperscalers monetizing platforms
- **Potential Acceleration:** may see acceleration of XPU demand into back half of 2026 for inference
- **Implied Math:** ~$19-20B FY2025 AI revenue at 60% growth → $30B+ FY2026 AI revenue (Stacy Rasgon math; Hock confirmed trajectory without giving specific numbers)
- **Basis:** improved visibility from lead times and hyperscaler deployment plans; not including contribution from 4 engagement partners

## Networking Technology Update
- **Tomahawk 6:** 102.4 Tbps switch capacity; enables 100K+ accelerator clusters in 2 tiers vs 3; lower latency, higher bandwidth, lower power; 512 connections/radix; tremendous demand; currently at proof-of-concept stage with customers
- **Scale-Up Transition:** rapidly converting to Ethernet; Broadcom's hyperscale customers using Ethernet for scale-up
- **Copper-to-Optical Transition:** currently scale-up on copper interconnects (~72-128 GPU/XPU interconnects); when clusters exceed this, optical will replace copper (1-2 year timeline)
- **Co-Packaged Optics:** in development; not necessarily the first step; pluggable optics may come first; either way Broadcom at the forefront
- **Ethernet vs Proprietary Protocols:** Hock strongly advocates open-standard Ethernet for scale-up; dismisses need for new proprietary protocols (UALink, NVLink open); Broadcom does not participate in NVLink
- **XPU Value Proposition:** not about cost alone; end-to-end hardware-software optimization creates learning curve that enables higher performance than merchant silicon; takes multiple cycles to master

## Capital Allocation Strategy
- **Priority Order:** dividends (50% of prior year FCF) → debt reduction → opportunistic buybacks
- **Debt Target:** ≤2x debt-to-EBITDA ratio
- **Share Buybacks:** opportunistic; $4.2B in Q2 was combination of RSU tax vesting and market-timing purchases
- **M&A:** would be significant/substantial; reducing debt preserves borrowing capacity; no specific plans but always open

## FY2027 Outlook
- **AI Revenue (chips only):** line of sight to "significantly in excess of $100 billion" in FY2027
  - This is silicon content only: XPUs, switch chips, DSPs — not rack-level revenue
  - ~10 gigawatts of compute capacity expected to ship in FY2027
  - Dollars per gigawatt varies by customer but broadly consistent with analyst estimates
- **Supply Chain Secured:** capacity for leading-edge wafers, HBM, substrates, T-glass secured through FY2028
- **FY2028 Growth:** confirmed ability to grow in FY2028 based on secured supply; "yes" to direct question

## AI Customer Pipeline
- **6 XPU Customers (expanded from 5):**
  1. **Google:** continued growth trajectory in FY2026 with strong demand for 7th-generation Ironwood TPU; even stronger demand expected from next-gen TPUs in FY2027+
  2. **Anthropic:** "very good start in 2026 for 1 gigawatt of TPU compute"; FY2027 demand expected to surge to "in excess of 3 gigawatts"
  3. **Meta:** MTIA custom accelerator roadmap "alive and well"; shipping now; next-gen XPUs will scale to "multiple gigawatts" in FY2027+; Hock: "Contrary to recent analyst reports"
  4. **Customer 4 (unnamed):** strong shipments this year; expected to more than double in FY2027
  5. **Customer 5 (unnamed):** strong shipments this year; expected to more than double in FY2027
  6. **OpenAI (NEW — 6th customer):** deploying first-generation XPU in volume in FY2027; over 1 gigawatt of compute capacity; 10 gigawatt multiyear agreement (FY2027-2029)
- **XPU Workload Specialization:** customers increasingly designing 2 chips per year — one for training, one for inference; architectures diverging from general-purpose GPU design (sparse core data routers vs dense matrix multipliers)
- **Inference Driving Demand:** "very, very interesting and surprising" — inference for LLM productization/monetization driving "substantial amount of compute capacity"
- **Strategic Nature:** all 6 customers view XPU partnership as "strategic, extremely strategic" — not optionality; multiyear, multi-generation commitments; customers share 2-4 year roadmaps

## AI Networking
- **AI Networking Revenue Mix:** 1/3 of AI revenue in Q1; projected to grow to 40% of AI revenue in Q2
- **Tomahawk 6:** 100 Tbps — "only one out there"; capturing demand from hyperscalers regardless of GPU vs XPU; record demand
- **Tomahawk 7 (FY2027):** next-gen, 2x performance; expected to be "by far, the first out there"
- **1.6T DSPs:** "we are again the only player out there doing DSP at 1.6 terabits"
- **200G SERDES:** first to market; capturing scale-out demand; 400G SERDES running in lab, expected in FY2028
- **AI Networking as % of AI Revenue:** expected to range 33-40% going forward
- **Ethernet Scale-Up:** industry converging on Ethernet for both scale-out and scale-up; multiple hyperscalers and semiconductor peers have announced Ethernet scale-up; XPU designs increasingly use Ethernet for scale-up

## Technology & Connectivity
- **Direct Attach Copper:** XPU customers "will likely continue to stay on direct attach copper" for scale-up through 400G SERDES in FY2028; copper is lowest latency, lowest power, lowest cost
- **CPO (Co-Packaged Optics):** "will come in its time — not this year, maybe not next year"; Broadcom leads in CPO but copper preferred for scale-up today
- **Silicon Photonics:** developing for 400G → 800G → 1.6T; technology exists but not deploying soon

## Gross Margin Trajectory
- **Rack-Level Sales Impact:** CEO Hock Tan dismissed concerns about rack-level sales diluting margins: "Our gross margin is solidly at the number Kirsten reports. We will not be affected."
- **CFO Kirsten Spears:** "On further study, the impact relative to our overall mix is actually not going to be substantial at all" — walked back prior quarter's commentary about potential margin dilution from rack sales

## Competitive Dynamics (COT — Customer-Owned Tooling)
- **COT Risk Assessment:** Hock: "We will not see competition in COT for many years to come. It will come eventually, but we're still a long way off."
- **COT Performance Gap:** analyst noted COT initiatives are "2x less performance" and "2x less complex" than Broadcom's current-generation solutions; 12-18 months behind on technology
- **Key Barriers:** best-in-class silicon design, cutting-edge SERDES, advanced packaging, high-volume production yields, time to market; "Anybody can design a chip in a lab. Can you produce 100,000 of those chips quickly at yields you can afford?"
- **Supply Chain Advantage:** secured supply of T-glass, substrates, leading-edge wafers, HBM through FY2028 — "probably the first to secure that up to '28 or beyond"

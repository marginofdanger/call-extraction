## R&D and Technology Investments
- **2nm AI XPU:** taping out industry's first 2-nanometer AI XPU with 3.5D packaging; targeting 10,000 teraflops XPU
- **Tomahawk 5 Enhanced:** doubled radix capacity of existing TH5 for hyperscaler scaling to 500K XPU clusters on Ethernet
- **Tomahawk 6:** taped out next-gen 100-terabit switch; 200G SerDes, 1.6T bandwidth; samples to customers within months
- **Tomahawk 7 & 8:** already planning next two generations; speeding up development cadence
- **3nm XPUs:** on track for volume shipments in 2H FY2025 (3 existing customers)
- **New Partner XPU Tape-outs:** 4 engagement partners on track to tape out their XPUs this year

## AI Architecture and Optimization
- **5 Degrees of Freedom in XPU Design:** compute capacity (teraflops), network bandwidth (scale-up/scale-out), memory capacity (HBM), memory bandwidth (latency), and power optimization
- **Workload-Specific Optimization:** training, pre-training, post-training/fine-tuning, inference, test-time scaling — each has unique chip architecture requirements
- **Why Custom Silicon Wins:** general purpose accelerator cannot be optimized across multiple frontier models; each hyperscaler's frontier model has unique requirements
- **Broadcom's Role:** provides fundamental semiconductor hardware technology; hyperscalers provide software/models/algorithms; close collaboration to optimize
- **Design Win Philosophy:** only count production-volume deployments (not proof of concept); only select customers with sustainable large-scale needs; multiyear road maps; 15+ years of ASIC discipline

## Connectivity Portfolio for AI
- **Optical:** multimode lasers (VCSELs) and edge-emitting lasers (single mode); significant opportunity in greenfield scale-up
- **Protocols:** Ethernet switching (Tomahawk, Jericho) + PCIe Express; two switching architectures: intelligent switch with simple NIC (Jericho) vs smart NIC with dumb switch (Tomahawk)
- **Networking as % of AI Revenue:** typically ~30% (70% XPUs); Q1 was atypical at ~40%; norm is 70/30

## Enterprise AI and VMware
- **On-Prem Trend Accelerating:** data sovereignty and privacy driving enterprises to reconsider public cloud for AI workloads; pushing towards on-prem data center upgrades
- **VMware Private AI Foundation:** virtualizes both GPU and CPU servers on common VCF platform; 39 enterprise customers; open ecosystem with NVIDIA; load balancing and automation across GPU/CPU infrastructure

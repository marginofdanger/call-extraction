# Broadcom Inc. FQ1_2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** March 3, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Fiscal Q1 2022 (Nov '21 - Jan '22)

---

## FQ1 2022 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $7.7B (+16% YoY)
- **Gross Margin:** 76% of revenue (+227 bps YoY)
- **Operating Expenses:** $1.2B (+6% YoY), driven by R&D investment
- **Operating Income:** $4.7B (+23% YoY)
- **Operating Margin:** 60% of revenue (+362 bps YoY)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $4.8B (62.5% of revenue; excludes $136M depreciation)
- **EPS (Non-GAAP):** $8.39 (consensus $8.13, beat by 3.2%)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $3.4B (44% of revenue)
- **CapEx:** $101M

### FQ1 2022 Segment Revenue
- **Semiconductor Solutions:** $5.9B (+20% YoY), 76% of total revenue
  - Semiconductor GM: ~71% (+347 bps YoY)
  - Semiconductor OpEx: $817M (+9% YoY); R&D $725M (+10% YoY)
  - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 57%; operating profit grew 31% YoY
- **Infrastructure Software:** $1.8B (+5% YoY), 24% of total revenue
  - Software GM: 90% (+71 bps YoY)
  - Software OpEx: $348M (+1% YoY)
  - Software Operating Margin: 71%; operating profit grew 7% YoY

### FQ1 2022 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown
- **Networking:** $1.9B (+33% YoY), 32% of semiconductor revenue — driven by Tomahawk 4 deployment and compute offload at hyperscale
- **Wireless:** $2.0B (+4% YoY, +10% QoQ), 34% of semiconductor revenue — strong demand from North American OEM
- **Broadband:** $911M (+23% YoY), 16% of semiconductor revenue — next-gen PON, DOCSIS, WiFi 6/6E deployment
- **Server Storage Connectivity:** $801M (+32% YoY), 14% of semiconductor revenue — enterprise IT recovery and content growth
- **Industrial:** $243M, ~4% of semiconductor revenue; resales $239M (+37% YoY) — EVs, renewable energy, factory automation, health care; channel inventory ~1 month
- **Custom Silicon/ASIC (Offload Computing):** Revenue grew >50% YoY — hyperscaler custom AI accelerators

### FQ1 2022 Software Metrics
- **Core Software Revenue:** +6% YoY
- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue):** $5.3B (+5% YoY)
- **Consolidated Renewal Rates:** 121% over expiring contracts
- **Strategic Account Renewal Rates:** 136%; $656M renewals on expiring, $164M cross-selling/PLAs
- **Recurring Mix:** >90% of renewal value is recurring subscription and maintenance

## Long-Term Targets
- **Semiconductor Industry CAGR:** ~5% annually over next 10 years (management view — no evidence for higher structural growth)
- **Semiconductor Gross Margin Expansion:** ~100 bps per year on average (long-term trend)
- **Networking Long-Term Growth:** mid- to high-single digits annually
- **Dividend Policy:** maintained per long-term outlined plan
- **M&A Strategy:** actively looking for acquisitions; capacity to handle good-sized deal

---

## AI/ML Networking Opportunity
- Jericho router deployed in back-end networks of large-scale AI/ML clusters at hyperscale
- Arista 7800 AI platform (built on Broadcom Jericho) scales Ethernet to connect tens of thousands of CPUs/GPUs
- Broadcom positions low-latency Ethernet as superior to proprietary InfiniBand for large-scale AI/ML networks — open architecture, congestion-managed, lossless, highest efficiency load balancing
- Custom training and inference SoC footprint expanding at several cloud customers

## Enterprise IT Demand
- Enterprise IT spending described as "on fire" — strongest demand driver
- Pent-up spending driving upgrades across campus switching, server compute, storage connectivity
- WiFi 5 to WiFi 6/6E upgrade cycle underway across enterprises as offices reopen

## Pricing & Supply Environment
- Lead times remain extended at 50 weeks, unchanged
- Passing on material cost increases (wafer, substrates, assembly) to customers
- Price increases contribute to revenue growth but "much less than you probably think" — underlying demand growth ~20% YoY
- Price stickiness expected to continue at least through first part of 2023
- Channel and customer inventory remains lean; 75% of semi revenue from ~100 direct customers with good visibility
- True end demand growth estimated at ~20% YoY (beyond pricing pass-through)

## Broadband Infrastructure
- U.S. infrastructure bill: $65B over next 5 years for high-speed broadband connectivity
- Next-gen broadband seen as better alternative to 5G for home connectivity globally
- WiFi 7 investment as strategic complement to 10G PON and cable modem

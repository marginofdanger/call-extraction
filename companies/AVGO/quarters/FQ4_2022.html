Quarterly Guidance (FQ1 2023)

  • Revenue: $8.9B (+16% YoY)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: ~63% of projected revenue
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: 435M (excludes potential repurchases)
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: could be down ~100 bps from Q4 (product mix shift — less networking, more broadband/wireless)
  • R&D Spending: could be up sequentially (stepping up engineer hiring for critical projects)
  • Semiconductor Revenue: ~+20% YoY
  • Networking: ~+20% YoY
  • Server Storage Connectivity: above +50% YoY
  • Broadband: ~+30% YoY
  • Wireless: sequentially flat, up low single digits YoY
  • Industrial: low single-digit % growth YoY
  • Infrastructure Software: flat YoY (core software +mid-single-digit % offset by Brocade enterprise decline)
  • Annual Outlook: fully booked for FY2023 but not providing annual guidance

Enterprise IT Spending Outlook

  • CIO surveys show enterprise IT budgets for 2023 flat to up vs 2022
  • Compelling need to modernize platforms, digitize business models
  • No clear reduction seen despite global recession concerns
  • Infrastructure spending remains strong across hyperscale, service providers, and enterprise

FQ4 2022 Segment Revenue

  • Semiconductor Solutions: $7.1B (+26% YoY), 79% of total revenue - Semiconductor GM: ~71% (+70 bps YoY) - Semiconductor OpEx: $825M (+4% YoY); R&D $731M (+4% YoY) - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 59%; operating profit grew 33% YoY
  • Infrastructure Software: $1.8B (+4% YoY), 21% of total revenue - Software GM: 91% - Software OpEx: $348M (-1% YoY) - Software Operating Margin: 72%; operating profit grew 6% YoY

FQ4 2022 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown

  • Networking: $2.5B (record), +30% YoY, 35% of semiconductor revenue — Tomahawk 4 data center switching, Jericho edge/core routing at cloud and service providers
  • Wireless: $2.1B, +13% YoY, 29% of semiconductor revenue — higher content from North American OEM
  • Server Storage Connectivity: $1.2B (record), +50% YoY, 17% of semiconductor revenue — next-gen MegaRAID and storage adapters; content increase from subsystem/board sales
  • Broadband: $1.0B, +20% YoY, 15% of semiconductor revenue — multiyear 10G PON and DOCSIS 3.1 deployments with WiFi 6/6E
  • Industrial: Resales $234M, +1% YoY — China weakness, North America/Europe automotive strength

FQ4 2022 Custom Silicon/ASIC

  • Compute Offload ASIC Revenue: surpassed $2B in FY2022 (~33% increase from prior year)
  • Multiple hyperscaler programs for custom/semi-custom accelerators
  • Programs are lumpy and hard to forecast near-term

FQ4 2022 Software Metrics

  • Core Software Revenue: +5% YoY
  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): $5.4B (+4% YoY)
  • Consolidated Renewal Rates: 117% (trailing 12-month: 120%)
  • Strategic Account Renewal Rates: 128% (trailing 12-month: 135%)
  • Strategic Account Bookings: $357M, of which $101M cross-selling
  • Recurring Mix: >90% recurring subscription and maintenance

FQ4 2022 Balance Sheet & Capital Allocation

  • Cash & Equivalents: $12.4B
  • Gross Debt: $39.5B ($440M short-term)
  • DSO: 30 days
  • Inventory: $1.9B (+5% QoQ, positioned for Q1 mix with higher cost materials)
  • Fully Booked: for entire fiscal year 2023 (noncancelable orders)
  • Customer Request Dates (CRDs): significantly higher than actual shipments
  • Authorized Repurchase Program Remaining: $13B
  • Share Repurchases: paused since VMware announcement (SEC rules); will resume when allowed
  • Dividend Increase: Q1 FY2023 quarterly dividend of $4.60/share (+12%); implies FY2023 annual dividend of $18.40/share (record; 12th consecutive increase since FY2011)
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: 435M

VMware Acquisition Update

  • Merger clearance received in Brazil, Canada, and South Africa
  • Timeline more extended in other key regions given transaction size
  • Still confident transaction will close in Broadcom's fiscal year 2023
  • Combination about enabling enterprises to accelerate innovation in multi-cloud era

Gross Margin Dynamics

  • Networking has highest margins across semiconductor portfolio
  • Broadband margins lower than networking
  • Wireless has lowest margins
  • Q4→Q1 mix shift: less networking, more broadband; wireless remains large proportion → GM down ~100 bps
  • Long-term: new-generation product adoption drives 50–100 bps annual GM expansion
  • Price increases only pass through material cost increases — neutral to percentage margins

China Exposure

  • China represents <10% of total revenues
  • Slowdown in consumption across industrial and IT infrastructure
  • Too early to call improvement
  • COVID logistics impacting wireless customer shipment timing

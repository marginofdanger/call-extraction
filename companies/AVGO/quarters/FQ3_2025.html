FY2026 AI Revenue Outlook (Updated)

  • Outlook: significantly improved from prior quarter's indication (~60% growth); growth rate expected to ACCELERATE above FY2025's 50-60% rate
  • Hock's Language: "fairly material improvement" in FY2026 outlook; "we are seeing growth rate accelerate as opposed to just remain steady"
  • Key Drivers: - 4th customer with $10B+ orders (delivery Q3 FY2026) - Continued progressive share gains at 3 original customers (multigenerational, each customer increasing XPU consumption) - Inference demand layering on top of training
  • Networking Mix FY2026: expected to decline as percentage of AI revenue (XPU share growing; networking from non-XPU customers becomes relatively smaller)
  • Prospects: not included in FY2026 forecast; no comment on their contribution

FQ3 2025 Segment Revenue

  • Semiconductor Solutions: $9.2B (+26% YoY, accelerating from 17% in Q2), 57% of total revenue - Semiconductor GM: ~67% (-30 bps YoY, product mix) - Semiconductor OpEx: $951M (+9% YoY, increased R&D for leading-edge AI) - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 57% (+130 bps YoY, flat QoQ)
  • Infrastructure Software: $6.8B (+17% YoY, above $6.7B outlook), 43% of total revenue - Software GM: 93% (vs 90% year-ago) - Software OpEx: $1.1B - Software Operating Margin: ~77% (vs 67% year-ago; reflects completion of VMware integration)

FQ3 2025 AI Revenue

  • AI Revenue: $5.2B (+63% YoY) — 10th consecutive quarter of robust growth; better than expected
  • AI Revenue Mix: 65% XPUs, 35% networking (XPU share increased from 60% in Q2)
  • Custom AI Accelerators: demand continuing to grow; progressively gaining share at existing customers
  • 4th Customer (NEW): one of the 4 prospects released production orders; now qualified as customer; secured >$10B of orders for AI racks based on XPUs - Delivery: starts and ends in Q3 FY2026 - Very significant customer; material impact on FY2026 outlook
  • XPU Customers: now 4 customers + 3 remaining prospects (7 total; may see 1 more prospect)
  • 1M XPU Clusters: target maintained for 4 customers (previously 3); no comment on prospects
  • XPU Share Trajectory: expected to grow over time at each customer; multigenerational journey; at least 2 generation versions per customer; gaining share progressively as software stacks stabilize and prove out

FQ3 2025 Non-AI Semiconductor Revenue

  • Non-AI Revenue: $4.0B (flat QoQ)
  • Recovery Trend: slow; U-shaped recovery; only broadband showing consistent uptrend over last 3 quarters
  • Broadband: strong sequential growth in Q3
  • Enterprise Networking: down sequentially in Q3
  • Server Storage: down sequentially in Q3
  • Wireless: flat QoQ in Q3
  • Industrial: flat QoQ in Q3
  • Non-AI Bookings: up >20% YoY (~23%); but historically been "tricked before" on booking signals
  • Meaningful Recovery Timeline: potentially mid-to-late FY2026

FQ3 2025 Software/VMware Metrics

  • VCF Adoption: virtually >90% of top 10,000 customers have bought VCF (up from >87% at Q2) - Note: bought licenses does not mean fully deployed; deployment and operation is ongoing Phase 2 work
  • Total Contract Value Booked: >$8.4B in Q3
  • VCF 9.0 Released: fully integrated cloud platform; deploys on-prem or cloud; supports VMs and modern containers including AI workloads; "real alternative to public cloud" - 2 years of engineering development by 5,000+ developers
  • VMware Phase 2: focused on enabling customers to successfully deploy and operate VCF; expansion across IT footprint; will sell advanced services (security, disaster recovery, AI workloads on-prem)
  • Mid-Tier Customers: exploring value proposition for next 20,000-30,000 midsized companies beyond top 10,000; still learning
  • Hardware Commoditization: VCF consciously commoditizes underlying servers, storage, networking hardware to reduce enterprise data center costs

Networking Technology Update

  • Tomahawk 5: scale-up Ethernet, 512 compute nodes; already deployed with customers
  • Tomahawk 6: 102.4 Tbps; launched June 2025; 2-tier scale-out vs 3-tier; lower latency, lower power
  • Jericho4 (NEW): 51.2 Tbps Ethernet fabric router; deep buffering; intelligent congestion control; handles clusters >200,000 compute nodes across multiple data centers (~100km range) - Scale Across: new architecture for clusters spanning multiple data center sites; needed due to power/land limitations at single sites - Builds on 2 years of Jericho3 deployment with hyperscalers (256 connections) - Technology proven for 10-20 years in telco network routing
  • SUE (Scale-Up Ethernet): Hock's term for scale-up Ethernet; latency <250 nanoseconds (better than NVLink/InfiniBand); open source; XPUs designed with Ethernet-compatible interfaces
  • Ethernet vs Proprietary: strongly advocating open-standard Ethernet for all three networking tiers (scale-up, scale-out, scale-across); no need for new protocols; hyperscalers prefer Ethernet because of competition/choice

Custom Silicon Competition

  • ASIC Competition: acknowledged but countering through out-investment and out-innovation; first-mover advantage in XPU model; largest IP developer (SerDes, packaging, low-power design)
  • XPU Disaggregation: Ethernet networking kept separate from XPUs (open source principle); but optimized hand-in-hand for customers; XPU interfaces designed for Ethernet compatibility
  • GPU Clusters: less penetration in closed GPU systems (NVLink scale-up); but hyperscalers architecting GPU clusters with separate networking (scale-out, scale-across) where Broadcom Ethernet switches are used

CEO Succession

  • Hock Tan Extension: Board and CEO agreed he will continue as CEO of Broadcom through at least 2030

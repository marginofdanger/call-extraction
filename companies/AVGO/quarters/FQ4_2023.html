Full Year FY2024 Guidance (Annual Format — VMware Integration Year)

  • Revenue: $50B (+40% YoY)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: ~60% of revenue (full year); exit rate approaching 65% as VMware integration completes
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: ~494M (includes VMware shares, excludes potential repurchases)
  • No quarterly guidance — annual guidance only during VMware integration year; will report quarterly actuals and update annual outlook each quarter
  • Guidance Format Change: annual instead of quarterly due to VMware integration variability (transition spending, subscription conversion timing); expect to provide full year with quarterly updates

FY2024 Semiconductor Guidance

  • Semiconductor Revenue: mid-to-high single-digit % growth YoY
  • AI Revenue: >25% of semiconductor revenue (more than offsets flat non-AI semi)
  • Non-AI Semiconductor Revenue: flat — no growth expected
  • Networking: +30% YoY (accelerating deployment of AI networking connectivity + AI accelerator expansion)
  • Wireless: stable YoY (engagement deep, strategic, multiyear)
  • Server Storage Connectivity: down mid-to-high teens % YoY (cyclical weakness from late FY2023)
  • Broadband: down low-to-mid teens % YoY (service provider cyclical weakness continuing)
  • Industrial: down low single digits % YoY
  • Non-AI Recovery Timing: speculation that second half FY2024 starts looking better (not committed)

FY2024 Infrastructure Software Guidance

  • Total Software Revenue: $20B ($8B legacy + $12B VMware)
  • Legacy Software (CA/Symantec/Brocade): $8B (+4% YoY)
  • VMware Revenue: $12B (11 months contribution; excludes EUC/Carbon Black ~$2B to be divested)
  • VMware Subscription Conversion: converting >60% perpetual license base to mostly subscription by end FY2024
  • VMware Revenue Trajectory: accelerating through FY2024 (subscription ratable recognition creates growth curve); double-digit growth expected for next 3 years
  • VMware Transition Spending: ~$1B during FY2024 (largely completed by year end)
  • VMware Exit Rate Spending: ~$1.4B/quarter (down 40% from year ago)
  • VMware EBITDA Target: exit rate approaching ~$8.5B run rate as exiting FY2024; steady state 65% EBITDA margin

FQ4 2023 Segment Revenue

  • Semiconductor Solutions: $7.3B (+3% YoY), 79% of total revenue - Semiconductor GM: ~70% (-110 bps YoY, product mix) - Semiconductor OpEx: $822M (stable YoY) - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 58%; operating profit +2% YoY
  • Infrastructure Software: $2.0B (+7% YoY), 21% of total revenue (excludes VMware — closed Nov 22) - Software GM: 92% - Software OpEx: $339M - Software Operating Margin: 75%; operating profit +12% YoY

FQ4 2023 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown

  • Networking: $3.1B, +23% YoY, 42% of semiconductor revenue — AI custom accelerators + networking switches/routers/NICs for AI data centers; Ethernet deployed predominantly in hyperscaler AI networks
  • Wireless: $2.0B, +23% QoQ (seasonal launch), -3% YoY, 27% of semiconductor revenue — engagement with North American customer deep, strategic, multiyear
  • Server Storage Connectivity: $1.0B, -17% YoY, 14% of semiconductor revenue — cyclical weakness began late FY2023
  • Broadband: $950M, -9% YoY, 13% of semiconductor revenue — service provider cyclical weakness beginning
  • Industrial: $236M, stable YoY

FQ4 2023 AI Revenue

  • AI Revenue: ~$1.5B in Q4 (20% of semiconductor revenue) — custom AI accelerators + AI networking
  • Non-AI Semiconductor Revenue: ~$6B/quarter (stable, continued soft landing)
  • FY2023 AI Revenue: 15% of semiconductor revenue (in line with expectations)
  • FY2024 AI Revenue Outlook: >25% of semiconductor revenue (consistent with prior guidance)
  • Custom AI Accelerator Customers: has broadened beyond one hyperscaler — multiple customers now under NDA; driven by rapid evolution of LLMs where one-size-fits-all hardware no longer optimal
  • AI Demand Trend: accelerating rather than stable or decelerating (concurs with AMD's view)

VMware Acquisition & Integration

  • Closed: November 22, 2023 (~4 weeks into Broadcom's FQ1 FY2024)
  • Strategy: Refocus VMware on core business — VMware Cloud Foundation (VCF) for private/hybrid cloud
  • VCF Value Proposition: full software stack virtualizing compute, storage, networking + orchestration/management layer; creates hyperscaler-like environment on-prem
  • Divestiture: End-User Computing (EUC) and Carbon Black (~$2B combined revenue); noncore to data center focus; will find "good homes" — many interested parties
  • Integration Timeline: expected ~1 year
  • Difference from CA/Symantec: VMware is a "product of the present and the future" vs CA mainframe legacy; growth product creating virtualized cloud environments
  • VMware Private AI Cloud Foundation: partnership with NVIDIA announced at VM Explore Las Vegas; VCF stack runs NVIDIA GPU, virtualizes GPU for enterprise AI workloads
  • CIO Engagement: Hock Tan spoke with 150+ CIOs globally over 12-16 months pre-close; strong demand for full VCF stack
  • China Regulatory Conditions: conditions published on relevant authority website; do not affect synergy plans

AI Networking Technology

  • Ethernet vs InfiniBand: hyperscalers deploying Ethernet predominantly in AI networks; Ethernet is standard in front-end networks
  • AI Network Scaling: networks doubling in size across the board; no slowdown in AI network buildout
  • Networking Revenue FY2023: $10.8B; ex-AI accelerators networking connectivity ~$8B (pure silicon — not systems, cables, or subsystems)
  • Custom AI Accelerator Opportunity: rapid LLM evolution means one-size-fits-all hardware suboptimal; hyperscalers with scale/resources benefit from customized hardware matched to their foundation models
  • AI Demand Outlook: accelerating, not decelerating — Broadcom concurs with AMD's characterization

Customer Inventory Dynamics

  • Broadcom Inventory: 76 days on hand, well-managed vs peers
  • Customer Inventory (Broadcom products): mostly in good shape; sell direct (not through channels)
  • Server Storage: "could be a little excessive" at customers
  • Networking/Broadband: inventory not excessive
  • Customer Behavior: cautious buying broadly due to elevated inventory of other vendors' products; lead times maintained

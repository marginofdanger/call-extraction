FQ1 2025 AI Revenue

  • AI Revenue: $4.1B (+77% YoY) — beat $3.8B guidance due to stronger networking shipments to hyperscalers
  • AI Revenue Mix: 60% XPUs, 40% networking in Q1 (networking surge; norm is ~70/30 XPU/networking; Q1 had pull-ins and acceleration of networking shipments)
  • 3 Hyperscale Customers: shipping XPUs in production volume; multiyear road maps
  • 4 New Engagement Partners: (2 previously disclosed + 2 newly announced this quarter) - Selected Broadcom to develop custom AI accelerators for their frontier models - Not yet "customers" (require scale production deployment to qualify) - On track to tape out XPUs this year; first chip typically takes ~1.5 years from tape-out to production - No reason these 4 couldn't generate demand in range of current 3 customers, but later timeline - NOT included in $60B-$90B FY2027 SAM estimate
  • FY2027 SAM: reaffirmed $60B-$90B from 3 existing hyperscale customers; each racing towards 1M XPU clusters by end of 2027
  • XPU includes inference: SAM is combination of training AND inference accelerators; architecture differs between training and inference chips; larger part of SAM dollars from training
  • AI Diffusion Rules: no concerns about impact on current 3 customers
  • DeepSeek Impact: no negative impact; AI spending by hyperscalers remains aggressive; model efficiency developments are positive disruption

FQ1 2025 Non-AI Semiconductor Revenue

  • Non-AI Revenue: $4.1B (-9% QoQ, seasonal wireless decline)
  • Recovery Trend: slow but continuing; bookings growing YoY
  • Broadband: bottomed in Q4 FY2024; double-digit sequential recovery in Q1; expected up similarly in Q2 (telcos/service providers stepping up spending)
  • Server Storage: down single digits QoQ in Q1; expected up high single digits QoQ in Q2
  • Wireless: down QoQ (seasonal); flat YoY in Q1; expected flat YoY in Q2
  • Enterprise Networking: flattish in 1H FY2025; customers working through channel inventory
  • Industrial: down double digits in Q1; expected down in Q2; recovery 2H FY2025

FQ1 2025 Software/VMware Metrics

  • VCF Adoption: ~70% of largest 10,000 customers have adopted VCF (up from 45% at FQ4 2024)
  • VMware Private AI Foundation: 39 enterprise customers (collaboration with NVIDIA); virtualizes GPUs on VCF platform for on-prem AI workloads
  • Perpetual to Subscription: over 60% converted to subscription (from >60% perpetual at acquisition)
  • VCF Growth Driver: enterprises recognizing need to run AI workloads on-prem → VCF virtualizes both GPU and CPU on common platform → reduced costs
  • Q1 Software Revenue Elevated: includes deal slips from Q4; not expected to cause Q2 to be lower

R&D and Technology Investments

  • 2nm AI XPU: taping out industry's first 2-nanometer AI XPU with 3.5D packaging; targeting 10,000 teraflops XPU
  • Tomahawk 5 Enhanced: doubled radix capacity of existing TH5 for hyperscaler scaling to 500K XPU clusters on Ethernet
  • Tomahawk 6: taped out next-gen 100-terabit switch; 200G SerDes, 1.6T bandwidth; samples to customers within months
  • Tomahawk 7 & 8: already planning next two generations; speeding up development cadence
  • 3nm XPUs: on track for volume shipments in 2H FY2025 (3 existing customers)
  • New Partner XPU Tape-outs: 4 engagement partners on track to tape out their XPUs this year

AI Architecture and Optimization

  • 5 Degrees of Freedom in XPU Design: compute capacity (teraflops), network bandwidth (scale-up/scale-out), memory capacity (HBM), memory bandwidth (latency), and power optimization
  • Workload-Specific Optimization: training, pre-training, post-training/fine-tuning, inference, test-time scaling — each has unique chip architecture requirements
  • Why Custom Silicon Wins: general purpose accelerator cannot be optimized across multiple frontier models; each hyperscaler's frontier model has unique requirements
  • Broadcom's Role: provides fundamental semiconductor hardware technology; hyperscalers provide software/models/algorithms; close collaboration to optimize
  • Design Win Philosophy: only count production-volume deployments (not proof of concept); only select customers with sustainable large-scale needs; multiyear road maps; 15+ years of ASIC discipline

Connectivity Portfolio for AI

  • Optical: multimode lasers (VCSELs) and edge-emitting lasers (single mode); significant opportunity in greenfield scale-up
  • Protocols: Ethernet switching (Tomahawk, Jericho) + PCIe Express; two switching architectures: intelligent switch with simple NIC (Jericho) vs smart NIC with dumb switch (Tomahawk)
  • Networking as % of AI Revenue: typically ~30% (70% XPUs); Q1 was atypical at ~40%; norm is 70/30

Enterprise AI and VMware

  • On-Prem Trend Accelerating: data sovereignty and privacy driving enterprises to reconsider public cloud for AI workloads; pushing towards on-prem data center upgrades
  • VMware Private AI Foundation: virtualizes both GPU and CPU servers on common VCF platform; 39 enterprise customers; open ecosystem with NVIDIA; load balancing and automation across GPU/CPU infrastructure

M&A

  • Not thinking about M&A currently; too busy with AI and VMware transformation
  • Tariff/regulatory environment uncertainty — too early to assess impact (3-6 months for clarity)

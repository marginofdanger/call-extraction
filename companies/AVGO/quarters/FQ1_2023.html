Quarterly Guidance (FQ2 2023)

  • Revenue: $8.7B (+8% YoY)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: ~64.5% of projected revenue
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: 438M
  • Gross Margin: up ~150 bps sequentially (product mix improvement)
  • R&D Spending: up sequentially (continuing engineer hiring and seasonal payroll tax step-ups)
  • Semiconductor Revenue: high single-digit % growth YoY
  • Networking: ~+20% YoY (AI tailwinds)
  • Server Storage Connectivity: moderate towards +20% YoY (next-gen MegaRAID transition significantly complete)
  • Broadband: +10% YoY (sustained momentum sequentially)
  • Wireless: down sequentially, down high single-digit % YoY (seasonal)
  • Industrial: down low single-digit % YoY (continuing China softness)
  • Infrastructure Software: up low to mid-single-digit % YoY (core growth offset by Brocade weakness)
  • Annual Outlook: still fully booked for FY2023; not providing annual guidance; conceptually expect "soft landing" with continued YoY improvement in second half

Broadband Outlook

  • Multiyear deployment cycle: 10G PON by telcos, DOCSIS 3.1 by cable operators
  • High attach rates of WiFi 6/6E in gateways
  • DOCSIS 4.0 expected within next couple of years
  • Government funding driving global broadband infrastructure buildout

FQ1 2023 Segment Revenue

  • Semiconductor Solutions: $7.1B (+21% YoY), 80% of total revenue - Semiconductor GM: ~69% (-160 bps YoY, driven by product mix within end markets) - Semiconductor OpEx: $802M (-2% YoY); R&D $716M (-1% YoY) - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 58%; operating profit grew 23% YoY
  • Infrastructure Software: $1.8B (-1% YoY), 20% of total revenue - Software GM: 91% - Software OpEx: $346M (-1% YoY) - Software Operating Margin: 72%; operating profit stable YoY

FQ1 2023 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown

  • Networking: $2.3B, +20% YoY, 32% of semiconductor revenue — Tomahawk 4 deployment at hyperscale; growing share in AI networks
  • Wireless: $2.1B, +4% YoY (sequentially flattish vs Q4), 29% of semiconductor revenue — content increases from North American OEM
  • Server Storage Connectivity: $1.3B (record), +57% YoY, 18% of semiconductor revenue — rapid transition to next-gen MegaRAID; transition now significantly complete
  • Broadband: $1.2B (record), +34% YoY, 17% of semiconductor revenue — robust telco 10G PON and cable DOCSIS 3.1 deployments with WiFi 6/6E
  • Industrial: Resales $229M, -4% YoY — China softness, offset by renewable energy and medical strength

FQ1 2023 AI Revenue

  • AI Ethernet Switch Revenue: >$200M in FY2022; forecast to grow to well over $800M in FY2023
  • Compute Offload ASIC Revenue: >$2B in FY2022; on track to exceed $3B in FY2023
  • Very concentrated customer base for compute offload
  • Urgency from hyperscale customers around generative AI emerged in last 90 days
  • AI networks already deployed at certain hyperscalers through Jericho2 switches and Ramon fabric

FQ1 2023 Software Metrics

  • Core Software Revenue: +5% YoY (Brocade SAN business declined — lumpy enterprise consumption)
  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): $5.3B (+3% YoY)
  • Consolidated Renewal Rates: 119% (trailing 12-month: 119%)
  • Strategic Account Renewal Rates: 129% (trailing 12-month: 134%)
  • Strategic Account Bookings: $536M, of which $197M (37%) cross-selling
  • Recurring Mix: >90% recurring subscription and maintenance

Generative AI and Networking

  • Generative AI driving exponential demand for lossless, low-latency, scalable Ethernet networks
  • Large language models require thousands of AI engines working in parallel with massive parametric exchange
  • Network becomes critical constraint — must be lossless, low latency, and scale to thousands of engines
  • Broadcom investing in new generation Ethernet switching designed specifically for AI workloads (different from traditional data center workloads)
  • Customer pull-forward of future products (Tomahawk 5, Jericho3, next-gen compute offload ASICs) observed
  • Still early innings for generative AI but urgency from hyperscalers unprecedented

Tomahawk 5 and Silicon Photonics (Bailly)

  • Tomahawk 5: 51.2 Tbps Ethernet switching; conventional version launch early 2024
  • Bailly: fully integrated silicon photonics co-packaged optics + Tomahawk 5; launch shortly after conventional TH5
  • Performance: 2x Tomahawk 4, at same or lower power
  • Industry-first integrated silicon photonics networking solution announced this week

VMware Acquisition Update

  • Merger clearance received in: Brazil, South Africa, Canada
  • Foreign investment control clearance in: Germany, France, Austria, Denmark, Italy, New Zealand
  • Extended timeline in other key regions given transaction size
  • Still expect close within fiscal 2023

Wireless Business

  • Multi-product franchise: RF/FBAR front-end modules, WiFi/Bluetooth, touchscreen controllers, high-performance mixed signal
  • Nothing meaningful has changed in strategic relationship with North American OEM
  • 3-year product road map visibility
  • All products customized for specific customer requirements

Quarterly Guidance (FQ4 2022)

  • Revenue: $8.9B (+20% YoY, +5% QoQ)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: ~63% of projected revenue
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: 435M (excludes potential Q4 repurchases)
  • Semiconductor Revenue: +25% YoY
  • Networking: +30% YoY
  • Server Storage Connectivity: ~+45% YoY
  • Broadband: above +20% YoY
  • Wireless: seasonally up +20% QoQ, +10% YoY (new platform ramp)
  • Industrial: resales to rebound to high-single-digit growth YoY
  • Infrastructure Software: mid-single-digit % growth YoY
  • Cloud + Service Provider Growth: expected to accelerate YoY in Q4, driven by data center build-outs
  • Enterprise: expected to continue double-digit % growth YoY

Long-Term Targets

  • Semiconductor Industry CAGR: 5–6% over long term (unchanged view)
  • Semiconductor Gross Margin Expansion: 50–100 bps per year sustainable trend; accelerated recently due to pent-up adoption of next-gen products, expected to normalize
  • No China Export Restriction Impact: no products affected by recent restrictions; do not expect to be

FQ3 2022 Segment Revenue

  • Semiconductor Solutions: $6.6B (+32% YoY), 78% of total revenue - Semiconductor GM: ~72% (+220 bps YoY) - Semiconductor OpEx: $853M (+9% YoY); R&D $765M (+10% YoY) - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 59%; operating profit grew 44% YoY
  • Infrastructure Software: $1.8B (+5% YoY), 22% of total revenue - Software GM: 90% (stable YoY) - Software OpEx: $375M (+4% YoY) - Software Operating Margin: 70%; operating profit grew 5% YoY

FQ3 2022 Semiconductor End Market Breakdown

  • Networking: $2.3B (record), +30% YoY, 35% of semiconductor revenue — Tomahawk 4/Trident/Jericho adoption across cloud and enterprise
  • Server Storage Connectivity: $1.1B (record), +70% YoY, 17% of semiconductor revenue — exceeded expectations; next-gen content and deployment in cloud + enterprise
  • Broadband: $1.1B, +20% YoY, 17% of semiconductor revenue — next-gen fiber-to-the-home with WiFi 6/6E
  • Wireless: $1.6B, +14% YoY, 25% of semiconductor revenue — sustained demand from North American OEM
  • Industrial: Resales $244M, -4% YoY — China weakness partially offset by U.S. and Europe strength

FQ3 2022 Semiconductor Customer Mix

  • Consumer: 20–25% of semiconductor revenue (mostly wireless)
  • Enterprise: ~37–40% of semiconductor revenue
  • Service Providers (hyperscale + telco): ~37–40% of semiconductor revenue
  • China Exposure: ~13% of semiconductor revenue

FQ3 2022 Software Metrics

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): $5.5B (+5% YoY)
  • Consolidated Renewal Rates: 128% (trailing 12-month average: 122%)
  • Strategic Account Renewal Rates: 140% (trailing 12-month average: 137%)
  • Strategic Account Bookings: $461M, of which $136M cross-selling
  • Recurring Mix: 95% of renewal value is recurring subscription and maintenance

FQ3 2022 Balance Sheet & Capital Allocation

  • Cash & Equivalents: $10.0B
  • Gross Debt: $39.5B ($304M short-term)
  • DSO: 29 days (vs 30 days a year ago)
  • Inventory: $1.8B (+10% QoQ, due to higher material costs and revenue ramp)
  • Hardware Backlog: $31B (up from $25B prior quarter)
  • Lead Times: unchanged at 50 weeks
  • Cash Dividends Paid: $1.7B
  • Share Repurchases: $1.5B + $292M for tax on vesting (~3.2M shares eliminated)
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: 436M
  • Share Repurchases Paused: since VMware acquisition announcement (SEC regulatory rules)

FQ3 2022 Custom Silicon/ASIC

  • Compute Offload ASIC Revenue: on track for $2B in FY2022
  • ASIC Pipeline: over 70 programs at 7nm, 5nm, and 3nm
  • ASIC CAGR: 20% over past few years

VMware Acquisition Update

  • Pending acquisition making good progress with regulatory filings worldwide
  • Expect transaction to close within Broadcom's fiscal year 2023
  • No share repurchases since VMware announcement (subject to SEC regulatory rules)
  • Commitment to return excess cash to shareholders including buybacks resumes after SEC rules permit

Tomahawk 5 Launch

  • Announced in mid-August 2022
  • 51.2 terabit per second Ethernet switching capacity in single monolithic device — double the bandwidth of any other switch silicon
  • Industry-first silicon photonics co-packaged optics with Tomahawk
  • Enables new benchmark for low power in hyperscale data centers
  • Product generation coexistence: Tomahawk 4 still <30% of total Tomahawk volume; generations 1–4 all shipping simultaneously

WiFi 7

  • Industry leader investing in WiFi 7 as complement to next-gen broadband
  • First deployments expected in second half of 2023

Pricing & Supply Environment

  • Price increases driven solely by material cost pass-through — percentage margin impact is neutral
  • Gross margin improvement driven by accelerated adoption of next-gen products, not pricing
  • Lead times remain at 50 weeks; management values the visibility this provides
  • Backlog terms do not allow cancellation; no cancellations observed
  • Revenue growth constrained by demand scrubbing (shipping to true end demand), not supply

FQ3 2022 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $8.5B (+25% YoY)
  • Gross Margin: 76% of revenue (+80 bps YoY)
  • Operating Expenses: $1.2B (+8% YoY), driven by R&D investment
  • Operating Income: $5.2B (+32% YoY)
  • Operating Margin: 61% of revenue (+320 bps YoY)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $5.4B (63.5% of revenue; excludes $129M depreciation)
  • EPS (Non-GAAP): $9.73 (consensus $9.55, beat by 1.88%)
  • Free Cash Flow: $4.3B (51% of revenue)
  • CapEx: $116M

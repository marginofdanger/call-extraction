Quarterly Guidance (FQ1 2026)

  • Revenue: ~$19.1B (+28% YoY)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: ~67% of revenue
  • Gross Margin: down ~100 bps sequentially (higher mix of AI revenue)
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: ~4.97B
  • Non-GAAP Tax Rate: ~16.5% (Q1 and full year FY2026; up from 14% in FY2025; global minimum tax + geographic mix of income shift)
  • Semiconductor Revenue: ~$12.3B (+50% YoY) - AI Revenue: $8.2B (~100% YoY, doubling) - Non-AI Semiconductor Revenue: ~$4.1B (flat YoY, down QoQ on wireless seasonality)
  • Infrastructure Software Revenue: ~$6.8B (+2% YoY; renewals expected seasonal in Q1)

FY2026 Outlook

  • AI Revenue Growth: accelerating trend through FY2026; "likely to be an accelerating trend as we progress through '26" - Originally indicated 60-70% growth (last quarter); now expects acceleration above that rate - Q1 FY2026 AI revenue doubling YoY ($8.2B); not providing full-year guidance but trajectory accelerating - $73B AI backlog over 18 months is minimum floor; "expect much more as more orders come in"
  • System Sales (2H FY2026): beginning to sell full rack systems (not just XPU chips); will dilute gross margins (passing through more non-Broadcom components) but operating leverage maintains operating margin dollar growth; operating margin % will come down slightly
  • Infrastructure Software: low double-digit % growth for FY2026; driven by VMware
  • Non-AI Semiconductor: stable; broadband recovering, others flat

FQ4 2025 Segment Revenue

  • Semiconductor Solutions: $11.1B (+35% YoY, accelerating from 26% in Q3) — record; 61% of total revenue - Semiconductor GM: ~68% - Semiconductor OpEx: $1.1B (+16% YoY, increased R&D for leading-edge AI) - Semiconductor Operating Margin: 59% (+250 bps YoY)
  • Infrastructure Software: $6.9B (+19% YoY, above $6.7B outlook), 39% of total revenue - Software GM: 93% (vs 91% year-ago) - Software OpEx: $1.1B - Software Operating Margin: 78% (vs 72% year-ago; VMware integration complete)

FQ4 2025 AI Revenue

  • AI Revenue: $6.5B (+74% YoY) — 10x growth over 11 quarters of reporting; driven by custom accelerators more than doubling YoY
  • XPU Use Cases: training, inference, and extending externally (e.g., Google TPUs used by Apple, Cohere, SSI for AI cloud computing); this external usage could be significant in scale
  • 4th Customer (Anthropic — identified via TPU Ironwood rack reference to Google): $10B order in Q3 + additional $11B order in Q4 for delivery late 2026; total $21B
  • 5th Customer (NEW): $1B order placed in Q4 for delivery late 2026 - OpenAI connection: Hock declined to confirm 5th customer identity directly; discussed OpenAI separately - OpenAI Agreement: 10 gigawatt, multiyear (FY2027-2029); described as alignment agreement; separate from XPU development program; not expected to contribute meaningfully in FY2026
  • XPU Customers: now 5 customers + 2 remaining prospects (7 total; possibly 1 more)
  • Multiple XPU Versions per Customer: training vs inference variants; hardware differentiation (sparse core data routers vs dense matrix multipliers); varying memory capacity/bandwidth for different workloads (reasoning, decoding, prefill)
  • Customer Tooling Debate: Hock dismisses as "overblown hypothesis"; custom silicon technology keeps evolving; LLM players better served focusing on models; custom accelerators achieve much better performance than GPUs in hardware-driven optimization

FQ4 2025 AI Backlog & Orders

  • AI Backlog: $73B total; to be delivered over next 18 months (6 quarters) - XPUs: ~$53B - Networking/DSPs/lasers/other: ~$20B - Backlog is a minimum revenue floor; expect significantly more orders within the period given 6-month to 1-year lead times
  • AI Switch Backlog: exceeds $10B; Tomahawk 6 (102 Tbps) booking at record rates; fastest-growing switch product ever deployed; only one of its kind on market
  • Consolidated Backlog: $162B record (AI ~$73B + software ~$73B + non-AI semi remainder)
  • 1.6T DSPs: leading-edge connectivity; very strong demand

FQ4 2025 Non-AI Semiconductor Revenue

  • Non-AI Revenue: $4.6B (+2% YoY, +16% QoQ on wireless seasonality)
  • Broadband: solid recovery
  • Wireless: flat YoY
  • All Other End Markets: down YoY; enterprise spending shows limited signs of recovery
  • AI Crowding Effect: AI potentially "sucking oxygen" from enterprise spending elsewhere
  • Outlook: stable; no further deterioration but no sharp recovery except broadband

FQ4 2025 Software/VMware Metrics

  • Total Contract Value Booked: >$10.4B in Q4 (vs $8.2B year-ago)
  • Infrastructure Software Backlog: $73B (up from $49B year-ago)
  • Renewals: expected seasonal in Q1

FQ4 2025 Balance Sheet & Capital Allocation

  • Cash & Equivalents: $16.2B (+$5.5B QoQ on strong cash flow generation)
  • Gross Debt: $67.1B (principal; essentially all fixed rate) - Fixed rate: $67.1B at 4.0% WAC, 7.2 years maturity - Floating rate: eliminated (vs $500M in Q3)
  • DSO: 36 days (vs 29 year-ago)
  • Inventory: $2.3B (+4% QoQ); 58 days on hand (vs 66 days Q3)
  • Cash Dividends Paid: $2.8B ($0.59/share quarterly)
  • Dividend Increase (Q1 FY2026): $0.65/share (+10%); annual FY2026 $2.60/share (+10% YoY); 15th consecutive annual increase since FY2011
  • Share Repurchase Program: $7.5B remaining; extended through end of calendar year 2026
  • Non-GAAP Diluted Share Count: Q1 FY2026 expected ~4.97B

Supply Chain & Packaging

  • Singapore Advanced Packaging Facility: building substantial facility for in-sourcing advanced packaging; addresses multi-chip assembly needs; driven by supply chain security, delivery certainty, and cost
  • TSMC Silicon Supply: 2nm, 3nm wafer capacity; no constraint yet but monitoring as backlog builds
  • Bottleneck Management: Broadcom has good visibility across AI data center supply chain since it provides multiple key components; sometimes part of the bottleneck itself; feels "pretty good" about supply through 2026

Technology Update

  • Silicon Photonics: developing for 400G → 800G → 1.6T bandwidth; technology exists but not deploying soon; copper still used for scale-up within racks; pluggable optics for scale-out; silicon photonics is the "final straw" when other methods are exhausted
  • 1.6T DSPs: deployed and leading-edge; enabling optical interconnects for scale-out
  • Optical Components: EMLs, VCSELs, CW lasers; demand "going nuts"
  • Tomahawk 6: 102 Tbps; only product of its kind on market; record booking rates; exact product needed for latest GPU/XPU cluster expansion
  • Custom Accelerator Architecture: creating different hardware for different workloads (training vs inference; reasoning vs decoding vs prefill); sparse core data routers vs dense matrix multipliers; hardware-driven differentiation vs software kernels

Competitive Dynamics

  • GPU vs XPU: TPU/XPU being extended to external customers (Google model); substitution is GPU→TPU (transactional) not GPU→custom XPU (strategic multiyear journey); both paths coexisting
  • Customer Tooling Risk: dismissed; silicon technology keeps evolving; LLM players need to focus resources on competing in AI, not silicon design; Broadcom invests heavily in silicon IP (SerDes, packaging, power optimization)
  • Ethernet Dominance: Broadcom XPUs all designed with Ethernet-compatible interfaces; open-source, proven technology; no need for proprietary alternatives

  • Revenue: $18.0B (+28% YoY) — record; above guidance on better-than-expected AI semiconductors and infrastructure software
  • Gross Margin: 77.9% of revenue (better than guided on higher software revenues and product mix within semis; down 50 bps QoQ on semiconductor product mix)
  • Operating Expenses: $2.1B; R&D $1.5B
  • Operating Income: $11.9B (+35% YoY) — record; operating margin 66.2% (+70 bps QoQ despite GM decline, favorable operating leverage)
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $12.2B (68% of revenue; above 67% guidance) — record; +34% YoY; excludes $148M depreciation
  • Free Cash Flow: $7.5B (41% of revenue)
  • CapEx: $237M

Full Year FY2025 Actuals

  • Adjusted EBITDA: $43B (67% of revenue)

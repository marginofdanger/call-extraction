Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2024)

  • Revenue: $18.0B-$18.8B (-6.2% QoQ at midpoint)
  • Gross Margin: 52%-54% (flat at 53% midpoint QoQ; less favorable FX rate offset by product mix changes from smartphone seasonality)
  • Operating Margin: 40%-42%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 31.1 TWD
  • Drivers: Smartphone seasonality impact, partially offset by continued HPC-related demand

Full Year 2024 Guidance

  • Revenue growth: Low to mid-20s% in USD terms; QoQ growth expected throughout 2024
  • Full year GM: 53% and higher (specific full year guidance not given; confident in long-term 53%+ target)
  • HPC: Strongest growth platform, much higher than corporate growth rate
  • Other 3 platforms (Smartphone, IoT, Auto): All expected to grow, but slower than corporate average
  • Semi ex-memory: Expected to grow >10% YoY in 2024
  • Foundry industry: Expected to grow ~20% YoY in 2024

Long-Term Targets

  • Revenue CAGR: 15-20% over next several years in USD terms (reiterated; "well on track" despite challenging 2023)
  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher achievable (long-term; excluding FX impact; considering global manufacturing footprint expansion)
  • ROE: >25% sustainable
  • Capital spending rate of increase leveling off; capital intensity declining as revenue grows

Q4 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 3nm: 15% of Q4 wafer revenue
  • 5nm: 35% of Q4 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 17% of Q4 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 67% of Q4 wafer revenue

Q4 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 43% of Q4 revenue (+17% QoQ)
  • Smartphone: 43% of Q4 revenue (+27% QoQ)
  • IoT: 5% of Q4 revenue (-29% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 5% of Q4 revenue (+13% QoQ)
  • DCE: 2% of Q4 revenue (-35% QoQ)

Q4 Balance Sheet

  • Cash & marketable securities: TWD 1.7 trillion (USD 55 billion)
  • Current liabilities: decreased TWD 56B QoQ (mainly due to decrease in accounts payable)
  • AR turnover days: 31 days (-4 days QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 85 days (-11 days QoQ; primarily due to higher 3nm wafer shipment)

Q4 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: TWD 395 billion
  • CapEx: TWD 170 billion (USD 5.24 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 78 billion (Q1 2023 cash dividend)
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 154B to TWD 1.47 trillion at end of Q4

FY2023 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 3nm: 6% of 2023 wafer revenue (entered volume production 2H 2023)
  • 5nm: 33% of 2023 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 19% of 2023 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 58% of 2023 wafer revenue (up from 53% in 2022)

FY2023 Platform Revenue

  • HPC: 43% of 2023 revenue (flat YoY)
  • Smartphone: 38% of 2023 revenue (-8% YoY)
  • IoT: 8% of 2023 revenue (-17% YoY)
  • Automotive: 6% of 2023 revenue (+15% YoY)
  • DCE: declined -16% YoY

FY2023 Industry Context

  • Semiconductor industry ex-memory: declined ~2% YoY in 2023
  • Foundry industry: declined ~13% YoY in 2023
  • TSMC revenue declined 8.7% YoY in USD terms, outperforming foundry industry

CapEx / Capital Intensity

  • 2024 CapEx: $28B-$32B
  • CapEx allocation: 70-80% advanced process technologies; 10-20% specialty technologies; ~10% advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others
  • Depreciation: Expected to increase close to 30% YoY in 2024, mainly as 3nm ramps
  • Capital intensity trend: Peaked >50% in 2021, then 47%, then 43%; 2024 expected mid-30s%; next several years expected ~30%
  • Rate of increase in capital spending beginning to level off
  • Significant CoWoS capacity expansion within CapEx

N3 Technology

  • N3 entered volume production 2H 2023, accounting for 6% of 2023 wafer revenue
  • N3E entered volume production Q4 2023
  • 2024 revenue: Expected to more than triple from 2023; mid-teens % of total wafer revenue
  • 2024 GM dilution: 3-4 pp for full year (N3 volume much bigger in 2H vs 1H)
  • N5-to-N3 capacity conversion: 1-2 pp dilution in 2H 2024 (onetime short-term cost; brings capital efficiency in mid/long term)
  • N3 profitability improving; dilution expected to gradually reduce over next several years
  • Further enhancements: N3P, N3X
  • N3 family expected to be another large and long-lasting node
  • Almost all world's smartphone and HPC innovators working with TSMC on 3nm

N2 Technology

  • Transistor structure: Nanosheet (gate-all-around)
  • Volume production: On track for 2025; ramp profile similar to N3
  • Development: Progressing well; device performance and yield on track or ahead of plan
  • Customer engagement: Much higher level of interest at N2 vs N3 at similar stage, from both HPC and smartphone
  • Backside power rail: Available to customers 2H 2025; production in 2026 (suited for specific HPC applications based on performance, cost and maturity)
  • N2 fabs: Multiple fabs/phases planned in Hsinchu and Kaohsiung science parks; Taichung Science Park government approval process ongoing and on track

AI Demand

  • AI server processor revenue 2023: ~6% of total revenue
  • AI server processor revenue target: High teens % of total revenue by ~2027 (or higher); up from prior "low teens" target
  • AI revenue CAGR: ~50% over next several years
  • CoWoS capacity 2024: Doubling output from 2023; demand still exceeding supply, condition expected to continue into 2025
  • SoIC: >50% CAGR expected in next few years (at least)
  • Edge AI: Emerging; smartphones and PCs starting to incorporate AI (neural processors), increasing silicon content
  • AI is structural, multi-year growth driver; TSMC is key enabler
  • TSMC well positioned to capture major portion of AI semiconductor content (front-end + back-end/packaging)
  • Almost all AI innovators working with TSMC

Gross Margin Dynamics -- 2024

  • Headwinds: N3 dilution (3-4 pp in 2H), N5-to-N3 capacity conversion costs (1-2 pp in 2H), overseas fab ramp-up costs, FX uncertainty
  • Tailwinds: Higher utilization rate (recovering from 2023 downcycle), cost improvement, value-selling, technology mix improvement
  • Long term: 53% and higher achievable; N3 dilution gradually reducing; conversion costs onetime

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Japan (Kumamoto Fab 1): Opening ceremony Feb 24, 2024; volume production Q4 2024; 12/16nm and 22/28nm
  • Japan (Kumamoto Fab 2): In serious evaluation stage; still discussing with Japan government; technology would be either 7nm or 12/16nm
  • Arizona Fab 1: N4 process; volume production on track for 1H 2025; strong progress on facility, supply chain, utility, equipment installation; signed agreement with Arizona Building and Construction Trades Council
  • Arizona Fab 2: Shell under construction; technology still under discussion (depends on U.S. government incentives); timeline '27 or '28; technology loaded based on customer demand at that timing
  • Germany (ESMC): 22/28nm + 12/16nm; JV with NXP/Bosch/Infineon; fab construction scheduled to begin Q4 2024; automotive and industrial applications focus; strong support from German federal, state and city governments
  • Taiwan: Expanding 3nm capacity in Tainan Science Park; N2 fabs in Hsinchu and Kaohsiung science parks (multiple fabs/phases); Taichung Science Park approval ongoing
  • Overseas GM dilution: Initial costs higher than Taiwan fabs; committed to manage and minimize cost gap; delivering profitable growth
  • Arizona fab majority U.S. customers, but open to everybody

Pricing

  • Strategic, value-based pricing approach
  • TSMC's technology value in supply chain is increasing (especially with AI/advanced technology leadership)
  • No specific ASP trends shared

Dividend

  • Q3 2023 dividend: TWD 3.5/share (approved Nov 2023; up from TWD 3.0 previously; new minimum quarterly dividend level; distributed April 2024)
  • 2023 annual dividend received by shareholders: TWD 11.25/share
  • 2024 annual dividend: At least TWD 13.5/share
  • Policy focus shifting from "sustainable" to "steadily increasing" cash dividend per share

Tax Rate

  • ~15% (Taiwan Industrial Innovation Statute benefit continuing)

Mature Node / Specialty Technology

  • ~70% of TSMC total revenue is 16nm and more advanced; mature node exposure ~20% of total revenue
  • Industry-wide mature node overcapacity concern is valid
  • TSMC mature node strategy: specialty technology, differentiated; work closely with strategic partners
  • Effective capacity with customer loading commitments
  • Profitability of mature nodes around corporate average gross margin
  • 28nm forecast as sweet spot for embedded memory applications; long-term structural demand supported by multiple specialty technologies
  • Expanding 28nm specialty capacity overseas to support long-term structural demand

Inventory / Cycle

  • 2023 downcycle concluded; prolonged by weakening macro, high inflation/interest rates
  • Fabless semiconductor inventory returned to healthy level exiting 2023
  • 2024 expected to be a healthy growth year; broad-based recovery
  • AI demand accelerating on top of cyclical recovery
  • Macroeconomic weakness and geopolitical uncertainties persist

Leadership Transition

  • Chairman Mark Liu announced retirement; will not seek Board renomination; retiring after June 2024 AGM
  • Board recommended Dr. C. C. Wei to succeed as Chairman; would also continue as CEO
  • Supported by deep and experienced team of senior executives

Q4 2023 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: +14.4% QoQ in NT dollar; +13.6% QoQ in USD (supported by strong 3nm ramp)
  • Gross Margin: 53.0% (-130 bps QoQ; decline primarily due to margin dilution from 3nm ramp)
  • Operating Margin: 41.6% (-10 bps QoQ; slightly ahead of guidance due to operating leverage on higher revenue)
  • EPS: TWD 9.21
  • ROE: 28.1%

Full Year 2023 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: USD 69B (-8.7% YoY in USD; TWD 2.16 trillion, -4.5% YoY in NT)
  • Gross Margin: 54.4% (-5.2 pp YoY; mainly reflecting lower capacity utilization and 3nm ramp, partially offset by more favorable FX)
  • Operating Margin: 42.6% (-6.9 pp YoY; continued R&D investment in 3nm and 2nm despite lower revenue base)
  • EPS: TWD 32.34 (-17.5% YoY)
  • ROE: 26.2%
  • CapEx: USD 30.45B (TWD 950B); below prior guidance of ~USD 32B due to tightening amid near-term uncertainties
  • Operating cash flow: TWD 1.7 trillion
  • Free cash flow: TWD 292 billion
  • Cash dividends paid: TWD 292 billion

Quarterly Guidance (Q4 2022)

  • Revenue: $19.9B--$20.7B (+0.4% sequential increase at midpoint; flattish as customer inventory adjustment balanced by continued 5nm ramp for smartphone and HPC)
  • Gross Margin: 59.5%--61.5% (60.5% at midpoint; flattish QoQ as more favorable FX assumption offset by lower capacity utilization rate)
  • Operating Margin: 49%--51%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 31.5 TWD

Full Year 2022 Guidance

  • Revenue growth: Mid-30% in USD terms
  • CapEx: ~$36 billion (lowered from $40B--$44B range; previously guided to closer to lower end of the range). About half of the reduction due to continued tool delivery challenges; other half due to capacity optimization based on current medium-term outlook (mainly N7/N6 adjustment)
  • CapEx allocation: 70--80% advanced process technologies; ~10% advanced packaging and mask making; 10--20% specialty technologies (unchanged)
  • Depreciation: Mid-single-digit increase year-on-year for 2022

2023 Outlook

  • Semiconductor market (including memory): Expected to decline in 2023
  • TSMC: Expects 2023 to be a growth year; business to be more resilient than overall semiconductor industry during both downturn and upturn
  • Three resilience factors: (1) Technology leadership and differentiation much stronger today vs. previous years, (2) comprehensive design ecosystem and strong HPC portfolio capturing structural demand increase, (3) strategic long-term customer relationships in technology development, capacity planning and pricing
  • Inventory correction: Supply chain inventory peaked in Q3 2022, started to reduce in Q4 2022; biggest impact expected in 1H 2023; will take a few quarters through 1H 2023 to rebalance to healthier level
  • 2023 profitability challenges: N3 ramp dilution, higher YoY increase in depreciation costs, rising inflationary costs, semiconductor cyclicality, overseas fab expansions
  • 2023 depreciation: Expected to be meaningfully higher YoY (specific guidance to be provided in January)
  • Demand segments: Consumer end market soft; data center and automotive-related steady for now at TSMC, but possibility of adjustment down the road not ruled out

Long-Term Targets

  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher is achievable (reiterated; confident even through cyclical downturn and cost challenges)
  • Revenue CAGR: 15--20% over next several years in USD terms (reiterated)
  • Capital intensity: Mid-to-high 30% range longer-term
  • Structural megatrend: Energy-efficient computing accelerating in intelligent and connected world; semiconductor industry value in supply chain increasing; technology platform value expanding beyond geometry shrink toward greater power efficiency

Q3 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 5nm: 28% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 26% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 54% of Q3 wafer revenue

Q3 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • Smartphone: 41% of Q3 revenue (+25% QoQ)
  • HPC: 39% of Q3 revenue (+4% QoQ)
  • IoT: 10% of Q3 revenue (+33% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 5% of Q3 revenue (+15% QoQ)
  • DCE: 2% of Q3 revenue (-2% QoQ)

Q3 Balance Sheet

  • Cash & marketable securities: TWD 1.5 trillion
  • Current liabilities: decreased TWD 38 billion QoQ (TWD 116B decrease in short-term loans, partially offset by TWD 70B increase in accrued liabilities and others)
  • Long-term interest-bearing debt: increased TWD 88 billion QoQ (mainly from TWD 60B corporate bond issuance)
  • AR turnover days: 36 days (-1 day QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 90 days (-5 days QoQ; primarily due to higher wafer shipment during the quarter)

Q3 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: TWD 413 billion
  • CapEx: TWD 266 billion (USD 8.75 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 71 billion (Q4 2021 cash dividend)
  • Corporate bond issuance: raised TWD 60 billion
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 43 billion to TWD 1.3 trillion at end of Q3

Profitability Framework

  • Six factors determining TSMC profitability: (1) leadership technology development and ramp-up, (2) pricing, (3) cost reduction, (4) capacity utilization, (5) technology mix, (6) foreign exchange rates
  • Q3 GM beat vs. guidance: Guidance based on FX assumption of 29.7 TWD/USD; actual was 30.32 TWD/USD, creating ~80 bps favorable difference
  • Q4 GM outlook: Flattish QoQ at 60.5% midpoint; more favorable FX assumption offset by lower capacity utilization rate
  • Long-term gross margin: 53% and higher is achievable (excluding FX impact, taking other 5 factors into consideration)

N3 Technology

  • Volume production: On track for Q4 2022 (later in the quarter) with good yield
  • 2023 ramp: Smooth ramp expected, driven by both HPC and smartphone applications
  • Demand: Exceeds ability to supply (partially due to ongoing tool delivery issues, which are broad-based including lithography/EUV)
  • Utilization: Expect N3 fully utilized in 2023
  • Revenue contribution 2023: Higher than N5 revenue in its first year (2020); mid-single-digit % of wafer revenue in 2023 (overall revenue base much larger today than in 2020)
  • N3 gross margin dilution: 2 to 3 percentage points on a whole year basis for 2023
  • N3E: Enhanced performance, power and yield; complete platform support for smartphone and HPC; development progressing ahead of plan; volume production now scheduled for 2H 2023
  • N3E pull-in: Originally 1 year apart from N3; pulled in by 2--3 months due to better-than-expected progress
  • Tape-outs: High level of customer engagement at both N3 and N3E; number of tape-outs more than 2x that of N5 in its first and second year
  • Capacity: Working closely with tool suppliers to address delivery challenges and prepare more 3nm capacity for 2023, 2024 and beyond
  • Assessment: Most advanced semiconductor technology in both PPA and transistor technology when introduced; confident N3 family will be another large and long-lasting node for TSMC
  • Technology roadmap: N5, N4P, N4X ramping successfully; N3E upcoming ramp to continue expanding customer product portfolio and increase addressable market

N7/N6 Utilization

  • Current: Not as high as past 3 years due to end market weakness in smartphone and PCs, and customers' product schedule delays starting Q4 2022
  • Outlook: Expect lower utilization to persist through 1H 2023 as supply chain inventory rebalances
  • Recovery: Expect N7/N6 demand to pick up in 2H 2023
  • Assessment: Cyclical issue, not structural; most smartphone and PC customers use N7/N6, and market weakness in both segments coincided with customer product schedule delays
  • CapEx: Adjusted N7/N6 CapEx down accordingly (capacity optimization is the main area of CapEx reduction)
  • Longer term: Working closely with customers to develop specialty and differentiated technology to drive additional wave of structural demand from consumer, RF, connectivity and other applications to backfill N7/N6 capacity over next several years; 7nm family will continue to be a large and long-lasting node
  • Conversion to N5: Not planned; N7 demand issue is cyclical rather than structural

N2 Technology

  • Production: Scheduled for 2025 (maintained); progress slightly ahead of schedule
  • Development status: Comparable to N5 and N3 at similar stage; acknowledged as very difficult but progress is ahead
  • Customer engagement: Comparable to N3 and N5
  • Technology: N3 and N2 will deliver full node strides in performance and power benefits while offering the industry's most advanced transistor scaling

Pricing

  • Strategic and consistent; not opportunistic
  • Will not change pricing strategy during cyclical downturn
  • Value-based pricing reflecting leadership technology value
  • Working closely with customers to provide value and help them win in their end markets
  • Pricing not differentiated by manufacturing location (same policy regardless of country)

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Arizona: Work continuing and on schedule; described as N5 family, which still has very strong demand
  • Japan (Kumamoto/JASM): On schedule to meet customers' demand
  • Nanjing: Received 1-year authorization covering the Nanjing facility for both 28nm and 16nm; 28nm expansion continuing and on schedule
  • Kaohsiung: Initially planned 2 fabs (28nm expansion and N7); N7 has been adjusted; 28nm expansion continuing and on schedule
  • Europe: In preliminary evaluation; not ruling out any possibility; decision based on customers' need, business opportunities, operational efficiency and cost economics
  • Overseas cost differential: Initial cost of overseas fabs higher than Taiwan, mainly due to higher labor costs across different layers of the supply chain; working closely with U.S. government, customers and supply chain partners to manage and minimize cost gap
  • Financial overhead from US regulations: No additional overhead cost expected at this time
  • Overseas expansion philosophy: Based on customers' need, business opportunity, operational efficiency and cost economics

Capacity / Cycle Management

  • N3 capacity tight despite downturn in other nodes
  • Strong demand for leading nodes except N7; steady demand for differentiated specialty technologies and mature nodes
  • Will not build speculative capacity; based on long-term structural market demand profile
  • Maintaining investment discipline while protecting long-term growth position
  • Taking conservative approach for 2023 planning
  • Will continue to work closely with customers to plan long-term capacity and invest in leading-edge and specialty technologies

US Regulation Impact (October 2022 Export Controls)

  • Initial assessment: Impact to TSMC is limited and manageable
  • Scope: New regulation sets control threshold at very high-end specification, primarily for AI or supercomputing applications (e.g., 600 bits per second bandwidth thresholds)
  • Compliance: Regulations described as simple to understand; continuing to work with customers to ensure full compliance
  • Companion chips: No restriction on companion chips used alongside restricted high-end chips
  • Additional overhead cost: Not expected at this time
  • China market: Every region is important to TSMC; will continue to serve all customers worldwide under condition of full compliance with all rules and regulations
  • Long-term impact: Too early to fully assess; will provide updates in following earnings calls

Inventory Cycle Detail

  • Peak: Supply chain inventory peaked in Q3 2022
  • Q4 2022: Inventory started to reduce
  • Biggest impact: Expected in 1H 2023
  • Rebalance timeline: A few quarters through 1H 2023 to reach healthier levels
  • Customer inventory levels: At high levels (analysts noted 25-year high in days of inventory)
  • TSMC resilience: Technology position, strong HPC portfolio and long-term strategic customer relationships expected to make business more resilient than overall semiconductor industry

Dividend / Capital Returns

  • Committed to sustainable and steadily increasing cash dividend
  • Share buyback: Not being considered at this time; cash better kept to invest in CapEx for better shareholder returns
  • Cash dividend distributed in Q3: TWD 71 billion (Q4 2021 dividend)

Depreciation

  • FY2022: Mid-single-digit increase year-on-year
  • FY2023: Expected to be meaningfully higher YoY (specific guidance in January); depreciation going on and coming off the table each year; net result is what is reported
  • Depreciation is a headwind to gross margin going forward

FX Impact

  • NT dollar depreciation vs USD creates significant difference between NT and USD revenue growth
  • Q3 2022: 14.8% sequential growth in NT vs 11.4% in USD
  • Full year growth significantly different in NT vs USD terms
  • FX is one of the 6 profitability factors but is outside management's control

Leading Node Adoption Drivers

  • Geometry shrink slowing and becoming more challenging due to rising process complexity
  • However, demand for energy-efficient computing accelerating as technology becomes more pervasive
  • Semiconductor industry value in supply chain increasing
  • Technology platform value expanding beyond geometry shrink toward greater power efficiency
  • Customers value system performance and power efficiency, not simply transistor cost
  • Strong demand for leading node technologies driven by both smartphone and HPC to fuel long-term revenue growth
  • TSMC technology cadence remains constant; leadership in both leading-edge process technology and 3D solutions

Q3 2022 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: TWD 613.1 billion (USD 20.2 billion; +14.8% QoQ in NT; +11.4% QoQ in USD), supported by strong demand for industry-leading 5nm technology
  • Gross Margin: 60.4% (+130 bps QoQ; exceeded high end of guidance, driven by more favorable FX rate and cost improvement efforts despite continued inflationary cost pressures)
  • Operating Margin: 50.6% (+1.5 pp QoQ; mainly due to better operating leverage)
  • Operating Expenses: 9.8% of net revenue (vs. 10% in Q2 2022)
  • EPS: TWD 10.83
  • ROE: 42.9%
  • FX actual: 1 USD = 30.32 TWD (vs. guidance assumption of 29.7 TWD; created ~80 bps positive difference vs. original guidance)

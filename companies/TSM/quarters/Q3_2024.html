Quarterly Guidance (Q4 2024)

  • Revenue: $26.1B-$26.9B (+13% QoQ sequential increase at midpoint; +35% YoY at midpoint)
  • Gross Margin: 57%-59% (midpoint 58%; +20 bps QoQ at midpoint)
  • Operating Margin: 46.5%-48.5%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 32.0 TWD
  • Q4 GM drivers: Higher overall capacity utilization rate, partially offset by continued dilution from N3 ramp-up, higher electricity prices in Taiwan, and N5 to N3 tool conversion costs

Full Year 2024 Guidance

  • Revenue growth: Close to 30% in USD terms (significantly above original "low to mid-20s%" guidance)
  • CapEx: Slightly above $30B
  • CapEx allocation: 70%-80% for advanced process technologies; 10%-20% for specialty technologies; ~10% for advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others
  • Full year GM: Well above 53%

Long-Term Targets

  • Revenue CAGR 2021-2026: 15%-20% in USD terms (on track; nearing completion of window)
  • Next 5-year CAGR (2024-2029): Not providing specific number yet, but "very healthy growth" expected
  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher (not revising upward yet)
  • Semiconductor ex-memory: Near $1 trillion by 2030

Demand / Cycle Outlook

  • AI demand driving overall strength; described as "just the beginning" and will continue for many years
  • Non-AI demand (smartphone, PC, IoT) stabilized and starting to improve
  • PC and smartphone unit growth: low single digit; but silicon content increasing faster than unit growth due to AI-related applications
  • Automotive: steady growth
  • Overall healthy demand environment heading into 2025
  • 2025 expected to be a healthy growth year for TSMC

Q3 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 3nm: 20% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • 5nm: 32% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 17% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 69% of Q3 wafer revenue

Q3 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 51% of Q3 revenue (+11% QoQ)
  • Smartphone: 34% of Q3 revenue (+16% QoQ)
  • IoT: 7% of Q3 revenue (+35% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 5% of Q3 revenue (+6% QoQ)
  • DCE: 1% of Q3 revenue (-19% QoQ)

Q3 Balance Sheet

  • Cash & marketable securities: TWD 2.2 trillion (USD 69 billion)
  • Current liabilities: increased TWD 31B QoQ (primarily driven by reclassification of TWD 42B in bonds payable from noncurrent to current liabilities)
  • Long-term interest-bearing debt: decreased TWD 38B QoQ
  • AR turnover days: 28 days (steady QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 87 days (+4 days QoQ, primarily due to prebuild of N3 and N5 wafers)

Q3 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: TWD 392 billion
  • CapEx: TWD 207 billion (USD 6.4 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 91 billion (Q4 2023 cash dividend)
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 88B to TWD 1.9 trillion at end of Q3

Gross Margin Dynamics

  • Q3 2024 beat: Exceeded high end of Q3 guidance by 230 bps, mainly due to higher-than-expected overall capacity utilization rate
  • Q3 QoQ improvement: +460 bps, primarily due to higher capacity utilization rate and better cost improvement efforts including productivity gains
  • Q4 2024: Guided 57%-59% (midpoint 58%; +20 bps QoQ) -- higher utilization partially offset by N3 dilution, electricity, and N5-to-N3 conversion costs
  • Full year 2024: Well above 53%
  • Long-term target: 53% and higher (not revising upward yet despite multi-quarter performance well above)

2025 Gross Margin Puts & Takes

  • Tailwinds: N3 dilution gradually reducing (+); value-selling (+); higher utilization from healthy growth year (+)
  • Headwinds: Overseas fab dilution 2-3 percentage points when beginning to ramp (-); N5-to-N3 capacity conversion costs (-); N2 preparation costs for 2026 ramp (-); electricity and inflationary cost impact of at least 1% (-); FX uncertainty (1% USD/TWD movement = 40 bps GM impact)
  • Not providing specific 2025 revenue or GM guidance yet

Electricity Cost Impact

  • Taiwan electricity price for TSMC rose 14% in October 2024 (second increase in the year)
  • Prior increases: +15% in 2022, +17% in 2023, +25% in 2024
  • Price has doubled in the last few years
  • Taiwan electricity price expected to be highest among all regions TSMC operates
  • Higher electricity plus other inflationary costs expected to impact gross margin by at least 1%

AI Demand

  • Server AI processor definition: GPUs, AI accelerators, and CPUs performing training and inference functions; does not include networking, edge, or on-device AI
  • AI accelerator revenue 2024: More than tripling YoY (stronger than prior "more than doubling" language)
  • AI revenue share 2024: Mid-teens % of total revenue
  • AI revenue CAGR: ~50% over next several years (maintained)
  • AI demand is real and structural; TSMC validates through internal AI use (1% productivity gain = ~TWD 1B value) and deep customer engagement
  • Almost every AI innovator is working with TSMC, including hyperscalers building their own chips
  • AI demand described as "just the beginning"; non-AI demand stabilized and starting to improve

Foundry 2.0

  • Definition: Includes wafer manufacturing, packaging, testing, mask making, and others
  • 2023 total addressable market: Close to $250B
  • 2024 growth: ~10% YoY (forecast stays the same as prior quarter)
  • TSMC share: ~28-30% (C.C. Wei: "probably around 30%")
  • Advanced packaging, testing, mask making now a little higher than 10% of TSMC's total revenue
  • Leading process nodes and advanced packaging have much stronger growth vs. mature nodes and conventional packaging

N2 Technology

  • Production: H2 2025 (maintained)
  • Development: On track
  • Customer demand: More demand than TSMC ever dreamed about, compared with N3
  • Capacity: Preparing more capacity in N2 than in N3
  • Many customers interested in N2; chiplet designs do not reduce N2 demand
  • N2P (next version) in development

A16 (1.6nm)

  • Production: H2 2026
  • Backside power delivery (Super Power Rail)
  • Very attractive for AI server chips
  • Demand also very high
  • TSMC working hard to prepare both N2 and A16 capacity

N3 Technology

  • N3 dilution gradually reducing in 2025 as yield improves and scale increases
  • N5-to-N3 capacity conversion ongoing to meet strong N3 demand
  • N3 family large and long-lasting
  • N5-to-N3 conversion costs and N2 preparation costs becoming bigger with each leading node migration

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Arizona Fab 1: N4 process; entered engineering wafer production in April 2024 with very good yield; volume production expected beginning of 2025; clean room area ~2x the size of a typical logic fab
  • Arizona Fab 2: More advanced technology based on customer needs; volume production scheduled 2028; clean room area ~2x typical logic fab
  • Arizona Fab 3: Production by end of the decade; most advanced technology; clean room area ~2x typical logic fab
  • Plan to build 3 fabs in Arizona creating greater economies of scale
  • Strong commitment and support from U.S. customers and U.S. federal, state, and city governments (CHIPS Act)
  • Japan (Kumamoto Fab 1): Completed all process qualification; volume production started Q3 2024; delivering same quality and reliability as Taiwan fabs
  • Japan (Kumamoto Fab 2): Land preparation begun; construction to begin Q4 2025; supports consumer, automotive, industrial, and HPC-related applications; volume production targeted by end of 2027
  • Germany (ESMC): Groundbreaking ceremony August 2024; 12/16 FinFET and 28nm process technologies; automotive and industrial focus; JV with partners; volume production scheduled by end of 2027; strong commitment from European Commission and German federal, state, and city government
  • Overseas GM dilution: 2-3 percentage points when beginning to ramp (Arizona + Japan starting 2025); expected to persist for 3-5 years as new phases/fabs come online sequentially (Fab 1 improves, then Fab 2 ramps, etc.)
  • Overseas fab costs higher for everyone under fragmented globalization; TSMC leveraging manufacturing technology leadership and large-scale manufacturing base to be most efficient in each region

Advanced Packaging

  • Revenue share: About high single digit % of total revenue in 2024
  • Growth: Growing faster than corporate average over next several years (at least 5 years)
  • Margin: Approaching corporate average but not there yet; improving
  • Critical enabling technology for AI accelerators

CoWoS

  • 2024 capacity: More than 2x compared with 2023
  • 2025 capacity: Doubling again (still not enough to meet demand)
  • Customer demand far exceeds ability to supply; still supply-constrained into 2025
  • Multiple CoWoS variants serving different customer product architectures

Pricing / Value

  • Value-selling approach is a continuous and ongoing process
  • Technology value increasing significantly with AI
  • TSMC views customers and equipment suppliers as partners
  • Strategic pricing; working with customers on fair value sharing
  • Capital-intensive business requires high gross margin for sustainable healthy growth
  • Analysts pushing for higher LT GM target; management maintaining discipline at 53%+
  • C.C. Wei noted one of the biggest AI suppliers has gross margin TSMC would "probably never be able to reach"

Dividend

  • Policy: Sustainable and steadily increasing; harvesting past investments as free cash flow increases
  • Expect dividend increases to continue
  • Primary use of cash/balance sheet is organic growth; remaining free cash flow returned to shareholders

CapEx / Capital Intensity

  • 2024 CapEx: Slightly above $30B
  • 2024 CapEx allocation: 70%-80% advanced process technologies; 10%-20% specialty technologies; ~10% advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others
  • 2025 CapEx: Not providing specific guidance yet; very likely to be higher than 2024 given healthy growth outlook (more details in January conference)
  • Higher CapEx always correlated with higher growth opportunities in following years
  • Capital intensity expected in mid-30s%

IDM Outsourcing

  • Not interested in acquiring any IDM's fabs
  • Receiving sizable business from U.S. IDM customer; too early to comment on whether outsourcing will increase further
  • Samsung outsourcing: no specific comment

Power / Energy

  • Building many fabs in Taiwan requiring electricity, water, and land
  • Close communication with Taiwan government; received assurance of support for electricity, water, and land
  • Not ready to share specifics on energy sources (nuclear, green energy, etc.)

FX Sensitivity

  • 1% movement in USD/TWD exchange rate impacts gross margin by 40 basis points
  • Q4 2024 FX assumption: 1 USD = 32.0 TWD

Q3 2024 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $23.5B (+12.8% QoQ in NT; above guidance in USD terms)
  • Gross Margin: 57.8% (+460 bps QoQ; exceeded high end of guidance by 230 bps, driven by higher-than-expected overall capacity utilization rate)
  • Operating Margin: 47.5% (+500 bps QoQ)
  • Operating Expenses: 10.4% of net revenue (due to operating leverage)
  • EPS: TWD 12.54
  • ROE: 33.4%

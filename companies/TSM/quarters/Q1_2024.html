Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2024)

  • Revenue: $19.6B–$20.4B (+6% QoQ; +27.6% YoY at midpoint)
  • Gross Margin: 51%–53% (midpoint 52%; -1.1 pp QoQ, primarily due to earthquake impact and higher electricity costs)
  • Operating Margin: 40%–42%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 32.3 TWD
  • Tax rate: Slightly above 19% (due to accrual of tax on undistributed retained earnings)
  • Supported by strong demand for 3nm and 5nm technologies, partially offset by continued smartphone seasonality

Tax Rate Guidance

  • Q2 2024: Slightly above 19% (undistributed retained earnings tax accrual)
  • Q3/Q4 2024: 13%–14%
  • Full year 2024: 15%–16%
  • 2023 comparison: 14.5%

Full Year 2024 Guidance

  • Revenue growth: Low to mid-20% in USD terms (reaffirmed; expects QoQ growth throughout 2024, 2H stronger than 1H)
  • CapEx: $28B–$32B (reiterated)
  • CapEx allocation: 70%–80% for advanced process technologies; 10%–20% for specialty technologies; ~10% for advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others
  • Capital intensity: Mid-30s% for 2024 and next several years (rate of increase leveling off)
  • Full year GM: 53% and higher (long-term target)
  • Semi ex-memory: Expected to grow ~10% in 2024 (lowered from prior >10% forecast)
  • Foundry industry growth: Mid- to high-teens % in 2024
  • TSMC: To grow significantly above overall semi market

Long-Term Targets

  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher achievable (excluding FX impact, considering global manufacturing footprint expansion)
  • ROE: Greater than 25% (sustainable)
  • TSMC is most efficient and cost-effective manufacturer in every region it operates

Q1 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 3nm: 9% of Q1 wafer revenue
  • 5nm: 37% of Q1 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 19% of Q1 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 65% of Q1 wafer revenue

Q1 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 46% of Q1 revenue (+3% QoQ)
  • Smartphone: 38% of Q1 revenue (-16% QoQ)
  • IoT: 6% of Q1 revenue (+5% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 6% of Q1 revenue (flat QoQ)
  • DCE: 2% of Q1 revenue (+33% QoQ)

Q1 Balance Sheet

  • Cash & marketable securities: TWD 1.9 trillion (USD 60 billion)
  • Current liabilities: increased TWD 113B QoQ (TWD 140B increase in accrued liabilities and others, partially offset by TWD 44B decrease in accounts payable; accrued liabilities increase mainly due to reclassification of temporary receipt from customers from long-term liabilities)
  • AR turnover days: 31 days (flat QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 90 days (+5 days QoQ, primarily due to ramp of 3nm technologies)

Q1 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: TWD 436 billion
  • CapEx: TWD 181 billion (USD 5.77 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 78 billion (Q2 2023 cash dividend)
  • Bond issuances: raised TWD 23 billion
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 233B to TWD 1.7 trillion at end of Q1

Q2 Gross Margin Bridge

  • Earthquake impact: ~50 bps headwind (wafer scraps and material loss)
  • Electricity cost increase: 70–80 bps headwind (25% price increase effective April 1, 2024)

Earthquake Impact (April 3, 2024)

  • Magnitude: 7.2 (largest in Taiwan in 25 years)
  • Maximum magnitude at TSMC fabs: 5
  • Tool recovery: >70% within first 10 hours; fully recovered by end of day 3
  • Structural damage: None; no power outages; no damage to critical tools including all EUV lithography tools
  • Wafer impact: Certain number of wafers in process scrapped; most lost production expected to be recovered in Q2
  • Q2 revenue impact: Minimal
  • Q2 GM impact: ~50 bps (wafer scraps and material loss)
  • All TSMC personnel safe

Electricity Cost

  • Taiwan electricity price increased 25% starting April 1, 2024 (after 17% increase from April 1, 2023)
  • Q2 2024 GM impact: 70–80 bps
  • 2H 2024 GM impact: 60–70 bps
  • Higher electricity cost also expected to indirectly lead to higher materials, chemicals, gases and other variable costs

N3 Technology

  • Q1 2024 revenue contribution: 9% of wafer revenue
  • 2024 full year N3 revenue: Expected to more than triple vs 2023
  • 1H 2024 GM dilution: 2–3 percentage points
  • 2H 2024 GM dilution: 3–4 percentage points (revenue contribution from 3nm expected to increase significantly in 2H)
  • N5-to-N3 conversion dilution: 1–2 percentage points in 2H 2024 (converting some 5nm tools to support strong multiyear 3nm demand)
  • Time to corporate average GM: 10–12 quarters (vs 8–10 quarters for N5 and N7)
  • Dilution vs past nodes: More severe than N5 and N7 due to greater process complexity, higher corporate average baseline, and pricing set several years ahead of production followed by cost inflation
  • Strong customer demand across HPC and smartphone
  • Managing through internal cost improvement and value-selling

N2 Technology

  • Production: Volume production in 2025 (last quarter of 2025)
  • Meaningful revenue: Expected from end of Q1 / beginning of Q2 2026
  • Ramp profile: Similar to N3
  • Transistor structure: Nanosheet transistors (most advanced in industry for density and energy efficiency)
  • Development: Progressing well; device performance and yield on track or ahead of plan
  • Tape-outs: Expected to be higher than both N3 and N5 in first 2 years
  • Margin profile: Expect better margin profile than N3 (better job on cost and value-selling)
  • Revenue size: Expected to be a larger node than N3 in dollar value terms (each successive advanced node larger)
  • Strong customer demand; almost all AI innovators working with TSMC
  • N2 and its derivatives will further extend TSMC's technology leadership and capture AI growth opportunities

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Arizona Fab 1: N4 process; engineering wafer production started April 2024; on track for volume production 1H 2025
  • Arizona Fab 2: Upgraded to 2nm technology (in addition to previously announced 3nm); recently completed topping off (last steel construction beam raised); volume production scheduled to begin in 2028
  • Arizona Fab 3: 2nm or more advanced technologies; production beginning by end of decade
  • Each Arizona fab has clean-room area approximately double the size of a typical logic fab
  • 3 fabs in Arizona to create greater economies of scale
  • Japan (Kumamoto Fab 1): Opening ceremony held February 2024; 12/16nm and 22/28nm process technologies; on track for volume production Q4 2024
  • Japan (Kumamoto Fab 2): 40nm, 12/16nm, and 6/7nm process technologies; supporting consumer, automotive, industrial and HPC-related applications; construction scheduled to begin 2H 2024; production target end 2027
  • Germany (Dresden/ESMC): Specialty technology fab focusing on automotive and industrial applications; JV with partners; construction scheduled to begin Q4 2024
  • Overseas cost management: (1) pricing strategically to reflect value of geographic flexibility; (2) working closely with governments for support; (3) leveraging manufacturing technology leadership and large-scale manufacturing base
  • Confident to deliver long-term GM of 53% and higher and sustainable ROE >25% even after factoring in higher cost of overseas fabs

AI Demand

  • AI server processor revenue 2024: Low-teens % of total TSMC revenue
  • AI revenue growth 2024: More than double YoY
  • AI revenue CAGR: ~50% over next 5 years
  • AI revenue target by 2028: Higher than 20% of total revenue
  • AI processor definition: GPUs, AI accelerators, and CPUs performing training and inference functions (narrowly defined; excludes networking, edge, or on-device AI)
  • AI processors expected to be strongest driver of HPC platform growth and largest contributor to overall incremental revenue growth in next several years
  • CoWoS capacity more than doubling from 2023; leveraging OSAT partners (including Amkor) to complement; still not enough to meet demand in 2024; expected to remain tight into 2025
  • Edge AI: Early stages; AI PCs and AI smartphones generating demand through larger die sizes; replacement cycle expected to accelerate; positive for TSMC
  • AI demand driving strong N3 and N5 utilization
  • Structural multi-year growth driver; insatiable demand for energy-efficient computing

Gross Margin Dynamics — 2024

  • Headwinds: N3 dilution (2–3 pts 1H, 3–4 pts 2H), N5-to-N3 conversion (1–2 pts 2H), electricity price increase (70–80 bps Q2, 60–70 bps 2H), earthquake (~50 bps Q2), indirect cost increases from higher electricity
  • Tailwinds: Cost improvement efforts, value-selling, product mix
  • Full year 2024: 53% and higher long-term target

Pricing

  • Value-selling approach continuing
  • TSMC's value in the supply chain increasing with AI and advanced technology leadership
  • Expecting customers to share higher costs (overseas fabs, inflation, electricity)
  • Discussions with customers already started on cost sharing
  • Overseas fab pricing to reflect value of geographic flexibility
  • Pricing strategy details confidential between TSMC and customers

Dividend

  • Policy: 70% of free cash flow as cash dividends (annual basis, not quarterly)
  • Transitioning from "sustainable" to "steadily increasing" dividend as harvesting phase begins
  • Cash dividend expected to increase in coming quarters

CapEx / Capital Intensity

  • 2024 CapEx: $28B–$32B (reiterated)
  • CapEx allocation: 70%–80% advanced process; 10%–20% specialty; ~10% advanced packaging/testing/mask
  • Capital intensity: Mid-30s% for 2024 and next several years (rate of increase leveling off)
  • Higher CapEx always correlated with higher growth opportunity in following years
  • CapEx and capacity planning based on long-term structural market demand profile
  • Will invest accordingly if future growth opportunities arise

End Market Demand / Inventory

  • Smartphone: Gradual (not steep) recovery; seasonality impacting Q1 and Q2
  • PC: Bottomed out; recovery slower
  • AI data center: Very, very strong demand
  • Traditional server: Slow, lukewarm demand; budget shifting from traditional server to AI server (favorable for TSMC)
  • IoT and consumer: Remain sluggish
  • Automotive: Inventory continuing to correct; previously expected to increase in 2024, now expected to decrease (changed from 3 months ago)
  • Mature nodes (12nm+): Demand remains sluggish; expected to gradually improve in 2H 2024
  • N3 2024 revenue: Expected to more than triple vs 2023
  • Hyperscale wallet share shifting from traditional server to AI server is favorable for TSMC (higher semiconductor content capture in AI server)

Mature Node Strategy

  • Working closely with strategic customers to develop specialty technology solutions
  • Creating differentiated long-lasting value to customers
  • Less exposed to possible overcapacity environment from competitors building mature node capacity
  • Utilization and profitability on mature nodes expected to be well protected
  • N7 demand expected to pick up in next couple of years (similar to 28nm experience); no plans to convert N7 to N5

Advanced Packaging / 3DIC

  • CoWoS demand extremely high; capacity more than doubling YoY but still insufficient
  • Leveraging OSAT partners (including Amkor) to complement TSMC capacity
  • 3DIC/SoIC: HPC customers first to adopt; products coming to market now
  • Amkor announced advanced packaging facility near Arizona fabs
  • Advanced packaging in Arizona driven by customer decisions on back-end service location

Q1 2024 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $18.9B (-3.8% QoQ in USD; -5.3% QoQ in NT), slightly above guidance in USD terms
  • Gross Margin: 53.1% (+10 bps QoQ, driven by product mix changes due to smartphone seasonality, partially offset by less favorable FX)
  • Operating Margin: 42% (+0.4 pp QoQ)
  • Operating Expenses: 11.1% of net revenue (below 12% guidance, due to tighter expense controls)
  • EPS: TWD 8.7
  • ROE: 25.4%

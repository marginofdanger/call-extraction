Quarterly Guidance (Q4 2023)

  • Revenue: $18.8B-$19.6B (+11.1% QoQ at midpoint)
  • Gross Margin: 51.5%-53.5% (midpoint 52.5%; -180 bps QoQ at midpoint, primarily due to continued margin dilution from steep ramp of N3)
  • Operating Margin: 39.5%-41.5%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 32.0 TWD

Full Year 2023 Guidance

  • Revenue: Decline ~10% in USD terms (maintained; actual slightly better than -10% due to strong N3 ramp)
  • CapEx: Approximately USD 32 billion (tightened from $32B-$36B range; tightened throughout the year due to near-term uncertainties)
  • CapEx allocation: ~70% advanced process technologies; ~20% specialty technologies; ~10% advanced packaging, testing, mask-making and others
  • Full year GM: 53% and higher long-term target (excluding FX impact)
  • Depreciation: Expected to increase by low 20s% YoY in 2023 (vs. January forecast of ~30% YoY increase)
  • N3 wafer revenue contribution: Mid-single-digit percentage of total wafer revenue in 2023; much higher percentage expected in 2024

2024 Outlook (Preliminary)

  • Expect healthy growth year for TSMC
  • Growth drivers: N3 ramp, AI demand expansion, smartphone/PC recovery
  • Semi ex-memory industry expected to grow in 2024
  • CapEx 2024: Too early to comment; will share in January; capital intensity expected to decline over next few years (dollar amount not expected to reduce)
  • Expect smartphone inventory digestion to continue in 1H 2024; customers remain cautious
  • Fabless semiconductor inventory forecast to exit Q4 2023 at healthier level
  • No dramatic change in seasonality expected

Long-Term Targets

  • Revenue CAGR: 15%-20% over 2021-2026 period in USD terms (maintained)
  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher achievable (excluding FX impact)
  • ROE: Sustainable greater than 25%
  • Semiconductor value: No specific quantification this call

Q3 GM Bridge (vs. Q2)

  • Tailwinds: Higher capacity utilization rate, more favorable FX rate
  • Headwinds: Margin dilution from initial ramp-up of N3 technology

Q3 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 3nm: 6% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • 5nm: 37% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 16% of Q3 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 59% of Q3 wafer revenue

Q3 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 42% of Q3 revenue (+6% QoQ)
  • Smartphone: 39% of Q3 revenue (+33% QoQ)
  • IoT: 9% of Q3 revenue (+24% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 5% of Q3 revenue (-24% QoQ)
  • DCE: 2% of Q3 revenue (-1% QoQ)

Q3 Balance Sheet

  • Cash & marketable securities: TWD 1.55 trillion (USD 48 billion)
  • Current liabilities: increased TWD 159B QoQ (TWD 95B from accounts payable increase, TWD 59B from accrued liabilities and others)
  • Long-term interest-bearing debt: increased TWD 30B (TWD 10B new issuance, TWD 20B from FX rate movement)
  • AR turnover days: 35 days (+3 days QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 96 days (-3 days QoQ)

Q3 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: TWD 295 billion (approximate)
  • CapEx: TWD 227 billion (USD 7.1 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 71 billion (Q4 2022 cash dividend)
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 35B to TWD 1.31 trillion at end of Q3

Q4 GM Bridge (vs. Q3)

  • Headwinds: Continued margin dilution from steep ramp of N3 technology

AI Demand

  • AI revenue 2023: ~6% of total TSMC revenue (maintained from Q2 call)
  • AI revenue CAGR: ~50% over next several years (maintained)
  • AI revenue in ~5 years: Expected to grow to mid-teens percentage of total revenue
  • Edge AI: Started seeing activity now; customers adding AI capability to end devices (smartphones, PCs)
  • Edge AI incremental to data center AI demand -- not cannibalistic
  • AI-related die sizes increasing; mid-single-digit die size increase so far from AI functionality in edge devices; expected to continue growing
  • AI demand continues to grow stronger; TSMC has capacity limitation to support demand, working hard to increase capacity
  • Custom chips (CPU, GPU, AI accelerator, ASIC) for AI: all require leading-edge technology, stable yield for larger die size, and strong foundry design ecosystem -- all TSMC strengths
  • TSMC able to address and capture major portion of semiconductor component market in AI
  • Impact of new U.S. export controls on AI to China: limited and manageable for TSMC in the short term; still evaluating long-term consequences
  • CoWoS demand: Significantly exceeds supply
  • CoWoS capacity 2024: More than doubling from 2023; still expect tight supply through 2024; limited by supplier capability/capacity
  • CoWoS capacity expansion trend will continue into 2025

Advanced Packaging

  • CoWoS: Demand far exceeds supply; more than doubling capacity by end of 2024
  • Still tight through 2024 even with expansion; limited by supplier capability
  • Critical for AI accelerator production
  • Revenue contribution from packaging growing meaningfully
  • Backend business growth: Expected to grow slightly faster than corporate average over the 2021-2026 period
  • SoIC: Customer products ready to be announced with SoIC adoption; revenue generation starting now and increasing in 2024; expected to be one of the faster-growing advanced packaging solutions over the next few years

Six Factors Determining TSMC Profitability

  • 1. Leadership technology development and ramp-up 2. Pricing 3. Cost reduction 4. Capacity utilization 5. Technology mix 6. Foreign exchange rate

N3 Technology

  • Ramp: Strong; N3 in volume production with good yield; strong ramp in 2H 2023 supported by both HPC and smartphone applications
  • N3 wafer revenue 2023: Mid-single-digit percentage of total wafer revenue
  • N3 wafer revenue 2024: Much higher percentage expected, supported by robust demand from multiple customers
  • N3E: Passed qualification, achieved performance and yield targets; volume production starting in Q4 2023
  • N3 family extensions: N3P, N3X continuing development
  • N3P vs. competitor 18A: Comparable PPA with earlier time to market, better technology maturity, and much better cost
  • N3 GM dilution: Biggest headwind to margin; historically leading nodes reach corporate average margin in ~8 quarters, but N3 will take longer due to: (1) higher corporate margin baseline, (2) increasing node complexity, (3) inflation pressure on costs
  • Mature node margins: Congregated around corporate average in a narrow band due to specialty technology focus (not commodity capacity)
  • N3 family: Large and long-lasting node (reiterated)

N2 Technology

  • Production: 2H 2025 (maintained)
  • Development: Progressing well and on track; nanosheet (GAA) transistor structure with excellent power efficiency
  • N2 positioning: Most advanced semiconductor technology in the industry in both density and energy efficiency when introduced in 2025
  • N2 vs. N3P and 18A: N2 (without backside power) is more advanced than both N3P and competitor's 18A
  • Customer engagement: Strong; similar to or higher than N3 at comparable stage, from both HPC and smartphone applications
  • N2 with backside power rail: Best suited for HPC applications; available to customers in 2H 2025 with production in 2026
  • Technology cadence: Remains constant to support customers' growth; may slow capacity expansion (not technology development) according to demand
  • N2 cost: Biggest challenge is cost; working to reduce cost so more customers can afford it

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Arizona Fab 1: N4 process; volume production target 1H 2025 (revised from earlier timeline); making good progress on infrastructure, utilities, and equipment installation; situation improving
  • Arizona Fab 2: Begun early preparation
  • Arizona hiring: Close to 1,100 local TSMC employees hired; many brought to Taiwan for extensive hands-on fab experience
  • Japan (JASM Fab 1): 12/16nm and 22/28nm specialty technologies; ~800 local TSMC employees hired (majority brought to Taiwan for hands-on experience); equipment move-in began in October 2023; volume production on track for late 2024
  • Germany (ESMC): 22/28nm and 12/16nm specialty technology focusing on automotive and industrial applications; JV structure with strong commitment from JV partners, European Commission, German federal/state/city governments; fab construction scheduled to begin 2H 2024; production target late 2027
  • China (Nanjing): Received extension from U.S. Bureau of Industry and Security; applying for validated end-user authorization; expect to receive permanent authorization in near future
  • Overseas cost factors: (1) Smaller fab scale, (2) Higher costs throughout supply chain, (3) Early-stage semiconductor ecosystem vs. mature Taiwan ecosystem
  • Overseas GM dilution: TSMC will absorb higher cost of overseas fabs and still deliver long-term gross margin of 53% and higher and sustainable ROE of greater than 25%
  • Strategy: Expand global manufacturing to increase customer trust, expand future growth potential, reach for more global talent; pricing to reflect value including geographic flexibility; government support essential

Gross Margin Dynamics

  • Q3 2023 actual: 54.3% (exceeded high end of guidance by 80 bps due to favorable FX)
  • Q4 2023 guidance: 51.5%-53.5% (midpoint 52.5%); headwinds from steep N3 ramp dilution
  • Full year 2023: Long-term gross margin of 53% and higher achievable (excluding FX)
  • Key factors: N3 dilution biggest headwind; partially offset by cost improvement and value-selling
  • Leading-node profitability timeline: Historically ~8 quarters to reach corporate average; N3 expected to take longer due to higher baseline, complexity, and inflation

Pricing

  • Strategic, value-based approach
  • Selling value increasing as technology complexity and supply chain value grows
  • Geographic flexibility value reflected in pricing
  • Not providing specific ASP trends

Dividend

  • Committed to sustainable and steadily increasing cash dividend
  • Q3 2023 dividend paid: TWD 71B (Q4 2022 dividend distributed in Q3)

CapEx Intensity & Investment Philosophy

  • 2023 CapEx: Approximately USD 32B (tightened from $32B-$36B range)
  • Capital invested in anticipation of future growth
  • Past few years: invested very heavily to capture growth in next few years
  • CapEx increase expected to level off in next few years (dollar amount not reducing, but capital intensity expected to decline)
  • Capital intensity target of mid-30s% is 3-5 years out, not immediate

Inventory / Cycle

  • Fabless semiconductor inventory continuously reduced in Q3 2023
  • Persistent weaker macroeconomic conditions and slow demand recovery in China; customers cautious in inventory control
  • Inventory digestion to continue in Q4 2023
  • Forecast fabless semiconductor inventory to exit Q4 2023 at healthier level
  • Early signs of demand stabilization in PC and smartphone end markets
  • Some urgent POs for more devices, hinting inventory at healthier levels than previously thought
  • Automotive: Entered inventory adjustment mode in 2H 2023 after 3 years of very strong demand; expected to increase again in 2024 (more EV, more functionality)
  • 2024 expected to show recovery across most end markets; healthy growth year for TSMC

7nm Utilization

  • 7nm utilization below original plan
  • Impacted by smartphone unit decline from ~1.4B units to ~1.1B units over past decade
  • Further impacted by one major customer delaying product introduction
  • Plan to backfill 7nm/6nm capacity with specialty demand (consumer, RF, connectivity, other applications)
  • Expect return to healthy utilization over next several years (similar to 28nm recovery in 2018-2019 time frame)

5nm Demand

  • Q3 2023 N5 revenue increase mainly from HPC (including AI-related demand) and smartphone (customer product seasonality)

Silicon Photonics (CPO)

  • Growing in importance due to large amounts of data needing collection, processing, and transfer in energy-efficient manner
  • TSMC working on silicon photonics for years; collaborating with multiple leading customers
  • Activity and demand gradually increasing
  • TSMC believes its silicon photonics will be the best technology when customers roll out innovations

Revenue Growth Breakdown (2021-2026 CAGR Context)

  • Smartphone: Expected to grow slightly slower than corporate average over 2021-2026 period
  • HPC: Strongest growth; major contributor to multiyear growth
  • Backend/packaging: Expected to grow slightly faster than corporate average over the 5-year period

Q3 2023 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $17.3B (+10.2% QoQ in USD; +13.7% QoQ in NT), in line with guidance in USD terms
  • Gross Margin: 54.3% (+20 bps QoQ; exceeded high end of guidance by 80 bps, driven by more favorable FX rate)
  • Operating Margin: 41.7% (-30 bps QoQ)
  • Operating Expenses: 12.6% of net revenue (vs. 12.1% in Q2, higher R&D for 3nm and 2nm development)
  • EPS: TWD 8.14
  • ROE: 25.8%

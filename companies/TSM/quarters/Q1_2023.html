Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2023)

  • Revenue: $15.2B-$16.0B (-6.7% QoQ at midpoint)
  • Gross Margin: 52%-54% (midpoint 53%; mainly due to lower capacity utilization rate and higher electricity costs in Taiwan)
  • Operating Margin: 39.5%-41.5%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 30.4 TWD
  • Cycle bottom: Q2 2023 — believe passing through the bottom of TSMC's business cycle
  • Utilization: Expected to bottom in Q2 and improve in second half

Full Year 2023 Guidance

  • Full year revenue: Low to mid-single-digit decline in USD terms
  • 1H 2023 revenue: Decline ~10% YoY in USD terms (worsened from prior "mid- to high single-digit decline")
  • 2H 2023: Stronger than 1H, supported by customers' new product launches; ~25% HoH revenue growth implied by guidance math
  • Semi ex-memory market: Decline mid-single-digit percent; gradual recovery in 2H 2023
  • Foundry industry: Decline high-single-digit percent
  • TSMC vs. industry: Expects to outperform both semiconductor ex-memory and foundry industries, supported by strong technology leadership and differentiation
  • Full year GM: 53% achievable despite headwinds (excluding FX impact which TSMC has no control over)
  • CapEx: $32B-$36B (maintained from January guidance; already tightened up for 2023)

Long-Term Targets

  • Revenue CAGR: 15%-20% over next several years in USD terms (reiterated; near-term cyclical challenges do not change long-term structural view)
  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher achievable (long-term, excluding FX impact)
  • Capital intensity: Expected to come down to mid-30s% over 5-year outlook
  • Semiconductor ex-memory industry: Structural growth driven by AI, 5G megatrends

Q1 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 5nm: 31% of Q1 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 20% of Q1 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 51% of Q1 wafer revenue

Q1 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 44% of Q1 revenue (-14% QoQ)
  • Smartphone: 34% of Q1 revenue (-27% QoQ)
  • IoT: 9% of Q1 revenue (-19% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 7% of Q1 revenue (+5% QoQ)
  • DCE: 2% of Q1 revenue (-5% QoQ)

Q1 Balance Sheet

  • Cash & marketable securities: TWD 1.59 trillion (USD 52 billion)
  • Current liabilities: decreased TWD 71 billion QoQ (mainly due to decrease of TWD 65 billion in accounts payable)
  • AR turnover days: 34 days (-2 days QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 96 days (+3 days QoQ)

Q1 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: TWD 385 billion
  • CapEx: TWD 302 billion (USD 9.94 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 71 billion (Q2 2022 cash dividend)
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 42 billion to TWD 1.39 trillion at end of Q1

Gross Margin Dynamics

  • Q1 2023 actual GM: 56.3% (-590 bps QoQ; beat high end of guidance by 80 bps due to more stringent cost control)
  • Q2 2023 guided midpoint: 53%, mainly due to lower capacity utilization rate and higher electricity costs
  • Full year 2023: Above 53% target despite headwinds
  • Headwinds: Lower capacity utilization due to semiconductor cyclicality, N3 ramp-up dilution, overseas fab expansion costs, inflationary costs including higher utility costs in Taiwan, unfavorable FX
  • Tailwinds: Internal cost improvement efforts, value-selling
  • Electricity cost impact: +15% price increase in 2H 2022; +17% increase effective April 1, 2023; ~60 bps impact on Q2 gross margin; ~50 bps dilution to full year gross margin

Inventory / Cycle

  • Fabless semiconductor inventory continued to increase in Q4 2022, exiting at a much higher level than expected
  • Recovery in end market demand from channels reopening lower than expectation
  • Inventory adjustment in 1H 2023 taking longer than prior expectation; may extend into Q3 2023 before rebalancing to a healthier level
  • Customers continuing further inventory adjustment in Q2 2023
  • PC and smartphone markets continue to be soft; automotive demand holding steady but showing signs of softening into 2H 2023
  • Incremental upside observed in AI-related demand, helping ongoing inventory digestion
  • Utilization expected to bottom in Q2 2023 and improve in second half

AI / Generative AI (First Significant Discussion)

  • Incremental increase in AI-related demand observed; helping ongoing inventory digestion
  • Trend is very positive for TSMC
  • ChatGPT / large language models reinforce already strong conviction that HPC and AI are a structural megatrend for TSMC
  • AI demand partly embedded in prior 15%-20% CAGR target (server AI accelerators were already in consideration), but large language models are a new application not yet quantified in CAGR
  • Too early to quantify dollar content per server or addressable TAM
  • Most AI currently focused on training; inference expected to grow in future
  • Both training and inference require high-performance, power-efficient semiconductors — TSMC advantage
  • Semiconductor content expected to increase from data center to device and edge devices
  • AI servers require significantly more advanced silicon content
  • TSMC well positioned to capture AI demand with leading-edge technology and advanced packaging
  • Recently received customer phone calls requesting big increase in back-end capacity, especially CoWoS

N3 Technology

  • First in the semiconductor industry to high-volume production with good yield (started Q4 2022)
  • Most advanced semiconductor technology in both PPA and transistor technology
  • N3 demand exceeds TSMC's ability to supply; fully utilized in 2023
  • Developed strategy to support some N3 tools with N5, cross-node flexibility to fulfill N3 commitments
  • N3 demand higher than thought a year ago
  • Sizable N3 revenue contribution expected to start in Q3 2023
  • N3 to contribute mid-single-digit percentage of total wafer revenue in full year 2023
  • N3E: Passed qualification, achieved performance and yield targets; volume production scheduled for 2H 2023
  • N3E offers enhanced performance, power and yield; complete platform support for both HPC and smartphone
  • Number of tape-outs at N3/N3E more than 2x that of N5 in the same comparable period (first and second year)
  • Customer demand strong for both HPC and smartphone applications
  • Confident N3 family will be another large and long-lasting node for TSMC
  • N3 market share expected to be very similar to N5 — very high percentage

N2 Technology

  • Production: Volume production in 2025 (on track); ramp starts in 2025 with larger volume in 2026
  • Transistor: Nanosheet transistor structure (gate-all-around / nanosheet)
  • Provides best performance, cost and technology maturity
  • Nanosheet technology has demonstrated excellent power efficiency
  • N2 delivers full node performance and power benefits to address increasing need for energy-efficient computing
  • Most advanced semiconductor technology in the industry in both density and energy efficiency when introduced
  • Development progressing well and on track
  • High level of customer interest and engagement from both HPC and smartphone applications
  • Will further extend TSMC's technology leadership well into the future

N7/N6 Utilization

  • Still below historical highs; loading still mainly in HPC and smartphone
  • Expected to recover but slowly
  • Future loading from specialty applications (RF, connectivity, WiFi) expected to build up demand
  • Long-term N7 loading expected to become healthier

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Expanding global footprint to increase customer trust, expand future growth potential, and reach for more global talents
  • Arizona Fab 1: N4 process technology; production scheduled for late 2024 (despite some challenges in obtaining permits)
  • Japan (JASM): Specialty technology fab; volume production scheduled for late 2024; total CapEx ~$8B; ~50% government subsidy expected; technologies include 12nm/16nm/22nm/28nm
  • Europe: Engaging with customers and partners to evaluate possibility of building a specialty fab focusing on automotive-specific technologies; based on customer demand and level of government support
  • China (Nanjing): Expanding 28-nanometer capacity as planned to support customers in China; continuing to follow all rules and regulations fully
  • Kaohsiung: Fab construction continues; adjusted previous 28-nanometer expansion plan to now focus on capacity expansion for more advanced nodes (rationale: Japan fab, Nanjing expansion, and potential Europe fab together cover 28nm demand; building 28nm in Kaohsiung would not be financially feasible; Kaohsiung's proximity to Tainan provides flexibility for advanced node operations)
  • Construction cost: Overseas fab construction costs may be as high as 5x domestic Taiwan costs
  • Cost mitigation strategy: Selling value of geographic flexibility to customers; leveraging large base and volume to lower costs; securing necessary government support; goal is to minimize cost gap and make appropriate return
  • Government subsidies accounting: Accounted for as offset to depreciation

Talent / Hiring

  • U.S. (Arizona): Hired more than 900 U.S. employees to date
  • Japan: Hired more than 370 employees to date
  • Taiwan: Plan to hire more than 6,000 employees in 2023
  • Recruiting from top local colleges and universities in U.S. and Japan; progress well on track
  • Extensive training programs for new overseas employees; many brought to Taiwan for hands-on fab experience
  • Priority is to identify, attract, and hire talent whose core values align with TSMC's
  • Established Overseas Operations Office (OOO) to ensure headquarters support to overseas fabs is sufficient, performance aligned with Taiwan fabs, and to help overseas fabs succeed and become more profitable

Pricing

  • Strategic and long-term, not opportunistic
  • Value-selling approach continues — TSMC's value in the supply chain is increasing
  • Value includes geographic flexibility (overseas manufacturing footprint)
  • Not providing specific ASP trends; ASPs reflect technology value
  • Working internally and with supply partners to lower costs despite inflationary pressures

Dividend

  • Committed to sustainable and steadily increasing cash dividend on both annual and quarterly basis
  • 70% of free cash flow is a guideline, not a rigid rule
  • During periods of high capital intensity: focus more on "sustainable"
  • When harvesting capital investment: focus shifts to "steadily increasing"
  • If free cash flow is lower (higher CapEx or lower profit), payout ratio could exceed 70% to maintain sustainable dividend
  • If free cash flow is particularly high, ratio can be 70% or lower, looking forward to ensure sustainability

CapEx Framework

  • Every year, CapEx is spent in anticipation of growth in future years
  • $32B-$36B range for 2023 already reflects tightening; range still valid and appropriate under current economic environment
  • Short-term cyclicality does not change investment plans if structural long-term demand and future opportunities remain
  • Flexibility maintained: can increase quickly when demand picks up, can tighten when appropriate
  • Long-term capacity expansion planning continues with adjustments in between
  • Too early to comment on 2024 CapEx
  • Equipment under installation / construction in progress: ~$40B at end of 2022 (primarily from N3 ramp and N5 capacity increases); expected to gradually come down over next few years

Advanced Packaging

  • Back-end services growth expected to be slightly higher than corporate average over next ~5 years
  • 2023 revenue expected to be lower YoY due to customer demand; 2023 estimated at 6%-7% of total revenue (vs. ~7% in 2022)
  • CoWoS demand increasing driven by AI/HPC applications; recently received customer requests for big increase in CoWoS capacity — still evaluating
  • Advanced packaging becoming increasingly important for AI chips

Competitive Landscape

  • Does not comment on competitors' status
  • N3: first in industry to HVM; most advanced in PPA and transistor technology
  • N2: will be most advanced when introduced into mass production
  • Confident in maintaining very high market share at leading-edge nodes
  • N3 market share expected to be very similar to N5 — very high percentage
  • Technology leadership extends well into the future

CHIPS Act

  • Currently in application process; unable to comment on specific details
  • In close and constant communication with U.S. government
  • All decisions will be based on best interest of TSMC
  • Cannot quantify potential benefits (grants and investment tax credits) or disclose preferences between grants vs. tax credits at this time

cuLitho (Computational Lithography)

  • Initiative by NVIDIA; TSMC working with NVIDIA and ASML
  • Software and hardware together help speed up computational lithography by moving expensive operations to GPU
  • Enables broader deployment of lithography solutions like inverse lithography technology and deeper learning
  • Expected to provide TSMC advantages in cost improvement and competitiveness

End Market Dynamics (by segment)

  • PC / Smartphone: Continue to be soft; still in correction but signs of stabilization
  • Automotive: Demand holding steady for TSMC; showing signs of softening into 2H 2023
  • AI / Data Center: Incremental upside observed; positive trend; too early to quantify
  • HPC: Demand supported by AI tailwind emerging; second half stronger with new product launches
  • Specialty (RF, Connectivity, WiFi): Expected to build up N7 loading in the future

Q1 2023 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $6.7B (towards low end of guidance range); -18.7% QoQ in NT; -16.1% QoQ in USD
  • Gross Margin: 56.3% (-590 bps QoQ; exceeded high end of guidance by 80 bps, driven by more stringent cost control efforts)
  • Operating Margin: 45.5% (-6.5 pp QoQ)
  • Operating Expenses: 10.8% of net revenue (lower than 12% implied in Q1 guidance, due to stringent expense control and lower employee profit sharing)
  • EPS: TWD 7.98
  • ROE: 27.5%

Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2025)

  • Revenue: $25.0B–$25.8B (-5.5% QoQ at midpoint; +34.7% YoY at midpoint)
  • Gross Margin: 57%–59% (midpoint 58%; -100 bps QoQ at midpoint, primarily due to N2 and CoWoS expansion ramp costs and start of overseas fab dilution)
  • Operating Margin: 46.5%–48.5%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 32.8 TWD
  • Seasonality: Smartphone seasonality impact, partially offset by continued AI-related demand growth

Full Year 2025 Guidance

  • Revenue growth: Close to mid-20s% in USD terms (another strong growth year)
  • CapEx: $38B–$42B (up from $29.8B in 2024)
  • CapEx allocation: ~70% for advanced process technologies; 10%–20% for specialty technologies; 10%–20% for advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others
  • Depreciation: Expected to increase by high single-digit percentage YoY in 2025 (newly incurred depreciation partially offset by other nodes rolling off depreciation)
  • Effective tax rate: 16%–17%
  • Foundry 2.0 industry: Expected to grow ~10% YoY in 2025
  • Fabless semiconductor inventory: Expected to have returned to healthier level exiting 2024
  • Advanced packaging revenue: Expected to account for over 10% of total revenue in 2025

Long-Term Targets

  • Revenue CAGR (2024–2029): Approaching 20% CAGR in USD terms, fueled by all 4 growth platforms (smartphone, HPC, IoT, automotive)
  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher achievable (maintained; delivering on the "and higher" part since 2022 raise)
  • Semiconductor market: Approaching $1 trillion

Industry Outlook

  • 2024 recap: Mixed year -- AI demand strong; other applications saw only very mild recovery due to macro/consumer sentiment
  • Foundry 2.0 industry 2024: +6% YoY
  • Foundry 2.0 industry 2025 forecast: +10% YoY (supported by robust AI demand and mild recovery in other end markets)
  • Foundry 2.0: Can be used as proxy for semiconductor ex-memory
  • PC and smartphone 2025: Still mild growth; everything increasingly AI-related
  • HBM: Will grow very fast in 2025

Q4 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 3nm: 26% of Q4 wafer revenue
  • 5nm: 34% of Q4 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 14% of Q4 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 74% of Q4 wafer revenue

Q4 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 53% of Q4 revenue (+19% QoQ)
  • Smartphone: 35% of Q4 revenue (+17% QoQ)
  • IoT: 5% of Q4 revenue (-15% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 4% of Q4 revenue (+6% QoQ)
  • DCE: 1% of Q4 revenue (-6% QoQ)

Q4 Balance Sheet

  • Current liabilities: increased TWD 184B QoQ (TWD 71B from accounts payable increase, TWD 99B from accrued liabilities and others)
  • AR turnover days: 27 days (-1 day QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 80 days (-7 days QoQ, primarily due to shipment of N3 and N5 wafers)

Q4 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: ~TWD 620 billion
  • CapEx: TWD 362 billion (USD 11.2 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 104 billion (Q1 2024 cash dividend)
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 241B to TWD 2.1 trillion at end of Q4

FY2024 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 3nm: 18% of FY2024 wafer revenue
  • 5nm: 34% of FY2024 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 17% of FY2024 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 69% of FY2024 wafer revenue (up from 58% in 2023)

FY2024 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 51% of 2024 revenue (+58% YoY)
  • Smartphone: 35% of 2024 revenue (+23% YoY)
  • IoT: 6% of 2024 revenue (+2% YoY)
  • Automotive: 5% of 2024 revenue (+4% YoY)
  • DCE: not separately disclosed for full year (+2% YoY)

Advanced Packaging

  • Advanced packaging revenue: over 8% of total revenue in 2024
  • Advanced packaging GM: improved from prior years but still below corporate average

Gross Margin Dynamics -- 2025 Puts & Takes

  • 6 profitability factors: Leadership technology development and ramp-up, pricing, cost reduction, technology mix, capacity utilization, foreign exchange rate
  • Tailwinds: - N3 dilution impact expected to gradually reduce - Overall utilization rate expected to moderately increase in 2025 - Value-selling approach (increasing value)
  • Headwinds: - Overseas fab dilution: 2–3 percentage points for full year 2025 (less than 100 bps in Q1 2025, growing more pronounced throughout the year as Kumamoto and Arizona ramp up) - Inflationary costs including higher electricity prices in Taiwan: at least 1% impact in 2025 - N2 ramp-up costs + N5 to N3 capacity conversion costs: ~1% combined impact on gross margin - Foreign exchange rate uncertainty
  • Long-term GM target: 53% and higher remains achievable (excluding FX impact, considering global manufacturing footprint expansion)

AI Demand

  • AI accelerator definition: AI GPU, AI ASICs and HBM controller for AI training and inference in data center
  • AI accelerator revenue 2024 actual: Close to mid-teens % of total revenue; more than tripled YoY
  • AI accelerator revenue 2025: Expected to double again
  • AI revenue CAGR: Approaching mid-40% CAGR for 5-year period starting off the higher base of 2024
  • AI accelerators: Expected to be strongest driver of HPC platform growth and largest contributor to overall incremental revenue growth in next several years
  • CoWoS capacity: Very tight; cannot even meet all customers' demand; continuing to increase capacity; no order cuts (rumors denied)
  • SoIC adoption: Today focused on AI applications; PC and other areas coming but not yet
  • Non-AI CoWoS adoption: CPU and server chips expected to adopt CoWoS (hint from C.C. Wei); coming but not yet high volume

N2 Technology

  • Production: On track for volume production in 2H 2025, with ramp profile similar to N3
  • Performance vs N3E: 10–15% speed improvement at same power; or 20–30% power improvement at same speed; more than 15% chip density increase
  • Customer engagement: Almost all innovators working with TSMC
  • Tape-outs: Number of new tape-outs for N2 in first 2 years expected to be higher than both N3 and N5 in their first 2 years
  • Applications: Fueled by both smartphone and HPC applications

N2P (Enhanced N2)

  • Production: 2H 2026
  • Features: Further performance and power benefits on top of N2
  • Applications: Supports both smartphone and HPC

A16 (1.6nm)

  • Production: 2H 2026
  • Backside power delivery: Super Power Rail (SPR) -- innovative, best-in-class backside power delivery solution; first in the industry to incorporate novel backside metal scheme preserving gate density and device width flexibility
  • Performance vs N2P: 8–10% speed improvement at same power; or 15–20% power improvement at same speed; additional 7–10% chip density gain
  • Applications: Best suited for specific HPC products with complex signal routing and dense power delivery networks
  • Offered as: Separate offering from N2 family

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Arizona Fab 1: N4 process; already entered high-volume production in Q4 2024 (pulled ahead from original schedule); yield comparable to Taiwan fabs; expect smooth ramp-up
  • Arizona Fab 2: Building almost finished; facility installation underway; tool move expected in 2025; will utilize more advanced technologies (N3, N2, A16 based on customer need)
  • Arizona Fab 3: Plans to start very soon; announcement to come
  • Arizona pricing: Slightly higher price than Taiwan (geographic flexibility value; "Made in U.S.A." premium); customers agreed and happy to work with TSMC
  • Japan Fab 1 (Kumamoto): Specialty technology fab; started volume production at end of 2024; very good yield
  • Japan Fab 2 (Kumamoto): Specialty fab; construction scheduled to begin in 2025
  • Germany (ESMC - Dresden): Specialty technology fab focusing on automotive and industrial applications; progressing smoothly with support from European Commission and German federal/state/city government
  • Taiwan: Continuing to expand 3nm capacity in Tainan Science Park; preparing multiple phases of 2nm fabs in both Hsinchu and Kaohsiung Science Parks; expanding advanced packaging facilities across several locations in Taiwan
  • Overseas GM dilution: 2–3 percentage points per year for next 5 years; both variable and fixed costs are higher overseas
  • Overseas cost mitigation strategy: Copy Taiwan improvements to overseas fabs; continuously improve cost structure in both Taiwan and overseas; developing new methodologies to benefit Arizona fab; goal to minimize cost structure gap

Pricing

  • Strategic value-selling approach: Working hard to increase value
  • Overseas premium: Slightly higher price for U.S. wafers due to geographic flexibility value
  • Long-term GM target: Not revising above 53% yet due to overseas fab expansion (2–3 pts dilution/year for 5 years) and macro uncertainty; committed to earning healthy return

Dividend

  • 2024 cash dividends paid: TWD 363 billion (+24.5% YoY)
  • Policy: Committed to sustainable and steadily increasing cash dividend per share on both annual and quarterly basis

Tax Rate

  • 2024 effective tax rate: 16.7%
  • 2025 expected effective tax rate: 16%–17%

Depreciation

  • 2025 depreciation growth: High single-digit % YoY increase (newly incurred depreciation partially offset by other nodes rolling off)
  • CapEx and capacity planning based on long-term market demand profile; higher CapEx correlated with higher growth opportunities in following years

Edge AI / On-Device AI

  • Silicon content increase: Estimated 5–10% more silicon used per device (not recurring annually at that rate)
  • Replacement cycle: Expected to shorten as consumers want devices with AI functionality
  • Technology migration: Customers moving to next node for AI functionality; additional growth driver beyond die size increase
  • 2025 outlook: Customers starting to put more neural processing inside devices; ramp expected

U.S. Strategy / Government Relations

  • Government communication: Frank and open communication with both current and incoming (Trump) administration
  • Technology node approach: Taiwan always first for new node ramp (proximity to R&D required); not due to n-1 policy restriction but practical manufacturing reasons
  • IDM customers: Valued partners; strategy not based on IDM competitors' status; important to TSMC's business
  • IDM outsourcing: Part of long-term growth outlook; increasing trend observed

Silicon Photonics / CPO

  • Status: Working on silicon photonics with good initial results; customers happy
  • Volume timeline: Big volume not expected in 2025; likely 1–1.5 years to see volume production contribution

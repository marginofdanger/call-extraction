Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2022)

  • Revenue: $17.6B-$18.2B (+1.9% QoQ at midpoint)
  • Gross Margin: 56%-58% (midpoint 57%; +140 bps QoQ)
  • Operating Margin: 45%-47%
  • FX assumption: 1 USD = 28.8 TWD
  • Q2 GM drivers: More favorable FX assumption (28.8 vs 27.95 actual in Q1) contributing >100 bps tailwind, plus continued cost improvement and value-selling efforts
  • Demand outlook: Supported by HPC and automotive-related demand, partially offset by smartphone seasonality

Full Year 2022 Guidance

  • Revenue growth: Likely at or exceed high end of mid- to high 20s% in USD terms (upgraded from prior "mid- to high 20s%")
  • CapEx: $40B-$44B (maintained)
  • CapEx allocation: 70-80% advanced, ~20% specialty (unchanged from beginning of year)
  • Platform growth (full year): HPC and automotive faster than corporate average; IoT similar; smartphone approaching corporate average
  • HPC: Strongest growing platform in 2022 and the largest contributor to growth, fueled by structural megatrend driving increasing need for greater computation power and energy-efficient computing
  • Foundry industry growth: ~20% YoY for 2022
  • Capacity: Remains tight throughout 2022; not enough to support all customer demand
  • H2 2022 advanced tech mix: >50% of wafer revenue from 5nm + 7nm combined (higher than Q1's 50%)

Long-Term Targets

  • Gross Margin: 53% and higher is achievable (reiterated, citing 6 profitability factors; excluding FX impact which is uncontrollable)
  • ROE: Greater than 25% through the cycle
  • Semiconductor ex-memory industry CAGR: High single-digit % over next 5 years (vs ~4% CAGR past 10 years)

Capacity / Cycle Outlook

  • Capacity remains tight, not enough to support all customer demand
  • CapEx based on long-term demand profile, underpinned by megatrends; not based on speculation
  • Will continue investment plans even if short-term downturn occurs
  • Some softness in smartphone, PC, tablet; HPC, automotive, MCU, power management remain strong
  • Supply chain maintaining higher-than-historical inventory for longer (COVID supply chain disruptions, geopolitical tensions)
  • Structural megatrend of 5G and HPC driving increasing silicon content in smartphones, PCs, servers, networking, and automotive
  • Capacity planning not dependent on any single customer or product
  • Advanced technology: leading position; mature nodes: built to support differentiated specialty technologies with high yield (effective capacity)

Q1 Technology Node Breakdown

  • 5nm: 20% of Q1 wafer revenue
  • 7nm: 30% of Q1 wafer revenue
  • Advanced tech (7nm and below): 50% of Q1 wafer revenue

Q1 Platform Revenue Breakdown

  • HPC: 41% of Q1 revenue (+26% QoQ)
  • Smartphone: 40% of Q1 revenue (+1% QoQ)
  • IoT: 8% of Q1 revenue (+5% QoQ)
  • Automotive: 5% of Q1 revenue (+26% QoQ)
  • DCE: 3% of Q1 revenue (+8% QoQ)
  • Note: All 6 platforms increased in Q1

Q1 Balance Sheet

  • Cash & marketable securities: TWD 1.3 trillion
  • Current liabilities: increased TWD 83B QoQ (TWD 63B from accrued liabilities and others, TWD 30B from short-term loans, partially offset by TWD 21B decrease in accounts payable)
  • Long-term interest-bearing debt: increased TWD 19B QoQ (raised TWD 20B in corporate bonds during the quarter)
  • AR turnover days: 38 days (-2 days QoQ)
  • Inventory days: 88 days (flat QoQ)

Q1 Cash Flow

  • Cash from operations: TWD 372 billion
  • CapEx: TWD 262 billion (USD 9.38 billion)
  • Cash dividend distributed: TWD 71 billion (Q2 2021 cash dividend)
  • Bonds payable: increased TWD 20B (bond issuances)
  • Cash balance: increased TWD 87B to TWD 1.2 trillion at end of Q1

Profitability Drivers (Six Factors)

  • Technology leadership, pricing, cost reduction, capacity utilization, technology mix, FX
  • Q1 GM bridge: Guidance FX assumption 27.6 TWD/USD vs actual 27.95 TWD/USD = ~50 bps positive surprise; GM +290 bps QoQ driven by cost improvement, value-selling, and favorable FX
  • Q2 GM bridge: Guided +140 bps QoQ to 57% midpoint; FX assumption of 28.8 TWD/USD brings >100 bps tailwind plus continued cost improvement and value-selling
  • Challenges: Rising inflationary costs (including materials), increasing process complexity of leading nodes, new investments in mature nodes, overseas fab expansions

Pricing

  • Strategic, not opportunistic or short-term
  • Will NOT lower prices in a downturn
  • Value-based approach reflecting technology value; customers understand TSMC's effort to support their growth
  • Volume, price, and product mix all contribute to growth; no specific breakdown provided

N3 Technology

  • Volume production: H2 2022, on track with good yield
  • N3E: Volume production ~1 year after N3; progress ahead of schedule, considering pull-in
  • Customer engagement: High level; more tape-outs in first year vs N5 and N7
  • Ramp applications: Both HPC and smartphone
  • Gross margin: Achieving corporate average in 7-8 quarters is more challenging due to increasing process complexity and higher corporate profitability baseline (53%+ target); too early to specify exact timeline as volume production has not started
  • Revenue contribution: Too early to discuss; N3 volume production begins H2 2022, with revenue contribution starting 2023; historical pattern of ~10% first-year contribution may be less meaningful as structural demand drives growth across all nodes
  • Outlook: N3 family will be another large and long-lasting node, like N5 and N7 families
  • Capacity: Planning enough capacity for N3 and N3E to support strong customer demand

N2 Technology

  • Development: On track, including new transistor structure (gate-all-around); progressing to expectations
  • Risk production: End of 2024
  • Volume production: H2 2025 or end of 2025
  • Cadence: ~2-year cadence from N3 maintained
  • Lithography: Evaluating high NA EUV; largest user of EUV tools in the industry; will adopt high NA when necessary, ready, and cost effective; specific node adoption timing TBD
  • Positioning: Confident N2 will deliver best technology maturity, performance, and cost for customers and continue TSMC's technology leadership

Advanced Packaging / 3D IC

  • 2021 revenue: $4.1 billion
  • 2022 growth: Similar to corporate average
  • 5-year CAGR: Slightly higher than corporate average
  • SoIC: Serving HPC high-end applications (high bandwidth, very low power, very high performance); small volume production started 2022; expected to continue to grow

Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint

  • Arizona: 5nm fab under construction
  • Japan (JASM): 28nm and 16nm FinFET
  • China (Nanjing): 16nm FinFET and 28nm capacity expansion
  • Taiwan: Also building more 28nm capacity
  • Overseas capacity share: Not ready to share specific percentage target yet; plans will increase over next several years
  • Management focus: Best decisions for customers and in the best interest of TSMC; in close and constant communication with all customers; customer priorities are securing capacity and working with TSMC on technology development

Inventory Assessment

  • Supply chain expected to continue maintaining higher inventory levels for a longer period of time
  • Prolonged by recent COVID-related supply chain disruptions and geopolitical tension uncertainties
  • Some end market segments (smartphone, PC, tablet) show softness; industrial/automotive segments remain strong for inventory
  • Signing contracts to guarantee loading not a common practice; TSMC focuses on technology leadership, manufacturing excellence, and earning customer trust

Tool Delivery

  • Supplier supply chain challenges creating extended delivery lead times (both advanced and mature nodes)
  • Caused by continued COVID-19 impact: labor, component, and chip constraints in tool supplier supply chains
  • No impact to 2022 capacity plan
  • Working closely with suppliers on 2023 and beyond
  • Sent teams on-site to support suppliers; prioritizing wafer capacity for critical chips needed by tool suppliers
  • Increased regular high-level communications to trace progress
  • All leading-edge and mature node capacity expansion impacted

Materials Supply

  • Multi-source supply strategy; diversified global supplier base; improving local supply chain
  • Neon, specialty chemicals and gases: multiple suppliers in different regions with inventory stock on hand
  • No expected impact on operations
  • Rising materials inflationary cost acknowledged as a margin challenge
  • Working closely with suppliers to further strengthen resilience and sustainability of supply chain

Dividend

  • TWD 71 billion distributed in Q1 (Q2 2021 dividend)

IDM Customer / Competition

  • Well-established process and design enablement system to protect TSMC's IP and customers' IP; no anticipated issues
  • If IDM customers take business back in-house, already factored into capacity planning
  • Capacity planning based on long-term market demand, not dependent on any single customer or product
  • TSMC has never been short on competition in 35-year history

Q1 2022 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: TWD 491.1B (USD 17.6B); above high end of guidance, driven by better-than-expected smartphone and automotive demand plus customer need for supply security amid COVID uncertainties
  • Revenue growth: +12.1% QoQ in NT terms; +11.6% QoQ in USD terms
  • Gross Margin: 55.6% (+290 bps QoQ); driven by cost improvement, value-selling efforts, and favorable FX
  • Operating Margin: 45.6% (+3.9 pp QoQ); primarily due to lower vaccine donation expense vs Q4 2021
  • EPS: TWD 7.82
  • ROE: 36.2%
  • FX actual: 1 USD = 27.95 TWD (vs. guidance assumption of 27.6 TWD; ~50 bps positive surprise to gross margin)
  • Demand drivers: Strong HPC and automotive-related demand

21 Key Takeaways — 16-Quarter Evolution

1
AI transformed TSMC’s trajectory. From zero mentions in 2022 to the dominant growth driver by 2025, AI accelerator revenue went from nothing to high teens% of total revenue, with the CAGR raised from ~50% → mid-40s% → mid- to high 50s%.
2
Revenue nearly doubled in 4 years. Quarterly guidance midpoints grew from ~$17.9B to ~$35.2B (Q2 ’22 → Q1 ’26), driven by AI, advanced technology leadership, and value-selling.
3
The 2023 downcycle was sharp but short. Revenue guidance troughed at ~$15.6B (Q2 ’23) but recovered to $26.9B midpoint by Q4 ’24 — a 72% recovery in just 6 quarters.
4
N3 dilution was more painful than expected. Initially guided at 2–3 pts, it expanded to 3–4 pts. By Jan ’26, N3 was expected to cross corporate average margin — a full recovery after ~3 years.
5
Overseas expansion entered a new phase. From a single Arizona fab in 2022 to 4 Arizona fabs (+ advanced packaging), 2 Japan fabs, and 1 Germany fab — building an independent giga-fab cluster in Arizona with a total U.S. investment of $165 billion.
6
Arizona Fab 1 achieved Taiwan yield parity by Q4 2024. Fab 2 was upgraded from 3nm to 2nm and brought forward from 2028 to H2 2027 due to strong demand.
7
The 53% GM target was finally raised to 56%. After maintaining 53%+ for 15 consecutive quarters despite actual GMs of 57–62%, management raised the LT GM target to 56% and higher in Jan ’26, with an ROE target of high 20s% through the cycle.
8
CapEx surged to record levels: From $36B (2022) → trough ~$30B (2024) → $40.9B actual (2025) → massive step-up to $52–56B (2026), driven by N2, A16, Arizona expansion, and advanced packaging.
9
Long-term CAGR raised twice: From “15–20%” → “close to 20%” (Jan ’25) → “approach 25%” (Jan ’26) — the highest growth outlook in TSMC’s history, reflecting AI-driven structural acceleration.
10
CoWoS became the critical bottleneck. Advanced packaging went from a minor business (~7% of revenue) to the key constraint in AI chip production, with revenue climbing to ~10%+ of total by 2026 and capacity doubled multiple times.
11
TSMC’s pricing power increased. The shift from “strategic pricing” to “value-selling” and “earning the right return” reflected TSMC’s growing irreplaceability. Blended wafer ASPs rose ~20% in 2025 for the second consecutive year, enabling the 56% LT GM target.
12
Technology roadmap executed flawlessly. N2 maintained a 2H 2025 target for 16 consecutive quarters with zero delays, entering HVM at both Hsinchu and Kaohsiung in Q4 2025. N2P and A16 are advancing to H2 2026 volume production on schedule.

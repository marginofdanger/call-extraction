# TSMC Q2 2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 14, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2022

---

## Q2 2022 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** TWD 534B (USD 18.2B); +8.8% QoQ in NT, +3.4% QoQ in USD
- **Gross Margin:** 59.1% (+350 bps QoQ; slightly ahead of guidance, driven by favorable FX, cost improvement and value selling)
- **Operating Margin:** 49.1% (+3.5 pp QoQ, in line with GM increase)
- **EPS:** TWD 9.14
- **ROE:** 39.4%
- **FX actual:** 1 USD = 29.42 TWD (vs. Q2 guidance assumption of 28.8 TWD; created ~90 bps upside to GM vs. original guidance)

### Q2 Technology Node Breakdown
- **5nm:** 21% of wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 30% of wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 51% of wafer revenue

### Q2 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 43% of Q2 revenue (+13% QoQ)
- **Smartphone:** 38% of Q2 revenue (+3% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 8% of Q2 revenue (+14% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 5% of Q2 revenue (+14% QoQ)
- **DCE:** 3% of Q2 revenue (+5% QoQ)
- All 6 platforms increased in Q2

### Q2 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 1.4 trillion
- **Current liabilities:** increased TWD 22B QoQ (TWD 47B increase in accounts payable, partially offset by TWD 29B decrease in short-term loans)
- **Long-term interest-bearing debt:** increased TWD 124B QoQ (raised TWD 109B in corporate bonds during the quarter)
- **AR turnover days:** 37 days (-1 day QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 95 days (+7 days QoQ; prebuild of N5 wafers and increased raw materials inventory)

### Q2 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 339B
- **CapEx:** TWD 218B (USD 7.34B)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 71B (Q3 2021 cash dividend)
- **Bonds payable:** increased TWD 109B (bond issuances during the quarter)
- **Cash balance:** increased TWD 102B to TWD 1.3 trillion at end of Q2

---

## Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2022)
- **Revenue:** $19.8B-$20.6B (USD); +11.2% QoQ at midpoint
- **Gross Margin:** 57.5%-59.5% (midpoint 58.5%; -60 bps QoQ sequential decline)
- **Operating Margin:** 47%-49%
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 29.7 TWD

### Q3 Gross Margin Bridge
- Q3 GM guided down ~60 bps sequentially to 58.5% at midpoint
- Slightly more favorable FX rate assumption more than offset by higher inflationary costs (higher raw material and electricity costs)

### Q2 Gross Margin Bridge (Sequential)
- Q2 GM increased 350 bps QoQ to 59.1%, driven by more favorable FX, cost improvement and value selling
- Q2 guidance was based on FX assumption of 28.8 TWD/USD; actual was 29.42 TWD/USD, creating ~90 bps favorable surprise vs. original guidance

---

## Full Year 2022 Guidance
- **Revenue growth:** Mid-30s% YoY in USD terms
- **Capacity:** Expect to remain tight throughout 2022
- **CapEx:** $40B-$44B; "closer to the lower end" of the range (revised down due to tool delivery pushouts into 2023)
- **CapEx allocation:** 70-80% advanced process technologies; ~10% packaging/mask; 10-20% specialty
- **Tool delivery delays:** pushing some CapEx into 2023 (affecting both advanced and mature nodes)
- **No impact to 2022 capacity plan** from tool delivery issues
- **Implies Q4 sequential movement:** management noted mid-30s growth is in USD terms; NT dollar growth is higher than USD growth due to FX
- **Tax rate:** 10%-11% for 2022
- **Depreciation:** Mid-single-digit growth YoY for 2022 (lower than guided at beginning of year due to tool delivery schedule changes); 2023 depreciation expected to be much higher than 2022

---

## Long-Term Revenue CAGR
- **15-20% CAGR** over the next several years in USD terms (reiterated)
- Supported by silicon content increase (mid- to high single-digit % growth across end devices), flattish to low single-digit device unit growth, and value selling

## Long-Term Gross Margin Target
- **53% and higher is achievable** (excluding FX impact)
- Six factors: technology leadership, pricing, cost reduction, capacity utilization, technology mix, FX
- 53% target already includes the dilution effects from N3
- Challenges ahead: rising inflationary costs (raw materials, utilities, tools), increasing process complexity of leading nodes, new investments in mature nodes, overseas fab expansions

### Profitability Outlook Puts & Takes
- **Headwinds:** Rising raw material/utilities/tool costs, increasing process complexity at leading nodes, mature node investments, overseas fab expansion costs
- **Tailwinds:** Value-based pricing, cost improvement, technology leadership, high utilization

---

## N3 Technology
- **Volume production:** 2H 2022 (on track, good yield)
- **Revenue contribution:** Starting 1H 2023, with a smooth ramp in 2023
- **N3E:** Volume production ~1 year after N3 (i.e., 2H 2023); enhanced performance, power and yield; complete platform support for both smartphone and HPC
- **Customer engagement:** Both HPC and smartphone; high level of customer engagement at N3E
- **Gross margin dilution:** 2-3 pp impact in first year (2023), diluting from 2023 forecasted gross margin (not from 53% corporate target, and not from current ~59% level)
- **Profitability timeline:** Expect N3 to be as profitable as N5 in 3-4 years (similar to N5 trajectory)
- **Revenue significance:** Dollar amount at beginning stage is certainly bigger than previous nodes, though less meaningful as a % of revenue as total pie is larger
- **N3 family:** Confident N3 family will be another large and long-lasting node for TSMC

## N5 Technology
- **Revenue share:** 21% of total wafer revenue in Q2 2022
- Supported by both HPC and smartphone
- Continued strong demand for industry-leading 5nm and 7nm technologies into Q3

## N2 Technology
- **Risk production:** 2024
- **Volume production:** 2025
- **Transistor structure:** Nanosheet (gate-all-around)
- **Performance vs. N3E:**
  - 10-15% speed improvement at the same power
  - 20-30% power improvement at the same speed
  - >20% logic density increase
- **Backside power rail:** Super power rail is part of N2 platform offering; will be introduced at the proper time when customers need it
- Most advanced semiconductor technology in both density and energy efficiency when introduced

---

## Semiconductor Cycle / 2023 Outlook
- **Industry view for 2022:** Semiconductor (ex-memory) and foundry growth forecasts remain same as earlier projections
- **2023 industry view:** Too early to forecast, but inventory correction expected to continue
- **Inventory correction:** Supply chain already taking action; excess inventory expected to take a few quarters to rebalance to healthier level, likely through 1H 2023
- **Base case:** More typical down cycle (like 2015, 2019); not a big down cycle like 2008
- **Segments affected:** Softening in smartphone, PC, consumer end markets; data center and automotive remain steady
- **Fabless DOI:** Expected to come down gradually in 2H 2022
- **2023 still a growth year for TSMC** despite inventory correction, due to stronger technology position, strong HPC portfolio, and long-term strategic customer relationships
- **Capacity utilization:** Expected to remain healthy in 2023
- **TSMC business:** Less volatile and more resilient; capacity remains tight; customer demand still exceeds TSMC's ability to supply in 2022
- **Internal preparedness:** Internally modeled and prepared for various different scenarios

### High-End Segment Assessment
- **HPC/Data center:** Structural demand remains strong; not seeing excess inventory buildup
- **High-end smartphone:** Not seeing too much inventory buildup in high-end smartphone

---

## 5G Smartphone Penetration
- **5G penetration rate:** ~50% (unchanged from prior estimate)

---

## Pricing Strategy
- Strategic and value-driven, not opportunistic or cost-plus
- Reflects technology value, ecosystem, services and capacity support
- Will not raise prices solely to offset higher overseas fab costs
- Private discussions between TSMC and customers
- No pull-in effect observed from customers anticipating price increases

---

## Overseas Fabs
- **Arizona:** Construction progressing; cost higher than expected (labor costs, COVID supply chain disruptions). Production target: 2024. Discussing cost gap with U.S. government. Ample business opportunity from U.S. customers who want to load the fab. No joint venture arrangement planned; fab open to broad customer base.
- **Japan (JASM):** On track for 2024 production start. Specialty technology focus.
- **3DIC center in Japan:** Established to leverage Japan's fundamental advantage in raw materials and packaging (e.g., substrate technology); complements TSMC's 3DIC technology
- **Cost differential:** Recognized overseas costs higher; working with governments on subsidies and continuing cost reduction efforts

---

## Capacity & Utilization
- Tight capacity situation across both advanced and mature nodes in 2022
- **Specialty technology capacity:** 50% increase planned over next 3 years (specialty only, not overall capacity); includes both new capacity and conversion of existing capacity
- Driven by customer commitments, silicon content increase in edge devices requiring specialty content
- Capacity expected to remain tight and utilization healthy into 2023

---

## CapEx & Tool Delivery
- 2022 full year $40B-$44B ("closer to the lower end" due to tool delivery pushouts)
- Tool delivery challenges affecting both advanced and mature nodes
- Some planned 2022 CapEx pushed to 2023 due to tool supplier supply chain issues
- Situation improving; TSMC sending teams on-site to support suppliers and prioritizing wafer capacity for critical chips
- No impact to 2022 capacity plan
- 2023 CapEx: Too early to comment; will continue to invest for future growth opportunities in a disciplined manner
- CapEx invested each year for future growth opportunities

---

## Project Financing
- **Not considering project financing;** project financing entails stringent/stricter terms and higher costs
- Will finance expansion mainly from operating cash flow and borrowing using strong balance sheet

---

## Long-Term Agreements (LTAs)
- Details not disclosed; working diligently with customers on capacity planning including receiving prepayments for capacity support

---

## Advanced Packaging / 3DIC
- **SoIC:** Primarily adopted by HPC applications (high-speed, power-efficient); 20x capacity expansion planned by 2026
- **Mobile adoption:** Will depend on chiplet architecture, interconnect density, thermal and other requirements; TSMC will have other solutions for mobile
- **3DIC growth:** Expected to be healthy, slightly ahead of TSMC's corporate growth rate
- **Substrate strategy:** Focused only on very advanced substrate technology for high-performance computing / very large substrates; not developing commodity substrate technology
- **N2 and chiplets:** Number of customers using chiplet architecture at N2 expected to start increasing; will become a major approach at 2nm and beyond

---

## Structural Growth Drivers
- Three key factors: (1) Technology leadership and differentiation, (2) Strong HPC portfolio, (3) Strategic customer relationships
- Silicon content increasing across many end devices (CPUs, GPUs, AI accelerators in data center; 5G smartphone vs. 4G; automotive)
- Device unit growth: Flattish to low single-digit %
- Silicon content growth: Mid- to high single-digit %
- HPC expected to be the main engine of TSMC's long-term growth and largest contributor of incremental revenue growth
- Both x86 and ARM-based HPC significantly growing at TSMC
- Technology portfolio expanding: N5, N4P, N4X ramping; N3 upcoming

---

## Dividend
- Committed to sustainable and steadily increasing cash dividend
- Q2 2022 distributed TWD 71B (Q3 2021 dividend)

---

## Tax Rate
- **2022:** 10%-11%
- **2023 and beyond:** Certain tax exemptions in Taiwan expiring; effective tax rate will increase. Taiwan government drafting new tax exemption regulations (in comment period). Further update to be provided when details available.

---

## R&D and Operating Expenses
- R&D expense ratio: Expected to stay in similar range
- Depreciation: Mid-single-digit growth YoY in 2022 (lower than earlier guided due to tool delivery schedule changes); 2023 depreciation to be much higher than 2022

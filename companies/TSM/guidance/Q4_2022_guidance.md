# TSMC Q4 2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** January 12, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 2022 (Full Year 2022)

---

## Q4 2022 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** -1.5% QoQ in USD terms (at low end of prior guidance); +2% QoQ in NT dollar terms (due to more favorable FX)
- **Gross Margin:** 62.2% (+180 bps QoQ; 140 bps contributed by more favorable FX; cost improvement efforts offset lower capacity utilization; exceeded high end of prior guidance, mainly due to cost improvement efforts)
- **Operating Margin:** 52% (+1.4 pp QoQ)
- **Operating Expenses:** 10.3% of net revenue
- **EPS:** TWD 11.41
- **ROE:** 41.7%

### Q4 Technology Node Breakdown
- **5nm:** 32% of Q4 wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 22% of Q4 wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 54% of Q4 wafer revenue

### Q4 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 42% of Q4 revenue (+10% QoQ)
- **Smartphone:** 38% of Q4 revenue (-4% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 8% of Q4 revenue (-11% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 6% of Q4 revenue (+10% QoQ)
- **DCE:** 2% of Q4 revenue (-23% QoQ)

### Q4 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 1.56 trillion (USD 51 billion)
- **Current liabilities:** increased TWD 137B QoQ (TWD 48B from accounts payable, TWD 93B from accrued liabilities and others)
- **AR turnover days:** 36 days (flat QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 93 days (+3 days QoQ)

### Q4 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 487 billion
- **CapEx:** TWD 337 billion (USD 10.82 billion)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 71 billion (Q1 2022 dividend)
- **Cash balance:** increased TWD 47B to TWD 1.34 trillion at end of Q4

## Full Year 2022 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $76B (+33.5% YoY in USD; +42.6% in NT to TWD 2.26 trillion)
- **Gross Margin:** 59.6% (+8 pp YoY; driven by more favorable FX, value-selling efforts and cost improvement, partially offset by lower capacity utilization)
- **Operating Margin:** 49.5% (+8.6 pp YoY; driven by better operating leverage)
- **EPS:** TWD 39.2 (+70.4% YoY)
- **ROE:** 39.8%
- **CapEx:** $36.3B (TWD 1.1 trillion)
- **Operating cash flow:** TWD 1.6 trillion
- **Free Cash Flow:** TWD 528 billion
- **Cash dividends paid:** TWD 285 billion (up from TWD 266B in 2021)
- **Dividend paid:** TWD 11.25/share (up from TWD 10.25 in 2021)
- **R&D/revenue ratio:** 7.2%
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 53% of wafer revenue (up from 50% in 2021)
- **5nm:** 26% of FY2022 wafer revenue; **7nm:** 27%
- **Advanced packaging revenue:** about 7% of total revenue (grew at a similar rate to corporate average)

### FY2022 Platform Revenue
- **HPC:** 41% of 2022 revenue (+59% YoY)
- **Smartphone:** 39% of 2022 revenue (+28% YoY)
- **IoT:** 9% of 2022 revenue (+47% YoY)
- **Automotive:** 5% of 2022 revenue (+74% YoY)
- **DCE:** 3% of 2022 revenue (+1% YoY)
- All 6 platforms had year-on-year growth in 2022

### FY2022 Industry Context
- **Semiconductor ex-memory growth:** ~10% in 2022
- **Foundry industry growth:** ~27% in 2022
- **TSMC revenue growth:** 33.5% YoY in USD terms (outperformed industry)

## Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2023)
- **Revenue:** $16.7B-$17.5B (-14.2% QoQ at midpoint)
- **Gross Margin:** 53.5%-55.5% (midpoint 54.5%; decline driven by lower capacity utilization from further inventory adjustment and less favorable FX)
- **Operating Margin:** 41.5%-43.5%
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 30.7 TWD

## Full Year 2023 Guidance
- **Revenue:** Slight growth in USD terms; TSMC will outperform overall semiconductor market
- **1H 2023:** Revenue decline mid- to high single-digit % YoY in USD terms
- **2H 2023:** Revenue increase YoY in USD terms
- **Semiconductor cycle:** Bottom in 1H 2023; healthy recovery in 2H 2023 (not a U shape, but uncertain if strong V shape)
- **Semi ex-memory market:** Decline ~4% in 2023
- **Foundry industry:** Decline ~3% in 2023
- **Full year GM:** 53% and higher target; working diligently to maintain this even in 2023

## CapEx Guidance
- **2023 CapEx:** $32B-$36B (down from $36.3B in 2022)
- **Allocation:** ~70% advanced process technologies; ~20% specialty technologies; ~10% advanced packaging, mask making and others
- **Capital intensity:** >40% in 2023; expected to decline to mid- to high 30s% several years out (~5 years)
- Tightening spending where appropriate but continuing to invest for long-term structural growth

## Depreciation
- **2023 depreciation:** Expected to increase ~30% YoY, mainly as N3 ramps
- Major headwind to gross margin in 2023

## R&D Expense
- **2022 R&D/revenue ratio:** 7.2%
- **2023 R&D growth:** ~20% YoY increase; will account for 8%-8.5% of net revenue
- **R&D/revenue ratio going forward:** 8%-8.5% for next several years
- Driven by: newer technology development (N2, N1.4), new transistor architecture, design enablement, new equipment; technology complexity increasing exponentially

## Tax Rate
- **2023:** ~15% (with new Taiwan Industrial Innovation Statute amendment)
- Without amendment: would have been 18%-19%
- Certain prior tax exemptions/incentives expired

## Dividend
- **2022 annual dividend:** TWD 11.25/share (up from TWD 10.25 in 2021)
- **2022 cash dividends paid:** TWD 285 billion (up from TWD 266B in 2021)
- Committed to sustainable and steadily increasing cash dividend on both annual and quarterly basis

## Long-Term Revenue CAGR
- **15-20% CAGR** over next several years in USD terms (reiterated)
- Expect to grow faster than the overall semiconductor industry

## Long-Term Gross Margin Target
- **53% and higher is achievable** (reiterated; excluding impact of FX)
- Key GM headwinds for 2023: lower capacity utilization (semiconductor cyclicality), N3 ramp-up, overseas fab expansion, inflationary costs, higher depreciation (~30% YoY)
- Key GM tailwinds: internal cost improvement efforts, strategic value-selling

## Long-Term ROE Target
- **Greater than 25%** sustainable and healthy ROE

## N3 Technology
- **Volume production:** Started late Q4 2022 (on schedule with good yield)
- **N3E:** Volume production 2H 2023; enhanced performance, power, and yield; complete platform support for smartphone and HPC
- **2023 revenue contribution:** Mid-single-digit % of total wafer revenue; sizable revenue contribution starting Q3 2023
- **N3 vs N5 first year:** N3 revenue in 2023 expected to be higher than N5 revenue in its first year (2020)
- **Tape-outs:** Number of tape-outs more than 2x that of N5 in its first and second year
- **Utilization:** Expect N3 fully utilized in 2023 (customer demand exceeds supply)
- **N3 family:** Confident it will be a large and long-lasting node; strong demand expected in 2023, 2024, 2025 and beyond driven by HPC and smartphone
- **Profitability:** Initially dilutive to corporate average; managing through cost improvement and value-selling

## N7/N6 Utilization
- **Status:** Not as high as past 3 years; inventory correction impact; deteriorated further vs. 3 months ago
- **Key drivers of weakness:** End market weakness in smartphone and PCs; customers' product schedule delays
- **1H 2023:** Lower utilization persists (lower than expectation from 3 months ago)
- **2H 2023:** Expect mild pickup (improved from prior expectation of lower)
- **N7/N6 demand nature:** Cyclical not structural
- **Specialty technology conversion:** Working with customers to develop specialty/differentiated technologies (consumer, RF, connectivity, other applications) to backfill N7/N6 capacity over next several years
- **Confidence:** N7 family will continue to be a large and long-lasting node for TSMC
- **CapEx:** Already adjusted N7/N6 CapEx downward

## N2 Technology
- **Production:** Volume production 2H 2025 (maintained)
- **Risk production:** 2024
- **Development:** On track, actually better than expected; GAA nanosheet transistor structure
- **Customer engagement:** Active, not yet ready to share details
- **N2 location:** Hsinchu and Taichung Science Park

## Advanced Packaging
- **2022 revenue:** About 7% of total revenue
- **2022 growth:** Similar rate to corporate average (~33.5%)
- **2023 outlook:** Flattish (slightly lower than corporate growth rate)
- **SoIC:** Active development, customer interest

---

## Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint
- **Arizona Fab 1:** N4 process, production 2024 (on track; equipment arrival celebrated December 6, 2022)
- **Arizona Fab 2:** N3 process technology, production 2026
- **Arizona clean room:** Each fab will have clean room area approximately double the size of a typical logic fab
- **Japan (JASM):** Specialty technology fab utilizing 12nm, 16nm, N22, N28 process technologies; volume production late 2024 (on track)
- **Japan Fab 2:** Considering building a second fab, contingent on customer demand and level of government support
- **Europe:** Evaluating possibility of building a specialty fab, focusing on automotive-specific technologies; based on customer demand and level of government support
- **China (Nanjing):** Expanding 28nm as planned to support local customers; continuing to follow all rules and regulations fully
- **Taiwan:** Continuing to invest and expand capacity; N3 volume production in Tainan Science Park; preparing N2 in Hsinchu and Taichung Science Park
- **Overseas capacity target:** 28nm and below overseas capacity could be 20% or more of total 28nm and below capacity in 5 years or more, depending on customer demand and level of government support
- **Construction cost gap:** U.S. fab construction cost of building and facilities 4-5x greater than Taiwan (driven by labor cost, permits, occupational safety/health regulations, inflationary costs, people and learning curve costs)
- **Pricing approach:** Pricing remains strategic to reflect value including geographic manufacturing flexibility; overall pricing includes value of geographic flexibility
- **Overseas margin impact:** Goal to manage and minimize cost gap; leveraging large volume, economies of scale, manufacturing technology leadership to drive cost down; working with governments for support; confident 53% and higher long-term GM achievable even with overseas expansion
- **Advanced packaging overseas:** No current plan but do not rule out the possibility

## Semiconductor Industry Outlook
- **Semiconductor ex-memory value chain:** Expected to exceed $1 trillion by 2030
- **Long-term structural demand:** Intact despite near-term cyclical correction
- **Key drivers:** 5G, HPC, silicon content enrichment, AI beginning to emerge (HPC customers launching new products in AI/computing area in 2H 2023)
- **Semiconductor value:** Becoming more essential and pervasive; industry value in supply chain increasing; customers' structural gross margins rising over past 5-6 years

## Inventory / Cycle
- **Inventory peaked:** Q3 2022; gradually reducing in Q4 2022
- **Supply chain inventory:** Reducing sharply through 1H 2023 to rebalance to healthier level
- **Demand stabilization:** Initial signs observed
- **Recovery timeline:** Healthy recovery expected in 2H 2023
- **N3 demand:** Strong regardless of cycle position

## Cross-Node Capacity Flexibility
- Tools between N5/N3 (and future N2) can be shared/flexed across these nodes
- TSMC plans N5, N3, N2 capacity as total picture to maintain flexibility

## Mature Node / Specialty Technology Strategy
- Developing differentiated specialty technologies for customers
- Not adding commonly used logic technology; focus on specialty and differentiated for long-term structural market demand
- Working with customers to define needs and develop appropriate technology
- R&D resources allocated to cooperate with customers on specialty technologies
- Goal: generate profitability with reasonable utilization

## Moore's Law / Technology Value
- Value not only from geometry shrinking but importantly from power consumption efficiency
- Advanced 3D IC Fabric technology to improve system performance
- Future focus: greener, safer world requiring lower power consumption
- No slowdown observed in customer adoption of leading-edge technology
- Hyperscale customers developing own chips using TSMC leading-edge technology; not expected to cannibalize merchant CPU/GPU business

## Automotive
- Demand continued to be very tight entering 2023; still not 100% supplying enough wafers
- Shortage expected to be relaxed quickly
- Automotive units expected to grow in 2023

## Smartphone and PC
- Units expected to drop slightly in 2023
- Content per unit will continue to increase

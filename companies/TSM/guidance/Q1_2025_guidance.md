# TSMC Q1 2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** April 17, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2025

---

## Q1 2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $25.5B (-5.1% QoQ in USD; -3.4% QoQ in NT)
- **Gross Margin:** 58.8% (-20 bps QoQ)
- **Operating Expenses:** 10.2% of net revenue
- **Operating Margin:** 48.5% (-50 bps QoQ)
- **EPS:** TWD 13.94
- **ROE:** 32.7%

### Q1 Technology Node Breakdown
- **3nm:** 22% of Q1 wafer revenue
- **5nm:** 36% of Q1 wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 15% of Q1 wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 73% of Q1 wafer revenue

### Q1 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 59% of Q1 revenue (+7% QoQ)
- **Smartphone:** 28% of Q1 revenue (-22% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 5% of Q1 revenue (-9% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 5% of Q1 revenue (+14% QoQ)
- **DCE:** 1% of Q1 revenue (+8% QoQ)

### Q1 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 2.7 trillion (USD 81 billion)
- **Current liabilities:** increased TWD 135B QoQ (TWD 111B increase in accrued liabilities from income tax payables)
- **AR turnover days:** 28 days (+1 day QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 83 days (+3 days QoQ; primarily due to ramping of new overseas fabs)

### Q1 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 626 billion
- **CapEx:** TWD 331 billion (USD 10.06 billion)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 104 billion (Q2 2024 dividend)
- **Bond issuances:** TWD 16 billion raised
- **Cash balance:** increased TWD 267B to TWD 2.4 trillion at end of Q1

---

## Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2025)
- **Revenue:** $28.4B–$29.2B (+13% sequential increase or +38% YoY increase at midpoint)
- **Gross Margin:** 57%–59%
- **Operating Margin:** 47%–49%
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 32.5 TWD
- **Q2 tax rate:** ~20% (due to undistributed/retained earnings tax accrual)
- **Q3/Q4 tax rate:** falls back to 14%–15%; full year tax rate 16%–17%

## Gross Margin Bridge
- Q1 GM decrease: primarily due to 60 bps impact from January 21 earthquake (magnitude 6.4) and its aftershocks
- Q2 GM guided to decrease ~80 bps to 58% at midpoint
- Overseas fab dilution over next 5 years: starts at 2%–3% in early stages, widens to 3%–4% in latter stages

## Full Year 2025 Guidance
- **Revenue growth:** Mid-20s% in USD terms (maintained despite tariff uncertainty)
- **CapEx:** $38B–$42B (maintained)
- **CapEx allocation:** ~70% for advanced process technologies; ~10%–20% for specialty technologies; ~10%–20% for advanced packaging, testing, mask making and others
- **Full year GM:** Mid- to high 50s%
- **AI accelerator revenue:** Expected to double YoY (maintained)
- **AI revenue CAGR:** Mid-40s% over next 5 years starting from 2024 (raised from "close to 40%")
- **Foundry 2.0 industry:** Expected to grow ~10% YoY in 2025 (consistent with IDC forecast of 11%)

## Tariff Impact
- Monitoring U.S. tariff situation; current assessment: manageable
- Not expecting material impact to TSMC's business based on current tariff structure
- Customer demand remains strong
- Pricing may be adjusted to reflect changing cost environment

## Long-Term Targets
- **Revenue CAGR:** Close to 20% over next 5 years
- **Gross Margin:** 53% and higher
- **Semiconductor ex-memory:** ~$1 trillion by 2030

## N2 Technology
- **Production:** On track for H2 2025 volume production
- Performance specs: 10%–15% speed improvement at same power, or 20%–30% power improvement at same speed, >15% chip density increase vs. N3E
- Ramp profile similar to N3
- Yield progressing well
- Strong customer engagement for both HPC and smartphone

## N2P
- Volume production scheduled for H2 2026

## N3 Technology
- Major revenue node; dilution continuing to reduce
- N3P and N3X variants serving different segments

## A16
- **Production:** H2 2026 (maintained)
- Performance specs: 8%–10% speed improvement at same power vs. N2P, 15%–20% power improvement at same speed, 7%–10% chip density gain
- Strong customer interest

## Overseas Fabs
- **Total U.S. investment:** USD 165 billion (combining prior $65B + additional $100B)
- **Arizona expansion:** 3 additional wafer fabs, 2 advanced packaging fabs, and a major R&D center (about 1,000 engineers)
- **Arizona Fab 1:** N4, volume production started (on schedule); yield comparable to Taiwan fabs
- **Arizona Fab 2:** N3 technology; construction already complete; working on speeding up volume production (pull-in by at least a couple of quarters from original 2028 timeline)
- **Arizona Fabs 3 and 4:** N2 and A16 process technologies; construction scheduled to begin later in 2025
- After completion, ~30% of 2nm and more advanced capacity in Arizona
- **Japan Kumamoto Fab 1:** Volume production started late 2024 with very good yield; capacity 40,000 wafers/month when fully ramped
- **Japan Fab 2:** Construction on track
- **Germany:** Construction progressing
- **Taiwan:** Plans to build 11 wafer manufacturing fabs and 4 advanced packaging facilities over next several years
- **Overseas dilution:** 2%–3% in early stages, widening to 3%–4% in latter stages
- TSMC has 10,000 R&D people total (company-wide)

## CoWoS / Advanced Packaging
- Capacity continuing to expand; doubling CoWoS capacity in 2025
- Still tight; demand driven by AI accelerators
- Revenue contribution growing

## Gross Margin 2025
- **Tailwinds:** Utilization, value-selling, technology mix, N3 maturity
- **Headwinds:** Overseas dilution 2%–3%, earthquake impact (60 bps Q1), electricity, depreciation, N2 initial ramp
- Full year mid- to high 50s%

## Dividend
- Sustainable and steadily increasing

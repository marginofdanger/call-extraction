## Special Topics

### Usage-Based Insurance (UBI) Deep Dive — Main Presentation Topic

This was the featured presentation topic for the Q4 2022 Investor Event. Key details:

**Personal Auto UBI History & Evolution:**
- Progressive has had a UBI offering since **1996** (Autograph — required professional installation)
- **2004:** TripSense (first self-install option)
- **2008:** MyRate (first cellular technology for data upload)
- **2010:** Snapshot launch (considered start of modern UBI program)
- **2014:** Introduced surcharge possibility (previously discount-only)
- **2016:** Snapshot app launched (mobile phone alternative to plug-in device)
- **2022:** Continuous Monitoring rollout began in summer

**UBI is the most predictive rating variable** — provides "unparalleled rate accuracy" to customers

**Pricing Curve Evolution:**
- Early days: Discount only (up to 30%), no surcharge possible
- 2014: ~1/5 of customers receive surcharge; maximum surcharge introduced at 10%
- Incremental increases: max surcharge from 10% to 15% to 20%
- Current (legacy): Discount up to 30%, surcharge up to 40%
- Current (continuous): Discount up to 45%, surcharge up to 60%
- About **75% of customers still receive a discount**; about **1/5 receive a surcharge**

**UBI Algorithms:**
- Progressive **owns its own algorithm** — filed confidentially with states
- Uses Cambridge Mobile Telematics (CMT) as data collection platform, but algorithm is proprietary
- Most industry participants use CMT but cannot see each other's algorithms
- Variables include: hard braking, miles driven, time of driving
- Also uses OBD device data to calibrate mobile data

**Competitive Moat Discussion:**
- CEO: "Similar platform doesn't mean we're all using the same data or the same models or using the data in the same way"
- CFO: "We continue to advance to find additional data to use that can keep us with a gap between us and the competition"
- Commercial Lines has "significant loss ratio benefit" in telematics — means further pricing options available as competitors enter market
- "It's expensive to get into this" — cannot replicate overnight

### Commercial Auto UBI Programs

**Smart Haul (2018):**
- Targets truckers with electronic logging devices (ELDs)
- Launched to coincide with federal mandate requiring ELDs
- Uses ELD data (location, driving patterns) to generate score and rating factor
- Ideally accesses data during quoting process; provides participation discount if data not immediately available

**Snapshot ProView (2020):**
- Targets anyone without an ELD (including small business fleets)
- Uses Progressive's Personal Auto Snapshot device (leverages Personal Auto volume for competitive device cost)
- Participation discount at new business; discount or surcharge applied at renewal based on driving data
- Includes free driver portal for fleet monitoring
- Continuous monitoring from the start (due to employee turnover in small businesses)

**Commercial UBI Infrastructure:**
- Moved to cloud-based data storage and analysis (leveraging Personal Auto's cloud migration)
- "Significantly reduces processing time" at lower cost than traditional alternatives
- Near-term plans: improve enrollment funnel, automate manual processes, expand eligibility
- Expanding to small fleet customers with non-ELD telematics (e.g., service fleets like plumbing)
- Dashcam pilot running for over 1 year — exploring video data for claims and underwriting

### California

- Progressive would like to operate UBI in California but faces regulatory constraints from **Proposition 103** (enacted decades ago)
- CEO: "We'd love to be in California... It's the most populous state"
- Will continue working with the California department of insurance to get adequate rates
- No timeline provided for UBI rollout in California

### Frequency — Progressive vs. Industry

- Progressive's Personal Auto accident frequency trending "a lot better than the industry" (analyst observation, CEO confirmed)
- Management attributes to:
  1. **Segmentation** — UBI/Snapshot adverse selection flywheel (surcharge customers leave, discount customers stay)
  2. **Mix of business** — growth across all segments but higher in preferred segments (Diane, Wrights, Robinsons)
  3. **Underwriting restrictions** — maintained tight, pushing off higher-frequency customers
- CEO: "We see this data change quickly. So if we need to change anything we're doing from a pricing perspective, we'll see it pretty quickly"

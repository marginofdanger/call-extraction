## Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2022)

**Progressive does not provide explicit quarterly numerical guidance.** However, the following forward-looking commentary was provided during the Q&A:

### Rate Actions & Pricing
- **Personal Auto rate increases:** 7 points implemented in Q1 2022, in addition to 8 points taken in 2021 — totaling ~15 points over the period
- **7 points of rate still to earn in** over the remainder of 2022 in Personal Auto programs
- Management stated "the majority of the rate actions are behind us" but with "lots of caveats" and acknowledging more rate may be needed in certain states
- Renewal average written premium is "up significantly more" than new business average written premium, reflecting base rate revisions flowing through on renewals
- Rate acceptance by consumers is "at a little higher rate than we've seen historically," interpreted as reflecting market-wide pricing conditions
- Product managers make rate decisions at the local/state level on a "day-to-day" basis

### Growth Outlook
- **New applications are down year-over-year** in Personal Auto
- **Sequential PIF growth is negative** though YoY PIF growth is still positive
- **Policy life expectancy (PLE) is declining**, primarily due to pricing environment; less decline in direct channel vs. agency channel
- Management is "optimistic" that as more states reach rate adequacy, they will "increase marketing spend and reengage the growth engine"
- Growth decisions will be made "state by state, channel by channel" — some geographies have already loosened restrictions while others have not
- Expect to "pull the trigger on some of that growth" once results confirm rate adequacy
- CEO: "We are as anxious as anyone. We do not like having new business app negative."
- National advertising has NOT been shut off; local/segment-level media is where cuts occurred

### Underwriting Restrictions
- **Prebinding verification** intensified: more questions on garaging accuracy, underwriting characteristics
- **Low double-digit percentage** of incoming new business flows subject to additional scrutiny (up from ~half that level previously)
- Build plan options have been limited
- These restrictions to be loosened once rate adequacy is confirmed by state/channel

### Loss Trends & Severity
- **Used vehicle values:** Still 35% above January 2021 levels per Manheim Index, despite some flattening data points
- **Parts costs:** Up over 12%
- **Labor rates:** Only up a "couple of percentage points" — being watched closely given body shop hiring difficulties
- **Rental car extensions:** "Several days" longer due to body shop capacity constraints
- **Bodily Injury (BI) severity:** "Pretty good and pretty stable" — in the 6% to 8% range for the last 4 quarters
- **Attorney representation rate:** Slight increase observed, with some increase in general (nonmedical) damages
- **No-accident speed:** Accidents happening at higher rates of speed, causing more damage per incident
- **Vehicle miles traveled:** Recovered to Q4 2021 levels (9%-10% below prepandemic baseline); slight dip in January/February during Omicron wave, returned in March

## Full Year 2022 Guidance

**Progressive does not provide explicit full-year numerical guidance.** Key forward-looking commentary:

### Rate Adequacy & Profitability
- Management believes it is "ahead of competitors in taking rate actions" — expects this to translate to growth advantage when marketing resumes
- Return to profit prioritized over return to growth: "profit is one of our core values, and that will trump growth"
- Managing to **calendar year 96 combined ratio or better** as the objective function
- Product managers will pursue growth when they believe "the lifetime combined ratio for new customers they're writing is adequate"
- Additional rate increases possible if inflationary trends persist: watching labor rates, used car prices, body shop capacity

### Strategic Initiatives
- **U.S. Personal Auto product model** now available in over half the states — "showing early promising results, especially among more preferred segments"
- **Homeowners product (4.1):** Expanded to 4 additional states in Q1 2022, bringing total to 12 states
- **Continuous Snapshot:** Launched in 1 state (Oklahoma) approximately 30 days prior to the call — too early for results
- **Property derisking:** Plan to non-renew ~60,000 policies in Florida over the next year; targeting shift to 2/3 nonvolatile storm states (from current ~50%)

### Commercial Lines Outlook
- Just passed **$9 billion in trailing 12-month written premiums** milestone
- **Over 200% premium growth** in Commercial Auto over the last 5 years
- Ended March "just shy of 1 million Commercial Lines policies in force" (999.8K)
- About **half of the 63% Q1 NPW growth** was from transportation network company (TNC) renewals: shift from 6-month to 12-month policies, increased projected mileage, rate increases, and less cession to reinsurers
- Double-digit growth across all 5 business marketing channels (BMCs)
- Growth in for-hire transportation (FHT) driven by strong goods movement since pandemic
- New growth vectors: **BOP (Business Owner's Policy)** rolled out in 37 states with 3 new states in 2022; fleet program expanded from 10 to 40 power units; Protective acquisition adding medium-to-larger fleets

### Investment Portfolio
- New money yields as of March 2022: approximately **2.5% inclusive of treasuries, around 3% excluding**
- Portfolio duration ~3 years; approximately **$6-8 billion** rolls over every 12 months
- Investment strategy: focus on protecting the balance sheet first, then earning best risk-adjusted total return; no targeting of specific book yield or investment income level

## Property Segment — Derisking Strategy

- **Florida non-renewals:** ~60,000 policies to be non-renewed starting Q2 2022 over the next year
- **Nonvolatile state mix target:** Shift from ~50% to ~2/3 of book in nonvolatile storm states
- **Rate increases:** Continuing to raise rates and stay ahead of loss trends in property
- **Segmentation:** Deeper segmentation in homeowners focused on **age of home and age of roof** — primary driver of loss; better underwriting and pricing around roof age
- **Higher deductibles:** In many states, property policies include cost sharing and higher deductibles (not "maintenance policies" waiting for hailstorm/roof replacement)
- **Florida regulatory challenge:** State requires full replacement value on roofs; cannot depreciate roof in pricing — limits ability to profitably insure older-roof homes
- **4.1 Homeowners product:** Rolled out to 12 states total (4 new in Q1 2022); improved roof/home age segmentation and coverage expansion per agent requests

## Competitive & Market Positioning

- **#1 commercial auto carrier** in the U.S.
- **#3 personal auto carrier** in the U.S.
- **10th largest homeowners carrier** in the U.S.
- Total company-wide written premium has "nearly doubled" in the last 5 years
- Progressive's 85th year of operations
- Believe they are "ahead of competitors in increasing rates" — explains large part of growth slowdown
- More preferred customer segments show less price elasticity (retention holds up better)
- Direct channel retention degrading less than agency channel — attributed to brand strength
- Renewal retention improving: "more people, even if they're shopping, they're staying when they get the renewal, which likely means they can't find a better rate"

## Regulatory Environment

- **California:** Significant challenge; state looks backward vs. prospective in rate review; Progressive taking "very significant nonrate actions" due to inability to get rate
- **Texas and Florida:** Two big states where rate was obtained "pretty quickly"
- **General:** Working closely with regulators across states; regulators increasingly understanding the data showing real inflationary trends and industry-wide rate need
- No specific timeline given for California rate approval

## Expense Management

- **Policy acquisition costs (Q1 YTD):** $963.4M vs. $874.4M prior year (+10%)
- **Other underwriting expenses (Q1 YTD):** $1,506.3M vs. $1,481.1M prior year (+2%)
- **Q1 2022 Companywide Expense Ratio:** 19.5 (YTD), 19.9 (March)
- **Property segment expense ratio includes** 2.5 pts (YTD) / 2.4 pts (March) of amortization expense from acquisition of controlling interest (ARX/ASI)

## Q&A Key Quotes (Management Language Preserved)

**On rate adequacy (CEO Griffith):** "We feel confident, and of course, we have that 7 points to earn in. So more will come to the story, but we're watching things closely."

**On return to growth (CEO Griffith):** "What we're really thinking about now is when we can pull the trigger on some of that growth... we sit down with the controller from Personal Lines very frequently to talk about return to profit and return to growth in that order."

**On growth timing (CFO Sauerland):** "If they think the new business customers they're writing today are adequately priced over their lifetime, they will be happy to go for growth."

**On 96 CR philosophy (CEO Griffith):** "The first time we talked about 96 was in our annual report in 1971 when we went public, and it's worked really well for us."

**On no cross-subsidies (CEO Griffith):** "We have a very specific goal of not subsidizing and to have every one of our products in the aggregate coming to our 96 combined ratio goal."

**On severity (CEO Griffith):** "Used car prices... still 35% higher than January of 2021... parts are up over 12%... labor has only been up a couple of percentage points."

**On BI severity (CEO Griffith):** "I feel pretty good and pretty stable on the BI side. In the last 4 quarters, it's been the 6% to 8% range."

**On underwriting restrictions (CFO Sauerland):** "Low double digits, which is up from sort of half that when we were more comfortable with underwriting margins."

**On claims staffing (CEO Griffith):** "One of the things that I'm most proud of that we didn't do was to reduce our claims force during the pandemic... we were severely overstaffed for several months."

**On Commercial Lines (CEO Griffith):** "Commercial Lines just passed a major milestone of over $9 billion in written premiums on a trailing 12-month basis. We grew Commercial Auto premiums over 200% in the last 5 years."

**On investment strategy (CIO Bauer):** New money yields "about 2.5%... taking that out on either side of 3%... every 12 months anywhere from sort of $6 billion to $8 billion of that portfolio rolling up."

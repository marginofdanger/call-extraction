# The Progressive Corporation Q2_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** August 2, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2023

**Note:** Progressive does not hold traditional earnings calls with detailed financial presentations. The Q2 2023 (Second Quarter Investor Event) featured pre-recorded introductory comments by CEO Tricia Griffith, followed by presentations on reserving practices (Gary Traicoff, Corporate Actuary) and cohort pricing (Jim Curtis, Personal Lines Controller), then a live Q&A session with management. Quantitative data below is sourced from the earnings call transcript, management commentary during the Q&A, and the shareholder letter. No traditional financial presentation deck was used.

## Special Topics

### Cohort Pricing -- Key Framework (Jim Curtis Presentation)

- Progressive prices each policy to a **lifetime 96 combined ratio** across new business and all renewal terms
- **New business** has higher combined ratio (higher loss ratio + higher acquisition expense) than renewal business
- **Renewal business** has lower combined ratio (lower loss ratio + lower acquisition expense)
- **Lifetime blended** result achieves 96 CR target
- Three components: **(1) Loss & LAE** (improves new to renewal), **(2) Acquisition expense** (front-loaded in Direct, spread in Agency), **(3) Operational expense** (modest new/renewal differential)
- **Direct channel:** ~60% acquisition expense ratio on first term, 0% on renewals; entire marketing spend recognized in new business term
- **Agency channel:** Commission paid as % of premium each term; acquisition expense ratio relatively flat across terms
- **Policy Life Expectancy (PLE)** is critical variable: longer PLE = lower lifetime acquisition expense ratio
- **Robinsons** (bundled home/auto customers) have longer PLE, justifying lower rates and more acquisition investment
- **Calendar year management:** Periods of high new business growth push combined ratio above 96 due to mix; slowing growth reduces new business mix and brings CR back below 96
- Advertising expense is expensed in period incurred (hits expense ratio immediately); earned premium mix shifts 3-4 months later

### Reserving Practices -- Key Framework (Gary Traicoff Presentation)

- Reserves have grown from **$8.5B (2013)** to **almost $33B (June 2023)** gross of reinsurance -- nearly **4x** growth vs. premium growth of less than **3x** (due to growth in longer-tailed commercial lines)
- **Chain ladder method** is one of several approaches used; also case incurred data, severity/frequency separation, size-of-loss stratification
- **Tabular case reserves:** Actuarially-derived predictive model with over **200,000** unique combinations in personal auto; used when adjuster estimate is below reserve threshold
- **Reserve threshold:** When adjuster estimate equals or exceeds threshold, adjuster estimate is used as financial case reserve
- **Roll-forward process:** All tabular case reserves adjusted monthly for aging and inflation; allows up to 100% of reserves to be updated monthly
- **700 reviews** per year covering about **85%** of reserves quarterly
- Chief Actuary has **complete decision-making authority** on reserves; actuarial team has **complete independence**
- Checks and balances: close partnership with claims, pricing, product management; quarterly Audit Committee meetings; external audit

### Profit Core Value -- CEO Extended Commentary

- CEO provided extended defense of the 96 calendar year combined ratio target in response to analyst questioning about foregone growth opportunity
- Three dimensions discussed: **profit, growth, and capital**
- **On profit:** "For us, a 96 combined ratio is not a solve for variable in our business model equation, but rather a constant that provides direction to each product and marketing decision and a cultural shipping point that ensures 0 ambiguity"
- **On growth:** "In 6 months, we've added 2.2 million policies in force. And we've increased written premiums by over $5 billion, and that's the equivalent of adding a top 10 Personal Auto carrier. So I would say growth this year could be defined as spectacular."
- **On capital:** "Our capital position today is strong, and we believe we have plenty of capital to support the premium growth we expect"
- **On opportunity cost of restraint:** "Growth opportunities today do not preclude the possibility of growth opportunities tomorrow... current market conditions suggest there'll be opportunity to increase market share in the future"
- Historical precedent cited: "We've taken rates and other underwriting actions to focus on achieving our profitability goal. We've proven to be better positioned for strong growth in the following periods"

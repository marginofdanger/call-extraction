# The Progressive Corporation Q1_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** May 3, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2023

**Note:** Progressive does not hold traditional earnings calls with detailed financial presentations. The Q1 2023 (First Quarter Investor Event) featured pre-recorded introductory comments by CEO Tricia Griffith addressing key investor questions, followed by a live Q&A session with management. PGR releases detailed monthly financial results via press releases/8-Ks and quarterly shareholder letters. Quantitative data below is sourced from the earnings call transcript, the 10-Q filing, management commentary during the Q&A, and the shareholder letter. No traditional financial presentation deck was used.

## Forward-Looking Commentary / Guidance

**Progressive does not provide explicit quarterly or annual numerical guidance.** The following forward-looking commentary was provided during the Q1 2023 investor event:

### Rate Actions -- Forward Plans

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Additional Rate Planned (Balance of 2023, Personal Auto)** | Approximately **10 more points** on a countrywide aggregate basis -- "in the neighborhood of around 10 more points this year to catch up and stay ahead of trend in private passenger auto" |
| **Rate Range Estimate** | **8% to 12%** -- "that could be 8% to 12%. We don't know. Right now, that's our plan as of today" |
| **Rate in Addition to Q1** | The ~10 points is **in addition** to the 4 points already taken in Q1 2023 |
| **Total Expected 2023 Rate Take** | Approximately **14 points** (4 taken in Q1 + ~10 additional through balance of year) |
| **Rate Action Scope** | "Aggressive rate increases where needed across all lines through the balance of 2023" |
| **Regulatory Environment** | Most regulators understand the need; some challenging states remain (particularly on each coast) |
| **Rate Philosophy** | "We're going to take as much rate as we need to try to get to our target profit margin" |
| **Rate Flexibility** | "If things don't play out in terms of loss trend as we are expecting, we can always pull back. We can always even take rates down." |

### Growth Outlook

- Management **still bullish** on growth despite rate actions and advertising pullback
- Q1 2023 growth was "unbelievable" and management does not expect it to "come to a screeching halt"
- Reducing advertising will **likely slow growth** -- "normally, when we reduce media, it could have anticipated slow growth"
- **Still anticipate robust demand** for Progressive's products -- "still in a very hard market with lots of consumers quoting their insurance"
- Growth will depend significantly on **competitor actions** -- "So I won't speculate on specific growth predictions"
- Premium growth expected to partially offset any policy unit growth slowdown -- "there's also premium growth. So as we take more rate to get to our target profit margin, you're going to see some premium growth"
- Will resume advertising spend once confident of delivering target calendar year profitability

### Advertising & Expense Actions

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Advertising Direction** | **Reducing** media spend to lower expense ratio and help achieve 96 CR target |
| **Advertising Approach** | Pulling back on most **inefficient media** first; flexible and nimble |
| **Local Advertising** | Can "more easily turn local off" in markets where rate is inadequate |
| **Spending Resumption** | Will "very easily ramp up our media spend to grow" once at target profitability |
| **Non-Rate Actions** | Tighter verification, underwriting standards, limiting bill plan options -- "will reduce our growth in the segments we believe we can't currently write at our target margins" |

### Profitability Path

- Management committed to reaching **96 calendar year combined ratio** -- "we will do everything we can to treat our calendar year 96"
- Three main levers: **(1) Decrease expense ratio** (advertising reduction), **(2) Tighten underwriting scrutiny**, **(3) Increase rates**
- CEO: "If things turn around dramatically in the next 4 months, again, the caveats are regulatory approval, weather, inflationary trends, we'll be able to put on growth"
- Balance between calendar year commitment and lifetime cohort commitment

### Loss Trend Outlook

- Severity trends in fixing vehicle coverages are **short-tailed** -- "we can react to them really quickly, which we have"
- Manheim used car pricing "stayed higher than we had anticipated" -- projections had it coming down more aggressively
- Updated trend selects have been dialed into pricing indications
- Management watching frequency closely -- "we kind of just try to react to it"
- Bodily injury severity has been "fairly stable" but could change, particularly due to Florida

### Florida HB 837 -- Forward Impact

- **Long-term effect** of legislation expected to be **very positive** for Florida consumers
- Key provisions: (1) Comparative law changed from pure to modified comparative (>50% fault = no collection), (2) Statute of limitations from 4 years to 2 years, (3) Safe harbor for bad faith, (4) Repeal of one-way attorney fees
- **Short-term risk:** Plaintiff bar may challenge the legislation; "we don't know that they will not try to challenge those"
- March 2023 reserves captured best estimate of the **full effect** of pre-bill lawsuits
- "Going forward, we believe the legislation takes positive steps to help manage the cost of auto insurance for Florida consumers"
- Situation is **evolving** -- "we will continue to assess and react to information as it develops"

### Commercial Lines Outlook

- Seeing **very similar severity trends** to Personal Auto in core auto coverages
- Trucking segment: vehicles also expensive to fix
- Non-rate actions provide **significant leverage** in Commercial Lines -- "a lot of leverage, especially on the Commercial side"
- Restrictions on new business being put into play quickly to ensure target profit margins

### Underwriting Quality

- Management feels **very good** about quality of business written in Q4 2022 and Q1 2023
- "Constantly paranoid" -- watching mix of business carefully
- New business from competitor market share losses is "clean business" -- homeowner business, long prior insurance periods, multi-car
- No evidence of adverse selection against Progressive -- "adverse selection should happen, but not to us" (due to industry-leading segmentation and UBI)

## Special Topics

### Shift in Tone from Q4 2022

- **Q4 2022 call:** CEO was "a lot more bullish" on growth; said "major Personal Auto rate increases are likely behind us"
- **Q1 2023 call:** Tone reversed significantly -- "we're not on track to achieve our calendar year goal of a 96 combined ratio"
- CEO acknowledged: "you said that you were confident that the biggest rate increases were behind you, what changed? The answer is fairly basic. We started seeing data come in over the last few months that was not in line with what we were anticipating"
- Management pivoted from growth-first mode to margin-repair mode with urgency

### Pricing vs. Growth Tension

- At **99 combined ratio** (inclusive of reserve strengthening), management is urgently acting
- Ex-reserve strengthening the combined ratio was approximately **94** -- technically below the 96 target
- However, the reserve strengthening reflects **real loss costs** that pricing must account for prospectively
- CEO characterized it as "balance of accident year, calendar year commitments" -- not slamming on the brakes but managing carefully
- CFO noted that competitor actions are a major wildcard: "if they react aggressively as well, then obviously, our growth won't be as adversely affected"

### New Business Penalty

- CEO acknowledged that new business carries both **higher loss ratio** and **higher expense ratio** (front-loaded acquisition costs)
- This contributed to the elevated Q1 combined ratio alongside reserving actions and record weather losses
- Management views new business penalty as "an investment in our future"

### Severity Deep Dive -- Fixing Vehicle Coverages

- Higher-than-expected severity in **previously closed claims** -- older claims working through inbound subrogation at height of ACV
- Shop capacity constraints remain a major industry-wide issue
- Parts prices up just under 3%; labor rates up 4.5-5%
- Manheim index volatility: had been expected to decline more aggressively than it actually did
- These are **short-tailed coverages** -- management can recognize and react to trends relatively quickly

### Distracted Driving

- CEO declined to comment specifically on distracted driving as a standalone variable
- Progressive uses UBI/Snapshot data to observe related variables (hard braking as proxy for distracted driving)
- "We don't look at distracted driving only" -- examine holistic frequency and severity data including vehicle miles traveled, congestion, accident rates

### Investor Event Format Change

- Q1 2023 event did **not** include the traditional presentation on a specific business topic
- Instead, the full 60-minute schedule was used for CEO introductory comments addressing key investor questions, followed by live Q&A
- CEO opened with pre-recorded Q&A format addressing the most frequently asked questions from analysts

## Q&A Key Quotes (Management Language Preserved)

**On not meeting 96 target (CEO Griffith):** "Through the first quarter, we're not on track to achieve our calendar year goal of a 96 combined ratio. Given our extensive history of meeting our stated goals, this has prompted many questions about how we got here and where we're going."

**On the three levers (CEO Griffith):** "The 3 largest levers we have to manage our margin are to decrease our expense ratio, tighten our verification and underwriting scrutiny of new business and increased rates."

**On advertising reduction (CEO Griffith):** "Our largest controllable variable expense is advertising spend, and we are taking action to reduce these costs, which we expect could both slow growth and help to reduce our expense ratio."

**On rate plans (CEO Griffith):** "We think we'll take in the neighborhood of around 10 more points this year to catch up and stay ahead of trend in private passenger auto. So low single digits. But again, that could be 8% to 12%. We don't know. Right now, that's our plan as of today."

**On growth records (CEO Griffith):** "This quarter, we grew policies in force $1.4 million. We grew premium $2.6 billion, which is approximately like 1 point of market share. If you look at even like a 12-month span, we've grown more than $5 billion. And if you think about that, that's the size of a top 10 carrier."

**On conversion driving growth (Callahan, President of Personal Lines):** "Half of the incredible new business growth we had was from conversion improvements. And in the agency channel, it's more like 3/4."

**On urgency vs. braking (CEO Griffith):** "I would say we're not putting on the brakes. We're definitely going to reduce our media spend, but our intentions are that we still want to grow."

**On what changed from Q4 2022 (CEO Griffith):** "We started seeing data come in over the last few months that was not in line with what we were anticipating, which caused us to increase reserves and react with rate increases."

**On reserve confidence (CEO Griffith):** "For the 5 years ending 2022, prior year reserve development averaged about 0.25 points unfavorable on our year-end combined ratio, which I believe supports the statement that we always strive to set reserves adequately."

**On Florida HB 837 (CEO Griffith):** "In March, there were 280,000 lawsuits filed in Florida, not ours, overall in Florida, civil lawsuits, which is up nearly 130%, the all-time high of May 2021."

**On severity components (CEO Griffith):** "Parts prices are up, just a little bit under 3%, and labor rates... those repair rates are up between 4.5% and 5%."

**On frequency (CEO Griffith):** "Frequency in Q1 was relatively flat. We haven't quite seen it come back necessarily to solidly pre-COVID."

**On bodily injury (CEO Griffith):** "Bodily injury has started to be relatively stable over the last several quarters."

**On competitive environment (CFO Sauerland):** "Our actions I would say, all else equal, will certainly adversely affect growth, less advertising dollars, higher rates for sure. But as we saw last year, as the competitive marketplace was in a bit of disarray even with our aggressive actions, we saw growth."

**On new business quality (Callahan):** "We feel very good about the quality of business we put on during fourth quarter and first quarter. We're constantly paranoid. We always watch and we look closely... we are seeing clean business. We're seeing homeowner business... long prior insurance periods, multi-car."

**On adverse selection (CEO Griffith):** "Our goal, of course, is to rate accurately and be aggressive in getting the right rate at the right time on the street. And if you do that and you have segmentation like we do, adverse selection should happen, but not to us."

**On Commercial Lines non-rate actions (CEO Griffith):** "There's a lot of leverage, especially on the Commercial side... we're able to pretty quickly put those into play to make sure that the new business we're bringing on reach our target profit margins."

**On Manheim expectations (CFO Sauerland):** "Our projections around Manheim, if you look back, for it to come down more aggressively than it has. It has been -- car pricing, in general, has stayed higher than we had anticipated a while back."

**On regulatory environment (CEO Griffith):** "I think most of the regulators really do see and are watching these trends... for the majority, as we show the data, they understand what we need for prospective rates."

**On CMBS portfolio (CIO Bauer):** "We have no delinquencies in the portfolio. We have about $350 million of maturities over the next year in the overall CMBS portfolio, and we expect them, even in this type of environment, all to be refinanced without a problem."

**On nimbleness (CEO Griffith):** "The great part is we have lots of levers that we can quickly move. And our nimbleness, specifically here Progressive will be the benefit."

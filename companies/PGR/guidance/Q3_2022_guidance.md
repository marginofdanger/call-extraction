## Forward-Looking Commentary / Guidance

**Progressive does not provide explicit quarterly or annual numerical guidance.** The following forward-looking commentary was provided during the Q3 2022 investor event:

### Rate Adequacy & Pricing Outlook

- **Major personal auto rate revisions are behind us** — reiterated for the third consecutive quarter; "with the exception of a few markets" where more rate is needed
- **YTD rate increases of nearly 12%** with approximately **6.5 points still to earn in** — provides significant forward tailwind to earned premium
- CEO math on rate adequacy: "frequency is down 9%, severity is up 13% — so think about a 4% net loss ratio. Our average written premium is up 8%. So you want to think of that, we have about 6.5 points to earn in. So when I think about that, I feel good about where we're at"
- Expect "smaller bites of the apple" on future rate increases rather than the large increases taken earlier in 2022
- Rate increases will continue to be adjusted "as our loss experience develops" — remaining vigilant on inflation
- A few large coastal states still do not have adequate rates; local media remains off in those markets
- Local media off in fewer than **17 states** (down from 19 at Q2 2022, 26 in April 2022)

### Severity Outlook

- **Used car prices:** Wholesale prices trending down but "still at all-time high" — monitoring Manheim Index closely
- **Hurricane Ian impact on used car demand:** "A lot of total losses that have happened in Florida with Hurricane Ian — so that could even increase demand" for used cars
- Physical damage severity components: parts (+4-5%), labor (+3%), rental (flat); vehicle valuation ~half of PD severity increase
- **BI severity stable** in 6-8% range — leveling off after post-pandemic spike driven by attorney representation and social inflation
- Medical inflation factored into reserves and pricing; if medical inflation rate picks up, "we will be dialing that into our prices as soon as we can"

### Frequency Outlook

- **Frequency down ~9% YoY in Q3 2022;** down ~2% on trailing 12 vs. trailing 13-24 months; down nearly 20% vs. pre-pandemic
- Vehicle miles traveled down ~11% vs. pre-pandemic (improving from ~14% down previously)
- Mix shift contributing to favorable frequency: underwriting actions targeted highest-frequency/highest-loss-ratio business; growth slowed in large states with high frequency
- CEO: "I don't think we'll know where frequency sort of trends long-term until probably a quarter or 2" — watching post-Labor Day return-to-office trends
- Management sees "a little bit more stability" in recent quarters but wants "several more quarters" of data before drawing conclusions
- Frequency could change sharply with macroeconomic variables: gas prices, work-from-home shifts, COVID/flu variants

### Growth Outlook

- **PIF growth still negative YoY** but inflecting — "several months of sequential monthly PIF growth" led by Direct channel
- CEO: "We feel like we reached the bottom from our retention perspective and that PIF growth should start to continue to improve"
- **Record quote volume and new application volume** across multiple months — July, August, and September each set company records in their respective months
- Consumer shopping up 20%+ in both channels; new app growth up 20% YoY — driven by competitor rate increases pushing customers to shop
- **Growth opportunity is "unique"** — competitors pulling back on advertising, taking rate, creating ambient growth for Progressive
- Management's primary use of capital: "The best use of our capital is to grow the firm. And that's what we're going to try to do"
- CEO declined to forecast specific PIF growth rate: "I never like to guess. But 2 weeks from today, you'll have October results"

### Variable Dividend / Capital Return Outlook

- **Variable dividend:** "At this juncture, I can't say whether or not we'll have a variable dividend" — Board will decide in December meeting
- Primary capital priority is growth: "I want to take advantage and leverage this opportunity to grow the firm"
- If growth cannot be achieved, capital returned via dividends or stock buybacks

### Property Business — Forward Strategy

- **De-risking will take longer than planned** due to Florida legislation (roof inspection renewal provision) and Hurricane Ian moratorium (September 28 - November 28)
- Florida new business closed for HO-3, DP-3, HO-6 until executive order lifted
- Goal remains: shift property portfolio mix toward less volatile states to be "more reflective of the market"
- PIF growth in property slowed in Q3 as expected — progress on multiyear de-risking goal

### Reinsurance Cost Outlook

- Costs "likely" to go up in next renewal — market is hardening post-Hurricane Ian
- Progressive can pass increased reinsurance costs through rate filings
- Multiyear agreements and CAT bond diversification mitigate impact and allow more measured rate pass-through
- Management "confident that we'll continue to have access to the reinsurance that we need"

### New Business Quality & Competitive Dynamics

- Business coming from competitors at elevated levels — "significant spike in business coming from competitors, which is great"
- CFO on quality: "We're always paranoid that we're getting business someone else is shedding, but we're very comfortable with what we're writing"
- Conversion rate "slightly down" as record prospect growth includes less-motivated shoppers — described as "a normal dynamic"
- Segmentation advantage strongest in agency channel via comparative raters: pricing accuracy enables growth "in the right places while avoiding adverse selection"
- Robinson (bundled) segment growing faster than SAM (single auto/minimally insured); Robinson has longer policy life expectancy (~3x SAM) and higher lifetime value (~4-5x SAM)

### Expense Ratio Outlook

- "Very conceivable" expense ratio could increase if advertising spend ramps up
- September 2022 cost per sale was lowest since 2017 — extraordinary efficiency from competitor pullback
- Management will "spend as much as we can as long as we believe it's efficient" — constrained by 96 CR target
- Non-acquisition expense ratio being managed to create room for acquisition spending
- CEO: "We're really bullish on growth for this quarter because of what we're seeing in the ambient shopping"

## Special Topics

### Hurricane Ian Impact

- **Largest single event in Q3 2022** but "not the whole story"
- Over 1,500 Progressive employees deployed within hours of the storm
- Property retention of first $200M; $25M of that initially allocated to LAE
- Auto CAT losses from Ian not materially incremental to LAE (existing CAT team handles events)
- Florida moratorium on non-renewals/cancellations: September 28 - November 28, 2022
- New business closed in Florida for homeowners, dwelling fire, and condo products
- Hurricane Ian may increase used car demand in Florida, potentially supporting elevated used car prices

### Snapshot / UBI Telematics (Updated)

- New business attach rate in low 30% range overall; peaked at ~45% in Direct channel in July
- Continuous monitoring model in ~8 states; ~11% of earned premium
- Now pricing to full Snapshot curve (both surcharge and discount ends) — was not fully pricing to curve early on
- Consumer adoption growing, especially in inflationary environment where "consumers are squeezed and they want to save money"
- Agents being encouraged to position Snapshot for combined customers to get best rates
- CEO: "The best thing when we think of pricing for our customers is to encourage them to use Snapshot"
- Continuous monitoring provides more responsive pricing to changes in driving behavior and miles

### Capital Position (Detailed)

- $4.2 billion at holding company level in non-insurance subsidiary — "well in excess" of extreme contingency layer
- $1.5 billion raised in March 2022 at ~3% average rate across 5/10/30-year maturities
- No near-term bond maturities
- Debt-to-capital at 30.2% — taking no additional actions to reduce; expect organic normalization
- No plans to raise additional capital
- Investment portfolio "extremely conservative" — over half in cash/treasuries, duration 2.7 years
- Proactively reduced interest rate, credit, and equity risk throughout 2022

### Competitive Environment

- Competitors pulling back on advertising spend — creating ambient growth opportunity for Progressive
- Competitors taking aggressive rate increases — pushing customers to shop
- Progressive's cost per sale at lowest level since 2017
- CEO: "We took rate earlier than the industry, which initially negatively impacted volume, but more recently has created opportunities for growth"
- Quote-to-bind conversion slightly lower due to record prospect volumes (less motivated shoppers in the mix)
- Agency channel comparative raters create transparency that benefits Progressive's pricing segmentation

### Claims Operations

- CAT team sized to current scale of Progressive — incremental LAE from catastrophes is minimal for auto (travel, hotels only)
- Property claims use some independent appraisers/adjusters — incremental LAE more meaningful for property CATs
- Ian LAE allocation (property): $25M of $200M retention; will "evolve over time"

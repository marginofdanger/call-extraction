## Forward-Looking Commentary / Guidance

**Progressive does not provide explicit quarterly or annual numerical guidance.** The following forward-looking commentary was provided during the Q2 2022 investor event:

### Rate Adequacy & Pricing Outlook

- **Major personal auto rate revisions are behind us** — reiterated from Q1 2022, reflected in slowing pace of Q2 rate changes (17 states, ~2% countrywide vs. heavier actions earlier)
- **Year-to-date rate increase of ~9%; trailing YoY increase of ~16%** — with ~5 points still earning in
- Expects only **"modest rate increases"** going forward, with the caveat of a few large states (notably California) needing significantly more rate
- Average written premium trajectory (0% in 2021, +6% Q1, +11% Q2) provides the earning-in tailwind; denominator adjusts over ~4 months on average
- **Numerator (loss trend)** running at ~7%-8% from combined frequency/severity changes — management believes rate-on-the-street is sufficient to cover loss trend, with modest additional rate needed
- CEO: "with the exception of a couple of states where we need a fair amount of rate, we believe we'll be able to take modest rate increases... we'd like to get into small bites of the apple"

### Severity Outlook

- **Used car prices:** Manheim Index "ticked down a teeny bit, but still at all-time high" — roughly half of physical damage severity driven by vehicle valuation
- Secondary/tertiary drivers: parts prices (+5%), body shop labor (+3%), rental car extensions (+0.5-1%); watching closely for further labor cost inflation and tech shortages
- **BI severity:** Trending up a bit, particularly general damages on attorney-represented claims; medical inflation factored into BI/med pay pricing
- Management cannot predict exact future severity trends but incorporates every data point into rate filings

### Frequency Outlook

- **Q2 2022 frequency down ~8% YoY** — providing modest tailwind
- Vehicle miles traveled down ~12% (March-May) and declining further into June-July
- Three factors being monitored: (1) gas prices (elevated, somewhat inelastic), (2) ~15-20% of population still hesitant to travel due to COVID concerns, (3) work-from-home settlement — "I don't know that we'll know for a couple of quarters" how WFH settles
- Frequency benefit may normalize as driving patterns settle

### Growth Outlook

- **PIF growth is the preferred measure of growth** — will require both new business and retention improvement
- June 2022 was first month of YoY new app growth in Direct channel since May 2021
- June 2022 prospect volume was "our largest June ever"
- **Advertising still has upside:** 19 states still without local media (down from 26 in April); a "definite upside for us once we get rate adequate in those states" — includes some large states representing significant U.S. household share
- **Will not signal specific PIF growth reacceleration timeline** — "I'll let you know as it unfolds"
- CFO: PIF growth timing is "very dependent upon the competitive market" — competitors reporting 100+ CRs, taking aggressive double-digit rate increases, which benefits Progressive's relative position
- CEO on growth capacity: "We've added 7 million PIFs and grown Auto PIFs 70%" over the last 5 years; "I'm not going to say that we're going to grow that same amount in the next couple of years" — the business is now a "much bigger ship"
- **Retention focus:** PLE declined due to pricing environment; Direct channel retention degrading less than Agency; as competitor rate increases continue, "our retention will improve"

### Personal Auto — Competitive Position

- Competitors are "less aggressive due to their profitability challenges" — allowing Progressive to acquire leads more efficiently
- "Some of the Qs that have been reported" show competitors struggling
- At least one competitor reporting monthly rate increases; others taking "multiple double-digit increases in very short periods" in large markets
- One large competitor exited California auto — Progressive "would love to write more business in California" but cannot get adequate rates currently
- ~30 auto programs representing close to half the California market have rate increases pending; Progressive has 1 pending from January, likely another filing soon

### Non-Rate Actions — Personal Auto

- Actions are state-specific: underwriting restrictions, bill plan restrictions, turning off local media
- Designed to "stall growth a little bit when we don't have adequate rates"
- Will loosen as rate adequacy is confirmed state by state

### Property Business — Forward Strategy

- **Cannot change course overnight** — revised expected underwriting profitability led to $225M goodwill write-off
- Actions will **"require more time than originally anticipated"** to ensure consistent delivery of target combined ratio for property
- **Strategic plan:** (1) limit growth in volatile states, (2) grow share in less volatile states, (3) shift geographic mix, (4) use best-in-class product design (5.0 model)
- **5.0 property product model** to go live in first state **H1 2023** — full by-peril rating, more competitive rates for majority of shoppers, expanded eligibility
- **Platinum agent strategy shift:** likely add more Platinum agents in growth (less volatile) states and reduce in legacy states
- **Texas as case study:** Improved from 10 pts worse than industry (2018-2019) to 15 pts better (2021) through rate increases, higher deductibles, better segmentation; now "well positioned for profitable growth" but targeting smaller share of total property book over time
- **Robinsons growth:** Weighting currently heavier in legacy CAT states; geographic expansion of property product will enable more balanced Robinson penetration
- **Florida:** Uncertain how many of the 60,000 planned non-renewals will proceed due to new legislation requiring renewal if roof has 5+ years remaining life

### Commercial Lines Outlook

- **BOP (Business Owner's Policy):** In 37 states; currently limited to businesses with 20 employees or less ("very, very surgical"); expanding expertise daily
- Management: "Not at this point" comfortable with level of expertise in BOP, but "we'll get there"
- **Protective acquisition** (commercial fleet): "Very excited" — being leveraged "much more quickly" than ARX was; added medium-to-larger fleet capabilities

### Advertising & Marketing Strategy

- National brand advertising maintained — "one of our strategic pillars"
- Local marketing (primarily online search) is the lever turned on/off based on rate adequacy
- Shift toward more specifically attributable (digital) media vs. mass media over time
- "We are great at analyzing the data fairly real time and making decisions very quickly to redeploy dollars"
- Will "advertise more and open up more local markets as we feel necessary when we have the right rate"

### Claims Staffing & Operations

- Progressive "very adequately staffed" in claims (improvement from a couple of quarters ago)
- Moving some claims staff to **subrogation and salvage** through year-end to accelerate closure of outstanding claims
- Industry-wide staffing challenges have delayed claim closings and subrogation settlement timing
- Still considers auto a "short tail" line despite recent delays

## Special Topics

### Snapshot / Telematics

- No specific Snapshot updates provided in Q2 2022 call (presentation focused on Property)
- **Home analogy discussed:** Water damage monitoring systems, security systems, household characteristics explored as home-insurance equivalents of Snapshot/telematics
- CEO: "I think there are several analogies... water damage is a lot. If you can have systems in place that alert you... we look at all of those things"

### California Auto Market

- **~30 auto programs** (close to half the California market) have rate increases pending with regulators
- Progressive has 1 rate filing pending from January 2022; expects to file at least one more in near term
- "The moratorium on rate increases in California is unfortunate because we don't think it serves the consumers"
- Residual market potential acknowledged: "Is it possible? Absolutely. Is there precedent? Yes"
- State looks backward rather than prospective in rate reviews
- Progressive using non-rate actions (underwriting restrictions, media shutoff) to manage growth until rate adequacy achieved

### M&A Philosophy

- ARX experience taught lessons about needing "probably a lot more investments early on in order to get your arms around the product"
- Built dedicated M&A function within strategy team since ARX
- Protective (commercial fleet) acquisition going "much different than ARX" — leveraged more quickly
- Acquisition rationale: technology access, market access, portfolio expansion — not large-scale M&A for its own sake
- CEO: "We haven't been a big acquirer over the years"

### Distribution Channel Strategy

- **Bundled Robinson opportunity:** More than half of U.S. personal lines market; more than 80% distributed through agents
- **Captive-to-IA migration:** Ongoing shift from captive agents to independent and direct channels benefits Progressive given strong market share in both
- **Mortgage/Builder Partnerships:** Testing with digital lenders (e.g., Rocket Mortgage) and traditional lenders through IAs — "growth opportunity for us"
- **Progressive Advantage Agency (Direct):** Multi-carrier property agency with $1B+ in premiums on unaffiliated carrier balance sheets; market-leading desktop homeowner quote initiations
- **HomeQuote Explorer:** Proprietary multi-carrier shopping platform for direct property; online buy button in ~38-40 states

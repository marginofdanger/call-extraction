18 Key Takeaways

1
FRE: The Unstoppable Compounder. Fee-Related Earnings grew from $1,410M to $2,528M in three years (+80%), with growth rates of 11%, 25%, 17%, and 23%. Apollo has never missed an FRE guidance target. The shift to a 20%+ annual growth framework for 2026 and beyond reflects confidence in structural tailwinds across 6 demand channels.
2
SRE: The Most Revised Metric. SRE guidance was raised 4 times in 2022-2023 (from $3.35/share to ~$4; from ~20% to ~30% growth) and then cut twice in 2024-2025 (low double-digit to mid-single-digit; $3.5B to mid-single-digit off $3.2B). The COVID-era FABN runoff and hedging costs created the most significant headwinds, but normalized SRE still grew ~47% over the full period.
3
Origination: 3x in Two Years. Origination volume exploded from ~$100B (2022-2023) to $305B (FY2025). Every 5-year target was exceeded ahead of schedule: $150B original, raised to $200-250B, then $275B — and beaten in year 1 of the new plan. The sponsor ecosystem alone quadrupled from $20B to ~$80B.
4
Global Wealth: From Zero to $18B/Year. From 0 perpetual products at the 2021 Investor Day to 18+ strategies and $18B annual capital formation in 2025 (+50% YoY). The original $50B AUM target was achieved in ~2 years. ADS (>$25B) and AAA (>$25B) are each larger than most dedicated asset management firms.
5
Net Spread: Peak to Trough. Normalized net spread rose from 108 bps (Q1 2022) to 166 bps (Q2-Q3 2023) and then compressed to 120 bps (Q4 2025). The full cycle was driven by rising rates, floating-rate portfolio benefits, COVID-era liability maturity, and competitive channel dynamics. Management signals the trough is near, with new business underwritten at ~130 bps (the long-term average).
6
AUM: $500B to $938B in 16 Quarters. Total AUM nearly doubled, driven by $228B of record inflows in FY2025, organic growth across all channels, and the Bridge acquisition ($34B). The $1 trillion milestone should be crossed in early-to-mid 2026 at the current trajectory.
7
Capital Solutions: $500M Target Met in 2 Years. ACS revenue grew from $414M (FY2022) to $808M (FY2025). The original 5-year $500M target was achieved in year 2 ($538M in FY2023). Quarterly run rates have risen from ~$100M to ~$220M, with the $1B by 2029 target looking achievable.
8
Two 5-Year Plans, Both Exceeded. The first plan (2021 ID) was substantially exceeded across origination, capital solutions, and global wealth within 2-3 years. The second plan (October 2024 ID) — $10B FRE+SRE by 2029, >$15/share ANI, $275B origination — is already tracking ahead in year 1, with origination at $305B and FRE growth at 23% vs the 20% target.
9
Athene: $48B to $83B Annual Inflows. Athene organic inflows grew 73% in 3 years while management consistently emphasized discipline over volume ("leaving $10-20B on the table"). Channel diversification (retail, FA, reinsurance, PRT) and new market expansion (5+ markets not in 18 months ago) support the $85B 2026 target.
10
PII: The Missing Piece. Principal Investing Income has consistently underperformed the ~$1/share (later $900M) target, printing just $284M (2022), $83M (2023), $271M (2024), and $338M (2025). Industry-wide PE exit headwinds are the primary cause. Fund X's DPI discipline (0.3+ vs ~0 industry) and Fund IX's 0.6-0.7 DPI suggest Apollo is better positioned for a realization cycle than most peers.
11
ANI: 17% CAGR Since Merger. Adjusted Net Income grew from $5.21/share (2022) to $8.38/share (2025) — a 17% CAGR, more than 2x the S&P 500 over the same period. The $15/share by 2029 target requires ~15% CAGR from the $7.43 (2024) base. FY2025's $8.38 already puts Apollo ahead of the implied glide path.
12
The Platform Evolution. Over 16 quarters, Apollo has transformed from an alternative asset manager with a retirement services subsidiary into an integrated financial services platform spanning 6 demand channels, 16+ origination platforms, 18+ wealth products, and strategic partnerships with traditional asset managers (Schroders, State Street). The narrative has shifted from "private equity firm" to "the largest private credit business in the world" with FRE overtaking SRE on its way to becoming the dominant earnings stream by 2028.

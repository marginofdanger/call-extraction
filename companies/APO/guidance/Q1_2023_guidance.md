## Banking Crisis & Market Commentary

- Silicon Valley Bank/regional bank stress: "does not impact the retirement services industry"
- Annuity holders saving for retirement; not demand deposits; tax consequences on surrender
- No change in call center volume, surrender activity during SVB crisis
- De-banking accelerating since Dodd-Frank; banks now <20% of all debt capital in U.S. capital markets
- Regional bank retrenchment creates opportunity for private credit/securitized products
- Asset-backed finance opportunity: "where sponsor finance was 10 years ago"
- Securitized products likely to grow faster than direct lending as primary bank replacement mechanism
- IG securitized product is "better credit" than IG corporate at same ratings point — benefit of diversification

## Private Investment Grade Strategy

- Yield AUM: ~$440 billion (largest private credit business, but "not significant" vs $40 trillion addressable market)
- "We want the asset, not the client" — complementary to banking system, not competitive
- Banks want: client, cross-sell (equity, M&A, FX, derivatives, hedging, payments, credit cards)
- Apollo wants: the asset, top of capital structure, senior secured, floating rate
- Fixed income replacement: private IG offers 200-300 bps more than public IG at same ratings point
- Philosophy: "25% of everything and 100% of nothing" — creates recurring business and ecosystem

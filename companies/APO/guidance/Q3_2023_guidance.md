## Market Structure & Private Credit Commentary

- 4 historical tailwinds ending: falling rates, money printing, fiscal stimulus, globalization
- Public market liquidity: dealer capital ~10% of 2008 levels; markets 3x larger; expect more volatility
- Debanking: banks now ~20% of U.S. debt capital; Basel III to Basel IV further constraining; worldwide phenomenon
- Indexation/concentration: 80% of trading volume in S&P 500; 60% in ETFs; 10 stocks = 35% of S&P at 44-52x PE
- Private credit: secular trend, not cyclical; levered lending is "fraction of a fraction" of debanking opportunity
- IG private credit education: "Is single-A private security an alternative or fixed income?" — paradigm-shifting question
- Cost of funds: key competitive metric; late entrants buying degraded back books at high cost of funds

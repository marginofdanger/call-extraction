## Market & Competitive Commentary

- **Software Cycle:** Overallocation for 10+ years (40% of sponsor credit, 30% PE deployment); now repricing with quality companies down 50-70% publicly; dispersion of returns ahead; Apollo on offense with no growth software PE exposure, de minimis Athene, lowest among BDC peers
  - Parallels to prior cycles: influx of capital → overallocation → dispersion → patience rewarded
  - Institutional LPs not rethinking privates allocation; increased dispersion among managers will make disciplined managers "prettier"
- **Principal vs Agent Mindset:** Principal = prepared to own (Apollo's approach; side-by-side with clients); Agent = chasing hot dot; in priced-for-perfection markets, being a principal matters more than ever; no amount of fee overcomes bad principal decision
- **6 Markets Evolution:** Most visible is wealth; but insurance, institutional debt/equity, traditional AM, 401(k) are all institutional businesses (trustees, consultants, gatekeepers); industry built for 1 market (drawdown funds, quarterly marks); 5 new markets require different products, structures, delivery, daily NAV, daily liquidity
- **Traditional AM:** Schroders partnership; PRIV ETF ~$700M; daily NAV is unlock; potential largest single opportunity; open architecture supplier; portion of fee to leverage their distribution is margin accretive for both sides; 2-way benefit
- **401(k) / Guaranteed Lifetime Income:** >$1B 2025; executive order helpful but need rule making/guidance; moving from defined benefit → defined contribution → hybrid (guaranteed lifetime income provided commercially, not by employer); baby steps phase; 50-100% better outcomes from private assets over career
- **Competition in Retirement:** New entrants in lower-quality broker channels; lack origination, low-cost liabilities, efficient OpEx; subsidize with AM fees + offshore to Cayman; "not a recipe for success"; will not hit escape velocity; will need more capital; Athene is tough competitor with 15+ years of built advantages
- **ARI Transaction:** Public closed-end vehicles trading at discount to NAV when underlying assets scarce; transfer at fair value to Athene; displaces other SRE lending (not additive)
- **Origination Globalization:** Taking strategy global from primarily North American base; Japan partner summit; Europe and Asia build-out (same tools, platforms, partnerships)
- **Ecosystem Strategies:** Sports Capital ($6B fund → $30-50B origination); expect handful more in industries with specialized capital needs, quality cash flows, underserved by banking/public markets; prefer organic build to acquisition (cultural integration difficult)
- **Industry M&A View:** Unless exceptional, prefer to build; every acquisition brings people and integration challenges; organic build (Sports Capital model) preferred; white space ahead far exceeds what's behind
- **Market Volatility as Accelerant:** Public market volatility accelerates total portfolio approach adoption; trustees/plan heads see volatility → seek same return with less risk → drives private asset demand
- **IG Private Credit:** $35-40T opportunity vs $2-3T sub-IG; short-duration IG vehicle at $7B in 18 months; industry dialogue too focused on "the small pond"; IG is what's driving volume, profitability, and scale

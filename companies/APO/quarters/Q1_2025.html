Market & Competitive Commentary

  • Hyper Exceptionalism → Exceptionalism: U.S. still 60% of global funding needs; money leaving public indices is expected normalization; foreign LP reductions modest
  • Public Fixed Income Liquidity Crisis: Trading capital ~10% of 2008 levels; market 3x larger; 1/30 of liquidity; expect extreme price volatility in risk-off moments; no one filled dealer role post-2008
  • Private Credit Market-Making: Apollo stepping forward in IG private credit; expects public/private credit convergence within 18 months; illiquidity premium doesn't exist — premium from origination and structuring
  • Public Markets as Opportunity: Post Liberation Day, public markets faster to adjust → Apollo bought $25B in April mostly in public markets; equal opportunity investor pivoting between public/private, primary/secondary
  • ADS Positioning: Prudent deleveraging (1x → 0.5x over 2 years) earned right to deploy in volatile market; acceleration in returns expected vs peers who must reduce risk
  • Competitive Dynamics: Tight Q1 spreads + prepays → competitors chased volume in most competitive retail channels; Apollo pivoted to less competitive channels (funding agreements) and built cash/liquidity
  • Traditional Asset Managers: Redefining active management as public beta + private alpha; mutual funds, ETFs, interval funds with private assets; Apollo as supplier; early days but potentially transformative
  • Tokenization/Digital: Interval funds attractive to digital platforms (daily NAV, subscription attributes); early but expanding
  • Industry Consolidation: Larger established firms will garner largest share across individual, traditional AM, retirement channels; scale required for distribution, technology, education
  • Foreign LP Risk: Modest; pockets of government pressure; Apollo source of diversification; U.S. debt market alternatives limited ($2.9T Germany total vs massive U.S. market)
  • Australia: Super funds $4T → $7T; not large enough to service domestically; strong Apollo engagement

5-Year Plan (Reaffirmed from Investor Day October 2024)

  • FRE Growth: Average 20% annually (15-20% in any one year in non-PE flagship year)
  • SRE Growth: Average 10% annually (ex interest rate transitions)
  • FRE + SRE: $10 billion by 2029 ($5 billion each)
  • ANI: More than doubling to >$15/share by 2029
  • Capital Generation: $21 billion over 5 years
  • 4 Macro Tailwinds: Global Industrial Renaissance, Retirement, Individual investors, Public/Private convergence - Any single TAM could double the business
  • Challenge: 90% of partners agree challenge is internal execution, not external environment
  • ACS Fees: $1 billion target at Investor Day (vs $668M FY2024); management acknowledges momentum but "only 4 months since Investor Day"
  • DC/401(k) Opportunity: Approaching $12-13T market; 2 CITs running; target date fund innovation; not budgeted in plan; regulatory reform would be upside - Semi-liquid equity, semi-liquid IG credit, guaranteed income solutions as 3 near-term product vectors

Market & Competitive Commentary

  • Competitive Moat (4 Attributes): Capital + efficient scaled origination platform + IG asset production with spread + low-cost funding; "maybe 1 other player" has all 4
  • Cayman Offshore: $150B reserves moved offshore; ~half the capital; "will end badly"; FIO under pressure; state regulators must evolve or risk federal intervention
  • Bank Regulatory: Tone improving; banks will be stronger competitors in direct lending (appropriate); but still structurally borrow short/lend long; partnerships remain symbiotic (>12); regional bank consolidation expected
  • Retirement Regulatory: Expect increased access, fairness, recognition; fiduciary standard focused on best net returns (not lowest fees) would be transformative; not rule-making but advice
  • Public/Private Convergence: BlackRock acquisitions lay foundation; traditional asset managers will evolve to include public/private products; Apollo as supplier of private assets; co-branded products; massive managed accounts
  • Higher for Longer: Torsten Slok "has been right"; Apollo generally better off in higher rate environment; credit more attractive in absolute terms for income-solving/retirement clients
  • IMO Channel: Competitors paying up for liabilities where credit quality doesn't matter; Athene reduced share/presence but still active in products of interest
  • Cost of Funds Nuance: Some products have higher cost of funds but lower capital requirements and vice versa; look at net spread basis, not gross

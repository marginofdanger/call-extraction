Market & Competitive Commentary

  • 3 Secular Drivers: (1) Global industrial renaissance (infrastructure, energy, data centers, defense, manufacturing, robotics), (2) Retirement crisis (demographics-driven, worldwide), (3) Alternative to concentrated/correlated/indexed public markets (Mag 7 escape)
  • 6 Sources of Demand (Updated Framing): (1) Institutional alternatives (original 35-year market), (2) Individuals/wealth (expected to match institutional size), (3) Insurance (global adoption of Athene model), (4) Institutional debt/equity buckets (total portfolio approach), (5) Traditional asset managers (redefining active management; potentially largest), (6) 401(k)/DC
  • Supply vs Demand: Industry growth constrained by capacity to originate, not capacity to raise capital; origination is the scarce attribute
  • Credit Cycle View: Isolated incidents are idiosyncratic, not systematic; public vs private credit distinction is merely about syndication; good and bad actors in banks, asset managers, and insurance companies alike; late cycle behavior means bad actors will get called out
  • Private Letter Ratings (response to UBS/Kelleher): Colm Kelleher "just wrong" on systemic risk; Athene stats: <8% Kroll/DBRS-only rated, 70% have 2+ ratings, does not use Egan-Jones; offshore Cayman jurisdictions are real concern (not private letter ratings)
  • Agent vs Principal: Divide growing in industry; principal = prepared to hold risk; agent = prepared to distribute; in markets priced for perfection, being a principal matters more than ever; Apollo fully aligned with clients as largest co-investor
  • Risk Reduction Mode: Things not cheap; long rates unlikely to plummet; enhanced geopolitical risk → take risk down without reducing return needs; ADS exemplifies this (100% first lien, lower leverage, less PIC, 40% less software)
  • Traditional AM Opportunity: Traditional AMs struggling with passive vs active; private assets in mutual funds, ETFs, interval funds; ~10% potential allocation; not just distributing Apollo products but partnerships with open architecture; leveraging their distribution is margin accretive for both sides
  • 401(k) Progress: Couple of billion in managed account platforms; information gathering phase; administration guidance helpful but need formal guidance or ruling for massive take-up; guaranteed lifetime income is the holy grail for decumulation
  • Europe Infrastructure: EDF, RWE, BP, Ørsted transactions; Germany commitment; TenFifty CRE platform; massive capital needs for infrastructure, defense, reindustrialization, power generation

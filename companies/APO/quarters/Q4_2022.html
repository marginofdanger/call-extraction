Forward Guidance

2023 Guidance

  • FRE Growth: ~25%; equating to ~$1.75 billion or slightly under $3/share
  • Fee-Related Revenue Growth: >20% (management fees + capital solutions + other) - Key drivers: new strategic growth initiatives, Athene organic inflows, yield/hybrid deployment, Fund X additional closes
  • Capital Solutions Revenue: 2022 level (~$414M) is "good baseline" for 2023; tracking well vs $500M by 2026 target
  • Expense Growth: Rate of growth in total fee-related expenses to moderate through 2023; positive operating leverage vs FY2022 margin - Bulk of senior hiring now behind; Q4 2022 noncomp is good jumping-off point for 2023
  • FRE + SRE Combined Growth: >20% YoY
  • Normalized SRE Growth: ~20%
  • Normalized Net Spread: 135-140 bps for full year 2023 (vs 140 bps in Q4 2022, vs 123 bps FY2022) - Floating rate benefit starting to diminish; option cost headwinds; alts normalized at 11%
  • Athene Net Invested Assets Growth: Mid-single-digit rate in 2023
  • ADIP 2 Sidecar Utilization: Likely double 2022 level (~40% of capital, up from ~20% in 2022)
  • Athene Organic Inflows: Expect to exceed 2022 ($48B); record year expected; all channels strong - Retail/flow insurance strongest; solid pipeline in U.S. and Asia - PRT budget: ~$10B; business at acceptable spreads
  • PII: Below $1/share multi-year target; market volatility and muted capital markets at least H1 2023 - Back half may see more monetization if markets improve
  • Dividend: Raised 7.5% to $1.72/share annually (starting Q1 2023)
  • Share Buybacks: Expect programmatic buying; intrinsic value seen as "highly compelling"
  • Inflows (AM Third-Party): Expect record, exceeding 2022's $46B
  • Fund X: Expect majority of incremental commitments in back half of Q2; fund will "land within striking distance" of $25B target - Broad support from existing investors; gaining traction with new investors, esp. non-U.S. and global wealth
  • Atlas SP: Accretive financially in 2023; staged closing with rolling closes through midyear
  • Global Wealth Inflows: ~$9-10B expected in 2023 (~2/3 in perpetual products: AAA, ADS, nontraded REITs) - European product platform launching "in coming months" with AAA as first product - Athene Altitude: raised $300M in Q4 from first third-party insurance client for AAA
  • Clean Transition: Launching fundraising for clean transition finance and opportunistic vehicles in H2 2023
  • Equity Secondaries Fund: Formal launch fundraising in Q2 2023
  • Retirement Services Distribution to Third Parties: Aim to raise >$10B from others in retirement services space
  • IAIG Designation: Expect to be included as internationally active insurance group in 2023

Atlas SP (formerly Credit Suisse Securitized Products Group)

  • Renamed Atlas SP; announced with initial closing
  • Best-in-class asset-backed warehousing business ("finance company of finance companies")
  • 250+ underlying financing facilities/partnerships; 20+ year track record of negligible losses
  • Historical origination: >$50 billion annually; >$350B over 7-8 years
  • Staged closing: rolling closes through midyear 2023; regulatory and investor consents needed
  • Funded by AAA and third-party investors side by side (not by Athene balance sheet for warehouses)
  • Warehouse business: single-A credit spreads, very wide spreads; cleaned out through securitization
  • Represents Apollo's entry into the "largely untapped $40 trillion IG fixed income replacement market"
  • Counterparties eager to work with Apollo ecosystem; banks bring hold positions, boutiques bring clients
  • Not a competitor to banking system: "We don't want the client" — want the asset, not the advisory relationship

ADIP 2 (Athene Dedicated Investment Program)

  • First closing announced: ~$2 billion
  • Expected to be larger than ADIP 1 ($3.25 billion)
  • Will take increased share of Athene's new business
  • Expected to double sidecar utilization from ~20% to ~40% of capital supporting growth
  • Benefits: capital efficiency for Athene, FRE for Apollo (fees on gross assets), external validation
  • ADIP 1 nearly fully deployed

Market Positioning & Macro Commentary

  • Credit more attractive than equity on the margin; Athene shifting allocations accordingly
  • "Best investors will begin to reveal themselves" in post-low-rate environment
  • $50B dry powder; $160B deployed in 2022; "do better when markets are uncertain"
  • >100 asset management/insurance entities now pursuing Athene-like strategies
  • Vast majority of new entrants will not be successful — need capital, IG spread capability, cost structure, low cost of funds
  • Increased regulatory interest; Apollo leading transparency (stress tests, asset details, reinsurance disclosure)
  • Consumer preference for higher rates driving retail annuity tailwinds
  • Funded status of corporate pension plans exceeding 100% — supporting PRT pipeline

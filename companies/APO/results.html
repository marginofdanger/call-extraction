8 Key Takeaways

1
FRE Guidance: Perfect Record. Apollo met or exceeded FRE guidance in all 4 years (2022-2025). Growth rates of 11%, 25%, 17%, and 23% against targets that were never revised downward. This is the most reliable metric in Apollo's guidance framework and the foundation of the investment thesis.
2
SRE Guidance: Environment-Dependent. SRE guidance was revised 7 times across 4 years — 4 upward (2022-2023) and 3 downward (2024-2025). When measured against initial guidance, Apollo beat in 2 years and missed in 2. When measured against final/revised guidance, the record is 3/4 met. This asymmetry underscores why management shifted to "through-cycle 10% average" framing.
3
Athene Inflows: 4/4 Beat. Athene organic inflows exceeded guidance every year: $48B vs $45B (2022), $66B vs $48B (2023), $71B vs $70B (2024), $83B vs $70B+ (2025). Total growth of 73% over 3 years while consistently "leaving $10-20B on the table." This is the clearest evidence of Apollo's disciplined growth approach.
4
Origination: Massive Over-Delivery. The most dramatic outperformance in any metric. FY2024 delivered $222B vs a $125B target (+78%). FY2025 produced $305B, exceeding the new 5-year plan target ($275B) in year 1. Every 5-year origination target has been surpassed ahead of schedule.
5
Capital Solutions: 5-Year Target in 2 Years. The $500M target (by 2026) was hit in FY2023 ($538M). FY2025's $808M puts Apollo at 81% of the new $1B target with 4 years remaining. Quarterly run rates doubled from ~$100M to ~$220M.
6
Net Spread Guidance: The 2024 Miss. The most significant guidance miss was FY2024 net spread: ~137 bps actual vs 160-165 bps initial guidance (23-28 bps shortfall). COVID-era FABN maturity, hedging costs, and corporate purchase mix were underestimated. This miss drove the SRE guidance revision and remains the most instructive example of macro-sensitivity in Apollo's model.
7
The FRE vs SRE Dichotomy. The results scorecard reveals two fundamentally different earnings streams. FRE is controllable, predictable, and conservatively guided (4/4 met, never revised down). SRE is partially exogenous, volatile, and frequently revised (7 revisions in 4 years). This dichotomy is why management targets FRE = SRE by 2028 and FRE > SRE thereafter — shifting the earnings mix toward the more controllable stream.
8
Conservative Guidance as Strategy. Apollo's guidance framework is deliberately conservative on metrics it controls (FRE, inflows, origination, ACS) and more uncertain on macro-sensitive metrics (SRE, net spread). This creates a reliable "meet or exceed" pattern for the controllable metrics while providing transparency about the risks in environment-dependent earnings. The overall batting average: 18 of 22 metric-years were met or exceeded.

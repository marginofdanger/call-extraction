Full Year 2026 Guidance

  • Adjusted EPS: >$17.75 (growth of at least 8.6%)
  • GAAP EPS: At least $17.10
  • Revenue: ~$440B
  • Medical Care Ratio: 88.8% +/- 50 basis points (H1 notably below midpoint, H2 notably above)
  • Operating Cost Ratio: 12.8% +/- 50 basis points
  • Cash Flows from Operations: At least $18B (~1.1x net income)
  • Earnings Seasonality: Slightly under 2/3 in H1 (consistent with 2025; reflects Part D IRA changes + business mix)
  • Segment Growth: Double-digit at UHC; low to high single-digit adjusted growth across Optum segments
  • Segment Margin Expansion: 20–90 bps across portfolio
  • AI Investment: ~$1.5B in 2026; at least as much in 2027
  • Debt-to-Capital: Target ~40% before year-end
  • Capital Deployment: Return to historical practices (buybacks, acquisitions) in H2 2026
  • Dividend: Well supported; no changes to historical practices

Long-Term Targets

  • EPS Growth Rate: 13%–16% (reaffirmed as long-term potential)
  • Not speaking to 2027 specifically but "business is meaningfully stronger"; long-term growth rate "more compelling than ever"
  • Drivers: Solid organic growth + AI-driven productivity + innovation in health care platform + thoughtful capital stewardship + measured expansion into value-added markets

Q4 2025 OptumHealth Detail

  • Q4 results slightly disappointing; driven by restructuring actions and onetime items
  • Excluding restructuring and Optum Financial move: adjusted earnings baseline ~$1.5B
  • Medical remained elevated but consistent with expectations

UnitedHealthcare 2026

  • Adjusted Operating Earnings Growth: ~13%
  • Operating Margin Expansion: ~40 bps
  • Operating Cost Reductions: Nearly $1B (many AI-enabled; higher customer satisfaction at lower cost)
  • AI Usage: 80%+ of member calls leverage AI tools
  • Total Membership Contraction: 2.3M–2.8M

Medicare 2026

  • MA Membership Contraction: 1.3M–1.4M (greater than prior ~1M estimate; competitive AEP drove higher plan shopping) - ~600K from plan exits (primarily PPO; ~1/3 full market exits) - Balance from group MA (disciplined pricing + competitor dynamics) and individual MA
  • Medicare Margin Improvement: ~50 bps from 2025
  • 2025 MA Trend: ~7.5% (confirmed; supports 2026 pricing)
  • 2026 MA Trend Assumption: 10% (reflects elevated utilization + fee schedule changes + service intensity)
  • Commercial Trend: ~11% (2025 and 2026)
  • Medicare Supplement & Part D: Similarly positioned with prudent pricing
  • 2027 Advance Notice: Disappointing; further funding reduction; $130B in cumulative funding reductions over 3 years; will work with CMS; likely further benefit reductions and footprint review

Commercial 2026

  • Risk Membership Contraction: 1.3M–1.4M - Exchange: 500K+ - Remainder: general market decline, pricing for margin recovery, migration to self-funded/level-funded
  • Exchange Margins: ~1% (+/- 1%); voluntarily pledged to rebate ACA market profits to customers
  • Group Margins: Closing >50% of gap between 2025 and 7%–9% target range; expect to reach range in 2027
  • January Renewals: Firm and competitive market; supporting required yields and persistency
  • Maintained participation in all states served in 2025
  • Working with CMS on ACA marketplace consumer affordability solutions

Medicaid 2026

  • Membership Contraction: 565K–715K (reduced Medicaid eligibility + one state exit; includes D-SNP)
  • Rate Increases: 6%–7% in aggregate (below medical trend)
  • Margins: Expect contraction from 2025; 2026 viewed as trough
  • Rate Relief: Some received but mismatch continues
  • 2027: Hope for improvement; return to ~2% margins over 18–24 months

Optum 2026

OptumRx 2026

  • Earnings Growth: ~2% (margin expansion ~20 bps on adjusted basis)
  • New Customer Wins: 800+ relationships (backfilled about half+ of UHC membership loss)
  • Reauthorization Removal: 180 drugs (>10% of PAs eliminated)
  • Rebate Pass-Through: 95%+ of customers on full pass-through; all remaining by end of 2027
  • Member Savings: >$2,200 annually per member; >$100B total annual savings from manufacturer price moderation
  • New Pricing Models: Greater transparency; cost-based pharmacy reimbursement
  • Pharmacy NPS: Record high in home delivery; 90% in behavioral health; high 90s in hospital-based specialty
  • Growth Outlook: Return to high single-digit to low double-digit earnings growth expected

OptumInsight 2026

  • Earnings Growth: >4%
  • Margin Expansion: ~90 bps
  • Optum Financial Services realigned into OptumInsight
  • Optum Real + Financial Services: Integration creating closed-loop approach (real-time point-of-care approval + financing; moving from post-service reconciliation)
  • Growth Drivers: New sales, new AI product commercialization, cost management, volume increases
  • Investments setting stage for more growth in 2027+

OptumHealth 2026

  • Operating Earnings Growth: ~9%
  • Margin Expansion: ~30 bps
  • Affiliated Network: Narrowed ~20% since prior year
  • Risk Membership: Streamlined ~15% (PPO exits, market repositioning, payer de-delegation)
  • EMR Consolidation: From 18 systems to 3 strategic platforms (nearly 100% of employed providers)
  • Best Markets: Total cost of care 30% better than competitors; 4.5-star plans; NPS 90
  • Texas Example: 750K patients, 50+ clinics, primary/specialty/labs/imaging/surgery/home health/care coordination; margins in target range
  • Mature VBC Cohorts: ~30% already performing at or above long-term target margin range
  • Long-Term Targets: 6%–8% OptumHealth margin (confirmed); ~5% VBC margin (confirmed)
  • 2026 Progression: Modest basis point improvement with momentum building in H2; further progress in 2027

Transparency & Trust Agenda

  • Independent Reviews Published December 2025: Risk assessment, pharmacy services, care management; positive findings on controls/oversight/governance
  • Remediation Recommendations: To be completed and reported by March 2026
  • 2026 Reviews: 3–4 more (risk assessment accuracy/metrics, clinical policy accuracy, pharmacy services)
  • Public Reporting Commitment: Will publish prior authorization and claim approval rates; performance statistics and trends; rebate practices and prices; key business and management policies
  • Reviewed with administration

2027 Advance Notice

  • Published January 26, 2026 (day before call)
  • Disappointing; does not reflect reality of medical utilization and cost trends
  • Would have profoundly negative impact on seniors' benefits and access
  • $130B cumulative Medicare funding reductions over 3 years under prior administration
  • Will work with CMS for appropriate final growth rate calculation
  • Likely to require further benefit reductions and geographic/product footprint review
  • Similar dynamics to 2026 actions

  • Nearly $1B AI-enabled operating cost reductions at UHC in 2026

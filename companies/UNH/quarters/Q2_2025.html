Full Year 2025 Guidance (Second Major Revision)

  • Adjusted EPS: At least $16 (CUT from $26.00–$26.50; CUT from original $29.50–$30.00)
  • Revenue: Approaching $448B (+11% YoY; vs $450–$455B prior)
  • Medical Care Ratio: 89.25% +/- 25 basis points (raised from 87.5% +/- 50 bps; original 86.5% +/- 50 bps)
  • MCR Seasonality: H1 at midpoint; H2 at just under 91.5%; Q4 expected highest
  • Discrete/Settlement Items: $1.6B total contemplated ($600M recognized in Q2 + $1B incremental in H2)
  • Tax Rate: ~18.5% (lower due to reduced earnings; H2 rate just over 20%)
  • Cash Flows from Operations: ~$16B or 1.1x net income
  • Share Count: 912M–914M (vs original 918M–923M; reflects only share repurchases already completed)
  • Dividend: Increased 5% in June
  • VBC Patients: 300,000 net new (reduced from 650,000)
  • OptumHealth Revenue: Revised down further (see OptumHealth section)
  • OptumInsight: Outlook reduced by $1B (half Change recovery/cyber, half paused portfolio actions)
  • OptumRx Revenue Growth: $18B or 13%; earnings growth just over $200M or ~4%

2026 Outlook

  • Overall: "Solid but moderate earnings growth" expected
  • 2027: "Earnings growth outlook strengthening quickly"
  • Long-term EPS Growth: Framework intact (organic growth + productivity + buybacks + business model evolution); expects to "pace back steadily to low double-digit ranges and continue to advance"
  • 80% of premium revenues reprice January 1 — significant pricing reset for 2026

Long-Term OptumHealth Targets

  • Operating Margin: 6%–8% (revised; reflects investment in new cohorts and longer maturation timeline)
  • VBC Margin: ~5% long-term
  • Growth: Significant for the decade to come; addressable market much larger than 5M current patients

Q2 2025 UnitedHealthcare Segment

  • UnitedHealthcare Revenue: $86.1B (+$12B or ~16% YoY from $73.87B)
  • UHC Earnings from Operations: $2.1B (-$1.9B YoY from $4.00B)
  • ACA Exchange Impact: Just over $600M unfavorable (includes acceleration of anticipated H2 losses with premium deficiency reserve; driven by higher patient morbidity across entire exchange market)

Q2 2025 Optum Segment

  • OptumHealth Revenue: $25.2B (-$1.8B or -7% YoY from $27.05B); driven by contract adjustments and Medicare funding reductions
  • OptumInsight Revenue: $4.8B (+$285M or +6% YoY from $4.54B); revenue backlog $32.1B
  • OptumRx Revenue: $38.5B (+$6B or +19% YoY from $32.42B); adjusted scripts 414M (vs 399M year-ago)

Q2 2025 Membership & Growth

  • Medicare Advantage: YTD growth of 650,000 people (including dual-eligible); growth in D-SNP and C-SNP continuing; group MA trend pressure elevated
  • VBC Patients: Outlook reduced to 300,000 net new (from 650,000) to focus on improving operating performance
  • Commercial: Less member growth than initially anticipated; ACA exchange drives ~1/3 of reduced commercial risk member growth; group insured comprising much of rest

Q2 2025 Medical Cost Dynamics

  • Total Excess Medical Costs vs Initial 2025 Outlook: $6.5B - Medicare (~55%, $3.6B): Broad-based across individual, group, complex populations and Medicare Supplement; physician/outpatient 70% of YTD pressure; inpatient accelerating through Q2 - Commercial (~33%, $2.3B): Evenly split ACA exchange and employer; outpatient most notable; orthopedic and pharmacy infusions key drivers - Medicaid (remainder): Behavioral health trend running at 20%; elevated pharmacy and home health
  • Additional factors beyond medical trend: ~$1B from discontinued portfolio actions; ~$850M from prior period items and onetime settlements
  • MA Medical Cost Trend: Initially assumed just over 5%; now expect 7.5% for full year 2025
  • Medicare Supplement Trend: Over 11% (vs 8%–9% historical); confirms broad-based nature of care activity
  • Commercial Group Fully Insured Trend: Approaching 11% (~100 bps above initial expectations)
  • Service Intensity Per Encounter: Increasing; more services bundled per ER visit and clinical encounter; higher physician rounding, testing and specialist services
  • Inpatient: Accelerating through Q2; expect relatively larger portion of full year pressure vs physician/outpatient
  • Trend Broad-Based: Geographically and across membership including large retained base

Medicare Advantage 2026

  • Trend Assumption: Approaching 10% (vs 7.5% in 2025; vs original 5% assumption)
  • Target Margin Range: 2%–4% (revised from 3%–5% due to IRA revenue inflating denominator; economically equivalent)
  • 2025 MA Margins: 2%–2.5%
  • 2026 MA Margins: 2.5%–3% (lower half of new target range)
  • 2027 MA Margins: Midpoint of 2%–4% range; advancing further from there
  • Plan Exits: Exiting plans serving 600,000+ members (primarily PPO; ~1/3 full market/submarket exits)
  • Significant benefit adjustments across individual and group
  • Shifting to narrower networks and more disciplined managed products

Commercial 2026

  • Target Margin Range: 7%–9% (unchanged)
  • 2026 Margins: Closer to but modestly short of 7%–9% target; recover into range by 2027
  • Pricing: Double-digit premium increases for 2026
  • Trend Outlook: Above 11% for 2026
  • ACA Exchange: Participating in majority of 30 current markets but far more conservatively; may exit select markets if unable to achieve needed rates; premium subsidy expiration expected to reduce membership significantly; adverse selection risk
  • Membership: Expect decline; shifts into level-funded and self-funded products

Medicaid 2026

  • Target Margin (C&S Segment): 2%–4% (adjusted from 3%–5% due to IRA impact on D-SNP)
  • Core Medicaid (Non-Dual) Target: ~2% rated margin with states
  • 2025 Non-Dual Medicaid: Breakeven
  • 2026 Non-Dual Medicaid: Negative 1% to negative 1.7% margins
  • Rate Increases: ~6% combined in 2025; expect comparable in 2026
  • Rate-Acuity Mismatch: 12–18 month lag; expect to extend well into 2026
  • Membership Losses: From early adoption of recent legislation factored in

OptumHealth Deep Dive

2025 Earnings Gap: ~$6.6B Below Expectations

  • Value-Based Care (~55%, $3.6B): Three roughly equal drivers: 1. Enrollment mix: more complex/dual-eligible; new-to-Optum underserved patients from plan exits; negative margins near double digits for new patients 2. Accelerated medical trend: physician/outpatient in Medicare; behavioral in Medicaid 3. V28 risk model: underestimated impact on higher-acuity population; misexecuted transition
  • Discontinued Portfolio Actions (~30%, $2B): Previously planned divestitures/transactions paused
  • Other (~15%, $1B): Lower service volumes (19M vs 20M planned fee-for-service visits), nonrecurring prior period impacts, slowed tuck-in acquisitions

V28 Impact

  • Total 3-year headwind for OptumHealth: $11B ($2B and $1B respectively more than initial estimates)
  • Through 2025: $7B realized
  • Remaining 2026 headwind: ~$4B; expect pricing/benefit changes to mitigate ~50%; remainder from operating cost discipline and care program execution

Value-Based Care Performance

  • 2025 VBC Operating Margin: ~1% (vs 3%+ in 2024; ~5% in 2023)
  • Cohort Margins: - 2021 and prior (most mature): 8%+ margin - 2022–2023 cohorts: 2% margin - 2024–2025 cohorts (40% of patients): Negative margins
  • Long-Term VBC Margin Target: ~5%
  • 2026 VBC Margin: Expect ~1% (relatively consistent with 2025); advance in 2027+

Business Composition

  • Value-Based Care: ~65% of OptumHealth revenue; 5 million patients in fully accountable arrangements
  • Fee-for-Service Care Delivery: ~15% of revenue; most businesses at low double-digit margins; primary care/multi-specialty practices at negative margins (hundreds of millions in losses)
  • Services (behavioral, military/veterans, health financial): ~20% of revenue; ~10% combined margins
  • Total patients served: Nearly 20 million across 3 lines

Remediation Actions

  • 1. Improving clinical model consistency (primary care anchored, wraparound services) 2. Margin recovery: ceasing ~200K fully accountable PPO patients (~40% of PPO served); increasing rates; mitigate ~50% of remaining $4B V28 headwind 3. Concentrated regional operating model with 4 market leaders (from localized approach); completing technology integration; ~$1B cost reductions in 2026 4. Shifting risk back to original underwriters until hardened capacity; more disciplined risk arrangements

OptumRx Headwinds

  • Portfolio actions removed: ~$150M headwind
  • Ancillary business issues: ~$50M (taking corrective actions)
  • Nuvaila (private label) launch: ~$150M headwind (expected to mature into strong earnings)
  • GLP-1 impact: $160M headwind for pharmacy services businesses

Culture & Reform Agenda

  • "Tone of change and reform" — recommitment to mission, values, service, integrity, humility
  • Returned to monthly business reviews; returned to in-office work across enterprise
  • Critical processes under independent expert review (Analysis Group, FTI Consulting): risk status, care management, pharmaceutical services
  • Key policies/practices review by end of Q3; first performance measures report in Q4
  • Proactively engaged with regulators and all external stakeholders
  • Pursuing cost opportunities while also investing in underinvested areas (OptumInsight, AI, fintech)
  • Portfolio assessment paused; focused on performance of existing businesses
  • Some businesses require "fundamental reorientation"; others need building/nurturing; others must be "reconsidered and redirected to original purpose"

AI & Technology

  • AI-powered RCM solution launched; AI-powered real-time coordination of benefits launching later in 2025
  • Accelerating AI-first orientation across enterprise
  • AI to drive efficiency, forecasting, early insights, faster decision-making
  • Strengthening actuarial resource base and analytics; internal audit review of guidance process
  • OptumInsight and Optum Financial: combined unified solutions with AI foundation; "early innings" in product journey

Amedisys Transaction

  • Committed to closing; still working through regulatory process
  • Productive engagement with regulators
  • Home health capabilities viewed as foundational to value-based care

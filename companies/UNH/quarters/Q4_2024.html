MCR Dynamics — 2024 to 2025 Bridge

  • 2025 MCR ~100 bps above 2024 (86.5% vs 85.5%)
  • Upward drivers: IRA impacts (Part D catastrophic phase changes), V28 year 2 Medicare funding cuts, continued mix shift to public sector programs, respectful view of care activity
  • Offsetting factors: Cyber/SA impacts not repeating (~30 bps), trend/affordability initiatives, non-run-rate Q4 2024 revenue items not repeating
  • Hospital coding intensity and specialty Rx prescribing: stable at Q3 levels through Q4; no acceleration; incorporated into 2025 outlook
  • Medicaid rate gap: narrowing over course of year; January 1 renewal cycle actions support this view

Medicare Advantage 2025 Growth

  • Strong AEP results; aligned to expectations; on track for up to 800K full year
  • >50% of full year growth from AEP (selling season structure changes for 2025)
  • Balanced/diversified growth across products and geographies
  • Strength in HMO and full dual plan offerings (more managed products)
  • Near-record retention
  • 3x more returning members vs prior year
  • Mix improvement is key differentiator of 2025 vs 2024
  • Long-term MA target margins: consistent; no pricing catch-up needed
  • 2025 growth pace expected similar to 2024

PBM Reform & Drug Pricing

  • 98% rebate pass-through in 2024
  • Committed to 100% rebate pass-through by 2028 at latest; phasing out remaining non-pass-through arrangements
  • 100% already in fully insured employer offerings
  • GLP-1 example: $900 in U.S. vs ~$90 in Europe (10x)
  • OptumRx delivers "many tens of billions" in annual savings vs manufacturer list prices
  • Drug companies set and inflate prices; PBMs are the only effective mechanism holding them accountable
  • Encourage all clients to pass savings directly to patients at point of sale

Consumer Experience & Digital

  • UHC mobile app visits up 66% YoY (record); #1 health care app in Google/Apple stores
  • Digital engagement up ~1/3 across all of UHC
  • App registrations up ~100% YoY
  • Phone calls down ~10% YoY
  • OptumRx: 750 new clients / 1.6M new consumers onboarded at 1/3 less cost
  • Claims processing: <0.5% ultimately rejected for clinical reasons; ~85%+ of held claims due to wrong company/information/benefits (addressable with technology)
  • Working with industry and policymakers on standardized real-time claims processing

AI & Technology Modernization

  • Hundreds of AI use cases being scaled
  • Call center: 10% less calls for same consumer base; full scaling by end of 2025
  • Clinical summaries for nurses: getting fully scaled
  • 2025 focus: consumer experiences, provider documentation, digitizing paperwork, automated claims processing
  • Feels "very early stage"; just scratching the surface of opportunity
  • Key driver of OCR improvement (150 bps in 2024; further improvement expected in 2025)

OptumHealth Portfolio Refinement

  • Consumer count dropped ~4M from portfolio refinement (e.g., exiting standalone urgent care)
  • Q4 margin impacted by: restructuring/legacy contract refinement (onetime), membership mix changes, STARS/quality investments, 1/1 new member onboarding
  • Stepping into 2025 in more favorable position
  • Better understanding of V28 in year 2; payer relationships and contracts evolved
  • Confident in long-term margin targets

OptumInsight / Change Healthcare

  • Change clearinghouse business continuing to rebuild
  • Revenue backlog approaching $35B for 2025 (growth from new product sales)
  • Solutions delivered at scale: consumer experience, payment/claims flows, next-best-action guidance, life sciences
  • Core business (non-Change) performing strongly
  • FY 2024 Change business disruption: $867M ($0.77/share)

2026 Rate Environment

  • MA Advance Notice for 2026: preliminary; won't be finalized until April
  • Looking forward to engaging new administration
  • Hope for more rational rate-setting reflecting actual cost trends
  • Medicaid: seeing more rational behavior from states; historic offset lag narrowing

Cost Management

  • 2024 OCR improved ~150 bps YoY
  • Roughly half from business portfolio initiatives; half from accelerated operating efficiencies
  • AI-driven initiatives: customer service representatives more effective; early stage
  • Digital adoption leading savings across enterprise
  • 2025: continuing operating cost improvement; further OCR gains expected
  • Serving ~150 million people with 10% fewer phone calls = massive efficiency

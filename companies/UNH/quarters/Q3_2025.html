Q3 2025 Investments

2026 Headwinds

  • V28 final year: >$6B headwind to overall enterprise (nearly $50B in industry-wide Medicare cuts over 3 years by previous administration)
  • Further investment needed in OptumHealth and OptumInsight
  • Medicaid funding and program pressures
  • Tax rate normalizing higher vs 2025
  • Investment income declining with lower interest rates

2026 Tailwinds

  • Repricing: catalyst for earnings growth; returning to long-term target margins
  • Particularly solid YoY results expected in commercial and Medicare businesses
  • Stability and measured return to growth in Optum entities
  • Debt paydown reducing interest expense
  • Aggressive affordability initiatives to improve medical trend vs pricing

Medicare Advantage 2026

  • Repriced: Vast majority of UHC risk businesses including MA
  • 2026 Trend Assumption: ~10% (confirmed; vs 7.5% in 2025; reflects elevated care activity + fee schedule changes + aggressive provider coding/billing)
  • Target Margin Range: 2%–4% (IRA-adjusted equivalent of prior 3%–5%)
  • 2025 MA Margins: ~2%–2.5%
  • 2026 MA Margins: Margin improvements expected
  • 2027 MA Margins: Potential to reach upper half of 2%–4% range
  • Membership Contraction: ~1 million total (individual + group) - ~600K from plan exits (primarily PPO; ~1/3 full market/submarket exits) - ~200K from group MA (disciplined pricing + competitor dynamics) - ~200K from individual MA
  • AEP Early Results: In line with strategic positioning for 2026
  • STARS: Improvement YoY; focus already shifted to payment year 2028; incremental Q4 investments
  • Significant benefit adjustments and network reductions executed
  • Medicare Supplement: Prudent view for 2026; trend in excess of 11% in 2025
  • Part D: Benefit design adjusted (co-pay to co-insurance on Tier 3 branded drugs across most MAPD/PDP); cautious approach given demo program uncertainty

Commercial 2026

  • Pricing: ~60% of group commercial insured priced for 2026 as of call date; pricing reflects elevated costs
  • Trend: ~11% for 2025 and 2026
  • 2026 Margins: ~150 bps below low end of 7%–9% target range (~5.5%)
  • 2027 Margins: Return to 7%–9% normal range
  • Group Fully Insured: Expected to contract in line with broader market
  • Self-Funded: Strong traction continuing; Surest growing with robust 2027 pipeline
  • ACA Exchange: Rate filings in nearly all 30 states; average rate increases over 25%; enrollment reducing by ~2/3; targeted service area reductions; margin improvement but still below target
  • ACA Subsidy Expiration: Projected to significantly reduce membership; adverse selection risk
  • Employer Focus: Health care affordability top of mind; integrated advocacy solutions, combined medical/Rx benefits, Surest growing

Medicaid 2026

  • 2025: Breakeven (maintained)
  • 2026: Margins expected to decline further (viewed as trough); negative margins in non-dual segment
  • Return to ~2% margins: 18–24 months (2027–2028)
  • Draft Rates: Received on almost half of Jan 1 contracts; continue to advocate for updates reflecting elevated trends
  • Trend Drivers: Specialty pharmacy, behavioral health (20% trend), home health
  • One Big Beautiful Bill: Potential transformation; 18–24 month timeline; collaboration with states needed
  • Membership: Losses from early adoption of recent legislation factored in

OptumHealth Progress & 2026

VBC Restructuring

  • Three Critical Issues Identified: 1. Provider network grew too large 2. Rapid expansion → operating inconsistencies; over-reliance on affiliated physicians less aligned with VBC 3. Accepting risk in products/services unsuited for clinically oriented VBC model
  • Payer Contracting: 90%+ complete for 2026; on track to offset ~50% of 2026 V28 headwind through rates + product/benefit adjustments
  • PPO Exits: 200K lives finalized (majority PPO; exiting 40%+ of PPO footprint); may see additional exits
  • VBC Membership: Expect ~10% decline in 2026; return to growth in 2027
  • Network Actions: Fewer providers starting 2026; narrower, more integrated; employed or contractually dedicated physicians where possible; separating from less-aligned providers
  • National Operating Model: Regionally led, high-performing practices with consolidated locations; reduces fixed cost; drives purchasing economies; aligned technology
  • Remaining V28 Offset: From engagement (85% of high-risk members engaged in 2025) + operating cost discipline + care program execution

Performance Metrics

  • 2025 OH Margins: Just under 3% (VBC under 1%)
  • 2026: Margin improvement across ALL of OptumHealth (even with V28 year 3)
  • 2027: Further acceleration toward 6%–8% long-term targets
  • 6%–8% achievement: More back-end weighted but progress expected throughout
  • Star Ratings: 80% of Optum at Home in 4+ star plans; ~100% of I-SNP in 4+ star
  • NPS: 90 at highest performing facilities
  • Fee-for-Service: East region: 3% per visit productivity increase QoQ (scheduling, workflow, patient acquisition); similar efforts in South and West

Revenue Composition

  • VBC: ~65% of OptumHealth revenue (2/3 from UHC, 1/3 from diverse payers)
  • Fee-for-Service Care Delivery: ~15%
  • Services (behavioral, military/veterans, financial): ~20%

OptumInsight

  • Leadership: Sandeep Dadlani now CEO (from CDO/CTO role)
  • Competitive position strong but not performing at potential
  • Optum Financial Services realigned within OptumInsight platform
  • New AI Products: - Optum Real: First real-time platform for claims and reimbursements in the industry; dramatic early pilot results - Optum Integrity One: Most advanced AI auto-coding tool; 73% productivity improvement (ambulatory outpatient); 23% improvement (hospital inpatient) - Crimson AI: Clinical analytics for surgical costs/OR optimization; 6 wins in 90 days; 13:1 average ROI
  • Strategy: Evolving from traditional services → AI-first services → products → platforms; building AI-first workforce and sales teams
  • 2026: Investments will slow growth but accelerate 2027 results; expected to ignite top line revenue and operating earnings in line with long-term targets

OptumRx

  • Revenue Growth: Double-digit in pharmacies
  • Selling Season: Strong; higher retention and new customer growth
  • 2026 Membership: New growth more than offset by UHC membership attrition
  • Rebate Pass-Through: ~85% of customers participating in full pass-through; expect 95% by 2027; 100% by 2028
  • Independent Pharmacy: Increased branded drug payments to 14,000+ independent retail pharmacies
  • Disciplined pricing, transparency and quality outcomes

Provider Coding & Service Intensity

  • Higher-cost sites of service where lower-cost options available (lab, ER, surgery)
  • More services attached per ER visit and hospital visit
  • Increase in specialists rounding per inpatient stay
  • Bias toward higher DRG weighting
  • Fairly consistent nationally but with significant outliers
  • Taking network actions on outliers for affordability
  • AI deployed in payment integrity programs; clinical affordability programs
  • IDR process: not material trend driver at this distance

Capital & Balance Sheet

  • Debt-to-Capital: 44.1% (target ~40%)
  • Expect ~40% in H2 2026
  • Paused: Share buybacks and strategic acquisitions
  • Reinstating historical capital deployment practices in back half of 2026
  • Dividend: No changes to historical practices; maintained
  • Amedisys: Transaction closed late Q3; $3.4B net cash disbursement
  • Change Healthcare Loans: Finalizing recovery of remaining outstanding care provider balances

International & Portfolio

  • Dedicating energies to U.S. health care needs
  • Reducing footprint in international markets
  • Consolidating OptumHealth locations and finalizing geographic market plans
  • Preliminary non-GAAP charge: low single-digit billions (substantially noncash); details in Q4
  • Business assessment to be completed in Q4

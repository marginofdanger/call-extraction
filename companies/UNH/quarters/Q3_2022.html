Q3 2022 Optum Segment

  • Optum Revenue: $46.6B (+17.0% YoY)
  • Optum Operating Margin: 7.9% (vs 7.7% prior year)
  • Optum Earnings from Operations: $3.66B
  • OptumHealth Revenue: $18.46B (+34% YoY); revenue per consumer grew 31%
  • OptumInsight Revenue: $3.69B (+18% YoY); revenue backlog $24.1B (+$1.8B YoY); health system partnerships represent ~1/4 of backlog
  • OptumRx Revenue: $25.20B (+8% YoY); pharmacy care services revenue growing double digits

Q3 2022 Membership & Growth

  • Medicare Advantage: Grown 800,000 YTD; on pace for full year growth of 900,000 lives
  • 2024 Star Ratings: 81% of members in 4-star or better plans; UHC enters 2023 serving more people in 4- and 5-star plans than any other health plan
  • Domestic Commercial Insured: +100,000 over past half year; growth in Surest and virtual-first offerings
  • Medicaid: +350,000 YTD; won Nebraska TANF/CHIP/LTC (highest score overall and in each individual category)
  • D-SNP: NPS of 80
  • OptumCare: ~20 million people served; still under 15% in fully capitated arrangements
  • OptumHealth Revenue from Value-Based Care: Over 2/3 of revenue derived from value-based care constructs
  • National Accounts: Very strong renewal year and strong sales year; confident in growth year for 2023; net hiring among customers (no recession impact visible yet)

Q3 2022 Care Patterns

  • Similar to Q2; care patterns planning to continue normalizing
  • Inpatient: below baseline across all lines of business; most notable deviation
  • All other service types: largely near, at, or slightly above normal levels
  • Medicare: urgent care slightly higher, ER slightly lower ("good trade for the system")
  • Medicare outpatient surgeries: back to normal
  • Preventive screenings: broadly consistent with longer-term norms
  • Acuity patterns: remain stable
  • Taking "normal year view" for flu season

2023 Early Observations

  • Majority of analyst estimates "reasonably reflecting performance levels we would expect to offer in November"
  • Current consensus (~$24.87) at the top end of likely initial earnings outlook range
  • Planning for care patterns to continue normalizing
  • Pricing trend "a little higher than historical norms" due to: lower 2022 starting point, labor/inflation costs, pacing of care pattern normalization
  • MA industry growth expected at 8%–9%; expect to outperform industry with share gaining growth
  • ACA exchanges: expanding into 4 new states; footprint going from ~40% to ~2/3 of addressable market
  • Change Healthcare: will be accretive in 2023

Change Healthcare Q4 2022 Impact

  • ~$800M revenue added to OptumInsight without operating earnings impact in Q4
  • Transaction and integration costs expected; potential >$100M incremental integration investment to accelerate benefits
  • Second half seasonally weaker for Change

Change Healthcare Integration

  • Combined with OptumInsight effective October 2022 (11 days as of call date)
  • Focus areas: point-of-care insights, automated claims processing, payment integrity
  • Brings robust transaction network with strong payer and provider connectivity
  • "New OptumInsight" expected to emerge over next 2-3 quarters

Walmart Value-Based Care Collaboration

  • Long-term collaboration announced September 2022
  • Jointly developing 15 Walmart health clinics in Florida and Georgia starting 2023
  • Plans to extend into additional geographies over time
  • Will address social determinants of health (healthy foods, medications, dental, vision)

RVO Health Partnership

  • Partnership with Red Ventures combining health content platforms (Healthline, Healthgrades) with Optum marketplace (Optum Store, Optum Perks)
  • Reaches 100 million+ active monthly visitors

$0 Copay Expansion

  • July 2022 announcement: $0 consumer cost share on diabetes, allergic reaction, emergency drugs for commercial insured members starting January 2023
  • Now expanding to self-funded employers; benefit available to 1 million+ additional people
  • Sanofi insulin partnership: $35/month for consumers without insurance, home delivery available

Behavioral Health Expansion

  • Behavioral health network expanded 25% over last couple of years
  • Growing complement of Optum-owned behavioral clinical practices
  • Expanding digital offerings for 24/7 behavioral health support
  • Improved navigation tools for condition-specific behavioral health care

Medicare Advantage Clinical Outcomes

  • MA members with diabetes: 50%+ lower rates of any complication, 70%+ lower serious complications vs FFS
  • MA: people spend up to 40% less out of pocket vs FFS
  • Lower net cost to government
  • Nearly half of American seniors now enrolled in MA (vs ~25% a decade ago)

Medicaid Redeterminations Preparation

  • Assuming PHE ends January 2023; redeterminations resume Q1
  • State-by-state pacing over 12-14 months
  • Initiatives: data sharing, consumer outreach, community engagement, provider partnerships, pharmacy connections

21 Key Takeaways

1
The most dramatic EPS collapse in UNH history. Adjusted EPS fell from $27.66 (2024) to $16.35 (2025) — a 41% decline. Initial 2025 guidance of $29.50–$30.00 was cut twice, ultimately landing 45% below the original midpoint. No single factor caused this; it was a convergence of V28, IRA, medical cost acceleration, and OptumHealth margin compression.
2
Medical care ratio surged 710 basis points in 3 years. MCR went from 82.0% (2022) to 89.1% (2025), with the sharpest move in 2025 (+350 bps YoY). The 2025 total excess medical costs versus initial outlook was $6.5B, with Medicare (~55%), commercial (~33%), and Medicaid accounting for the remainder. Service intensity per encounter — more services bundled per visit — proved a novel and persistent cost driver.
3
The MA medical cost trend doubled from plan. Management initially assumed ~5% MA trend for 2025; actual was 7.5%. For 2026 pricing, the assumed trend jumped to 10%. Medicare Supplement ran over 11% (vs 8–9% historical). This wasn’t just utilization — it was service intensity (more tests per visit, more specialists rounding per inpatient stay, higher DRG weighting).
4
V28 proved far more damaging than modeled. The CMS risk adjustment model change created an $11B headwind for OptumHealth over 3 years — $3B more than initial estimates. Management admitted they “underestimated impact on higher-acuity population” and “misexecuted the transition.” Across the broader industry, management cited nearly $50B in cumulative Medicare funding reductions over 3 years.
5
Value-based care’s rapid scale-up created margin fragility. VBC patients nearly tripled from ~1.8M (end 2021) to ~5.0M (2025), but the 2024–2025 cohorts (40% of patients) ran at negative margins — near double-digit losses for new-to-Optum patients from plan exits. For 2026, UNH is deliberately contracting VBC membership ~10% and exiting 40%+ of its PPO footprint.
6
The Change Healthcare cyberattack cost $3.09B in 2024. Discovered February 21, 2024, it was the largest healthcare data breach in U.S. history. Initial impact estimates of $1.15–$1.35/share more than doubled to $2.60/share. Beyond direct costs, the attack disrupted care management, contributed to provider coding upshifts, and delayed OptumInsight recovery — with an additional ~$800M residual charge in 2025.
7
2026 is a deliberate reset: membership contraction, margin repair. UNH guides 2.3–2.8M total membership contraction for 2026 (MA –1.3–1.4M, commercial –1.3–1.4M, Medicaid –565–715K). Revenue declines to ~$440B from $448B. The focus shifts entirely to margin recovery: 20–90 bps expansion across all segments, double-digit earnings growth at UHC, and the beginning of OptumHealth’s path back toward 6%–8% margins.
8
AI investment scaled from zero to $1.5B in 3 years. AI wasn’t mentioned in 2022 calls. By 2026, UNH invests $1.5B annually, with 80%+ of member calls leveraging AI tools and nearly $1B in AI-enabled cost reductions at UHC. EMR consolidation from 18 systems to 3 platforms enables faster AI deployment. New AI products (Optum Real, Integrity One, Crimson AI) represent a strategic pivot from services to platforms.
9
Operating cost ratio is the one consistent bright spot. OCR improved from 14.7% (2022–2023) to 13.2% (2024) and is guided to 12.8% for 2026. The 150 bps improvement in 2024 was roughly half from portfolio actions and half from genuine operating efficiencies. This has been the one metric with an unbroken positive trajectory through the entire period.
10
Leadership reset signals cultural transformation. Brian Thompson’s murder (December 2024) and Stephen Hemsley’s return as CEO (mid-2025, replacing Andrew Witty) marked a fundamental shift. Hemsley reinstated monthly business reviews, mandated in-office work, launched independent expert reviews, and committed to public reporting of PA/claim approval rates and rebate practices — a transparency agenda unprecedented for UNH.
11
The 13–16% EPS growth target has never been revised. Despite EPS falling 41% in 2025, management reaffirmed the long-term 13–16% growth target on every call for 4 years. Actual delivery: +17% (2022), +13% (2023), +10% (2024), –41% (2025), +8.6%+ (2026 guide). Management expects “solid but moderate” growth in 2026 with “earnings growth outlook strengthening quickly” in 2027.
12
Surest represents a genuine product innovation success. UNH’s no-deductible, pre-service transparency commercial product went from 1 in 25 national accounts (2022) to 1 in 6 (2024), expanding to 30+ states with strong 2027 pipeline. Even as overall commercial membership contracts in 2026, self-funded and Surest growth remain robust, suggesting the innovation model works despite broader industry headwinds.

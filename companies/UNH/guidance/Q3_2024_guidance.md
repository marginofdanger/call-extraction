- **OptumRx Prescriptions:** 1.6 billion for full year

### Q3 2024 Care Patterns

- Three specific pressures persisted at higher levels than expected:
  1. **Provider coding intensity upshift:** Still pronounced; some entities with >20% increases in coding intensity factors; "notably and persistently aggressive"; few large systems driving it; actively addressing
  2. **Medicaid rate-acuity timing mismatch:** More pronounced through the period than anticipated; states use care activity data >1 year old; lagging data plus annual rate cycle timing = rates remain well short of current care activity
  3. **Specialty Rx acceleration:** Rapid acceleration in prescribing of high-cost specialty medications (cardiovascular, autoimmune, cancer); IRA eliminated individual coinsurance in catastrophic coverage phase; drug manufacturer campaigns pulled activity forward from 2025 into 2024
- All three factors roughly equivalent in Q3 impact vs expectations
- Specialty Rx and Medicaid pressures were more pronounced in Q3 than anticipated at Q2
- Commercial business NOT affected by these three government-program pressures; commercial trend driven by provider unit cost, pharmacy cost, behavioral access

## Gold Card Prior Authorization Program

- First-of-its-kind national Gold Card program launched October 2024
- Reduces prior authorizations by 500,000 per year for qualified in-network providers
- Intended to improve quality and affordability of care while reducing system friction

## Provider Coding Intensity

- Persisted beyond Q2 expectations; not related solely to cyber waivers as initially believed
- Some entities have upshifted coding intensity factors by >20%
- Higher mix of inpatient vs observation billing
- Few large systems driving the extreme behavior
- Actively addressing through utilization management
- Difference between inpatient and outpatient stays largely borne by consumers
- Awareness incorporated into 2025 MA bid

## Specialty Rx / IRA Impact

- Rapid acceleration in prescribing of high-cost specialty medications
- Primarily cardiovascular disease, autoimmune disorders, cancer treatments
- IRA eliminated individual coinsurance during catastrophic coverage phase
- More people enter catastrophic phase in H2; drug manufacturer campaigns accelerated adoption
- Had anticipated this becoming more meaningful in 2025; pull-forward was unexpected
- 2025 MA bids adequately priced for expected IRA-driven utilization (had planned for step-ups)

## OptumHealth Portfolio Refinement

- Refining portfolio to focus on care delivery and VBC-aligned businesses
- Exiting legacy contracts and businesses that don't align with VBC strategy
- Q3 sequential revenue decline primarily from these refinements
- ~$100B annualized business built over past decade
- 80% of members engaged YTD; 90% for highest-risk members
- ER safe discharge program in 8 markets; avoiding hundreds of inpatient stays monthly
- Post-discharge visits reducing readmissions 10% in mature markets

## OptumInsight / Change Healthcare Recovery

- Non-Change business performing well; outperforming in growth year-over-year
- Change recovery: systems restored; now focused on reconnecting customers
- Customer volumes not returning to pre-attack levels; customers seeking vendor redundancy
- Business disruption reduced quarter-over-quarter ($0.12/share vs $0.28 in Q2)
- Carry-over impact into 2025 estimated at roughly half of 2024 level
- AI-fueled innovation: payment integrity, revenue cycle products being transformed
- Core OptumInsight (~85% of business) feeling very confident going into 2025

## Value-Based Care Strategy

- Over 20-year bipartisan consensus across 4 presidential administrations
- VBC patients: better cancer screenings, diabetes/hypertension control; 10% less likely to visit ER or be readmitted
- Rising commercial employer interest in VBC models (early stage)
- Increased payer outreach in dynamic rate environment; productive discussions on funding and market planning
- Over 100 plan partners; diverse growth across MA, D-SNP, commercial, Medicaid
- OptumServe: new medical disability exam products; leading position for federal agencies

## AI & Technology

- Advanced practice clinicians using AI for patient history summarization; saving hundreds of hours
- Nurses using generative AI for documentation review
- AI powering tens of millions of consumer interactions and provider searches
- Higher NPS scores from AI-assisted consumer advocacy
- AI helping build software; enhancing speed and quality of technology modernization
- Payment integrity as front runner for business process reimagination

## Cost Management & V28 Response

- Relentless cost reduction as key response to V28 "price cut"
- Optum doing remarkable job of cost takeout; more to go
- SG&A improvements permanent and structural
- Focus areas: MEDEX management (waste elimination) and internal cost reduction
- Hospital unit costs: unusually aggressive asks; looking for new compact / new ways of working
- Drug prices: advocating for list price reductions; biosimilar adoption; Weight Engage wraparound programs

## Capital Deployment

- Oriented around 5 growth pillars: benefits, VBC, technology, pharmacy, financial services
- VBC as organizing principle binding all together
- Consumerization as cross-cutting priority
- Expect steady capital deployment agenda similar to past years
- First priority: building new capabilities for next-decade returns
- Then: share repurchase and dividends

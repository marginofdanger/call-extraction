- **OptumRx Non-PBM Revenue:** ~50% of OptumRx revenues
- **Consumer Innovation:** No-deductible commercial offerings among fastest growing; members paying ~50% less OOP; employers saving ~11% average

- Full year MCR expected toward upper end of initial 82.6% +/- 50 bps range

## GLP-1 / Weight Management Drugs

- GLP-1 spend: 80%+ on diabetic side; up to ~20% weight loss
- Weight loss performing in line with 2023 plan; confident for 2024 outlook
- Vast majority of coverage in fee-based (self-funded) business; ~30% of book
- Employers "mixed bag" on future coverage: some seeking coverage despite price concerns, some backing off
- Core issue: U.S. list prices ~10x Western European levels
- Pushing manufacturers for value-based constructs: pricing tied to outcomes, adherence levels, utilization risk
- Want pairing with therapies/programs to reduce lifelong adherence dependency
- Too early to see medical cost offsets from weight loss indication
- No directional view on whether more or less coverage ahead

## OptumHealth Margin Dynamics

- Three margin pressure sources persist from Q2: elevated senior MLR, behavioral costs, complex patient growth/mix
- Mix different than expected: more complex patients, different geographic distribution
- Takes time to build engagement capabilities for complex populations
- Many patients previously underserved; can't easily access clinics; home care build-out critical
- Confident in continued margin strengthening into 2024-2027
- "Make no mistake, Optum Health is having a very strong growth year"
- Reengineering cost base across entire organization in response to CMS pricing changes

## OptumInsight / Change Healthcare Integration

- Integration "gone really well"; Q3 had tail end of integration spend
- Q3 margin in line with expectations
- Longer-term margin target: 18%–22% (driven by software/services mix)
- Portfolio: clinical decision support, admin efficiency, payment optimization
- Health system partnerships: 9 out of 10 expanded from initial scope; growing portfolio
- New partnership: revenue cycle, analytics, IT services for Midwest health system (400,000+ people)
- Roger Connor named new CEO of OptumInsight

## Commercial Growth & Selling Season

- National accounts fee-based segment: settled business with strong growth
- Fully insured business: pricing/negotiation for January underway; comfortable with materialization
- Employers focused on affordability and innovation
- No-deductible offerings resonating strongly; among fastest-growing products
- Mobile platform continuing to add capabilities: on-demand care access, cost estimation, rewards

## Medicaid Redeterminations

- Significant disenrollment for procedural reasons; high variability across states
- Some states suspending terminations or reenrolling members based on guidance; "stops and starts" impact
- Re-enrollment rates: 15%–20% depending on state
- Comprehensive outreach: speaking with thousands of consumers daily
- States taking thoughtful, data-driven approach to rate setting
- ~45% of 2024 Medicaid revenue visibility
- Membership outlook in line with beginning-of-year expectations

## PBM Strategy & Transparency

- Recent survey: ~90% of clients satisfied with their PBM and transparency levels
- Optum Rx committed to continued innovation, comprehensive/integrated/transparent solutions
- Price Edge tool: millions of consumers accessing for lowest-cost medication at point of care
- Specialty and infusion growing double digits; community pharmacies expanding
- 2024 selling season: strong client additions continuing

## Wage Inflation & Provider Costs

- Seeing some upward pressure on unit costs related to wages
- Three-year contracts provide some cushion/muting
- Planned for and priced into outlook
- UNH not having trouble recruiting nurses, clinicians; people want to work for the mission
- Focused on labor capacity to manage growing risk portfolio

## AI & Technology Initiatives

- AI/NLP to expedite call documentation and generate accurate consumer interaction summaries
- Saving millions in administrative work; freeing capacity for higher-value engagement
- Using technology to translate/interpret unstructured data (physician notes) for life sciences insights
- Hundreds of AI applications powered by OptumInsight actively in development, testing, deployment
- Focus: elevating consumer and care provider experience while driving quality and lower costs

## Value-Based Care Strategy

- 2023 investments framed as preparation for 3-year CMS rate phase-in (2024-2026)
- Reengineering cost base, refocusing benefit strategies, strengthening affordability management
- Building consumer engagement, technology, digital-first capabilities
- Doubling down on VBC commitment
- VBC patients served across 100+ payer partners (national and regional)
- Growth diverse across MA, D-SNP, commercial, Medicaid

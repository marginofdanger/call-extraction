### Q1 2023 Care Patterns

- Largely consistent with recent trends
- Inpatient: reflects long-term shift to ambulatory; nearly 2/3 of orthopedic procedures now outpatient (vs <1/4 five years ago)
- Physician office: trending toward historic levels
- Pediatrics: remains lower
- Emergency room: modestly lower than historical; consumers more comfortable with virtual and walk-in care
- Cancer and cardiac screenings: roughly pre-pandemic levels
- No indications of increasing acuity emerging

## Medicare Advantage Rate Changes / V28 Risk Model

- 2024 MA notice provides greater short- to midterm clarity
- CMS phasing in risk adjustment model changes over 3 years; "we really appreciate CMS' decision to phase-in"
- Concerns about unintended consequences for diabetes, complex behavioral needs diagnosis/support
- Phase-in allows time to minimize beneficiary impacts using multiple levers including cost management
- Intend to grow again in 2024; expect marketplace to continue growing
- Not changing the 13%–16% EPS growth target; confirmed "firmly of the view" that target holds
- Competitive dynamics: "getting close to a bid cycle" and not sharing specific response strategies

## Prior Authorization Changes

- Beginning Q3 2023: eliminating ~20% of authorization volumes overall
- Eliminating most medical authorizations for high-quality provider groups; ~10% additional volume reduction
- Driven by intensifying focus on consumer experience and streamlining processes
- Leveraging new analytics in partnership with OptumInsight for surveillance capabilities
- Do not anticipate any pressure on trend as a result
- Change Healthcare network connectivity expected to further reduce friction

## GLP-1 / Weight Loss Drugs

- Increased trend in GLP-1s; overwhelming majority in diabetic care
- Weight loss use: low single digits
- On-label usage well managed with authorization requirements
- Pharmacy is ~20% of overall spend; any one therapeutic class <1%
- Trends consistent with plan assumptions for 2023

## PBM / OptumRx Strategy

- Responding to Washington scrutiny of PBM industry
- OptumRx leads in transparency; overwhelming majority of rebates passed back to payers
- Committed to lowest net cost regardless of mechanism (rebate or list price reduction)
- Record selling season; one of highest retention and new client levels in recent years
- Specialty NPS up nearly 10 points YoY
- Biosimilar strategy: up to 3 biosimilars at parity with originator on formulary
- Price Edge tool: scans market and compares cash pricing with member real-time out-of-pocket cost
- Behavioral health pharmacy launched (distinctive offering)

## Home-Based Care Expansion / LHC Group

- LHC Group combination completed; expands in-home capabilities
- LHC provides home health, hospice, post-acute care; 12 million+ patient encounters/year
- Four home-based care capabilities:
  1. Patient assessments: 2.5 million+ in-home visits expected in 2023
  2. Care transitions: managing nearly 12 million transitions (2x vs 3 years ago)
  3. Senior community care: clinical care in skilled nursing, assisted living, senior housing
  4. Individual care coordination for D-SNP/chronic special needs patients: NPS ~80; 99% in 4-star+ plans; 15%+ reduction in hospitalizations; average patient managing 9 chronic conditions

## Value-Based Care Diversification

- VBC model expanding beyond Medicare into commercial and Medicaid
- Commercial VBC in Texas, California, Massachusetts today; meaningful part of 4 million capitated lives
- Growing with multiple national and regional payers, not just UHC
- Comprehensive clinical model of care appealing to all large national payers
- Large fraction of 700,000 Q1 transfers are UHC but substantial non-UHC delegation growing
- ~10,000+ clinicians added last year; expect similar this year

## EMIS Acquisition (UK)

- CMA phase 2 investigation underway; cannot comment on regulatory specifics
- EMIS connects to ~40% of British primary care providers
- Strategic rationale: complement Optum software/data capabilities for primary care
- Potential node of innovation for non-U.S. health care systems
- Optum has had UK business for many years

## Medicaid Redeterminations

- Process resumed in 20+ states
- Working with state partners and members to provide information and support
- Live outreach calls; extended employer enrollment periods; coverage awareness
- Many will qualify for alternative plans, some with $0 monthly premium
- Expect to be serving more people in benefit programs when process completes
- UHC executing detailed plans for uninterrupted coverage access

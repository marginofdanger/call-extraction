### Q2 2022 Membership & Growth

- **Medicare Advantage:** On track to add up to 800,000 people in 2022; ~3/4 individual and group MA, remainder D-SNP; 7th consecutive year of share gaining growth
- **D-SNP:** NPS of 80 for 1.5 million people served
- **Medicaid:** Grew by 180,000 in Q2; redetermination impact expected in 2023
- **Domestic Commercial:** Added 80,000 new people in Q2; fully-insured grew 60,000 from Q1; 250,000+ more people served over past 12 months
- **90%** of individual/family plan growth chose plans featuring virtual visits
- **Plan Satisfaction:** Up 450 bps over past 5 years, nearly 2x industry rate
- **3.4 million** additional seniors chose UHC plans over past 5 years
- **Physician Growth:** Tracking toward adding 10,000 physicians and advanced practitioners during year (including Atrius, Kelsey-Seybold)
- **Community Pharmacies:** On track for ~700 by year-end; 7,000+ pharmacists
- **Digital Engagement:** +170% among Medicare members over last couple years
- **ASC High-Acuity Procedures:** Nearly tripled vs 2 years ago (joint, spine, cardiovascular); NPS consistently >90

### Q2 2022 Care Patterns

- COVID Q2 inpatient admissions ~1/3 of Q1 levels
- Non-COVID utilization not returning as quickly with lower COVID care
- Annual wellness visits among Medicare patients: back to pre-pandemic baseline
- First fill prescriptions: trending above baseline
- Colonoscopies: back to baseline
- Pediatric and ER visits: still below historical levels
- Rising COVID hospitalizations in recent weeks, lower average length of stay
- No patterns indicating shifting acuity from deferred care

## Value-Based Care Clinical Outcomes

- MA patients in VBC with Optum Care physicians: wellness check adherence 5 points higher than Medicare FFS; nearly 20% lower hospitalization rate
- COPD patients in VBC: 80% higher medication adherence vs Medicare FFS; ~60% fewer respiratory complications
- Medicare Advantage: people spend ~40% less out of pocket vs FFS (~$2,000/year savings)
- MA plans: up to 30% more resources to primary care vs FFS; up to 50% more preventative screening/testing

## $0 Copay Initiative (2023)

- Starting 2023, $0 copay / $0 out-of-pocket for critical medicines on preferred drug list for UHC group fully insured members
- Includes: insulin, epinephrine (severe allergic reactions), albuterol (acute asthma)
- Builds on prior point-of-sale discount actions

## Acquisitions & Capital Deployment

- Atrius Health and Kelsey-Seybold: brought into Optum Health; both have existing value-based care capabilities; multi-year conversation/pipeline process typical
- LHC Group: acquisition pending; CMS proposed home health rate cut for 2023
- Optum in "record selling season" across all businesses in H1 2022
- Specialty tools delivered >$1B value over last 2.5 years

## Inflation & Consumer Impact

- Respectful of provider labor cost pressures; more impact in 2023 than 2022 as contracts renew
- Consumer responses: increased virtual care use, decreased ER use
- Bind product puts consumer in driver's seat with pre-service guaranteed costs
- Innovation focus: virtual care, behavioral health, consumer transparency, high-performing networks

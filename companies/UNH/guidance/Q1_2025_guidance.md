### Q1 2025 Membership & Growth

- **Commercial Self-Funded:** +700,000 in Q1; strong product innovation; early 2026 selling season showing strong retention
- **Commercial Insured:** Some attrition from disciplined exchange pricing
- **Medicaid:** 7.6 million people served; growth with expansions in Kentucky, New York, Florida
- **VBC Patients:** On track for 650,000 net new; 5.4 million by year-end; >50% of all members engaged, 75% of complex members engaged in Q1

### Q1 2025 Care Activity & MCR Dynamics

- **MA Care Activity:** Increased at 2x expected rate vs 2024 (had expected similar to 2024 trend)
- **Units consumed** are ~1/3 of total Medicare trend drivers; the unit consumption component doubled in Q1 2025
- **Most pronounced:** Physician and outpatient services; inpatient to a lesser extent
- **Limited to senior MA business:** NOT seen in commercial, Medicaid, D-SNP, dually eligible, or new members
- **Group MA:** Disproportionate increase in public sector retirees; premiums went from ~$50 to ~$200 due to Medicare funding cuts; 98% retention provides same-member metric; significant increases in elective care
- **Individual/Community MA:** Higher wellness visits and in-home clinical assessments driving follow-on specialist/outpatient utilization
- **IRA Part D Impact:** ~90 bps MCR impact in Q1 from Part D seasonality changes; tracking as expected
- **V28 Year 2 Impact:** ~60 bps MCR impact in Q1
- **2024 Pressures (Resolved):** Provider upcoding and specialty drug pressures from 2024 tracking in line with plan; not a factor in Q1 2025
- **Assumption:** Elevated care activity persists through 2025 AND into 2026 pricing
- **No onetime favorable items in Q1:** No prior period development or other "good guys"

### Q1 2025 OptumHealth Patient Profile Issues

- **New patients from plan exits:** Markets with meaningful plan exits (V28-driven); patients not engaged by prior plans for 6-8 months; reimbursement levels well below expected/normal; multi-payer geographies (e.g., Texas, Washington)
- **V28 risk model execution:** Higher-acuity patient population more impacted by risk model change; running two distinct model versions concurrently is operationally complex; execution below standards
- **Revenue reduction:** >$10B revenue change at OptumHealth; >half from transitioning legacy risk-based arrangements to fee-based (neutral to earnings)
- **Actions taken:** Enhancing PCP access for new patients; expanding home-based visits and wraparound services; accelerated EMR unification; smarter clinical workflows and point-of-care tools for V28 adaptation

## 2026 Rate Environment & Outlook

- 2026 MA rate notice reflects accelerating care cost trends (positive signal; first time in years rates begin to reflect reality)
- V28 year 3 final phase-in still a factor, but represents end of transition period
- Management assumes current elevated care activity persists into 2026 pricing
- At this distance, can accommodate current care activity levels and return to historical planning target MA margin levels for 2026
- Expect ramp into reacquiring target growth rate momentum in 2026
- Q1 2025 insights provide ample time for 2026 planning

## PBM Reform & Drug Pricing

- 100% commercial rebate pass-through implemented (first large PBM)
- Removing prior authorizations on 80 drugs (>10% of pharmaceutical PAs)
- Removing 25% of prior authorization categories
- Cost-based reimbursement for pharmacies: all pharmacies, all drugs, all clients (rolling out)
- Independent and community pharmacies expressed support for changes
- President's executive order encouraging — reviews whole pharmacy value chain, not just PBMs
- Drug manufacturer pricing: common procedures 4x more expensive in U.S. than Germany/Australia/UK; hip replacements 2x as much
- Arkansas PBM/pharmacy ownership legislation: significant patient access concern (mental health, specialty, home infusion, home delivery); working with state on regulatory process

## Medicaid Update

- 7.6 million people served; growth with expansions in KY, NY, FL
- 32 states, variety of programs and products
- Rate-acuity gap narrowing with each rate cycle plus off-cycle adjustments
- H2 2024 progress continued into 1/1 rate cycle
- 7/1 cycle: 35% of revenue; too early to call but base data increasingly reflects recent experience
- Optimistic gap will continue to narrow over course of year
- Funding remains insufficient to meet full health needs of patients
- Confident in managed care value proposition regardless of federal policy changes

## Tariff Exposure

- Dynamic situation; don't know what may happen
- Feel well-positioned due to: preexisting contract price protections, IRA manufacturer inflation penalties on Medicare, Medicaid best price protections, OptumRx contractual price protections
- Multiple layers of protection
- Not a significant exposure for 2025
- Will work thoughtfully on 2026 bids if tariffs materialize

## AI & Technology

- 26 million consumer calls directed by AI agent in Q1; improving experience and reducing wait times
- Expect AI to direct >50% of all calls during 2025
- Sparq tool: 40% increase in digital engagement among seniors
- AI-powered claims efficiency tools: 20%+ productivity increase for RCM customers at OptumInsight
- Long runway for further technology advances driving sustained operating efficiency
- Focus on operating cost containment to mitigate external pressures

## Value-Based Care Strategy

- Older VBC cohorts (2023 and earlier) outperforming on basically all metrics
- Current challenges are NOT a challenge to VBC underlying principle; driven by V28 price cuts (~9% across industry)
- Second-order effects of V28: group premium increases driving utilization changes, plan exits creating disengaged patient populations
- VBC delivers: more healthy years, less acute treatment, early detection, lower total cost of care
- MA costs less than traditional Medicare; VBC managed clinic saves even more
- High patient retention in VBC; work done today supports 2026 growth
- Still committed to membership growth but pacing specific geographies to ensure PCP network and in-home service engagement

### Q1 2022 Membership & Growth

- **Medicare Advantage:** On track to add up to 800,000 people in 2022; ~3/4 in individual and group MA, remainder in dual special needs plans
- **Medicaid:** Grew by 150,000+, approaching 8 million total
- **Commercial:** 350,000 more people in innovative offerings (physician-led, virtual-first) over past year
- **OptumHealth Value-Based Patients:** Adding 1 million+ more people; 30% more patients in value-based relationships YoY
- **OptumRx:** Providing 20 million+ more prescriptions YoY
- **Total Health Benefits:** Serving 1.5 million more people YoY
- **Nearly 90%** of newly enrolled individual exchange members selected plans with significant virtual components
- **Nearly 30%** selected a virtual-first offering

### Q1 2022 Care Patterns & COVID

- **January COVID Hospitalizations:** ~40,000 (highest month since pandemic onset)
- **March COVID Hospitalizations:** ~2,000
- Overall care about at baseline levels; pockets modestly below (ER, pediatric visits)
- No signs of increasing acuity from deferred pandemic care; oncology diagnosis levels consistent with historical averages
- Commercial utilization a little higher; government programs a little lower
- Omicron hospitalizations about half of Delta levels

## Value-Based Care Expansion

- Now expect 600,000 patients added under value-based arrangements in 2022 (raised from 500,000)
- 1/3 of value-based growth in dual special needs (D-SNP) population
- In Ohio and New York, skilled nursing facility admits declining 25% in first quarter of operation
- "Lot of runway left" for value-based care expansion — now includes duals, complex care, home
- Highest retention, satisfaction, lowest trend, best benefits/quality/growth when UHC partners with Optum

## LHC Group Acquisition (Pending)

- Home health care company, founded 1994 in Louisiana
- 85% of LHC providers are 4-star or better quality rating
- Quality of care 33% higher in Stars quality ratings than national average for home health care
- Will reinforce in-home and ambulatory care delivery capabilities
- Complements naviHealth, Landmark, and original Optima Home product

## Change Healthcare Acquisition (Pending)

- Health IT company; will connect and simplify clinical, administrative, and payment processes
- Extended agreement reflects "firm belief in potential benefits"
- Transaction not yet closed as of call date

## Inflation & Cost Management

- Response to inflation is "innovation" — productivity, virtual care, value-based care
- Virtual products 15% lower than other prevailing products in those markets
- Targeted investments in clinicians and customer service staff where higher attrition
- Provider agreements offer some multiyear predictability
- Specialty drug costs lowered by over 15% through Optum analytical capabilities

## Investment Portfolio & Interest Rate Sensitivity

- ~$70B in cash and investments total
- ~40% ($28B) tied to floating rates; ~60% in fixed rate
- ~$10B of floating rate liabilities (swaps, commercial paper)
- 100 bps increase: ~$280M impact on investment income, ~$100M offset from floating liabilities = ~$180M net benefit
- Fixed rate portfolio: ~$5B/year rolling off and reinvested at higher rates

## Digital & Operational Efficiency

- Provider support costs declined ~12% from historical averages
- 38% increase in providers using digital documents instead of paper (vs prior year)
- Expect to save 80 tons of paper over next 5 years
- In-home testing reaching 1 million members suspected underdiagnosed; 35% completion rates

Key Insight: AXON 2.0 was the single most important event in AppLovin’s history. 13 Key Takeaways

1
13/13 EBITDA beats, averaging +11.7% vs midpoint. AppLovin has beaten EBITDA guidance every quarter since quarterly guidance began (Q4 2022). The average beat of +11.7% vs midpoint is the most systematically conservative guidance in this dataset. Revenue beat rate is 13/13 vs midpoint (+5.5% average).
2
AXON 2.0 was the defining inflection. Launched mid-Q2 2023, AXON 2.0 produced a step-function improvement: Q3 2023 EBITDA of $490M crushed the $360M high end by $130M (+36%). This single quarter revealed the AI model’s transformative power and kicked off nine consecutive quarters of accelerating growth.
3
Advertising revenue 5.4× in 12 quarters. From $306M (Q4 2022) to $1,660M (Q4 2025), advertising revenue grew 5.4× while margins expanded from 61% to 84%. This simultaneous growth-margin expansion is exceptionally rare — it reflects a self-improving AI model that drives revenue without proportional cost increases.
4
E-commerce from zero to $1B run rate in five quarters. First mentioned in Q1 2024, web/e-commerce advertising reached ~$1B run rate by Q2 2025 with 600+ advertisers and <0.1% market penetration. This TAM expansion is in its earliest stages and is excluded from the 20–30% long-term growth target.
5
Apps divestiture completed the strategic pivot. Apps revenue declined from ~$458M/quarter (Q2 2022) to divestiture at ~$900M total value (Q2 2025). The segment’s 15–19% margin dragged on the 78–84% advertising margins. Divestiture transformed AppLovin into a pure-play AI advertising company.
6
FY 2025: $5.48B revenue (+70%), $4.51B EBITDA (+87%), 82% margin. These are among the highest margins for any public company at this scale. EBITDA per advertising employee reached ~$4M run rate — reflecting the leverage inherent in an AI-driven, capital-light model.
7
FCF conversion improved from ~50% to ~80%. FY 2025 FCF of $3.95B represented ~79% of ad revenue and ~88% of EBITDA. The conversion target was raised three times: 50–60% → 65–75% → ~70% → consistently exceeding 75%. Cash grew to $2.5B by year-end.
8
Self-serve platform is the key catalyst. Launched in referral-only mode (Oct 2025), the self-serve platform showed 57% qualified-lead-to-go-live conversion and ~50% WoW spend growth from early adopters. GA launch is planned for H1 2026. Management explicitly excludes self-serve revenue from guidance, meaning any adoption is pure upside.
9
AXON self-learning compounds at 3–5% per quarter. The AI model improves autonomously without human intervention, generating compounding returns. This organic improvement alone supports the 20–30% annual growth target — actual growth of 70% in FY 2025 suggests massive upside from e-commerce and self-serve layered on top.
10
Guidance structure conceals the beat. By providing narrow ±$15–20M ranges that embed only organic model improvement (not e-commerce, not self-serve, not step-function model upgrades), AppLovin systematically understates its trajectory. The Q3 2023 (+40% EBITDA beat) and Q1 2025 (+14.9% EBITDA beat) demonstrate how guidance excludes known upside vectors.

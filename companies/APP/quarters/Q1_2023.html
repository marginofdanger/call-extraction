Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2023)

Commentary on Q2 2023 Outlook

  • Herald Chen: Anticipating continuing growth from Software Platform business, offset to some degree by the Apps business
  • AXON 2.0 rollout will be a key factor in the quarter
  • Free cash flow expected to be approximately 50%-60% of Adjusted EBITDA on a normal run rate basis (noting Q1 was elevated by delayed customer payments and lower cash taxes)
  • Adam Foroughi: Q1 guidance confidence reflected in AXON 2 staged rollout; early results are encouraging

Full Year 2023 Guidance

  • No formal FY 2023 guidance issued (per policy change from Q3 2022: one-quarter-ahead guidance only)
  • Management declined to provide specific full-year targets but expressed confidence in Software Platform growth trajectory
  • Ralph Schackart asked if anything would preclude the current growth rate persisting through 2023; Adam Foroughi pointed to AXON 2 as a potential catalyst but did not give numerical guidance

  • Revenue Flow-Through: Technology-driven lifts to Software Platform revenue have very high flow-through to EBITDA (Herald Chen: "a significant amount of that revenue does flow through to the bottom line...a very high percentage increase in the margin as well")

Apps Segment

Free Cash Flow Conversion

  • Normal Run Rate: FCF expected to be approximately 50%-60% of Adjusted EBITDA on a normalized basis (may deviate in any particular quarter)

New Business Initiatives (3-Year Horizon)

  • Adam Foroughi on Array (Carriers/OEMs) and CTV/Wurl: "new businesses that we're going after that are related to our core business aren't going to move the needle on a $1 billion-plus software business in the short term. These are businesses that we expect when we're talking to you all in 3 years, we're talking about big parts of our business"

AXON 2.0 / Core Technology Upgrade

  • Q1 2023 Impact: Zero — AXON 2.0 had NOT been rolled out at all during Q1 2023; Q1 performance was driven entirely by AXON 1.0 improvements and advertiser confidence
  • Current Status (as of call date May 10): In the midst of a staged rollout of AXON 2.0 (post-Q1)
  • Deployment Approach Change: Now described as "staged rollout" (vs. Q4 2022 call where Foroughi described it as impacting "the whole business at once when it does go to full scale")
  • Expected Financial Impact: Difficult to predict precise timing; Herald Chen: "we do believe that there will be a significant impact on the software line. When it hits is difficult to predict"
  • Margin Uplift: Herald Chen: "given the software margins, we do think a significant amount of that revenue does flow through to the bottom line. So it will be a very high percentage increase in the margin as well"
  • Q2 2023 Guidance Incorporates AXON 2.0: "The impact of the AXON 2.0 rollout will be a key factor in the quarter" — Herald Chen
  • Technology Comparison: Adam Foroughi analogized upgrade from AXON 1 to AXON 2 as similar to OpenAI moving from ChatGPT-3 to ChatGPT-4; models are continuously improvable
  • Long-Term Roadmap: AXON 2 improvements will compound over time; incremental gains ongoing; then AXON 3, AXON 4, AXON 5 in future — "this core expertise is going to drive our business for a very, very long time"

Connected TV (CTV) / Wurl Strategy

  • Near-Term Financial Impact: "we don't expect this to have a material financial impact in the near term" — Adam Foroughi
  • Status: Early, early stage; AppDiscovery solution plugging into Wurl's SSP for CTV inventory
  • Product Positioning: Performance-based CTV advertising for mobile app developers — described as unprecedented at scale in CTV on a performance basis
  • Advertiser Response: "The advertisers that are leaning into it are leaning into it and very excited by the potential of us scaling that offering"
  • Wurl Role: CDN-like for media companies; helps bring content online, schedule content, build channels across connected TV devices; also has SSP business
  • Data Signal: "excited about the early data we are seeing" — management sees promise but not ready to quantify

Array (Carrier/OEM Marketing Platform)

  • Status: Still in early stages; not material to financial results
  • Strategic Rationale: Extending core marketing technology expertise (market-leading in mobile gaming) to the nascent carrier/OEM space
  • Differentiation: Bringing new products to carriers/OEMs beyond traditional marketing, expected to make material impact to their businesses
  • Timeline: Long-term investment; "we expect when we're talking to you all in 3 years, we're talking about big parts of our business. But this is not a short-term bet"

Apps Segment Portfolio Update

  • Optimization Status: Substantially complete — cost optimization, earn-out renegotiation, closure/divestiture of underperforming studios finished
  • Remaining Studios: ~11 studios invested against, each with new game launch plans
  • Q1 Revenue Decline Breakdown: ~Half of the QoQ decline from studio closures/divestitures; remainder from ad trend softness and seasonality coming off Q4
  • New Game Launches: Several games launched in Q1 (increased UA spend); every studio has a game plan to launch new games going forward
  • Margin Impact of Launches: Herald Chen: "if there's a whole bolus of games that we thought were really awesome, that margin could come down. I think it will still be a double-digit number"

Google Partnership and Open Bidding

  • Google Open Bidding/RTB: Google now leaning into external mediation solutions for the first time; open to all publishers in the ecosystem
  • MAX Positioning: "MAX, obviously, is the majority share of mediation in gaming" — can extend Google's demand efficiently to bulk of mobile gaming publishers
  • RTB Penetration on MAX: Real-time bidding already the majority of the platform; Google's entry expected to push toward "vast majority"
  • Ecosystem Impact: Expected to create upside for the whole market by bringing Google demand more efficiently; not zero-sum

Competitive Environment

Unity/ironSource Merger

  • Adam Foroughi: "bringing 2 big companies together and integration takes time. It's difficult"
  • Not commenting on peers directly; ecosystem requires all marketing companies to do well for mutual growth
  • AppLovin products described as "very, very sticky"

Market Share vs. Market Growth

  • Adam Foroughi on Q1 software platform growth: "I don't think the market grew 20% quarter-over-quarter. Much more so efficiency gains in technology, our advertisers investing more"
  • "We don't really think about this market as zero sum...our improved efficiency creates gains in the market for all of our partners"

Macro and Industry Commentary

  • Mobile Ad Market: "Partial stabilization" — more confidence in ecosystem; developers returning to growth mindset; reaching the trough point
  • Mobile Gaming Resilience: "always believed it would be stable even in weaker economic times. We're seeing just that"
  • Advertiser Behavior: Advertisers seeing improved performance on platform, returning to more confident spending; growth mindset returning after difficult 2022

KPI Reporting Changes (Now Effective)

  • Metrics Discontinued (starting Q1 2023): 1. Total Software Transaction Value (TSTV) 2. Software Platform Enterprise Clients (SPECs) 3. Revenue per Software Platform Enterprise Client (Revenue per SPEC)
  • Continuing Metrics: Revenue and Adjusted EBITDA by segment, MAPs, ARPMAP, Free Cash Flow

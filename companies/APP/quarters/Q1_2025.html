Commentary on Q2 2025 Guidance

  • Seasonality: Q2 is historically the weakest seasonal quarter for AppLovin's advertising business; CEO: "Q2, you actually don't have almost anything as only the tail end gets into summer"; last year Q2 was the only single-digit sequential growth quarter
  • Conservative Posture Continues: 3-5% QoQ sequential growth embedded in guidance represents the "reinforcement learning" baseline only; does not assume step-function model enhancements; CFO: "that component, we think, is about kind of 3% to 5%, which is how we're guiding on a quarterly basis. That's the component that we know is very stable, very predictable"
  • No Apps Segment Guidance: Apps divestiture to Tripledot expected to close late Q2; guidance excludes any incremental uplift from former studios becoming third-party advertisers; CFO: "within the guidance, we have not assumed any incremental uplift from having those studios be external parties"
  • Guidance Posture vs. Actuals History: Management has beaten the high end of guidance every quarter for at least 6 consecutive quarters. Q1 2025 saw the largest beat yet ($110M+ above high end of advertising revenue guidance). The 3-5% QoQ guidance framework systematically excludes known upside vectors.

Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

Advertising Business Long-Term Growth Algorithm

  • Long-Term YoY Growth Rate: 20% to 30% (reaffirmed); CFO: "we continue to believe that 20% to 30% is the right long-term growth rate for the company"
  • Component 1 -- Reinforcement Learning (Predictable): ~3% to 5% QoQ sequential growth; CFO: "that component, we think, is about kind of 3% to 5%...that's the component that we know is very stable, very predictable"
  • Component 2 -- Directed Model Enhancements (Step-Functions): ~10%+ annual contribution historically; CFO: "Roughly around 10% is what we've kind of seen thus far"; analogy: "models going from ChatGPT-3 to -4"
  • Expectation: At least one step-function model enhancement per year; CFO: "we think that we should be able to do at least one of those step function increases per year going forward. And it's a relatively conservative assumption"

Q2 Guidance Appearing Conservative (Jason Bazinet, Citi)

  • Analyst noted 3-5% implied sequential growth seems below aspiration
  • CEO clarified Q2 is seasonally weakest; prior year Q2 was the only single-digit QoQ growth quarter
  • CEO noted Q1 saw nearly 20% QoQ growth (day-normalized) over Q4
  • Guidance framework intentionally conservative, embedding only reinforcement learning baseline

Key Guidance Notes

  • No Apps segment guidance provided due to pending divestiture to Tripledot
  • No consolidated guidance provided -- pure-play advertising business post-close
  • Q2 is seasonally weakest quarter -- 3-5% QoQ base growth consistent with reinforcement learning component only
  • Guidance does NOT include: (1) Step-function model enhancements; (2) Incremental revenue from divested studios becoming third-party advertisers; (3) International expansion of web advertising; (4) Self-serve dashboard scaling benefits
  • Given persistent beat pattern (6 consecutive quarters, widening magnitude), actual Q2 results likely to materially exceed guidance

Consolidated Results

  • Total Revenue: $1.5B (+40% YoY; CFO: "Total revenue soared 40% from the same period last year to $1.5 billion")
  • Adjusted EBITDA: $1.0B (+83% YoY; CFO: "adjusted EBITDA increased a remarkable 83% to an impressive $1 billion")
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin: 68% (CFO: "achieving a fantastic 68% adjusted EBITDA margin"; +600bps QoQ vs. 62% in Q4 2024)
  • Free Cash Flow: $826M (+113% YoY; +19% QoQ vs. $695M in Q4 2024; CFO: "we generated $826 million in free cash flow, up a staggering 113% year-over-year")
  • FCF-to-EBITDA Flow-Through: 82% (CFO: "representing an impressive 82% flow-through from adjusted EBITDA to free cash flow")
  • Cash and Cash Equivalents (End of Q1): $551M (vs. $741M end of Q4 2024)
  • Shares Outstanding (End of Q1): 338M (vs. 340M end of Q4 2024; net reduction)
  • Adjusted EBITDA Per Employee Run Rate (Advertising): ~$4M annually (CEO: "Our run rate adjusted EBITDA per employee in our advertising business has risen to approximately $4 million annually"; up from ~$3M in Q4 2024)

Advertising Segment

  • Advertising Revenue: $1.16B (CFO: "We generated $1.16 billion in revenue"; vs. $999M in Q4 2024 = +16.1% QoQ; vs. $678M in Q1 2024 = +71.1% YoY)
  • Advertising Adjusted EBITDA: $943M (CFO: "$943 million in adjusted EBITDA")
  • Advertising Adjusted EBITDA Margin: 81% (CFO: "achieving an incredible 81% margin"; vs. 78% in Q4 2024 = +300bps QoQ)
  • QoQ Revenue-to-EBITDA Flow-Through: 104% (CFO: "Quarter-over-quarter flow-through from revenue to adjusted EBITDA was an exceptional 104%"); ~100% after adjusting for nonrecurring costs in prior quarter (CFO: "after adjusting for these costs, our flow-through was still a robust 100% despite the step-up in data center costs")
  • Revenue Growth Drivers: (1) Continued AI/ML model enhancements driving better performance for AppDiscovery advertisers; (2) Full quarter impact of web-based advertising solution; CEO: "the greater than 50% of the quarter's growth came from the gaming side"
  • Key Metric -- Net Revenue Per Install: +49% YoY (analyst-referenced; CFO confirmed inclusive of web-based advertiser revenue but NOT inclusive of web-driven actions/installs in the denominator, inflating the metric)
  • Web Advertising Run Rate: ~$1B (CEO: "I think I said 600-some-odd advertisers in the blog, $1 billion run rate")
  • Web Advertisers: 600+ advertisers onboarded; pace of new additions slowed due to resource constraints (team of ~20 people); CEO: "pace has slowed down because we just don't have the resources"
  • Web Advertiser Churn: Sub-3% churn for advertisers spending run rate $250K+/year (CEO: "For advertisers that spent run rate $250,000 a year, we had sub-3% churn, so very little")
  • Market Penetration (Web): Less than 0.1% of potential market of total advertisers (CEO: "we're currently less than 0.1% of the potential market of total advertisers")
  • Conversion Rate: ~1%+ transaction rate per 1,000 impressions, up from prior disclosure but still very low (CEO: "I think in the past, we've said, look, we're driving a 1% transaction right now. We've grown a lot, so that's probably gone up a little bit")
  • Ad Spend on Platform Since AXON 2: Roughly quadrupled (CEO: "ad spend on the platform has roughly quadrupled since we rolled out AXON 2")
  • Headcount: E-commerce go-to-market team is still ~20 people

Margin Profile

  • Data Center Cost Growth: ~10% of overall revenue growth added annually; CFO: "we've been adding on data center costs on an annual basis at least around kind of a 10% of the overall revenue growth, and we expect that, that should continue"
  • Other Costs Largely Fixed: CFO: "the primary components of the cost base should remain relatively flat with obviously the exception of data center costs, which are more variable"
  • Margin Trajectory: Advertising EBITDA margin expected to continue expanding from current 81% toward an asymptote; CFO: "the margin profile of the business should continue to grow from where we're at today on the Advertising business up to that kind of asymptote"
  • Lean Operating Model: CEO: "Our lean team continues to impress, showcasing a model for how AI-based businesses can operate efficiently"

Web-Based Advertising Contribution

  • Previously Guided: 10%+ of overall advertising revenue in 2025
  • Updated View (RAISED): Could exceed 10% this year; CFO: "it's quite likely that it could represent a larger than 10% portion of the revenue this year"
  • Uncertainty: CFO: "it's very difficult to guess where that might go within the year because we've got a lot of factors"

Capital Allocation

  • Buyback-Focused: Q1 2025 saw $1.2B in repurchases/withholdings vs. $826M in FCF, using temporary revolver draw
  • Priority: Continue returning capital to shareholders via share repurchases

Apps Divestiture

  • Definitive Agreement Signed: May 7, 2025 (day of the earnings call); CEO: "we're thrilled to announce the signing of the definitive agreement to sell our games business in its entirety"
  • Buyer: Tripledot Studios
  • Consideration: $400M cash + 20% ownership stake in the combined business (CFO: "Consideration includes $400 million in cash and a 20% ownership stake in the combined business")
  • Prior Disclosure (from Q4 call): ~$900M total consideration ($500M cash + minority equity); consideration appears revised downward ($400M cash vs. prior $500M cash)
  • Expected Close: Q2 2025, subject to regulatory clearance; CFO: "we anticipate closing this transaction in the second quarter"
  • Timing Within Q2: Expected toward end of Q2; CFO: "our expectation is that the transaction's going to close towards the end of the quarter"
  • Post-Close Impact: All Apps revenue and EBITDA removed; company becomes pure-play advertising; no incremental uplift from former studios as third-party advertisers assumed in Q2 guidance
  • Studio Spend Post-Close: Not expected to be material; CFO: "It's a nice premium to have, but we don't expect that it will move the needle"

E-Commerce / Web Advertising Expansion

Status and Scale

  • Run Rate: $1B in web advertising revenue (CEO-referenced)
  • Advertisers Onboarded: 600+ (from prior blog disclosure); pace slowed due to resource constraints
  • Team Size: ~20 people handling the entire web advertising go-to-market
  • Market Penetration: <0.1% of total potential advertisers
  • Churn (Run Rate >$250K): Sub-3%
  • Model Maturity (CEO Self-Rating): "B+" -- performing well for how early it is, but significant improvement expected
  • Geographic Scope: U.S. only currently; CEO: "we've constrained the audience in web advertising to U.S. only. That's by design"
  • International Expansion: CEO: "there's a lot of opportunity to just flip that switch and go global"

Product Roadmap

  • Self-Service Dashboard: Launching to select customers this quarter (Q2 2025); CEO: "we'll be launching it this quarter for select customers" - Rollout Phases: (1) Current customers feedback group (Q2 2025); (2) All current customers; (3) Staged opening to new types of clients; (4) Fully open/global (several quarters out) - CEO: "we'll go through stages of opening it up, where eventually long term, and this isn't too long term but several quarters, we'll go completely open"
  • Attribution Integrations: Enhancing integrations with third-party platforms and attribution vendors; CEO: "The web advertising space is more fragmented than apps. So this will take time, but it's a straightforward task"
  • Automated/Generative Ad Creative: Priority for 2025; vision is LLM-powered dynamic video generation personalized per user; CEO: "we want to do is generative AI-based ad creative" - Monetization lever: higher response rates without advertiser effort - CEO: "It's free effectively. It's a very low cost amount of production to get a much higher response rate"

Model Architecture

  • Two Separate Models: Gaming and e-commerce operate on different models; CFO: "there are 2 separate models. So both models are learning at the same time"
  • Independent Improvement Cycles: Each model has its own reinforcement learning and directed enhancement pipeline; step-function improvements can happen independently on either model
  • E-Commerce Model Still Early: CEO: "the e-com model is early...it hasn't been around for that long. It also doesn't have as much of a data feedback loop because it doesn't have as much impressions, transactions, scale"

Not Targeting Largest Chinese E-Commerce

  • CEO: "we focus on mid-market web advertisers and aren't yet working with the largest players as we're refining our tools first"
  • Tariff impact on de minimis exemption changes considered immaterial given low penetration

Conversion Window / Attribution

  • No Conversion API: Cannot attribute beyond cookie window; CEO: "we don't have a conversion API. We don't have what other companies have with e-mail address and phone number"
  • Cookie Window Limitation: Especially short on Safari; model must drive ad-to-transaction quickly
  • Self-Attributing on Web: Built own attribution platform for web
  • Working on Third-Party Integrations: For advertiser confidence at scale; CEO: "we are working on integrations in the web space as well so that the advertisers can have multiple reference points"

Categories Beyond DTC

  • Early Success Noted: Fintech, health care, insurance mentioned on prior call; not actively pushing into new categories due to resource constraints
  • Works Broadly: Model performs across categories without custom builds
  • Non-Gaming App Install: Still on the roadmap, not a 2025 focus

AXON / Core Technology Update

Q1 Model Performance

  • Step-Function Enhancement Occurred: Unlike Q4 (which had no step-function), Q1 2025 saw model efficiency improvements; CEO: "there was better efficiency in the model, so the team is constantly improving the model"
  • Not a Major Version Jump: CEO: "we don't call it like with self-directed lift or a model change. This wasn't a material change to the model. But the team found wins in the model, and it became more effective"
  • Self-Learning Also Contributing: Ongoing reinforcement learning continues
  • Gaming Drove Majority of Growth: CEO: "the greater than 50% of the quarter's growth came from the gaming side"

Conversion Rate Expansion Potential

  • Current: ~1%+ transaction rate per 1,000 impressions
  • Target Over Time: 2% to 5%; CEO: "We think that can go up to become 2%, 3%, 4%, 5% over time"
  • Impact: Full-screen video ads with high attention capture; each percentage point increase is massively impactful

AXON Versioning

  • No Formal "AXON 3": CEO suggested dropping version naming; "Maybe it's just the AXON model. There's going to be many different iterations"
  • Incremental Updates (2.2, 2.3, 2.4): Each incremental change has shown double-digit QoQ growth in a single quarter; CEO: "those incremental changes have shown to be double-digit growth in a single quarter quarter-over-quarter"
  • Long Pipeline: CEO: "we have a long pipeline of things to work on, and we expect that the research science team...is going to be constantly improving this model"
  • AI Research Still Early: CEO: "this form of understanding how to work with neural nets is really new...the reality is it's really, really early"

Tariff Impact Assessment

  • 90%+ of Revenue from Mobile Games: Not directly impacted by tariffs; CEO: "90% plus of our revenue in advertising comes from mobile games, which aren't directly impacted by tariffs"
  • Web Advertising Exposure: Mid-market focused, not reliant on large Chinese e-commerce; CEO: "we focus on mid-market web advertisers and aren't yet working with the largest players"
  • Low Market Penetration Insulates: CEO: "with such low market penetration, we're well positioned to grow through macroeconomic changes like tariffs without any visible impact on our business trajectory"
  • Digital Economy Resilient: CEO on gaming: "free-to-play gaming is a very cheap and accessible form of entertainment. So we've always felt that it's quite insulated from economic change or distress"

App Store Fee Reduction Impact

  • Net Positive for AppLovin: Lower fees for game developers means more revenue per dollar of consumer spend, which flows back into higher user acquisition budgets
  • Example Math (CEO): If App Store fee drops from 30% to 15%, developer gets $0.85 per $1 instead of $0.70 -- an immediate 20% uplift to their revenues
  • Auction Dynamics: In a competitive auction, if one advertiser bids 20% more, all must follow; benefits advertising platforms like AppLovin
  • CEO: "we're the largest channel there is today for mobile gaming customers to spend their money...So maybe we're likely to be the biggest beneficiary of this change"

TikTok Bid

  • Proposal Published: Blog post published same day as earnings call
  • Scope: Pursuing operational control of TikTok outside of China (global ex-China), not just U.S.
  • Rationale: Leverage AppLovin's AI/ML model expertise to rewrite parts of the algorithm and ensure national security compliance
  • CEO Assessment: "It's absolutely a long shot"; CEO: "we think we may be the only one who could do that with the knowledge that we have of models"
  • Strategic Upside: CEO: "that paired with an audience as large scale as TikTok's could unlock immense economic potential"

Supply Dynamics

Inventory Monetization

  • 1B+ Daily Active Users: In mobile games (unchanged)
  • Monetization Ceiling Far Off: Conversion rate can expand from ~1% to 2-5% over time
  • Demand-Led Growth Model: More advertiser categories/diversity = better matching = higher response rates = more monetization of same supply base
  • Publisher Benefit: Non-gaming ad categories (e-commerce, fintech, etc.) are welcomed by publishers who prefer diverse ad content

New Supply Opportunities

  • In-App Purchase Games: As non-gaming ad categories grow, large IAP games (e.g., Candy Crush) that historically avoided showing competing game ads may be incentivized to run non-gaming ads
  • Global Expansion: Currently U.S.-only for web ads; international represents roughly half of total business

Seasonality Framework

  • Q1: Benefits from holiday carryover (New Year's), Ramadan, spring break; relatively strong for mobile gaming; e-commerce steps down from Q4 holiday peak
  • Q2: Weakest seasonal quarter; CEO: "Q2, you actually don't have almost anything as only the tail end gets into summer"; last year was the only single-digit QoQ growth quarter
  • Q3: Summer engagement boost
  • Q4: Holiday season; strongest for both gaming engagement and e-commerce advertising

Key Q&A Insights

Exclusionary Audiences / Retargeting (Chris Kuntarich, UBS)

  • Advertisers (used to Meta) want exclusion lists and new vs. retarget audience splits
  • CEO: Not a current focus; still too early in product; "we don't want to build products that are similar to what others do. We want to build products that are improved"
  • May introduce over time but not a priority

Self-Service Dashboard Launch Dynamics (Ralph Schackart, William Blair)

  • Advertisers expected to ramp budgets gradually, not flood immediately
  • CEO: "advertisers need to prove to themselves that the dollars are worth spending"
  • Agencies and advertisers will know how to use it since all other major platforms have similar tools

Longer Consideration / Higher-Priced Products (James Heaney, Jefferies)

  • Model excels at quick-conversion, lower-priced products (sub-$250)
  • For higher-priced products, may optimize for earlier-funnel events (phone call, email registration)
  • Not currently a priority given massive addressable market of quick-conversion products

Incremental Margins (Eric Sheridan, Goldman Sachs)

  • Costs largely fixed except data centers (~10% of revenue growth)
  • Self-serve platform will drive growth without adding to cost base
  • Margin profile should continue expanding from 81% toward asymptote

Web Advertising Contribution Target (Matt Thornton, FBN / Rob Sanderson, Loop Capital)

  • 10% of advertising revenue target for 2025 likely to be exceeded; CFO: "quite likely that it could represent a larger than 10% portion"
  • Measured on a net revenue basis; CFO: "that would be on a net basis"

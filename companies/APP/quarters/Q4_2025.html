Commentary on Q1 2026 Guidance

  • Sequential Growth Despite Seasonality: CEO: "despite typical seasonality where Q1 should be softer than Q4, we are guiding to meaningful sequential growth. That reflects both continued strength in gaming and the scaling of our e-commerce and our self-service customers"
  • Above-Typical Q1 Sequential: CFO in Q&A (Cory Carpenter, JPMorgan): The 5-7% sequential is above what AppLovin typically guides to in Q1, especially given e-commerce seasonality headwinds from Q4 holiday
  • Guidance Drivers (CFO): (1) Very strong Q4 exit rate; (2) Performance of mobile gaming business; (3) E-commerce launch ramp; (4) Prospecting model impact; CFO: "consistent with kind of our normal practice...we're guiding to the level where we have a very high level of confidence"
  • Seasonal Offsets (CFO): (1) Normal Q4-to-Q1 seasonal softness; (2) A couple fewer days in Q1 vs. Q4; CFO: "that's partially offset by just seasonality going from Q4 into Q1 normally being a weaker seasonal period. And then we also had a couple of days less in Q1 versus Q4"
  • Margin Held at 84%: EBITDA margin guidance of ~84% represents a flat hold vs. Q4 2025 actual, and a raise from the 82-83% guided for Q4 2025; margin now at a new steady-state level
  • Given persistent beat pattern (9 consecutive quarters) and conservative guidance philosophy, actual Q1 2026 results may again exceed guidance

Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

Advertising Business Long-Term Growth Algorithm

  • Long-Term Core Gaming YoY Growth Rate: 20% to 30% (previously stated; not explicitly reaffirmed this quarter but CEO noted: "We continue to grow very quickly despite the numbers getting much bigger")
  • Current Overall Growth: 66% YoY in Q4, 70% for FY 2025; significantly above long-term target

Self-Service Platform Launch (AXON Ads) Update

Status as of Q4 2025

  • Launched: October 1, 2025 (referral only); CEO: "In Q4, we opened up the self-service platform, referral only. So we're not at the point yet where we're sort of a GA type launch"
  • GA Timeline Still On Track for H1 2026: CEO: "We said first half of this year, that's still on track"
  • Not Yet GA: CEO: "We'll get there. We said first half of this year, that's still on track"

Self-Serve Advertiser Metrics

  • Conversion Funnel: 57% of qualified leads go live; CEO: "Right now, we're seeing of qualified leads, 57% of advertisers go live. That's 43% breakage. We think we can get that number much closer to 100% before we open up"
  • Biggest Breakage Cause: Lack of video ads in the required format; CEO: "The biggest one is that they just don't have video ads for our platform made to the type of format that we need"
  • Generative AI tools expected to solve breakage: CEO: "those generative AI tools should be able to raise that to a much higher percentage of customers going live"

Paid Marketing for Advertiser Acquisition

  • LTV-to-CAC Breakeven: ~30 days; CEO: "right now, we're seeing somewhere around day 30 LTV to cost of user acquisition...to be able to break even on the media buy in 30 days is exceptional"
  • Still in Testing Phase: CEO: "right now, we're testing. So numbers aren't that big. You didn't see anything reflected in our EBITDA margins"
  • Channels Being Tested: Ads on social, search, measurement company sponsorships (Triple Whale), podcast sponsorships (TBPN); CEO: "we're testing in light volume on ads on social and search, and then we're doing referral programs"
  • SEO Not Yet Built: CEO: "we don't have any SEO yet. Once we get history and put more content out on our blog...we'll also have SEO"
  • No Rush to Scale Marketing: CEO: "we're in no rush. We're seeing 30-day LTV to CAC roughly. If we see that at scale, we're going to scale, but we're not in a rush because we want the tools to catch up to the opportunity"
  • Prior $1M/Day Spend Target (from late 2025): Not explicitly reaffirmed; marketing spend is still in light testing volumes; CEO indicated growth rate high enough that marketing ramp won't materially impact EBITDA margins

Minimum GMV Gating Removed

  • CEO: "with opening up self-service, we're not gating to a minimum GMV anymore. So in the prior year, we had pretty high minimum standards for businesses. Now you're getting companies that have a few hundred thousand dollars of GMV, a couple of million dollars of GMV a year buying on our platform"

E-Commerce / Web Advertising Update

Current Status

  • E-Commerce Live for 1.5 Years: CEO: "the e-commerce business obviously has been live with us for 1.5 years. It's doing really well"
  • No Breakout of E-Commerce Revenue: CEO: "we're not going to do that because we think of our platform as a unified platform"; rationale: if gaming model improves 50%, e-commerce share would decline even though overall business is doing well
  • Prior Disclosure Reference: CEO referenced Q1 2025 when e-commerce was "roughly 10% of our business"
  • Year-Over-Year Cohort Growth: Customers who lapped Q4 2024 into Q4 2025 saw material increases in spend; CEO: "the current customers that had lapped Q4 2024 into Q4 2025 saw material increases in spend as our models just keep getting better"

Model Improvement

  • Recent Material Uplift: CEO: "just a few weeks ago, we had a pretty sizable uplift...those same customers from the prior year cohort saw big growth"; "We rolled it out. Advertisers saw a huge improvement in return on ad spend. They started putting more budget into our system quickly"
  • E-Commerce Model Maturity: Still early vs. gaming; CEO: "the e-commerce side is starting from a worse place. It's just earlier stage, less data"
  • Data Penetration Very Low: Thousands of sites pixeled vs. potential 10M+; CEO: "the reports show thousands of sites, right? So we're starting with thousands of sites in a world where we can pixel 10 million plus"
  • Competitive Performance Already: CEO: "if you talk to the customer base, a lot of the customers are seeing equal performance on us to any other top-of-funnel discovery channel, including the largest social platforms today"

Ad Count Gap (E-Commerce vs. Gaming)

  • Gaming: Top companies run tens of thousands of ads at any given time
  • E-Commerce: Top companies in the hundreds; CEO: "you've got a huge discrepancy here"
  • Bridge Plan: (1) Customers learning which creatives work and multiplying production; (2) Generative AI tools for low-cost automated creative generation

Conversion Rate Opportunity

  • Historical ~1% Baseline: Previously disclosed; CEO: "our conversion rates on 1,000 impressions were about 1%. They're obviously higher now"
  • Peak Conversion Rate (Confident Predictions): ~5%; CEO: "we think that could go as high as 5%. That's what we see when we're serving an ad where the model is confident that the user is going to take an action"
  • Current Dilution Effect: CEO: "Other users aren't [converting well] and the conversion rate is atrocious. It might be 2 over 1,000, and it dilutes you to 1%"
  • Path to 5%: More advertisers = more diverse content = model can find relevant ad for more users at any given moment

Case Study: Israeli Cookware Company

  • CEO: "We had, a year ago, this Israeli cookware company come live that did $4 million of revenue...65% of all their user acquisition spend was on our platform. They scaled to $16 million of revenue and are profitable in doing so...This year, they're ramping so quickly, again, putting most of their UA spend on our platform. It's looking like they're projecting that $80 million of revenue"
  • Growth trajectory: $4M -> $16M -> projected $80M revenue, primarily driven by AppLovin ad spend

New Categories Beyond E-Commerce

  • Transactional Businesses: CEO: "we're still early in these other categories because for each one of these cases, we have to tune the model somewhat, and we're focused right now on anything that's transactional-based business versus a lead-based business"
  • Fintech Example: CEO: "if you're a transactional-based business, you're not e-commerce, but you're fintech or something. That could work really well out of the box"
  • Lead Gen Businesses Coming: CEO: "there's a huge sector of lead gen businesses. You want to collect the user's information and sell them in a call center. That's going to come too for us in the coming months"
  • Terminology Shift: CEO: "I started calling it e-commerce, I call it web advertising now. Anyone with the website with a transactional business model should be able to work on our platform"

Generative AI-Based Ad Creatives

Interactive Page Generation (Live)

  • Piloting with 100+ Customers: CEO: "We already have in a pilot with over 100 customers, generative AI-based tools for one part of the ad unit"
  • Component: The interactive page (middle part of the ad unit: video + interactive page + shop preview/dynamic product page)
  • Performance: CEO: "it's showing good performance"; broader rollout planned soon
  • Why Needed: E-commerce advertisers don't build interactive pages because "they don't need them on social or search"

Video Generation (Coming Soon)

  • Video Model Going Live Shortly: CEO: "shortly, we're going to have the video model go live as well. We're going to run the same pilot process"
  • Cost Reduction Potential: CEO: "if we get to a place where the video model can help these customers create new video ads in bulk in cost of dollars versus cost of thousands of dollars, we expect the count of ads for these new customers on our platform is going to go up a lot"
  • Impact: Expected to dramatically close the ad count gap between e-commerce (hundreds) and gaming (tens of thousands)

Prospecting Campaigns Product (New in Q4 2025)

  • Launched in Q4 2025: Allows advertisers to upload historical purchase data and target only new (non-existing) customers
  • CEO: "We rolled out this product in Q4. We let them upload their historical purchases. Using that data, our model can start tuning away from purchasers from the past and towards users that they've never seen before"
  • Adoption: CEO: "we've seen really quick adoption on this one because when they flip the switch, they instantly start seeing many more new customers"
  • Rationale: Universal campaigns with mixed new + retargeting didn't fully address advertiser goals; CEO: "it's really easy to understand the incremental value of a new customer"

Competition / META Discussion

Meta Bidding on MAX

  • Meta is a Long-Standing MAX Launch Partner: CEO: "Meta was a launch partner of MAX. They're a good partner. They've been in the MAX platform for a long time"
  • Current Meta Bidding Scope: Bids on IDFA-available traffic only (~1/3 of full-screen ad units); CEO: "They are a bidder on anything that has an ID today, and they're not a bidder on anything that does not have an ID...That's about 2/3 of the full-screen ad units that they currently bid on [have no IDFA]. They started bidding more on that [IDFA traffic], as you saw on LinkedIn"
  • No-ID Traffic (IDFA-Off): Meta has NOT started bidding on no-ID traffic; CEO: "They did not start bidding on no ID traffic, and they might very well bid on no ID traffic in the next couple of quarters"
  • Impact Assessment: More competition in MAX auction expands the pie, does not shrink AppLovin; CEO: "Every time this has happened, there's been this confusion around, well, in an auction dynamic-based system, competition should decay AppLovin's edge. You've never seen that happen"
  • Economics of Competition: CEO: "when competition wins an impression, it's very likely to be the one that we value less. This leads to the publisher making more. And in many cases, we do as well because instead of winning a low-value impression, we get to charge the winning bidder 5%"

Meta Deterministic Bidding Risk

  • CEO: "I think it's possible from a technology perspective. I think it's bluntly against Apple's terms. I don't think the space is big enough for Meta to say they want to violate the platform they depend on terms"
  • Historical context: CEO: "Five years ago, when Meta was really big in the space...People recall a time Meta was half the space. They think it's going to be half the space again...What's changed in the marketplace is that the other ad platforms that are built for this category...have gotten better"

MAX Competitive Moat

  • Not About Mediation Alone: CEO: "The moat around our mediation is not because of the mediation"; mediation is a few percentage points better than competitors
  • True Moat = Advertising Solutions (UA tools): CEO: "for a lot of these publishers, we're over 50% of all their user acquisition spend. They can't go get that anywhere else. If they go off MAX, that decays"
  • 360 Solution Lock-In: Best buying tools + best monetization tools = strong sticky 360 solution; CEO: "their growth depends on it"
  • MAX Marketplace Scale: CEO: "the MAX marketplace is well over $10 billion a year"

AI / LLM Disruption Risk

  • CEO on game creation: "AI will dramatically lower the cost of creation, which means content will explode. And when content becomes abundant, discovery becomes a scarce resource"
  • CEO on chatbot substitution: Unlikely for casual gaming audience; "This audience skews older. It skews female. This is an audience that's very unlikely to stop playing crosswords in 10 years because they're talking to a chatbot"
  • CEO on LLM productivity gains: "If LLMs increase productivity, people likely have more time to go to outlets like games"

Supply / Demand Dynamics

Demand-Constrained, Not Supply-Constrained

  • CEO: "right now, we're focused on the demand side because that conversion rate can go up so much more than where it is today. We've got a lot of growth in front of us just by doing the demand generation well and improving our core models"
  • CEO: "We will eventually go out to new publishers. We get calls all the time"

MAX Market Growth

  • Double-Digit Growth Continuing: CEO: "MAX is growing really quickly" and implied double-digit growth is continuing
  • Broad Industry Growth: Unity Vector, Moloco, Liftoff all growing quickly; CEO: "When you've got all these market players growing quickly, that MAX market is growing in a way that people, I don't think, understood or would have expected, and we're not seeing any slowdowns there"
  • Not Zero-Sum: CEO: "people should believe it's not a zero-sum market. This isn't ride-share where one person takes a ride and another loses"
  • Audience Scale: 1B+ daily active users playing casual mobile games; CEO: "1 billion users playing mobile games, casual games every single day"
  • Monetization Gap vs. Meta: CEO: "if you just translate to Meta's users, it's 3x more users, a little bit more time spent. But the revenue that they're driving to advertisers over what we're driving and the whole space is driving to advertisers is probably 8x"

Ad Spend Scale

  • $11B+ Annual Ad Spend Through Platform (Blog Post Reference): CEO in Q&A (Clark Lampen, BTIG): "the $11 billion or over $11 billion number that you provided in the blog post"

Key Q&A Insights

E-Commerce Self-Service Launch Learnings (Benjamin Black, Deutsche Bank)

  • Self-service opened Q4 2025, referral only; GA planned H1 2026
  • Lapped Q4 2024 e-commerce customers saw material increases in spend from model improvements
  • Will not break out e-commerce revenue separately; unified platform logic
  • CEO: "Fundamentally, we're one single auction, but getting more diversity will give the model more ways to serve the end consumer"

Generative AI Creative Automation Timeline (Benjamin Black, Deutsche Bank)

  • Interactive page gen-AI tools piloting with 100+ customers; broader rollout soon
  • Video model going live shortly; same pilot process
  • Top gaming companies run tens of thousands of ads; top e-commerce in the hundreds
  • Gen-AI tools expected to close this gap dramatically; CEO: "the video model can help these customers create new video ads in bulk in cost of dollars versus cost of thousands of dollars"

Investor P x Q Framework for E-Commerce (Jason Bazinet, Citi)

  • Too early for P x Q model; thousands of pixeled sites vs. Meta's 10M+
  • CEO: "We can't build a P times Q model in a period of time that we expect to have really outsized growth"
  • Will provide P x Q once advertiser count stabilizes
  • 30-day LTV-to-CAC breakeven on marketing tests; 57% qualified-lead-to-go-live conversion rate

Meta Competitive Dynamics (Matthew Cost, Morgan Stanley)

  • Meta started bidding more on IDFA traffic (LinkedIn observation confirmed)
  • Has NOT started bidding on no-ID traffic (2/3 of full-screen ad units)
  • Competition historically expands the pie; CEO: "Every time this has happened...You've never seen that happen [decay in AppLovin's edge]"
  • MAX 5% take on competitive bidder wins on low-value impressions = positive economics

MAX Competitive Moat (Matthew Cost, Morgan Stanley)

  • True moat is UA/advertising tools, not mediation tech alone
  • Publishers depend on AppLovin for 50%+ of UA spend
  • CEO: "If they go off MAX, that [UA spend capability] decays"

Axon Marketing Spend in 2026 (James Heaney, Jefferies)

  • Currently in light testing; nothing reflected in EBITDA margins
  • LTV-to-CAC looks very favorable; but not rushing; want tools (gen-AI creatives) to catch up first
  • CEO: "We think the growth in the business is so high and our LTV to CAC is going to look so juicy that you're not going to see much decay in our EBITDA margins from ramping up marketing"

Meta Deterministic Bidding Risk (Alec Brondolo, Wells Fargo)

  • Technically possible but against Apple's terms; unlikely Meta would risk Apple relationship
  • Even if it happens, Meta would compete against Axon 2.0 model which didn't exist 5 years ago
  • CEO: "there's no world where Meta is going to end up becoming that kind of a dominant player in the face of this competition"

Self-Serve Advertiser Size / New Categories (Bernard McTernan, Needham)

  • No minimum GMV gating anymore; companies with a few hundred thousand dollars of GMV can join
  • Non-e-commerce apps with pixels are being observed; new growth vector being pursued
  • Lead gen businesses coming "in the coming months"
  • Transactional non-e-commerce (fintech) can work "really well out of the box"

Q1 2026 Guide Assumptions (Cory Carpenter, JPMorgan)

  • Above-typical Q1 sequential (5-7%) reflects strong Q4 exit rate, gaming performance, e-commerce ramp, and prospecting model
  • Partially offset by normal Q1 seasonality and fewer days vs. Q4
  • CFO: "consistent with kind of our normal practice...we're guiding to the level where we have a very high level of confidence"

Recent Model Uplift (Cory Carpenter, JPMorgan; Ralph Schackart, William Blair)

  • Material lift from team improvements a few weeks before the call
  • Advertisers saw huge improvement in ROAS and started increasing budget quickly
  • E-commerce model improvements can be much more substantial in percentage terms than gaming (earlier stage, less data)
  • But e-commerce is smaller base (~10% of business as of Q1 2025); so 40% improvement = only 4% overall uplift

Self-Service Timeline and Impact (Ralph Schackart, William Blair)

  • Self-service GA won't immediately move overall numbers given scale; CEO: "I wouldn't imagine day 1 or month 1 or month 2 is really going to move the needle on the overall numbers. It's going to build over time"
  • Will be "noticeable in our numbers" but not a "major growth catalyst" immediately

Supply Growth (Martin Yang, Oppenheimer)

  • MAX marketplace well over $10 billion a year
  • Focused on demand side; one large new publisher wouldn't meaningfully move the needle
  • CEO: "right now, we're focused on the demand side because that conversion rate can go up so much more than where it is today"

E-Commerce Model Confidence (Vasily Karasyov, Cannonball)

  • Already competitive with largest social platforms; not starting behind
  • Very low data penetration (thousands of sites vs. 10M+ potential)
  • CEO: "if you talk to 10 [e-commerce customers], at least 5 are going to tell you performance on our platform is really good"

Cost Structure and Use of Cash (Tim Nollen, SSR)

  • CFO very confident in ~84% margin level; only potential headwind is performance marketing ramp
  • Use of cash: (1) Organic growth / talent; (2) Share repurchases
  • Balance sheet: $2.8B cash, $3.5B debt (per Nollen); company reports $2.5B cash at year-end
  • CFO: "we've been very active with our repurchase program, and we continue to plan to be"

Seasonality Context for Upcoming Quarters

  • Q1 2026 (Guided): Typically softer than Q4 due to post-holiday seasonality and fewer days; however, guided 5-7% sequential growth reflects gaming strength, e-commerce scaling, and prospecting model ramp
  • H1 2026: Self-serve GA launch expected; generative AI creative tools rolling out to broader customer base; paid marketing for advertiser acquisition may begin scaling
  • 2026 Full Year: Multiple growth catalysts converging: (1) Self-serve GA opening; (2) Paid marketing to acquire advertisers; (3) Lead gen category expansion; (4) Generative AI creative tools reducing advertiser breakage; (5) Video gen-AI model; (6) Continued model improvements in both gaming and e-commerce; (7) International expansion / EU opening eventually

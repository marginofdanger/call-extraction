  • Software Platform Revenue: $318M (+118% YoY)
  • Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA: $197M (+114% YoY)
  • Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA Margin: 62%
  • Enterprise Clients: 503 (record; surpassed 500 milestone)
  • Net Dollar-Based Revenue Retention (LTM): 204% (improved YoY; over 200% for 5 consecutive quarters)

But given our strategic position today, some of the new investments we've been making, acquisitions we've made, that remains our target -- internal target."

Apps Segment

  • Apps FY 2023 Goal: Position portfolio to have a "healthy business model" where it can grow top line and earn a solid margin
  • Apps Target EBITDA Margin (Long-term): 15%-20% at least ("industry-type margins" per Herald Chen)

Unity Merger Proposal Details

  • Proposal Type: Unsolicited, nonbinding partnership proposal to Unity Software Board
  • Proposed Structure: Merger of equals; approximately 51-49 voting control (AppLovin-Unity); Adam Foroughi would serve as COO, John Riccitiello as CEO
  • Estimated Synergies: Over $700M in EBITDA within 3 years
  • Synergy Breakdown: - Revenue Synergies: 20% improvement to AXON algorithm accuracy could generate $500M incremental net revenue with "nearly 100% flow-through" - Gross-Up to Advertiser Spend: $500M net revenue equates to ~$2B extra performance advertising spend on the combined platform - Cost Synergies: Combined Operate + Software Platform revenue projected over $2.5B with $1B-$1.5B cost base; "apply any reasonable percentage" for efficiencies plus back-office/corporate overlap
  • Combined 2023 Revenue Projection: Software Platform + Unity Operate projected over $2.5B combined in 2023
  • Comparison to ironSource Deal: AppLovin proposal at half the EBITDA multiple vs. Unity-ironSource deal; ironSource deal contemplated $300M synergies vs. AppLovin's $700M+
  • AXON Historical Growth: Over 500% software revenue growth in 7 quarters since AXON launch (Q1 2021 Software Revenue: $88M vs. Q2 2022: $318M)
  • Status as of Call: One-way proposal; waiting for Unity Board response

AXON Technology and Software Platform Growth Drivers

  • Primary Revenue Driver: AppDiscovery powered by AXON machine learning engine
  • AXON Growth Mechanism: Reducing prediction error rates leads to step-function revenue gains; each accuracy improvement increases business in a step function, not gradual
  • Revenue Growth Track Record: More than doubled Software Platform revenue YoY; more than quintupled over past 2 years
  • Two Paths to AXON Improvement: (1) Code improvements to lower error rates in predictions; (2) access to more data to strengthen algorithms
  • Contextual Data Approach: AppLovin uses contextual/device engagement data, not personal user data; never relied on IDFA — "we've always been a contextual data platform"
  • IDFA Impact: Minimal direct impact on AppLovin's business; broader ecosystem impact through reduced ability for gaming companies to target effectively on other channels

MoPub Integration and ALX Exchange

  • MoPub Publisher Migration: Complete; fully integrated into MAX
  • MoPub DSP Migration: Over 90% migrated; ongoing process to ramp spend
  • ALX Take Rate Decision: Reduced by nearly half; short-term decision; "manual toggle" — can be reversed when economy improves
  • Brand CPM Trends: Brand CPMs through MAX platform decayed; broader digital landscape brand dollar pullback
  • Publisher Support Strategy: Flowing more dollars to publishers to offset brand dollar decline; reinvesting in MAX ecosystem

New Growth Initiatives

Connected TV (CTV) / Wurl

  • Status: Wurl successfully integrated; testing mobile gaming advertising campaigns on CTV
  • Results: "Still in early stages of testing, the results are promising"
  • Vision: Bring "true performance-driven approach to advertising for the television market"
  • Wurl Team: Headcount now included in Software Platform costs

Array (Carrier/OEM Marketing Platform)

  • Description: Marketing platform for carriers and OEMs; lifecycle native marketing solution; revenue-sharing model
  • Status: Early stage; small, nimble team
  • Customer Outreach: Active dialogues with several key OEMs/carriers; co-developing product roadmap
  • Revenue Timeline: Meaningful revenue contribution expected to start in 2023, scaling up in 2024-2025

NFTs / Blockchain

  • Status: Still working on NFTs and blockchain initiative
  • Vision: Enable transfer of ownership of digital goods; create payment mechanism through AppLovin's own blockchain

Macro and Industry Commentary

  • Mobile Gaming Advertising Market Size: $25B (per prior shareholder letter)
  • Macro Headwinds: Weaker economy; brand dollar pullback from digital; supply chain issues
  • Industry Headwinds: Post-COVID overconsumption hangover (people traveling, less gaming); IDFA privacy changes constricting growth opportunities
  • Management View: Current under-consumption period is anomalous; mobile gaming remains "most accessible and most affordable form of entertainment"; expect return to normal traffic patterns and industry growth
  • Privacy Impact Assessment: IDFA removal lowered capacity for mobile gaming companies to target effectively; ~4-5 quarters into post-IDFA adjustment period; AppLovin less directly impacted due to contextual data approach

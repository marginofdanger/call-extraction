3 Key Takeaways

1
Perfect guidance record: 13/13 beats on both revenue and EBITDA. Since quarterly guidance began (Q3 2022 call), AppLovin has never missed the midpoint of either metric. Revenue beat rate vs high end is 85% (11/13); EBITDA beat rate vs high end is 92% (12/13). Zero quarters missed the low end on either metric.
2
EBITDA conservatism is extreme: +11.7% average beat vs midpoint. This means guidance systematically understates results by ~12%. Even excluding the Q3 2023 outlier (+40%), the average beat is +9.3%. Revenue conservatism is more modest at +5.5% average beat.
3
Q3 2023: the biggest guidance beat in the dataset. EBITDA of $490M vs $340–$360M guidance (+$130M, +40% vs midpoint) and revenue of $864M vs $780–$800M (+$64M, +9.4%). This was AXON 2.0’s first full quarter, revealing a step-function model improvement that guidance structurally could not anticipate.
4
Revenue guidance range width reveals confidence. Early ranges were ±$10M ($685–$705M for Q1 2023). By Q4 2025, ranges widened to ±$15M ($1,570–$1,600M), but the beat magnitude grew even faster. The guidance “envelope” consistently captures only the organic baseline, not e-commerce, self-serve, or model step-functions.
5
Q2 2024 was the only quarter at the high end (revenue). Revenue of $1,080M matched the $1,080M high end exactly — the tightest guidance outcome in the dataset. EBITDA still beat by +$31M. This quarter followed the e-commerce pilot launch, suggesting management may have calibrated guidance upward slightly, only to resume wider beats in Q3.
6
Guidance conservatism increased after the scope narrowed. When guidance shifted to ad-only (Q2 2025+), EBITDA beats averaged +5.7% vs mid — lower than the +13.0% average during total-company guidance (Q1 2023–Q1 2025). However, this reflects cleaner scope, not less conservatism: the ad business has less variance than the combined company.
7
Nine consecutive quarters beating the high end (Q4 2023–Q4 2025). This streak spans the period of maximum acceleration: AXON 2.0 maturation, e-commerce TAM expansion, margin expansion from 50% to 84%, and the Apps divestiture. Every quarter delivered surprise beyond the already-beaten-before-it-started guidance.
8
EBITDA grew from $260M to $1,400M in 12 quarters (5.4×). Q4 2022 EBITDA of $260M at 37% margin grew to Q4 2025 EBITDA of $1,400M at 84% margin. Revenue doubled; EBITDA grew 5.4×. This reflects the extraordinary operating leverage of an AI-driven advertising platform with near-zero marginal costs.

- **Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA YoY Growth:** 49%
- **Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 62%
- **Enterprise Clients (SPECs):** 538 (record; up from 503 in Q2 2022)
- **Net Dollar-Based Revenue Retention (NDRR, LTM):** 166% (down from 204% in Q2 2022)

### Apps Segment
- **Apps Revenue YoY Change:** Down 24% YoY

- **Apps Strategy:** Reduced UA spend to focus on ROAS and profitability; selling/shutting down nonstrategic assets; renegotiating earnouts
- **Nonstrategic Asset Sales:** One very small asset sold back to founders during Q3

### Capital Structure and Cash Flow
- **Net Leverage:** Just over 2x
- **Credit Rating:** 4 Bs (BB-rated)
- **Interest Rate Hedging:** More than half of interest fixed for the year
- **Debt Maturity Profile:** Term loans maturing in 2025 and 2028; no maintenance covenants
- **Share Repurchase Authorization Remaining:** Over $400M available (from $750M program; ~$340M already used as of Q2)
- **FX Impact:** Not a significant direct headwind on P&L; software and apps largely transacted in USD

## 2023 Outlook Commentary (No Formal Guidance)

- **Management declined to say whether 2023 will be a growth year:** Depends on (1) macro environment recovery / advertiser willingness to invest in growth vs. cash flow; and (2) AppLovin's ability to improve core technologies
- **Adam Foroughi on 2023 growth:** "And our overall ability to improve our core technologies" (in addition to macro)
- **Two Paths to Growth:** (1) Advertisers lower ROAS goals and invest more (external, macro-dependent); (2) AppLovin improves AXON predictive accuracy to fulfill excess budget at current advertiser goals (internal, within control)

## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

- **EBITDA Generation (2022):** Over $1B, growing nearly 50% over 2021
- **Cash Flow Conversion:** High percentage of EBITDA converts to free cash flow given very limited CapEx, modest tax rate, and small lease expenses
- **Share Buyback Consideration:** Over $400M remaining authorization; management views buybacks as potentially "very attractive return" given low stock multiples combined with high operating leverage
- **Long-Term Return Thesis (Adam Foroughi):** "The market today presents a low multiple entry point into our company...we're going to create outsized returns, private market like returns"

## AXON Technology and Core Technology Upgrades

- **AXON Upgrade History:** Initial AXON release ~2 years ago drove ~30% growth for 4 subsequent quarters; described as a "step function" upgrade
- **Current Technology Investment:** Working on both (1) incremental changes and (2) a significant rebuild of core systems; last major rebuild was the initial AXON release
- **Error Rate Focus:** Management continuously measures the error rate in the predictive engine; improving accuracy unlocks ability to fulfill excess advertiser budget without requiring higher CPIs
- **Excess Budget Opportunity:** "We have a lot of excess budget that we're not able to fulfill today. So if we're able to improve the accuracy of our predictive engine, we'll be able to fulfill that excess budget and maintain advertiser rates similar to where they are."
- **Growth Mechanism:** Not about raising CPI; about fulfilling more of existing advertiser budgets at their current ROAS goals by improving prediction accuracy

## MAX Mediation and ALX Exchange

- **MoPub DSP Migration:** Essentially complete; "pretty much all the MoPub DSPs" are cut over at this point
- **MAX + MoPub Combined Scale:** Over 2x bigger than what MoPub was alone for DSP partners
- **DSP Spending Outlook:** Pipes are connected; DSPs have capacity to spend "materially more" than in the past; full ramp depends on economic recovery
- **Brand Dollars:** A year ago, more brand dollars were in the ecosystem; DSPs are advantaged because they tap into brand dollars (not AppLovin's traditional focus)
- **Real-Time Bidding Transition:** Google announced investment in real-time bidding; AppLovin is one of the launch partners; strong early success; Google moving to RTB expected to tilt majority of the market
- **RTB Benefits:** (1) Efficiency gains for publishers; (2) incremental publisher revenue flowing back into UA; (3) incremental revenue opportunity for MAX product

## Apps Segment Strategic Review

- **Studios Remaining:** Approximately a dozen
- **Three-Lever Strategy:**
  1. **UA Discipline:** Cut back on UA spend to drive strong ROAS (taking revenue hit in exchange for profitability)
  2. **Portfolio Winnowing:** Evaluating which studios can drive long-term enterprise value; shutting down or selling back underperformers; renegotiating earnouts
  3. **Selective Investment:** Investing in "quite a few new games" in studios with potential
- **Earnout Payments:** Vast majority already paid out; projected remaining earn-out payments "not terribly significant"; renegotiating to align with new expectations
- **Potential Monetization:** Depends on M&A market recovery; may sell all, some, or none; current focus is on optimizing and owning assets
- **Value Assessment (Herald Chen):** "It is a significant amount of value relative to our current enterprise value and would be very accretive and deleveraging to us, we think, with the right price for the right assets"

## Macro and Industry Commentary

- **Market Environment:** Difficult; companies focused on cash flow over revenue growth; cost-cutting and budgetary constraints across the advertising sector
- **IDFA Impact (Ongoing):** Combined with macro headwinds; reduced ability of gaming companies to grow via targeted UA on social platforms; both factors already "baked in" to current business levels
- **Advertising Market Stability:** No share loss in core technologies; diverse advertiser base with no dependency on any single advertiser; commoditized nature of mobile gaming advertising means other advertisers fill any gaps
- **Consumer Engagement:** "People are still consuming the same amount of mobile games" -- demand side is stable
- **Competitive Dynamics (IDFA Recovery):** If large platforms (e.g., Facebook) improve their privacy-enhancing technologies and targeting, it would be a tailwind for AppLovin -- growing the overall pie benefits everyone with market leadership technologies
- **Talent Market:** "More recently, we're getting resumes from incredibly qualified individuals better than we've ever seen before" -- taking advantage of tech hiring environment

## Advertiser Mechanics (Q&A Detail)

- **How ROAS Tightening Flows Through:**
  - Advertiser sets daily budget (e.g., $1,000/day) with a ROAS goal (e.g., 10% D1 return)
  - This implies a breakeven period (e.g., D270 payback)
  - In tight economy, advertiser raises ROAS goal (e.g., to 20% D1) to shorten payback
  - Change flows programmatically through AppDiscovery; system finds users meeting the tighter criteria
  - Net effect: same budgets but tighter goals = less volume AppLovin can fulfill
  - Reverse will happen when economy improves: advertisers lower goals, AppLovin automatically fulfills more volume
- **ALX vs. AppDiscovery Impact:** ROAS tightening directly impacts AppDiscovery; ALX business involves third-party demand sources with potentially different models

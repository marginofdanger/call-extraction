# AppLovin Corporation Q1 2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** May 11, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2022

---

## Q1 2022 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated
- **Total Revenue (GAAP):** ~$625M (implied; includes $210M contra revenue reduction from publisher bonuses)
- **Total Revenue (normalized, ex-contra revenue):** ~$835M (management stated this was 38% greater than Q4 number)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $276M (record); $1B+ annualized run rate
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin (reported/GAAP revenue):** 44%
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin (normalized, ex-contra revenue):** 33% (500 bps increase over Q4 2021's 28%)
- **Contra Revenue (one-time):** $210M in publisher bonuses related to MoPub migration; added back to adjusted EBITDA; nonrecurring

### Software Platform Segment
- **Software Platform Revenue (GAAP):** $119M (after $210M contra revenue deduction)
- **Software Platform Revenue (normalized, ex-contra revenue):** $329M (+33% QoQ; +4x YoY vs Q1 2021)
- **Software Platform EBITDA Margin (normalized estimate):** 65%-70%
- **Software Platform share of EBITDA:** Over 80% of total EBITDA
- **Software Platform share of revenue:** ~40% of total (up from 14% at IPO one year ago)
- **MoPub contribution in Q1:** ~$40M of revenue (Twitter was still running that business during Q1; MoPub fully shut down March 31, 2022 and integrated into MAX)
- **MoPub publisher migration:** Over 90% of MoPub publishers moved to MAX
- **Net Dollar-Based Revenue Retention:** 258% (normalized for contra revenue); ~265% referenced in Q&A
- **Enterprise Clients (specs):** 519 (normalized); increase of 58 customers; nearly tripled in last 12 months
- **Average Revenue per Spec:** $603,000 (normalized); steady increase over past 3 quarters
- **Daily Active Users monetized via MAX:** 700M+
- **App Installs discovered (cumulative milestone):** Over 10 billion

### Apps Segment
- **Apps Revenue:** Declined modestly QoQ (~$40M sequential decline referenced by analyst)
- **Apps EBITDA Margin (current):** Low single digits (management stated they "really should be in the 20s")
- **Apps EBITDA Margin (estimated normalized):** 5%-10%
- **Apps business trend:** Fairly flat in both audience and revenue for 4 consecutive quarters
- **Own games share of software business:** Was 35%-40% at IPO; continues to shrink

### Cash Flow & Capital Allocation
- **EBITDA-to-Unlevered Free Cash Flow Conversion (normalized run rate):** 65%-75%
- **CapEx:** Low (characterized as low CapEx business)
- **Stock Buyback Authorization:** $750M program; ~$45M used thus far
- **MoPub Total Acquisition Cost:** $1.26B ($1.05B purchase price + $210M contra revenue/publisher bonuses)

## Strategic & Operational Updates

### Apps Business Restructuring
- Management exploring how to structure apps business as a more efficient standalone business unit
- Exploration may result in operational changes and possibly plans to sell or spin off some studios
- Nearly 20 game studios distributed globally; some profitable, some generating losses
- Already started reducing UA spend in late Q1; historically spent aggressively to acquire users, valuing scale and data
- Will operate studios at a more profitable spend level; targeting 20%+ EBITDA margins (from current low single digits)
- No M&A dollars allocated to apps side going forward

### MoPub Integration
- MoPub fully shut down March 31, 2022; fully integrated into MAX
- $210M one-time publisher migration bonuses paid to incentivize rapid 90-day transition
- Over 90% of MoPub publishers moved to MAX (exceeded expectations)
- Going forward, MoPub revenue will not be separately discernible as it is fully integrated into MAX
- MoPub revenue expected to grow over the course of 2022
- Publisher bonuses are nonrecurring; will not be added back to EBITDA going forward if any occur

### AXON Machine Learning Engine
- AXON is 18 months old and continues to improve
- Growth inflection point was AXON's release; continued expansion from ML efficiencies
- Customers spending more due to improved ML performance, not just market growth
- Data from own games no longer a necessity to fuel ML improvements; third-party data now sufficient
- Machine learning systems improve with more data and scale from broader customer adoption

### MAX & Marketplace
- Combined MAX + MoPub marketplace is largest in mobile apps; 700M+ DAU in one exchange
- Traditional DSPs buying on MoPub now getting 2x+ scale in unified solution
- Strong positive feedback from migrating customers
- In-app bidding is the default for publishers coming to MAX
- MAX built to push the market toward in-app bidding; partnership with Facebook as first major bidders

### New Initiatives
- **Connected TV (Wurl acquisition):** Performance marketing opportunity on streaming TV; ad slots with immense reach on full screen
- **NFT/Blockchain:** Still pursuing; still bullish; does not require as much investment dollars in 2022
- **OEM strategy:** Still pursuing alongside CTV and blockchain
- Management expects to report progress on all new initiatives within 12 months

### Competitive & Market Positioning
- Customers paying for performance; shielded against macroeconomic volatility
- Platform success directly influences growth in total addressable market
- Privacy changes (IDFA, ATT) viewed as potential opportunity to gain share; nimble team able to adapt quickly
- Non-gaming demand growing via The Trade Desk partnership and programmatic exchange access (ALX)
- Non-gaming monetization increasing on the 700M+ DAU base

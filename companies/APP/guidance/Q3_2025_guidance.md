## Q3 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Advertising Segment Results (Pure-Play Advertising Business)
*Note: Apps business was divested to Tripledot Studios at the end of Q2 2025. Company is now a pure-play advertising business. All figures below are advertising segment / continuing operations only.*

- **Revenue:** ~$1.405B (+68% YoY; CFO: "Revenue was approximately $1.405 billion, up 68% year-over-year due to model updates in the core gaming business"; vs. ~$1.260B in Q2 2025 = +11.5% QoQ; vs. $835M in Q3 2024 = +68.3% YoY)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $1.158B (+79% YoY; CFO: "adjusted EBITDA was $1.158 billion, up 79%"; vs. $1.020B in Q2 2025 = +13.5% QoQ; vs. $653M in Q3 2024 = +77.3% YoY)
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 82% (CFO: "at an 82% margin, up 1% quarter-over-quarter from operating leverage and a modest reduction in operational FX"; vs. 81% in Q2 2025 = +100bps QoQ; vs. 78% in Q3 2024 = +400bps YoY)
- **QoQ Revenue-to-EBITDA Flow-Through:** 95% (CFO: "Quarter-over-quarter flow-through to adjusted EBITDA was 95%, slightly above Q2")
- **Free Cash Flow:** $1.049B (+92% YoY; CFO: "Free cash flow was $1.049 billion, up 92% year-over-year"; vs. $768M in Q2 2025 = +36.6% QoQ)
- **FCF Higher QoQ Reason:** Timing -- no semiannual cash interest paid on debt this quarter (those payments occur in Q2 and Q4); CFO: "Free cash flow margin improved sequentially given no semiannual cash interest paid on our debt this quarter as those payments occur in Q2 and Q4 of each year"
- **Cash and Cash Equivalents (End of Q3):** $1.7B (CFO: "We ended the quarter with $1.7 billion in cash and cash equivalents"; vs. $1.2B end of Q2 2025 = +$500M QoQ)
- **Share Repurchases & Withholding (Q3 2025):** ~1.3M shares for $571M (CFO: "we repurchased and withheld approximately 1.3 million shares for $571 million funded by free cash flow")
- **Weighted Average Diluted Shares Outstanding:** 341M (CFO: "we have reduced our weighted average diluted common shares outstanding from 346 million in Q4 of last year to 341 million this quarter"; vs. 342M WA diluted in Q2 2025 = -1M QoQ; vs. 346M in Q4 2024)
- **Share Repurchase Authorization Increase:** +$3.2B incremental (CFO: "our Board of Directors increased our share repurchase authorization by an incremental $3.2 billion")
- **Revenue Growth Drivers:** (1) Model updates in core gaming business (CFO: "up 68% year-over-year due to model updates in the core gaming business"); (2) Multiple incremental lifts in core models during the quarter (CEO: "Our teams delivered multiple incremental lifts in our core models this quarter"); (3) MAX supply-side platform growing at healthy rates

### Commentary on Q4 2025 Guidance
- **Sequential Acceleration:** Guidance implies 12-14% QoQ growth, a significant step-up from typical 3-5% reinforcement learning baseline, reflecting holiday seasonality + e-commerce self-serve ramp + model enhancements + international expansion
- **Guidance Philosophy (Conservative):** CFO: "we guide to kind of where we feel very comfortable that we could potentially land. So we guide to what we know. We don't guide to try to estimate for something that's unpredictable"
- **No Incremental New Advertiser Assumption in Guide:** CFO: "there is no incremental assumption built into the guide for onboarding of incremental customers" -- i.e., the guide does NOT assume spend from new self-serve referral advertisers ramping in Q4
- **Not Best YoY Comp for E-Com:** CFO: "last year, we had an entirely new e-commerce business that was ramping. And then this quarter, right, we had an existing base. So it's not best comparison year-over-year"
- **Drivers Baked into Guide:** (1) Optimism around e-commerce referral program; (2) Continued model enhancements; (3) Normal holiday seasonality (CFO: "the combination of those factors led to the larger guidance that we're projecting quarter-over-quarter")
- **Margin Step-Up:** EBITDA margin guidance of 82-83% represents a **raise from 81%** that had been the steady-state target for the prior 4+ quarters, driven by operating leverage and operational FX improvement

## Self-Service Platform Launch (AXON Ads)

### October 1, 2025 Launch -- Status Update
- **Launched on Schedule:** CEO: "we also delivered our major October 1 launch of our self-service platform and referral form"
- **Execution Quality:** CEO: "We did so without any significant hiccups, no major bugs and effective filtering out of low-quality ad accounts"
- **Launch Method:** Referral-based (not open to all); existing advertisers invite/refer new advertisers via referral codes
- **Early Spend Growth:** ~50% week-over-week spend growth from new self-serve advertisers (CEO: "we're already seeing spend from these self-service advertisers grow around roughly 50% week-over-week")
- **Still Very Early:** CEO: "It's too soon to be significant, but this type of early growth gives us even more confidence that our platform will excel at being an open platform to any type of advertiser"

### New Advertiser Characteristics (Post October 1)
- **Advertiser Profile:** Predominantly shops/e-commerce; filtered through referrals (not open sign-up); CEO: "They are predominantly shops. They're not going to be as large as they were in the cohort last year, but they're not going to be materially smaller either...think of them as comparable in mix"
- **Broad Categories:** CEO: "a broad set of shopping categories being represented"
- **Integration Timeline:** Not plug-and-play; takes 1+ weeks typically to integrate pixels and go live; CEO: "it does take a while for these customers to ramp up...just to get to a point of go-live is a week plus usually"
- **Low Customer Support Burden:** CEO: "We're not seeing a huge influx of customer support tickets"

### Referral Program Mechanics
- **Dynamic Code Allocation:** CEO: "if there's a partner that we gave X codes to and they run through X and they deliver quality leads, we give them more"
- **Success Tracking:** Company can measure back to every referral partner the success of the customers they bring
- **Gate Purpose:** Deliberately slowing intake to allow time to build/refine the tool
- **Codes Not Exhausted:** Ongoing issuance; will continue through early 2026

### Path to General Availability
- **Planned for 2026:** CEO: "I think I'd said on our prior earnings call for '26"
- **Gating Criteria:** Conversion funnel optimization, quality of onboarding experience, low customer support burden; CEO: "If we open up today, we may be okay, we may not"
- **NOT Gated By:** Generative AI creative tools or other new features; CEO: "I don't think so"
- **Focus for Q4/2026:** (1) Tune onboarding flows; (2) Ramp more AI agents into workflows; (3) Once satisfied with quality/experience, open broadly beyond referral; (4) Test generative AI-based ad creatives; (5) Test paid marketing to promote Axon Ads platform

## E-Commerce / Web Advertising Update

### Current Status
- **Self-Serve Referral:** Live since October 1, 2025
- **International Traffic for Web/Shop Advertisers:** Opened in Q3, ahead of schedule (CEO: "We also opened up international traffic for advertisers promoting websites or shops in Q3 ahead of schedule")
- **Geographic Coverage:** Everywhere in the world except EU traffic for web shops/web advertisers (CEO: "it's everywhere in the world, except for EU traffic for web shops and web advertisers")
- **EU Traffic Status:** Not yet open; EU is low teens percentage of business; GDPR restrictions require separate build-out; CEO: "EU tends to be somewhere in the low teens percentage of our business...GDPR rules are more restrictive and require a build-out for us. So we'll get to it in due time"
- **Successful International Markets:** English-speaking countries (Canada, Australia, New Zealand, etc.) most successful for current Western shop base

### Conversion Rate and Data Flywheel
- **Primary Growth Lever:** Increasing conversion rate (not impression volume); CEO: "the biggest lever for growth on our business, given what we report on a net revenue basis is increasing the conversion rate"
- **Advertiser Density Still Very Low:** CEO referenced Q1 disclosure of $11B+ ad spend across only low thousands of advertisers over 1B+ daily active users; CEO: "you've got such a high amount of spend for such a low amount of advertisers across over 1 billion daily active users"
- **Ad Viewership Differential:** AppLovin average ad viewership ~35 seconds vs. social media ~7 seconds; CEO: "The average viewership of our ads is roughly 35 seconds. The average viewership of an ad on social is roughly 7 seconds"
- **Current Conversion Rate:** ~1% baseline referenced from prior disclosures; CEO: "our average conversion rate is that 1% that we've given a year plus ago. So maybe it's higher now"
- **Data Flywheel Effect:** Cross-category data improves all advertiser outcomes; CEO example: someone buying a $5,000 handbag provides data useful for gaming customer targeting

### Demand vs. Supply Dynamics
- **Demand-Constrained, Not Supply-Constrained:** CEO: "we're not supply constrained today, we're demand constrained"
- **Many Years of Conversion Rate Improvement Ahead:** CEO: "there's going to be quarters, possibly many years of growth in conversion rate to come before we start worrying about supply"
- **No Displacement of Gaming Advertisers:** More advertiser density actually benefits gaming customers through better targeting and more data; CEO: "I'm really confident that we're not going to squeeze anyone in our platform. We're probably going to have expansion across the board"

### E-Commerce Take Rate
- **Same as Gaming:** Unified auction; no structural difference; CEO: "The business is not built to say web advertising or shops is treated differently than games. It's one unified auction. We're a single platform"
- **Advertising Credits Immaterial:** CEO: "consider that immaterial"

## Generative AI-Based Ad Creatives

- **Testing Timeline:** CEO: "I'm hoping, in a matter of weeks or months to be able to test generative AI-based creative"
- **Process:** Create with tooling on top of LLMs, submit to advertisers for approval
- **Potential Impact:** Could dramatically increase ad count on platform; CEO: "that in itself can really explode the count of ads on the platform"
- **Why It Matters:** Many e-commerce advertisers porting short social-format ads that are mismatched for AppLovin's ~35 second viewership format; automated creative generation would optimize for platform best practices at no cost to advertiser
- **External Tools Improving:** CEO cited Sora 2 and Veo 3 as advancing tools; CEO: "Sora 2 came out this past quarter...Veo 3 keeps getting better"

## AI / Automation Capabilities

### AI Agents in Workflows
- CEO: "We'll continue tuning our onboarding flows and ramping more AI agents into the workflow to support a seamless experience for new advertisers"
- **Quality Checking Bot:** Already live; automatically checks uploaded ads and promoted websites for quality standards; CEO: "There's a bot there that's automatically checking for our quality"
- **LLM-Powered Dashboard Tools:** Customers getting best practices extracted from AI tools in dashboard without human support; CEO: "large language model powered tools that allow the customer to get support without interfacing with us"
- **AXON Ads Site Chatbot:** Currently a prompt-based bot; being tuned using external-facing inputs to bring internal; CEO: "So the Axon Ads site is a prompt at this point"

## Paid Marketing for Advertiser Acquisition

- **Currently Testing:** CEO: "we're testing right now actively"
- **Budget:** Small testing budget; even at scale would not be a large line item relative to revenue; CEO: "the scale of our business is quite large. So like some of the largest advertisers in the world can only spend a couple of hundred million dollars a year"
- **Launch Timing:** After platform opens beyond referral basis in 2026; CEO: "when we launch the platform beyond referral in 2026, we can scale advertiser count without a reliance on a large sales force"
- **Unit Economics Focus:** CEO: "because our LTV is so high, and we're optimizing our conversion rate...the setup is really good for promoting our product to potential end customers"
- **Sales Team:** Will remain lean; CEO: "we can keep a very lean sales team in line with our traditional culture if we can automate onboarding through advertising all the way to point of go live"

## Apple Direct Payments / Platform Tax Impact

- **No Contribution Yet:** CEO: "I don't think it's contributing much at all yet"
- **Expected Impact Trajectory:** Over coming quarters, not imminent; CEO: "I think it is going to be over the quarters that it's going to take impact"
- **Magnitude:** If 30% tax drops to ~15% midpoint, implies ~20% LTV lift for in-app purchasing games; CEO: "That's a material lift in LTV for a lot of these in-app purchasing games, roughly 20%"
- **Allocation of Savings by Publishers:** (1) Develop more content / better games; (2) Bank into bottom line; (3) Increase marketing spend (benefits AppLovin)

## Supply Dynamics

### MAX Marketplace
- **Growth:** Very healthy rates, continuing double-digit growth; CEO: "our MAX supply-side platform, one of the best indicators of our end market growth continues to grow at very healthy rates"
- **In-App Advertising vs. In-App Purchasing:** MAX (ad monetization) growing much faster than in-app purchasing market; CEO: "we're seeing much faster growth rates on the MAX platform...multiples faster than the in-app purchasing market grows"
- **Expansion Drivers:** (1) Higher quality ads from e-commerce demand diversity improve user retention and expand ad supply; (2) In-app purchasing publishers adding ad monetization as e-commerce ads provide non-competitive content; (3) Better growth and monetization tools for publishers

### Future Supply Expansion
- **Interest in Broader Supply:** Open web publishers, CTV, other app categories; CEO: "we've talked about CTV in the past, too. Everywhere else is struggling to monetize except in the walled gardens and except on games"
- **Timing:** Demand-first approach; CEO: "we're really heads down focused on the demand side of the platform because there's so much work to do there. At some point, you'll see us talking about both sides"
- **Potential Supply Interest:** CEO acknowledged being "pinged all the time" given performance advertising reputation

## Key Q&A Insights

### On Self-Serve Advertiser Profile (James Heaney, Jefferies)
- New advertisers are filtered through referrals; predominantly shops; comparable in mix to prior curated cohort; broad set of shopping categories
- CEO: "They're not going to be as large as they were in the cohort last year, but they're not going to be materially smaller either"

### On Q4 Guidance Philosophy (James Heaney, Jefferies)
- CFO: Guide reflects combination of e-commerce referral program, model enhancements, and holiday seasonality
- Not a good YoY comp since last year had entirely new e-commerce business ramping; this year has an existing base
- No incremental assumption for new advertisers from referral program

### On Conversion Rate as Primary Growth Lever (Omar Dessouky, BofA)
- Significant growth in impressions NOT required to absorb more e-commerce advertisers
- Key levers: (1) Model enhancements (continuous); (2) Advertiser density expansion + recommendation personalization; (3) Generative AI-based ad creatives
- CEO: "we are in the very, very beginnings of understanding how to extract this kind of value out of these tools and technologies"

### On Supply Growth Drivers (Omar Dessouky, BofA)
- Combination of: higher quality ads from diverse demand improving user retention; in-app purchasing publishers adding ad monetization; improved publisher tools
- CEO: "if we can unlock this material demand shift, then we're going to be able to bring more supply into the ecosystem"

### On 50% WoW Spend Growth Context (Jason Bazinet, Citi)
- CEO acknowledged it is very early (only 1 month in) and the absolute base is small
- Integration takes time: typically a week+ from sign-up to go live
- Value is proving the tool works and converts customers, not near-term revenue extrapolation
- CEO: "the fact that it's working already a month in, and we're not getting bombarded with customer complaints...implies that we're on the right track"

### On Gaming Displacement Risk (William Lampen, BTIG)
- No displacement expected; more demand density actually benefits gaming advertisers
- Model can better utilize the ~80-90% of impressions currently wasted (showing games to users unlikely to convert)
- Gaming CPMs may rise with fewer but more targeted impressions; net revenue outcome same or better
- Cross-category data enriches gaming targeting; CEO example: handbag buyer data helps identify gaming whales

### On Generative AI Creative Timeline (William Lampen, BTIG)
- Testing expected in weeks or months, not years
- Not gating to general platform release
- External tools (Sora 2, Veo 3) improving rapidly
- CEO: "we're probably not very far away...to be able to test generative AI-based creative"

### On Direct Payments / Platform Tax (Alec Brondolo, Wells Fargo)
- Not contributing materially yet; will take quarters
- If 30% drops to ~15% midpoint: ~20% LTV lift for IAP games
- Savings split among: better games, bottom line, and marketing spend

### On Potential Google Ad Tech Asset Acquisition (Alec Brondolo, Wells Fargo)
- CEO did not comment on specific assets; focused on organic product expansion
- Currently demand-constrained, not supply-constrained
- CTV, open web publishers remain potential future supply extension; CEO: "Everywhere else is struggling to monetize except in the walled gardens and except on games"

### On MAX Growth vs. In-App Purchasing (Vasily Karasyov, Cannonball)
- MAX (in-app advertising) growing multiples faster than in-app purchasing market
- In-app purchasing market more mature with fewer new monetization tools
- Direct payment tax reduction would be biggest tailwind for IAP growth

### On Platform Open Timeline & Criteria (Matthew Cost, Morgan Stanley)
- Gating on conversion funnel optimization, not a specific spend target
- CEO: "one day, I'd like to make sure that that local laundromat that signs up gets a great experience"
- Previous call guidance of 2026 for open launch reaffirmed
- CEO: "this isn't a very long time away"

### On Paid Marketing Magnitude (Matthew Cost, Morgan Stanley)
- Currently in active testing phase; testing budget is small
- Even at scale, "never going to be a very large line item against the revenue potential"
- Performance-marketing approach; will break out unit economics once at scale
- CEO: "We're not brand marketers. The dollars will be much greater than the dollars we spend on user acquisition"

### On E-Commerce Take Rate (Benjamin Black, Deutsche Bank)
- No structural difference between e-commerce and gaming take rates; unified auction
- Advertising credits are immaterial vs. overall customer value
- More density potentially means higher take (better conversion rate = more value captured)

### On Compute/AI Investment Outlook (Benjamin Black, Deutsche Bank)
- Pay-as-you-go model; GPUs purchased ~1 year in advance
- Runs through P&L (not capitalized)
- Cost discipline maintained; CEO: "we don't try to really overinvest ahead of revenue"

### On EU Web Traffic (Chris Kuntarich, UBS)
- EU traffic for web/shop advertisers NOT yet open; GDPR build-out required
- EU is low teens percentage of business; not a priority vs. general platform release and other tools

### On International Expansion (Rob Sanderson, Loop Capital)
- Available everywhere except EU and China for web/shop advertisers; app advertisers available everywhere except China
- Most success in English-speaking markets (Canada, Australia, New Zealand)
- Localization no longer a challenge with LLMs
- CEO: "humans aren't behaving much differently in Japan or Korea or Canada or Australia to the U.S."

### On Existing Cohort Performance (Martin Yang, Oppenheimer)
- Continuously improving ROAS for customers over the past year
- Tooling improvements (old dashboard to Axon Ads Manager)
- Customer knowledge compounding over time (e.g., understanding 35-second vs. 7-second ad format)

### On October PSU Issuance (Martin Yang, Oppenheimer)
- Pool for group of engineers; also future recruiting tool for new engineering hires
- CFO: "It's a pool, Martin, for a group of engineers, but it's also a future tool that we can use for recruiting as well for new hires into the engineering team"

### On Ad Quality Filtering (James Callahan, Piper Sandler)
- Current bar: team would be willing to buy the product themselves
- Standard will loosen over time as advertiser density increases and impression frequency dilutes
- Low-quality advertisers today are often real/substantial businesses running on social and search; just not meeting AppLovin's current high bar

### On Supply Runway (Nat Schindler, Scotiabank)
- Many years of conversion rate improvement ahead before supply becomes a constraint
- Social media analogy: growth has come from higher conversion rates enabled by better technology, not more users
- Broader supply (open web, CTV) is of interest but demand-side work is priority

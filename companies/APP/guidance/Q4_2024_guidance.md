## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

### Advertising Business Long-Term Growth
- **Baseline Growth:** 20% YoY organic growth from gaming alone; CEO: "our business predictably has got 20% year-over-year growth in it...from just the baseline of operating within the category that we've always traditionally operated"
- **Upside Catalysts (Unpredictable but Positive):** (1) AXON model enhancements -- "really, really early still in AI development and research, both inside our company and then obviously externally"; (2) E-commerce and new vertical expansion -- additive to the 20% baseline
- **Data Center Cost Drag:** CFO: "roughly 10% on an annual basis, is that drag from incremental data center costs as we grow revenue"
- **Adjusted EBITDA Per Employee:** CEO's "favorite metric going forward"; ~$3M run rate per advertising employee in Q4 2024; "we expect that number to rise as we refine processes and scale our business"
- **Advertising EBITDA Margin Target:** 78%-79% guided for Q1 2025; flow-through expected to normalize post-GPU step-up

### Capital Allocation
- **Commitment:** CFO: "illustrating the commitment we've communicated to drive shareholder value through prudent capital allocation and an investment in our own shares"
- **FY2024 example:** $2.1B FCF generated, $2.1B returned via buybacks/withholdings

## Apps Divestiture

- **Transaction Announced:** Signed exclusive term sheet to sell entire Apps business
- **Total Consideration:** $900M ($500M cash + minority equity stake in combined private company for the remainder)
- **Expected Close:** Targeting Q2 2025; CFO: "we're targeting for that to close within Q2. Obviously, we may be subject to regulatory approval, so that timing may change slightly"
- **Structure:** Entirety of Apps business sold at once; CFO: "all come off of the P&L and the balance sheet all at once"
- **Strategic Rationale:** CEO: "we've never been a game developer at heart"; studios originally acquired to train earliest ML models; company transitioning to "pure advertising platform"
- **Post-Close Impact:** Apps revenue (~$325M-$335M/quarter) and Apps EBITDA (~$50M-$60M/quarter) will be removed entirely; remaining company will be pure-play advertising at ~78%+ EBITDA margins

## E-Commerce / Web Advertising Expansion

### Status and Results
- **Q4 2024 was the first quarter with meaningful e-commerce holiday shopping revenue contribution**; CEO: "For the first time, we captured meaningful holiday shopping advertising dollars and witnessed the impact of an advertising category beyond solely gaming contributing to our growth"
- **Not Breaking Out Revenue by Vertical:** CEO: "we're not breaking out revenue by vertical, because that's not how we view our business"
- **Still in Limited Pilot:** E-commerce pilot includes "not in the tens...certainly not in the thousands" of advertisers; limited by the ~20-person go-to-market team
- **2025 Material Contribution Expected:** CFO: "We still feel very confident...in the ability for us to contribute a material portion of revenue from the e-commerce opportunity in 2025"
- **Timeline Unpredictable:** CFO: "it's very difficult to predict when and how much that growth is going to come in during the period in 2025"

### Go-to-Market Focus
- **Current Target:** Mid-market D2C brands with $10M-$250M GMV; CEO: "companies that tend to move fast, and they don't need a big team on the other side"
- **Not Yet Targeting:** Very large brands or very small businesses (until self-serve is ready)
- **Demand Exceeds Capacity:** CEO: "there's a long, long line out the door, which means we're onboarding as we can onboard"
- **Self-Serve Priority for 2025:** CEO: "Our priority this year is to develop and roll out more automated tools to allow countless new businesses to tap into our platform"; self-serve will include AI agents to help advertisers onboard
- **Content Moderation Required:** CEO: "you have to build a lot of tools and content moderation controls when you open up a platform as big as ours"
- **No Rush to Accelerate:** CEO: "I don't know that we have any need to accelerate growth right now. We're growing really, really quickly as it is and controlling the pace"

### Beyond DTC / Multi-Vertical Validation
- **Works Across All Categories:** CEO: "we're seeing success across any category that comes on to the platform"; "we've been seeing that the models are working there" across a wide range of non-DTC categories
- **Works Out of the Box:** CEO: "we don't have the resources to be building custom models for every single vertical...these companies in other categories across a wide range are just plugging in and it works"
- **Early Pilots:** Positive outcomes for fintech, automotive, and other categories beyond DTC; CEO: "any business in any vertical can harness the power of our platform"
- **TAM:** "over 10 million businesses worldwide who advertise online that could eventually use our platform profitably"
- **Incrementality Value Proposition:** CEO: "The users engaging with our network aren't just shifting existing purchases. They're discovering new products while playing the games they love, generating truly incremental demand"
- **Not Cannibalizing Competitors:** CEO: "we're not a platform that's taking the same dollars away from someone else...they're now going to be spending $10,000 a day" (i.e., expanding total ad spend rather than shifting)

### Non-Gaming App Install Advertising
- **Not a 2025 Inflection:** CEO: "it's just something that's in our road map that we're actively working on...it's not something that is a '25 thing"; expected to open new categories later in 2025 and into 2026+

## AXON / Core Technology Update

### Q4 Model Performance
- **No Step-Function Enhancement in Q4:** CEO: "This quarter, we did not have one of those [step-function model enhancements]"
- **Three Growth Vectors Outlined:**
  1. **Ongoing self-learning:** Model continuously improving itself; "We haven't seen a slowdown there"
  2. **Iterative incremental improvements:** "happens along the way...not huge...not those step functions"
  3. **Major model upgrades (AXON version jumps):** Did not occur in Q4; analogy to ChatGPT version upgrades
- **Q4 Growth Drivers Instead:** Holiday seasonality (both gaming engagement and e-commerce shopping) plus baseline model self-learning
- **E-Commerce Model Maturity:** CEO: "That model in this category is a couple of years behind on the evolution of a model to the gaming model...we're playing catch-up to the efficacy of that gaming model"
- **28-Day Models:** Rolled out a couple of quarters ago; "the most highly, most performant models that we deliver"; have driven increased ability for gaming customers to spend more profitably

### Personalized Ad Experiences (2025 Initiative)
- **Generative AI-Enabled Ads:** On the road map for 2025; vision is to create many variations of ads personalized per user instead of showing 1 of 20 static human-uploaded creatives
- **Challenges:** Full-screen video is harder than text ads; HTML/mini-games in gaming realm add complexity; GPU processing constraints
- **Status:** Research and development stage; CEO: "It's something that we're working on and we think will be impactful when it rolls out"

## Supply Dynamics and CTV / Wurl

### Current Supply
- **1 billion+ daily active users** in mobile games; CEO: "supply has been pretty constant, and I think we grew 80%, 90%"
- **Supply Not Currently Constraining:** CEO: "The only reason we're not better monetizing today where we would say maybe we're supply constrained is because we don't have every type of advertiser across the world on our platform yet"
- **Demand Expansion is Primary Growth Driver:** CEO: "The supply side is just gravy on top...we certainly don't think it's going to be anywhere near as impactful as that demand expansion"

### New Supply Opportunities
- **In-App Purchase Games:** Large gaming apps that currently don't run ads (because ads were for competing games) will now be incentivized to join MAX as non-gaming ad categories grow; King/Activision precedent cited
- **MAX Publisher Excitement:** Publishers commenting positively on impressions shifting to non-gaming categories; CEO: "When you see that commentary, they're absolutely excited about it"

### CTV / Wurl
- **Not Yet Focused On:** CEO: "we haven't really focused on it yet at all"
- **Supply Infrastructure Built:** Wurl connected to media companies; supply is live
- **Historical Limitation:** Only gaming ads available for CTV slots; "a gaming ad on a full-screen television asking a user to go download a game on the phone, it's just not that compelling"
- **Future Opportunity:** As consumer/D2C/fintech/automotive ads come on platform, extending to big screen could be compelling; CEO: "it will open up another massive channel of performance advertising on the big screen probably for the first time in history"
- **Challenges:** Attribution problems; lack of call to action; CEO: "we're not saying, hey, this is a layup. It's a challenge"
- **Timeline:** Building into 2025; CEO: "one that we're going to be going after building into this year"

## Take Rate Dynamics

- **Take Rate Not Managed Explicitly:** CEO: "we don't optimize the take rate. We never have. So the system is willing to buy a user at 1% or 99%"
- **Natural Expansion:** As models improve, take rate naturally increases; CEO: "as we get better at monetizing the inventory that we buy, the take rate naturally goes up"
- **Market Dynamics:** CEO: "We're paying the highest price the publishing can get from anywhere"

---

## Seasonality Considerations

- **E-Commerce Seasonality:** CFO: "obviously, Black Friday and the holiday periods, we'll see slightly larger periods of revenue"
- **Q1 Seasonality:** CEO: "in a traditional advertising business, typically, 1Q steps down. You have 2 less days, and you've got seasonality effect in the fourth quarter"
- **Expense Stability:** CFO: "On the expense side, it shouldn't affect us much whatsoever. Obviously, the expenses are primarily data center costs and payroll"
- **Q4 to Q1 E-Commerce Behavior:** CEO: inventory depletion after holidays leads to temporary spend pullback, then ramp back; "they'll spike. They'll drop. They'll come back"

## Operational / HR Update

- **CEO Took Over HR Function:** Focus on streamlining teams and processes; CEO: "my job was to go, where are areas of the business that are not perfectly aligned with these organic opportunities, and let's start shedding those"
- **Restructuring Costs:** $16.8M in Q4 2024; $22.8M for full year 2024
- **Lean Culture Emphasis:** CEO: "We're going to continue to stay diligent and lean, and we're going to use tools and AI and automation"; automation and AI agents preferred over hiring

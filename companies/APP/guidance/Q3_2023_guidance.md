## Q3 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Consolidated Results
- **Total Revenue:** $864M (+21% YoY; **BEAT** top end of prior-quarter guidance range of $780-$800M by ~$64M)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $490M (+63% YoY; **BEAT** top end of prior-quarter guidance range of $340-$360M by ~$130M)
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 48.5% — **highest EBITDA margin in 5 years** (vs. 44%-45% guided; improvement of ~400bps QoQ on top of 600bps improvement from Q2 over Q1 2023; ~100bps benefited from onetime nonrecurring cost items)

|--------|--------------------------------|----------------|-------------|

| Adjusted EBITDA | $340M - $360M | $490M | **+$130M above high end** |

### Software Platform Segment
- **Software Platform Revenue:** $504M — **record quarterly revenue** (+65% YoY vs. ~$307M in Q3 2022; +24% QoQ vs. $406M in Q2 2023; 3rd consecutive quarter of double-digit QoQ revenue growth)
- **Software Platform Annualized Run Rate:** $2B+
- **Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA:** $364M (+91% YoY; +33% QoQ vs. $273M in Q2 2023)
- **Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 72% — **record margin** (vs. 67% in Q2 2023; vs. 62% in Q3 2022)
- **Software Platform EBITDA % of Total:** ~90% of company's total Adjusted EBITDA (up from >80% in Q2 2023)
- **Key Revenue Driver:** AXON 2.0 engine — first full quarter plus continued self-learning improvements; better ROAS driving higher advertiser spend across gaming and non-gaming verticals

### Apps Segment
- **Apps Revenue:** $360M (+5% QoQ vs. $344M in Q2 2023) — **first quarter of sequential revenue growth** since portfolio optimization program began
- **Apps Adjusted EBITDA:** $55M
- **Apps Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 15%
- **Growth Driver:** Increased UA spend leveraging own AXON 2.0 platform (AppLovin as customer of its own system)

### Cash Flow and Balance Sheet
- **Free Cash Flow:** $194M (lower flow-through from Adjusted EBITDA than normal due to temporary delay in certain cash collections; expected to reverse in Q4)
- **Free Cash Flow as % of Adjusted EBITDA:** ~40% (below normalized 50%-60% range due to timing of collections)
- **Cash and Cash Equivalents (End of Period):** $332M
- **Revolver Draw:** $185M drawn on revolving credit facility during the quarter

### Capital Returns
- **Share Repurchases (Q3 2023):** $582M of cash used to buy back stock
- **Share Repurchases (YTD through End of Q3 2023):** $1.2B of Class A common stock at a weighted average price of under $25 per share
- **Capital Allocation Focus:** Consistent with asset allocation plan focused on driving long-term shareholder value

### Debt Activity
- **Term Loan Amendment:** Amended a portion of term loans in Q3 — extended maturity to 2030, reduced principal by ~$249M ($250M), and improved credit spread
- **Term Loan Paydown:** $249M used to pay down term loan in the quarter

## Quarterly Guidance (Q4 2023)

### Consolidated
- **Total Revenue:** $910M to $930M (midpoint $920M; vs. Q3 2023 actual of $864M; +5% to +8% QoQ)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $420M to $440M (midpoint $430M; vs. Q3 2023 actual of $490M)
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 46% to 47% (vs. Q3 2023 actual of 48.5%)

### Commentary on Q4 2023 Margin Outlook
- Margin guided slightly down from Q3's 48.5% due to two factors:
  1. **Onetime items rolling off:** ~100bps of Q3 margin benefit was from nonrecurring cost items that will not repeat in Q4
  2. **Incremental investments:** Potential for further investments in the business in Q4 on both the software and apps side — Herald Chen: "the fourth quarter, we are considering some additional investments on the growth side, both on the software and app side"

### Implied Q4 2023 Segment Outlook (Based on Management Commentary)
- **Software Platform:** Continued growth expected; if Apps are roughly flat, implies ~9% to 13% QoQ Software Platform revenue growth (per analyst question); AXON 2.0 compounding effect continues as primary driver
- **Apps:** Both sides of the business expected to grow — Herald Chen: "we see both sides of the business growing"; Apps expected to continue positive momentum from first sequential growth quarter in Q3; increased UA spend rolling into Q4
- **EBITDA Translation:** Software Platform growth should continue to translate to EBITDA; some step-down from Q3 due to onetime items and incremental investments

## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

### Software Platform
- **Revenue Run Rate:** $2B annually as of Q3 2023; management excited about further growth potential within core markets and new initiatives (Wurl, Array)
- **Margin Trajectory:** 72% Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA margin is a new record; Herald Chen expects software margin to continue growing and expanding as AppDiscovery grows, given net revenue model and relatively fixed R&D cost structure
- **Flow-Through:** High flow-through from revenue to Adjusted EBITDA as the business scales — Herald Chen: "Our software platform continues to demonstrate high flow-through from revenue to adjusted EBITDA as we scale"
- **Cost Structure:** Data center infrastructure costs largely fixed; company has grown into/through initial scale contract from approximately a year ago and is fully utilizing capacity
- **New Initiatives (CTV, Array):** Will likely require fixed investments and team hires as they scale; also strong margin businesses at scale but unlikely to start as high as AppDiscovery contribution — Herald Chen: "those are also strong margin businesses as they scale up as well, but unlikely to start as high as the contribution that we do get from app discovery"

### Apps Segment
- **Run-Rate EBITDA Margin:** 15% in Q3 2023; portfolio optimization major parts complete; management focused on balancing growth and cash flow to optimize financial performance and enterprise value
- **Growth Strategy:** Investing more in UA leveraging AXON 2.0 platform; expect continued investment rolling into Q4

### Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA Run Rate
- **Annualized Run Rate:** >$1.6B (referenced by Herald Chen: "adjusted EBITDA as multiplied from a couple of hundred million dollars of run rate to over $1.6 billion run rate today")

### Free Cash Flow Conversion
- **Normalized Run Rate:** 50% to 60% of Adjusted EBITDA (unchanged from prior guidance)
- **Q3 2023 Actual:** ~40% (below normalized range; temporary due to delayed cash collections expected to reverse in Q4)
- **Long-Term Trajectory:** Flow-through percentage should increase over time as high cash flow converting Software Platform business continues to grow faster than Apps — Herald Chen: "This flow-through percentage should increase over time as our high cash flow converting software platform business continues to grow faster than the apps"

## AXON 2.0 / Core Technology Update

- **Status:** Brand-new technology in "first inning" — Adam Foroughi: "a new technology that we've seen is one, been game changing for our business and is too in the first inning"
- **Self-Learning:** AI models continuously improve themselves as they scale; engineering team also continuously improves them in parallel
- **Performance:** Delivers better results for advertisers; advertisers spending more dollars in a material way at better returns; compounding effect
- **Q3 Over-Performance Attribution:** AXON 2.0 was the "vast majority" of Q3 over-performance
- **Competitive Positioning:** Management views technology as cutting-edge and one of the leading AI solutions in the market; large deployment of software and hardware; material amount of data — Foroughi: "We've set a new standard, and we're going to go build on that and that hopefully will lead to many quarters of growth coming up"
- **AXON 3 Timeline:** Not being discussed; focus remains on extracting growth from AXON 2.0 for multiple quarters ahead — Foroughi: "We're not talking about AXON 3. We're talking about a lot of excitement about multiple quarters of growth coming up from what we put together here"
- **Privacy Adaptability:** Company has dealt with privacy changes since 2014; nimble and able to rewrite core technology as needed; always adapts and performs through platform/regulatory changes

## Advertiser Diversification Beyond Gaming

- **Non-Gaming Performance:** AXON 2.0 works agnostically across all app categories — Foroughi: "we've seen it agnostic of the category"
- **Non-Gaming Growth Rate:** Growing faster than gaming because starting from a materially smaller base; "accelerated growth on non-gaming advertisers"
- **Gaming Dominance:** Gaming remains majority of business
- **Sales Effort Required:** Foroughi: "It will take a sales effort to substantially grow non-gaming so that it can become a much more material part of the business"
- **Technology Validation:** "the technology works very effectively regardless of the type of app"

## Connected TV (CTV) / Wurl Strategy

- **AXON 2.0 Integration:** AXON 2.0 integrated into CTV initiative during testing phase in Q3; planning to scale up in subsequent quarters — Foroughi: "This quarter, we made strides by integrating AXON 2 into our CTV initiative during its testing phase"
- **Wurl Timeline:** Acquired approximately 1.5 years ago (April 2022); team fully integrated; built out SSP and product offerings on CTV; large inventory base available
- **Performance Marketing on TV:** Foroughi: "performance marketing on TV hasn't really been a thing anywhere near as much as it has been on desktop or on mobile devices" — sees substantial opportunity to fill the gap with a superior performance solution
- **Revenue Impact:** No specific revenue quantification; management believes CTV and Array have "the potential to become meaningful contributors to our annual revenue" given the magnitude of the software platform business ($2B run rate)

## Array (OEM/Carrier Marketing Platform)

- **AXON 2.0 Extension:** Extending AXON 2.0 to Array this quarter; expected to "materially accelerate the potential to scale that business" — Foroughi
- **Positioning:** Gets AppLovin on Android devices in a "much more intimate way"; presents multiple new ad offerings to consumers
- **Revenue Impact:** No specific financial details; combined with CTV, expected to become meaningful revenue contributor over time
- **Foundation:** Foundation is in place; AXON 2.0 application expected to accelerate growth path

---

## MAX Mediation Update

- **Google Bidding Transition:** Google shifting to header bidding (real-time bidding); AppLovin has taken most of the market to bidding already; above 60% of market on bidding; once Google moves, will be very close to full market conversion
- **Competitive Impact of Google Bidding:** Not a zero-sum game — when networks move to bidding, share moves around only slightly, the bidder may get more efficient, but the overall pie grows
- **Ecosystem Flywheel:** Publisher yields more from efficient marketplace -> publishers spend more on UA -> dollars flow back into ecosystem -> pie grows -> all parties benefit — Foroughi: "that's the trend we've seen now for 5 to 6 years since we launched the MAX platform"
- **Philosophy:** Foroughi: "we never look at share. It's not a zero-sum game. You need all the marketing companies to do well"

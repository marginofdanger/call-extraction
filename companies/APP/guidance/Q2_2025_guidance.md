## Q2 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Advertising Segment Results (Continuing Operations Only)
*Note: Apps business sale to Tripledot Studios closed at the end of Q2 2025. Apps segment results are reported within discontinued operations. All commentary is limited to the advertising business only.*

- **Advertising Revenue:** ~$1.260B (+77% YoY; CFO: "revenue increased by a very healthy 77% from last year to approximately $1.260 billion"; vs. $1.16B in Q1 2025 = +8.6% QoQ; vs. $711M in Q2 2024 = +77.2% YoY)
- **Advertising Adjusted EBITDA:** $1.020B (CFO: "adjusted EBITDA nearly doubled to an impressive $1.020 billion"; vs. $943M in Q1 2025 = +8.2% QoQ; ~+96% YoY vs. $520M in Q2 2024)
- **Advertising Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 81% (CFO: "achieving an 81% adjusted EBITDA margin"; flat QoQ vs. 81% in Q1 2025; vs. 73% in Q2 2024 = +800bps YoY)
- **QoQ Revenue-to-EBITDA Flow-Through:** 81% (CFO: "Quarter-over-quarter flow-through from revenue to adjusted EBITDA was a very strong 81%"; i.e., 81% of incremental QoQ revenue converted to incremental EBITDA)
- **Revenue Growth Drivers:** (1) Majority of growth from core gaming business (CFO: "The majority of our revenue growth in the quarter was driven by our core gaming business"); (2) E-commerce performing well but onboarding constrained
- **Free Cash Flow:** $768M (+72% YoY; CFO: "we generated $768 million in free cash flow, up a staggering 72% year-over-year"; vs. $826M in Q1 2025 = -7.0% QoQ)
- **FCF Lower QoQ Reason:** Timing of semiannual bond interest payments and certain taxes associated with the prior year (CFO: "Our free cash flow was slightly lower than last quarter due to the timing of payments for interest on our bonds, which are semiannual and certain taxes associated with the prior year")
- **Cash and Cash Equivalents (End of Q2):** $1.2B (CFO: "we had $1.2 billion in cash and cash equivalents"; includes $425M net cash from Apps sale; vs. $551M end of Q1 2025)
- **Net Cash from Apps Sale:** $425M (CFO: "which includes $425 million in net cash received from the sale of the Apps business")
- **Share Repurchases & Withholding (Q2 2025):** ~900K shares for a total cost of $341M (CFO: "we repurchased and withheld approximately 900,000 shares for a total cost of $341 million funded through free cash flow")
- **Weighted Average Diluted Shares Outstanding:** 342M (CFO: "reduce our weighted average diluted common shares outstanding this year from 346 million in the fourth quarter to 342 million this quarter"; vs. 346M in Q4 2024, 338M end of Q1 2025)

## AXON Self-Service Platform Launch

### AXON Ads Manager
- **Quietly Launched:** CEO: "Recently, we took the first step towards opening up our platform broadly, quietly launching our new AXON ads manager, our self-service portal, which will serve as the foundation for our next decade of growth"
- **Features/Benefits:**
  - Day-to-day controls directly in advertisers' hands, reducing friction
  - Credit card billing, eliminating monthly invoicing hassle
  - Architecture for agents to eventually automate every workflow
  - Framework for automatically generated ads
  - Simplified onboarding through Shopify app integration
  - Deeper integrations with attribution providers for more accurate reporting
- **Shopify App:** Live in the Shopify App Store; creates one-click integration (CEO: "We got the Shopify app integration live...you can find the app in the Shopify App Store")
- **Dynamic Product Ads:** Launched; CEO: "We got dynamic product ads live that made a material uplift"; pairs video with dynamic product ad for higher intent

### Launch Timeline
- **October 1, 2025 -- Referral-Based Opening:** CEO: "On October 1, 2025, we plan to open the AXON ads manager on a referral basis, perfectly timed for the holiday season"
  - Existing advertisers can invite/refer their colleagues to the platform
  - CEO: "accounts on the platform...will get the chance to refer their colleagues into our platform and have them go live in a self-service way"
  - Not a full public open; still curated onboarding via invitation
  - CEO: "They're still going to have to get invited to get into the platform"
- **October 1, 2025 -- International Expansion:** CEO: "On October 1, we plan to open our platform to most major international markets"
  - Web advertising campaigns have been limited to the United States through Q2
  - Opening to most major international markets simultaneously
- **H1 2026 -- Global Public Launch:** CEO: "Feedback from these partners will guide our global public launch in the first half of 2026"
- **Post-Public Launch -- Paid Marketing:** CEO: "Once AXON is fully open next year, we plan to begin paid marketing to recruit new advertisers, which will drive predictable compounding growth"

### AXON Brand Standalone
- CEO: "The opportunity is so big that we will be launching the platform under its own brand, AXON"

### Agentic / AI Capabilities (Phased)
- **Phase 1 (Near-Term):** Onboarding chatbot/widget to answer questions and guide advertisers; CEO: "That's sort of an onboarding widget...is something that's probably going to be in present early in the release of this product"
- **Phase 2 (In Development):** Analysis agent for campaign performance, ad creative analysis; CEO: "an agent that does everything for you...analyze the performance, talk to me about the ads I'm running, that's a complex agent task"
- **Phase 3 (Tools):** Generative AI-based ad creative tools for automatic video/end card creation; CEO: "we're going to give our customers the ability to automatically create advertisements, whether video or end card"
  - Enables small businesses without ad production resources to compete with larger brands
  - Increases ad diversity on the platform for better model personalization

## E-Commerce / Web Advertising Expansion

### Status and Scale
- **E-Commerce Share of Revenue:** ~10% of advertising revenue for the year (as targeted); CEO: "where we were at was that sort of 10% range that we were targeting for the year"
- **E-Commerce Share May Decline Near-Term Due to Gaming Strength:** CEO: "There's a reason I expect it to have grown above that because gaming is growing so quickly"
- **Advertiser Count:** Hundreds; ~600 previously disclosed; CEO: "hundreds of e-commerce businesses live on the platform"
- **Market Penetration:** Sub-1% of total addressable market; CEO: "we're certainly sub-1%"
- **GMV Threshold for New Onboarding:** Raised to $100M+ in Q2 to constrain platform (CEO: "We raised it up this quarter to $100 million plus to really constrain what's coming on the platform")
- **Constrained Onboarding:** Deliberate pause on new advertiser onboarding for 2 quarters while building out platform capabilities; CEO: "we ended up constraining the advertiser onboarding process for a couple of quarters while we made sure that the product was at the level that we wanted to get it to"

### Q4 2025 E-Commerce Inflection Expected
- CEO: "Q4 is going to end up being a fun quarter"
  - (1) Existing advertiser cohort will be primed for full holiday season (unlike Q4 2024 when cohort was still ramping)
  - (2) Referral-based onboarding opens October 1, bringing new advertisers
  - (3) International markets open simultaneously
  - CEO: "not only are you going to see the cohort that we have live spend a lot more. You're also going to have new onboarding happening for the first time in our history at a rate that's much higher than we will have ever seen before"

### Long-Term E-Commerce Vision
- **Potential Future Mix:** CEO: "will 1 day our business become 90% web-based advertising, 10% mobile gaming? I don't know"
- **TAM Much Larger Than Gaming:** CEO: "The opportunity in that category, both in terms of advertiser count and in terms of scale of TAM is much greater than gaming"
- **Data Flywheel:** E-commerce data improves gaming model and vice versa; CEO: "the data in our platform is not unique to one or the other part of the business"; example: someone buying a $4,000 handbag is likely a whale for a match-three game
- **International Revenue Opportunity:** U.S. vs. rest of world roughly half-half in total business; CEO: "it's roughly half-half on domestic U.S. versus rest of world"
- **International E-Commerce Upside:** CEO: "if we open up all these other countries for the most part, is 1 going to become 2 overnight. Probably not...but there's a lot of markets that look and feel similar to the U.S."

### Web Advertising Product Progress (Completed in Q2)
- Shopify app integration -- live and approved in Shopify App Store
- Dynamic product ads -- live, material uplift to advertiser performance
- In-depth integrations with attribution companies
- Self-service ads manager (AXON) -- quietly launched
- CEO: "last call, I laid out a list that I thought would take quarters, and our team knocked out most of that list in a single quarter"

### Comparison to Meta
- Meta is dominant incumbent in D2C web advertising; CEO: "it surprised us how much of the market Meta advertising was in the D2C space"
- Key differences from Meta: (1) No persistent email identifier for exclusion targeting; (2) Full-screen ad format drives higher intent; (3) Faster conversion window (vast majority within 1-2 hours vs. Meta's longer attribution); (4) Fully automated targeting -- no manual audience overrides
- CEO philosophy: "We don't allow gaming companies to use any sort of manual targeting in our platform...We bring the same view to this category"

## Supply Dynamics

### MAX Marketplace
- **Growth Rate:** Double-digit, consistently; CEO: "the base platform, just the audience inside MAX, is growing swiftly, double-digit growth, so multiples greater than what you would expect when people try to size up the gaming market growth rates of 3% to 5%"
- **Penetration:** Very high; not growing primarily from taking mediation share; CEO: "we're not going to be able to grow it particularly quickly by taking more mediation from other platforms at this point"
- **Revenue Model:** (1) MAX fee on every transaction in the marketplace (including non-AppLovin bidders); (2) AppDiscovery DSP revenue when it wins inventory
- **Ecosystem Dynamic:** As marketing platforms improve, the whole marketplace grows; CEO: "The eyeballs playing games play more games every single day. Inventory goes up"

### Future Supply Expansion
- Not years away if demand broadens sufficiently; CEO: "I don't think it's going to be years down the road"
- Could include: social networks, music apps, news apps, sports apps, websites
- CEO: "There's absolutely no reason why we wouldn't want to plug into other properties"
- Games represent only 10-15% of user time on mobile; CEO: "games are probably somewhere in the neighborhood of 10% to 15% of their time spent on mobile"

## Paid Advertiser Acquisition Strategy (New)

### Timing and Approach
- **When:** After AXON platform is fully open in H1 2026; CEO: "Once AXON is fully open next year, we plan to begin paid marketing to recruit new advertisers"
- **Channels:** Own inventory (ads in games to small business owners), Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok; CEO: "It's plausible that we will be using our own models to recruit advertisers off of our own inventory...You can imagine us running ads on Facebook, on LinkedIn, on TikTok"
- **Philosophy:** CEO: "we've got such a lucrative financial model...we believe that if we can automate the onboarding of advertiser flow from when an advertiser can find out about us from an ad all the way through to going live and then scaling on our platform, the model will be very lucrative on an LTV to CAC basis, and we won't have to staff up a large sales force"
- **Unit Economics:** Expect high LTV-to-CAC ratio given >80% EBITDA margins; CEO: "we won't have to staff up a large sales force"
- **P&L Impact:** Will appear in sales and marketing line; not expected to meaningfully compress margins given business scale; CEO: "I wouldn't expect you'll even see it in the margin number. It's just the business is way too big"

### Referral Program (October 1, 2025)
- No formal referral bonuses planned; CEO: "we don't think a ticket into our system is really worth paying for"
- Existing clients expected to invite peers organically; CEO: "we think as we give them the capacity to invite their peers, they're going to do it on their own because the platform has been restrictive and exclusive for so long"
- Expected to increase advertiser count "quite quickly"

## Apple vs. Epic Lawsuit Impact

- **No Impact Yet:** CEO: "Not yet"; expects longer timeline than market anticipates
- **Timeline:** 2-4 quarters for some impact; 4-8 quarters for material pricing impact on AppLovin's platform; CEO: "I would guess it will probably take 2 to 4 quarters from some impact. And by 4 to 8 quarters, you're going to get pretty material impact in pricing on our platform"
- **Mechanism:** Largest gaming companies move slowly and operate cautiously around big platforms; CEO: "the biggest gaming companies tend to move really slowly and tend to operate in fear of the big platforms"
- **Dynamic:** Once leaders adopt, smaller/mid-sized developers will follow quickly, compounding benefit to ad platforms

## Key Q&A Insights

### On Paid Marketing Decision (Matthew Cost, Morgan Stanley)
- CEO explained rationale for paid advertiser acquisition starting in 2026: small penetration, lucrative financial model, and strong performance marketing capabilities
- CEO: "It's plausible that we will be using our own models to recruit advertisers off of our own inventory. There's plenty of moms with small businesses and dads with small businesses sitting in games, playing games all day that could use our platform to market themselves"
- Key philosophy: automate the entire funnel from ad exposure to advertiser going live, making LTV-to-CAC highly attractive without large sales force

### On Supply Growth Sources (Matthew Cost, Morgan Stanley)
- Not primarily from mediation share gains (already very high penetration)
- Growth from expanding audience/inventory within existing MAX marketplace
- Double-digit growth in MAX audience base vs. 3-5% in-app purchasing gaming market growth
- AppLovin benefits from being marketplace operator (MAX fee) plus DSP bidder

### On Self-Serve Platform Impact (Ralph Schackart, William Blair)
- CEO framed historical context: Q4 2024 saw huge ramp from e-commerce pilot recruitment; Q1 2025 saw full benefit of that run rate
- Subsequent quarters constrained by deliberate onboarding pause while building tools (ads manager, dynamic product ads, attribution integrations, Shopify app)
- Q4 2025 expected to see new inflection: existing cohort primed for holidays + new referral onboarding + international opening

### On E-Commerce Percentage of Revenue (Ralph Schackart, William Blair)
- Roughly at 10% target for the year
- May actually be declining as a percentage because gaming growth is so strong (30-40% YoY)
- CEO expects substantial ramp in Q4 from holiday season, referral-based onboarding, and international expansion

### On Game Engine Data / Unity (Omar Dessouky, BofA)
- CEO dismissive of "game engine data" concept; AppLovin has behavioral data from being deeply integrated as publisher and advertiser within apps
- Market penetration "materially higher than 70%" gives strong visibility into gaming category
- Future expansion across categories will provide much richer cross-category consumer behavior data

### On Referral Program Mechanics (Chris Kuntarich, UBS)
- No referral bonuses planned; platform exclusivity itself is the perk
- Not all waitlisted advertisers will be auto-admitted; still need an invite
- Iterative approach to referral program mechanics

### On Q3 Guide Including Apps Divestiture Benefit (Chris Kuntarich, UBS)
- CFO confirmed Q3 guide is "slightly different than kind of our typical cadence" by including benefit from divested studios becoming external advertisers
- Incremental pickup in revenue beyond the 3-5% reinforcement learning baseline

### On Model Parity Between Gaming and E-Commerce (Jason Bazinet, Citi)
- Two architecturally distinct models (app-to-app store vs. app-to-website)
- Gaming model benefits from years of data and very high market penetration
- E-commerce model still early but showed immediate efficacy even with no prior category knowledge
- Cross-category data flywheel is key: CEO: "every single quarter, you're going to have that flywheel effect"

### On Web Advertiser Feature Requests (Rob Sanderson, Loop Capital)
- Advertisers want Meta-like targeting features (exclude existing customers, etc.)
- AppLovin lacks persistent email identifier for exact exclusion matching
- CEO pushes fully automated approach: no manual targeting overrides; same philosophy as gaming
- CEO: "All these technologies in advertising are going to move to AI, automating most of this funnel in the future"

### On Self-Serve Readiness (Rob Sanderson, Loop Capital)
- CEO: "last call, I laid out a list that I thought would take quarters, and our team knocked out most of that list in a single quarter"
- Confidence to open platform driven by substantial growth from existing cohort and strong feedback

### On Incremental Margins (Bernie McTernan, Needham)
- New supply sources will be reported as net revenue; no margin compression
- Paid advertiser acquisition will be a new cost line in sales & marketing, but tested and only deployed if unit economics prove out
- CEO: "we're only going to spend the dollars on performance marketing if we're printing cash on the other side"

### On Customer Profile Changes (Clark Lampen, BTIG)
- GMV threshold was raised to $100M+ this quarter to constrain onboarding
- Goal is to serve any small business of any type; CEO: "any small business of any size be able to acquire customers profitably"
- If referral phase proves model works for all business sizes, platform will open broadly

### On Supply Expansion Beyond Gaming (Clark Lampen, BTIG)
- CEO: "I don't think it's going to be years down the road"; could happen "pretty quickly" if demand broadens sufficiently
- Potential supply: social networks, music apps, news apps, sports apps, websites
- CEO: "There's absolutely no reason why we wouldn't want to plug into other properties"

### On Apple vs. Epic Impact (James Heaney, Jefferies)
- No impact yet; 2-4 quarters for some impact, 4-8 quarters for material pricing impact
- Largest gaming companies moving slowly; smaller ones will follow once leaders adopt

### On Capital Allocation (James Heaney, Jefferies)
- CFO: Very consistent with past approach -- organic investment first, then share buybacks
- Free cash flow margin 60%+

### On International E-Commerce Sizing (Martin Yang, Oppenheimer)
- U.S. vs. international roughly half-half for total advertising business
- International e-commerce opening will be meaningful but won't immediately double the $1B run rate
- Local market advertisers (Japan, Korea, etc.) will come over time, especially as platform opens and paid marketing begins

### On E-Commerce Attribution / Integration Challenges (Jim Callahan, Piper Sandler)
- Web advertising space much more fragmented than mobile apps (which have 2 major MMPs)
- Required building Shopify integration, attribution company integrations, and self-serve dashboard
- CEO: "This was one of the bigger lifts that we had to go accomplish over the last quarter or 2"

### On Latent Advertiser Demand (Alec Brondolo, Wells Fargo)
- Queue of advertisers built over the past year; not all will be auto-admitted
- Referral approach expected to drive "multiples bigger" onboarding than prior manual curation
- CEO: "our advertisers are going to cause an onboarding moment that will be multiples bigger than what we were manually curating"

### On Agentic Capabilities Timeline (Alec Brondolo, Wells Fargo)
- Phase 1 (onboarding chatbot): expected at or near October 1 launch
- Phase 2 (analysis agent): in development, more complex
- Phase 3 (generative ad creative tools): enables small businesses to produce ads at large-brand quality; key for maximizing platform performance

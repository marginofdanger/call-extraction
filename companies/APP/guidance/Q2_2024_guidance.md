## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

### Software Platform Long-Term Growth Target
- **Long-Term Growth Goal:** 20% to 30% annual revenue growth for Software Platform "for many years" -- **REITERATED** from Q1 2024 call; CEO stated: "I typically don't communicate externally about our goals if I don't have confidence in it. I'm communicating it now because I do have strong confidence in it, and I see many years of growth ahead of us"
- **Growth Goal Breakdown (3 Drivers):**
  1. **Continued AXON model improvement via data:** Models get more accurate with more data processed; estimated at an extra 3%-4% per quarter of organic lift
  2. **Engineering-driven enhancements:** Research science and core engineering team deliver additional model lifts; can produce step-function gains (e.g., Q1 2024's double-digit QoQ growth)
  3. **Demand expansion into new verticals:** Web advertising / e-commerce pilot launched in Q2; not yet factored into long-term growth targets
- **Independence from New Verticals:** CEO stated the 20%-30% target is "not very dependent outside of gaming at all" -- achievable from mobile gaming alone
- **Quarterly Growth Rate Needed:** CEO noted 5%-7% QoQ growth translates to the 20%-30% annual range; expressed confidence the company can "be confidently in those ranges for quite some time"

### Mobile Gaming Market Growth
- **Market Growth Rate:** Low single digits (few percentage points per year); no change in trend from prior quarters
- **Advertising-Supported Segment:** Growing faster than overall mobile gaming market
- **In-App Purchasing Market:** ~$100B (per analyst estimate)
- **In-App Advertising Market:** ~$20B-$30B (per analyst estimate); smaller base but growing faster

### Apps Segment Long-Term Margin
- **Long-Term Apps Margin Expectation:** ~15% Adjusted EBITDA margin, "consistent with industry standards"
- **Q2 2024 22% Margin is Temporarily Elevated:** Driven by readjusted UA return goals reducing costs 11% QoQ while revenue only decreased 3% QoQ; management explicitly guided for normalization to ~15%

### Capital Allocation Priorities (3 Key Areas)
1. **Organic Growth Investment:** Engineering and business development headcount to support AXON technology development and e-commerce expansion; "we do not expect significant capital investment here since we plan to expand our teams in a very lean and targeted manner"
2. **Share Management:** Combination of withhold-to-cover on future share vesting and strategic repurchases; continuing historical trend
3. **Balance Sheet Strengthening:** Enhance operational flexibility and liquidity while reducing net debt

## AXON 2.0 / Core Technology Update

- **AXON 2.0 Anniversary:** System passed 1-year anniversary in Q2 2024; every month has been bigger than the prior month since launch
- **Self-Learning Flywheel:** Models process more data each quarter, math gets stronger, predictions get more accurate; CEO: "every single quarter we launch this system... every month has been bigger than the prior month. So we get more data, processes more data. With those incremental data points, the math gets stronger, the model gets stronger, and it can drive more value"
- **Organic Model Improvement:** ~3%-4% per quarter of additional growth from models self-improving via data accumulation alone (not engineering-driven; purely volume/data-driven)
- **Engineering-Driven Step Functions:** Research science team can deliver step-function gains on top of organic improvement (e.g., Q1 2024's outperformance was engineering-driven); these are less predictable but add upside
- **Advertiser Spend Dynamics:** Advertisers have target ROAS goals (e.g., breakeven in 30 days); current system has a "limit" on how much spend it can deliver at those goals; as models improve, the system can deliver more spend at the same ROAS, and advertisers will increase budgets immediately since they are "buying at a profit"
- **ROAS Accuracy Example:** CEO described accuracy within ~1% error -- advertiser targeting $1,000 spend with 30-day breakeven gets $990-$1,010 back; as models improve, same advertiser could spend $2,000 at identical accuracy
- **No Ceiling in Sight:** CEO: "these AI technologies are very, very new. So it's not like in the world, we have 10 years of history to look back on and go, when do these models stop improving themselves?"
- **Model Improvement is Primarily Volume/Data-Driven:** CEO confirmed to analyst that the key input to model improvement is "primarily volume driven" / "volume data driven"

## E-Commerce / Web Advertising Expansion

- **Launch Status:** Pilot launched in Q2 2024 (live for "a few months" as of August 7, 2024 call)
- **Product Description:** E-commerce shops with websites can buy video ad inventory on AppLovin's 1B+ daily active user base in mobile gaming; users are routed to the shop's website; purchase attribution occurs on a performance basis (measurable revenue and result)
- **Early Results:** "Results are looking really promising, materially better than what we would have expected this early in our progression" -- CEO characterized performance as above internal expectations
- **Expected Impact Timeline:** CEO expects "material impact in '25 and beyond"; not yet a needle mover -- CFO confirmed nongaming "is still a relatively small portion of the existing business, and it's still kind of in pilot mode"
- **Not Limited to E-Commerce:** "It is not limited to just e-commerce. It opens the door to advertising for any website of any type that wants to drive transactions that are measurable on a performance basis on our platform"
- **Go-to-Market Approach:** More of a "go-to-market problem" than a technology problem at this stage; CEO: "we know we put the pieces in place, and now it's more of a go-to-market problem and less of a technology problem"
- **AXON 2.0 Enablement:** CEO credited the success to AXON 2.0: "The models in Axon 2.0 are so much more sophisticated than technologies we've had in the past that they should enable success there"
- **Audience Demographics:** CEO noted gaming audience "skews female and middle age" at massive scale, which is "completely different than what people would assume a gaming audience is" -- directly applicable to e-commerce
- **Not Included in Long-Term Targets:** CEO explicitly stated new categories are not backed into the 20%-30% long-term growth goal since they are still in pilot

## Advertiser Diversification / Customer Dynamics

- **Large Publisher Adoption:** Continued trend from Q1 2024; even publishers who previously viewed AppLovin as a competitor are now adopting the platform: "our platform is so successful in mobile gaming. It's very, very hard for any publisher to look the other way"
- **Full Market Penetration:** CEO: "There isn't really a customer that I know of in mobile gaming that does not find success and scalable success on our platform at this point today"
- **Total Advertiser Dollars on Platform:** "Many billions of dollars" (CEO's characterization when backing out total advertiser spend)
- **Nongaming Installations:** Growing, but still a "relatively small portion of the overall installations" -- CFO confirmed it is "not needle moving today"
- **Unlimited Advertiser Budget Appetite:** Advertisers have appetite to spend more than the system can currently deliver; constraint is model accuracy at scale, not advertiser budgets

## MAX Mediation Platform

- **Market Position:** MAX described as running the "vast, vast majority" of the mobile gaming in-app advertising auction
- **Bidding Transition:** Market has moved from waterfall to programmatic bidding; "vast, vast majority of the auction is now on a bidding state" and "continuously gotten more efficient"
- **In-App Advertising Market:** Growing faster than in-app purchasing market (from a smaller base); AppLovin positioned as "one of the main catalysts of growth in this category"

## Apps Segment / Portfolio Strategy

- **Optimization in Progress:** Management readjusted UA return goals in Q2 2024, resulting in 11% QoQ decrease in total Apps segment costs while revenue decreased only 3% QoQ
- **Margin Profile:** Q2 2024 margin at 22% (elevated); expected to normalize to ~15% long term
- **Divestiture Stance:** CEO: "we continue to be open to it, and we're really just waiting for the market to improve" -- no active transactions but management remains willing
- **Strategy:** "Continue to maximize those businesses for profit, and we'll be open to transactions in the future"

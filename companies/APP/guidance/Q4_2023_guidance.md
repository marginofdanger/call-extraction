- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $476M (+83% YoY vs. $260M in Q4 2022; **BEAT** top end of prior-quarter guidance range of $420M-$440M by ~$36M; ~10% higher than analyst consensus expectations)
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 50% (vs. 48.5% in Q3 2023; vs. 46%-47% guided from Q3 call; vs. 37% in Q4 2022)

### Software Platform Segment
- **Software Platform Revenue:** $576M — **record quarterly revenue** (+88% YoY vs. $306M in Q4 2022; +14% QoQ vs. $504M in Q3 2023; 4th consecutive quarter of double-digit QoQ revenue growth)
- **Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA:** $420M (+126% YoY vs. $186M in Q4 2022; +15% QoQ vs. $364M in Q3 2023)
- **Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 73% — **record margin** (vs. 72% in Q3 2023; vs. 61% in Q4 2022)
- **Incremental Revenue Flow-Through:** ~80% of incremental Software Platform revenue flowed through to Adjusted EBITDA, reflecting relatively fixed cost base and continued cost discipline
- **Key Revenue Drivers (not rank-ordered per CFO):** Strong holiday season; growth in mobile advertising market; market shift to real-time bidding; early contributions from Array business; AXON technology enhancements; expansion of advertiser base; growth in advertiser budgets

### Apps Segment
- **Apps Revenue:** $377M (+5% QoQ vs. $360M in Q3 2023; slight decline YoY due to H1 2023 portfolio optimization)
- **Apps Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 15% (consistent with Q3 2023)
- **Apps Adjusted EBITDA (implied):** ~$57M (15% of $377M)

### Cash Flow and Balance Sheet
- **Free Cash Flow (Full Year 2023):** $1B (~69% flow-through from Adjusted EBITDA of $1.5B)
- **Free Cash Flow Conversion Rate (FY 2023):** 69% of Adjusted EBITDA (above prior normalized guidance of 50%-60%)

### Capital Returns
- **Share Repurchases & Withholdings (FY 2023):** Combined 54.3 million shares repurchased and withheld during 2023
- **Net Share Reduction (FY 2023):** ~10% reduction in total shares outstanding after considering share-based compensation
- **New Buyback Authorization:** Board approved an increase of $1.25 billion to the share repurchase authorization
- **Buyback Strategy:** Directed larger repurchases from existing shareholders rather than open-market purchases; Q4 2023 had no buyback activity because the opportunity for a directed large repurchase did not present itself

### Debt Activity
- **Term Loan Maturity Extension:** Extended maturity to 2030 during 2023, while simultaneously reducing the interest rate

## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

### Software Platform Margin / Flow-Through
- **Incremental Revenue Flow-Through Target:** ~80% of incremental Software Platform revenue expected to convert to Adjusted EBITDA going forward — Adam Foroughi: "when you see roughly 80% flow-through, we don't really expect that to be different as that software business continues to grow"
- **Non-Gaming Margin Profile:** No material difference expected between margin profiles of gaming vs. non-gaming Software Platform revenue — CFO: "we don't see any material difference between the margin profiles of the existing mobile gaming business or non-gaming"
- **Cost Structure:** Relatively fixed cost base; cost discipline maintained; high operating leverage

### Free Cash Flow Conversion
- **Target:** ~70% flow-through from Adjusted EBITDA on an annual basis (with quarterly fluctuations due to working capital and tax payments) — **RAISED** from prior normalized guidance range of 50%-60%
- **Clarification (Q&A):** CFO noted the company is seeing better free cash flow conversion from EBITDA than previously expected, driven by technology improvements; the 70% is a "hope" rather than a hard guide, with quarterly variability expected

### Revenue Growth Drivers / Market Outlook
- Company growth is materially exceeding overall market growth — Foroughi: "the bigger part of our growth is driving more efficient value to advertisers, unlocking more budget and expanding the advertiser total than it is the market. The market isn't growing anywhere near as fast as what we are"
- Mobile ad market broadly inflecting positively; combination of AI-driven marketing technology improvements and economic recovery from weak 2022 comps
- Performance-based advertising model means advertisers expand budgets when ROI improves (not zero-sum)

## AXON 2.0 / Core Technology Update

- **Status:** Launched in Q2 2023; only live for slightly over half a year as of Q4 2023 call; described as "early-stage technology" still improving rapidly
- **Self-Learning:** Technology continuously improving; engineering team also continuously enhancing it
- **Performance:** Drives more efficient value to advertisers; unlocks more budget; expands advertiser base; ~50% Software Platform revenue growth in the two quarters since launch
- **AXON 1 vs. AXON 2 Difference:** "It's just better" — built to scale better, more efficient, more effective at predictions; analogy drawn to GPT-3 vs GPT-4 progression
- **Competitive Positioning:** Management views it as "amongst the most advanced across all markets"; Foroughi: "Our team has built really cutting-edge technology that works better than any other... advertisers or our peers have seen in the sector"
- **Longer-Term Guidance Difficulty:** The newness and rapid scaling of AXON 2.0 is the primary reason management does not provide longer-term financial guidance

## Advertiser Diversification Beyond Gaming

- **Non-Gaming Growth:** Growing faster than gaming, but starting from a smaller base — Foroughi: "Non-gaming is growing faster than gaming, it's smaller. So obviously, there's more room to grow"
- **Non-Gaming Advertiser Adoption Pace:** Slower than gaming due to enterprise budget planning cycles; non-gaming advertisers have fixed budgets and plan quarters/years out vs. gaming advertisers who move more opportunistically — Foroughi: "non-gaming enterprises tend to have a brand and they have fixed budgets and they normally plan their budgets in every quarters and years out"
- **Technology Validation Across Verticals:** Success observed across fintech, rentals, e-commerce, and other non-gaming categories — Foroughi: "as we get a fintech advertiser live, or a rentals company or an e-commerce company, we're seeing success across the board"
- **Investment:** Increasing headcount in non-gaming verticals (lean and efficient hiring); committed to broadening the platform
- **Chinese Advertisers:** AppLovin has lower exposure to Chinese revenue concentration compared to Meta; Chinese developers returning to market after COVID-era lockdowns is a positive overall market trend but not a specific driver for AppLovin

## Connected TV (CTV) / Wurl Strategy

- **Status:** Early stage — Foroughi: "the CTV testing and the rollout is early stage"
- **Advertiser Interest:** All intrigued by ability to buy on a performance basis on CTV; no other platform offers comparable performance-based CTV buying — Foroughi: "you won't find another place where these advertisers can buy on a performance basis, the way we can enable it on Connected TV today"
- **Timeline to Material Impact:** Multiple years before CTV becomes material given the scale of the ~$2B Software Platform business — Foroughi: "for any new initiative to make a material impact, we're talking multiple years"
- **Revenue Contribution:** Early contributions noted in Q4 results

## Array (OEM/Carrier Marketing Platform)

- **Status:** In progress; AXON 2.0 being applied to carrier/OEM marketing solutions
- **Revenue Contribution:** Early contributions noted in Q4 results — CFO referenced "early contributions from our array business" as one of the Q4 growth factors

## MAX Mediation / Real-Time Bidding Transition

- **Google Bidding Transition:** Google announced shift to predominantly real-time bidding in mobile mediated auctions in ~October 2023, with commitment to complete by January 2024; by Q4 2023, "the vast majority of the market was traded in a programmatic real-time manner"
- **MAX Take Rate:** 5% fee charged on real-time bidding transactions; no charge when not real-time bidding; shift to RTB is a positive economic development for MAX
- **RTB Efficiency Benefits:** Faster auction clearing; less infrastructure load; quicker ad delivery; publishers can show more ads and yield more revenue per user; publishers reinvest more in UA; flywheel effect benefits all ecosystem participants

---

## Privacy / Regulatory Environment

- **DMA (Digital Markets Act):** Coming into effect in EU ~March 2024; Apple's response (adding $0.50 charge) makes it economically unappealing for developers to go off-store currently (organic value of being on store ~10-15 points; total developer expense of alternatives ~20 points, offsetting the 30% fee savings)
- **Long-Term View on Store Fees:** Management expects continued global regulatory pressure over time will eventually create real economic benefits for developers; if store take falls from 30% to 15%, developers make ~20% more, and a large portion of that would be reinvested in marketing — directly beneficial to AppLovin's platform
- **Google Android ID Deprecation / iOS 17.4:** Timing uncertain; AppLovin's contextual/behavioral model is less dependent on sensitive user data than many Open Web properties, giving it a better starting point for navigating privacy changes
- **Privacy Adaptability:** Company described as "very entrepreneurial, very nimble" with historical track record of adapting well to privacy changes

---

## Apps Segment / Portfolio Strategy

- **Portfolio Optimization:** Major optimization in H1 2023 caused slight full-year revenue decline; H2 2023 showed recovery with 5% QoQ growth in both Q3 and Q4
- **EBITDA Margin:** Consistent 15% Adjusted EBITDA margin in Apps segment
- **Strategic Positioning:** Management does not believe Apps segment discounts overall company valuation since Software Platform financials are broken out separately — Foroughi: "We don't frankly believe so because the core software business is growing so quickly, and we break out financials. So it's pretty easy to just say, let's just look at the software segment"

---

## Capital Allocation / Share Repurchases

- **New Authorization:** $1.25B increase to share repurchase program
- **Strategy:** Directed large block repurchases from existing shareholders (not open-market); committed to ongoing buybacks as a means of returning value to shareholders
- **Rationale:** Management believes stock is undervalued; internally can "unpack the value much more easily today than investors are"
- **Q4 Activity:** No shares repurchased in Q4 (no directed large-block opportunity arose)

---

## Competitive Landscape

- **Positioning:** Strong independent leader in the sector since going public
- **Non-Zero-Sum Dynamic:** When AppLovin drives better performance for advertisers, budgets expand rather than shift from competitors — Foroughi: "they go, okay, we have a budget over here. We have budget over here. But AppLovin is now 5x better than they were. So let's expand the budget with them, too"
- **Competitor Restructuring:** No specific impact noted from a competitor undergoing major restructuring; focus remains on own execution

## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

### Advertising Business Long-Term Growth Algorithm
- **Long-Term YoY Growth Rate:** 20% to 30% (reaffirmed); CFO: "we continue to believe that 20% to 30% is the right long-term growth rate for the company"
- **Component 1 -- Reinforcement Learning (Predictable):** ~3% to 5% QoQ sequential growth; CFO: "that component, we think, is about kind of 3% to 5%...that's the component that we know is very stable, very predictable"
- **Component 2 -- Directed Model Enhancements (Step-Functions):** ~10%+ annual contribution historically; CFO: "Roughly around 10% is what we've kind of seen thus far"; analogy: "models going from ChatGPT-3 to -4"
- **Expectation:** At least one step-function model enhancement per year; CFO: "we think that we should be able to do at least one of those step function increases per year going forward. And it's a relatively conservative assumption"

### Margin Profile
- **Data Center Cost Growth:** ~10% of overall revenue growth added annually; CFO: "we've been adding on data center costs on an annual basis at least around kind of a 10% of the overall revenue growth, and we expect that, that should continue"
- **Other Costs Largely Fixed:** CFO: "the primary components of the cost base should remain relatively flat with obviously the exception of data center costs, which are more variable"
- **Margin Trajectory:** Advertising EBITDA margin expected to continue expanding from current 81% toward an asymptote; CFO: "the margin profile of the business should continue to grow from where we're at today on the Advertising business up to that kind of asymptote"
- **Lean Operating Model:** CEO: "Our lean team continues to impress, showcasing a model for how AI-based businesses can operate efficiently"

### Web-Based Advertising Contribution
- **Previously Guided:** 10%+ of overall advertising revenue in 2025
- **Updated View (RAISED):** Could exceed 10% this year; CFO: "it's quite likely that it could represent a larger than 10% portion of the revenue this year"
- **Uncertainty:** CFO: "it's very difficult to guess where that might go within the year because we've got a lot of factors"

### Capital Allocation
- **Buyback-Focused:** Q1 2025 saw $1.2B in repurchases/withholdings vs. $826M in FCF, using temporary revolver draw
- **Priority:** Continue returning capital to shareholders via share repurchases

## E-Commerce / Web Advertising Expansion

### Status and Scale
- **Run Rate:** $1B in web advertising revenue (CEO-referenced)
- **Advertisers Onboarded:** 600+ (from prior blog disclosure); pace slowed due to resource constraints
- **Team Size:** ~20 people handling the entire web advertising go-to-market
- **Market Penetration:** <0.1% of total potential advertisers
- **Churn (Run Rate >$250K):** Sub-3%
- **Model Maturity (CEO Self-Rating):** "B+" -- performing well for how early it is, but significant improvement expected
- **Geographic Scope:** U.S. only currently; CEO: "we've constrained the audience in web advertising to U.S. only. That's by design"
- **International Expansion:** CEO: "there's a lot of opportunity to just flip that switch and go global"

### Product Roadmap
- **Self-Service Dashboard:** Launching to select customers this quarter (Q2 2025); CEO: "we'll be launching it this quarter for select customers"
  - **Rollout Phases:** (1) Current customers feedback group (Q2 2025); (2) All current customers; (3) Staged opening to new types of clients; (4) Fully open/global (several quarters out)
  - CEO: "we'll go through stages of opening it up, where eventually long term, and this isn't too long term but several quarters, we'll go completely open"
- **Attribution Integrations:** Enhancing integrations with third-party platforms and attribution vendors; CEO: "The web advertising space is more fragmented than apps. So this will take time, but it's a straightforward task"
- **Automated/Generative Ad Creative:** Priority for 2025; vision is LLM-powered dynamic video generation personalized per user; CEO: "we want to do is generative AI-based ad creative"
  - Monetization lever: higher response rates without advertiser effort
  - CEO: "It's free effectively. It's a very low cost amount of production to get a much higher response rate"

### Model Architecture
- **Two Separate Models:** Gaming and e-commerce operate on different models; CFO: "there are 2 separate models. So both models are learning at the same time"
- **Independent Improvement Cycles:** Each model has its own reinforcement learning and directed enhancement pipeline; step-function improvements can happen independently on either model
- **E-Commerce Model Still Early:** CEO: "the e-com model is early...it hasn't been around for that long. It also doesn't have as much of a data feedback loop because it doesn't have as much impressions, transactions, scale"

### Not Targeting Largest Chinese E-Commerce
- CEO: "we focus on mid-market web advertisers and aren't yet working with the largest players as we're refining our tools first"
- Tariff impact on de minimis exemption changes considered immaterial given low penetration

### Conversion Window / Attribution
- **No Conversion API:** Cannot attribute beyond cookie window; CEO: "we don't have a conversion API. We don't have what other companies have with e-mail address and phone number"
- **Cookie Window Limitation:** Especially short on Safari; model must drive ad-to-transaction quickly
- **Self-Attributing on Web:** Built own attribution platform for web
- **Working on Third-Party Integrations:** For advertiser confidence at scale; CEO: "we are working on integrations in the web space as well so that the advertisers can have multiple reference points"

### Categories Beyond DTC
- **Early Success Noted:** Fintech, health care, insurance mentioned on prior call; not actively pushing into new categories due to resource constraints
- **Works Broadly:** Model performs across categories without custom builds
- **Non-Gaming App Install:** Still on the roadmap, not a 2025 focus

## App Store Fee Reduction Impact

- **Net Positive for AppLovin:** Lower fees for game developers means more revenue per dollar of consumer spend, which flows back into higher user acquisition budgets
- **Example Math (CEO):** If App Store fee drops from 30% to 15%, developer gets $0.85 per $1 instead of $0.70 -- an immediate 20% uplift to their revenues
- **Auction Dynamics:** In a competitive auction, if one advertiser bids 20% more, all must follow; benefits advertising platforms like AppLovin
- **CEO:** "we're the largest channel there is today for mobile gaming customers to spend their money...So maybe we're likely to be the biggest beneficiary of this change"

## Supply Dynamics

### Inventory Monetization
- **1B+ Daily Active Users:** In mobile games (unchanged)
- **Monetization Ceiling Far Off:** Conversion rate can expand from ~1% to 2-5% over time
- **Demand-Led Growth Model:** More advertiser categories/diversity = better matching = higher response rates = more monetization of same supply base
- **Publisher Benefit:** Non-gaming ad categories (e-commerce, fintech, etc.) are welcomed by publishers who prefer diverse ad content

### New Supply Opportunities
- **In-App Purchase Games:** As non-gaming ad categories grow, large IAP games (e.g., Candy Crush) that historically avoided showing competing game ads may be incentivized to run non-gaming ads
- **Global Expansion:** Currently U.S.-only for web ads; international represents roughly half of total business

## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

- **Apps Segment Long-Run EBITDA Margin Target:** Mid-teens% (Herald Chen: "longer run rate is probably a mid-teens type of margin for that business")
- **Apps Revenue Near-Term Trajectory:** Revenue expected to decline further in near term before reaching trough in H1 2023 (Herald Chen: "we do think in the first half of this coming year...we'll start to [reach] the trough of revenue")
- **Apps Margin Range:** Currently very high teens (19%); could fall back to low teens if investing in new game launches; mid-teens is steady state
- **M&A Bar Elevated:** "The bar for our M&A program has gone up...they would need to be highly synergistic, strategic and a great valuation"
- **Capital Allocation:** Over $1B cash on balance sheet; management still views stock as "a great place to put money to work" but being patient; $750M buyback authorization with over $400M remaining

## AXON 2.0 / Core Technology Upgrade

- **AXON 2 Development:** Working on next-generation upgrade to core AI advertising engine, leveraging recent advancements in AI technologies
- **Expected Timeline:** "We do expect it to go full scale in '23" (Adam Foroughi)
- **Deployment Model:** Not a phased rollout to customers; "effectively taking the old engine and upgrading it. So it will impact the whole business at once when it does go to full scale"
- **Expected Impact:** "We believe this new platform and upgrade to our core technology will make an immense impact on our business and for our business partners"
- **Growth Mechanism:** More efficiency in the system; advertisers can hit their goals at larger scale; expected to be a "step function" improvement that continues to build over time
- **AXON 1 Track Record:** Released ~3 years ago; business grew ~10x from AXON 1 to present
- **Investment Magnitude:** Built into existing P&L; R&D team is adding people "in the tens, not hundreds or thousands"; lean engineering culture
- **Margin Uplift Potential:** Herald Chen: "If we can lift...our biggest business, in particular on the AppDiscovery side with an AXON 2.0 implementation...those are very high-margin dollars to flow through the bottom line, and that would be a pretty meaningful increase in EBITDA"

## Connected TV (CTV) / Wurl Strategy

- **Strategic Vision:** Extending performance marketing capabilities from mobile to CTV; "This category of advertising is bigger than the one we operate in today, and it's much faster growing"
- **Revenue/Numbers:** "This is still really early days...we're not ready to talk numbers at this point"
- **Full Stack Approach:** AppLovin DSP + Wurl SSP = full-stack CTV offering (same model as mobile)
- **Advertiser Diversification:** CTV expected to open up non-gaming advertiser categories; Wurl works with high-profile streaming businesses
- **Adjust Integration:** Majority of Adjust's 4,000+ customers are non-gaming; these customers are "really excited about our Connected TV offering"
- **Attribution Asset:** Invested in an attribution company (one of the leaders in CTV attribution)
- **Timeline:** Longer-term play; expected to "become a big part of our Software Platform in the coming years"

## Apps Segment Strategic Review and Portfolio Optimization

- **Optimization Status:** "Nearing completion" — spent last 6 months on headcount reductions, reformatting earn-outs, sale/spin/closure of underperforming assets
- **Remaining Portfolio:** 11 studios plus several publishing relationships
- **Studios Assessment:** All 11 studios have opportunities for growth; many have new games planning to launch in 2023
- **New Game Development:** Bar has gone way up; developers taking longer-term bets, more innovative concepts; success rate expected lower (1 in 4 or 5 vs. 1 in 2); hybrid IAP + IAA monetization model now a requirement
- **Ownership Approach:** "We're going to manage these things as if we own them forever. And if people do show up at the right price, we'll look to optimize the portfolio"
- **Management Distraction Removed:** Adam Foroughi: "We've removed the distraction of weaker-performing studios"

## KPI Reporting Changes (Effective Q1 2023)

- **Metrics Being Discontinued:**
  1. Total Software Transaction Value (TSTV)
  2. Software Platform Enterprise Clients (SPECs)
  3. Revenue per Software Platform Enterprise Client (Revenue per SPEC)
- **Rationale:** Revenue mix shifting with additions of Adjust, Wurl, Array, ALX; structural differences in revenue models and client nature make aggregate metrics non-comparable and non-predictive
- **Continuing Metrics:**
  - Revenue per install and number of installs for AppDiscovery + ALX
  - Segment revenue and Adjusted EBITDA by segment
  - Free Cash Flow (newly introduced this quarter)

## Macro, Industry, and Competitive Commentary

### Advertising Market Environment
- **Stability Theme:** "Soft but stable" — no further deterioration expected; performance marketing advertisers have reset expectations to current interest rate environment
- **Performance Marketing Dynamics:** Advertisers operate on LTV-to-CAC model; got conservative as cost of capital rose; values reset "a couple of quarters ago" to current interest rate levels
- **IDFA Impact (Ongoing):** Category was growing almost double digits consistently for a decade before IDFA change impaired growth; all marketing companies working on improving algorithms for privacy era
- **Brand Dollar Softness:** CPMs from brands have decayed as economy worsened; reduced density in ad auction (more impact on monetization partners than on AppLovin directly)

### MAX Platform and Advertiser Density
- **MAX Market Share:** ~2/3 of top downloaded apps in both app stores monetize using MAX
- **Advertiser Density:** "No single category makes up a huge part of our business"; super-dense auction with tons of advertisers across many genres provides resilience
- **Hyper-Casual Impact:** No meaningful exposure; stability in Q4 numbers and Q1 guidance confirm limited impact from hyper-casual publisher retrenchment

### Google Partnership and Policies
- **Google Relationship:** Large cloud customer; Google launched bidding solution on AppLovin's platform as one of the first large-scale external implementations
- **Google Better Ads Policy:** "We actually don't expect any effects from the policy" — highest quality, at-scale publishers don't engage in disruptive ad practices

### In-App Purchase Market
- **IAP Market Dynamics:** IDFA change handicapped whale-hunting model; developers adapting to more casual games with hybrid monetization
- **Chinese Developer Impact:** Top grossing IAP games often from Chinese developers who were inefficient under zero-COVID; that constraint has reversed
- **Hybrid Monetization Trend:** Now a requirement — 95% of users were previously unmonetized; must monetize broad audience to subsidize cost of acquiring the 5% high-value users; good for MAX and AppDiscovery (more inventory)

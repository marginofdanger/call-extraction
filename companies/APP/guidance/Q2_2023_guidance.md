## Long-Term Targets and Strategic Vision

### Software Platform
- **Margin Trajectory:** 67% Software Platform Adjusted EBITDA margin in Q2 2023 is a new high; management expects further margin improvement as revenue grows — Herald Chen: "we do believe that the margin will continue to expand on software as we're able to grow the top line over the medium and long term, maybe not quarter-to-quarter"
- **Flow-Through:** >100% incremental EBITDA flow-through in Q2 2023; Herald Chen: "flow-through will fluctuate in the future, it does highlight the impressive operating leverage from our Software Platform segment"
- **Revenue Scale:** Software Platform marketplace is "in the billions of dollars"; $406M in net revenue for Q2 2023 alone; typical ad network take rates in the 20s-30% range
- **Growth Trajectory:** 2.8x revenue growth and 3.0x EBITDA growth over 2 years (Q2 2021 to Q2 2023)

### Apps Segment
- **Run-Rate EBITDA Margin Target:** Mid-teens% over medium term — Herald Chen: "As we invest a bit more on the UA side to grow the games, that will probably bring down that margin down into the mid-teens"
- **Stability:** Apps reaching stability; continued investment in new game development and UA marketing
- **H2 2023 Investment:** Increasing UA spend, particularly leveraging own platform (AXON 2.0 benefits for own apps)

### Consolidated Margin Framework
- **Blended EBITDA Margin Target:** Mid-40s% — Herald Chen explained the math: expanding software margins + stable/slightly lower Apps margins (mid-teens) = blended mid-40s
- **Historical Context:** 44% margin is highest in 5 years; prior model had contemplated mid-30s to low-40s

### Free Cash Flow Conversion
- **Normal Run Rate:** FCF expected to be approximately 50%-60% of Adjusted EBITDA on a normalized basis (may deviate in any particular quarter)
- **Q2 2023 Actual:** 66% conversion (above target range)
- **H1 2023:** >$500M in free cash flow

## AXON 2.0 / Core Technology Upgrade

- **Q2 2023 Impact:** Rolled out toward the second part of Q2; drove the significant beat vs. guidance; "growth in our platform really 100% from the advancements in our core technology" — Adam Foroughi
- **Performance vs. Expectations:** "performing at our expectations" — Foroughi; the beat came from difficulty precisely measuring impact of a brand-new technology replacement
- **Full Deployment Timeline:** Fully deployed by end of Q2; Q3 2023 will be the first full quarter of contribution
- **Advertiser ROAS Improvement:** Technology is "much more predictive, much more powerful"; advertisers seeing better return on ad spend, which should compound over time as they invest more
- **Advertiser Breadth Expansion:** AXON 2.0 opens the door to a wider breadth of advertisers and new verticals beyond gaming — Foroughi: "opens up the door to the possibility of a lot of advertiser expansion"
- **Self-Learning:** At billions of transactions per year, the technology itself will learn and self-improve; engineering team also working on continuous improvements in parallel
- **Market vs. Execution:** Market has been steady for a couple of quarters; the Q2 outperformance was "100%" attributed to AXON 2.0 technology upgrade, not market recovery
- **Long-Term Compounding:** Multiple growth vectors: (1) self-learning AI improvement, (2) engineering-driven improvements, (3) advertiser base expansion, (4) more inventory coming online, (5) more accurate transaction predictions; Foroughi: "We think it's going to get a lot stronger over time, and we think it's going to be able to fuel our growth for quarters and years"

## Connected TV (CTV) / Wurl Strategy

- **Status:** Extending AXON 2.0 to CTV is a stated strategic priority — Foroughi: "as we extend AXON 2.0 over to CTV, we think it's a really strong extension point for the advertisers that buy on our platform"
- **Wurl Execution:** Team executing strongly on relationships with media companies to access CTV inventory (CDN, SSP, mediation-like model)
- **Value Proposition:** Performance media on television — "something that people have talked about for years, but very few companies have been able to deliver any sort of solution there"
- **Potential Impact:** "extending it to Connected-TV could pose a very big growth channel for our business and that of our partners"
- **Timeline:** No specific revenue quantification provided; remains early-stage

## Advertiser Diversification Beyond Gaming

- **Non-Gaming Advertisers:** AXON 2.0 is "working within gaming and outside of gaming" — Foroughi
- **Sales Cycle:** New non-gaming customers take a quarter or 2 to ramp up (vs. existing gaming customers who can ramp faster/more automated)
- **Word-of-Mouth:** Only a couple of months into the new platform, there is already "a lot of word-of-mouth spreading amongst app developers, both within gaming and [outside]" that the platform is working well
- **Vertical Expansion:** "service new verticals" — management excited about broadening the advertiser base beyond mobile gaming

---

## MAX Mediation Update

- **Market Position:** "majority of the mobile gaming market is on our MAX mediation product" — no change in competitive position
- **Google Bidding:** Google shifting to in-app bidding and away from traditional methods; AppLovin was a launch partner for Google bidding product
- **Google Bidding Impact:** Expected to be a benefit to MAX product and publishers; will improve auction trueness; expected to scale up over next few months
- **Publisher Strength:** Publishers using both MAX mediation and AppDiscovery are seeing better growth than peers

---

## Privacy / Regulatory Environment

- **Near-Term (6-12 Months):** No material changes expected to iOS or Android privacy frameworks that would impact business — Foroughi: "We don't see anything in the 6- to 12-month term...that's going to be a material change to how we would be able to operate our business"
- **Long-Term Posture:** Privacy evolution is ongoing but company's speed of adaptation is a competitive advantage — "we're going to be able to move faster than peers"

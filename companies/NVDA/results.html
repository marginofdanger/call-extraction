Key Insight: NVIDIA’s gross margin guidance has been remarkably accurate in “clean” quarters (12/12 beat or met, averaging +70 bps vs midpoint). The three misses were all caused by extraordinary one-time charges that could not have been anticipated in the prior quarter’s guidance: the FY2023 gaming inventory crisis ($1.9B combined) and the FY2026 H20 export control write-down ($4.5B). The V-shaped margin recovery from 45.9% (Q2 FY2023) to 78.9% (Q1 FY2025) and then from 61.0% (Q1 FY2026) to 75.2% (Q4 FY2026) demonstrates NVIDIA’s pricing power and operational resilience.

5 Key Takeaways

1
Systematic under-guidance became the norm. From Q1 FY2024 onward, NVIDIA beat revenue guidance high-end in 12 consecutive quarters. The average beat vs midpoint was +5.7% across all 15 quarters, and +7.3% excluding the single miss. Management consistently could not (or chose not to) fully price in AI demand acceleration.
2
Q2 FY2024 was the all-time guidance surprise. Revenue of $13.5B vs $11.0B guidance midpoint (+22.8%) was the largest single-quarter beat in NVIDIA history. This quarter, when Hopper demand for generative AI first fully materialized, marked the moment AI demand outstripped any framework management had for guidance-setting.
3
One-time charges were the only margin misses. In all 12 “clean” quarters (no extraordinary charges), non-GAAP GM met or beat guidance 12/12 times, averaging +70 bps above midpoint. The 3 misses were caused by $1.22B (Q2 FY2023), $702M (Q3 FY2023), and $4.5B (Q1 FY2026) inventory write-downs — events that were inherently unpredictable at guidance time.
4
Gross margin showed remarkable V-shaped recoveries. From 45.9% (Q2 FY2023 trough) to 78.9% (Q1 FY2025 peak) in 10 quarters, and from 61.0% (Q1 FY2026, H20 charge) to 75.2% (Q4 FY2026) in just 3 quarters. These recoveries demonstrate NVIDIA’s pricing power and the structural profitability of AI infrastructure.
5
Revenue grew 8x in 4 fiscal years. From $27.0B (FY2023) to $215.7B (FY2026), with Data Center alone going from $15.0B to $194B (13x). This is unprecedented for a company of NVIDIA’s scale and makes it the fastest company in history to cross $200B in annual revenue from a $27B base.
6
Beat magnitude narrowed as scale grew. The average revenue beat shrank from +16.8% in FY2024 (when AI demand was most unpredictable) to +7.5% in FY2025 and +4.1% in FY2026. This suggests management’s supply chain visibility and demand forecasting improved with scale, though they maintained a conservative bias throughout.
7
Architecture transitions did not create margin troughs in revenue. Unlike many semiconductor companies, NVIDIA experienced zero digestion pauses. Each architecture transition (Hopper → H200 → Blackwell) was additive to the prior. In Q4 FY2025, $11B in Blackwell revenue came on top of continued Hopper growth. Margins dipped during transitions but revenue never did.
8
The Q2 FY2023 miss was the exception that proved the rule. Revenue of $6.7B vs $8.1B guidance (−17.3%) during the gaming/crypto collapse was the only quarter in 15 that missed the midpoint. It also established a pattern: NVIDIA guided conservatively after the miss, setting up the string of beats that followed as AI demand emerged.
9
Export controls created margin volatility but not revenue shortfalls. The $4.5B H20 write-down in Q1 FY2026 crushed GAAP/non-GAAP GM to 61%, but revenue still beat guidance ($44.0B vs $43.0B, +2.3%). NVIDIA’s ability to redirect supply from China to the rest of the world neutralized the revenue impact of export controls.
10
Data Center revenue was the guidance engine. Data Center went from $3.8B/quarter (FY2023) to $62B/quarter (Q4 FY2026), composing 91% of total revenue by FY2026. Every revenue guidance beat was primarily driven by Data Center upside, as Gaming, Pro Viz, and Automotive were relatively predictable.
11
Free cash flow scaled with revenue. NVIDIA generated $97B in FCF in FY2026, up from negligible levels in FY2023. At 45% FCF margins, the business model proved that massive revenue growth was translating to proportional cash generation, with $41B returned to shareholders in FY2026 alone.
12
Q1 FY2027 guidance of $78.0B signals continued acceleration. The pending quarter represents a $10B sequential increase from Q4 FY2026 ($68.0B), a +15% QoQ jump. If NVIDIA maintains its average +4% beat vs midpoint, actual revenue could approach $81B — which would make a single quarter larger than FY2024’s entire $60.9B annual revenue.

# NVIDIA Corporation Q3_2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** November 21, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 FY2024 (Aug-Oct 2023)

---

## Q3 FY2024 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $18.12B (record), +34% QoQ, +206% YoY (well above $16.0B guidance)
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 74.0%
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 75.0% (includes ~1pp benefit from release of previously reserved Ampere inventory)
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** +12% QoQ
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** +10% QoQ (compensation and benefits increase)

### Q3 FY2024 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $14.51B (record), +41% QoQ, +279% YoY — compute quadrupled YoY; networking nearly tripled YoY; Hopper HGX vast majority of revenue with lower Ampere contribution; consumer Internet + enterprises ~50% of DC revenue (outpacing total growth); CSPs ~50%
- **Gaming:** $2.86B, +15% QoQ, +81% YoY — strong back-to-school demand; doubled vs pre-COVID levels; RTX 40 Series at price points as low as $299; 475+ RTX games/apps
- **Professional Visualization:** $416M, +10% QoQ, +108% YoY — Ada Lovelace desktop workstations launched; AI emerging as demand driver (healthcare inference, edge AI)
- **Automotive:** $261M, +3% QoQ, +4% YoY — DRIVE Orin ramp + AI cockpit solutions; Foxconn partnership extended to DRIVE Thor

### Q3 FY2024 Key Operating Metrics
- **Networking Revenue Run Rate:** Exceeds $10B annualized; InfiniBand grew 5x YoY
- **Software & Services Revenue:** On track to exit year at $1B annualized run rate for recurring software/support/services
- **NVIDIA AI Enterprise Pricing:** $4,500 per GPU per year
- **H200 Announced:** First GPU with HBM3e; up to 2x inference speed vs H100 for LLMs; combined with TensorRT LLM = 4x cost reduction in 1 year
- **TensorRT LLM:** 2x inference performance on same GPU (software optimization alone)
- **Grace Hopper:** First revenue quarter; ramping to multibillion-dollar product line; 200+ exaflops of AI compute capacity in supercomputers next year
- **RTX Installed Base:** 100M+ NVIDIA RTX GPUs
- **Gaming:** Doubled vs pre-COVID levels; 475+ RTX games/apps; GeForce NOW 1,700+ titles

## Updated China Export Controls (October 2023)
- New U.S. regulations require licenses for Hopper, Ampere 100/800 series exports to China, Vietnam, certain Middle East countries
- China and affected destinations consistently 20-25% of DC revenue in recent quarters
- Expected to "decline significantly in Q4" but offset by growth in other regions
- Q4 guidance would have been "a little higher" without export controls (some unsold non-supply-constrained products)
- Working on regulation-compliant products that don't require licenses; "may become available in next coming months" but not material to Q4
- Pursuing licenses for some customers in China and Middle East; "too early to know" if granted

## Sovereign AI Infrastructure
- Nations awakening to need for domestic AI compute capacity
- Countries using own data to train LLMs and support local generative AI ecosystems
- Examples: India (Infosys, Reliance, Tata), France (Scaleway H100 cloud), UK (Isambard-AI with 5,500 Grace Hopper), Germany (Jülich with 24,000 Grace Hopper)
- "National investment in compute capacity is a new economic imperative"
- Multibillion-dollar opportunity over next few years

## H200 and Inference Cost Reduction
- H200: first GPU with HBM3e memory (faster, larger); up to 2x inference speed vs H100 for LLMs (Llama 2)
- 18x performance increase vs A100 for inference models like GPT-3
- Combined TensorRT LLM (2x) + H200 (2x) = 4x cost reduction in 1 year — without customers changing their stack
- AWS, GCP, Azure, OCI among first to offer H200 instances next year
- CUDA architecture compatibility: "benefits everybody that uses our GPUs"

## Grace Hopper Ramp
- First revenue quarter in Q3; in high-volume production
- Ramping to multibillion-dollar product line — NVIDIA's first data center CPU
- Supercomputers: UK Isambard-AI (5,500 Grace Hopper), Germany Jülich (24,000 Grace Hopper), Los Alamos, Swiss NCC
- Combined AI compute capacity of Grace Hopper supercomputers next year: 200+ exaflops
- Key use cases: vector databases, RAG (retrieval augmented generation), long context windows
- Appeals to ARM ecosystem customers (European supercomputing, CSPs with custom ARM CPUs)

## Networking Strategy
- InfiniBand: 5x YoY growth; "undeniable" value for AI factories (20-30% efficiency advantage at $2B+ infrastructure scale)
- Spectrum-X: new Ethernet-for-AI platform; 1.6x networking performance for AI vs traditional Ethernet; available Q1 FY2025
- BlueField SuperNIC + Spectrum-4 switch + software = end-to-end Ethernet AI solution
- Go-to-market through Dell, HPE, Lenovo for enterprise
- InfiniBand for dedicated AI factories; Spectrum-X Ethernet for enterprise AI

## AI Foundry and Enterprise Strategy
- AI Foundry service: customers bring data/domain expertise, NVIDIA provides AI technology + DGX Cloud factories + NeMo framework
- SAP, Amdocs as first AI Foundry customers on Azure; expanding beyond Microsoft
- Monetization: NVIDIA AI Enterprise license ($4,500/GPU/year) — NVIDIA is "wholesale," customers build retail models on top
- Enterprise AI partnerships: Adobe, Dropbox, Getty, SAP, ServiceNow, Snowflake, Genentech
- Waves of AI adoption: (1) LLM start-ups → (2) CSPs → (3) consumer Internet → (4) enterprise software platforms → (5) enterprise custom AIs → (6) industrial AI (Omniverse)
- VMware Private AI Foundation + L40S + BlueField-3 as enterprise AI entry point

## Product Cadence Acceleration
- New architecture/product every ~6 months (up from every 2 years)
- Drives cost reduction for customers
- Expands reach and segmentation: training, optimization, inferencing, CPU, GPU, DPU, networking
- Maintaining architecture compatibility across all products

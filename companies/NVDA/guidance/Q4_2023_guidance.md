# NVIDIA Corporation Q4_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** February 22, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 FY2023 (Full Year FY2023)

---

## Q4 FY2023 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $6.05B, +2% QoQ, -21% YoY
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 63.3%
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 66.1%
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** up 21% YoY, flat QoQ
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** up 23% YoY, -1% QoQ
- **EPS (Non-GAAP):** $0.88
- **Share Repurchases & Dividends:** $1.15B returned to shareholders
- **Share Repurchase Authorization Remaining:** ~$7B through December 2023

### Q4 FY2023 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $3.62B, -6% QoQ, +11% YoY — hyperscale posted strong sequential growth but short of expectations as some CSPs paused to recalibrate build plans; lower China sales (largely in line with expectations); H100 revenue already much higher than A100 in Q4 (second quarter of H100 ramp)
- **Gaming:** $1.83B, +16% QoQ, -46% YoY — driven by strong RTX 40 Series reception (Ada Lovelace); channel inventory correction "largely behind us"; demand solid in most regions, China somewhat impacted by COVID but early signs of recovery
- **Professional Visualization:** $226M, +13% QoQ, -65% YoY — driven by desktop workstations; automotive and manufacturing verticals strong; channel inventory correction ongoing
- **Automotive:** $294M (record), +17% QoQ, +135% YoY — driven by AI automotive solutions and new DRIVE Orin program ramps at EV and traditional OEMs

### Q4 FY2023 Key Operating Metrics
- **H100 vs A100:** H100 revenue already much higher than A100 in second quarter of ramp; H100 up to 9x faster than A100 for training, up to 30x faster for transformer-based LLM inference
- **CSP Revenue Mix:** ~40% of Data Center revenue on trailing 4-quarter basis from CSP customers
- **Software Revenue:** "Hundreds of millions" and growing; Q4 was "probably a record level"
- **RTX Games & Apps:** 400+ supporting RTX technology
- **GeForce NOW Members:** 25M+ across 100+ countries
- **Omniverse Downloads:** ~300,000; 185 connectors to third-party apps
- **NVIDIA AI Enterprise:** Version 3.0 released; 50+ AI frameworks and pretrained models

## ChatGPT and Generative AI Inflection
- "AI adoption is at an inflection point" — ChatGPT reached 150M users in 60-90 days
- Compared to iPhone moment: new computing platform has emerged
- Every CSP, Internet service provider, and software company is "alerted or shocked into alert or actively working on" generative AI
- Activity around AI infrastructure and inference "has just gone through the roof in the last 60 days"
- "Whatever our views are of this year as we enter the year has been fairly, dramatically changed as a result of the last 60, 90 days"
- Generative AI applications: text generation, document summarization, image/video creation, code generation, customer service
- Language as programming: "human language is a perfectly good computer programming language"

## DGX Cloud and NVIDIA AI Cloud Services
- DGX Cloud announced — "fastest and easiest way to have your own DGX AI supercomputer"
- Available through Oracle Cloud Infrastructure, Microsoft Azure, Google GCP
- Three service layers: (1) DGX AI supercomputer infrastructure, (2) NVIDIA AI Enterprise platform software, (3) NeMo/BioNeMo pretrained model layer
- Model: NVIDIA infrastructure hosted within CSP clouds; NVIDIA sells directly and through go-to-market partners
- "AI factories" concept: data centers that manufacture intelligence; expected to proliferate globally
- NeMo and BioNeMo currently in early access with customers

## H100 Hopper Ramp and Data Center
- H100 revenue already much higher than A100 in Q4 (second quarter of ramp)
- H100: 9x faster than A100 for training; up to 30x faster for transformer-based LLM inference at cluster scale
- Transformer engine "arrived just in time" for large language models
- Some CSPs paused build plans at end of year to recalibrate (timing issue, not demand issue)
- China Data Center revenue declined as expected (COVID and domestic issues)
- Networking: InfiniBand led growth; Quantum-2 400Gbps platform driven by cloud, enterprise, supercomputing
- Spectrum-4 Ethernet gaining momentum at 400Gbps
- Strong CSP demand: "accelerating growth through the year" expected

## Gaming and Ada Lovelace
- RTX 4090, 4080, 4070 Ti desktop GPUs selling out at retail
- RTX 4090 claimed top spot on Steam for Ada architecture
- First Ada gaming laptops launched — largest-ever generational leap in performance/efficiency
- First time: enthusiast-class GPU performance in 14-inch slim laptops
- First time: 90-class GPUs in laptops (5th-gen Max-Q)
- 170+ gaming and creator laptops planned with RTX 40 Series
- Microsoft 10-year partnership: Xbox PC games coming to GeForce NOW (Minecraft, Halo, Flight Simulator + Activision titles upon acquisition close)
- Channel inventory correction "largely behind us"
- DLSS 3: Cyberpunk 2077 added DLSS 3, enabling 3-4x frame rate boost at 4K

## Automotive Milestones
- Record Q4 revenue of $294M (+135% YoY)
- Foxconn strategic partnership announced at CES: developing automated/autonomous vehicle platforms; Foxconn as Tier 1 manufacturer for DRIVE Orin ECUs
- NVIDIA DRIVE OS received TÜV SÜD safety certification — meets functional safety standards for autonomous transportation
- Mercedes-Benz: long-term software revenue sharing; Mercedes outlined 10-year software opportunity
- FY2023 automotive revenue: $903M (+60% YoY)

## Software Monetization
- Software revenues "in the hundreds of millions" and growing
- Q4 was "probably a record level" for software
- NVIDIA AI Enterprise 3.0: 50+ frameworks and pretrained models
- Revenue model: per-GPU instance hour (cloud) or license/subscription (on-prem)
- Multiple growth drivers: AI Enterprise, DGX Cloud, NeMo/BioNeMo services

# NVIDIA Corporation Q2_2026 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** August 27, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 FY2026 (May-Jul 2025)

---

## Q2 FY2026 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $46.7B (record), exceeded outlook; grew sequentially across all market platforms
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 72.4% (includes $180M / 40 bps benefit from releasing previously reserved H20 inventory)
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 72.7% (includes $180M / 40 bps H20 inventory release benefit); excluding benefit: 72.3% (still exceeded outlook)
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** +8% QoQ (higher compute/infrastructure costs + compensation/benefits)
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** +6% QoQ
- **Inventory:** Increased from $11B to $15B sequentially (supporting Blackwell and Blackwell Ultra ramp)
- **Share Repurchases & Dividends:** $10B returned to shareholders; Board approved $60B new share repurchase authorization (adding to $14.7B remaining at end of Q2)

### Q2 FY2026 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** +56% YoY; grew sequentially despite $4B decline in H20 revenue; Blackwell platform reached record levels, +17% QoQ; GB300 production shipments began in Q2; Blackwell Ultra generated tens of billions in revenue; lighthouse model builders (OpenAI, Meta, Mistral) using GB200 NVL72 at data center scale for training and inference
- **Gaming:** $4.3B (record), +14% QoQ, +49% YoY — Blackwell GeForce ramp; GeForce RTX 5060 desktop GPU shipped; GeForce NOW Blackwell upgrade in September (RTX 5080 performance, 5K@120fps); GeForce NOW catalog doubling to 4,500+ titles; OpenAI partnership to optimize GPT models for RTX devices
- **Professional Visualization:** $601M, +32% YoY — RTX workstation GPUs and AI workloads (design, simulation, prototyping); Activision Blizzard using RTX workstations; Figure AI using RTX-embedded GPUs for humanoid robots
- **Automotive:** $586M, +69% YoY — self-driving solutions driving growth; NVIDIA Thor SoC shipments began (successor to Orin); Thor = most successful robotics/AV computer ever; DRIVE AV software platform in production; vision language model architecture transition accelerating

### Q2 FY2026 Key Operating Metrics
- **GB300 (Blackwell Ultra) Ramp:** Production shipments commenced in Q2; tens of billions in revenue; seamless transition from GB200 (shared architecture/software/footprint); factory builds in late July/early August converted to GB300; full production underway at ~1,000 racks/week; expected to accelerate further in Q3; CoreWeave preparing GB300 instances (10x more inference performance on reasoning models vs H100)
- **GB300 Performance:** 50x increase in energy efficiency per token vs Hopper; $3M investment in GB200 infrastructure → $30M token revenue (10x return); 7x faster training than H100 using NVFP4 (FP4 achieves FP16 accuracy with FP4 speed/efficiency); industry adopting: AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, OpenAI, Cohere, Mistral, Kimi AI, Perplexity, Reflection, Runway
- **Blackwell Software:** Performance improved by more than 2x since launch; CUDA, TensorRT-LLM, Dynamo advances; open source community CUDA library contributions integrated into millions of workflows; NVIDIA is top contributor to OpenAI models, data and software
- **Hopper Demand:** H100/H200 shipments increased sequentially; broad data center workloads still running on accelerated computing; CUDA libraries and full stack optimizations continuously enhance performance; $650M H20 sold to unrestricted customer outside China
- **MLPerf Training:** GB200 delivered clean sweep; MLPerf Inference results in September to include Blackwell Ultra benchmarks
- **Rubin Platform:** All 6 chips in fab at TSMC (Vera CPU, Rubin GPU, CX9 SuperNIC, NVLink 144 scale-up switch, Spectrum-X scale-out/scale-across switch, silicon photonics processor); on schedule for volume production next year; third-generation NVLink rack scale AI supercomputer; mature full-scale supply chain expected
- **Networking:** Record revenue $7.3B, +46% QoQ, +98% YoY; strong demand across all platforms
- **Spectrum-X Ethernet:** Double-digit sequential and YoY growth; annualized revenue exceeding $10B (up from $8B+ last quarter); Spectrum-XGS announced at Hot Chips (giga-scale, unifies disparate data centers into AI super factories; CoreWeave initial adopter; doubles GPU-to-GPU communication speed)
- **InfiniBand:** Revenue nearly doubled sequentially; XDR technology adoption (double bandwidth vs predecessor); preferred by model builders and supercomputing
- **NVLink:** Strong growth; NVLink Fusion positive reception; Japan's FugakuNEXT will integrate Fujitsu CPUs via NVLink Fusion; CUDA-Q quantum platform: 300+ ecosystem partners
- **RTX PRO Servers:** Full production; nearly 90 companies adopting; air-cooled PCIe-based for standard IT environments; Hitachi (digital twins), Lilly (drug discovery), Hyundai (factory design/AV validation), Disney (immersive storytelling); poised to become multibillion-dollar product line
- **Sovereign AI:** On track for over $20B revenue this year (more than double last year, raised from prior guidance); EU EUR 20B for 20 AI factories including 5 gigafactories; U.K. Isambard-AI supercomputer unveiled (21 exaflops)
- **Robotics:** Jetson Thor now available (order of magnitude greater AI performance vs AGX Orin); 2M+ developers, 1,000+ partners; adopted by Agility Robotics, Amazon Robotics, Boston Dynamics, Caterpillar, Figure, Hexagon, Medtronic, Meta; Omniverse + Cosmos for physical AI; Siemens partnership expanded for AI automatic factories
- **AI Infrastructure Market:** $3T-$4T in AI infrastructure spend by end of decade; top 4 CSP CapEx doubled in 2 years to ~$600B/year; data center infrastructure + compute on track for $600B in calendar 2025 alone
- **China Geography:** Low single-digit % of DC revenue; declined sequentially; Singapore 22% of Q2 billed revenue (>99% for U.S.-based customers)

### H20 / China Update
- Late July: USG began reviewing licenses for H20 sales to China customers
- Select customers received licenses; NVIDIA has not shipped based on those licenses
- USG expressed expectation of receiving 15% of revenue from licensed H20 sales (no regulation published)
- $650M H20 sold to unrestricted customer outside China in Q2
- Q3 outlook excludes H20 shipments to China
- If geopolitical issues resolve: $2B-$5B H20 revenue possible in Q3; more available if more orders/licenses arrive
- Advocating for Blackwell approval for China; "real possibility" per Jensen
- China AI market: ~$50B opportunity this year; expected to grow ~50% annually
- ~50% of world's AI researchers in China; vast majority of leading open source models from China
- DeepSeek, Qwen, Kimi among best open source AI models globally

## Long-Term Targets

- **Annual Product Cadence:** Blackwell (GB200) → Blackwell Ultra (GB300) → Rubin (6 chips in fab, volume production next year) → follow-ons through decade
- **Rubin:** Third-generation NVLink rack scale AI supercomputer; NVLink 144; mature full-scale supply chain; "a lot of great ideas" to be revealed at GTC
- **Growth Drivers:** (1) Reasoning/agentic AI requiring orders of magnitude more compute; (2) Sovereign AI global build-outs; (3) Enterprise AI adoption; (4) Physical AI and robotics
- **AI Market Growth:** ~50% CAGR for China AI market; $180B in AI-native startup funding this year (vs $100B last year); top AI-native startup revenue $20B this year (vs $2B last year); 10x next year conceivable
- **Scaling Trajectory:** Thousands of Hopper GPUs in tens-of-megawatt DCs → hundreds of thousands of Blackwells in 100MW facilities → millions of Rubin GPU platforms in multi-gigawatt multisite AI super factories
- **Every Factory Needs Two Factories:** One to build machines, another to build robotic AI
- **Custom ASICs vs NVIDIA:** Many ASIC projects start, very few go into production; AI is full-stack co-design; NVIDIA in every cloud, every framework supports NVIDIA, full pipeline acceleration, longest lifetime usefulness; perf per watt drives revenues in power-limited data centers; perf per dollar drives gross margins

## Blackwell Platform and Inference Economics
- Blackwell is new standard for AI inference performance
- NVFP4 + NVLink 72 on GB300: 50x energy efficiency per token vs Hopper
- $3M GB200 investment → $30M token revenue (10x return)
- Software optimizations improved Blackwell performance 2x+ since launch
- GB300 NVFP4 training: 7x faster than H100 FP8; FP16 accuracy at FP4 speed
- Reasoning agentic AI: 100x-1,000x+ more compute than one-shot chatbots; long thinking produces better answers
- Blackwell NVLink 72: order of magnitude higher perf per watt for reasoning vs Hopper

## Networking Platforms
- **NVLink (Scale-Up):** 14x bandwidth of PCIe Gen 5; NVLink 72 → NVLink 144 (Rubin); NVLink Fusion for semi-custom AI infrastructure; FugakuNEXT integration
- **InfiniBand (Scale-Out):** Quantum InfiniBand; lowest latency/jitter; XDR technology (2x bandwidth); preferred for model builders and supercomputing; benchmarks show best AI factory performance
- **Spectrum-X (Scale-Out Ethernet):** Annualizing $10B+; enhanced with low latency, congestion control, adaptive routing; improves cluster utilization from ~50-65% to 85-90%; ~1.5 years old; "home run"
- **Spectrum-XGS (Scale-Across):** New; connects multiple data centers/AI factories into giga-scale super factories; CoreWeave initial adopter; doubles GPU-to-GPU communication speed

## Sovereign AI and Enterprise
- Sovereign AI: on track for $20B+ this year; every nation investing in AI infrastructure
- EU: EUR 20B for 20 AI factories, 5 gigafactories (France, Germany, Italy, Spain); 10x AI compute increase
- U.K.: Isambard-AI (21 exaflops) for drug discovery and climate modeling
- RTX PRO servers: full production; nearly 90 companies; enterprise AI on-prem in standard IT environments
- Agentic AI reaching maturity: enterprises building domain-specific AI agents; enterprises opened up by open source models

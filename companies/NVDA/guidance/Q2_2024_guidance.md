# NVIDIA Corporation Q2_2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** August 23, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 FY2024 (May-Jul 2023)

---

## Q2 FY2024 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $13.51B (record), +88% QoQ, +101% YoY (above $11.0B guidance)
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 70.1%
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 71.2% (driven by higher Data Center sales and software content)
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** +6% QoQ
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** +5% QoQ (compensation and benefits increase)
- **Share Repurchases & Dividends:** ~$3.4B returned to shareholders
- **Share Repurchase Authorization:** Board approved additional $25B, adding to ~$4B remaining at end of Q2

### Q2 FY2024 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $10.32B (record), +141% QoQ, +171% YoY — compute nearly tripled YoY; HGX systems (Hopper + Ampere) the largest driver; networking almost doubled YoY driven by InfiniBand; CSPs >50% of DC revenue; consumer Internet next largest; enterprise/HPC smallest
- **Gaming:** $2.49B, +11% QoQ, +22% YoY — RTX 40 Series GPUs for laptops and desktops; laptop GPUs outpacing desktop in several regions; RTX 4060/4060 Ti launched at $299; end demand solid consistent with seasonality
- **Professional Visualization:** $375M, +28% QoQ, -24% YoY — Ada architecture ramp; desktop workstation refresh coming Q3; 3 new desktop GPUs announced (RTX 5000, 4500, 4000)
- **Automotive:** $253M, -15% QoQ, +15% YoY — solid YoY from DRIVE Orin ramp at NEV makers; sequential decline from lower demand particularly in China; MediaTek partnership announced (GPU chiplets for automotive SoCs)

### Q2 FY2024 Key Operating Metrics
- **China Data Center Revenue:** 20-25% of DC revenue (within historical range)
- **Gaming Installed Base:** 47% upgraded to RTX; ~20% at RTX 3060 or higher performance
- **RTX Games & Apps:** 330+ accelerated games and apps; 35 new DLSS titles including Diablo IV, Baldur's Gate 3
- **NVIDIA AI Enterprise:** 4,500 software packages with ~10,000 dependencies; continuously updated
- **Software Revenue:** "Hundreds of millions of dollars annually" (standalone software business, growing)
- **Supply Chain:** Additional CoWoS capacity and suppliers qualified; supply expected to increase each quarter through next year
- **GH200 Grace Hopper Superchip:** Entered full production; shipping in Q3 to OEM servers and supercomputing customers (Los Alamos, Swiss NCC); HBM3e version coming Q2 CY2024
- **DGX GH200:** 256 Grace Hopper Superchips connected via NVLink Switch System (vs 8 GPUs prior gen); available end of 2023; Google Cloud, Meta, Microsoft among first customers

## China Export Controls
- U.S. government considering additional export restrictions on Data Center GPUs to China
- NVIDIA: "do not anticipate that additional export restrictions, if adopted, would have an immediate material impact to our financial results"
- Long-term: "restrictions prohibiting the sale of our Data Center GPUs to China will result in a permanent loss of an opportunity for the U.S. industry"
- Current regulation "is achieving the intended results"
- China demand in Q2 was within historical 20-25% range of DC revenue

## Spectrum-X Ethernet for AI
- Announced: NVIDIA Spectrum-X accelerated networking platform for Ethernet-based AI workloads
- Couples Spectrum Ethernet switch with BlueField-3 DPU
- 1.5x better overall AI performance and power efficiency vs traditional Ethernet
- Designed for multi-tenant cloud data centers running generative AI (between pure InfiniBand AI factory and traditional Ethernet)
- BlueField-3 DPU in qualification with major OEMs, ramping across multiple CSPs

## InfiniBand Dominance
- InfiniBand: "gold standard for AI" — more than 2x performance of traditional Ethernet
- For $1B+ AI infrastructure: 10-20% throughput advantage makes InfiniBand "effectively free"
- Only InfiniBand can scale to hundreds of thousands of GPUs
- Record quarter; InfiniBand led networking growth
- Suited for dedicated AI factory environments

## L40S Universal GPU
- Designed for different use case than H100: fine-tuning pretrained models, enterprise AI, cloud scale-out
- Has Transformer Engine; supports multiple GPUs per server
- Standard rack, standard server — easy to install in hyperscale data centers
- Not limited by CoWoS supply
- ~100 server configurations from 20+ system makers (Dell, HPE, Lenovo)
- Combined with VMware Private AI Foundation for enterprise deployment

## VMware Private AI Foundation
- Major enterprise offering announced with VMware
- Fully integrated platform: NVIDIA AI software + accelerated computing + VMware multi-cloud software
- VMware's hundreds of thousands of enterprise customers get access to generative AI
- Represents entry point for generative AI into world's enterprise IT
- Combined with L40S servers and BlueField-3 DPU

## Enterprise AI Partnerships
- Snowflake: accelerated clouds for customized generative AI using proprietary data
- ServiceNow + Accenture: AI Lighthouse program for enterprise AI
- Hugging Face: new service for enterprise model training on DGX Cloud
- WPP: content engine using Omniverse for marketing AI
- Shutterstock: NVIDIA Picasso for 3D content generation
- OpenUSD Alliance: Adobe, Apple, Autodesk, Pixar, NVIDIA

## Gaming and Ada Lovelace
- Global end demand returned to growth after prior year slowdown
- Large upgrade opportunity: only 47% on RTX, only 20% at RTX 3060+
- Laptop shipments outpacing desktop in several regions; seasonality shifting (Q2/Q3 stronger for laptop back-to-school/holiday)
- NVIDIA ACE (Avatar Cloud Engine) announced for game NPCs using generative AI
- GeForce NOW continues expanding

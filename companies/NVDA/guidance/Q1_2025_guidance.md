# NVIDIA Corporation Q1_2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** May 22, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 FY2025 (Feb-Apr 2024)

---

## Q1 FY2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $26.0B (record), +18% QoQ, +262% YoY (well above $24.0B guidance)
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 78.4%
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 78.9% (lower inventory targets; Q4 and Q1 both benefited from favorable component costs)
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** +10% QoQ
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** +13% QoQ (higher compensation-related costs + compute/infrastructure investments)
- **Share Repurchases & Dividends:** $7.8B returned to shareholders in Q1
- **Stock Split:** 10-for-1 announced; June 10 first day of split-adjusted trading
- **Dividend Increase:** 150% increase announced

### Q1 FY2025 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $22.6B (record), +23% QoQ, +427% YoY — compute revenue grew 5x+ YoY; networking revenue 3x+ YoY; enterprise and consumer Internet companies led sequential growth; CSPs in mid-40s% of DC revenue; inference ~40% of DC revenue (trailing 4 quarters); 100+ customers building AI factories (hundreds to 100,000 GPUs)
- **Gaming:** $2.65B, -8% QoQ, +18% YoY — consistent with seasonal decline outlook; GeForce RTX SUPER GPUs strong reception; end demand and channel inventory healthy; 100M+ RTX PC installed base
- **Professional Visualization:** $427M, -8% QoQ, +45% YoY — generative AI and Omniverse industrial digitalization driving next wave; Omniverse Cloud APIs announced at GTC
- **Automotive:** $329M, +17% QoQ, +11% YoY — AI Cockpit solutions ramp with global OEMs; self-driving platforms driving YoY growth; Xiaomi SU7 launched on DRIVE Orin

### Q1 FY2025 Key Operating Metrics
- **CSP Revenue Mix:** Mid-40s% of Data Center revenue (down from >50% in Q4 as enterprise/consumer Internet grew faster)
- **Cloud Provider ROI:** For every $1 spent on NVIDIA AI infrastructure, cloud providers can earn $5 in GPU hosting revenue over 4 years
- **H200 ROI:** For every $1 spent on HGX H200 servers at current token prices, API providers can generate $7 in revenue over 4 years (Llama 3 700B)
- **Inference Revenue:** ~40% of Data Center revenue (trailing 4 quarters); both training and inference growing significantly
- **H200:** Sampling in Q1; production shipments on track for Q2; nearly doubles H100 inference performance; first H200 system delivered to OpenAI
- **Blackwell:** In full production; production shipments start Q2, ramp Q3, customer data centers stood up in Q4; "a lot of Blackwell revenue this year"
- **Supply Status:** H100 supply grew; H200 still constrained; demand for H200 and Blackwell well ahead of supply; may exceed supply well into next year
- **Grace Hopper:** Shipping in volume; 80% attach rate in supercomputing; top 3 most energy-efficient supercomputers worldwide
- **Spectrum-X:** Started shipping in Q1; ramping with multiple customers including massive 100,000 GPU cluster; expected to become multibillion-dollar product line within a year
- **Sovereign AI Revenue:** From nothing the prior year, expected to approach high single-digit billions this year
- **NIM (NVIDIA Inference Microservices):** New software product announced; secure, performance-optimized containers for enterprise generative AI deployment
- **Blackwell Configurations:** Available in 100+ OEM/ODM system configurations at launch (2x+ Hopper)
- **CUDA LLM Inference:** H100 inference accelerated up to 3x through CUDA algorithm innovations (3x cost reduction for serving Llama 3)
- **Tesla:** Expanded training cluster to 35,000 H100 GPUs; FSD v12 breakthrough enabled by NVIDIA AI
- **Automotive in DC:** Expected to be largest enterprise vertical within Data Center this year; multibillion revenue opportunity across on-prem and cloud
- **AI Factory Customers:** 100+ customers building AI factories; some reaching 100,000 GPUs

## Blackwell Platform Launch
- Launched at GTC in March 2024
- Up to 4x faster training and 30x faster inference than H100
- Up to 25x lower TCO and energy consumption than Hopper
- 5th-generation NVLink with multi-GPU spine; 72-node NVLink domain (72 Blackwells = 1 giant GPU)
- New InfiniBand and Ethernet switches (X800 series) for trillion-parameter scale AI
- Supports: hyperscale to enterprise, training to inference, x86 to Grace, Ethernet to InfiniBand, air to liquid cooling
- 100+ OEM/ODM system configurations at launch (2x+ Hopper launch)
- First customers: Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI, Oracle, Tesla, XAi
- Backwards compatible with existing Hopper data centers (H100→H200→B100 transition path)
- Ecosystem primed for liquid cooling; CSPs, ODMs, supply chain prepared

## Sovereign AI Infrastructure
- Nations building domestic computing capacity through various models
- Procurement models: state-owned telecom/utilities, local cloud partner sponsorship
- Japan: $740M+ investment in KDDI, Sakura Internet, SoftBank for sovereign AI infrastructure
- France: Scaleway (Iliad Group) building Europe's most powerful cloud-native AI supercomputer
- Italy: Swisscom Group building first NVIDIA DGX-powered supercomputer for Italian LLM
- Singapore: National Supercomputer Centre upgraded with Hopper GPUs; Singtel building AI factories across Southeast Asia
- Revenue expected to approach high single-digit billions this year (from nothing prior year)

## China Market
- Ramped new products designed specifically for China (H20) that don't require export control license
- Data Center revenue in China down significantly from pre-October restriction levels
- Market "very competitive" going forward due to limitations on NVIDIA technology
- Business "substantially lower than the levels of the past"
- Demand for H20 exists but overall supply constrained across all Hopper products

## Supply and Demand Dynamics
- Demand outstripping supply across the entire market
- Particularly constrained on H200 and Blackwell toward end of year
- CSPs building as fast as possible — "only 5% into the build-out"
- Time-to-train matters enormously: 3 months earlier on a 3-month project is everything
- Companies racing to announce next groundbreaking AI vs "0.3% better"
- 1-year product rhythm means constant refresh; customers should "performance-average" their way in

## NVIDIA NIM (Inference Microservices)
- Secure, performance-optimized containers powered by CUDA acceleration
- Includes Triton, TensorRT-LLM with industry-standard APIs
- Use cases: LLMs, text, speech, imaging, vision, robotics, genomics, digital biology
- Leading models from NVIDIA, AI21, Adept, Cohere, Getty, Shutterstock + open models (Google, Hugging Face, Meta, Microsoft, Mistral, Snowflake, Stability AI)
- Part of NVIDIA AI Enterprise software platform for production deployment (cloud or on-prem)

## Competitive Position
- Versatile platform (not general purpose): accelerates everything from CV to graphics to all modalities
- In every cloud + on-prem; developers start on NVIDIA platform
- AI factory approach: systems-level optimization, not just chips; $5B infrastructure with 2x improvement = $5B value
- Highest performance = lowest TCO (infrastructure, power, real estate costs dominate)
- Custom ASICs: too brittle; each AI model evolution breaks specialized designs; NVIDIA's CUDA programmability adapts

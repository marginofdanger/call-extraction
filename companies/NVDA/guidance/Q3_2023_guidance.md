# NVIDIA Corporation Q3_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** November 16, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 FY2023 (Aug-Oct 2022)

---

## Q3 FY2023 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $5.93B, -12% QoQ, -17% YoY
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 53.6%
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 56.1% (includes $702M inventory charges related to lower China Data Center demand, partially offset by ~$70M warranty benefit)
- **Inventory Charges:** $702M — primarily A100 write-down due to reduced China demand expectations
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** up 31% YoY, single-digit % QoQ
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** up 30% YoY, single-digit % QoQ
- **EPS (Non-GAAP):** $0.58

### Q3 FY2023 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $3.83B (record), +1% QoQ, +31% YoY — compute approximately flat QoQ; networking posted strong growth; U.S. cloud providers and consumer Internet companies driving YoY growth (large language models, recommendation systems, generative AI workloads)
- **Gaming:** $1.57B, -23% QoQ, -51% YoY — lower sell-in to align channel inventory; sell-through relatively solid in Americas/EMEA, softer in Asia Pac (China lockdowns)
- **Professional Visualization:** $200M, -60% QoQ, -65% YoY — lower sell-in to correct channel inventory levels
- **Automotive:** $251M, +14% QoQ, +86% YoY — DRIVE Orin production ramps scaling

### Q3 FY2023 Key Operating Metrics
- **Share Repurchases & Dividends:** $3.75B returned to shareholders
- **Share Repurchase Authorization Remaining:** ~$8.3B through December 2023
- **Gaming Sell-Through:** Q2+Q3 combined normalized sell-through ~$5B (two quarters); notebook sell-through solid, desktop softer in China/Asia
- **Supercomputer Rankings:** 72% of top 500 systems NVIDIA-powered (highest ever); 90% of new systems on list; 23 of top 30 Green500
- **RTX Games & Apps:** 350+ (up from 250+ in Q1)
- **Steam Concurrent Users:** Record 30M (prior peak 28M in January)

- **Non-GAAP OpEx:** Plan to keep OpEx relatively flat at current levels over coming quarters (reduced hiring)

## Long-Term Targets
- **Gross Margin:** Long-term profile believed intact despite inventory charges

## U.S. Export Controls and China Impact
- New U.S. government restrictions on A100 and H100 exports to China announced during the quarter
- Q3 revenue impacted but "largely offset" by sales of alternative products (A800) into China
- A800 meets export control requirements in hardware — cannot be reprogrammed to exceed limits
- Original $400M A100 concern addressed through rapid A800 introduction and supply chain execution
- Demand in China "more broadly remains soft" — expected to continue in Q4
- $702M inventory charges largely related to A100 write-down for lower China Data Center demand expectations
- China lockdowns and economy challenges drove the write-down decision

## H100 Hopper Production Ramp
- Started shipping H100 in Q3
- H100 systems available from Dell, HPE, Lenovo, Supermicro starting November 2022
- Cloud instances available early 2023 (Q1 FY2024) from AWS, GCP, Azure, OCI
- MLPerf benchmark leader: 3x lower TCO, 5x fewer server nodes, 3.5x less energy vs prior gen
- Production volume expected in Q4; large volumes expected Q1 FY2024
- Faster transition than Ampere due to pent-up demand for transformer workloads
- Transformer Engine designed for large language models, recommender systems, generative AI
- Microsoft partnership: building one of world's largest AI supercomputers with A100/H100 + Quantum-2 InfiniBand
- Oracle partnership: AI training/inference at scale with tens of thousands of NVIDIA GPUs

## Ada Lovelace Launch (Gaming)
- RTX 4090 launched mid-October — "tremendous demand," sold out quickly worldwide
- RTX 4080 available November 16 (day of earnings call)
- DLSS 3 neuro rendering — AI generates entire frames for faster gameplay
- Portal RTX reimagined with full ray tracing + DLSS 3 in Steam's top 100 most wish-listed
- "Home run" launch per Jensen; targeting upper range/top half of market
- Many more Ada products to come

## Gaming Channel Inventory Correction
- Channel inventories on track to approach normal levels exiting Q4
- Sell-in intentionally below end demand to correct channel
- Sell-through relatively solid (Americas, EMEA); softer in Asia Pac/China
- Stronger end demand expected Q4: holiday season + Ada adoption
- Don't expect blockchain/crypto to be important part of business going forward
- Secondhand GPU market (mining cards) exists but Ada targets upper-end, less affected

## Networking and InfiniBand
- Strong growth driven by hyperscale customers and easing supply constraints
- Quantum-2 400Gbps InfiniBand and Spectrum Ethernet building momentum
- Data Center networking revenue ~$800M per quarter (per analyst, not corrected by management)
- VMware vSphere rearchitected to run on BlueField DPUs — available on Dell PowerEdge servers
- BlueField DPU design win pipeline growing; partners: Arista, Checkpoint, Juniper, Palo Alto Networks, Red Hat
- InfiniBand critical for AI workloads: 10%+ efficiency improvement translates to billions of dollars in savings
- Microsoft AI infrastructure fully powered by NVIDIA InfiniBand 400Gbps

## DRIVE Thor and Automotive
- DRIVE Thor announced at GTC — successor to Orin in automotive SoC roadmap
- 2,000 TFLOPS performance; leverages Grace Hopper and Ada architectures
- Runs automated driving and infotainment simultaneously
- Available for automakers' 2025 models; ZEEKR as first announced customer
- Automotive described as "next multibillion-dollar platform"
- Recent design wins: Volvo EX90 (DRIVE Orin + DRIVE Xavier), Hozon Auto, NIO, Polestar

## Grace CPU
- Production samples expected Q1 FY2024 (on track)
- Memory coherent with GPU — expands effective GPU memory by 10x
- Designed for large data processing at high speeds (recommender systems, ML training)

## AI and Large Language Models
- Three key AI dynamics driving demand: (1) deep recommender systems, (2) large language models (transformer → BERT → GPT-3), (3) generative AI
- "Beginning of the era of generative AI" — generating images, videos, text
- NeMo and BioNeMo large language model services entering early access
- NVIDIA AI Enterprise bundled with H100
- Accelerated computing recognized as "the path forward" as general purpose computing slows

## Omniverse and Digital Twins
- NVIDIA Omniverse Cloud Services announced at GTC — first SaaS offering
- OVX reference designs featuring L40 GPU (Ada Lovelace) available early 2023 from Inspur, Lenovo, Supermicro
- Lockheed Martin and Jaguar Land Rover among first OVX customers
- Customers: Lowe's (store digital twins), Charter Communications (wireless network), Deutsche Bahn (rail network)

## Data Center Customer Classification Change
- NVIDIA expanding hyperscale definition to better reflect customer breadth
- Two dynamics: (1) growing number/scale of Internet service companies deploying NVIDIA AI; (2) cloud computing at tipping point with enterprises going cloud-first/multi-cloud
- CSP relationship increasingly "sell with" (public cloud) in addition to "sell to" (internal consumption)
- Public cloud side expected to become vast majority of hyperscaler consumption

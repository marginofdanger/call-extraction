# NVIDIA Corporation Q3_2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** November 20, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 FY2025 (Aug-Oct 2024)

---

## Q3 FY2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $35.1B (record), +17% QoQ, +94% YoY (well above $32.5B guidance)
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 74.6%
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 75.0% (down sequentially due to mix shift from H100 to more complex/higher-cost systems within Data Center)
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** +9% QoQ
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** +9% QoQ (higher compute, infrastructure, and engineering development costs for new product introductions)
- **Share Repurchases & Dividends:** $11.2B returned to shareholders in Q3

### Q3 FY2025 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $30.8B (record), +17% QoQ, +112% YoY — Hopper demand exceptional; H200 sales increased significantly to double-digit billions (fastest product ramp in company history); CSPs ~50% of DC revenue (2x+ YoY); consumer Internet revenue 2x+ YoY; GPU regional cloud revenue jumped 2x YoY (North America, India, Asia Pacific sovereign cloud build-outs)
- **Gaming:** $3.3B, +14% QoQ, +15% YoY — notebook, console, desktop all grew; strong back-to-school RTX demand; channel inventory healthy; new GeForce RTX AI PCs with Copilot+ capabilities (ASUS, MSI)
- **Professional Visualization:** $486M, +7% QoQ, +17% YoY — RTX workstations preferred for pro graphics; AI emerging as demand driver (AV simulation, gen AI prototyping, content creation)
- **Automotive:** $449M (record), +30% QoQ, +72% YoY — self-driving platform ramps (DRIVE Orin) and robust NAV demand; Volvo Cars EV SUV built on NVIDIA Orin and DriveOS

### Q3 FY2025 Key Operating Metrics
- **H200:** Sales increased to double-digit billions (fastest product ramp in company history); 2x faster inference vs H100; up to 50% improved TCO; available on AWS, CoreWeave, Azure (Google Cloud, OCI coming soon)
- **Blackwell:** In full production after successful mask change; shipped 13,000 GPU samples to customers in Q3; first DGX engineering sample to OpenAI; demand is "staggering"
- **Blackwell Systems:** Oracle announced Zettascale AI cloud (131,000+ Blackwell GPUs); Microsoft first to offer GB200 cloud instances (private preview) with Quantum InfiniBand; systems being stood up at Dell, CoreWeave, Oracle, Google
- **MLPerf Training:** Blackwell swept per-GPU benchmarks; 2.2x leap over Hopper; GPT-3 benchmark: 64 Blackwell GPUs vs 256 H100s (4x cost reduction)
- **Inference Software:** Hopper inference throughput boosted 5x in 1 year through CUDA algorithm innovations; cut time-to-first-token 5x; upcoming NIM release: additional 2.4x Hopper inference boost
- **Blackwell for Inference:** NVLink Switch enables up to 30x faster inference; excellent for reasoning inference (OpenAI o1)
- **Networking:** Revenue +20% YoY; sequentially down (but demand strong; expects sequential growth in Q4); InfiniBand, Ethernet switches, SmartNICs, BlueField DPUs all grew areas of sequential growth
- **Spectrum-X:** Revenue increased 3x+ YoY; xAI Colossus 100K Hopper supercomputer: 0 latency degradation, 95% data throughput (vs 60% traditional Ethernet); multiple CSPs planning large cluster deployments
- **NVIDIA AI Enterprise:** Full year revenue expected to increase 2x+ from last year; ~1,000 companies using NIM
- **Software/Service/Support Revenue:** Annualizing at $1.5B in Q3; expects to exit year at $2B+ annual run rate
- **Enterprise AI:** Accenture launched 30,000-professional business group trained on NVIDIA AI; Deloitte also taking NVIDIA AI to enterprises; Accenture using agentic AI internally (25-35% reduction in manual marketing steps)
- **Sovereign AI:** Pipeline intact; growth in Europe and Asia Pacific; India GPU deployments nearly 10x by year-end; Infosys, TCS, Wipro upskilling ~500,000 developers/consultants on NVIDIA AI
- **China:** DC revenue grew sequentially (export-compliant Hopper products); as percentage of total DC revenue, well below pre-export-control levels
- **Three Scaling Laws:** (1) Pretraining scaling intact and continuing; (2) Post-training scaling (RLHF → RLAI + synthetic data); (3) Inference-time/test-time scaling (OpenAI o1 "Strawberry" — longer thinking = better answers)

## Three Scaling Laws for AI
- **Pretraining Scaling:** Intact and continuing; empirical law, not physical; last-gen foundation models: ~100K Hoppers; next-gen starts at 100K Blackwells; multimodality models training on petabytes of video
- **Post-Training Scaling:** RLHF → RLAI + synthetic data; improving model quality post-training
- **Inference-Time Scaling (Test-Time Scaling):** OpenAI o1 ("Strawberry"); longer thinking = higher quality answers; chain of thought, multi-path planning, reflection; fundamentally new scaling dimension
- Result: all three scaling dimensions driving more compute demand simultaneously

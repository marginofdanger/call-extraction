# NVIDIA Corporation Q1_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** May 25, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 FY2023 (Feb-Apr 2022)

---

## Q1 FY2023 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $8.3B (record), +8% QoQ, +46% YoY
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 65.5%
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 67.1%, +90 bps YoY, +10 bps QoQ
- **GAAP Operating Margin:** 22.5% (impacted by $1.35B Arm acquisition termination charge)
- **Non-GAAP Operating Margin:** 47.7%
- **EPS (Non-GAAP):** $1.36

### Q1 FY2023 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $3.8B (record), +15% QoQ, +83% YoY — hyperscale and cloud computing revenue more than doubled YoY
- **Gaming:** $3.6B, +6% QoQ, +31% YoY — powered by GeForce RTX 30 Series
- **Professional Visualization:** $622M, -3% QoQ, +67% YoY
- **Automotive:** $138M, +10% QoQ, -10% YoY

### Q1 FY2023 Key Operating Metrics
- **Share Repurchases:** $2.0B in Q1
- **Share Repurchase Authorization:** $15B through December 2023 (Board increased and extended)
- **Automotive Design Win Pipeline:** $11B over next 6 years (up from $8B a year ago)
- **Channel Inventory (Gaming):** Nearly normalized, expected to remain around these levels in Q2

## Quarterly Guidance (Q2 FY2023)
- **Revenue:** $8.1B, +/- 2%
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 65.1%, +/- 50 bps
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 67.1%, +/- 50 bps
- **GAAP Operating Expenses:** $2.46B
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** $1.75B
- **GAAP/Non-GAAP Other Income & Expense:** ~$40M expense (excluding gains/losses on nonaffiliated investments)
- **Tax Rate (GAAP/Non-GAAP):** 12.5%, +/- 1% (excluding discrete items)
- **CapEx:** $400M–$450M
- **Russia & China COVID Impact Assumption:** ~$500M total ($400M Gaming sell-in, $100M Data Center from absence of Russia sales)

### Q2 FY2023 Segment Expectations
- **Gaming:** Expected to decline sequentially "in the teens" — driven by China COVID lockdowns and Russia impact; end demand expected to still grow YoY in Q2
- **Data Center:** Expected to grow sequentially to another record quarter; continued strong demand across hyperscale, cloud, and vertical industries
- **Automotive:** Significant sequential growth expected as DRIVE Orin ramps in Q2 and beyond

## Full Year FY2023 Guidance
- **Non-GAAP OpEx Growth:** High 20s % range for the year
- **OpEx Trajectory:** Sequential increases to level off after Q2; first half includes significant expenses for bring-up of multiple new products that should not recur in second half

## Long-Term Targets
- **Gross Margin:** Opportunities to increase longer term as new products ramp and software becomes a larger percent of revenue

## H100 Hopper Architecture
- Announced at GTC as next-generation Data Center GPU
- 80 billion transistors, "order of magnitude leap in performance over A100"
- Ideal for large language models and deep recommender systems
- Working with leading server makers and hyperscale customers to qualify
- Ramp in volume late calendar year (late 2022)
- DGX H100 AI supercomputing system also ramping in volume late calendar year
- Hopper expected in Q3 FY2023, starting to ramp closer to end of calendar year

## Grace CPU
- NVIDIA's first-ever Data Center CPU, on track to launch first half of 2023
- "Ideal CPU for AI factories"
- Dozens of server models announced at Computex by ASUS, Foxconn, GIGABYTE, QCT, Supermicro, Wiwynn
- Grace CPU Superchip: 2 CPUs; Grace Hopper Superchip: Hopper GPU + Grace CPU integrated
- New reference designs: CGX (cloud graphics/gaming), OVX (digital twins/Omniverse), HGX (HPC/AI)

## NVLink and Interconnect Strategy
- Fourth generation of NVLink — chip-to-chip, die-to-die, system-to-system coherent interconnect
- NVLink being made open to customers and partners for custom chip integration
- Enables mix-and-match of chips, dies, packages, systems across configurations
- Future NVIDIA chips (CPUs, GPUs, DPUs, NICs, SoCs) will all integrate NVLink

## Networking (Spectrum-4 and InfiniBand)
- Spectrum-4: world's first 400-gigabit per second end-to-end Ethernet networking platform
- Includes Spectrum-4 switch, ConnectX-7 SmartNIC, BlueField-3 DPU, DOCA software
- Networking revenue accelerated on strong broad-based demand for 25-, 50-, 100-gig ethernet adapters
- Networking products still supply constrained; continued improvement expected throughout rest of year

## Gaming Market Dynamics
- 100 million new PC gamers added in past 2 years (per Newzoo)
- ~1/3 of GeForce Gaming GPU installed base now on RTX
- 250+ RTX-optimized games and apps (double from last year)
- Softness in parts of Europe (Ukraine war) and China (COVID lockdowns)
- Cryptocurrency mining contribution: "diminishing contribution going forward"; reduced pace of increase in Ethereum network hash rate
- Preparing for new architectural transition later in the year (Ada Lovelace)

## Automotive and Robotics (DRIVE Orin)
- DRIVE Orin SoC now in production — kicks off major product cycle
- 35+ customer wins from automakers, truck makers, and robo taxi companies
- BYD and Lucid announced next-generation fleets on DRIVE Orin
- Design win pipeline: $11B over next 6 years (up from $8B a year ago)
- 4 pillars of robotics strategy: AI training, simulation (Omniverse), mapping, robotics processor/application

## Data Center and AI Demand
- Hyperscale and cloud computing customers remain supply constrained in infrastructure needs
- Vertical industries growth: strong double-digit % YoY; top verticals: consumer Internet, financial services, telecom
- Top workloads: recommender systems, conversational AI, large language models, cloud graphics
- 10,000+ AI inception startups working with NVIDIA
- Transformers enabling unsupervised learning and breakthrough NLP capabilities
- Large language models (GPT-3, Megatron 530B) driving enormous compute demand

## Omniverse
- In second quarter of general availability for enterprise software
- 10% of world's top 100 companies are licensing customers
- Downloaded nearly 200,000 times, tried in ~700 companies
- Key customers: Amazon (warehouse digital twins), Kroger (store optimization), PepsiCo (supply chain)
- Expanding GPU sales pipeline, driving higher-end and multiple GPU configurations

## Russia and China Impact
- Russia: ~2% of historical company revenue; NVIDIA not selling to Russia
- China COVID lockdowns: impacting both supply (logistics) and demand (Gaming)
- Expected to resolve; demand for products expected to come back

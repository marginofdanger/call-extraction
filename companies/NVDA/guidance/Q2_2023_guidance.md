# NVIDIA Corporation Q2_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** August 24, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 FY2023 (May-Jul 2022)

---

## Q2 FY2023 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $6.7B, -19% QoQ, +3% YoY (below $8.1B guidance from Q1 call)
- **GAAP Gross Margin:** 43.5% (includes $1.22B inventory charges + $122M warranty reserves)
- **Non-GAAP Gross Margin:** 45.9% (includes charges)
- **Non-GAAP Operating Expenses:** up 38% YoY, up 9% QoQ — driven by employee growth costs, salary increases, engineering development
- **EPS (Non-GAAP):** $0.51
- **Inventory & Related Charges:** $1.22B — purchase commitments from shortage period, primarily prior architectures (NOT Hopper-related)
- **Warranty Reserves:** $122M

### Q2 FY2023 Segment Revenue
- **Data Center:** $3.81B (record), +1% QoQ, +61% YoY — impacted by supply chain disruptions; North American hyperscale increased sequentially but offset by lower China hyperscale sales
- **Gaming:** $2.04B, -44% QoQ, -33% YoY — macroeconomic slowdown, channel inventory correction, crypto decline
- **Professional Visualization:** $496M, -20% QoQ, -4% YoY — OEM inventory correction, enterprise demand slowed
- **Automotive:** $220M, +59% QoQ, +45% YoY — Q2 described as "inflection point"; strong self-driving and new energy vehicle wins

### Q2 FY2023 Key Operating Metrics
- **Share Repurchases (H1 FY2023):** $5.5B returned (repurchases + dividends)
- **Share Repurchase Authorization Remaining:** ~$12B through December 2023
- **Gaming Sell-Through:** Up 70%+ since pre-pandemic; estimated Q2+Q3 combined end demand ~$5B
- **Hyperscaler vs Vertical Split (Data Center):** Approximately 50/50
- **Automotive Design Win Pipeline:** $11B over next 6 years (unchanged from Q1)
- **Supercomputer Rankings:** 72% of top 500 systems powered by NVIDIA; 22 of top 30 Green500

## Gaming Inventory Correction
- Sell-in intentionally set "far below" market sell-through to allow channel/OEM inventory correction
- Macro conditions turned "sharply worse"; sudden slowdown in consumer demand
- Price-positioning programs implemented with channel partners for current high-end desktop GPUs
- Expect to be "in a good shape going into next year" (FY2024)
- Lower units AND lower ASPs contributed to decline
- Next-gen architecture launch preparation (Ada Lovelace) underway

## H100 Hopper Production Status
- H100 in full production; racing to get it to all CSPs
- Expect to ship "substantial Hoppers in Q4" (Q4 FY2023 = Nov 2022-Jan 2023)
- Designed specifically for Transformer-type AI models with Transformer Engine
- Not affected by inventory charges — charges relate to prior architecture only
- Supports previous-gen and next-gen CPUs (Sapphire Rapids, Genoa, Graviton)

## Data Center and China Demand
- China hyperscalers "really slowed down infrastructure investment" — accelerated slowdown in Q2
- North American hyperscale revenue doubled YoY, offsetting China decline
- Supply chain challenges: complex system integration (GPUs, HGX boards, networking, cables, switches)
- Some supply arrived late in the quarter causing logistics disruption
- Networking led growth — strong sequential and YoY; ConnectX-6 and ConnectX-7 design win momentum
- Growing CSP interest in Spectrum-4 400G Ethernet platform
- GPU supply in shortage in the cloud; demand for GPU rentals far exceeds supply

## Large Language Models and Transformers
- LLMs described as "one of the most important neural networks today"
- Ranging from tens of billions to over 1 trillion parameters
- Use cases: content generation, summarization, chat, Q&A, conversational AI
- Also applied to life sciences: chemistry (MegaMolBART), proteins, DNA, genomics
- "Nobody could have predicted the profound importance of large language models"
- NeMo Megatron framework update: up to 30% faster LLM training

## Cryptocurrency and Ethereum Merge
- Crypto mining contribution diminishing as expected
- Ethereum merge approaching — unable to quantify exact impact on Gaming demand decline

## Automotive (NVIDIA DRIVE Orin)
- New vehicle rollout plans from NIO, Li Auto, JIDU, Human Horizons
- Pony.ai self-driving trucks and robotaxis on NVIDIA DRIVE
- $11B design win pipeline expected to translate to continued growth
- Automotive expected to be "next billion-dollar business"

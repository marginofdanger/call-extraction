Quarterly Guidance (Q1 FY2026)

  • Revenue: $43.0B, +/- 2%
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 70.6%, +/- 50 bps
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 71.0%, +/- 50 bps
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$5.2B
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$3.6B
  • GAAP/Non-GAAP Other Income & Expense: ~$400M income (excluding gains/losses on nonmarketable and publicly held equity securities)
  • Tax Rate (GAAP/Non-GAAP): 17%, +/- 1% (excluding discrete items)

Full Year FY2026 Guidance

  • Operating Expenses: GAAP and Non-GAAP expected to grow in the mid-30s% range
  • Gross Margin Trajectory: Low 70s% during Blackwell ramp → mid-70s% by late FY2026 (H2) as yields improve and manufacturing matures

Long-Term Targets

  • Three Scaling Laws Driving Demand: (1) Pretraining (multimodality, still growing); (2) Post-training (RL, fine-tuning, distillation — orders of magnitude more compute than pretraining); (3) Inference-time scaling (reasoning models, 100x+ more compute per task)
  • Product Roadmap: Annual rhythm; Blackwell Ultra in H2 FY2026 (same system architecture, slots right in); Vera Rubin after that; GTC to reveal details
  • AI Has Gone Mainstream: Every industry, every service infused with AI; beginning of a multi-decade modernization
  • Three Types of AI Computers: (1) Agentic AI for employees/enterprises; (2) AI factories for physical AI/robotics/autonomous systems; (3) Robotic systems themselves
  • Enterprise Long-Term: Expected to become "by far the larger" segment over time (industrial + physical AI)
  • GPU Longevity: Even older architectures (Volta, Pascal, Ampere) remain productive for data processing/curation workloads; CUDA programmability enables multi-generation utilization

Q4 FY2025 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $35.6B (record), +16% QoQ, +93% YoY — Blackwell ramp commenced + H200 continued sequential growth; Blackwell: $11B revenue (exceeded expectations, fastest product ramp in company history); compute revenue +18% QoQ, 2x+ YoY; CSPs ~50% of DC revenue (nearly 2x YoY); consumer Internet revenue grew 3x YoY; enterprise revenue grew nearly 2x YoY
  • Gaming: $2.5B, -22% QoQ, -11% YoY — demand remained strong throughout holidays; Q4 shipments impacted by supply constraints; expects strong sequential growth in Q1 as supply increases; GeForce RTX 50 Series launched (Blackwell architecture, up to 3,400 AI TOPS, 2x performance leap, DLSS 4)
  • Professional Visualization: $511M, +5% QoQ, +10% YoY — automotive and healthcare key verticals; ANSYS, Cadence, Siemens driving RTX workstation demand
  • Automotive: $570M (record), +27% QoQ, +103% YoY — self-driving platform ramps (cars and robotaxis); Toyota announced next-gen vehicles on NVIDIA Orin; Aurora/Continental driverless trucks on DRIVE Thor; NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion passed TÜV SÜD and TÜV Rheinland safety assessments (first AV platform)

Q4 FY2025 Key Operating Metrics

  • Blackwell Revenue: $11B in Q4 (fastest product ramp in company history); exceeded expectations; full production across multiple configurations
  • Blackwell Clusters: Common for clusters to start at 100,000+ GPUs; shipments started for multiple infrastructures of this size
  • Blackwell Manufacturing: 350 manufacturing sites; 1.5 million components per Grace Blackwell rack; ~100,000 factory operators
  • Blackwell Deployments: Azure, GCP, AWS, OCI bringing GB200 systems to cloud regions; CoreWeave, Microsoft, OpenAI successfully stood up; xAI adopting GB200 for Grok
  • Blackwell Inference: 25x higher token throughput and 20x lower cost vs H100 for reasoning AI; many early GB200 deployments earmarked for inference (first for a new architecture)
  • Reasoning AI: OpenAI o3, DeepSeek-R1, Grok 3 — can require 100x more compute per task vs one-shot inference
  • Post-Training Compute: Higher than pretraining; reinforcement learning generates enormous synthetic data; Hugging Face: 90,000+ Llama derivatives
  • 200x Inference Cost Reduction: Achieved in last 2 years
  • Networking: Revenue -3% QoQ; networking attached to GPU compute systems >75%; transitioning from NVLink 8 + InfiniBand to NVLink 72 + Spectrum-X; Spectrum-X and NVLink Switch revenue increased; expects growth in Q1
  • Spectrum-X Adoption: Microsoft Azure, OCI, CoreWeave building large AI factories; first Stargate data centers will use Spectrum-X; Cisco announced Spectrum-X integration into their networking portfolio
  • Automotive Vertical Revenue (DC): Expected to grow to ~$5B in FY2026
  • Sovereign AI: France EUR 200B AI investment; EU EUR 200B InvestAI initiative; regional cloud revenue increasing as percentage of DC revenue
  • China: DC revenue as percentage of total DC remained well below pre-export-control levels; approximately same percentage as recent quarters (~half of pre-export-control); expected to remain roughly at current percentage absent regulatory changes; very competitive market
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise: Llama Nemotron model family NIMs for AI agents; SAP, ServiceNow among first adopters
  • Healthcare AI: IQVIA, Illumina, Mayo Clinic, ARC Institute using NVIDIA AI
  • NVIDIA Cosmos: World Foundation Model Platform announced at CES for physical AI and robotics; Uber among first adopters

Q1 FY2026 Segment Expectations

  • Data Center: Sequential growth expected from both compute and networking; significant Blackwell ramp continuing
  • Gaming: Sequential growth expected (strong rebound from Q4 supply constraints as supply increases)
  • Networking: Sequential growth expected (return to growth)

Gross Margin Trajectory

  • Q1 FY2026 (guided): Non-GAAP 71.0% — likely the bottom during Blackwell ramp
  • During Blackwell Ramp: Low 70s% (focus on expediting manufacturing to meet demand)
  • Mid-70s Recovery: Expected later this fiscal year (H2 FY2026) as Blackwell fully ramps and cost improvements are realized
  • Many levers: Multiple configurations, customizable systems, 7 chip types — opportunity to improve costs once ramping is complete

Blackwell Ramp and Production

  • $11B revenue in Q4 (exceeded expectations); fastest ramp in company history
  • Full production across multiple configurations: air/liquid cooled, NVLink 8/36/72, x86/Grace
  • 350 manufacturing sites; 1.5M components per rack; ~100K factory operators
  • Demand "extraordinary"; customers anxious and impatient
  • Successful deployments: CoreWeave, Microsoft, OpenAI, Google, Oracle
  • Initial Blackwell mask change cost ~2 months but fully recovered
  • Blackwell Ultra (H2 FY2026): same system architecture, slots right in; new networking, memories, processors
  • Vera Rubin: the click after Blackwell Ultra; partners getting up to speed on transition

Reasoning AI and Inference Scaling

  • Three scaling laws all driving demand simultaneously
  • Pretraining: multimodality (petabytes of video); data from reasoning used for pretraining
  • Post-training: RL human feedback, RL AI feedback, RL verifiable rewards; compute higher than pretraining; generates enormous synthetic data
  • Inference-time scaling (test-time compute): chain of thought, search; already 100x more compute than one-shot; future potential: thousands to millions of times more
  • Blackwell designed for reasoning: FP4 transformer engine, NVLink 72 scale-up fabric; 25x faster reasoning AI vs Hopper
  • DeepSeek-R1: "ignited global enthusiasm"; open-sourced world-class reasoning AI model; developers applying R1 and chain-of-thought techniques broadly
  • NVIDIA architecture fungible: easily shift between pretraining, post-training, and inference scaling

Custom ASICs vs NVIDIA GPUs

  • NVIDIA advantages: (1) General — runs every model type (auto-regressive, diffusion, vision, multimodal, text); (2) End-to-end — data processing to training to post-training to inference; (3) Everywhere — every cloud, on-prem, robots; (4) Performance — 2x-8x perf/watt translates directly to revenue in power-limited data centers
  • Software stack 10x more complex than 2 years ago; bringing ecosystem to multiple chips is very hard
  • "Just because the chip is designed doesn't mean it gets deployed" — business decision involves size, power, time constraints
  • NVIDIA's deployment speed is "lightning fast"

Enterprise and Physical AI

  • Enterprise AI: agentic AI for companies; every department (marketing, legal, supply chain, engineering)
  • Physical AI: understanding physical world (friction, inertia, object permanence, cause/effect); Cosmos World Foundation Model
  • Three computers per company: (1) agentic AI for employees, (2) AI factory for training physical systems, (3) robotic system itself
  • Automotive: virtually every AV company developing on NVIDIA; future: 1 billion robotic cars, each collecting data, improved via AI factory
  • Enterprise will be "by far the larger" long-term market vs consumer AI
  • AI can address larger part of world GDP than any previous software tool

Networking Transition

  • Transitioning from NVLink 8 + InfiniBand to NVLink 72 + Spectrum-X
  • Networking attach rate to GPU compute systems: >75%
  • Spectrum-X: Microsoft Azure, OCI, CoreWeave building large AI factories; first Stargate data centers using Spectrum-X
  • Cisco integrating Spectrum-X into their networking portfolio — bringing NVIDIA Ethernet to every industry
  • Three networking platforms: NVLink Switch (scale-up), Quantum InfiniBand (HPC/supercomputing), Spectrum-X (Ethernet for AI)

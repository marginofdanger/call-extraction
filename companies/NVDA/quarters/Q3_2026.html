Quarterly Guidance (Q4 FY2026)

  • Revenue: $65.0B, +/- 2% (14% sequential growth at midpoint)
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 74.8%, +/- 50 bps
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 75.0%, +/- 50 bps
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$6.7B
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$5.0B
  • GAAP/Non-GAAP Other Income & Expense: ~$500M income (excluding gains/losses on nonmarketable and publicly held equity securities)
  • Tax Rate (GAAP/Non-GAAP): 17%, +/- 1% (excluding discrete items)
  • China: No data center compute revenue from China assumed (consistent with prior quarter)

Full Year FY2027 Outlook

  • Gross Margin: Input costs rising; working to hold gross margins in the mid-70s% through cost improvements, cycle time optimization, and mix management
  • Operating Expenses: Continuing to invest in innovation — new architectures, software, and systems

Long-Term Targets

  • $3T-$4T Annual AI Infrastructure: By end of decade; top 4 CSP CapEx ~$600B/year (doubled in 2 years); NVIDIA ~$35B per GW data center (out of $50-60B total); Rubin content per GW higher
  • $500B Revenue Visibility: Blackwell + Rubin through end of CY2026; growing with additional demand (KSA, Anthropic, etc.)
  • Annual Product Cadence: Blackwell → Rubin (7 chips in fab, H2 2026) → follow-ons; each generation delivers X-factor performance leap
  • ~$500B Free Cash Flow: Expected over next couple of years; funding growth, supply chain, stock buybacks, and strategic investments
  • Five Competitive Advantages: (1) Every phase of acceleration/transition; (2) Every phase of AI (pretrain, post-train, inference); (3) Every AI model; (4) Every cloud, on-prem, edge; (5) Diverse offtake/ecosystem
  • Three Platform Shifts: Accelerated computing (foundational), generative AI (transformational), agentic/physical AI (revolutionary) — all contribute to infrastructure growth
  • Scaling Trajectory: Thousands of Hopper GPUs in tens-of-MW DCs → hundreds of thousands of Blackwells in 100MW → millions of Rubin GPUs in multi-GW multisite AI super factories
  • AI-Native Startups: $180B funded this year (vs $100B last year); top AI-native revenue $20B this year (vs $2B last year)

Q3 FY2026 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $51.2B (record), +66% YoY — compute +56% YoY (driven by GB300 ramp); networking more than doubled YoY; GB300 crossed over GB200, contributing ~2/3 of total Blackwell revenue; Hopper ~$2B revenue (13th quarter since inception); H20 ~$50M (sizable orders never materialized due to geopolitical issues + competitive China market)
  • Gaming: $4.3B, +30% YoY — Blackwell momentum; robust end-market sell-through; channel inventories normal heading into holiday season; Steam broke concurrent user record (42M)
  • Professional Visualization: $760M (record), +56% YoY — DGX Spark (world's smallest AI supercomputer on Grace Blackwell) driving growth; evolved into computers for engineers and developers
  • Automotive: $592M, +32% YoY — self-driving solutions driving growth; Uber partnership for Level 4 autonomous fleet on NVIDIA Hyperion L4 robotaxi reference architecture

Q3 FY2026 Key Operating Metrics

  • Revenue Visibility: $500B in Blackwell and Rubin revenue from start of CY2025 through end of CY2026; on track; additional demand on top (KSA: 400K-600K GPUs over 3 years; Anthropic net new)
  • AI Factory Projects: ~5 million GPUs in aggregate AI factory and infrastructure projects announced this quarter; xAI Colossus 2 (world's first GW-scale data center); Lilly AI Factory (pharma's most powerful); AWS + HUMAIN (up to 150K accelerators including GB300); xAI + HUMAIN (jointly developing GPU data centers, 500MW flagship)
  • CSP CapEx: Aggregate ~$600B for 2026 expectations (top CSPs/hyperscalers); $200B+ higher than start of year
  • Blackwell Ramp: GB300 crossed over GB200; production shipments to major CSPs, hyperscalers, GP clouds; seamless transition
  • Hopper Platform: ~$2B revenue in Q3; 13th quarter since inception; H100/H200 still selling
  • H20 / China: ~$50M; sizable orders never materialized; disappointed but committed to engagement with U.S. and China governments; Q4 outlook excludes China DC compute revenue
  • Networking: $8.2B (record), +162% YoY — NVLink, InfiniBand, Spectrum-X all contributing; largest networking business in the world; majority of AI deployments include NVIDIA switches; Ethernet GPU attach rates roughly on par with InfiniBand
  • Spectrum-X: Meta, Microsoft, Oracle, xAI building GW AI factories with Spectrum-X; each runs own OS (platform flexibility); Spectrum-XGS scale-across technology for gigascale AI super factories
  • NVLink Fusion: Fujitsu collaboration (October); Intel collaboration (multiple generations of custom DC and PC products); Arm integrating NVLink IP for customer CPU SoCs; 5th generation NVLink = only proven scale-up technology
  • MLPerf Training: Blackwell Ultra 5x faster than Hopper; NVIDIA swept every benchmark; only platform leveraging FP4 meeting MLPerf strict accuracy standards
  • InferenceMAX Benchmark: Blackwell highest performance, lowest TCO across every model and use case; DeepSeek-R1: 10x higher perf/watt and 10x lower cost/token vs H200
  • NVIDIA Dynamo: Open source inference framework; adopted by every major CSP; disaggregated inference for MoE models; AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, OCI boosted inference performance
  • Rubin Platform: On track for H2 2026 ramp; 7 chips; Vera Rubin platform; X-factor improvement vs Blackwell; silicon back from supply chain, bring-up proceeding well; third-gen rack-scale system; improved manufacturability while Grace Blackwell compatible; ecosystem ready for fast ramp
  • Rubin CPX: Designed for long context workloads (reading PDFs, watching videos, 3D images); excellent perf/dollar and perf/watt
  • CUDA Longevity: A100 GPUs shipped 6 years ago still running at full utilization; most non-CUDA accelerators become obsolete within a few years as models evolve
  • OpenAI Partnership: Strategic partnership for 10+ GW AI data centers; opportunity to invest in the company; serving through Microsoft Azure, OCI, CoreWeave; supporting self-build infrastructure growth
  • Anthropic Partnership: First time on NVIDIA architecture; deep technology partnership; up to 1GW compute commitment with Grace Blackwell and Vera Rubin; optimizing Claude models for CUDA
  • Strategic Investments: OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral, Reflection, Thinking Machines — expanding CUDA ecosystem; getting share of consequential companies in return
  • OpenAI Growth: 800M weekly users; 1M enterprise customers; healthy gross margins
  • Anthropic Growth: $7B annualized revenue (up from $1B at start of year)
  • Agentic AI Proliferation: Cursor, Claude Code, OpenEvidence, Epic, Abridge experiencing surge in user growth; fastest-growing applications in history
  • Enterprise AI: ServiceNow, CrowdStrike, SAP integrating NVIDIA stack; Palantir new partner (CUDA-X libraries for oncology platform); Lowe's supply chain; RBC analyst productivity (hours to minutes); Unilever 2x content creation, 50% cost cut; Salesforce 30% engineering productivity increase with Cursor
  • Meta GEM: Generative AI for ad recommendations; 5%+ Instagram ad conversion increase; 3% Facebook feed gain in Q2
  • Physical AI: Already multibillion-dollar business; PTC and Siemens new Omniverse services; Caterpillar, Foxconn, Lucid Motors, Toyota, TSMC, Wistron building digital twin factories; Agility Robotics, Amazon Robotics, Figure, Skild AI building on platform
  • U.S. Manufacturing: First Blackwell wafer produced on U.S. soil (TSMC partnership); continuing to grow U.S. presence over next 4 years with Foxconn, Wistron, Amkor, SPIL
  • Three Platform Shifts: (1) CPU to GPU accelerated computing (Moore's Law slowing); (2) Generative AI replacing classical ML; (3) Agentic and physical AI (revolutionary, new applications/companies)
  • GPU Installed Base: Fully utilized — Blackwell, Hopper, Ampere all in use; clouds sold out
  • NVIDIA Content per GW: Hopper ~$20-25B; Grace Blackwell ~$30B±; Rubin higher; increases each generation

Q4 FY2026 Segment Expectations

  • Data Center: Continued Blackwell architecture momentum driving growth; GB300 lion's share; Hopper (H100/H200) still selling
  • Networking: Continued growth across NVLink, InfiniBand, Spectrum-X

Gross Margin Trajectory

  • Q3 FY2026 (actual): Non-GAAP 73.6% (exceeded outlook)
  • Q4 FY2026 (guided): Non-GAAP 75.0% — achieving mid-70s exit target
  • FY2027: Input costs rising; working to hold mid-70s% through cost improvements, cycle time, and mix

Blackwell Platform Performance

  • GB300 (Blackwell Ultra) crossed over GB200; ~2/3 of total Blackwell revenue
  • MLPerf Training: 5x faster than Hopper; only FP4 platform meeting accuracy standards
  • InferenceMAX: highest performance, lowest TCO across every model; DeepSeek-R1: 10x perf/watt, 10x lower cost/token vs H200
  • NVLink 72 for reasoning/MoE models: 10-15x higher performance vs prior architectures
  • GB300 NVL72 vs Hopper: order of magnitude higher perf/watt for reasoning systems
  • Blackwell leadership in inference: multiyear advantage

Strategic Partnerships and Investments

  • OpenAI: Strategic partnership for 10+ GW AI data centers; investment opportunity; deep co-development; serving through Azure, OCI, CoreWeave; 800M weekly users, 1M enterprise customers
  • Anthropic: First time on NVIDIA; deep tech partnership; up to 1GW compute (Grace Blackwell + Vera Rubin); Claude models optimized for CUDA; $7B ARR (from $1B start of year)
  • xAI: Deep partnership; Colossus 2 (first GW data center); connected xAI to HUMAIN/KSA
  • Other Investments: Mistral, Reflection, Thinking Machines — all expanding CUDA ecosystem
  • Investment Thesis: Deep technical co-development → expanding ecosystem reach → share in consequential once-in-a-generation companies → extraordinary returns expected

Networking Leadership

  • $8.2B revenue, +162% YoY — largest networking business in the world for AI
  • NVLink (Scale-Up): 5th generation; only proven scale-up technology; NVLink Fusion expanding (Fujitsu, Intel, Arm)
  • InfiniBand (Scale-Out): Quantum-X InfiniBand; unambiguously lowest latency/jitter; model builders' choice
  • Spectrum-X (Scale-Out Ethernet): Annualizing $10B+; Meta, Microsoft, Oracle, xAI building GW factories; Ethernet GPU attach rates now on par with InfiniBand
  • Spectrum-XGS (Scale-Across): Gigascale AI super factories; NVIDIA only company with scale-up, scale-out, and scale-across platforms

Custom ASICs vs NVIDIA GPUs

  • Many ASIC projects started, very few reach production — AI factories are extremely complex
  • Complexity has grown enormously: 3 types of switches (scale-up, scale-out, scale-across), memory architecture implications, model diversity (MoE, dense, diffusion, physics-based)
  • Five NVIDIA advantages: every phase of transition, every phase of AI, every model, every cloud, diverse offtake
  • After exploring alternatives, increasing number of customers returning to NVIDIA platform
  • Non-CUDA accelerators become obsolete within years as models evolve; CUDA provides multi-year useful life

Full Year FY2024 Guidance

  • H2 Supply: "Substantially larger than H1" — procured substantially higher supply for second half
  • OpEx: Now expect to increase investments while delivering operating leverage (after holding flat for 4 quarters)
  • Data Center Growth: Expects year-over-year growth to accelerate past Q1 through the year

Long-Term Targets

  • TAM: $1 trillion installed data center infrastructure transitioning from CPUs to accelerated computing over next 10 years
  • Generative AI: "Killer app" for accelerated computing platform

Q1 FY2024 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $4.28B (record), +18% QoQ, +14% YoY — generative AI driving exponential compute demand; broad-based global growth across CSPs, consumer Internet companies, and enterprises; DGX H100 shipping; H100 available on Azure, GCP, OCI (private preview), upcoming on AWS; GPU-specialized CSPs (CoreWeave, Lambda) also adopted
  • Gaming: $2.24B, +22% QoQ, -38% YoY — RTX 40 Series strong for notebooks and desktops; end demand solid consistent with seasonality; RTX 4070 ramped; RTX 4060/4060 Ti launched at $299
  • Professional Visualization: $295M, +31% QoQ, -53% YoY — channel inventory correction "behind us"; stronger workstation demand across mobile/desktop; Ada Lovelace kicking off major product cycle
  • Automotive: $296M, +1% QoQ, +114% YoY — DRIVE Orin ramp across NEVs; BYD partnership expanded (Dynasty/Ocean series, 2024 production); China NEV demand growth slower than expected, expected to linger rest of calendar year

Q1 FY2024 Key Operating Metrics

  • Automotive Design Win Pipeline: $14B over next 6 years (up from $11B a year ago)
  • Gaming: 1,600+ games on GeForce NOW; RTX 4060 Ti available ($299 tier); 2x performance of latest gaming console at mainstream price
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise: 4,000+ packages; enterprise-safe, enterprise-supported stack
  • Acceleration Libraries: 400+ across the platform
  • Grace CPU: Sampling with customers; University of Bristol supercomputer announced (6x more energy efficient)
  • BlueField-3: In production; adopted by Azure, OCI, CoreWeave, Baidu

Q2 FY2024 Segment Expectations

  • Data Center: Vast majority of sequential growth driven by Data Center; "steep increase in demand related to generative AI and large language models"; visibility extended "out a few quarters"
  • Gaming: Expected to continue recovery trajectory
  • Professional Visualization: Expected to continue recovery
  • Automotive: Growth expected to moderate as China NEV demand adjusts

Generative AI Demand Explosion

  • Generative AI described as "2 simultaneous transitions" — accelerated computing and generative AI
  • "iPhone moment" analogy: ChatGPT crystallized how to turn LLM technology into products
  • Demand surge is "broad-based" — CSPs, consumer Internet companies, enterprises, AI start-ups
  • Both Hopper and Ampere architectures seeing strong demand
  • Deep recommender systems also driving growth alongside LLMs
  • Inference now a "major driver" of accelerated computing for generative AI
  • Three core engines of future computing: recommender systems, large language models, vector databases
  • $1 trillion installed data center infrastructure being retooled for accelerated computing over 5-10 years

DGX Cloud and AI Cloud Services

  • DGX Cloud launched: available on Azure, GCP, OCI
  • NVIDIA AI Foundations model foundry services: NeMo (LLMs), Picasso (images/video/3D), BioNeMo (life sciences)
  • Six elements per service: pretrained models, data processing frameworks, vector databases, fine-tuning systems, optimized inference engines, NVIDIA expert support
  • ServiceNow as early adopter — building custom LLMs for enterprise platform
  • Ideal DGX Cloud mix: ~10% NVIDIA DGX Cloud, 90% CSPs' own clouds
  • Model: NVIDIA infrastructure hosted within CSP clouds; attracts customers to CSPs while extending NVIDIA reach
  • Omniverse Cloud available in Azure starting H2

Inference as New Growth Driver

  • Inference workloads require acceleration due to size and complexity of generative AI models
  • MLPerf benchmarks: NVIDIA inference platforms "orders of magnitude ahead"
  • Four new inference platforms: L4 (AI video), L40 (Omniverse/graphics), H100 NVL (LLMs), Grace Hopper Superchip (LLMs/recommenders/vector DBs)
  • Google Cloud first to adopt L4 (G2 virtual machines)
  • Model distillation: large models teach smaller ones; inference ranges from tiny (phone) to very large
  • "Never done with training" — continuous data collection, retraining, vector database updates

InfiniBand vs Ethernet Strategy

  • InfiniBand: designed for AI factories; dedicated single-workload environments; 15-20% throughput advantage = "effectively free" at scale
  • Ethernet: right for multi-tenant cloud with many small jobs
  • New middle segment emerging: multi-tenant cloud running generative AI workloads
  • Major new Ethernet product line for this segment to be announced at Computex (Spectrum-X)
  • InfiniBand had record quarter; "going to have a giant record year"
  • Networking revenue ~$1B per quarter (per analyst, not corrected by management)

Competition and Moat

  • Competition from "every direction": start-ups, semiconductor companies, CSP internal projects
  • Three competitive advantages: (1) Full-stack optimization (400+ acceleration libraries), (2) Data center scale architecture, (3) Data center expertise (delivery to operations in weeks vs months/years)
  • Universality: cloud to edge, every framework, every model, every cloud
  • Utilization: multi-domain versatility keeps infrastructure highly utilized
  • End-to-end acceleration: pre/postprocessing consumes 50-60%+ of workload; accelerating everything = 10-50x speedups vs 2x theoretical max from partial acceleration

Automotive

  • Design win pipeline: $14B over 6 years (up from $11B)
  • BYD expanded: DRIVE Orin for next-gen Dynasty and Ocean series (2024 production start)
  • China NEV demand slower than expected; adjustment expected to linger rest of calendar year
  • DRIVE Orin ramping across multiple NEV customers

Software Monetization

  • DGX Cloud, AI Enterprise, AI Foundations, Omniverse as four revenue streams
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise: operating system for AI; 4,000+ packages; enterprise-safe/supported
  • Continuous patching and security updates
  • Runs in all clouds and on-prem (Dell Helix partnership)
  • Software included in newer architectures; growing revenue contribution

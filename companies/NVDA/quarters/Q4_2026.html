Full Year FY2027 Guidance

  • Gross Margin: Mid-70s%; will update on progress as Vera Rubin transition approaches
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: Expected to grow in the low 40s% YoY
  • Tax Rate: 17-19% (excluding discrete items and material tax environment changes)
  • Revenue Growth: Sequential revenue growth expected throughout CY2026; exceeding $500B Blackwell/Rubin revenue opportunity; supply/inventory secured extending into CY2027
  • Gaming: Too early to determine FY2027 YoY growth given supply constraints; potential improvement by end of year

Long-Term Targets

  • $3T-$4T Annual AI Infrastructure: By end of decade; top 5 CSP CapEx approaching $700B/year; $300-400B of classical computing now supplemented by AI compute requiring 1,000x more computation
  • NVIDIA Content per GW: Increasing each generation; currently ~$35B per GW data center; Rubin higher
  • Token Economics: Compute = revenues; inference tokens per watt translates directly to CSP revenues; every CSP understands this now; choosing right architecture directly affects earnings
  • Agentic AI Inflection: Has arrived; Claude Code, CoWork, OpenClaw, Codex generating profitable tokens; enterprises adopting; next inflection: physical AI
  • Sequential Growth: Throughout CY2026; exceeding $500B Blackwell/Rubin opportunity; demand visibility extending into CY2027
  • Annual Product Cadence: Blackwell → Rubin (6 new chips each generation); committed to X-factor leaps in perf/watt and perf/dollar every generation; extreme codesign across compute, networking, chips, systems, algorithms, software
  • ~$100B Annual FCF: $97B in FY2026; supporting supply chain, ecosystem investments, share repurchases, dividends
  • NVIDIA as AI Infrastructure Company: Evolved from gaming GPU company over 25 years; compute, networking, software; cost per token reduction leadership unmatched
  • Every Software Will Depend on AI: Token generation is future of computing; AI factories like energy infrastructure; every company will produce tokens

Q4 FY2026 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $62.0B (record), +75% YoY, +22% QoQ — Grace Blackwell systems ~2/3 of DC revenue; Blackwell Ultra ramp sustained; even Hopper and 6-year-old Ampere sold out in cloud; ~9 GW of Blackwell infrastructure deployed and consumed by CSPs, hyperscalers, AI model makers, enterprises; diverse customer base: CSPs, hyperscalers >50% of DC revenue but growth led by rest (model makers, enterprises, sovereigns, supercomputing)
  • Gaming: $3.7B, +47% YoY — strong Blackwell demand + improved supply; DLSS 4.5 launched; G-SYNC Pulsar; 35% faster LLM inference on AIPC; supply constraints expected as headwind in Q1 and beyond
  • Professional Visualization: $1.3B (record, crossed $1B for first time), +159% YoY, +74% QoQ — RTX Pro 5000 Blackwell Workstation launched (72GB memory for AI developers, LMs, agentic workflows)
  • Automotive: $604M, +YoY — self-driving solutions; Alpamayo reasoning-VLA models announced at CES (first in Mercedes-Benz CLA); robotaxi rides growing exponentially (Waymo, Tesla, Uber, WeRide, Zoox); thousands of vehicles in 2025 → millions over next decade

Q4 FY2026 Key Operating Metrics

  • Inference Leadership: Semi Analysis declared NVIDIA "Inference King"; GB300-NVL72: up to 50x perf/watt, 35x lower cost/token vs prior generation; CUDA software: 5x performance improvement on GB200/NVL72 in just 4 months; NVLink 72 delivered 50x generational perf/watt leap
  • Networking: $11.0B (record), +3.5x YoY — NVLink scale-up primary YoY growth driver (Grace Blackwell rack systems); Spectrum-X Ethernet and InfiniBand both grew double digits sequentially; NVLink to integrate with AWS custom silicon; Spectrum-XGS momentum for gigascale AI factory unification
  • Rubin Platform: Unveiled at CES; 6 chips (Vera CPU, Rubin GPU, NVLink 6 Switch, ConnectX-9 SuperNIC, BlueField-4 DPU, Spectrum-6 Ethernet Switch); trains MoE models with 1/4 GPUs; reduces inference token costs up to 10x vs Blackwell; first Vera Rubin samples shipped to customers this week; production shipments on track for H2 2026; modular cable-free tray design (improved resiliency/serviceability vs Blackwell); expects every cloud/model builder to deploy
  • Vera CPU: Only DC CPU with LPDDR5; focused on high data processing capability; excellent single-threaded performance; designed for post-training tool use and data processing phases of AI pipeline
  • OpenAI Partnership: GPT-5.3-Codex launched, trained/inferenced on Grace Blackwell NVLink 72; deployed broadly inside NVIDIA; close to definitive partnership agreement; relationship since 2016
  • Meta Partnership: Meta SuperIntelligence Labs deploying millions of Blackwells and Rubin GPUs + NVIDIA CPUs + Spectrum-X Ethernet
  • Anthropic Partnership: $10B investment announced; will train and inference on Grace Blackwell and Vera Rubin; Claude Code Work agent platform described as revolutionary; first time on NVIDIA architecture
  • Grok Licensing: Nonexclusive licensing agreement for low latency inference technology; team of engineers joining NVIDIA; extending architecture like Mellanox acquisition
  • Agentic AI Inflection: Claude Code, Claude CoWork, OpenClaw, GPT-5.3-Codex achieved useful intelligence; adoption skyrocketing; tokens profitable; ChatGPT moment of agentic AI arrived; compute demand driven by agents generating thousands to hundreds of thousands of tokens per session
  • China: Small amounts of H200 approved by USG but zero revenue generated; unknown if imports will be allowed; China competitors bolstered by IPOs making progress; potential to disrupt global AI structure long-term; NVIDIA advocates for U.S. ability to compete globally
  • CSP CapEx: Top 5 CSPs/hyperscalers 2026 CapEx expectations up ~$120B since start of year, approaching $700B; they account for slightly over 50% of NVIDIA DC revenue; non-hyperscaler segment growing fastest
  • Physical AI: North of $6B NVIDIA revenue in FY2026; robotaxi fleets growing from thousands to millions; Omniverse + Cosmos + Isaac GR00T + Alpamayo; partnerships with Dassault Systemes, Siemens, Synopsis; Boston Dynamics, Caterpillar, Franca Robotics, LG Electronics, Neura Robotics
  • Space Data Centers: Early stage; GPU already in space (Hopper); imaging and high-res processing use cases; different thermal/cooling challenges but viable long-term
  • SBC Policy Change: Starting Q1 FY2027, including stock-based compensation in non-GAAP results
  • Meta GEM: 3.5% increase in ad clicks on Facebook; 1%+ gain in conversations on Instagram; translating to meaningful revenue growth
  • Hugging Face: 1.5M AI models; NVIDIA runs them all
  • Anthropic Revenue: 10x in a year; severely capacity constrained

Networking Growth

  • $11B quarterly record; exceeded $31B for full year; +10x vs FY2025
  • Largest networking company in the world; likely largest Ethernet networking company
  • NVLink (Scale-Up): Each rack has 9 switch nodes with 2 chips each (more in future); primary YoY growth driver; revolutionized computing
  • Spectrum-X (Scale-Out Ethernet): Annualized $11-12B in H2 FY2026; 10-20% utilization improvement on $10-20B AI factory = $1-4B effective benefit; "home run"
  • InfiniBand (Scale-Out): Lowest latency/jitter; model builders' choice
  • Spectrum-XGS (Scale-Across): Unifying distributed DCs into gigascale AI factories

Agentic AI Inflection

  • ChatGPT moment of agentic AI has arrived
  • Claude Code, Claude CoWork, OpenClaw, GPT-5.3-Codex, Cursor — fastest-growing applications in history
  • Agents generating thousands to hundreds of thousands of tokens per session (running minutes to hours)
  • Enterprise AI adoption floodgates opened
  • Anthropic revenue 10x in a year; severely capacity constrained
  • AI-native startup funding: explosive growth
  • Three platform shifts: (1) CPU→GPU accelerated computing; (2) classical ML→generative AI; (3) agentic/physical AI — all running simultaneously

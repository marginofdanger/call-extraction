Quarterly Guidance (Q1 FY2025)

  • Revenue: $24.0B, +/- 2%
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 76.3%, +/- 50 bps (benefiting from favorable component costs, similar to Q4)
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 77.0%, +/- 50 bps (benefiting from favorable component costs, similar to Q4)
  • Beyond Q1 Gross Margin: Expected to return to mid-70s% range for remainder of FY2025
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$3.5B
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$2.5B
  • GAAP/Non-GAAP Other Income & Expense: ~$250M income (excluding gains/losses on nonaffiliated investments)
  • Tax Rate (GAAP/Non-GAAP): 17%, +/- 1% (excluding discrete items)

Full Year FY2025 Guidance

  • Operating Expenses: GAAP and Non-GAAP expected to grow in the mid-30% range YoY
  • Supply: Next-generation products (Blackwell) expected to be supply constrained as demand far exceeds supply

Long-Term Targets

  • Data Center Infrastructure: Expect doubling of world data center infrastructure installed base in next 5 years
  • Annual Market Opportunity: Hundreds of billions of dollars
  • Two Platform Shifts: (1) General-purpose to accelerated computing; (2) generative AI as new computing paradigm
  • Sovereign AI: Nations investing in domestic AI infrastructure — growing demand driver outside US/China
  • AI Factories: New type of data center for AI generation; expected in every industry, every company, every region

Q4 FY2024 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $18.4B (record), +27% QoQ, +409% YoY — driven by Hopper GPU computing platform + InfiniBand end-to-end networking; compute revenue grew 5x+ YoY; networking revenue tripled YoY; ~40% of DC revenue estimated from AI inference; CSPs >50% of DC revenue; consumer Internet companies next largest; China declined to mid-single-digit % of DC revenue (Oct 2023 export controls)
  • Gaming: $2.87B, flat QoQ, +56% YoY — better than outlook on solid holiday consumer demand; RTX 40 Super Series launched at CES ($599+); 100M+ RTX PCs installed base; 500+ AI-enabled PC apps and games
  • Professional Visualization: $463M, +11% QoQ, +105% YoY — Ada architecture GPUs continuing to ramp; enterprises refreshing workstations for generative AI workloads (data prep, LLM fine-tuning, RAG)
  • Automotive: $281M, +8% QoQ, -4% YoY — DRIVE Orin continued adoption; Li Auto, Great Wall Motor, ZEEKR, Xiaomi EV announced new NVIDIA-based vehicles; DRIVE Thor available next year

Q4 FY2024 Key Operating Metrics

  • Networking Revenue Run Rate: Exceeded $13B annualized; InfiniBand grew 5x+ YoY
  • Software & Services Revenue: $1B annualized run rate in Q4
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise Pricing: $4,500 per GPU per year
  • Spectrum-X: On track to ship this quarter (Q1 FY2025); 1.6x higher AI networking performance vs traditional Ethernet
  • H200: On track to ramp; initial shipments in Q2 FY2025; nearly doubled inference performance vs H100
  • DGX Cloud: Expanded to include AWS, joining Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle Cloud
  • RTX Installed Base: 100M+ RTX PCs
  • Automotive Vertical DC Revenue: Exceeded $1B in FY2024 (AI infrastructure for autonomous driving training + simulation); ~80 vehicle manufacturers using NVIDIA AI infrastructure
  • Supply Status: Hopper supply improving; demand remains very strong; next-gen products expected supply constrained

Q1 FY2025 Segment Expectations

  • Data Center: Sequential growth expected
  • Professional Visualization: Sequential growth expected
  • Gaming: Seasonal decline expected
  • China: Mid-single-digit % of Data Center revenue expected to continue

Updated China Export Controls

  • New U.S. regulations (October 2023) required pausing shipments to China market
  • NVIDIA reconfigured products to be regulation-compliant and "not software hackable"
  • Started shipping alternative products that don't require a license; now sampling to Chinese customers
  • China represented mid-single-digit % of DC revenue in Q4 (down from 20-25% historical)
  • Expected to remain at similar mid-single-digit level in Q1 FY2025
  • After Q1, NVIDIA will compete for business within restrictions; "we'll see how it turns out"
  • No licenses received from U.S. government to ship restricted products

Sovereign AI Infrastructure

  • Countries investing in AI infrastructure to build LLMs in their own language on domestic data
  • Regions want to protect their data, transform it into AI, and provision services domestically
  • Infrastructure being built in Japan, Canada, France, and many other regions
  • Expected to replicate U.S. AI investment patterns globally
  • AI generation factories expected in every industry, company, and region

Supply Chain and Allocation

  • Overall supply improving; supply chain "doing an incredible job"
  • Hopper GPU has 35,000 parts, weighs 70 pounds — "people call it an AI supercomputer for good reason"
  • Inventory shipped immediately upon receipt due to strong demand
  • Purchase commitments include capacity reservations for multiple years
  • Allocation approach: allocate fairly, avoid allocating unnecessarily (don't ship to unready data centers)
  • NVIDIA brings end customers to CSPs — "twofer" allocation strategy connecting partners and end users

Inference Growth

  • ~40% of Data Center revenue from inference (estimate "probably understated")
  • Recommender systems migrated from CPU to GPU acceleration (embeddings, nearest neighbor search, reranking, augmented generation)
  • Every step of modern recommender systems now requires GPU acceleration
  • New inference workloads: ChatGPT, Midjourney, Sora videos, Runway, Firefly — "100% new, none existed a year ago"
  • Recommender systems described as "single largest software engine on the planet"

Gross Margin Dynamics

  • Q4 FY2024 and Q1 FY2025 margins uniquely elevated due to favorable component costs across compute, networking, and manufacturing stages
  • Beyond Q1, margins expected to return to mid-70s% range
  • Mix remains largest driver of gross margin variation
  • Component cost benefits are temporary (2 quarters: Q4 FY2024 and Q1 FY2025)

Software Monetization

  • Software & services at $1B annualized run rate
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise: operating system for AI; $4,500/GPU/year
  • Every enterprise deploying AI expected to run on NVIDIA AI Enterprise
  • DGX Cloud expanded to AWS (joining Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle Cloud)
  • NVIDIA uses DGX Cloud for its own AI R&D
  • Accelerated computing requires domain-specific software stacks (hundreds of libraries)
  • CSPs have large engineering teams; enterprises need NVIDIA to manage/optimize/patch their AI software

Product Roadmap

  • H200: Ramping, initial shipments Q2 FY2025; nearly 2x inference performance vs H100
  • Spectrum-X: Shipping Q1 FY2025; Ethernet for AI with 1.6x performance vs traditional Ethernet; Dell, HPE, Lenovo, Supermicro partnering
  • DRIVE Thor: Available next year; designed for vision transformers; integrates autonomous driving, parking, monitoring, AI cockpit
  • Blackwell (next-gen): Expected supply constrained; demand far exceeds supply
  • RTX 40 Super Series: Launched at CES ($599+); AI capabilities (836 AI TOPS)
  • ACE Microservices: Generative AI digital avatars; won Best of CES 2024 awards
  • TensorRT LLM for Windows: Open-source library; up to 5x faster LLM inference on RTX PCs

Enterprise and Industry AI Adoption

  • Enterprise AI adoption across automotive, healthcare, financial services
  • ServiceNow: largest-ever net new ACV contribution from generative AI products
  • American Express: improved fraud detection accuracy by 6% using NVIDIA AI
  • ~80 vehicle manufacturers using NVIDIA AI infrastructure for autonomous driving
  • Digital biology: BioNeMo generative AI for drug discovery; Recursion making proprietary model available
  • Omniverse: automotive industry digitizing workflows (design, build, simulate, operate)
  • Car configurators: Brickland, WPP, ZeroLight building Omniverse-powered solutions; Lotus adopting

Q4 FY2024 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $22.1B (record), +22% QoQ, +265% YoY (well above $20.0B guidance)
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 76.0%
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 76.7% (benefited from favorable component costs across compute, networking, and manufacturing)
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: +6% QoQ
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: +9% QoQ (higher compute/infrastructure investments and employee growth)
  • Share Repurchases & Dividends: $2.8B returned to shareholders in Q4

Full Year FY2024 Actuals

  • Revenue: $60.9B, +126% YoY
  • Data Center Revenue: $47.5B, more than tripling YoY (3x+)
  • Gaming Revenue: $10.45B, +15% YoY
  • Professional Visualization Revenue: $1.55B, +1% YoY
  • Automotive Revenue: $1.09B, +21% YoY (crossed $1B mark for first time)
  • Share Repurchases & Dividends: $9.9B returned to shareholders ($9.5B in share repurchases)

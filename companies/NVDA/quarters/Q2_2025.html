Quarterly Guidance (Q3 FY2025)

  • Revenue: $32.5B, +/- 2%
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 74.4%, +/- 50 bps
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 75.0%, +/- 50 bps
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$4.3B
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$3.0B
  • GAAP/Non-GAAP Other Income & Expense: ~$350M income (including gains/losses from nonaffiliated investments and publicly held equity securities)
  • Tax Rate (GAAP/Non-GAAP): 17%, +/- 1% (excluding discrete items)

Full Year FY2025 Guidance

  • Gross Margin: Mid-70s% range (consistent with prior guidance); Q4 may dip slightly due to product transitions and new product cost structures but "not as low" as analyst estimate of 71-72%
  • Operating Expenses: GAAP and Non-GAAP expected to grow in the mid- to upper 40% range (raised from low 40% last quarter)
  • Blackwell Revenue: Several billion dollars expected in Q4 FY2025; additive to Hopper growth
  • Hopper: Will continue to grow in H2 vs H1; Hopper growth Q3 to Q4 expected on top of Blackwell
  • Sovereign AI Revenue: Expected to reach low double-digit billions (raised from high single-digit billions)
  • Software Revenue: Expected to approach $2B annual run rate exiting the year

Long-Term Targets

  • Data Center Growth: Expects to grow Data Center business quite significantly next year (FY2026); Blackwell will carry into following year
  • $1 Trillion Transition: Next $1 trillion of world's data center infrastructure will be vastly accelerated; in future, every data center will have GPUs
  • Blackwell Platform: 7 one-of-a-kind chips (Grace CPU, Blackwell dual GPU, ConnectX DPU, BlueField DPU, NVLink switch, Quantum, Spectrum-X); took ~5 years to realize
  • Three Networking Platforms: NVLink (GPU scale-up), Quantum InfiniBand (supercomputing/dedicated AI), Spectrum-X (AI on Ethernet)
  • NVLink Scale: 144 GPUs in 72 GB200 packages, 259 TB/s aggregate bandwidth per rack (~10x Hopper)
  • Product Cadence: Annual rhythm; more CPUs, GPUs, NVLink switches, DPUs, InfiniBand, and Ethernet products each year

Q2 FY2025 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $26.3B (record), +16% QoQ, +154% YoY — compute revenue grew 2.5x+ YoY; networking revenue 2x+ YoY; CSPs ~45% of DC revenue; consumer Internet + enterprise >50%; inference >40% of DC revenue (trailing 4 quarters); H200 began ramping; Blackwell widely sampling; Hopper demand strong
  • Gaming: $2.88B, +9% QoQ, +16% YoY — sequential growth in console, notebook, and desktop; demand strong and growing; channel inventory healthy; 100M+ RTX installed base; 600 AI-powered apps/games; 200+ RTX AI laptops
  • Professional Visualization: $454M, +6% QoQ, +20% YoY — AI and graphic use cases driving demand; model fine-tuning and Omniverse workloads; automotive and manufacturing key verticals
  • Automotive & Robotics: $346M, +5% QoQ, +37% YoY — self-driving platform ramps and AI cockpit solutions; NVIDIA Isaac robotics platform adopted by Boston Dynamics, BYD Electronics, Figure, Siemens, and others

Q2 FY2025 Key Operating Metrics

  • Inference Revenue: >40% of Data Center revenue (trailing 4 quarters); growing significantly
  • H200: Began ramping in Q2; shipped to large CSPs, consumer Internet and enterprise companies; 40%+ more memory bandwidth vs H100
  • Blackwell: Widely sampling; mask change executed to improve production yields; no functional changes; ~100 system configurations shown at Computex; production ramp scheduled Q4 FY2025; several billion dollars in Blackwell revenue expected in Q4
  • Hopper: Demand strong; shipments expected to increase in H2 FY2025; supply and availability improved
  • Networking: Revenue +16% QoQ; Ethernet for AI (Spectrum-X) doubled sequentially; hundreds of customers adopting; xAI using Spectrum-X for largest GPU cluster in world; 1.6x performance vs traditional Ethernet
  • Spectrum-X: Well on track to become multibillion-dollar product line within a year; new products planned every year; scaling from tens of thousands of GPUs to millions of DPUs
  • Sovereign AI Revenue: Expected to reach low double-digit billions this year (raised from high single-digit billions last quarter)
  • Enterprise AI: Most Fortune 100 companies working on AI initiatives; enterprise wave has started
  • Software/SaaS/Support Revenue: Expected to approach $2B annual run rate exiting this year (up from $1B in Q4 FY2024); NVIDIA AI Enterprise notably contributing
  • NIM Adoption: 150+ partners embedding NIMs; AT&T realized 70% cost savings and 8x latency reduction; NIM Agent Blueprints launched
  • AI Foundry: New service announced with Meta Llama 3.1; Accenture first adopter
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise: $4,500/GPU/year; available through NIM, NIM Agent Blueprints, AI Foundry
  • Enterprise AI Examples: Amdocs (30% customer service cost reduction), ServiceNow (Now Assist fastest-growing product), SAP (Joule copilot), Cohesity, Snowflake (3B+ queries/day for 10,000+ customers)
  • Automotive in DC: Every automaker developing AV using NVIDIA data centers; multibillion-dollar revenue opportunity; will grow as next-gen AV models require significantly more compute
  • Healthcare: On its way to multibillion-dollar business (AI for medical imaging, surgical robots, drug discovery, EHR processing)
  • Next-Gen Models: Expected to require 10-20x more compute with significantly more data
  • MLPerf Inference: NVIDIA Hopper + Blackwell won gold medals on all tests
  • GB200 NVL72: 72 GPUs act as single GPU; 30x faster inference for LLM workloads; enables trillion-parameter models in real time

Q3 FY2025 Segment Expectations

  • Revenue Drivers: Continued growth of Hopper architecture + sampling of Blackwell products
  • Blackwell Production: Ramp expected in Q4 FY2025 (not Q3)
  • Hopper: Shipments expected to increase in H2; new Hopper products continuing to ramp in Q3

Blackwell Platform Update

  • Mask change completed; no functional changes necessary; functional samples being distributed
  • ~100 different Blackwell-based system configurations built (shown at Computex)
  • Production ramp scheduled Q4 FY2025; "shipping out" in Q4 (not just starting production)
  • Several billion dollars in Blackwell revenue expected in Q4, additive to Hopper
  • Configurations: air-cooled (HGX form factor) and liquid-cooled (Grace Blackwell); most CSPs deploying some of both
  • Liquid cooling: 3-5x more AI throughput in power-limited data center vs air-cooled; cheaper TCO
  • GB200 NVL72: 72 Grace Blackwell packages = 144 GPUs connected via NVLink; game-changing for low-latency LLM inference
  • MGX modular reference architecture: 100+ Blackwell-based systems designed quickly and cost-effectively
  • Blackwell components sold disaggregated; rack integration done by ecosystem partners close to data center locations

ROI and Demand Dynamics

  • Cloud providers getting immediate ROI from NVIDIA infrastructure
  • For every $1B of general-purpose CPU infrastructure, rental revenue is less than $1B (commoditized)
  • NVIDIA infrastructure: saves money (data processing acceleration), gets rented immediately (AI startups), improves own services (ad systems, recommender systems, search)
  • Hopper demand remains strong despite Blackwell anticipation; no pause expected
  • Data processing acceleration is "probably just a giant part" of ROI (mundane but ubiquitous)
  • GPU cloud capacity availability: "basically none" — all deployed or rented

China Market

  • Data Center revenue in China grew sequentially in Q2
  • Significant contributor to Data Center revenue
  • As percentage of total DC revenue, remains below pre-export-control levels
  • Market expected to remain very competitive going forward

Sovereign AI

  • Revenue expected to reach low double-digit billions this year (raised from high single-digit billions)
  • Japan AIST building AI Bridging Cloud Infrastructure 3.0 with NVIDIA
  • Countries recognizing AI as national imperative for society and industries
  • Data as national natural resource; countries building own AI infrastructure for regional digital intelligence

Enterprise AI and Software

  • Enterprise AI wave has started; most Fortune 100 on AI initiatives
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise platform: NeMo + NIMs + NIM Agent Blueprints + AI Foundry
  • $4,500/GPU/year; ecosystem partners (world-leading IT companies) bringing to enterprises
  • Software TAM grows as CUDA-compatible GPU installed base scales from millions to tens of millions
  • Software/SaaS/support revenue expected to exit year at $2B run rate
  • NIM Agent Blueprints: customizable reference applications for customer service, drug discovery, enterprise RAG

General Robotics

  • Omniverse opening next era of AI: general robotics
  • Physical AI learned from video, human demonstration, and synthetic data generation
  • Reinforcement learning from Omniverse simulations
  • Working with "just about every robotics company"
  • NVIDIA Isaac platform: Boston Dynamics, BYD Electronics, Figure, Intrinsyc, Siemens, Skilled AI, Teradyne Robotics
  • Foxconn using Omniverse for digital twins of Blackwell manufacturing plants
  • Mercedes-Benz: multiyear Omniverse Cloud contracts for industrial digital twins

Q2 FY2025 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $30.0B (record), +15% QoQ, +122% YoY (well above $28.0B guidance)
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 75.1%
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 75.7% (down sequentially due to higher mix of new products within Data Center + inventory provisions for low-yielding Blackwell material)
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: +12% QoQ
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: +12% QoQ (higher compensation-related costs)
  • Cash Flow from Operations: $14.5B
  • Share Repurchases & Dividends: $7.4B returned to shareholders in Q2
  • Share Repurchase Authorization: Board approved additional $50B, adding to ~$7.5B remaining at end of Q2

Full Year FY2026 Guidance

  • Gross Margin Trajectory: Working towards mid-70s% range late this year (reaffirmed)

Long-Term Targets

  • Four Growth Drivers: (1) Inference/reasoning AI exponential demand growth; (2) AI Diffusion Rule rescinded — sovereign AI unlocked globally; (3) Enterprise AI — agents work, agentic AI is game-changing; (4) Industrial AI — onshoring/reshoring creating AI factory demand alongside physical factories
  • AI as Infrastructure: Intelligence is essential infrastructure for every country, industry, society; AI factories produce tokens like energy factories produce electricity; build-out is very early stages
  • Enterprise AI Systems: RTX Pro enterprise server, DGX Spark, DGX Station — modernize $500B on-prem IT infrastructure; every major IT provider partnering with NVIDIA
  • Robotics Era: Billions of robots, hundreds of millions of autonomous vehicles, hundreds of thousands of robotic factories and warehouses
  • Product Roadmap: Annual cadence extending through 2028; GB300 → Blackwell Ultra → future generations
  • Sovereign AI: New growth engine; every nation investing in AI infrastructure like electricity and Internet before it

Q1 FY2026 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $39.0B, +73% YoY — Blackwell contributed nearly 70% of DC compute revenue; Hopper transition nearly complete; AI workloads transitioned strongly to inference; AI factory build-outs driving significant revenue; customer commitments firm
  • Gaming: $3.8B (record), +48% QoQ, +42% YoY — Blackwell fastest gaming ramp ever; GeForce RTX 5060/5060 Ti launched at $299; RTX 5060 laptops from $1,099; 100M+ RTX installed base; supply and availability greatly improved in Q1; Nintendo Switch 2 with NVIDIA custom RTX GPUs and DLSS
  • Professional Visualization: $509M, flat QoQ, +19% YoY — tariff-related uncertainty temporarily impacted Q1 systems demand; AI workstation demand strong; DGX Spark (1 PFLOP) and DGX Station (20 PFLOPS, GB300 Superchip) announced
  • Automotive: $567M, -1% QoQ, +72% YoY — self-driving ramps and NEV demand driving YoY growth; GM partnership for next-gen vehicles, factories, and robots; Mercedes-Benz CLA in production (full-stack solution); Isaac GR00T N1 humanoid robot foundation model announced; Cosmos World Foundation models launched

Q1 FY2026 Key Operating Metrics

  • H20 Export Controls (April 9): $4.6B H20 revenue recognized before April 9; $2.5B unable to ship; $4.5B charge (inventory write-down + purchase obligations); charge less than initially anticipated (reused certain materials); total Q1 H20 should have been $7B; China AI TAM ~$50B effectively closed; no replacement product at present; Hopper cannot be reduced further to comply with new limits
  • Blackwell Ramp: Fastest ramp in company history; nearly 70% of DC compute revenue; GB200 NVL racks generally available; significant manufacturing yield improvement; rack shipments at strong rates
  • Hyperscaler Deployments: Major hyperscalers each deploying ~1,000 NVL72 racks (72,000 GPUs) per week; on track to further ramp output this quarter; Microsoft deployed tens of thousands of Blackwell GPUs, ramping to hundreds of thousands of GB200s
  • GB300: Sampling began earlier this month at major CSPs; production shipments expected later this quarter; same architecture/footprint/electrical/mechanical specs as GB200 (drop-in design); 50% more HBM; 50% more dense FP4 inference compute vs B200; CSPs can seamlessly transition manufacturing
  • Product Roadmap: Annual cadence; extends through 2028; tightly aligned with customer multi-year planning cycles; Blackwell Ultra is next phase after GB200
  • Inference Demand: Sharp jump; Microsoft processed 100+ trillion tokens in Q1 (5x YoY); inference serving startups tripling token generation rate on B200; reasoning AI requires 100-1,000x more tokens per task
  • NVIDIA Dynamo: On Blackwell NVL72: 30x inference throughput for reasoning models; Capital One reduced agentic chatbot latency by 5x
  • MLPerf Inference: First GB200 NVL72 results: up to 30x higher throughput vs 8-GPU H200 on Llama 3.1 (3x per-GPU performance + 9x more GPUs on single NVLink domain)
  • Blackwell Software Optimizations: 1.5x improvement in last month alone; Hopper inference improved 4x over 2 years (CUDA architecture benefit)
  • Grace Blackwell vs Hopper: ~40x higher speed and throughput for inference
  • AI Factories: ~100 NVIDIA-powered AI factories in flight (2x YoY); average GPUs per factory also doubled; line of sight to projects requiring tens of gigawatts of NVIDIA AI infrastructure
  • Llama Nemotron: Open reasoning models for agentic AI; 20% accuracy boost; 5x inference speed increase; adopted by Accenture, Cadence, Deloitte, Microsoft
  • NeMo Microservices GA: Cisco 40% accuracy improvement + 10x response time; NASDAQ 30% accuracy improvement; Shell 30% accuracy increase; 20% training time acceleration vs other frameworks
  • Enterprise AI: Yum! Brands partnership: 500 restaurants this year → 61,000 over time; CrowdStrike 2x faster detection with 50% less compute cost; RTX Pro enterprise AI server announced
  • Networking: Revenue $5.0B, +64% QoQ; NVLink shipments exceeded $1B; NVLink Fusion announced at COMPUTEX (hyperscale semi-custom CCUs/accelerators connect via NVLink); partners: MediaTek, Marvell, Alchip, Astera Labs, Fujitsu, Qualcomm
  • Spectrum-X: Annualizing over $8B revenue; added Google Cloud and Meta as customers; silicon photonics switches announced (3.5x power efficiency, 10x network resiliency, 1.3x time-to-market); customers: CoreWeave, Microsoft Azure, Oracle Cloud, xAI, Google Cloud, Meta
  • Four Networking Platforms: NVLink (scale-up), InfiniBand (supercomputing), Spectrum-X (Ethernet for AI), BlueField (control plane for security/storage/multi-tenancy)
  • Omniverse: Integrated with Databricks, SAP, Schneider Electric; TSMC saves months in fab design; Foxconn 150x thermal simulation acceleration; Pegatron 67% assembly defect reduction
  • Robotics: Isaac GR00T N1 (first open humanoid robot foundation model); new Cosmos World Foundation models; adopted by 1X, Agility Robotics, Figure AI, Uber, Waabi, Boston Dynamics, XPENG Robotics; GE Healthcare using Isaac for robotic imaging/surgery
  • China Geography: Singapore ~20% of Q1 billed revenue; 99% of H100/H200/Blackwell billed to Singapore was for U.S.-based customers; China DC revenue slightly below expectations, down sequentially
  • U.S. Manufacturing: TSMC building 6 fabs + 2 advanced packaging in Arizona (volume production by year-end); SPIL and Amkor also investing in Arizona; Foxconn 1M sq ft factory in Houston; Wistron in Fort Worth; goal: chip to supercomputer built in America within a year
  • AI Diffusion Rule: Rescinded by President Trump; new policy to promote U.S. AI tech with trusted partners; Saudi Arabia 500MW AI infrastructure project; UAE 5GW AI campus
  • Sovereign AI: Taiwan first AI factory (Foxconn + Taiwan government); Sweden national AI infrastructure; Japan, Korea, India, Canada, France, U.K., Germany, Italy, Spain building national AI factories

H20 Export Controls and China Market

  • April 9: U.S. government issued new export controls on H20 (no grace period)
  • H20 had been sold with approval of previous administration; in market for over 1 year; no market outside China
  • Q1 H20 revenue: $4.6B (recognized before April 9)
  • Unable to ship: $2.5B in Q1
  • Total Q1 H20 should have been: $7B
  • Write-down: $4.5B (inventory + purchase obligations for orders received before April 9); less than initially anticipated (reused certain materials)
  • Q2 impact: ~$8B in planned H20 orders lost
  • Forward impact: additional orders beyond Q2 that cannot be fulfilled (incorporated in $4.5B write-down)
  • China AI TAM: ~$50B, effectively closed to U.S. industry
  • No replacement product: new limits make it impossible to reduce Hopper further for productive use; "end of the road for Hopper" in China
  • Limited options being explored but nothing to announce
  • China has enormous manufacturing capability; assumption it cannot make AI chips "was always questionable, and now it's clearly wrong"
  • Export controls have spurred China's innovation and scale; risk of strengthening foreign competitors

Reasoning AI and Inference Scaling

  • Reasoning AI (DeepSeek-R1, ChatGPT reasoning) requires 100-1,000x more tokens per task vs one-shot chatbots
  • Step-function surge in inference demand; inference is now significant part of compute workload
  • AI scaling laws intact for both training and inference at massive scale
  • Grace Blackwell NVL72: ideal "thinking machine" for reasoning AI; ~40x higher speed vs Hopper
  • Open source AI strategic value: DeepSeek and Qwen among best open source models; U.S. platforms must remain preferred for open source AI
  • Microsoft processed 100+ trillion tokens in Q1 (5x YoY); exponential growth in Azure OpenAI

Sovereign AI and Onshore Manufacturing

  • AI Diffusion Rule rescinded; President Trump proposed new policy promoting U.S. AI tech with trusted partners
  • Saudi Arabia: 500MW AI infrastructure project
  • UAE: 5GW AI campus
  • Taiwan: first AI factory (Foxconn + Taiwan government)
  • Sweden: national AI infrastructure launched
  • Japan, Korea, India, Canada, France, U.K., Germany, Italy, Spain building national AI factories
  • U.S. manufacturing: TSMC 6 fabs + 2 packaging in Arizona; SPIL and Amkor also in Arizona; Foxconn Houston; Wistron Fort Worth
  • Goal: chip to supercomputer built in America within a year
  • GB200 NVL72 rack: 1.2M components, weighs nearly 2 tons

Networking Growth

  • Four networking platforms: NVLink (scale-up), InfiniBand (supercomputing), Spectrum-X (Ethernet for AI), BlueField (control plane)
  • NVLink: 5th generation, 14x bandwidth of PCIe Gen 5; NVLink 72 carries 130 TB/s per rack (equivalent to world's peak Internet traffic); Q1 shipments exceeded $1B; NVLink Fusion announced at COMPUTEX
  • Spectrum-X: annualizing $8B+; improved Ethernet cluster utilization from ~50% to 85-90%; on $10B cluster, 40% utilization improvement = $4B value; silicon photonics switches announced
  • BlueField: used for storage, security, multi-tenant isolation; bare-metal performance in shared clusters

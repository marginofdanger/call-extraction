Quarterly Guidance (Q4 FY2025)

  • Revenue: $37.5B, +/- 2%
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 73.0%, +/- 50 bps
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 73.5%, +/- 50 bps
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$4.8B
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: ~$3.4B
  • GAAP/Non-GAAP Other Income & Expense: ~$400M income (excluding gains/losses on nonaffiliated investments)
  • Tax Rate (GAAP/Non-GAAP): 16.5%, +/- 1% (excluding discrete items)

Long-Term Targets

  • No Digestion Until $1T Modernized: Jensen expects continued growth until world's $1T data center infrastructure modernized for machine learning
  • Data Center Modernization Timeline: Could take ~4 years; by 2030, world's computing data centers could be ~$2 trillion
  • AI as New Industry: Multi-trillion-dollar AI industry; AI factories manufacturing digital intelligence
  • Annual Product Roadmap: On track; Ultra coming next year; transition to Rubin in FY2027
  • Physical AI: New frontier — AI that understands physical world structure; enables industrial AI and robotics
  • Inference Time Scaling: New scaling law (test-time scaling) dramatically increases compute demand
  • Scaling Continues: Pretraining, post-training, and inference time scaling all growing simultaneously

Q3 FY2025 Segment Revenue

  • Data Center: $30.8B (record), +17% QoQ, +112% YoY — Hopper demand exceptional; H200 sales increased significantly to double-digit billions (fastest product ramp in company history); CSPs ~50% of DC revenue (2x+ YoY); consumer Internet revenue 2x+ YoY; GPU regional cloud revenue jumped 2x YoY (North America, India, Asia Pacific sovereign cloud build-outs)
  • Gaming: $3.3B, +14% QoQ, +15% YoY — notebook, console, desktop all grew; strong back-to-school RTX demand; channel inventory healthy; new GeForce RTX AI PCs with Copilot+ capabilities (ASUS, MSI)
  • Professional Visualization: $486M, +7% QoQ, +17% YoY — RTX workstations preferred for pro graphics; AI emerging as demand driver (AV simulation, gen AI prototyping, content creation)
  • Automotive: $449M (record), +30% QoQ, +72% YoY — self-driving platform ramps (DRIVE Orin) and robust NAV demand; Volvo Cars EV SUV built on NVIDIA Orin and DriveOS

Q3 FY2025 Key Operating Metrics

  • H200: Sales increased to double-digit billions (fastest product ramp in company history); 2x faster inference vs H100; up to 50% improved TCO; available on AWS, CoreWeave, Azure (Google Cloud, OCI coming soon)
  • Blackwell: In full production after successful mask change; shipped 13,000 GPU samples to customers in Q3; first DGX engineering sample to OpenAI; demand is "staggering"
  • Blackwell Systems: Oracle announced Zettascale AI cloud (131,000+ Blackwell GPUs); Microsoft first to offer GB200 cloud instances (private preview) with Quantum InfiniBand; systems being stood up at Dell, CoreWeave, Oracle, Google
  • MLPerf Training: Blackwell swept per-GPU benchmarks; 2.2x leap over Hopper; GPT-3 benchmark: 64 Blackwell GPUs vs 256 H100s (4x cost reduction)
  • Inference Software: Hopper inference throughput boosted 5x in 1 year through CUDA algorithm innovations; cut time-to-first-token 5x; upcoming NIM release: additional 2.4x Hopper inference boost
  • Blackwell for Inference: NVLink Switch enables up to 30x faster inference; excellent for reasoning inference (OpenAI o1)
  • Networking: Revenue +20% YoY; sequentially down (but demand strong; expects sequential growth in Q4); InfiniBand, Ethernet switches, SmartNICs, BlueField DPUs all grew areas of sequential growth
  • Spectrum-X: Revenue increased 3x+ YoY; xAI Colossus 100K Hopper supercomputer: 0 latency degradation, 95% data throughput (vs 60% traditional Ethernet); multiple CSPs planning large cluster deployments
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise: Full year revenue expected to increase 2x+ from last year; ~1,000 companies using NIM
  • Software/Service/Support Revenue: Annualizing at $1.5B in Q3; expects to exit year at $2B+ annual run rate
  • Enterprise AI: Accenture launched 30,000-professional business group trained on NVIDIA AI; Deloitte also taking NVIDIA AI to enterprises; Accenture using agentic AI internally (25-35% reduction in manual marketing steps)
  • Sovereign AI: Pipeline intact; growth in Europe and Asia Pacific; India GPU deployments nearly 10x by year-end; Infosys, TCS, Wipro upskilling ~500,000 developers/consultants on NVIDIA AI
  • China: DC revenue grew sequentially (export-compliant Hopper products); as percentage of total DC revenue, well below pre-export-control levels
  • Three Scaling Laws: (1) Pretraining scaling intact and continuing; (2) Post-training scaling (RLHF → RLAI + synthetic data); (3) Inference-time/test-time scaling (OpenAI o1 "Strawberry" — longer thinking = better answers)

Q4 FY2025 Segment Expectations

  • Data Center: Continued Hopper demand + initial Blackwell ramp; on track to exceed previous "several billion dollars" Blackwell revenue estimate; Hopper may or may not grow sequentially; Blackwell ramping from zero in Q3 to billions in Q4
  • Gaming: Sequential decline expected due to supply constraints; expects recovery in new calendar year
  • Networking: Sequential growth anticipated in Q4

Gross Margin Trajectory

  • Q4 FY2025 (guided): Non-GAAP 73.5% — Blackwell ramp beginning; 7 different chip types, multiple configurations
  • Early FY2026: Low 70s% (71-72.5% range) as Blackwell ramp continues; Blackwell expected to cross over Hopper in April quarter
  • H2 FY2026: Mid-70s% recovery as yields improve and ramp matures; "reasonable assumption" per Colette
  • Fully Ramped Blackwell: Mid-70s% target

Blackwell Production Ramp

  • Full production after successful mask change; 7 custom chips
  • 13,000 GPU samples shipped to customers in Q3
  • Configurations: air-cooled or liquid-cooled, NVLink 8/36/72, x86 or Grace
  • Q4 revenue expected to exceed previous "several billion dollars" estimate
  • Blackwell expected to cross over Hopper revenue in April quarter (Q1 FY2026)
  • Shipping more Blackwells next quarter, and more the quarter after that
  • "Almost every company in the world seems to be involved in our supply chain"
  • Key partners: TSMC, Amphenol, Vertiv, SK Hynix, Micron, Amkor, KYEC, Foxconn, Quanta, Wiwynn, Dell, HP, Super Micro, Lenovo
  • Integration into diverse data center configurations is "nothing short of a miracle"

Three Scaling Laws for AI

  • Pretraining Scaling: Intact and continuing; empirical law, not physical; last-gen foundation models: ~100K Hoppers; next-gen starts at 100K Blackwells; multimodality models training on petabytes of video
  • Post-Training Scaling: RLHF → RLAI + synthetic data; improving model quality post-training
  • Inference-Time Scaling (Test-Time Scaling): OpenAI o1 ("Strawberry"); longer thinking = higher quality answers; chain of thought, multi-path planning, reflection; fundamentally new scaling dimension
  • Result: all three scaling dimensions driving more compute demand simultaneously

Inference Market

  • NVIDIA is largest inference platform in the world (large installed base)
  • Hopper inference throughput improved 5x in 1 year (software alone); upcoming NIM release adds 2.4x
  • Blackwell with NVLink: 30x faster inference; enables reasoning inference (o1-style)
  • 64 Blackwell GPUs replace 256 H100s for GPT-3 (4x cost reduction)
  • Goal: every company doing inference 24/7; every computer interaction generating tokens
  • Training clusters become inference infrastructure as newer training clusters deployed

Enterprise AI and Agentic AI

  • Enterprise AI "in full throttle"
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise = NeMo + NIM microservices; operating platform for agentic AI
  • Industry leaders: Cadence, Cloudera, Cohesity, NetApp, Nutanix, Salesforce, SAP, ServiceNow — racing to build copilots and agents
  • Potential for billions of agents deployed in coming years
  • Consulting: Accenture (30K professionals), Deloitte taking NVIDIA AI to enterprises
  • NVIDIA AI Enterprise revenue expected 2x+ YoY; pipeline continues to build

Sovereign AI and Regional Cloud

  • India: leading CSPs (Jio, Yotta) building AI factories with tens of thousands of GPUs; GPU deployments nearly 10x by year-end
  • India IT: Infosys, TCS, Wipro upskilling ~500K developers on NVIDIA AI Enterprise
  • Japan: SoftBank building most powerful AI supercomputer with DGX Blackwell + Quantum InfiniBand; partnering on AI-RAN (5G + AI on CUDA)
  • U.S.: T-Mobile launching AI-RAN with NVIDIA
  • Japan IT: Fujitsu, NEC, NTT adopting NVIDIA AI Enterprise; EY Strategy consulting
  • Regional clouds: North America, India, Asia Pacific ramped cloud instances 2x YoY

Physical AI and Robotics

  • Breakthroughs in physical AI: foundation models understanding the physical world
  • Can predict and "roll out a short future"
  • Valuable for industrial AI and robotics
  • Omniverse created specifically to enable physical AI development
  • Synthetic data generation + reinforcement learning with physics feedback
  • Fired up many AI-native companies, robotics companies, and physical AI companies
  • Foxconn using Omniverse digital twins for Blackwell factories (30%+ annual kWh reduction in Mexico facility)

Q3 FY2025 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $35.1B (record), +17% QoQ, +94% YoY (well above $32.5B guidance)
  • GAAP Gross Margin: 74.6%
  • Non-GAAP Gross Margin: 75.0% (down sequentially due to mix shift from H100 to more complex/higher-cost systems within Data Center)
  • GAAP Operating Expenses: +9% QoQ
  • Non-GAAP Operating Expenses: +9% QoQ (higher compute, infrastructure, and engineering development costs for new product introductions)
  • Share Repurchases & Dividends: $11.2B returned to shareholders in Q3

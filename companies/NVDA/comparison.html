20 Key Takeaways

1
The greatest revenue ramp in semiconductor history. Revenue guidance grew 9.6x from $8.1B to $78.0B across 16 quarters. Full-year revenue went from $27.0B (FY2023) to ~$216B (FY2026) — an 8x increase in 4 years, with 12 consecutive sequential guidance increases after the ChatGPT inflection.
2
Data Center devoured the company. DC revenue grew 16.3x ($3.8B→$62.0B/quarter), rising from 56% to 90% of total. FY2026 DC revenue of $194B exceeds the entire company’s FY2025 total ($130.5B). The Q2 FY2024 quarter (+141% QoQ) was the defining inflection.
3
Architecture transitions follow a predictable V-shape. Non-GAAP GM ranged from 45.9% (inventory crisis trough) to 78.9% (Hopper peak). Each new architecture compresses margins then recovers to the “mid-70s%” steady-state target. The pattern repeated with Blackwell: 71.3% underlying trough → 75.2% recovery.
4
Networking became a $31B+ business from near-zero. From ~$800M/quarter (FY2023) to $11.0B/quarter (Q4 FY2026), reaching $31B+ for the year (10x vs FY2025). Spectrum-X alone hit an $11–12B annualized rate within 2 years of announcement. NVIDIA is now the world’s largest networking company.
5
China went from 20–25% of DC to zero. Three escalating export controls eliminated access to China’s ~$50B AI market. The April 2025 H20 ban triggered a $4.5B write-down. Jensen warned this “spurred China’s innovation” and competitors are making progress via IPOs.
6
Sovereign AI: zero to $30B+ in 2 years. From “multibillion over a few years” (Nov 2023) to $30B+ actual (FY2026). Every major nation is building domestic AI infrastructure, partially offsetting the loss of the China market.
7
The AI revolution unfolded across these 16 calls. LLMs as research (Q1 FY2023) → ChatGPT “iPhone moment” (Q4 FY2023) → inference scaling at 40%+ of DC (FY2024) → reasoning AI with 100x compute (FY2025) → agentic AI “ChatGPT moment” with Claude Code & GPT-5.3-Codex (Q4 FY2026). Each stage multiplied compute demand.
8
Annual architecture cadence is the strategic moat. From 2-year to annual cycles: Hopper → Blackwell → GB300 → Vera Rubin. Each generation: 9x→30x→50x→10x performance leaps. Content per GW grows each generation (~$20–25B → ~$35B → higher), compounding revenue independent of total GW deployed.
9
NVIDIA transformed from GPU vendor to AI infrastructure investor. Strategic investments in OpenAI (10+ GW, since 2016), Anthropic ($10B, first on NVIDIA arch), Meta (millions of GPUs), xAI (Grok licensing). The thesis: expand CUDA ecosystem, get equity in consequential companies, lock in multi-year demand.
10
Free cash flow reached $97B — approaching prior-year revenue. $41B returned to shareholders (43% of FCF). R&D approaching $20B/year. The company went from zero buybacks (Q1 FY2024) to returning $11.2B in a single quarter as the balance sheet exploded.
11
Gaming became the forgotten founding segment. Q4 FY2026 Gaming ($3.7B) is nearly identical to Q1 FY2023 ($3.6B) — flat in 16 quarters. Revenue share collapsed from 43% to 5.4%. Yet 100M+ RTX PCs and Nintendo Switch 2 make it strategically valuable for consumer AI.
12
The $3T–$4T infrastructure vision is now quantifiable. TAM grew from $600B (FY2023) to $3T–$4T annual by decade end. The “content per GW” metric (~$35B Blackwell, increasing for Rubin) provides a compounding mechanism: NVIDIA’s share expands each generation even if total GW growth moderates. CSP CapEx approaching $700B/year validates the trajectory.

# Carvana Co. Q4_2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** February 18, 2026 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 2025 (Full Year 2025)

---

## Q4 2025 Actuals (Reported)

**Retail Units Sold:** 163,522 — increase of **43% YoY** (new company record; 8th consecutive quarter of industry-leading retail unit growth)

**Revenue:** $5.603 billion — increase of **58% YoY**
- Note: Revenue growth exceeded retail unit growth primarily due to traditional gross revenue treatment for certain vehicles acquired from a large retail marketplace partner

**Non-GAAP Retail GPU:** decreased **$255 YoY**
- Drivers: higher non-vehicle costs (reconditioning elevated), lower shipping fees passed through to customers (~$60 reduction), higher retail depreciation rates

**Non-GAAP Wholesale GPU:** decreased **$148 YoY**
- Driver: faster growth in retail units sold than wholesale marketplace units

**Non-GAAP Other GPU:** increased **+$49 YoY**
- Drivers: improvements in cost of funds, higher finance and VSC attach rates; partially offset by decision to lower interest rates for customers (~1 percentage point rate reduction relative to benchmark on average)

**Non-GAAP SG&A Expense per Retail Unit Sold:** decreased **$340 YoY**
- Operations expenses: decreased **$57 per unit**
- Overhead expenses: decreased **$344 per unit**
- Advertising expense: increased **$83 per unit** (investment in building awareness/trust)

**Net Income:** $951 million — increase of **$792 million YoY**
- Net income margin: **17.0%** — increase from **4.5%** prior year
- Noncash benefit: $618 million total, including:
  - Net noncash tax benefit: **$685 million** (deferred tax asset valuation allowance release, Up-C structure; >$600 million flowing to Carvana common shareholders)
  - Fair value of warrants reduction: **-$67 million**

**Adjusted EBITDA:** $511 million — increase of **$152 million YoY** (new Q4 record)
**Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** **9.1%** — decrease from **10.1%** prior year
- Decline driven by increased retail revenue per unit from traditional gross revenue treatment noted above

**Adjusted EBITDA Dollars per Unit (Q4):** approximately flat YoY (down ~$14 YoY per management)

**GAAP Operating Income:** $424 million (83% of adjusted EBITDA) — increase of **$164 million YoY** (new Q4 record)

**Variable Adjusted EBITDA Margin (Q4):** ~7% (noted deceleration this quarter; management attributed largely to revenue accounting change)

## Special Topics

### Reconditioning Costs — Q4 2025 Headwind and Q1 2026 Outlook
- Q4 2025 reconditioning costs were elevated vs. prior quarters; driven by rapid new site openings (34 total locations), single-line vs. multi-line site ramp dynamics, and newer site managers
- Management expects elevated non-vehicle reconditioning costs to persist into Q1 2026 on a YoY basis
- Guidance: sequential improvement in retail GPU in Q1 2026 despite the YoY cost headwind
- Estimated improvement if all locations reached top-quartile performance: **$220 per car** (referenced by analyst from shareholder letter; management confirmed as relevant metric)
- Timeline for reversion: management guided 3–6 months to reach a better position

### ADESA Integration
- 10 additional ADESA locations integrated in full year 2025
- Full build-outs of ADESA locations underway in 2026 (incremental CapEx)
- Digital auction capabilities expanded nationwide in 2025

### Revenue Accounting Change — Marketplace vs. Gross Revenue
- Certain vehicles previously counted under marketplace (net revenue) treatment shifted to traditional gross revenue treatment via a large retail marketplace partner agreement
- This inflated reported revenue growth (58%) relative to retail unit growth (43%) in Q4 2025
- This also reduced reported adjusted EBITDA margin (9.1% vs. 10.1% prior year) as the denominator (revenue) grew faster

### Balance Sheet and Financing
- Cash and equivalents: **$2.3 billion** at year-end 2025
- Corporate notes retired: **$709 million** in 2025
- Net debt / TTM adjusted EBITDA: **1.3x** (strongest ever)
- Total loan sale platform capacity (new agreements): **$12 billion** new partner agreements + **$6 billion** Ally agreement = **$18 billion** total committed purchase capacity through 2026–2027

### Tax / Up-C Structure (Q4 Noncash Item)
- Deferred tax asset valuation allowance released in Q4 2025 following sustained profitability
- Total noncash tax benefit recognized: **$685 million**
- Offset by: **$67 million** fair value reduction in warrants
- Net noncash benefit to net income: **$618 million**
- Portion flowing to Carvana common shareholders: **more than $600 million**
- Tax receivable liability reflects portion shared with pre-IPO LLC unitholders under Up-C structure

### Related Party Transactions (Short Report Response)
- Management stated: Carvana does **not** sell loans to related parties and has not done so for any year from **2017 through 2025**
- All related party transactions are disclosed in financial statements
- Management characterized recent short reports alleging otherwise as **"100% inaccurate"**

### Market Share and Competitive Context
- Carvana: ~**1.6%** used vehicle retail market share
- nonautomotive e-commerce adoption: ~**20%** (used as secular runway benchmark)
- Total addressable market: management characterizes Carvana as ~**1%** of the total car market

### AI and Automation
- "Sebastian" AI brain cited as central tool; feeds all customer-facing systems
- **30%** of retail customers complete the entire purchase without speaking to a person
- **60%** of car sellers complete the process without speaking to anyone
- Customers completing fully digital purchases have **higher NPS** than those who call
- AI investment accelerating internally; "last 3–6 months" described as another large step-up in tool quality

### Title and Registration
- Approximately **99%** of packets completed by deadline as of call date
- Management characterized this as likely **best-in-class** across automotive retailers despite cross-state complexity

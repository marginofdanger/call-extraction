# Carvana Co. Q4 2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** February 23, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 2022 (Full Year 2022)

---

## Q4 2022 Actuals (Reported)

### Retail Units & Revenue
- **Retail Units Sold:** 86,977 (-23% YoY; -15% sequentially vs. Q3 2022's 102,570)
- **Sequential Decline Context:** Only slightly larger than industry's sequential decline of 12%, despite several profitability-focused actions (normalizing inventory, reducing advertising, proactively adjusting to benchmark rate increases, profitability initiatives)
- **Total Revenue:** $2.8 billion (-24% YoY; -16% sequentially), approximately in line with retail units sold decline
- **Second Largest Used Vehicle Seller in the U.S.:** For the third consecutive year
- **Market Position:** ~1% market share in a ~40 million unit/year market

### Industry Context
- **Industry Sequential Decline (Q4 vs. Q3):** ~12%
- **Market Outlook:** Generally looking at the market being "flattish" going forward
- **Wholesale Market (early 2023):** "Reasonably strong" with potential appreciation; seasonal inventory build and car price appreciation typical around tax refund season observed in early 2023
- **Vehicle Price Outlook:** "Smarter guess over the next year or 2 is that it will be a depreciating environment on average because car prices are still elevated relative to other goods"

### Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU)
- **Non-GAAP Total GPU:** $2,667 (vs. Q3's $3,870; decrease of -$1,203 sequentially)
- **Non-GAAP Retail GPU:** $632 (vs. Q3's $1,267; decrease of -$635 sequentially)
- **Non-GAAP Wholesale GPU:** $552 (vs. Q3's $682; decrease of -$130 sequentially)
- **Non-GAAP Other GPU:** $1,483 (vs. Q3's $1,921; decrease of -$438 sequentially)

### GPU Unique Items & Adjustments (Q4)
- **Retail Inventory Allowance Adjustment:** $52 million, or $598 per unit (primarily driven by elevated industry-wide retail depreciation rates and higher-than-normalized inventory size relative to sales volumes)
- **Wholesale Inventory Allowance Adjustment:** $5 million (included in wholesale GPU impact)
- **Wholesale Loss on Retail Vehicles Sold in Wholesale Market:** $4 million (combined with wholesale allowance = $103 per unit impact on wholesale GPU)
- **Loan Sale Timing Shift (Ally):** ~$42 million, or ~$483 per retail unit sold (sale of loan pool shifted from December to January to align with upsize and extension of forward flow purchase agreement)
- **Total Unique Items Impact on Adjusted EBITDA:** $103 million negative (combined retail, wholesale, and other GPU items)
- **Vehicles Sold Within 90 Days of Acquisition:** Realized approximately $600 per unit higher retail GPU than aggregate retail GPU in Q4 (illustrating turn-time impact on margins)

### Retail GPU Sequential Drivers (Q4 vs. Q3)
- **Negative:** $598 per unit retail inventory allowance adjustment
- **Negative:** Higher retail depreciation rates
- **Positive:** Wider spreads between retail prices and acquisition prices
- **Positive:** Lower cost of sales

### Wholesale GPU Sequential Drivers (Q4 vs. Q3)
- **Negative:** Combined $103 per unit impact from $5 million wholesale inventory allowance + $4 million loss on retail vehicles sold in wholesale channel
- **Negative:** Lower seasonal wholesale marketplace volume

### Other GPU Sequential Drivers (Q4 vs. Q3)
- **Negative:** Shift in timing of loan sale to Ally from December to January (-$483 per unit / -$42 million)

### GPU Reporting Change (New in Q4)
- **New Non-GAAP Metrics:** Introduced non-GAAP gross profit and non-GAAP SG&A expense that adjust for certain noncash and nonrecurring revenues and expenses
- **Adjusted EBITDA Definition Update:** Now excludes revenue from route warrants, share-based compensation, and restructuring expenses
- **Supplemental Tables:** Posted on IR website Events and Presentations page

### SG&A
- **Non-GAAP SG&A Reduction (Q4 vs. Q3):** -$60 million sequentially (following >$60 million sequential reduction in Q3)
- **SG&A Reduction Components:** Broad-based, including advertising, compensation and benefits, logistics, and other
- **SG&A Per Unit Leverage:** NOT yet meaningfully levered on a per-unit basis because retail units sold have declined at a pace similar to SG&A expense reductions
- **Leverage Expectation:** Stabilizing weekly retail unit sales will allow SG&A expense savings to "catch up" to retail unit volumes, enabling SG&A leverage

### EBITDA & Margins
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** Loss of $291 million, or -10.1% of revenue
- **EBITDA Impact of Unique Items:** Negatively impacted by $103 million due to the unique retail, wholesale, and other GPU items described above

### Goodwill Impairment
- **Goodwill Impairment Expense:** $847 million (recorded due to decline in trading prices of securities by end of Q4)
- **Not Related to:** Changes in long-term expectations for the business or operations of any prior acquisitions

### Inventory & Working Capital
- **Inventory Normalization:** "Significant moves" in normalizing inventory size in Q4 and so far in Q1 2023
- **Inventory Size (Q1 2023 status):** "Much closer to a normal size relative to sales volumes" than in Q4
- **Inventory Reduction Approach:** Purchasing fewer retail vehicles; accelerated in Q4
- **Turn Time Impact:** Higher-than-normalized inventory size led to longer turn times, increasing vehicle depreciation and negatively impacting retail GPU

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity (as of December 31, 2022)
- **Total Liquidity Resources:** ~$3.9 billion (down from ~$4.4 billion at end of Q3)
- **Cash and Revolving Availability:** $1.9 billion (down from $2.3 billion at end of Q3)
- **Unpledged Real Estate and Other Assets:** $2.0 billion, including >$1.1 billion of real estate acquired with ADESA
- **Approximate Real Estate Breakdown:** ~$2 billion total; ~half ADESA, ~half original Carvana real estate; majority is inspection centers described as "high-quality financeable properties"
- **Finance Receivables (loans on balance sheet, end of Q4):** ~$1.3 billion (referenced by analyst; ~$1 billion extra loans shifted over year-end due to Ally renewal timing)
- **Additional Capital Options (management cited):** Real estate financing, additional secured debt capacity, additional unsecured debt capacity, equity issuance

### Interest Expense Context
- **Annual Interest Expense (analyst reference):** ~$600 million
- **Annual CapEx (analyst reference):** $100 million+
- **Implied Annual Cash Burn at EBITDA Breakeven (analyst estimate):** ~$700 million (interest + CapEx)

### IRC / Production Capacity
- **Annual IRC Capacity at Full Utilization:** ~1.3 million units (including ADESA locations; up from ~1.2 million at end of Q3)

### ADESA Integration
- **ADESA Sites:** 56 locations nationwide
- **Wholesale Vehicle Routing to ADESA:** 75% of cars bought from customers planned for wholesale now landing at ADESA properties
- **ADESA Benefits:** Lower transportation costs (closer proximity), rapid inspection and sale, efficiency gains in wholesale, logistics, and ultimately reconditioning
- **ADESA Timing of Benefits:** Most benefits expected in "step 3" (return to growth phase); ADESA team described as doing "a great job" in a tough post-pandemic auction environment

### Operational Efficiency Metrics (Cited in Prepared Remarks)
- **Average Delivery Distance:** Down 25% since early 2022
- **Activity Pairing Rate:** Now 1 out of 3 retail deliveries paired with a vehicle pickup (vs. 1 out of 14 one year ago)
- **Customer Care Time Per Sale:** Advocates spending 40% less time on the phone per sale vs. early 2022
- **Vending Machine Pickup Rate:** 40% of customers nationwide now picking up at a vending machine (roughly double vs. early 2022); only available in a subset of markets
- **Customer Experience Quality:** Improved over the last 6 months despite all cost-reduction initiatives

### Ally Partnership
- **Renewal:** Completed seventh-year renewal of Ally forward flow purchase agreement (upsized and extended)
- **Ally Spread:** Ally has "more spread" than historically, but "reflective of market conditions"; Carvana passes spreads on and earns "similar finance GPU to what we've earned in the past"
- **Securitization:** Also closed first securitization of 2023 (as of call date)
- **Multi-Channel Strategy:** Will continue to operate multichannel loan sale strategy; plan to "catch up on loan sales in the first half of this year"

## Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2023)

### Retail Units
- **Direction:** Expect sequential reduction in retail units sold in Q1 vs. Q4
- **Drivers:** Continuing to normalize inventory size, optimizing marketing spend, making progress on profitability initiatives
- **Weekly Sales Volume:** Expect weekly retail unit sales volume to stabilize relative to the declines seen in H2 2022 as seasonal headwinds transition to seasonal tailwinds

### GPU
- **Total GPU:** Expect sequential increase in total GPU in Q1 vs. Q4
- **Retail GPU:** Expect increase in Q1 vs. Q4, due to multiple offsetting effects:
  - **Negative:** Purchasing fewer retail vehicles means fewer low-age units added to website, increasing average age of inventory and retail units sold, reducing retail GPU
  - **Positive:** Expect lower inventory size to lead to a retail inventory allowance adjustment benefit in Q1
  - **Net:** Q1 retail GPU expected to be higher than Q4
- **Other GPU:** Expect sequential increase in Q1, following the shift in loan sale timing from December to January
- **Retail GPU Context (no meaningful gains expected in Q1):** "While we don't expect to see meaningful gains on retail GPU in Q1, we expect to see the benefits of reducing inventory size become apparent in the following quarters"

### SG&A
- **Target:** Approximately $100 million aggregate reduction in quarterly non-GAAP SG&A expense by Q2 2023 compared to Q4 2022 (over the next 2 quarters)
- **Note:** Original transcript says "$1 million reduction" but contextually this is "$100 million" based on all other references throughout the call; confirmed by Ernie Garcia's remark: "driving down SG&A dollars by $100 million over the next 2 quarters"
- **Scope:** Broad-based across all large SG&A expense components, including payroll, advertising, logistics, and the "other" expense bucket (including categories traditionally thought of as fixed / corporate and technology expenses)
- **No Additional Forced Reductions:** Do NOT expect future reductions to involve force reductions (layoffs)
- **Cumulative SG&A Reduction:** Will complete over $1 billion annualized SG&A cost reduction since Q1 2022

### EBITDA
- **Near-Term Direction:** Expect the combination of (1) SG&A reduction, (2) stabilizing weekly unit sales, and (3) reduced inventory to lead to "significantly improved adjusted EBITDA profitability over the next 2 quarters"
- **No Specific Timeline for Breakeven:** "As fast as possible"; no explicit quarter named for EBITDA breakeven

## Long-Term Targets

### Three-Step Plan
- **Step 1 (Current Focus):** Drive business to breakeven adjusted EBITDA
  - Believe achievable at current volume levels
  - Believe can achieve "significantly positive EBITDA at current volumes"
- **Step 2:** Drive business to significant positive unit economics (positive free cash flow)
  - "Breakeven adjusted EBITDA is a milestone, but it is not our goal. Our goal is positive free cash flow."
- **Step 3:** Return to growth
  - Will head into growth as a "much more efficient company" with an "infrastructure advantage that is materially different than the infrastructure advantage we had when we were growing last time"

### Long-Term GPU Target
- **Total GPU Target:** >$4,000 (reiterated from prior quarters)
- **Achievability:** "At current, higher, or lower volume levels"
- **GPU Bridge from Q4 2022 Reported ($2,667) to >$4,000 (management walk):**
  - Retail inventory allowance normalization: +$600/unit
  - Wholesale inventory allowance normalization: +$100/unit
  - Loan sale timing normalization: +~$500/unit
  - Turn time normalization (sub-90-day aged vehicles): +$600/unit
  - Non-vehicle COGS opportunity: +$600 (called out for Q1/Q2 vs. prior achievement levels); additional $300-$400 beyond that
  - Total identified bridge: ~$2,700+ per unit of identified upside from Q4 reported levels

### Long-Term Financial Goal
- **Ultimate Goal:** Generate significant net income and free cash flow
- **Capital Plan:** Plan is to NOT need to raise additional capital, but "subtle changes in the shape of those curves can change the answer quite a bit"
- **Growth Timing:** Not yet known; "not obviously that far away"; currently third step in plan after EBITDA breakeven and positive unit economics

### Long-Term Market Opportunity
- **Market Size:** ~40 million units per year
- **Carvana Market Share:** ~1%
- **Vision:** "The nation's largest and most profitable automotive retailer"

## Special Topics

### Debt / Liquidity / Going Concern Risk
- **Interest Expense Burden:** ~$600 million annually, even at EBITDA breakeven implies significant cash burn
- **Analyst Concern (Rajat Gupta, JPMorgan):** With ~$600 million interest + ~$100 million CapEx, reaching EBITDA breakeven still implies ~$700 million+ annual cash burn; "you also need to add more debt at some stage to continue to just pay the ongoing additional debt"
- **Management Response on Restructuring:** Did not commit to any restructuring; focused on 3-step operating plan; "believed and continue to believe that we have the opportunity to run that plan and to not need to raise additional capital"
- **Capital Options Enumerated (if needed):** (1) ~$2 billion of real estate (high-quality, financeable inspection centers), (2) capacity for more secured debt, (3) capacity for more unsecured debt, (4) equity issuance
- **Analyst Estimate (Michael Montani, Evercore):** 2023 EBITDA loss of $400 million to $600 million; ~$1 billion+ cash burn including interest expense; assumes slight decline in units, low $3,000s total GPU, SG&A improvements as discussed. Management declined to confirm or deny, directing to the shareholder letter.

### Advertising Strategy
- **Direction:** Pulling back "pretty dramatically" on marketing; more to come
- **Channel Mix:** More likely to cut direct marketing channels; will be more careful with brand channels to preserve long-term brand equity
- **Brand Strength:** "Highly likely that our brand is materially stronger than it was a couple of years ago"
- **Testing Approach:** Using markets as "laboratories" to turn on and off different marketing channels; finding room to pull back in most cases due to lower ROI in current environment
- **Share of Voice:** Not specifically quantified but acknowledged as declining

### Vending Machine / Omnichannel Evolution
- **Vending Machine Pickup:** 40% of customers nationwide now picking up at vending machines (despite vending machines only in a subset of markets); roughly double since early 2022
- **Non-Vending Machine Pickup:** Testing pickup at non-vending machine locations
- **Incentivization:** Company IS incentivizing customers to do pickups
- **Customer Experience Impact:** Testing all kinds of opportunities to decrease operational costs while monitoring customer speed and experience; seeing "really strong results"

### Inventory Mix Strategy
- **Approach:** Evaluate customer shopping patterns on site, balance against market supply signals
- **Current Direction:** No particular patterns or shifts in mix worth calling out at this time; focus remains on normalizing total inventory size

### Cost Reduction Progress (Operational Metrics Not Yet Fully in Financials)
- **Management Characterization:** "The progress is really beginning to show up. This will continue to get clearer and to require less explanation over time"
- **Transition Costs:** Shrinking inventory = buying fewer cars = IRC overhead spread over fewer cars = efficiency temporarily suppressed; described as "transitory"
- **Negative Feedback Loops from Shrinking:** Acknowledged: "when we are shrinking, that creates negative feedback loops that we have to be mindful of and make it a little bit harder"
- **Recent Learning (Last 3-4 Months):** "More of our projects are probably having bigger operational gains than we may have hoped for, but they're also pushing in the direction of fewer sales"

# Carvana Co. Q3 2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** November 3, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2022

---

## Q3 2022 Actuals (Reported)

### Retail Units & Revenue
- **Retail Units Sold:** 102,570 (-8% YoY; -12.7% sequentially vs. Q2 2022's 117,564)
- **Total Revenue:** $3.386 billion (-3% YoY)
- **ADESA Revenue Contribution:** $193 million (first full quarter of consolidation)
- **Market Share (YoY):** Gained market share versus Q3 2021 despite headwinds from high used vehicle prices, rising interest rates, and profitability-focused initiatives
- **Market Share (QoQ):** Most likely NOT taking realized sales market share sequentially versus industry as a whole; depends on data source
- **Top-Funnel Demand Market Share:** Likely seeing "somewhat meaningful gains" after adjusting for differential interest rate conversion impacts and profitability-driven pull-backs
- **Cash Customer Sales Growth:** Grew >20% YoY; >30% faster than the rest of the company (used as proxy for underlying demand)
- **Middle of Country vs. Coasts:** Middle of country grew +4% YoY; coasts shrank meaningfully YoY; middle of country outgrew coasts by >10%

### Industry Context
- **Industry Used Sales:** Estimated down ~10% to 15% YoY in Q3
- **Forward-Looking Indicators:** Web searches and carvana.com activity indicate further slowing recently (as of call date)
- **Customer Affordability:** For a customer utilizing financing, monthly payment is up ~160% vs. pre-pandemic levels
- **Retail Price Depreciation:** Retail prices dropped ~10% year-to-date through Q3
- **Interest Rate Impact:** 2-year treasury (benchmark for auto loans) rose 3.9% over last year and 2.6% since 2019; credit spreads rose ~1% over last year
- **Affordability Equivalence:** Combined move in 2-year treasury yields + credit spreads over the past year is equivalent in monthly payment impact to a ~$3,000 price increase
- **Cars at Most Unaffordable Point Ever:** As of end of Q3, for customers using financing, despite ~10% retail price decline

### Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU)
- **Total GPU:** $3,500 (decrease of -$1,172 YoY; increase of +$132 sequentially vs. Q2's $3,368)
- **Total GPU (ex-D&A and Gift SBC):** $3,870 (increase of +$221 sequentially)
- **Retail GPU:** $1,131 (flat sequentially vs. Q2's $1,131)
- **Retail GPU (ex-D&A and Gift SBC):** $1,267 (vs. Q2's $1,276; decrease of -$9 sequentially)
- **Wholesale GPU:** $448 (sequential increase of +$65 vs. Q2's $383)
- **Wholesale GPU (ex-D&A):** $682 (sequential increase of +$163 vs. Q2's $519)
- **Other GPU (Finance + Ancillary):** $1,921 (sequential increase of +$67 vs. Q2's $1,854)

### GPU Reporting Change
- **New Presentation:** Now reporting Total GPU, Retail GPU, and Wholesale GPU both including and excluding D&A and Gift SBC
- **Rationale:** Historically Carvana reported GPU including D&A; ADESA historically reported gross profit excluding D&A; now providing both for clarity
- **Supplemental Tables:** Posted supplemental financial tables for Q3 to assist investors with ADESA consolidation moving pieces; do NOT intend to provide these going forward

### Retail GPU Sequential Drivers (Q3 vs. Q2)
- **Positive:** Higher spreads between retail prices and acquisition prices
- **Positive:** Lower retail cost of sales
- **Negative:** Higher retail depreciation rates in Q3 vs. Q2
- **Net Impact:** Roughly flat retail GPU sequentially

### Wholesale GPU Sequential Drivers (Q3 vs. Q2)
- **Primary Driver:** Consolidating a full quarter of ADESA in Q3 (vs. partial quarter in Q2)

### Other GPU Sequential Drivers (Q3 vs. Q2)
- **Positive:** Higher origination rates relative to benchmark interest rates
- **Positive:** Improved securitization credit spreads relative to Q2
- **Negative:** Lower ancillary product attachment rates

## Headwinds & Risk Factors

### Three Key Headwinds Identified by Management
1. **Industry-Level Demand:** Used sales down ~10-15% YoY in Q3; forward indicators showing further slowing; planning for "next year to be a difficult one"
2. **Interest Rate Increases:** 2-year treasury up 3.9% over past year; credit spreads up ~1%; combined impact equivalent to ~$3,000 price increase for financed customers
3. **Vehicle Price Depreciation:** Near-term negative as faster depreciation rates create margin risk; medium-term positive as it restores affordability; recent wholesale-retail spread widening approximately offsetting depreciation but volatile

### Near-Term Planning Assumptions
- **Industry Outlook:** "Building our plans around assumptions that the next year is a difficult one in our industry and in the economy as a whole"
- **Next 6-12 Months:** "Certainly more uncertain than they have been in recent periods"
- **Consumer Sentiment:** "Sentiment broadly is decreasing" across consumer confidence and willingness to buy a car metrics

### Positive Signals / Potential Recovery Indicators
- **Forward Rate Curve:** Suggests majority of interest rate increases are behind us
- **Historical Resilience of Used Cars:** Current depressed sales levels are similar to periods of fairly severe economic difficulty in the past; potentially less medium-term downside than other categories
- **Relative Affordability:** Cars depreciating while other goods inflating -- potentially moving in "good direction" on relative value basis
- **Historical Recovery Timing:** In 2008-2009, used car market bottomed ~6 months before stock market and roughly corresponded with when the Fed started cutting rates

## Q&A Key Data Points (Incremental)

- **Hertz Partnership Update:** "Continues to go very well"; putting even more focus and attention on it; alignment between the two groups; ADESA has led to additional opportunities; "excited about the results there"
- **Third-Party Listings:** Analyst referenced potential pull-back or pause; management did not directly confirm or deny, instead discussed framework for prioritizing more profitable sales channels
- **IRC Closures / Market Exits:** Analyst suggested closing IRCs and pulling out of low-volume markets given 1.2 million unit capacity vs. ~400,000 run rate; management declined to comment specifically but emphasized $90 million QoQ expense reduction demonstrates willingness to take actions
- **Retail GPU Stability Framework:** Management expressed high confidence in historical stability of retail margins across cycles due to dealers having similar cost structures, profit goals, no variation in input costs, and inability to sustain losses over time; expects this pattern to continue but warned of near-term volatility
- **Finance GPU Less Stable:** Fewer players, more variation in input costs, larger cash cushions allowing different strategies; "more risk that could move around in more abnormal ways relative to history"
- **Interest Rate Dynamics:** Finance companies passing on cost of funds more in line with fed funds than 2-year treasury; once treasury correctly predicts fed funds over next 2 years, gap should collapse; would be helpful for both finance GPU and retail volumes
- **Wholesale-Retail Spread Pattern:** Over last 2 quarters, depreciation rates increased and acquisition spreads widened "approximately offsetting"; recently both spreads and depreciation rates moved up further
- **Revenue Optimization Levers:** Setting price, setting interest rates, setting bids on cars bought from customers; "probably a little bit of room" to adjust in each area to offset rising costs
- **Expense vs. Unit Independence:** At current overcapacity, expenses and unit volumes moving "somewhat independently" within reasonable unit ranges; had units been higher, could have made "a material difference" to per-unit results

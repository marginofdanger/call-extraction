# Carvana Co. Q2 2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 19, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2023

---

## Q2 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Retail Units & Revenue
- **Retail Units Sold:** 76,530 (-35% YoY; -3% sequentially vs. Q1 2023's 79,240)
- **Sequential Decline Drivers:** (1) Reduced inventory size, (2) reduced advertising, (3) increased benchmark interest rates and credit spreads, (4) continued focus on executing profitability initiatives
- **Total Revenue:** $2.968 billion (-24% YoY; +14% sequentially)

### Industry / Macro Context
- **Used Car Market:** Total used car sales approximately 10% below baseline levels
- **Independent Dealer Market:** Likely impacted more than franchise dealers over last 1.5 years (i.e., Carvana's direct market impacted more than the 10% overall decline)
- **Depreciation Trends:** Depreciation clearly elevated; looks very similar to prior year but started approximately one month earlier; slightly more severe than prior year so far
- **Depreciation Character:** Happening in orderly fashion; wholesale market positioned to absorb depreciation and pass through to retail market; not yet impacting retail spreads meaningfully
- **Best Case Scenario (per management):** Significant depreciation that is foreseen, enabling wholesale market to organize and buy at sufficient margins to absorb depreciation, while improving customer affordability
- **Affordability:** Function of both elevated car prices and elevated interest rates; most consumers buy with financing; monthly payment = f(car price, rates); signs of improvement on car price side
- **Consumer Dynamic:** Elevated prices + elevated interest rates = more difficult affordability for customers

### Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU)
- **Non-GAAP Total GPU:** $7,030 (+$2,234 sequentially vs. Q1 2023's $4,796) -- **company all-time record**
- **Non-GAAP Retail GPU:** $2,862 (vs. Q1's $1,591; +$1,271 sequentially)
- **Non-GAAP Wholesale GPU:** $1,228 (vs. Q1's $1,236; -$8 sequentially; approximately flat)
- **Non-GAAP Other GPU:** $2,940 (vs. Q1's $1,969; +$971 sequentially)

### GPU Nonrecurring Items & Adjustments (Q2)
- **Total Nonrecurring Benefits to GPU:** ~$900 per unit
- **Total Nonrecurring Benefits to Adjusted EBITDA:** ~$70 million
- **Retail Inventory Allowance Adjustment:** ~$250 per unit benefit to retail GPU (~$20 million benefit to adjusted EBITDA)
- **Excess Loan Sales (Other GPU):** Sold more receivables than originated; greater volume of loans sold in Q2 vs. Q1; estimated ~$650 per unit benefit to other GPU from higher-than-normalized loan volume held and sold (~$50 million benefit to adjusted EBITDA)
- **Implied Underlying Total GPU (ex-nonrecurring):** ~$6,130 ($7,030 - $900)
- **Management Commentary on Underlying GPU:** "Still $6,000, which is a number that I think was unimaginable for the vast majority of our history and completely unimaginable from the perspective of even 3 months ago"

### Retail GPU Sequential Drivers (Q2 vs. Q1)
- **Positive:** ~$250 retail inventory allowance adjustment benefit
- **Positive:** Lower average days of sale (normalized inventory now flowing through)
- **Positive:** Lower retail market depreciation rates
- **Positive:** Wider spreads between wholesale and retail market prices
- **Positive:** Lower reconditioning and inbound transport costs
- **Positive:** Higher customer sourcing rate vs. FY 2021
- **Positive:** Higher revenue from additional services vs. FY 2021
- **Seasonal Tailwind:** Q2 is on average the strongest quarter of the year for retail GPU
- **Market Tailwind:** Period of high wholesale-to-retail spreads followed by lower-than-expected retail depreciation
- **Market Tailwind:** Some unexpected appreciation early in year that was not in plan

### Wholesale GPU Sequential Drivers (Q2 vs. Q1)
- **Negative:** Higher wholesale market depreciation rates in Q2 vs. Q1 (negatively impacted wholesale vehicle gross profit per wholesale unit sold)
- **Positive (Offset):** Operational improvements allowing higher wholesale units sold volume
- **Net:** Approximately flat quarter-over-quarter ($1,228 vs. $1,236)

### Other GPU Sequential Drivers (Q2 vs. Q1)
- **Positive:** Greater volume of loans sold in Q2 compared to Q1
- **Positive:** Estimated ~$650 benefit from higher-than-normalized volume of loans held and sold
- **Positive:** Higher origination interest rates relative to benchmark interest rates
- **Positive:** Higher average loan size

### Finance Receivables Balance
- **March 31, 2023:** ~$1.6 billion on balance sheet (built up over Q4 2022 and Q1 2023)
- **June 30, 2023:** ~$1.1 billion (down $500 million sequentially)
- **Normalized Level Context:** $1.1 billion is still higher than normalized; additional loans available to sell over coming quarters yielding additional nonrecurring revenue in GPU

### SG&A
- **Non-GAAP SG&A Reduction (Q2 vs. Q1):** -$21 million sequentially
- **Primary Driver of Q2 Reduction:** Continued reductions in non-advertising expenses
- **Advertising Expenses (Q2 vs. Q1):** Approximately flat quarter-over-quarter
- **Advertising Low Point:** Q1 2023 expected to be the near-term low point for quarterly advertising expense; company seeks to optimize spend to balance unit volume and profitability
- **Other SG&A (Q2 vs. Q1):** Basically flat quarter-over-quarter
- **Cost Reduction Sustainability:** Management believes cost reductions achieved over the past year are long-lasting and sustainable; believes further efficiencies remain in all areas
- **Annualized Cost Reduction Achieved:** $1.1 billion over the last year

### SG&A Per Unit Reduction Framework (3 Drivers)
1. **Step 2 Operations Efficiency (Per Unit Basis):** Opportunities to reduce operations expenses per retail unit sold by completing pipeline of projects to automate manual work, optimize staffing and routing, increase deep funnel conversion, among other initiatives
2. **Step 2 Corporate/Technology/Facilities (Absolute Dollar Basis):** Opportunities to reduce and optimize corporate technology and facilities expenses through continued focus on zero-based budgeting and other efficiency gains
3. **Step 3 Volume Leverage (Per Unit Basis):** Significant opportunity to leverage corporate technology and facilities expense base at higher volumes, leading to significant per-unit SG&A leverage

### EBITDA & Margins
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** +$155 million, or +5.2% of revenue -- **company all-time record; first positive adjusted EBITDA quarter; completes Step 1 of 3-step plan**
- **Adjusted EBITDA (ex-nonrecurring):** ~$85 million ($155 million - $70 million nonrecurring items)
- **Sequential Improvement:** From -$24 million in Q1 (i.e., +$179 million improvement)

### Inventory
- **Inventory Down YoY:** >55% (over 50%, "something in the over 55%")
- **Inventory Down Sequentially:** Down again quarter-over-quarter
- **Implied Turn Time (inventory/daily sales):** ~62 days (end-of-Q2 inventory relative to Q2 retail units sold)
- **Turn Time Context:** ~62 days is in a normal range that Carvana has operated in historically

### Inspection Center Utilization
- **Current Utilization:** Approximately 25% capacity, maybe slightly more
- **Cost Performance:** Reconditioning costs in inspection centers are below full year 2021 levels despite operating at only ~25% capacity
- **Reconditioning Cost Per Retail Unit Sold:** Pushed down over last year; below FY 2021 levels
- **Reconditioning Gains:** Process-driven and technology-driven, not quality-driven (enhanced inventory management system, in-sourcing of previously outsourced services, rightsized staffing)

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity (as of June 30, 2023)
- **Total Liquidity Resources:** ~$3.5 billion
- **Cash and Revolving Availability:** ~$1.5 billion (mix of just over $500 million cash + just under $1 billion revolving availability)
- **Unpledged Real Estate and Other Assets:** ~$2.0 billion, including >$1 billion of real estate acquired with ADESA
- **Committed Liquidity Resources:** Stepped up quarter-over-quarter (increased liquidity in Q2)

### Customer Sourcing
- **Customer Sourcing Rate:** Higher share of retail units sourced from customers vs. FY 2021 (continuing trend)
- **Platform Advantage:** Transactional website with large traffic; accurate valuations; convenient process (online value, schedule pickup, Carvana picks up)
- **Enhancements:** Built improvements to differentiate vehicle value better and pass some benefits to customers
- **Disposition Channels:** Cars planned for retail go to inspection centers; cars planned for wholesale go to ADESA

### ADESA
- **Performance:** Great quarter; on a great path; flowing through to GPU
- **Dealer Business Market Share:** Improved over last many months
- **Commercial Business Market Share:** Improved over last many months
- **Market Recovery Signs:** More normal activity from rental fleets; starting to get a sense off-lease volume may come back in not-too-distant future (not quite there yet)
- **Infrastructure Value:** Reduces capital investments needed for future growth; supports logistics network

## Long-Term Targets

### Three-Step Plan (Updated Status)
- **Step 1 (COMPLETED Q2 2023):** Drive business to positive adjusted EBITDA -- **COMPLETED; $155 million adjusted EBITDA in Q2**
- **Step 2 (NOW IN PROGRESS):** Drive business to repeated and significant positive unit economics
  - "Maintaining the exact same operating cadence" as Step 1
  - Rolling out similar internal efficiency program over next year (prior year's program targeting Q2 was "incredibly successful")
  - Holding units approximately flat and focusing on efficiencies
  - Significant gains yet to be had
- **Step 3 (FUTURE):** Return to growth
  - Not yet making this pivot; "in due time, we certainly plan to"
  - Multiple factors will drive transition: internal operational progress, capital structure improvements
  - Growth pivot expected to be relatively comfortable given history and infrastructure (ADESA)
  - When growth restarts: fixed cost leverage expected to outweigh incremental variable cost increases
  - Capital investments (larger/more inspection centers, logistics pathways) already largely built; ADESA reduces future CapEx needs

### Long-Term Financial Goal (Reiterated)
- **Ultimate Goal:** Generate significant GAAP net income and free cash flow
- **Key Non-GAAP Metrics (Management Focus):** Non-GAAP gross profit, non-GAAP SG&A expense, adjusted EBITDA

### Long-Term Market Opportunity (Reiterated)
- **Vision:** Largest and most profitable automotive retailer
- **Scale Ambition:** Selling millions of cars per year
- **Historical Growth Capability:** ~100% per year for first 8 years in a flat market

### Growth Cost Framework
- **Variable Cost Position:** In a great spot; all-time best efficiencies
- **Fixed Cost Leverage:** Disproportionately larger share of expenses are now fixed vs. history; growth math is beneficial (fixed costs leveraged by volume)
- **Management View:** Growth cost increases "will probably be helpful from an expense perspective because the fixed component is probably a larger and more mechanical component than the variable component"
- **Growth Investments:** Some incremental costs inevitable, but expected to be "dwarfed by the improvements in the business otherwise due to the leveraging of fixed costs"

## Debt Restructuring / Transaction Support Agreement

### Transaction Overview (Announced July 19, 2023)
- **Agreement Reached With:** >90% of holders of senior unsecured notes
- **Exchange:** ~$5.2 billion of senior unsecured notes exchanged for new senior secured notes
- **New Note Maturities:** December 2028 through June 2031
- **Total Debt Reduction:** >$1.2 billion face value reduction
- **Cash Interest Expense Reduction:** >$430 million per year savings over the next 2 years
- **Near-Term Maturity Elimination:** >83% of 2025 and 2027 senior unsecured note maturities extended
- **First Meaningful Note Maturity Post-Exchange:** December 2028

### Mechanism
- **Majority of Debt Reduction:** Exchange of collateral (pledging ADESA real estate and other assets) for reduction in face value of debt; NOT primarily a cash paydown
- **Cash Consideration Component:** Funded by $350 million equity raise
- **Remainder of Exchange:** Straight swap of existing senior unsecured notes for new tranches of senior secured notes

### Equity Component
- **Total Equity Raise Commitment:** $350 million (part of transaction support agreement)
- **Garcia Family Commitment:** ~$125 million
- **Market Raise:** ~$225 million
- **ATM Program:** Larger ATM facility also established (characterized as "good corporate housekeeping")

### New Note Features
- **PIK (Pay-in-Kind) Interest Option:** Option to pay PIK interest for 2 years (reduces near-term cash interest burden)
- **2028 Notes Call Feature:** 1-year no-call period only; callable at par + half a coupon after 1 year
- **2030 Notes Call Feature:** 2-year no-call period (shorter than typical); callable at par + half a coupon after 2 years
- **Equity Prepayment Provision:** 2028 and 2030 notes allow prepayment with proceeds from equity raises
- **Deleveraging Optionality:** Multiple features facilitate decreasing leverage over time if desired
- **Garcia Family Commitment Adjustment:** If more equity raised over time via ATM, family commitment decreases proportionally

### Management Commentary on Transaction
- Characterized as "win-win" for Carvana and senior unsecured noteholders
- Business performance in 2023 created room for mutually beneficial deal
- Bondholder group described as "very productive to work with" and "solution oriented"
- Preserving collateral for years in unsecured structure now being deployed for debt reduction / better terms
- Multi-dimensional improvement: lower leverage, longer maturities, more prepayment flexibility, lower cash interest expense

## Q&A Supplemental Data

### Market Share / Competitive Positioning
- **Carvana Market Share:** ~1% of ~40 million unit/year market (reiterated from prior quarters)
- **Business Replication Difficulty:** Emphasized as capital intensive, complicated, hard to replicate
- **Competitive Moat:** Customer offering quality, business scalability, difficulty of replication

### Reconditioning Cost Detail (from Q&A)
- **Reconditioning Cost Per Retail Unit Sold:** Below FY 2021 levels
- **Utilization Context:** Inspection centers operating at ~25% capacity, yet costs still below FY 2021 levels
- **Gains Source:** Process and technology driven (enhanced inventory management, in-sourcing, rightsized staffing); explicitly NOT from reducing quality of reconditioning

### Inbound Transport / Logistics (from Q&A)
- **Inbound Transport Costs:** Improving; fulfillment teams making logistics network more efficient
- **Further Opportunities:** Continue to see opportunities in reconditioning and inbound transport despite significant progress already made

### Return to Growth Timing (from Q&A)
- **Not Yet Pivoting:** Company explicitly not yet making the pivot to growth
- **Spreadsheet Math:** "If you put it in a spreadsheet, it's going to tell you that we're supposed to grow as quickly as possible" given GPU and variable cost position
- **Discipline:** Maintaining Step 2 focus despite favorable unit economics math; benefiting from simplicity of holding units flat
- **Growth Readiness:** Well positioned for growth when time comes; ADESA significantly reduces capital investment needed

### Affordability / Market Recovery (from Q&A)
- **Used Car Sales vs. Baseline:** Approximately 10% below normal
- **Independent Market Impact:** Likely impacted more than franchise market
- **Recovery Catalyst:** Car prices normalizing + interest rates normalizing = improved affordability
- **Management Stance:** "Most importantly, when it's time to grow, we'll take control of that, and we'll control our own destiny"

### FCF / Liquidity Outlook (from Q&A)
- **PIK Feature Impact:** Could potentially generate reported positive FCF depending on EBITDA levels over next year (since PIK eliminates cash interest payments for 2 years)
- **Post-Exchange Liquidity:** Strong position with $1.5 billion cash/revolving availability + $2 billion unpledged assets
- **Liquidity Trend:** Committed liquidity resources actually increased quarter-over-quarter in Q2

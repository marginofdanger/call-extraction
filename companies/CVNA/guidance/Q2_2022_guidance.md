# Carvana Co. Q2 2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** August 4, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2022

---

## Q2 2022 Actuals (Reported)

### Retail Units & Revenue
- **Retail Units Sold:** 117,564 (+9% YoY; +10% sequentially vs. Q1 2022's 105,185)
- **Total Revenue:** $3.884 billion (+16% YoY)
- **ADESA Revenue Contribution:** $108 million (ADESA wholesale marketplace; acquisition closed May 9)
- **Market Share:** Continued to gain significant market share; grew units +9% YoY while industry was shrinking ~15%
- **July Demand Note:** Another industry-wide reduction in demand levels in July, impacting Carvana as well

### Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU)
- **Total GPU:** $3,368 (decrease of -$1,752 YoY; increase of +$535 sequentially vs. Q1's $2,833)
- **Retail GPU:** $1,131 (sequential increase of +$323 vs. Q1's $808)
- **Retail GPU (ex-items):** $1,216 (excluding $51/unit Ernie milestone gift and $34/unit May RIF impact; vs. adjusted Q1 of $884)
- **Wholesale GPU:** $383 (sequential increase of +$164 vs. Q1's $219)
- **ADESA Impact on Wholesale GPU:** +$43/unit, net of $128 of depreciation and amortization expense
- **Other GPU (Finance + Ancillary):** $1,854 (sequential increase of +$48 vs. Q1's $1,806)
- **GPU Bridge Target:** $4,500+ referenced as bridge-back target level (from shareholder letter); execution is the primary gap

### Retail GPU Sequential Drivers (Q2 vs. Q1)
- **Shipping Revenue Recovery:** ~$100/unit recovery (Q1 had lost ~$100/unit from refunds due to logistics delays; recovered in Q2)
- **Acquisition Price Mix Improvement:** Positive impact from selling fewer cars purchased during Q4 2021 (a very high-cost purchasing period)
- **Spread Widening:** Higher spreads between retail sales prices and acquisition prices
- **Reconditioning & Inbound Transport:** Similar in Q2 and Q1; still elevated; vehicles sold in Q2 were primarily reconditioned before May cost efficiency initiatives began

### Other GPU Drivers
- **Sequential Drivers:** Higher customer rates relative to benchmark interest rates, partially offset by wider credit spreads and a change in loan sales channel mix
- **Interest Rate Pass-Through:** Increases in benchmarks and risk spreads have not been fully passed through to consumers yet; industry finance companies have been raising rates ~25-50 bps/month starting March/April
- **Loan Sales Channel:** Leaned more toward whole loan sales (Ally) vs. securitization in Q2 to reduce volatility

### One-Time Items Impacting Q2
- **Ernie's 1 Million Unit Milestone Gift:** -$51/unit impact on Retail GPU
- **May Reduction in Force (RIF):** -$34/unit impact on Retail GPU
- **ADESA Onetime Expenses:** $3 million of onetime expenses included in ADESA EBITDA contribution

### EBITDA & Margins
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** -6.2% (improvement of +4.0 percentage points sequentially vs. Q1's -10.2%)
- **ADESA Impact on Adjusted EBITDA:** -$2 million (inclusive of $3 million onetime expenses and reallocation of $2 million of gross profit from Carvana business internalized post-acquisition)
- **EBITDA Excludes:** Ernie's gift to employees, other income/expense (primarily fair value changes in securities)
- **EBITDA Includes:** Non-gift share-based compensation and May RIF expenses

### SG&A
- **Cash SG&A Per Unit (ex-D&A, SBC, ADESA):** $5,400 (sequential decline of -$850 vs. Q1; sequential decline of -$942 ex-D&A, SBC, ADESA)
- **Total SG&A Dollars:** Declined -5% sequentially while units grew +10% sequentially
- **Payroll Decline (Q2 vs. Q1):** ~$20 million sequential reduction
- **Advertising Decline (Q2 vs. Q1):** ~$25 million sequential reduction (~15% decrease in marketing budget QoQ)
- **Other SG&A:** Also declined sequentially
- **Logistics SG&A:** Stepped up in Q2 vs. Q1 due to third-party transport services used to clear logistics backlogs; not expected to recur at same level in Q3

### Inventory & Working Capital
- **Inventory Balance:** Reduced meaningfully QoQ (does not include ADESA, which has minimal inventory)
- **Inventory Target Range:** $2.0 billion to $2.5 billion; above target at end of Q2, expected to continue lowering in Q3
- **Visible Inventory:** ~20% fewer cars visible to customers vs. recent peaks (purposeful inventory management)
- **Unit Inventory Decline:** Approximately in line with balance decline (units not specifically disclosed)

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity (as of June 30, 2022)
- **Total Liquidity Resources:** ~$4.7 billion
- **Cash and Revolving Availability:** $2.7 billion
- **Unpledged Real Estate and Other Assets:** $2.1 billion (including ~$1 billion of real estate acquired with ADESA)
- **Ally Forward Flow Capacity Remaining:** $3.2 billion (agreement upsized in March)

### IRC / Production Capacity
- **Annual IRC Capacity at Full Utilization:** ~1.2 million units (as of end of Q2)

## Long-Term Targets

### Long-Term Financial Model
- **Goal:** Largest and most profitable automotive retailer
- **Volume Vision:** Millions of sales per year; "execution is all that separates us from millions of sales per year"
- **GPU Bridge:** Path back to 2021 levels ($4,500+) described as "straightforward"; from there, opportunity remains
- **SG&A Long-Term:** "Biggest opportunity"; midterm goal of $3,000/unit; long-term model targets below that
- **Cohort Evidence:** Historical SG&A performance in most mature cohorts used as proof points for achievability

### ADESA Long-Term Vision
- **IRC Network Scale:** Expect ADESA to dramatically increase the scale and customer proximity of inspection center network
- **Logistics Simplification:** Expect to strengthen and simplify logistics capabilities
- **Cost Savings / Revenue Opportunities:** Expect to find savings and revenue opportunities not possible without combined capabilities
- **Reconditioning Closer to Customers:** Multi-year plan to recondition more cars at ADESA sites closer to customers
- **Auction Volume Recovery:** Referenced 2008 parallel -- after the GFC, volume took ~5 years to fully recover; current recovery could potentially be faster because fundamental disruption was less severe even though technical impact on auction volumes was more severe

## Operating Priority Shift

### Strategic Pivot (First Time in Company History)
- **New #1 Priority:** Driving efficiency and cash flow (shifted from growth for first time ever)
- **Timing:** Biggest changes began in May 2022
- **Scope:** Prioritization shifts across every group in the business; new processes created; concrete goals in every group
- **Internal Cadence:** Monday -- every group meets and reports progress against expectations; Tuesday -- all operational groups compare performance via internal benchmarking; rest of week -- execution
- **Marketing Budget:** Reduced ~15% QoQ (from elevated Q1 levels)
- **Growth Levers:** Have meaningful latent demand and several levers to drive growth; speed of pulling those levers tied to progress on higher-priority efficiency goals

## Industry & Macro Commentary

### Used Vehicle Market
- **Industry Sales Trend:** Market shrinking ~15% YoY in Q2; another industry-wide reduction in demand levels in July
- **Used Vehicle Prices:** Inflated more than other goods/services; depreciation returning to the market in 2022
- **Expected Price Path:** Likely to depreciate faster going forward to realign with other goods/services
- **Spread Dynamics:** Historically, when depreciation increases, spread between wholesale and retail widens (dealers price in expected depreciation); early evidence of this pattern playing out currently; could support stable retail GPU even as prices decline
- **Auto as Economic Indicator:** Large-ticket, financed, discretionary purchase that historically leads the economy; auto industry has already taken deeper stress than rest of economy, implying additional stress may be less and recovery may be more than expected

### Consumer Trends
- **Higher Income / Higher FICO:** Faring somewhat better in this environment
- **Higher ASPs:** Higher income customers driving higher purchase prices
- **Credit Performance:** Most finance companies seeing strong performance; slow drift back toward normalized 2019 levels from exceptional 2020-2021; nothing too notable happening yet
- **Affordable Cars:** Dearth of lower-priced cars industry-wide; demand shifted to cheaper cars less than expected due to relative strength of higher-income consumers

### Interest Rate Environment
- **Rate Pass-Through:** Sum of benchmark rate and risk spread increases has NOT been fully passed to consumers
- **Industry Behavior:** Larger banks/institutions began raising rates ~March/April; raising ~25-50 bps/month
- **Sticky Rates:** Finance companies (including Carvana) pass through rate increases more slowly than they appear
- **Securitization vs. Whole Loan Sales:** Securitization typically offers better monetization but more variability; whole loan sales offer stability; currently leaning toward whole loan sales given market volatility

### Registration / Title Issues
- **Current Status:** No states where Carvana is unable to sell vehicles; no clean title issues
- **Delayed Plates Rate:** ~1/3 of the rate from a year ago; "best levels we've ever been in our company history"
- **Industry Comparison:** In majority of states, performing better than majority of dealers
- **Illinois Situation:** Was temporarily shut down; judge granted time to work with state; 6,000 customer petition signatures in 48 hours supporting Carvana
- **Time Buffers:** Added time buffers in certain states to ensure clean registration experience; extends visible delivery time to customers, which impacts conversion; expected to be pulled back over time

## Q&A Key Data Points (Incremental)

- **Third-Party Reconditioning Phase-Out:** Moving away from third-party reconditioning (longer cycle times); first-party recon has shorter cycle times; cost initiatives are also speed initiatives
- **Inventory Days Improvement Levers:** (1) Faster time from acquisition to IRC, (2) Faster inspection-to-fully-reconditioned time, (3) Shift from third-party to first-party recon
- **Comp & Benefits Efficiency Metrics:** Hours per delivery coming down; customer care phone time per sale coming down; logistics network utilization going up
- **ADESA Volume Upside Indicators:** OEMs starting to increase production; car prices dipping slightly (making franchise dealers less likely to retain every off-lease car); OEMs selling more cars to rental companies (rental companies are big auction sellers); room for finance companies to start selling more cars
- **Growth Algorithm Post-$4,000 SG&A:** Lower SG&A per unit means less work per sale, enabling more growth from the same effort; efficiency gains expected to be positive for growth in the medium term
- **$3,000/Unit Midterm SG&A Goal:** Bottoms-up models built across every group; concrete projects mapped to achieve it; now converting from models into actual products and projects

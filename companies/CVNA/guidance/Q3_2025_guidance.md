## Q3 2025 Actuals (Reported)

### Volume & Revenue
- **Retail Units Sold:** 155,941 — increase of **+44% YoY** — new company record
- **Revenue:** $5.647 billion — increase of **+55% YoY** — new company record
- **Annual Revenue Run Rate:** exceeded $20 billion (milestone noted; exact figure not stated beyond ">$20 billion")

### Gross Profit per Unit (GPU) — Non-GAAP
- **Retail GPU:** decreased **-$77** YoY (driven by higher retail depreciation rates)
- **Wholesale GPU:** decreased **-$168** YoY (driven by higher wholesale depreciation rates; retail unit growth outpaced ADESA marketplace growth)
- **Other GPU:** increased **+$63** YoY (driven by improvements in cost of funds, higher finance and VSC attach rates; partially offset by higher-than-normalized loan sales relative to originations in Q3 2024)
- **Other GPU (absolute):** record-high level — described as "highest level ever"

### SG&A — Non-GAAP
- **Total Non-GAAP SG&A per Retail Unit:** decreased **-$319** YoY
  - **Carvana Operations portion:** decreased **-$96** per retail unit sold YoY (driven by operational efficiency initiatives)
  - **Overhead portion:** decreased **-$314** per retail unit sold YoY (leverage of overhead with greater retail unit volume)
  - **Advertising expense:** increased **+$139** per retail unit sold YoY (investment in brand awareness)

### Profitability
- **Net Income:** $263 million — increase of **+$115 million** YoY
- **Net Income Margin:** 4.7% — increase from 4.0% YoY (+70 bps)
- **GAAP Operating Income:** $552 million — increase of **+$215 million** YoY — new company record
- **GAAP Operating Margin:** 9.8% — increase from 9.2% YoY (+60 bps)
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $637 million — increase of **+$208 million** YoY — new company record
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** 11.3% — decrease from 11.7% YoY (-40 bps)
- **Adjusted EBITDA-to-GAAP Operating Income Conversion:** ~87% — increase from 79% YoY

### Balance Sheet & Debt
- **Retired in Q3:** remaining $559 million of 2028 senior secured notes (funded primarily via $539 million equity issuance through ATM program)
- **Post-quarter:** retired $98 million of 2025 senior unsecured notes due October 2025
- **Total corporate debt retired (2024 + 2025 combined):** $1.2 billion
- **Cash on Balance Sheet:** more than $2.1 billion
- **Net Debt / Trailing 12-Month Adjusted EBITDA:** 1.5x — described as "strongest financial position ever"

### Loan Sale Partnerships (announced in October, post-Q3)
- **Ally agreement:** upsized from $4 billion through April 2026 → **$6 billion through October 2027** (+$2 billion increase)
- **New partner agreement #1:** up to $4 billion of loan purchases through October 2027
- **New partner agreement #2:** up to $4 billion of loan purchases through December 2027
- **Total new loan sale capacity announced:** up to $14 billion of future loan principal across expanded/new agreements

### Operational Metrics
- **Inventory Selection:** customers have nearly **50% more cars** to choose from YoY; inventory turn time approximately flat YoY
- **Same/Next-Day Delivery — Phoenix test market:** 40% of customers — vs. **~10% nationwide**
- **Same-day available inventory in Phoenix:** ~2,500 cars on any given day
- **Self-service customers (no advocate interaction until delivery/pickup):** more than **30%** of retail customers
- **Self-service customers selling to Carvana:** more than **60%**
- **Reconditioning capacity additions:** 15 ADESA locations added in last 18 months
- **Customer delivery time improvement:** reduced by **1 day** in the last 5 quarters
- **ADESA Clear (digital auction) additions:** added to 12 inspection centers

### Site Network (as of Q3 2025)
- **Total sites:** 74
  - **Wholesale-only sites:** 41 (original 56 ADESA sites minus 15 integration sites)
  - **Retail-only sites:** 6 (18 original inspection centers minus 12 that added ADESA Clear)
  - **Both retail + wholesale capable sites:** 27 (12 inspection centers with ADESA Clear + 15 ADESA sites with reconditioning)

### Competitive Context (qualitative, with quantitative references)
- **Profit margin vs. industry average:** described as "more than 2x the industry average"
- **Retail growth rate:** 44% vs. other public retailers "approximately flat"
- **Carvana share of used car market:** implied small (98.5% of used cars sold by traditional retailers; 99% of all cars)

- **Retail Units Sold:** above **150,000** (note: Q3 actual was 155,941; this guidance implies potential sequential decline; management cited normal industry seasonality variability)
- **Adjusted EBITDA (Full Year 2025):** at or above the **high end** of the previously communicated range of $2.0 billion–$2.2 billion (see Full Year section; this is the Q4 implicit guide)

### GPU Guidance for Q4 2025
- **Retail GPU:** sequential change from Q3 2025 expected to be in a **similar range to Q4 2024 vs. Q3 2024** (driven by typical Q4 seasonality: higher depreciation rates, lower demand)
- **Wholesale GPU:** sequential change from Q3 2025 expected to be in a **similar range to Q4 2024 vs. Q3 2024** (higher wholesale depreciation rates, lower auction volumes in Q4)

### SG&A Guidance for Q4 2025
- **Advertising Expense:** Q4 expected to be **similar to or slightly higher** than Q3 2025 on a dollar basis

### Qualitative Conditions
- All Q4 guidance is conditional on the environment remaining stable

## Special Topics

### Loan Portfolio Health
- 2024 and 2025 loan originations performing "extremely well" in absolute terms and relative to industry comparables
- Credit was tightened in late 2023 and maintained through Q3 2025
- 2022 and 2023 industry-wide cohorts cited as underperforming initial expectations (industry-wide, not unique to Carvana)
- No incremental reserve needs signaled

### Tariff / Depreciation Dynamics
- Q2 2025: stronger-than-normal retail depreciation — attributed to late March auto tariff announcements
- Q3 2025: softer-than-normal retail depreciation — attributed as near-offset to Q2 strength
- These dynamics are described as partially explaining the YoY retail GPU movement in Q3

### EV Demand / Tax Credit Expiration
- Reduction in EV purchases observed following expiration of federal used EV tax credit
- Described as a shift in vehicle preference, not a change in aggregate demand
- Impact characterized as "basically not something that you really see" in the numbers

### Same-Day Delivery (Phoenix Test Market)
- 40% of Phoenix customers receiving same or next-day delivery vs. ~10% nationwide
- ~2,500 cars available for same-day delivery in Phoenix on any given day
- Investment characterized primarily as technology and process investment, not significant incremental cost
- Some incremental staffing cost (described as "not a very large dollar amount in the grand scheme of things")
- Rollout path: nail Phoenix → expand to other inventory pools near large population centers → broader rollout

### ADESA Integration / Network Expansion
- 15 ADESA sites now have reconditioning (retail) capabilities added (last 18 months)
- 12 inspection centers now have ADESA Clear (wholesale) capability added
- 27 sites now dual-capable (retail + wholesale)
- Future: continued addition of integration sites expected to expand dual-capability footprint

# Carvana Co. Q1 2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** May 4, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q1 2023

---

## Q1 2023 Actuals (Reported)

### Retail Units & Revenue
- **Retail Units Sold:** 79,240 (-25% YoY; -9% sequentially vs. Q4 2022's 86,977)
- **Sequential Decline Drivers:** (1) Reduced inventory size, (2) reduced advertising, (3) increased benchmark interest rates and credit spreads, (4) continued focus on executing profitability initiatives
- **Total Revenue:** $2.6 billion (-25% YoY; -8% sequentially)
- **Market Share:** ~1% of ~40 million unit/year market (down from slightly above 1% six to twelve months ago; decline approximately in line with overall market decline)

### Industry / Macro Context
- **Car Prices (through most of 2022):** Depreciation offset by increasing interest rates
- **Early 2023:** Volatility in interest rates; car prices started going back up
- **Recent Trends (as of call date):** Wholesale prices declining for several weeks; retail prices still barely appreciating but expected to start depreciating shortly
- **Consumer Mix Shift:** Across entire auto industry, shift toward higher incomes and higher FICOs due to affordability challenges
- **Regional Performance:** Middle of the country performing slightly better than coastal markets (consistent with prior quarters)

### Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU)
- **Non-GAAP Total GPU:** $4,796 (+$2,129 sequentially vs. Q4 2022's $2,667) -- **company best for a Q1**
- **Non-GAAP Retail GPU:** $1,591 (vs. Q4's $632; +$959 sequentially)
- **Non-GAAP Wholesale GPU:** $1,236 (vs. Q4's $552; +$684 sequentially)
- **Non-GAAP Other GPU:** $1,969 (vs. Q4's $1,483; +$486 sequentially)

### GPU Unique Items & Adjustments (Q1)
- **Retail Inventory Allowance Adjustment:** +$593 per unit benefit (positive adjustment to retail inventory allowance)
- **Wholesale Inventory Allowance Adjustment:** +$50 per unit benefit
- **Wholesale Market Appreciation:** Estimated +$150 per unit benefit from abnormal wholesale market appreciation in the quarter
- **Loan Sale Volume:** Sold slightly less than normalized volume due to uncertainty in securitization market in March; GPU impact of less-than-normalized volume largely offset by higher interest income and other improvements, leading to approximately normalized other GPU
- **Q1 Results vs. March 22 Updated Guidance:** Beat on GPU; units, revenue, SG&A expense, loan originations all near top end of range; P&L from loan hedging and retail inventory allowance (key uncertainties) both resolved favorably

### Retail GPU Sequential Drivers (Q1 vs. Q4)
- **Positive:** $593 retail inventory allowance adjustment benefit (likely not sustainable/recurring)
- **Positive:** Wider spreads between wholesale and retail market prices
- **Positive:** Higher shipping revenue (delivery fees)
- **Positive:** Lower reconditioning and inbound transport costs
- **Negative:** Higher average days to sale (120 days average for vehicles sold)

### Retail GPU by Inventory Age
- **Vehicles Sold <90 Days Old:** Retail GPU over $2,000 (average turn time ~65 days for this subset)
- **All Vehicles Sold (Q1 average):** Retail GPU $1,591 (average actual turn time ~120 days)
- **Implied Turn Time (inventory/daily sales):** ~65 days (vs. actual 120 days sold, illustrating aged inventory overhang)
- **Key Insight:** The $400+ per unit gap between <90-day and average retail GPU demonstrates the benefit of normalizing inventory size and turning vehicles more quickly

### Wholesale GPU Sequential Drivers (Q1 vs. Q4)
- **Positive:** $50 wholesale inventory allowance adjustment benefit
- **Positive:** ~$150 estimated benefit from abnormal wholesale market appreciation
- **Positive:** Higher wholesale marketplace volume (ADESA)

### Other GPU Sequential Drivers (Q1 vs. Q4)
- **Positive:** Greater volume of loans sold in Q1 compared to Q4 (Q4 had Ally timing shift)
- **Partial Offset:** Sold slightly less than normalized volume due to March securitization market uncertainty
- **Offset to Offset:** Higher interest income and other improvements largely offset the less-than-normalized sales volume

### SG&A
- **Non-GAAP SG&A Reduction (Q1 vs. Q4):** -$119 million sequentially (following -$60 million sequential reduction in Q4)
- **Non-GAAP SG&A Expense Per Unit:** Decreased by more than $900 sequentially in Q1
- **SG&A Expense Level:** Below $405 million (beat target of ~$420 million by Q2 by >$15 million, one quarter early)
- **$1 Billion Cost Reduction Goal:** Completed -- cut $1 billion of annualized costs out of the business (year-long effort completed in Q1)
- **Reductions Broad-Based:** Advertising, compensation and benefits, logistics, and other SG&A

### SG&A Detail by Category (Qualitative)
- **Market Occupancy:** Largely fixed expense (facilities, vending machines); significant opportunity to scale into existing fixed cost base with volume over time
- **Compensation & Benefits:** Tremendous progress made; further opportunities across operations efficiency, logistics network leverage, and corporate expense savings
- **Other SG&A (3 sub-categories):**
  - *Transaction Expenses:* Opportunity to bring down per unit costs (progress on limited warranty, title/registration)
  - *Corporate Expenses:* Opportunity for leverage with increased volume plus dollar expense reductions
  - *Technology Expenses:* Opportunity for leverage with volume plus dollar expense reductions

### Advertising & Customer Acquisition
- **Ad Spend YoY:** Down ~64%
- **Ad Spend Sequential (Q1 vs. Q4):** Down ~35%
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (company level):** ~$700 (lowest ever by "a pretty long way")
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (oldest cohort):** Low $300s (in alignment with best-in-class industry peers and long-term financial model)
- **Cohort Performance:** 4 cohorts performing better than company average
- **Channel Strategy:** Pulled back more from digital channels than brand channels; brand channels preserved due to long-term value of brand building; continually testing and running market-level A/B tests
- **Outlook:** More likely than not no large further reductions from current levels; also unlikely to shoot back up dramatically; will continue testing; over time likely to grow marketing spend; per-unit spend could increase given higher GPU and lower variable costs

### EBITDA & Margins
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** Loss of $24 million, or -0.9% of revenue (massive improvement from Q4 2022's -$291 million loss / -10.1% of revenue)
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin Trend:** Returned to historical trend line; company-best result for Q1

### Operational Efficiency Metrics
- **Variable Costs:** Operating at all-time best efficiencies across virtually all operating groups; variable costs at or near all-time lows across all groups (despite higher input costs from inflation, gas, etc.)
- **Semi-Fixed Costs:** Biggest bucket of progress over last year; most achieved, some remaining room
- **Fixed Costs Per Unit:** Probably at or near all-time highs due to low volume; plan to reduce dollar amounts over time; some go away with scale, some with passage of time
- **Logistics Network Utilization:** Trucks driving with more cars; miles traveled decreased ~40% over time; down ~12% quarter-over-quarter
- **Underutilized Facilities:** Many facilities massively underutilized; office space may go away over time; inspection centers expected to scale with volume

### Inventory
- **Inventory Down YoY:** ~55%
- **Inventory Down in Q1 Alone:** ~20%
- **Inventory Size Relative to Sales:** Now in alignment (implied turn time ~65 days vs. historical 55-68 day normalized range)
- **Remaining Normalization:** ~1.5 to 2 quarters to get fully into alignment (aged inventory still working through)
- **Historical Normal Turn Time Range:** High 50s to mid-to-high 60s average days to sale (2018, 2019, late 2020, 2021)
- **Customer Vehicle Sourcing:** Higher share of retail units sourced from customers in Q1 2023 vs. FY 2021 (more profitable than auction-sourced)

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity (as of March 31, 2023)
- **Total Liquidity Resources:** ~$3.5 billion (down from ~$3.9 billion at end of Q4)
- **Cash and Revolving Availability:** $1.5 billion (down from $1.9 billion at end of Q4)
- **Unpledged Real Estate and Other Assets:** $2.0 billion, including >$1 billion of real estate acquired with ADESA
- **Loan Receivables Backlog:** ~$1.2 billion above normalized levels (tying up capital in warehouses)
- **Average Discount in Warehouses:** ~$0.15 (i.e., ~15 cents on the dollar discount)
- **Cash Unlocked by Selling Down Backlog:** ~$180 million (using $1.2 billion x $0.15 discount)
- **Warehouse Capacity:** Couple of billion dollars in warehouses to house loans prior to sale
- **Financing Preference:** Asset-based/secured financing; largest unpledged asset is nearly $2 billion of real estate (slightly more than half is ADESA locations)

### Loan / Securitization Activity
- **Q1 Loan Sale Volume:** Slightly less than normalized (March securitization market disruption from regional banking crisis)
- **Post-Quarter (Q2 to date):** Sold or securitized ~$1.3 billion of loan principal (increase vs. Q1)
- **Largest Subprime Securitization:** Recently completed; largest subprime securitization to date; many times oversubscribed across all classes
- **Loan Performance Quality:** High-quality loan performance recognized by securitization market
- **Plan for Excess Receivables:** Sell down in coming quarters in orderly fashion; onetime tailwind to other GPU when completed
- **Benefit of Carrying Excess Loans:** Additional finance GPU from yield-y assets prior to sale
- **Residual Strategy:** Generally look to sell residuals; acknowledges current high yields for residual buyers but intends to continue selling given liquidity goals

### ADESA
- **Wholesale Marketplace:** Generated significant gross profit in Q1; long-term addition to total gross profit
- **Inbound Transport Cost Reduction:** Since Q1 2022, reduced inbound transport costs on wholesale vehicles by ~$200 per wholesale unit sold, or ~$90 per retail unit sold, supported by ADESA locations
- **Continued Investment:** Investing in wholesale platform through product and process improvements; goal of growing these businesses over time

## Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2023)

### Retail Units
- **Direction:** Expect sequential reduction in retail units sold in Q2 vs. Q1 (i.e., below 79,240)
- **Drivers:** Continuing to normalize inventory size, optimizing marketing spend, making progress on profitability initiatives
- **Volume Bottoming:** Believe "probably pretty close to where sales will bottom out"; will learn more over coming quarters; lag effects from dramatic inventory and advertising cuts may continue for a couple more quarters
- **Plan Horizon:** Plan to operate at somewhat similar volumes to current levels for next 9-12 months, then pivot to growth

### GPU
- **Non-GAAP Total GPU:** Expect >$5,000 in Q2 (conditioned on macro/industry environment remaining similar to Q1) -- **RAISE vs. prior >$4,000 long-term target; first time guiding >$5,000**
- **Non-GAAP Retail GPU:** Expect >$2,000 in Q2
  - Driven primarily by efforts to normalize inventory size, accelerate turn times, and generate additional revenue from services
  - FY 2021 comparison: ~$1,700 retail GPU
  - Fundamental improvements since 2021: (1) higher share sourced from customers, (2) more revenue from unique services (nationwide shipping, home delivery fees), (3) reconditioning and inbound transport costs (ex-D&A) expected to be below FY 2021 over time
- **Non-GAAP Wholesale GPU:** Expect >$1,000 in Q2
  - Split between first-party wholesale vehicle sales and ADESA third-party wholesale marketplace
  - FY 2021 comparison: ~$450 wholesale GPU
  - Fundamental improvements since 2021: (1) ADESA acquisition, (2) improved efficiency of buying from customers and selling wholesale, (3) ~$200/wholesale unit or ~$90/retail unit inbound transport cost reduction from ADESA locations, (4) continued product and process investments
- **Non-GAAP Other GPU:** Expect >$2,000 in Q2
  - Driven primarily by normalization of loan sale volume
  - Since beginning of Q2, already sold/securitized ~$1.3 billion of loan principal (increase vs. Q1)
  - FY 2021 comparison: ~$2,450 other GPU
  - Medium-term opportunity: Improve cost of funds spread relative to mature securitization participants; expand ancillary product platform

### GPU Sustainability Commentary
- **Management View:** Gains demonstrated in 2023 are sustainable and reflect significant fundamental improvements made in last 12 months
- **Further Opportunities:** See additional GPU improvement opportunities in the future beyond Q2

### SG&A
- **Non-GAAP SG&A Expense (Q2):** Expect similar dollar amount to Q1 (i.e., below ~$405 million)
- **Context:** Q1 already beat the ~$420 million by-Q2 target by >$15 million, one quarter early; Q2 guidance of "similar" partly reflects the very significant gains already made in Q1
- **Beyond Q2:** Significant opportunities to further reduce non-GAAP SG&A expenses over time; automation of manual tasks, routing/scheduling efficiency, customer behavior incentives (pickup vs. delivery, closer cars), all in progress

### EBITDA
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** Expect positive adjusted EBITDA in Q2 -- **achieving Step 1 of 3-step plan**
- **Context:** Q1 adjusted EBITDA loss was only -$24 million (-0.9% of revenue); management characterizes Q2 positive EBITDA as "a milestone, not a change of direction"

## Long-Term Targets

### Three-Step Plan (Reiterated / Updated)
- **Step 1 (Expected Q2 2023):** Drive business to positive adjusted EBITDA -- **ON TRACK, expected to be completed in Q2**
- **Step 2 (Next Focus):** Drive business to significantly positive unit economics
  - Plans for next 9-12 months: keep pushing on unit economics at somewhat similar volumes
- **Step 3 (Future):** Return to growth
  - "That's something that we clearly know how to do"; will be better positioned than ever (more efficient, infrastructure advantage from ADESA)
  - "Still incredibly small compared to this huge opportunity. It is still a 40 million unit market."

### Long-Term GPU Framework
- **FY 2021 Total GPU Baseline:** ~$4,500 (retail ~$1,700, wholesale ~$450, other ~$2,450)
- **Current Structural Improvements vs. 2021:**
  - Delivery fee revenue: ~$500/unit (did not exist in 2021)
  - ADESA wholesale marketplace: significant gross profit contribution (did not exist in 2021)
  - Higher customer sourcing share (more profitable than auction)
  - Lower reconditioning and inbound transport costs (efficiency gains)
  - Wholesale inbound transport cost reduction: ~$200/wholesale unit or ~$90/retail unit from ADESA locations
- **Medium-Term Other GPU Opportunity:** Improve cost of funds spread relative to mature securitization market participants; expand ancillary product platform
- **Carvana Spread Compression Opportunity:** Currently wider spreads than mature issuers; room to narrow over time as Carvana matures as issuer

### Long-Term Financial Goal
- **Ultimate Goal:** Generate significant GAAP net income and free cash flow
- **Key Non-GAAP Metrics (Management Focus):** Non-GAAP gross profit, non-GAAP SG&A expense, adjusted EBITDA

### Long-Term Market Opportunity
- **Market Size:** ~40 million units per year
- **Carvana Market Share:** ~1%
- **Vision:** Largest and most profitable automotive retailer

## Special Topics

### Debt / Liquidity / Going Concern Risk
- **Total Liquidity Decline:** $3.9 billion (end Q4) to $3.5 billion (end Q1), a -$400 million decline in one quarter
- **Cash and Revolving Availability Decline:** $1.9 billion (end Q4) to $1.5 billion (end Q1)
- **Excess Loan Receivables:** ~$1.2 billion above normalized levels tying up capital; selling down would unlock ~$180 million in cash
- **Exchange Offer:** Referenced as "ongoing" by analyst; management did not provide details
- **Management Stance on Restructuring:** Did not address restructuring alternatives (debt-for-equity swap, unsecured bond restructuring); focused on operating plan and asset-based/secured financing preference
- **Financing Preference:** Generally favor asset-based/secured financing; biggest unpledged asset is nearly $2 billion in real estate

### Regional Banking Crisis Impact (March 2023)
- **Securitization Market:** Choppy at end of Q1; planned securitization pushed back
- **Post-Quarter Recovery:** Largest subprime securitization to date completed; many times oversubscribed across all classes
- **Spread Dynamics:** Spread widening observed across market; Carvana itself has seen spread widening over the last year; room for Carvana spreads to compress toward mature issuer levels over time
- **2-Year Treasury vs. Fed Funds Dynamic:** Normalization expected over time; good proxy for Carvana's cost of funds; half of auto loan market provided by banks using similar reference rates

### Cost Reduction / Operational Transformation
- **$1 Billion Annualized Cost Reduction:** Completed (year-long effort)
- **Q1 Specific SG&A Reduction:** >$100 million quarter-over-quarter (exceeded $100 million reduction goal one quarter early)
- **Operational Efficiency:** All-time best efficiencies across virtually all operating groups
- **Variable Costs:** At or near all-time lows across all groups (despite higher input costs from inflation)
- **Semi-Fixed Costs:** Biggest bucket of savings achieved; most progress already made, some room remains
- **Fixed Costs Per Unit:** At or near all-time highs due to low volume; plan to reduce dollar amounts; some costs dissipate with time (underutilized office space), some scale with volume (inspection centers)
- **Logistics Efficiency:** Miles traveled down ~40% over time, ~12% quarter-over-quarter; trucks carrying more cars; delivery fees for distant vehicles

### ADESA Integration
- **Wholesale Marketplace:** Generated significant gross profit in Q1; long-term gross profit addition
- **Integration Benefits Realized:** $200/wholesale unit or $90/retail unit inbound transport cost reduction since Q1 2022
- **Continued Investment:** Product and process improvements; goal of growing wholesale businesses over time
- **Strategic Value:** Improved efficiency of customer vehicle sourcing to wholesale; 56 locations nationwide

### Inventory Strategy & Turn Times
- **Historical Normalized Turn Time:** High 50s to mid-to-high 60s average days to sale (2018-2021, excluding early COVID)
- **Q1 2023 Actual Turn Time on Vehicles Sold:** ~120 days (reflecting aged inventory overhang from 2022 overbuilding)
- **Q1 2023 Implied Turn Time (inventory/daily sales):** ~65 days (now back in normalized range)
- **Remaining Normalization Timeline:** ~1.5 to 2 quarters for full alignment (aged inventory to fully work through)
- **Retail GPU Sensitivity to Turn Time:** >$400/unit difference between <90-day vehicles (>$2,000 GPU) and Q1 average ($1,591 GPU)

### Growth Positioning Commentary
- **Volume Bottoming:** Believe "probably pretty close" to where sales will bottom
- **Growth Capability:** "We certainly know how" to grow; would have lag time if decision made to grow immediately; not the current plan
- **Infrastructure Advantage:** ADESA acquisition provides materially different infrastructure advantage vs. prior growth period
- **Customer Demand:** Believe strong consumer demand exists for Carvana's offering; will satisfy when it's time
- **NPS:** High and improving despite cost-reduction initiatives

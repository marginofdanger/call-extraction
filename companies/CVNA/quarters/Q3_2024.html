Retail Units & Revenue

  • Retail Units Sold: 108,651 (+34% YoY vs. Q3 2023's ~81,078; +7.1% sequentially vs. Q2 2024's 101,440) -- NEW QUARTERLY RECORD
  • YoY Growth vs. Q2 Guidance: Q2 call guided for "a sequential increase in retail units sold" vs. Q2's 101,440; actual came in at 108,651 (+7.1% sequential) -- BEAT
  • YoY Growth Rate Acceleration: +34% YoY in Q3 vs. +32.5% YoY in Q2; third consecutive quarter of fastest-growing public auto retailer
  • Total Revenue: $3,655 million (+32% YoY vs. Q3 2023's ~$2,769 million; +7.2% sequentially vs. Q2's $3,410 million) -- per XBRL/10-Q filing: $3.65 billion
  • Market Share: ~1% of ~40 million annual used car transactions; continued significant market share gains
  • Industry Context: Third consecutive quarter as fastest-growing AND most profitable public automotive retailer; broke Q2's record for most profitable quarter among automotive public retailers ever

Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU) -- Non-GAAP

  • Non-GAAP Total GPU: $7,685 (implied from components: $3,617 + $1,123 + $2,945)
  • Non-GAAP Retail GPU: $3,617 (+$740 YoY vs. Q3 2023's ~$2,877; +$78 sequentially vs. Q2's $3,539) -- NEW ALL-TIME COMPANY RECORD; sixth sequential quarter with new record
  • Non-GAAP Wholesale GPU (per retail unit): $1,123 (+$172 YoY vs. Q3 2023's ~$951; +$19 sequentially vs. Q2's $1,104)
  • Non-GAAP Other GPU: $2,945 (+$377 YoY vs. Q3 2023's ~$2,568; +$244 sequentially vs. Q2's $2,701)

Retail GPU Drivers (Q3 YoY)

  • Fundamental Gains: Continued strength in nonvehicle cost of sales, customer sourcing, inventory turn times, and revenues from additional services
  • Market Factors: Higher spreads between wholesale and retail market prices; lower retail depreciation rates (YoY comparison favorable)
  • Record Streak: Sixth sequential quarter with a new company record for non-GAAP retail GPU

Wholesale GPU Drivers (Q3 YoY)

  • +$172 YoY: Primarily driven by growth in both wholesale vehicle and wholesale marketplace gross profit

Other GPU Drivers (Q3 YoY)

  • +$377 YoY: Primarily driven by higher spreads between origination interest rates and benchmark rates
  • Partially Offset By: Impacts of hedging benchmark rate interest rate changes; selling a smaller amount of loans relative to originations in Q3 2024 compared to Q3 2023
  • Loan Sale Timing Effect (Q3): Selling a greater volume of loans than originated generated approximately $150 per unit of incremental other GPU in Q3
  • Rate Movement Benefit (Q3): Decline in benchmark interest rates between loan origination and sale generated approximately $100 per unit of incremental other GPU in Q3, net of hedging
  • Combined Tailwinds: ~$250/unit of non-recurring or situational benefit in Q3 other GPU; normalized other GPU approximately ~$2,695

SG&A

  • Non-GAAP SG&A: $406 million (+10% YoY vs. Q3 2023's ~$369 million; +$16 million sequentially vs. Q2's $390 million)
  • Non-GAAP SG&A Per Retail Unit: $3,736 (-$832 YoY; -$109 sequentially vs. Q2's $3,845) -- CONTINUED EXCEPTIONAL LEVERAGE
  • YoY Leverage: Retail units sold increased 34% YoY while non-GAAP SG&A increased only 10%, leading to $832 reduction in SG&A per retail unit YoY

SG&A Component Breakdown (Non-GAAP Per Unit)

  • Carvana Operations Expense Per Unit: $1,731 (-$220 YoY; +$35 sequentially vs. Q2's ~$1,696)
  • Operations Sequential: Small tick up of ~$30 sequentially; Jenkins: "Nothing really to call out there, just a number of small items... we still see opportunity to drive that number down over time"
  • Overhead Expense (Dollar): $147 million (+$6 million YoY, primarily driven by $4 million of nonrecurring benefits in Q3 2023)
  • Overhead Per Unit Reduction: -$388 per retail unit sold YoY
  • Overhead Trend: Very steady with very little movement over past many quarters despite very significant growth

EBITDA & Margins

  • Adjusted EBITDA: $429 million -- NEW ALL-TIME COMPANY RECORD (+$281 million YoY vs. $148 million in Q3 2023; +$74 million sequentially vs. Q2's $355 million)
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin: 11.7% -- NEW ALL-TIME COMPANY RECORD (+6.4pp YoY vs. 5.3% in Q3 2023; +1.3pp sequentially vs. Q2's 10.4%)
  • EBITDA Margin vs. Long-Term Model: Surpassed the midpoint of 8% to 13.5% long-term EBITDA margin range
  • 9-Month 2024 Adjusted EBITDA: $1,019 million ($235M + $355M + $429M)
  • EBITDA Quality: "Very high quality compared to many rapidly growing companies due to relatively low noncash expenses"
  • GAAP Operating Income: $337 million -- NEW ALL-TIME COMPANY RECORD (vs. Q2's $259 million) -- per XBRL
  • GAAP Operating Margin: 9.2% -- NEW ALL-TIME COMPANY RECORD; leading the public auto retail industry
  • GAAP Net Income: $148 million (Q3 CEO remarks); Net income per XBRL: $85 million (Net income attributable to Carvana Co. may differ from consolidated NI)
  • Third consecutive quarter of positive net income

Employee Bonus (Q4 Impact, Announced on Q3 Call)

Debt Reduction Actions in Q3

  • 2028 Senior Secured Note Repurchase: $100 million additional repurchased in Q3
  • Cumulative 2028 Note Repurchases: $350 million ($250M in Q2 + $100M in Q3)
  • Deleveraging: Coupled with adjusted EBITDA generation and strong liquidity, further improved leverage ratios

Infrastructure & Capacity (Updated Q3)

  • Built Reconditioning Capacity: Over 1 million retail units per year
  • Physical Real Estate Capacity: Over 3 million retail units per year (includes ADESA sites)
  • ADESA Integration Playbook: Built and executed at 5 of 56 ADESA sites (up from 2 completed + Kansas City megasite at Q2 call)
  • Integration Lead Time: Approximately 90 days per site (vs. 1-3 year process historically for building new reconditioning centers)
  • Integration CapEx: Minimal CapEx required; utilizing existing ADESA land and buildings
  • Integration Benefits: Reconditioning more cars closer to customers; more efficient access to large pools of inventory; lower inbound and outbound shipping distances/costs; greater selection and faster delivery

Delivery & Logistics (Updated)

  • Average Time to Delivery: Down approximately 25% YoY (year-over-year); simultaneously driving down cost
  • Shipping Distance Reduction: ~300 miles reduction in markets with newly converted ADESA sites
  • Drivers: Getting cars closer to customers + running logistics system materially more efficiently

Same-Day Delivery

  • Population Coverage: Now 35% of the U.S. population has access to or is in a market where they could get same-day delivery
  • Current Penetration: Still a "relatively small fraction" of total sales; many limitations based on which cars and customer credits qualify
  • Phase 1 (Underway): Rolling out capability across all geographies
  • Phase 2 (Future): Diving into unlocking more cars, more customers, more car-customer combinations for same-day delivery
  • Ernie Garcia: "Very early in unlocking that... incredibly hard to replicate... reflects the value of vertical integration"

Inventory Dynamics

  • Inventory Status: Starting to grow inventory "just a bit"; remain below target available website inventory levels
  • Website Units: Surpassed 40,000 units on website (per Jeff Lick / Stephens observation)
  • Production: Reconditioning teams doing a great job scaling capabilities in a cost-effective way
  • Near-Term Focus: Returning to more optimal inventory levels remains a key near-term focus
  • Marketing Impact: Inventory growth functions similarly to marketing -- drives conversion by offering more selection

Customer Milestones

  • Cumulative Cars Sold: Over 2 million cars sold total
  • Cumulative Cars Bought from Customers: Over 2 million cars purchased from customers
  • Sourcing Mix: Significant majority of acquisitions still coming from customers (retail sourcing)

3P Retail Marketplace

  • Status: Active for at least 3 years; historically a "pretty small overall share of sales"
  • Q4 Expectation: Expect retail marketplace to increase in Q4
  • Current Focus: Large commercial sellers (not individual dealers); benefits from joint Carvana + ADESA capabilities
  • Reconditioning: Reconditioning the majority of marketplace cars (different from past dealer marketplace)
  • Accounting: Most fee revenue from commercial sellers shows up in retail revenue; small portion in other; gross sales price of car NOT recorded as revenue on marketplace transactions
  • Revenue Impact: Marketplace units will have less revenue per unit than core retail due to accounting (no gross sales price recorded)
  • Economics Framework (Ernie Garcia): Aiming for the same per vehicle dollar economics across the sum of all transactions; long-term financial model goals unchanged -- think about marketplace units as if they were normal core retail units for underlying economics
  • Genesis: Progress incorporating Carvana reconditioning into ADESA centers + making IRCs highly efficient opened up opportunities for commercial sellers to access both wholesale and retail platforms simultaneously

Retail Units -- Sequential Increase in YoY Growth Rate

  • Direction: Expect a sequential increase in the year-over-year growth rate of retail units sold in Q4 vs. Q3's +34% YoY
  • Conditioned On: "As long as the environment remains stable"
  • Implication: Q4 2024 YoY growth rate > 34%; at Q3 2023's ~81,078 base, even 34% growth = ~109K units; accelerating growth rate implies potentially 35-40%+ YoY which would be ~109K-113K+ units
  • Context: Q4 is historically the weakest seasonal quarter for used car demand; management leaning into growth via $5-$10M incremental advertising spend
  • Prior Q2 Guidance: Q2 call guided for "sequential increase in retail units sold" for Q3 (delivered: +7.1% sequential, 108,651 units)

GPU Seasonality Commentary (Q4 Specific)

  • Retail GPU Seasonality (Q4): Expect seasonality "more similar to our average seasonality in 2018 through 2021 than our seasonality in 2022 and 2023"; 2022 and 2023 both impacted by unique internal factors
  • Wholesale Gross Profit Dollar Seasonality (Q4): Expect seasonality "similar to our average seasonality in 2018 through 2023" -- typical wholesale gross profit dollar seasonality
  • Q4 Seasonal Factors (Mark Jenkins): Used car demand is typically lowest in Q4; highest depreciation rates typically in Q4; impacts both retail and wholesale GPU
  • Other GPU Normalization: Q3 had ~$250/unit of situational tailwinds ($150 loan timing + $100 rate movement); these may not repeat in Q4

Advertising Investment

  • Purpose: Further raise awareness of offering; test and understand sensitivity of growth levers
  • Timing Rationale: Q4 is a natural time to test -- traditionally less demand, operationally simpler to understand the power of advertising levers
  • Celebrity Campaign: Bringing back Dax (Shepard) and Kristen (Bell) again for advertising
  • Q3 Ad Spend Context: Growth through first 3 quarters benefited from increasing brand awareness and consumer shifts toward e-commerce on approximately flat advertising spend

Employee Bonus Impact

Strategic Goals (Reiterated)

  • Ultimate Goal: Become the largest and most profitable automotive retailer; buy and sell millions of cars per year
  • Market Opportunity: ~$1 trillion market; ~40 million used car transactions annually; Carvana at ~1% market share
  • Market Share Headroom: Opportunity "still 99% as large as it was on day 1"
  • Moat (Ernie Garcia): "11 years and $10 billion" of investment; complex, capital-intensive business; doing hard and valuable things is the ultimate competitive moat
  • Scaling Path: 3 million units/year capacity = ~8x current run rate = ~7.5% market share, "which feels very achievable for a leader"

Fundamental Gains Framework (Reiterated)

  • Ongoing Opportunity: "Continue to see significant opportunities for further improvement in every part of the business"
  • Growth-Efficiency Balance: Finding the balance between pursuing growth and capturing remaining fundamental gains; "what we're seeing right now... fundamental gains show up in the bottom line and they make it easier to go get more top line" -- doubly valuable
  • Not Done: "Don't expect this to end anytime soon"; team creativity and ambition driving continued improvements
  • Retail GPU Opportunity (Brian Nagel Q&A): Many building blocks -- smarter bidding, minimizing car movement costs, more reconditioning centers closer to customers, better merchandising, intelligent differential pricing across tens of thousands of SKUs distributed across enormous geography
  • SG&A Opportunity: "Continue to see opportunities for significant SG&A expense leverage over time and as we scale, driven by both continued improvements in operational expenses as well as leverage in the fixed components of our cost structure"

Three-Step Plan Status (Updated)

  • Step 2 (COMPLETED / EXCEEDED): Drive repeated and significant positive unit economics
  • Step 3 (UNDERWAY -- ACCELERATING): Drive profitable growth -- third consecutive quarter of fastest-growing AND most profitable public auto retailer

Growth Positioning & Transition Period

Interest Rate Sensitivity (from Q&A)

  • Ernie Garcia: "Our economist hats are broken"
  • First Order Effect: Not a huge change; rates benefit Carvana and competitors equally; competitive positioning is what matters
  • Second Order Effect: Lower rates make cars more affordable, potentially bringing more people into the market -- beneficial but "wouldn't call it like a central driver of our performance"

Coiled Spring Analogy (Nick Jones Q&A)

  • Ernie Garcia: "Love the coiled spring analogy... it seems that it is likely the case"
  • Supporting Factors: More efficient than ever (growth is easier); big spread vs. competition; significant fundamental gains yet to be had; visibility to better financial performance; enormous latent capacity (3M units/year = ~8x current)
  • Governing Growth: Active discovery of the right balance between growth rate and focus on fundamental gains; "our eyes are big"

Advertising Strategy (from Q&A)

  • Ad Spend Still Very Low: Growth achieved on approximately flat advertising spend through first 3 quarters
  • Q4 Testing: Investing $5-$10M more vs. Q3 to understand sensitivity of growth levers
  • Contribution Margin Spread: "No doubt that given where the contribution margins are of incremental sales relative to customer acquisition costs through various customer acquisition channels... there's likely meaningful gaps there"
  • Constraint is Operational Chain: What stands between Carvana and unlocking ad-driven growth is expanding the operational chain and continuing to execute

Notable Q&A Highlights

ADESA Integration Progress (Sharon Zackfia, William Blair)

  • Q: CapEx for integrations coming in below the ~$20M/site originally planned at acquisition? Also, what percent of sales are same-day delivery?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Integrations going very well. Doing a lighter CapEx version to unlock a portion of ultimate capacity at each site. Allows many benefits (closer inventory, shorter delivery, lower inbound costs) in a capital-light and quick way. Will circle back to sites over time for additional CapEx to unlock additional lines. Currently filling in footprint and maximizing joint reconditioning + auction capabilities at these sites.
  • A (Mark Jenkins): All integrations utilize ADESA land and buildings -- "very limited amount of CapEx" required.
  • Same-Day Delivery: 35% of U.S. population has access; still small fraction of actual sales due to system limitations on eligible cars/credits. Phase 1 is geographic rollout; Phase 2 is deepening eligible car-customer combinations.

Q4 EBITDA Margin vs. Q3 (Adam Jonas, Morgan Stanley)

  • Q: Other than employee bonuses, any reason EBITDA margin doesn't hold or grow from Q3 to Q4?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): Q4 seasonality is real -- used car demand typically lowest in Q4, highest depreciation rates in Q4. Retail GPU seasonality expected more like 2018-2021 average (not 2022/2023 which had unique internal factors). Wholesale GP dollar seasonality similar to 2018-2023 average. Q3 had ~$150/unit loan sale timing benefit and ~$100/unit rate movement benefit in other GPU. "Seasonal industry... but we're very pleased with the progress and expect a strong Q4."

ADESA Shipping Distance Savings (Adam Jonas, Morgan Stanley)

  • Q: 300-mile shipping distance reduction -- how much does that save? Other savings from ADESA conversions?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Average time to delivery is down about 25% YoY. Simultaneously driving down cost while doing that. Done partially by getting cars closer to customers, partially by running system materially more efficiently. "A lot of room for continued improvement in both time and cost."

Operations Expense Per Unit Sequential Uptick (Jeff Lick, Stephens)

  • Q: Operations per unit was $1,731, slight sequential uptick from Q2. Any read into that?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): Down just over $200 YoY -- reflects efficiency initiatives. Picked up by ~$30 sequentially. "Nothing really to call out there, just a number of small items." Most importantly, still see opportunity to drive that number down over time.

Other GPU Sustainability (Chris Pierce, Needham)

  • Q: Other GPU at $2,945 with ~$250 in tailwinds. Is $2,700 the normalized run rate? What's changed vs. years ago?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): Area where constantly working to make fundamental gains -- more streamlined customer experiences, better data, better scoring, algorithms, technology. See opportunities for more fundamental gains over time from here.

On 3P Retail Marketplace (Chris Bottiglieri, BNP Paribas)

  • Q: Accounting for marketplace revenue? Expanding with Hertz or other partners?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): Retail marketplace is where cars are sold on behalf of commercial sellers on the site. View it as another acquisition source (substitute for auction purchase). Most fee revenue in retail revenue line; small portion in other. Gross sales price NOT recorded as revenue. Historically a "pretty small percentage of total units" but expect to increase in Q4.
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Previously went down the path of smaller dealer listings briefly; no longer the focus. Current focus is large commercial sellers where joint Carvana + ADESA capabilities create efficiencies. Now reconditioning majority of marketplace cars.

Marketplace Economics (Rajat Gupta, JPMorgan)

  • Q: How should we think about GPU, other GPU, SG&A per unit for marketplace vs. traditional retail?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Think of it as substitute for auction purchases. Same per vehicle dollar economics across sum of all transactions. Operations are the same -- same sellers, same cars, same reconditioning, same customer experience. Different transaction structure wraps around it to move cars more quickly with fewer moves and lower costs. Best to model using long-term financial model as if all units were normal core retail units; major difference is less revenue per marketplace unit due to accounting.

Inventory and Customer Behavior at Scale (Jeff Lick, Stephens)

  • Q: As you surpass 40,000 website units and scale beyond 30,000 units/month, is there any difference in incremental customer behavior?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Starting to grow inventory a bit; remain below target levels. Don't see meaningful demographic differences in customers as they scale. Now sold 2 million+ cars; moved beyond early adopters to "necessarily a big part of the consuming public." Headroom is "very, very large."

Ad Spend Timing (Seth Basham, Wedbush)

  • Q: Why lean into advertising when demand still exceeds supply?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Business performing incredibly well; time to get practical understanding of growth levers. Investments are relatively small on a per-unit basis. Q4 is a natural time -- traditionally less demand, operationally simpler to understand lever sensitivity. Contribution margins of incremental sales vs. customer acquisition costs show "meaningful gaps." Constraint is expanding the operational chain.

Labor Expenses and Growth (Brian Nagel, Oppenheimer)

  • Q: As business ramps, are there other expenses from labor or elsewhere that need to come in?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): Labor in cost of sales (reconditioning), operations expense, and overhead. Even at today's growth rates, all coming down. Reconditioning and transport costs continuing to improve. Operational expenses on meaningful downward trend. Overhead expenses very steady despite significant growth. "Improving across labor efficiency metrics while growing."

Growth Rate Sustainability (John Healy, Northcoast Research)

  • Q: Can you replicate this year's growth rate? How do you govern growth? Effect of 100bp lower rates?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Very small compared to ultimate scale. Would like to get to much bigger scales quickly. Entire business gets better with positive feedback at scale. This last year has been about exploring different rates of growth, understanding how much focus it requires, and ensuring enough focus remains for fundamental gains. "Continuing to try to find that balance." On rates: second-order helpful (more affordable cars, more people in market) but "wouldn't call it a central driver."

Coiled Spring Potential (Nick Jones, Citizens JMP)

  • Q: Is Carvana a coiled spring if industry normalizes and you deploy marketing?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): "It seems that it is likely the case." More efficient than ever, big spread vs. competition, significant fundamental gains remaining, visibility to better financial performance, enormous latent capacity (3M units/year = ~8x current = ~7.5% market share).

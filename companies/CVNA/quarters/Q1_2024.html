Retail Units & Revenue

  • Retail Units Sold: 91,878 (+15.9% YoY vs. Q1 2023's 79,240; +20.8% sequentially vs. Q4 2023's 76,090)
  • YoY Growth vs. Guidance: Q4 call guided for "slightly up on a year-over-year basis"; actual came in at +15.9% YoY -- MASSIVE BEAT (guided ~1-5%, delivered ~16%)
  • Sequential Growth: +20.8% sequential increase (Q4's 76,090 to Q1's 91,878), described as "21% sequentially" by management
  • Annualized Volume: ~360,000 units annualized run rate (management cited this figure)
  • Market Share: Significant market share gains on both a year-over-year and sequential basis
  • Industry Context: Achieved in "a difficult automotive environment at a time when most in the industry are moving backward on both unit economics and volume"
  • Total Revenue: $3,061 million (+17.5% YoY vs. Q1 2023's $2,606 million; +26.3% sequentially vs. Q4 2023's $2,424 million)

Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU) -- Non-GAAP

  • Non-GAAP Total GPU: $6,802 (+$1,072 sequentially vs. Q4's $5,730; +41.8% YoY vs. Q1 2023's $4,796) -- NEW Q1 RECORD
  • Non-GAAP Retail GPU: $3,211 (+$241 sequentially vs. Q4's $2,970) -- NEW ALL-TIME COMPANY RECORD
  • Non-GAAP Wholesale GPU (per retail unit): $1,153 (+$272 sequentially vs. Q4's $881)
  • Non-GAAP Other GPU: $2,438 (+$559 sequentially vs. Q4's $1,879)
  • GPU vs. Prior Normalized Record: Exceeds previous record from Q2 2023 after normalizing for Q2 2023's excess loan sale volume

GPU Sequential Drivers (Q1 vs. Q4)

  • Retail GPU Strength (+$241): Driven by fundamental gains in nonvehicle cost of sales, customer sourcing, inventory turn times, and revenues from additional services
  • Wholesale GPU Increase (+$272): Primarily driven by more favorable depreciation rates and Q1 seasonality
  • Other GPU Increase (+$559): Primarily driven by (1) more normalized loan sale volume relative to originations, (2) lower securitization credit spreads, and (3) credit scoring and pricing optimizations including credit tightening in Q4

Strategic Goals (Reiterated)

  • Ultimate Goal: Become the largest and most profitable automotive retailer; buy and sell millions of cars per year
  • Financial Goal: Generate significant GAAP net income and free cash flow
  • Market Opportunity: ~$1 trillion market; ~40 million used cars bought/sold annually; Carvana at ~1% market share
  • Competitive Positioning: "Competitively, we sit in a better position than we have at any point in our history"

Margin Expansion Opportunity Framework (from Shareholder Letter, Referenced in Call)

  • Starting Point: 7.7% adjusted EBITDA margin in Q1
  • +0.3% from Loan Normalization: Undersold loans in Q1; selling at similar premium to originations would have added ~0.3% to reach ~8.0%
  • +0.8% from Ad Spend Convergence: Oldest 4 cohorts at 1.0% ad/revenue vs. overall 1.8%; as newer cohorts mature, 0.8% of revenue in savings
  • +3%+ from Overhead Leverage: Overhead expenses held flat at ~$150 million in Q1 despite 21% sequential growth; growing into infrastructure could yield >3 points of leverage
  • Cumulative Illustrative Margin: ~12%+ adjusted EBITDA margin potential before additional fundamental gains in GPU and operations expenses
  • Management Note: "That also leaves out the fundamental gains that we're continuing to work very hard on across the business"

Infrastructure & Capacity

  • Existing IRC Capacity: 1.3 million units per year (>3x current ~360,000 annualized volume)
  • Total Capacity Including ADESA: Approximately 3 million units annually
  • ADESA CapEx to Unlock: ~$1.2 billion estimated (sized at time of acquisition, "still a good way to think about it")
  • ADESA Locations with Last-Mile Logistics: 30 of 56 ADESA locations
  • ADESA Locations with Multicar Haulers: 9 locations
  • ADESA Reconditioning Conversion: First site completed in Buffalo, New York -- now running Carli software and proprietary reconditioning processes
  • Buffalo Playbook: "Absolute belief is that it is repeatable across the majority of ADESA sites"
  • ADESA Reconditioning Capacity: ~2 million additional units across ADESA sites as playbook rolls out

Three-Step Plan Status (Updated)

  • Step 1 (COMPLETED): Drive to positive adjusted EBITDA -- achieved FY 2023
  • Step 2 (COMPLETED / EXCEEDED): Drive repeated and significant positive unit economics -- Q1 GPU of $6,802 and EBITDA margin of 7.7% validates model
  • Step 3 (UNDERWAY): Return to profitable growth -- Q1 marks first quarter of significant growth (+16% units) with record profitability simultaneously
  • Transition Period: Management coined "transition period" -- balancing continued efficiency gains with growth investments over next several quarters

Capital Structure & Deleveraging

Current Status

  • Liquidity: "Ample liquidity" and business generating "very strong EBITDA and cash flow"
  • Equity Raise: No indication of plans for equity raise; management focused on operating results to improve capital structure metrics
  • Interest Expense: $173 million in Q1 (includes $142 million PIK interest)
  • PIK Interest on 2028/2030 Notes: Plan to pay cash interest on both 2028 and 2030 senior secured notes on both semiannual payment dates in 2025
  • Deleveraging Impact: Paying cash interest reduces overall debt outstanding (vs. PIK which adds to principal) and reduces long-term cash interest expense
  • Class A Shares Outstanding: 116,558 thousand (vs. 114,239 thousand at Dec 31, 2023)
  • Diluted Shares: 212,239 thousand (reflects full Class A + Class B conversion potential)

Growth Positioning & Transition Period

Inventory Constraint

  • Current State: Inventory constrained; 13-day average time on website before purchase in March 2024
  • Impact: Constraining sales volumes; management believes more inventory would lead to more sales and additional EBITDA
  • Near-Term Focus: Growing production to increase selection to more optimal levels
  • Production Ramp: Reconditioning volumes up ~50% YoY; not seeing per unit costs tick up despite ramp

Transition Period Philosophy

  • Definition: Period of balancing continued efficiency gains (still meaningful gains available in every operating group) with growth investments
  • Duration: "Figure that out over the next several quarters"
  • Not Yet Growth Mode: Most operating teams have not been focused on growth; annual planning cycle is up in June
  • Cost of Growth: Does NOT expect to outsource full car reconditioning (as done in 2021); incremental costs include hiring/training and temporary outsourcing of select services
  • Growth Risk Assessment: Financial position much stronger, unit economics much higher, infrastructure exists, variable costs lower -- risk of growth "is less" than in 2021-2022 period

Pricing Strategy

  • Current Approach: Vehicle pricing stable relative to competitors over last several years; "generally held that pretty consistent"
  • Forward View: Not planning on pricing changes right now; "over time, as we continue to make gains, there's no question that there's opportunities there"
  • No GPU Sacrifice for Growth: Not currently planning to flex GPU lower to drive more units; inventory constraint is the binding constraint, not pricing

AI & Technology Update

Reconditioning Technology (Carli)

  • Status: Carli (proprietary reconditioning software) deployed across IRCs and now at first ADESA conversion (Buffalo)
  • Impact on Recon Costs: Down "hundreds of dollars" relative to full year 2021 on a per unit basis, despite overall cost and wage inflation
  • Ramp Quality: Per unit reconditioning costs NOT ticking up despite ~50% YoY increase in reconditioning volume

Customer Experience Improvements

  • Conversion Drivers: Simpler, faster experiences; fewer customer touch points; better document processing; faster call handling
  • NPS Trend: Up "pretty linearly for the last 18 months"
  • Sales Per Advocate: Up 61% YoY -- customers have fewer touch points, less likely to call in, get better and faster information when they do

Notable Q&A Highlights

GPU Sustainability vs. Reinvestment (Chris Bottiglieri, BNP Paribas)

  • Q: With 3,000+ retail GPU, why not sacrifice GPU to drive higher units?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Currently inventory constrained; "until we catch up, it's pretty clear what our move is supposed to be -- work hard to catch up." Once caught up on inventory, "there may be other moves that we can make, but we'll have to make that call at that time." Not planning to sacrifice GPU for growth now.

Gross Margin Sustainability (Seth Basham, Wedbush)

  • Q: Can you sustain or drive gross margins higher than 19% long-term?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): See "meaningful opportunities for reductions in reconditioning costs from here" through technology, process, and scale; opportunities in inbound transport cost reduction via ADESA; opportunities in wholesale vehicle business, finance and ancillary products. "Even though we are experiencing exceptional results, we still see opportunities for fundamental gains."
  • Analyst Day Update (Ernie Garcia): "It's been almost 6 years since we had the Analyst Day... We haven't given any plans for an Analyst Day in the future, but I think that's something that we could pursue at some point in time."

Capital Structure (Adam Jonas, Morgan Stanley)

  • Q: Do you have enough equity? Longer-term leverage targets?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): "I think we have ample liquidity and the business is generating very strong EBITDA and cash flow today." Plan to delever over time through operating results. $235M EBITDA "meaningfully exceeds our capital expenditures and interest" -- "this quarter resoundingly repositions us."

Marketing Spend Path (Ron Josey, Citi)

  • Two Opposing Forces: (1) Fundamental gains and inventory improvement should drive ad spend as % of revenue down; (2) leaning into incremental customer acquisition at higher CAC could push it up
  • Resolution: "I don't think that we want to call our shot just yet... that's also something that we'll be figuring out as we go through the transition period"

Growth Challenges (Nick Jones, Citizens JMP)

  • Q: As growth accelerates, will you hit choke points?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): "Short answer is yes. Growing is always hard... there will be growing pains. There always are."

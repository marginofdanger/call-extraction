Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2022)

Full Year 2022 Guidance

Long-Term Targets

Long-Term Financial Model

  • Goal: Largest and most profitable automotive retailer
  • Volume Goal: Buying and selling millions of cars per year
  • GPU Target: Resume march toward long-term financial model after returning to >$4,000 GPU
  • EBITDA Path: Return to positive EBITDA then resume march toward long-term financial model
  • Total Addressable Market: ~40 million units/year (fragmented market with significant margins)
  • Current Market Penetration: ~1% of TAM

Other GPU Long-Term Opportunity

  • Significant expansion opportunities: Fundamental improvements in finance platform, fundamental improvements in existing ancillary products, and new ancillary products
  • Management View: "Built a business to generate very significant other revenues and very significant Other GPU over time"

Retail Units & Revenue

  • Retail Units Sold: 105,185 (increase of +14% YoY)
  • Total Revenue: $3.497 billion (+56% YoY)
  • Market Share: Continued to rapidly gain; grew 14% while the overall market was shrinking
  • YoY Unit Growth Context: Deceleration from Q4 2021 (+57% YoY) and Q3 2021 (+74% YoY)

Gross Profit Per Unit (GPU)

  • Total GPU: $2,833 (decrease of -$823 YoY)
  • Total GPU (ex-milestone gift): ~$2,909 (adjusted for $76/unit Ernie milestone gift impact)
  • Retail GPU: $808 (decrease of -$403 YoY)
  • Wholesale GPU: $219 (decrease of -$8 YoY; higher volume offset by lower profit per unit)
  • Other GPU (Finance + Ancillary): $1,806 (decrease of -$412 YoY)

Retail GPU Drivers (YoY)

  • Reconditioning & Inbound Transport Cost Increase: >$600/unit increase YoY (driven by IRC/logistics inefficiencies from Omicron, severe weather, recovery timelines, and lower volume deleveraging)
  • Shipping Revenue Decrease: >$100/unit decrease YoY (driven by refunds to customers for extended delivery times)

Other GPU Drivers

  • Benchmark Interest Rate / Credit Spread Impact (Sequential): >$600/unit negative impact on Other GPU in Q1 vs. Q4 (rapid rise in benchmark rates and widening credit spreads following Russia-Ukraine conflict)
  • 2021 Used Vehicle Price Tailwind (Prior Year): ~$150/unit tailwind in 2021 Other GPU from elevated used vehicle prices driving higher average loan sizes
  • Q1 2022 Other GPU characterized as: "Certainly an outlier, not representative at all of what we would expect to see on a normalized basis"

Ernie Garcia Milestone Gift

  • Impact on Total GPU: -$76/unit
  • Impact on EBITDA Margin: -0.8 percentage points

EBITDA & Margins

  • EBITDA Margin: -11.6% (decrease from -1.3% in prior year period)
  • EBITDA Margin (ex-milestone gift): -10.8%

SG&A

  • SG&A Per Unit: At levels not seen "in a really long time"; historically has been ~$2,500/unit lower at various points
  • SG&A Dollar Context: Total SG&A dollars were "largely on plan" but much higher per unit due to lower volume than planned
  • Advertising Spending: +$21 million YoY despite selling ~8,000 fewer cars (preplanned spend; brand-building investment)
  • Excess Capacity Cost Impact (Illustrative): If SG&A per unit had been $2,500 lower across ~100,000 units = ~$250 million difference; management used this to illustrate the impact of building for higher growth than materialized

Demand & Customer Mix

  • Customers with FICO >700: Grew ~50% YoY (despite logistics constraints and suppressed inventory visibility)
  • Cohort Growth: All cohorts saw YoY growth despite shrinking market
  • Higher Income vs. Lower Income: Higher income households outgrowing lower income by an even wider margin than prior quarters
  • Affordability Theme: Continued to intensify; customers with higher incomes/better credit buying at much higher rates than lower income/lower credit

Balance Sheet & Liquidity (as of March 31, 2022)

  • Total Liquidity Resources: ~$1.7 billion (includes cash, revolving availability, and financeable real estate and securities)

Revenue Milestone (Historical Context)

  • Revenue Run Rate: >$12 billion (9 years from founding)
  • Market Share: ~1% of total addressable market
  • Historical GPU Progress: From negative gross profit to >$4,500 GPU (referenced as a historical high)
  • Historical EBITDA Progress: From -$0.30 EBITDA per $1 revenue to approximately EBITDA breakeven "just last year" (2021)

Sequential Improvement Expected (Q2 vs. Q1)

  • Retail Units Sold: Meaningful sequential improvement expected
  • Revenue: Meaningful sequential improvement expected
  • Total GPU: Meaningful sequential improvement expected
  • SG&A Per Retail Unit Sold: Meaningful sequential improvement expected (i.e., lower SG&A/unit)
  • EBITDA Margin: Meaningful sequential improvement expected
  • Management Quote Context: "We expect meaningful sequential improvement in Q2 versus Q1 in retail units sold, revenue, total GPU, SG&A per retail unit sold and EBITDA margin"

Early Q2 Trends (Qualitative)

  • Order Metrics: Shareholder letter referenced "pretty substantial sequential improvement already" in order metrics including sales (per analyst question)
  • Logistics Network: Approximately half the gap recovered between peak disruption and year-ago levels, depending on metric
  • Reconditioning Costs: Moving further away from Omicron acute effects; seeing improvements vs. most disrupted points
  • Interest Rate Pass-Through: Successfully passing on benchmark rate increases through higher APRs in April

GPU & EBITDA Targets -- PUSHED BACK

  • Prior Guidance (from Q4 2021 shareholder letter): Over $4,000 total GPU and approximately EBITDA breakeven in the last 3 quarters of 2022 taken in aggregate
  • Updated Guidance: Return to >$4,000 GPU and positive EBITDA now expected to be "pushed back a few quarters" beyond that original timeline, then resume march toward long-term financial model
  • Flag: GUIDANCE CUT -- Prior target of >$4,000 GPU and ~EBITDA breakeven for Q2-Q4 2022 aggregate has been deferred by "a few quarters"

Other GPU Normalization

  • Spread between funding costs and origination rates: Expected to "move toward more normalized levels over the coming quarters"
  • Retail Costs: Expected to "move toward more normalized levels over the coming quarters" as logistics network normalizes and expenses better balanced with sales volumes

CapEx

  • Direction: Expected to be "meaningfully less" going forward with ADESA locations online; significant reduction from levels seen in 2021/early 2022 (previously spending heavily on greenfield Carvana IRCs)

Capacity

  • Total Annual Capacity at Full Utilization (by end of 2022): ~1.4 million units (combining Carvana IRCs + ADESA U.S. locations)

ADESA U.S. Acquisition Details

Transaction Overview

  • Expected Close: May 2022
  • Status as of Call: On track to close in May

ADESA Physical Footprint

  • Number of Sites: 56
  • Building Square Footage: ~6.5 million square feet
  • Land: >4,000 acres

ADESA Capacity

  • Current Facility Capacity (if fully staffed): ~200,000+ units/year of annual retail reconditioning
  • Current Utilization: "Far below" the 200,000 capacity; needs hiring and training to unlock
  • Long-Term Production Capacity Target (at full utilization, fully staffed): ~2 million units/year
  • Greenfield IRC Equivalent: ~30 greenfield Carvana IRC locations in terms of production volume
  • Estimated Investment to Reach Full Capacity: ~$1 billion over a period of multiple years

ADESA Logistics Benefits

  • Reconditioned Inventory within 50 miles of: 58% of U.S. population (with ADESA locations added)
  • Reconditioned Inventory within 200 miles of: 94% of U.S. population (with ADESA locations added)
  • Key Benefit: Reducing shipping distances, times, and costs; accelerating path to long-term financial model

ADESA Wholesale Auction Business

  • Plan: Continue operating ADESA's wholesale auction business alongside Carvana reconditioning
  • OEM Relationship Attrition: Small handful of buyers and sellers elected not to do business in short term; "probably even maybe a little less than we would have anticipated"; not flowing through results in a noticeable way yet; "not at a level that is concerning"
  • Industry-Wide Auction Volume: Some reduction across all auction groups, similar to retail volume declines

Transaction Financing

  • Unsecured Notes: $2.275 billion
  • Preferred Equity: $1 billion
  • Common Equity: $1 billion
  • Total Capital Raised: ~$4.275 billion
  • Use of Proceeds: ADESA purchase price, future real estate improvements on ADESA sites, working capital, general corporate purposes

Pro Forma Liquidity (Post-Transaction)

  • Net Cash Proceeds (after ADESA purchase price): ~$1.9 billion
  • Financeable Real Estate (from ADESA): >$900 million
  • Total Liquidity Resources (pro forma): ~$4.5 billion
  • Characterization: "Strongest total liquidity resources and production capacity position ever"

Project Catapult (Internal Efficiency Initiative)

  • Scope: Cross-functional efficiency program spanning every operational team and several product teams
  • Goal: Gain cost efficiencies and process improvements during period of excess capacity; reduce variable costs
  • Theme: "Coil the catapult" -- use period of lower growth pressure to make efficiency gains that are difficult during rapid scaling
  • Mechanism: Clear goals, clear metrics, and accountability cadence across every operational team
  • Nature: Not purely cost-cutting; described as efficiency program (lower cost + simpler to scale up)
  • Expected Outcome: All else constant, expected to reduce variable costs

Industry & Macro Commentary

Used Vehicle Market

  • Industry Sales Trend: Fewer industry-wide sales than prior periods; market was shrinking throughout Q1
  • Used Vehicle Prices: Many cars ~40% more expensive than a year ago vs. economy-wide inflation ~8%
  • Wholesale Prices: Moved up late 2021/early 2022, then started to fall back more recently
  • Retail Prices: Lagging wholesale; tend to move more smoothly; more stable due to dealer floor plan dynamics
  • Management Preference: Hopes car prices come down; would benefit customers and help return industry to normal volumes
  • Benchmark Rate Moves: Most dramatic since the Great Financial Crisis

Tax Season

  • Q1 2022 Tax Season: "Much softer in terms of impact than tax seasons historically have been"
  • Prior Year Comp: Q1 2021 benefited from both tax season and some stimulus

Pent-Up Demand View

  • Long-Term Industry Average: ~40 million transactions/year (270 million cars on road, consumers trade every ~6.75 years)
  • Management View: Current slowdown likely generating pent-up demand; historically alleviates relatively quickly
  • Added Complexity: Inflation, reduced new car production, car prices massively outpacing general inflation

Interest Rate Environment

  • Interest Rate Pass-Through: Rates are "somewhat sticky" -- takes some time to fully pass through increases to customers; adjustment period "not super long"
  • APR Dynamics: Mix shift to higher FICO doesn't materially impact economics because spreads are priced thoughtfully for each segment
  • Key Risk in Q1: Gap between origination rates and actual funding costs at time of loan sale; driven by dramatic benchmark moves

Operational Updates

Logistics Network

  • Disruption Timeline: Started with Omicron, exacerbated by winter storms, recovery slowed by IRC expansion and inventory growth creating network complexity
  • Recovery Progress (as of late Q1/early Q2): ~50% of gap recovered between peak disruption and year-ago metrics
  • Impact on Sales: Ongoing suppression of inventory visibility to customers; unable to make full inventory visible across network
  • 3 New IRCs Opened: In Q1 2022 (plus 1 in Q4 2021)
  • Inventory Visibility: Still suppressed; not yet increased visibility of inventory across network to all customers

Growth Planning

  • Planning Horizon: 6-12 months in advance depending on lead times for each operational team
  • Q1 Mismatch: Built capacity for growth rates closer to Q4 2021 (+57%) and Q3 2021 (+74%); actual growth came in at +14%
  • Costs: Relatively fixed in short term; led to higher COGS/unit and higher SG&A/unit

Affordable Car Strategy

  • Near-Term Actions: Buying greater quantity of less expensive cars
  • Medium-Term Actions: Changes to IRC processes to produce more affordable cars; product enhancements to make it easier for customers to find and purchase less expensive cars

Q&A Key Data Points (Incremental)

  • ADESA Investment Timeline: ~$1 billion over multiple years to move from ~200K to ~2M annual capacity
  • ADESA CapEx Benefit: Meaningfully less quarterly CapEx expected going forward vs. recent quarters (greenfield IRC buildout was major use of CapEx)
  • Headcount: Significant quarter-on-quarter reduction in job openings noted by analyst; management declined to give precise timeline for rightsizing cost structure
  • Marketing Philosophy: Careful about "yanking the wheel too much too quickly"; brand-building has long payoff; marketing impressions compound over time
  • Used Car Price Direction Impact on GPU: In transition, falling wholesale prices could provide "something of a headwind to retail GPU" but the gap between wholesale and retail markets may limit the headwind severity
  • Ideal Environment (Ernie's Framework): (1) Affordable cars (combination of low prices + low rates), (2) Stability/predictability in market, (3) Less competition -- stability matters more than absolute levels of rates or prices

Retail Units & Revenue

  • Retail Units Sold: 114,379 (+50.3% YoY vs. Q4 2023's 76,090; +5.3% sequentially vs. Q3 2024's 108,651) -- SECOND HIGHEST QUARTERLY RETAIL UNIT SALES IN COMPANY HISTORY
  • YoY Growth vs. Q3 Guidance: Q3 call guided for "a sequential increase in the year-over-year growth rate of retail units sold" vs. Q3's +34% YoY; actual came in at +50.3% YoY -- MASSIVE BEAT (growth rate accelerated from 34% to 50%)
  • YoY Growth Rate Acceleration: +50.3% YoY in Q4 vs. +34% YoY in Q3; significant acceleration driven by all 3 long-term growth drivers
  • Total Revenue: $3,547 million (+46.3% YoY vs. Q4 2023's $2,424 million; -3.0% sequentially vs. Q3's $3,655 million)
  • Retail Vehicle Sales: $2,552 million (+43.6% YoY vs. $1,777 million)
  • Wholesale Sales & Revenues: $678 million (+35.9% YoY vs. $499 million)
  • Other Sales & Revenues: $317 million (+114.2% YoY vs. $148 million)
  • Industry Context: Fourth consecutive quarter of fastest-growing AND most profitable public automotive retailer; set new Q4 records for retail units sold, adjusted EBITDA, adjusted EBITDA margin, GAAP operating income, GAAP operating margin, net income, and net income margin
  • Q4 is Seasonally Lower: Despite being a seasonally lower demand period, delivered second highest quarterly retail unit sales ever

Retail GPU Drivers (Q4 YoY, +$361)

  • Positive: Lower retail depreciation rates; reductions in reconditioning and inbound transport costs; lower average days to sale
  • Partially Offset By: Lower spreads between wholesale and retail market prices
  • Seasonality: Q4 retail GPU sequential decline was "nearly exactly what we saw on average in the pre-COVID years" -- consistent with typical Q4 seasonal pattern

Wholesale GPU Drivers (Q4 YoY, -$24)

  • Negative: Faster growth in retail units than wholesale vehicle and wholesale marketplace units (denominator effect)
  • Partially Offset By: Lower vehicle depreciation rates
  • Wholesale Unit Performance: Wholesale vehicle units +43% YoY; wholesale gross profit (non-GAAP dollars) +50% YoY -- all-time Q4 record for wholesale units
  • Wholesale Marketplace Units: 231,659 (+11.2% YoY vs. 208,370)

Other GPU Drivers (Q4 YoY, +$849)

  • Positive: Higher spreads between origination interest rates and benchmark rates; lower credit spreads on securitization transactions; changes in loan sale channel mix; selling a greater volume of loans relative to originations in Q4 2024 compared to Q4 2023
  • Fixed Loan Sales: Stepped up to ~$700 million in Q4; whole loan sales channel expanding
  • Funding Strategy: Expect to continue using diversified funding strategy -- combination of whole loan sales and securitization market transactions

Other GPU Sub-Component (from Q&A)

  • VSCs + Gap (ex-Gain on Sale) Per Unit: $901 in Q4 (-$38 sequentially vs. Q3's $939; -$72 YoY vs. Q4 2023's $973)
  • Management Commentary: "Can fluctuate quarter-to-quarter... see continued opportunity for fundamental gains" in ancillary products

Retail Units

  • Direction: Expect a sequential increase in retail units sold in Q1 2025 vs. Q4 2024's 114,379
  • Conditioned On: "Assuming the environment remains stable"
  • Context: Q1 includes tax season, which management expects to drive increased sales; Q4 call noted they are "heading into very -- in very short order tax season, where we expect sales to go up again"
  • Historical Seasonal Pattern: Q1 typically stronger than Q4 due to tax refund season

Adjusted EBITDA

  • Direction: Expect a sequential increase in adjusted EBITDA in Q1 2025 vs. Q4 2024's $359 million
  • Conditioned On: "Assuming the environment remains stable"
  • Context: Q1 benefits from seasonal GPU strength (tax season = lower depreciation, higher demand)

GPU Seasonality Commentary (Q1 vs. Q4)

  • Retail GPU Seasonality: Management views Q4 and Q1 as the "lowest quarters of the year" for retail GPU, with Q2 and Q3 being higher -- correlated with industry depreciation trends where depreciation rates are highest late in the year and lowest mid-year
  • Q1 vs. Q4 Historical Pattern: Mark Jenkins did NOT explicitly guide Q1 retail GPU higher than Q4; stated broadly that Q4 and Q1 are both low GPU quarters
  • Seth Basham Q&A Clarification: Asked if Q4-to-Q1 typically sees "material improvement through tax refund season" -- Jenkins responded that both Q4 and Q1 are typically the lower quarters and Q2/Q3 are higher; no specific directional guidance on Q1 vs. Q4 retail GPU

Retail Units

  • Direction: Expect significant growth in retail units sold in full year 2025 vs. FY 2024's 416,348
  • Conditioned On: "Assuming the environment remains stable"
  • No Specific Quantification: Management did not define "significant" or provide a range; Sharon Zackfia noted "I won't ask you to define significant growth in 2025"

Adjusted EBITDA

  • Direction: Expect significant growth in adjusted EBITDA in full year 2025 vs. FY 2024's $1,378 million
  • Conditioned On: "Assuming the environment remains stable"
  • No Specific Quantification: Management did not define "significant" or provide a dollar range

Interest Expense Step-Down

  • Meaningful Step Down in GAAP Net Interest Expense in 2025 vs. 2024: Called out specifically in shareholder letter
  • Secured Notes Trading at ~7% Yield: Meaningfully below the coupon on the notes; provides longer-term opportunity to reduce interest expense even further through potential refinancing
  • No Near-Term Refinancing Expectation: "We don't have any near-term expectations of refinancing of our secured notes"
  • Implication: Lower interest expense in 2025 will mechanically improve credit ratios even without EBITDA growth; combined with significant EBITDA growth, path to materially better credit ratios

Fundamental Gains Sharing Strategy

  • Majority of Future Gains Shared with Customers: "We do expect the majority of those [fundamental gains] from here to be passed on to customers"
  • Three Options for Gains: (1) Share with customers via conversion benefits; (2) Invest in foundational capabilities to further differentiate; (3) Show up on the bottom line
  • Optimization Framework: Optimizing the sum of dollars generated + size of business achievable + long-term value; "aiming for the high end of our long-term financial model is a very reasonable thing to do"

Capacity & Scale

  • Current Reconditioning Capacity (Pre-ADESA Sites): Over 1 million retail units/year (~3x current volume)
  • Full ADESA Integration Capacity: Approximately 3 million retail units/year (~8x current volume)
  • ADESA Sites Integrated to Date: 6 of 56 ADESA mega sites integrated in 2024
  • 2025 ADESA Integration Plan: Expect to open approximately 10 more mega sites in 2025, 2 of which already announced
  • Current Market Share: ~1%
  • Competitive Landscape: Largest direct competitor has ~2% market share; largest 100 direct competitors combined have ~10% market share
  • Market Size: ~270 million cars in the U.S. car park; ~40 million transactions per year

GAAP Operating Income Growth

  • Expectation: "Expect our GAAP operating income to grow faster than adjusted EBITDA over time" -- due to currently carrying expenses supporting 1M+ unit retail capacity vs. ~416K actual volume
  • Operating Leverage: As capacity utilization improves and D&A leverage kicks in, GAAP metrics improve faster than adjusted EBITDA

Deleveraging Goals

  • Long-Term Target: Drive toward investment-grade quality credit ratios over time
  • Current Net Debt / EBITDA: 2.8x
  • Current EBITDA / Interest Expense: >2.0x
  • ATM Program: View as "good corporate housekeeping"; generally used opportunistically; no specific near-term goals or targets disclosed
  • Balance Sheet Philosophy: "We want to have a very strong balance sheet that is paired with that significant, long-term opportunity"

Cumulative Milestones

  • Cumulative Cars Bought and Sold: Over 4 million
  • Market Coverage: Delivering to contiguous 48 states; in 316 markets (approximately steady)
  • Fortune 500: Previously tied for third fastest company to make the Fortune 500

Special Topics

Tariffs Impact Assessment

  • Management View: "We certainly don't know what's going to happen with tariffs and wouldn't want to take a strong stand there"
  • If New Car Prices Increase: Generally speaking, would "probably lead to dampened price increases" in used cars indirectly
  • Operating Stance: Running the business "with the kind of idea of operating like it's any other year"
  • Uncertainty: "A lot of uncertainty around exactly how those things will play out"; likely to be many iterations before final outcomes are clear

Retail Marketplace (3P Commercial)

  • Status: Ramped sequentially in Q4; management describes it as "replacement source of inventory that we think is highly scalable"
  • Fundamental Role: Getting a car from previous owner to next owner "as efficiently as we possibly can" -- joint distribution channels of retail and wholesale at same location
  • Current State: "Most of those fundamental gains that we believe are achievable in those partnerships are not yet achieved"; best to think of as a "substitute vehicle acquisition source" for now
  • ADESA Digital Auction Tool: Working with ADESA teammates to build a "very high-quality digital auction tool that's going very well and also feeds into that system"
  • Scalability: "No reason why that can't scale meaningfully from here... the portion of the market that could be served in a similar way is very large"

AI / Technology (Sebastian Chatbot)

  • Philosophy: "Enormous believers in the power of this technology... unlikely that we will see another technology in our lifetimes that's this powerful"
  • Current Application: Sebastian chatbot is the "first and currently most developed application" -- represents "the brain that we're building" with Carvana's skin on top
  • Unique Advantages for AI: (1) Scale -- bought and sold 4M+ cars with deep transaction data; (2) Deterministic, no-haggle process -- easier for AI calculations; (3) Vertical integration -- single customer experience; (4) Dedicated tech team
  • Plan: "Opportunity for us to put that [AI brain] throughout the entirety of the customer experience, and I think we plan to be very aggressive there"

Delivery Speed & Same-Day Delivery

  • FY 2024 Achievement: Delivery times down more than 20% YoY (per shareholder letter)
  • Same-Day Delivery Status: Rolled out to a number of markets; "very early stages"; currently used more as efficiency benefit (filling underutilized slots) rather than maximized for customer reach
  • Future Plans: Opportunity to expand same-day to larger portion of credit spectrum; technology enhancements to make more cars eligible; ADESA integrations reduce miles and get cars closer to customers
  • Broader Speed Improvement: Same principle applies to next-day, 2-day, 3-day deliveries -- shifting volume into faster deliveries

Operations Expense Breakdown

  • $1,696 Per Unit (Total Operations): Management broke this into ~$1,400 core operations + ~$300 expected warranty expense
  • $1,400 Core Functions: Getting a car to customer, managing return policy, finance, verifications, delivery
  • Improvement Trajectory: Down $328 YoY; down $150 improvement in prior year reference (Ernie cited "$330 year-over-year" improvement)
  • Continued Opportunity: "Room for improvement in every part of that... logistics, customer care, verifications, title work, last mile delivery, handling the return policy"

Market Cohort / Growth Recovery

  • Peak Volume Proximity: In Q4 2024, came within "just shy... a couple of thousand units shy of our peak unit volume in '22"
  • Cohort Patterns: Market cohorts "clearly light the path to significantly more scale and are back on the path to growing very quickly... showing all the same patterns that they showed back then"
  • Market Share Recovery: "At similar places where they were back then in terms of market share"

NEW: 5-to-10-Year Objective (Introduced This Quarter)

  • Target: Grow to 3 million annual retail sales with 13.5% adjusted EBITDA margins in the next 5 to 10 years -- MAJOR NEW LONG-TERM OBJECTIVE
  • Current Run Rate: 133,898 Q1 units x 4 = ~536K annualized (vs. 3 million target)
  • Current EBITDA Margin: 11.5% in Q1 (vs. 13.5% target)
  • Path Description: "Continue marching straight to 13.5% EBITDA margin and rapidly grow to 3 million units while sharing significant additional value with our customers along the way"
  • Priority Framework: Over the next 5-10 years, will prioritize growth over margin within reasonable margin ranges; plan to manage speed of growth to ensure exceptional customer experiences and high-quality, efficient operations
  • NOT An Imminent Margin Reduction: Sharon Zackfia clarification -- "We are not creating this long-term plan with kind of reasonable flexibility built into it to set ourselves up for an imminent reduction in margin. That is not our plan."
  • Market Context: 40 million used cars + 16 million new cars = 56 million total annual vehicle transactions; at Q1 annualized run rate, Carvana is ~1% of total market; 3 million units would be ~7.5% of 40 million used car market

Production Ramp Mechanics (from Shareholder Letter & Q&A)

  • Last 12 Months Production Growth: Averaged increasing production by ~80 units per week; done "very consistently"
  • 10-Year Path to 3M Units: Would need to increase production growth to ~90 units per week -- "feels very achievable"
  • 5-Year Path to 3M Units: Would need to increase production growth to ~180 units per week -- "sounds a bit further away" but aided by facility expansion
  • Current Producing Locations: ~23 locations on average over the last year
  • Future Producing Locations: Expect over time to have ~60 locations producing cars
  • Historical Context: In 2018-2021, grew significantly faster per facility on a per-facility basis

Fundamental Gains Sharing Strategy (Reiterated & Expanded)

  • YoY Gains Unlocked: ~$1,000 per unit of EBITDA margin value unlocked YoY (Ernie, Q&A)
  • Future Gains Direction: Will seek to share "the significant majority" of future fundamental gains with customers
  • Sharing Mechanisms: (1) Economic -- lower prices, lower rates, higher bids on customer sell-to-us vehicles; (2) Experience -- faster deliveries, better digital tools, better phone responsiveness; (3) Investment in operational capabilities
  • Not Just Price: "Simple, faster and fun are also areas that are worthy of investment" -- investing focus more than dollars in some cases
  • NPS: Near 3-year highs driven by these experience investments

Special Topics

Tariff Impact Assessment (Updated)

  • Directional View: If tariffs drive up car prices, "all else constant, that's bad"; but likely would drive up new car prices by more than used car prices, so "may be a directional benefit to used cars"
  • Benefit to Value Players: May benefit business models able to offer value to consumers -- Carvana believes they fit that description
  • Observed Behavior: When tariffs first announced, saw "a small pull forward of demand"; reacted by pulling levers to keep sales on plan; then saw "probably a little bit of a trough thereafter"; since then "feels like it's stabilized"
  • Operating Approach: Running business "with the kind of idea of operating like it's any other year"; adaptive system inherently adjusts (e.g., if customers prefer less expensive cars, automatically starts buying less expensive cars)
  • Parts Tariffs on Recon Costs: "We'll see exactly how these tariffs end up playing out"; not making any particular prediction; mental model is that shared input cost changes get "passed through" in a competitive market (retail GPUs have been stable despite enormous swings in car prices over the last 5-6 years)

Franchise Dealership Acquisition

  • Status: Purchased a small franchise dealership (new vehicle opportunity)
  • Details: "Very early... in the process now of experimenting and learning"
  • Cost: $24 million acquisition cost (from cash flow statement)
  • Goodwill: $2 million recorded
  • TAM Implication: Management now referencing "40 million used cars sold every year... an additional 16 million new cars sold every year" as total addressable market

Recession Resilience (Ernie, Q&A)

  • Current vs. 2022 Position: "Very, very different company... we were not a money-making company [in 2022]"; now "the most profitable automotive retailer by a pretty long way"
  • Profitability Buffer: "Approximately twice as profitable as the average public automotive retailer" -- "significant margin to work with" plus "significant cash balances" while "simultaneously growing very quickly"
  • Expected Behavior in Downturn: Would look "much more like other highly profitable automotive retailers have dealt with difficult environments in the past" rather than like 2022-2023
  • Finance GPU Resilience: Through COVID, saw finance GPU "go down a little bit, but recover a quarter later"; through 2022-2023, "GPU go down a little bit, but not all that much" -- competitive market means inputs get priced in

Lending & Loan Monetization

  • Securitization Program: "Stronger than it's ever been"
  • Residual Buyer Base: More recurring buyers in the residual sale portion than ever before
  • Ally Arrangement: Continues to be "great for us over a long period of time"
  • New Pool of Buyers: "Added another large pool of buyers this quarter" (pooled loan sales)
  • Origination-to-Benchmark Spread: Wider this quarter than in some previous quarters (beneficial for Other GPU)
  • Fundamental Gains in Lending: (1) Improving credit scoring, pricing, underwriting; (2) Using more data; (3) Lowering cost of funds by adding new buyers; "still see meaningful opportunities for further fundamental gains in both areas"

Retail Marketplace / ADESA Integration

  • Commercial Seller Offering: Developing offerings where commercial sellers can send cars to Carvana and either: (1) wholesale through physical ADESA auction; (2) wholesale through ADESA Clear (digital auction, expanding in 2025); (3) sell via retail marketplace
  • ADESA Clear: Digital auction offering that "has been expanding this year"
  • Status: "Incredibly early in that story"; see "real fundamental opportunity for a faster and lower-cost offering"
  • Ancillary Revenue (3PL, Reconditioning): Opportunities exist but "not a near-term focus"; near-term focus is growing core car-selling business

Inventory Sourcing (Marvin Fong, Q&A)

  • Consumer Sourcing Mix: ~80-85% of retail units sourced from consumers (analyst reference)
  • Stability Expectation: Economics have been "remarkably stable over a long period of time" across several orders of magnitude of growth; expects same going forward

Customer Awareness & Adoption

  • Awareness: Generally pretty good -- consumers recognize the Carvana logo
  • Understanding: Lower levels -- many customers don't fully know about broad selection, simple financing, 7-day returns, trade-in values
  • Trust: Built through friends/family/neighbor referrals; takes time given people buy cars once every 5-6 years
  • All Markets Growing: Both newer and older markets continue to grow at exciting rates; growth is not just newer markets catching up to older ones

Vehicle Mix at Scale

  • No Major Shift Needed: "Not obvious that our average sale needs to move all that much" as they scale to 3 million units
  • Current Distribution: Selling across the spectrum of cars less than 10 years old; ~85% of used cars sold in the U.S. are less than 10 years old
  • Opportunity: Room to go both upmarket and "a little bit downmarket" but core offering likely to remain reflective of current mix

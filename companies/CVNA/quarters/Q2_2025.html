Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2025)

  • Retail Units Sold: Sequential increase vs. Q2 2025 (143,280 units); no specific unit number provided
  • Advertising Spend: Larger sequential increase in Q3 vs. the Q2 sequential increase ($12 million Q1-to-Q2 increase implies Q3 step-up will exceed $12 million)
  • Average Selling Price (ASP): Expected to increase further in Q3 vs. Q2; management noted ASP could decline after Q3 depending on algorithmic inventory selection signals
  • Condition: All guidance contingent on "environment remaining stable"

Full Year 2025 Guidance

  • Adjusted EBITDA: $2.0 billion to $2.2 billion - RAISED from prior year actual of $1.38 billion (2024); implied growth of ~45%–59% YoY - Prior year (FY 2024) adjusted EBITDA: "just shy of $1.4 billion" (~$1.38 billion per management)
  • Condition: Contingent on stable macro environment

Long-Term Targets

  • Retail Unit Sales Target: 3 million cars per year
  • Adjusted EBITDA Margin Target: 13.5%
  • Timeframe: 5 to 10 years from call date - 5-year path implies approximately 40% compounded annual growth rate from current base - 10-year path implies approximately 20% compounded annual growth rate
  • ADESA Full Build-Out CapEx: Originally guided at ~$1 billion total across all ADESA locations (multi-year); noted to be "a little bit higher" now due to inflation since acquisition; spend will occur over a number of years

Volume & Revenue

  • Retail Units Sold: 143,280 — new company record; +41% YoY
  • Revenue: $4.84 billion — new company record; +42% YoY
  • Market Share (U.S. used car): ~1.5%
  • Market Share (total U.S. car market): ~1%
  • Estimated used car market growth (industry peers): <5% in units; comp-store peers averaged ~+1% YoY growth

Gross Profit per Unit (GPU) — Non-GAAP

  • Retail GPU: +$195 YoY - Driven by: reductions in reconditioning and inbound transport costs (~half of gain); ~$100 one-time benefit from tariff-related pricing impact in April (other half) - April retail GPU was notably elevated vs. remaining months of Q2 due to tariff-driven demand surge in late March
  • Wholesale GPU: -$85 YoY - Driven by: faster growth in retail units sold than wholesale marketplace units, partially offset by lower wholesale vehicle depreciation rates
  • Other GPU: +$126 YoY - Driven by: better cost of funds; higher Vehicle Service Contract (VSC) attachment rate - Partially offset by: ~$100 positive impact in Q2 2024 from selling additional (stored) loans that did not recur in Q2 2025

SG&A — Non-GAAP

  • Total SG&A per Retail Unit Sold: -$460 YoY (reduction) - Carvana Operations portion: -$147 per retail unit sold YoY (operational efficiency initiatives) - Overhead portion: -$328 per retail unit sold YoY (leverage from higher unit volume)
  • Advertising Expense: +$29 million YoY; +$44 per retail unit sold YoY; +$12 million sequentially (Q1 to Q2)

Special Topics

Tariff Impact (Q2 2025)

  • Tariff announcements in late March 2025 drove stronger demand and higher margins in April
  • April retail GPU elevated relative to May/June; net Q2 benefit estimated at approximately +$100 per retail unit
  • Industry peer YoY unit growth for the quarter: approximately +1% (prior quarter: approximately flat); consistent seasonal trends overall with modest pull-forward and brief subsequent slowdown

ADESA Integration Strategy

  • Current phase: CapEx-light integrations of existing ADESA structures using Carvana/Carli technology and retail reconditioning processes
  • Next phase: Fuller physical build-outs of ADESA locations to expand capacity; expected to continue through H2 2025 and into 2026
  • Inventory pool expansion: Converting all ADESA facilities gives capacity for more than twice the number of inventory pools vs. today; reduces miles cars travel

Finance Platform / Other GPU

  • Q2 2024 other GPU included approximately +$100 per unit from selling additional loans (stored in Q1 2024, sold in Q2 2024); this did not recur in Q2 2025 — a headwind to YoY other GPU comparison
  • Q2 2025 sell-through of loans was approximately normal (loan sales roughly in line with originations)
  • Cost of funds improvements driven by: expanding pool of loan investors; continued strong asset performance; vertically integrated model advantages
  • Management view: Auto loan buyer base is expandable; being larger has made it easier (not harder) to attract new investors

Inventory / ASP

  • Three drivers of sequential inventory balance increase in Q2: (1) sales and selection growth; (2) accounting reclassification from a large retail marketplace partner (inventory moved to Carvana balance sheet); (3) higher average vehicle cost/ASP

Unit Economics Philosophy (Q2 2025 Letter)

  • Management signaled intent to focus more holistically on total units and total EBITDA rather than individual GPU line items; individual line items can "jump around" quarter-to-quarter
  • GPU + SG&A together vs. total operating expenses gap is described as "very, very large," providing significant room for advertising and other investments

GPU Sequential Drivers (Q2 vs. Q1)

  • Retail GPU Strength (+$328): Driven by fundamental gains in nonvehicle cost of sales, customer sourcing, inventory turn times, revenues from additional sources; also higher spreads between wholesale and retail market prices, partially offset by higher retail depreciation rates and lower retail inventory allowance adjustment
  • Wholesale GPU Decrease (-$49): Growth in both wholesale vehicle and wholesale marketplace gross profit, offset by growth in retail units sold (denominator effect)
  • Other GPU Increase (+$263): Continued impact of credit scoring improvements, pricing optimizations, and credit tightening begun in Q4 2023; partially offset by Q2 2023 having held/sold greater volume of loans relative to originations (Q2 2023 other GPU inflated by ~$650)

Strategic Goals (Reiterated)

  • Ultimate Goal: Become the largest and most profitable automotive retailer; buy and sell millions of cars per year
  • Market Opportunity: ~$1 trillion market; ~40 million used cars bought/sold annually; Carvana at ~1% market share
  • Competitive Positioning: "We are a company with just a 1% market share in a $1 trillion industry with highly fragmented competition and barriers to entry that have recently been proven to be extremely hard to overcome"
  • Barriers to Entry: "10 years and $10 billion ago, we set out to build an entirely new way to buy and sell cars"; 10-12 international companies attempted similar models; Carvana "by far and away, the most successful of those big swings, and success in the middle looked like down 99%"

Margin Expansion Opportunity Framework (Shareholder Letter Bridge, Referenced)

  • Starting Point: ~10.0% normalized adjusted EBITDA margin (10.4% reported minus 0.4% loan timing)
  • Opportunity from Ad Spend Convergence: More mature markets at lower ad spend as % of revenue; as newer markets mature, additional margin potential
  • Opportunity from Overhead Leverage: Overhead held flat for 5-6 quarters; plan to continue holding flat while growing into 3x capacity
  • Opportunity from Fundamental Gains: "Very clear plans and high confidence in achieving further fundamental gains across each area of variable costs and gross profit"
  • Management View: "Even though we are experiencing exceptional results, we still see opportunities for fundamental gains" (Mark Jenkins on Q1 call, reiterated in Q2 context)

Growth Positioning & Transition Period

Transition Period (Continued)

  • Definition: Period of balancing continued efficiency gains with growth investments
  • Status: Still in transition; trying to find the "healthy balance of growth where we can grow significantly and get all the clear benefits that come with that, but also continue to make fundamental gains"

  • Marketing Not Yet Leaned Into: "We haven't invested in marketing yet. There's -- our marketing expense relative to our gross profits leaves a lot of room"

Giving Gains Back to Customers

Notable Q&A Highlights

SG&A Sustainability (Adam Jonas, Morgan Stanley)

  • Q: Carvana grew 32% with flat SG&A. CarMax units fell 3% with SG&A up 13%. How long can you keep SG&A flat?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Broke SG&A into 3 categories: (1) Overhead -- held flat for 5-6 quarters, plan to continue; (2) Marketing -- $542/unit, down from long-term average >$1,000; (3) Operations -- $1,696/unit, several hundred dollars below pre-pandemic, despite industry costs up ~$1,000. Operations ex-warranty gains vs. pre-pandemic are ~$400/unit. "The business model is not done being fully flexed."

EBITDA Guide Implying H2 Step-Down (Adam Jonas, Morgan Stanley)

  • Q: Midpoint implies ~$255M/quarter in H2 vs. $355M in Q2. What's baked in?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): Acknowledged math is correct. Two factors: (1) ~$12M / 0.4% favorable loan timing in Q2; (2) H2 seasonality. "We're extremely excited with the results we just had. We're extremely excited about the outlook for the rest of this year."

Retail GPU Ceiling (Chris Pierce, Needham)

  • Q: Where is the upper bound of retail GPU?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): "We believe there are significant fundamental gains to be had in every GPU line item, retail GPU included." Industry SG&A up ~$1,000 post-pandemic; auto retail is cost-plus; wholesale-retail spreads up ~$1,000. All pre-pandemic heuristics should be moved up ~$1,000. Plus Carvana-specific gains in reconditioning, inbound transport, sourcing. "We think it can go up."
  • Passing Gains to Customers: "Likely now getting to a place where it's more likely than not that it will make more sense to pass additional gains back to customers."

GPU Seasonality in Q3 (Sharon Zackfia, William Blair)

  • Q: Should we expect lower sequential GPU in Q3?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): Seasonality "most prominent" in Q4, "and then maybe to an extent in the early first quarter." Did NOT confirm Q3 GPU weakness. Higher depreciation rates and softer demand are seasonal forms.

Other GPU Direction (Seth Basham, Wedbush)

  • Q: Can other GPU go higher from here? Or will there be a step back?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): "Strong first half on other GPU" driven by scoring, pricing, monetization, and ancillary product gains. See opportunities for fundamental gains. Primary callout for Q2: ~$12M favorable loan timing effect (0.4% of revenue).

Production Growth Avenues (Chris Pierce, Needham)

  • Q: Should we think about adding second shifts? Or throughput increases?
  • A (Mark Jenkins): Three avenues: (1) Adding lines in existing IRCs with excess capacity; (2) Adding shifts to full IRCs; (3) Increasing production at ADESA locations (Kansas City "megasite" next). "More flexibility than we've ever had before for growing production efficiently."

ADESA Conversion Timing (Brian Nagel, Oppenheimer)

  • Q: How should we think about timing of ADESA conversions?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): "Something we're figuring out in this transition period." Done very efficiently so far with almost no CapEx. More sites will lean toward more CapEx but with offsetting benefits (closer to customers, lower transport costs, more inventory pools). "Speed at which we do that is something that we're figuring out."

Competitive Response (Nick Jones, Citizens JMP)

  • Q: Do you sense competitors might react differently given your results?
  • A (Ernie Garcia): "We try to not be too focused on competition. We try to be focused on our customers." Things built are "really differentiated and take a long time and a lot of effort." 10-12 international examples of similar attempts; Carvana is "by far and away, the most successful." "We're going to keep trying to put more space between us and everyone else."

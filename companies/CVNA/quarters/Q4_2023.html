  • Inbound Transport Distance: Moving cars ~20% fewer miles inbound to inspection centers vs. a year ago

  • Activity Pairing Rate: Nearly 50% of activities paired (deliver a car, pick up another on way back); up 75% YoY
  • Customer Care Advocate Efficiency: Roughly 2x as efficient as in the past (sales per advocate per period)
  • Title & Registration: Better performance metrics, "now extremely high quality"
  • Reconditioning Cost Reduction: >$900 step-down in non-vehicle retail cost of sales since peak over a year ago (referenced from Q3 call context)

Strategic Goals (Reiterated)

  • Ultimate Goal: Become the largest and most profitable automotive retailer; buy and sell millions of cars per year
  • Financial Goal: Generate significant GAAP net income and free cash flow
  • Market Opportunity: ~$1 trillion market with approximately 1% current market share

Infrastructure & Capacity

  • Land: 6,500 acres
  • Parking Capacity: Over 500,000 parking spots
  • Market Coverage: 300+ markets nationwide
  • Capacity Utilization: Far below capacity; existing footprint supports "multiples of profitable growth"
  • Overhead Leverage: Significant underutilized overhead costs; expects "significant operating leverage" as growth resumes

Three-Step Plan Status (Updated)

  • Step 1 (COMPLETED): Drive to positive adjusted EBITDA
  • Step 2 (TRANSITIONING): Drive repeated and significant positive unit economics -- "complete the projects that we've got underway today"
  • Step 3 (BEGINNING): Return to growth -- Q1 2024 marks the first quarter with guided YoY retail unit growth
  • Transition Philosophy: "We will try to be intelligent about how we transition between the goal of efficiency and the goal of growth. We think that there are still gains to be had in both areas."
  • Growth Efficiency Advantage: Infrastructure exists, operational costs are lower, activity pairing is higher, transport distances are shorter -- "the work that it takes to grow is meaningfully less than it's been in the past"

Variable Cost Opportunities (Ongoing)

  • Credit Scoring & Pricing: Continued enhancement in credit scoring, credit pricing, credit structuring; innovations allowing more precise connection of credit pricing/structuring to individual loans
  • Car Sourcing: Getting smarter about how they buy cars, data-driven evaluation, cutting out biggest losers at point of receipt
  • Reconditioning Centers: Continued technology gains (Carli proprietary system), process standardization, parts procurement efficiency
  • Inbound Transport: Expanding reconditioning sites brings down inbound cost and days
  • Outbound Transport: Pairing technology with process excellence
  • Additional Services Revenue: Long-distance shipping revenue from nationwide logistics network; "fairly steady over the last 3 or 4 quarters"

Growth Cost Framework (from Mark Jenkins, Q&A)

  • Incremental Costs of Growth in Retail COGS: (1) Direct hiring and training expenses, (2) newer employees take time to reach full efficiency, (3) may outsource more services temporarily on a select basis
  • Key Caveat: Does NOT expect to outsource full car reconditioning (as done in 2021)
  • Sizing: "We do not view those as being a particularly significant part of the overall retail GPU story"
  • Partial Offset: Getting leverage on fixed facilities expenses in inspection and reconditioning centers

Loan / Finance Business Detail

Q4 Underselling of Loans

  • Q4 Behavior: Undersold originations by a couple hundred million dollars (lower volume of loan sales relative to retail units)
  • Q1 Baseline: Outlook for "significantly above $100M EBITDA" does not assume any loan sales above originations
  • Forward Expectation: Q4 is NOT the new base level; management expects to "transition back toward more normalized levels by overselling originations at various points from time to time over the course of the year"
  • Net Position: Excess loan sales represent upside to the EBITDA guidance, not embedded in baseline

Other GPU Pricing Adjustment

  • Rate Pass-Through: Higher industry-wide loss expectations that impacted Q4 other GPU have "since been passed through to our pricing"
  • Implication: Q1 Other GPU should benefit from pricing adjustments made in late Q4/early Q1

Same-Day Delivery Update

Current Status

  • Markets: Live in 11 markets
  • Conversion Impact: "Absolutely, there's no question that speed drives conversion" -- management confirmed same-day drives conversion but declined to quantify
  • Current Strategy Focus: Efficiency gains (filling delivery schedule gaps) rather than growth lever
  • Inventory Availability: Vehicles closest to customers in markets with nearby inspection centers are available same-day
  • Customer Reception: Notable uptake; customers "very positively surprised"

Future Potential

  • Expansion Path: As more capabilities open at ADESA locations, same-day delivery can expand to many more markets
  • Growth Lever Potential: Over time, can shift from efficiency focus to growth tool by leaning into staffing model to enable more same-day slots
  • Competitive Moat: "Only possible with vertical integration... only possible with an enormous footprint... very hard to replicate"

AI & Technology (from Q&A)

AI Positioning

  • Key Advantages: (1) Large scale -- enormous data points collected daily across all car evaluations and customer interactions, (2) highly vertically integrated -- parts of the chain connecting and learning from each other, (3) technology-centric organization
  • Deterministic System: No negotiation; customer gets complete answer instantly driven by logic; enables more complete AI-powered experiences
  • Current Gains: Customer care efficiency improvements -- better document processing, faster call handling
  • Numerical Impact: "I'm not sure it's driving numerical results yet outside of some things you're seeing in customer care"
  • Unique Example: AI can answer complex customer questions like "Do you have any similar cars with the sunroof that will cost me $20 less per month?" -- only possible with vertical integration, deterministic pricing, and organized data

Affordability & Market Context (from Q&A)

Used Car Pricing

  • Inflation-Adjusted Car Prices: Still approximately 10% higher than other goods in the economy (inflation-adjusted using CPI)
  • Monthly Payments: Approximately 20% higher relative to other goods vs. pre-pandemic (compounds price + interest rate increases)
  • Direction: Room for moderation; model of "post-shortage glut" has been predictive for last 2 years
  • New Car Incentives: Seeing "a lot more new car incentives and aggressiveness right now" which supports continued used car price normalization

Retail GPU Resilience Through Depreciation

  • Observation: Retail GPU near $2,800+ for last 3 quarters despite ongoing depreciation
  • Explanation: Business sits between wholesale and retail markets; wholesale market has anticipated depreciation, keeping margins stable
  • Management View: "Best case scenario" -- depreciation making cars more affordable while margins remain stable

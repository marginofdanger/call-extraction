5 Key Takeaways

1
Perfect directional record. Across 27 directional guidance calls (14 unit, 13 EBITDA), Carvana has been correct every single time. No public company in this dataset has a comparable streak of directional accuracy over 16 quarters.
2
Systematically conservative numerical targets. Every explicit numerical floor (>$5,000 GPU, >$100M EBITDA, >150K units) has been beaten, often by wide margins. The >$5,000 GPU target was exceeded by an average of $1,385/unit. The >$100M EBITDA floor was beaten by +135%.
3
Q1 2024: the signature sandbagging quarter. Guided “slightly up YoY” in units and “significantly above $100M” in EBITDA. Delivered +15.9% unit growth and $235M EBITDA. This single quarter established the pattern of dramatically exceeding modest guidance.
4
FY EBITDA guidance: guide low, raise, beat. Both years with explicit dollar guidance followed the same pattern — initial conservative range at Q2, mid-year raise at Q3, then beat the raised target. FY 2024: $1.0–$1.2B → “sig. above $1.2B” → $1,378M. FY 2025: $2.0–$2.2B → “≥$2.2B” → $2,237M.
5
$3.4B EBITDA swing in 3 years. From ~-$1,138M (FY 2022) to +$2,237M (FY 2025) represents a $3,375M improvement — one of the largest profitability turnarounds in recent public market history. Quarterly EBITDA went from -$406M to +$637M.
6
GPU consistently above $5,000 since Q2 2023. After setting the >$5,000 target for three consecutive quarters (Q2–Q4 2023), Carvana stopped guiding the floor because actual GPU consistently exceeded $6,800+. By FY 2024, average GPU was approximately $7,200/unit — 44% above the original target.
7
Directional guidance masks the beat magnitude. By using qualitative language (“sequential increase,” “significant growth”), Carvana avoids setting a precise bar that would expose the degree of conservatism. Units grew 43% YoY in every quarter of 2025, but the guidance was simply “sequential increase.”
8
Only one miss in the entire dataset. The Q4 2022 SGA stretch goal of $4,000/unit was missed ($4,942 actual), but management proactively warned it wouldn’t be met on the Q3 2022 call. Every other numerical target has been beaten. Every directional call has been correct.
9
FY 2022 breakeven withdrawal was the right call. Management withdrew the Q2–Q4 2022 EBITDA breakeven target on the Q1 2022 call, recognizing macro deterioration. The year ended at -$1.1B. This honest assessment, followed by the $1B+ cost restructuring, laid the groundwork for the turnaround.
10
Long-term targets frame massive runway. The 3M annual unit / 13.5% EBITDA margin target (set Q1 2025) implies ~$40B+ revenue and ~$5.4B+ EBITDA — roughly 5× current levels. At FY 2025’s 597K units, Carvana is at ~20% of the volume target with margin already at 11%.

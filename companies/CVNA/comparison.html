14 Key Takeaways

1
The most dramatic corporate turnaround in recent memory. Carvana went from going-concern questions and −$1B EBITDA (2022) to +$2.2B EBITDA and investment-grade-adjacent leverage ratios (2025) in just 3 years. The three-step plan (breakeven → positive unit economics → profitable growth) was executed with remarkable precision.
2
Unit economics improved structurally, not cyclically. Total GPU went from $2,667 (Q4 ’22 trough) to $7,344 (Q2 ’24 record) — a $4,677/unit improvement driven by faster inventory turns, lower recon costs, ADESA wholesale integration, and expanding finance GPU. Even as GPU has moderated to ~$6,500–$7,500, it remains 2–3x pre-crisis levels.
3
SGA leverage is the compounding engine. SGA/unit declined ~45% from ~$6,250 to ~$3,400 while absolute SGA barely grew. This is operating leverage in its purest form: the technology platform, logistics network, and ADESA infrastructure absorb 40%+ volume growth with minimal incremental fixed cost.
4
ADESA transformed from liability to strategic moat. The 2022 acquisition nearly bankrupted Carvana. By 2025, ADESA provides 74 sites, physical real estate for >3M units/year, and the reconditioning backbone for the 3M-unit long-term target. Only 15 of 56 sites have been integrated, meaning the majority of the value is still ahead. Utilization at integrated sites is still below 50%.
5
Volume reaccelerated to 40%+ growth with better economics. Retail units went from a 76K trough (Q2 ’23) to 164K (Q4 ’25) — more than doubling — while maintaining >$6,500 Total GPU and >10% EBITDA margins. FY 2025 volume of 597K exceeded the FY 2022 pre-crisis peak of 412K by 45%.
6
The debt restructuring bought critical time. The 2023 restructuring (>$1.3B face value reduction, >$455M/year interest savings) prevented bankruptcy and gave the operational improvements time to compound. Net Debt/EBITDA improved from near-infinite (negative EBITDA) to 1.3x. Total corporate debt retired in 2024–2025: $1.2B.
7
Every financial target has been met or exceeded. $4,000 GPU: achieved (actually $7,344). EBITDA breakeven: achieved ($155M first positive quarter). $1.0–$1.2B FY EBITDA: delivered $1.4B. $2.0–$2.2B FY EBITDA: delivered at high end. This track record lends credibility to the 3M-unit / 13.5%-margin long-term target.
8
Finance GPU is the underappreciated growth driver. Other GPU (primarily gain on loan sales) grew from ~$1,500/unit to ~$2,900/unit, becoming the largest GPU component.

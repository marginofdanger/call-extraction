# Meta Platforms Q3_2022 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 26, 2022 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2022 (July-September 2022)

---

## Q3 2022 Actuals

### Consolidated Results

- **Revenue:** $27.7B (-4% YoY; +2% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **FX Impact (Actual):** ~$1.8B headwind to total revenue (had FX rates remained constant with Q3 2021, revenue would have been ~$1.8B higher)
- **Total Expenses:** $22.1B (+19% YoY), includes $413M impairment of certain operating leases related to office facilities footprint consolidation
- **Operating Income:** $5.7B
- **Operating Margin:** 20%
- **Tax Rate:** 21%
- **Net Income:** $4.4B
- **Diluted EPS:** $1.64
- **Capital Expenditures (including principal payments on finance leases):** $9.5B
- **Free Cash Flow:** $173M
- **Share Repurchases:** $6.5B of Class A common stock in Q3
- **Debt Offering:** $10B inaugural debt offering completed in Q3
- **Cash and Marketable Securities (end of quarter):** $41.8B

### Expense Line Items (YoY Changes)

- **Cost of Revenue:** -1% YoY (reduction in Reality Labs hardware costs offset by growth in infrastructure and content-related expenses)
- **R&D:** +45% YoY (driven by hiring within Family of Apps and Reality Labs segments, plus Reality Labs technology development costs)
- **Marketing & Sales:** +6% YoY (driven by headcount-related costs)
- **G&A:** +15% YoY (driven by headcount-related costs)

### Headcount

- **Net New Hires in Q3:** 3,700 (down from Q2 net additions of 5,700, despite Q3 typically being a seasonally stronger hiring period)

### Engagement Metrics

- **Family of Apps DAP (Daily Active People):** ~2.9B (September)
- **Family of Apps MAP (Monthly Active People):** ~3.7B (September)
- **Facebook DAU:** 1.98B (+3% YoY, +54M YoY)
- **Facebook DAU/MAU Ratio:** ~67%
- **Facebook MAU:** 2.96B (+48M YoY, +2% YoY)
- **Instagram Monthly Actives:** 2B+
- **WhatsApp Daily Actives:** 2B+ (North America is now fastest-growing region)
- **Reels Plays (Facebook + Instagram):** 140B+ per day (+50% from 6 months ago)
- **Instagram Reels Reshares via DMs:** 1B per day
- **Aggregate Time Spent on Instagram:** Up YoY (both U.S. and globally)
- **Aggregate Time Spent on Facebook:** Up YoY (both U.S. and globally)

### Ad Monetization

- **Ad Impressions:** +17% YoY
- **Average Price Per Ad:** -18% YoY (driven by strong impression growth from lower monetizing surfaces/regions, FX depreciation, and lower advertiser demand)
- **Ad Revenue by Region (YoY):**
  - Asia Pacific: +6%
  - Rest of World: +3%
  - North America: -3%
  - Europe: -16%
- **Strongest Verticals:** Health care and travel (largest positive contributors to growth)
- **Weakest Verticals:** Online commerce, gaming, financial services, and CPG (continued softness)
- **Advertiser Size Trends:** Revenue growth from large advertisers remains challenged; more resilience among smaller advertisers

### Family of Apps Segment

- **FoA Revenue:** $27.4B (-4% YoY)
- **FoA Ad Revenue:** $27.2B (-4% YoY; +3% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **FoA Other Revenue:** $192M (+9% YoY), driven by strong business messaging growth from WhatsApp business platform, partially offset by decline in other line items
- **FoA Expenses:** $18.1B (+18% YoY), representing 82% of overall expenses
- **FoA Operating Income:** $9.3B
- **FoA Operating Margin:** 34%

### Reality Labs Segment

- **RL Revenue:** $285M (-49% YoY, due to lower Quest 2 sales)
- **RL Expenses:** $4.0B (+24% YoY, driven primarily by employee-related costs and technology development expenses)
- **RL Operating Loss:** $3.7B

---

## Reels & Business Messaging Monetization

- **Reels Annual Revenue Run Rate (Instagram + Facebook combined):** $3B (up from $1B+ for Instagram Reels alone as of Q2 2022)
- **Reels Quarterly Revenue Headwind:** More than $500M (Reels displaces revenue from higher-monetizing Feed/Stories surfaces)
- **Reels Neutral Timeline:** Expected to reach revenue-neutral in 12 to 18 months
- **Click-to-Messaging Ads Annual Run Rate:** $9B (one of fastest-growing ad products)
- **Click-to-WhatsApp Annual Run Rate:** $1.5B+ (growing 80%+ YoY)
- **AI Impact on Facebook Reels:** A single AI advancement in scaling recommendations models led to 15% watch time gain for Facebook Reels (shared on Q2 call), with continued growth since then
- **Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns:** Launched in August 2022; early tests across a cross-section of advertisers showed 32% increase in return on ad spend

## Q4 2022 Guidance

- **Revenue:** $30.0B to $32.5B
- **FX Assumption:** ~7% headwind to YoY total revenue growth based on current exchange rates

## Full Year 2022 Guidance

- **Total Expenses:** $85B to $87B (narrowed from prior range of $85B to $88B), includes estimated $900M in additional Q4 charges related to office facilities footprint consolidation
- **CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases):** $32B to $33B (narrowed from prior range of $30B to $34B)

## Full Year 2023 Guidance

- **Total Expenses:** $96B to $101B, includes estimated $2B in charges related to office facilities footprint consolidation
- **Expense Growth Composition:** Slight majority of expense dollar growth from operating expenses; remainder from cost of revenue
- **OpEx Growth Drivers:** Primarily headcount-related costs from employees already hired in 2022 (concentrated in technical and senior roles)
- **Cost of Revenue Growth Drivers:** Increased depreciation from prior-year CapEx, plus Reality Labs hardware costs from next-gen consumer Quest headset launch
- **Headcount (End of 2023):** Expected approximately in line with Q3 2022 levels (roughly flat)
- **CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases):** $34B to $39B (increase in AI capacity driving substantially all of CapEx growth)
- **Reality Labs Operating Losses:** Expected to grow significantly YoY in 2023 (biggest drivers: next-gen consumer Quest headset launch + first full year of salaries for 2022 hires)
- **Tax Rate (Full Year 2023):** Expected to be similar to Q3 2022 rate (~21%)
- **Tax Rate (Q4 2022):** Expected to be similar to Q3 2022 rate (~21%), absent changes to U.S. tax law

## Long-Term Targets & Strategic Framework

- **Family of Apps Operating Income Goal:** Grow FoA operating income such that even with AI infrastructure and Reality Labs investments, overall company operating income can still meaningfully grow in the long term
- **CapEx as % of Revenue:** Current surge largely due to AI infrastructure buildout; expected to come down as a percent of revenue over the long term
- **Reality Labs Investment Pacing (Beyond 2023):** Expected to pace Reality Labs investments to ensure achievement of overall company operating income growth goal
- **Capital Allocation Philosophy:** Allocate a portion of FoA profits toward future-focused areas (AI, metaverse) while enabling greater return of capital to shareholders
- **AI CapEx ROI Approach:** Future AI CapEx investment level will depend on demonstrated returns from engagement and revenue gains; spending will be paced accordingly if returns do not materialize

---

## AI Discovery Engine & Infrastructure Investment

- **AI-Powered Content Recommendations:** Discovery engine plays an increasingly important role, enabling recommendations of content from across the network beyond just followed accounts
- **Content Types Recommended:** Reels, photos, text, links, communities, short- and long-form video
- **AI Infrastructure Investment:** Substantially all CapEx growth in 2023 driven by AI capacity expansion
- **Infrastructure Specifics:** More expensive servers and networking equipment required; new data centers specifically equipped for next-gen AI hardware
- **Expected AI Returns:** Meaningful improvements anticipated across Feed, Reels, and Ads
- **Data Center Strategy:** Stepped-up investment in recent years to build ahead of anticipated capacity needs; additional capacity expected to provide greater flexibility in server types and longer useful life, generating cost efficiencies over time

---

## Metaverse & Reality Labs Strategy

- **Quest Pro:** Announced at Connect conference; shipping started; high-end VR headset with high-resolution mixed reality
- **Target Addressable Market for Quest Pro Line:** 200M people who get new PCs every year (mostly for work)
- **Quest Pro Partnerships:** Microsoft (productivity and enterprise management), Adobe (creative tools), Autodesk (creative tools), Zoom (communication), Accenture (enterprise solutions)
- **Four Major Metaverse Platforms Under Development:**
  1. Social metaverse platform (Horizon, avatar system)
  2. VR (consumer-focused and work-focused product lines)
  3. Augmented reality (still internal, significant R&D effort)
  4. Neural interfaces (wrist-based EMG interface)
- **Product Launch Timeline:** Different products and platforms expected to launch at different cadences over the next 5 to 10 years

---

## Ad Tech Rebuilding & Signal Loss

- **ATT Impact Trend:** Headwind from Apple's ATT changes diminished in Q3 as Meta lapped the first full quarter post-iOS 14.5 launch; however, offset by weak advertising demand from macroeconomic environment
- **Q3 Constant Currency Growth Rate:** In line with Q2 rate (ATT improvement offset by macro weakness)
- **Ad Tech Investments:** Continued broader work to rebuild ad tech for improved performance and measurement
- **Key Initiatives:** Evolving ad system by growing on-site conversions (lead ads, click-to-message ads); investing in AI/ML to improve measurement, targeting, and delivery
- **Privacy-Enhancing Technologies:** Longer-term investment in proprietary solutions and industry collaboration

---

## Efficiency & Cost Discipline

- **2023 Budget Approach:** Significant changes across the board to operate more efficiently; higher scrutiny on all areas of operating expenses
- **Headcount Strategy:** Some teams held flat, others shrinking, headcount growth only in highest priorities
- **Expected End-of-2023 Headcount:** Approximately in line with Q3 2022 levels
- **Organization Size Outlook:** Expected to end 2023 as either roughly the same size or slightly smaller than current levels
- **Three Primary Investment Focus Areas:** (1) AI discovery engine powering Reels and recommendations, (2) Ads and business messaging platforms, (3) Metaverse
- **Office Facilities Consolidation:** Ongoing rationalization of real estate footprint; $413M impairment charge in Q3 2022; estimated $900M additional charges in Q4 2022; estimated $2B charges in 2023

---

## Q&A Quantitative Highlights

- **Reels Revenue Headwind Confirmation:** Reels was still negative overall to revenue by about $500M in the quarter (confirmed by CFO Dave Wehner)
- **Reels Tailwind Timeline:** Expected to become a tailwind to revenue in the 12- to 18-month time frame
- **Click-to-Messaging Run Rate Confirmation:** $9B annual run rate (mostly click-to-Messenger; click-to-WhatsApp at $1.5B+ run rate growing 80%+ YoY)
- **CORKCICLE Case Study (Reels Advertising):** Insulated drinkware brand that added Reels to its business-as-usual strategy saw 34% higher return on ad spend and 34% higher sales
- **Transatlantic Data Transfers:** Continuing to monitor developments regarding viability and potential impact on European operations

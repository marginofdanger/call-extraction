# Meta Platforms Q3_2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 25, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2023 (July - September 2023)

---

## Q3 2023 Actuals — Consolidated Results

- **Revenue:** $34.1B (+23% YoY; +21% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **Total Expenses:** $20.4B (-7% YoY)
  - Cost of revenue increased 9% (higher infrastructure-related costs, partially offset by lower content costs)
  - R&D increased 1% (higher headcount-related costs from FoA and RL, partially offset by lower non-headcount RL expenses)
  - Marketing & sales decreased 24% (lower marketing spend and headcount-related costs)
  - G&A decreased 39% (lower legal-related expenses)
- **Operating Income:** $13.7B
- **Operating Margin:** 40% (highest operating margin in 2 years)
- **Tax Rate:** 17%
- **Net Income:** $11.6B
- **Diluted EPS:** $4.39
- **Capital Expenditures (incl. principal payments on finance leases):** $6.8B (below prior year levels, primarily due to lower server and data center construction spend as Meta prepared to shift to new data center design, and payment timing)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $13.6B (benefiting from a deferral of income taxes, which was paid in Q4)
- **Share Repurchases:** $3.7B of Class A common stock in Q3
- **Cash and Marketable Securities:** $61.1B (end of quarter)
- **Debt:** $18.4B (end of quarter)
- **Headcount:** ~66,100 employees (-7% QoQ from Q2 2023; Q3 headcount no longer included the substantial majority of employees impacted by previously announced layoffs)

---

## Q3 2023 Actuals — Family of Apps Segment

- **Family DAP (Daily Active People):** ~3.14B in September
- **Family MAP (Monthly Active People):** ~3.96B in September
- **Total Users Across Family (monthly):** 3.9B+ (estimated by Mark Zuckerberg)
- **Facebook DAU:** 2.09B (+5% YoY, +101M YoY)
- **Facebook DAU/MAU Ratio:** ~68%
- **Facebook MAU:** 3.05B (+91M YoY, +3% YoY)
- **Family of Apps Revenue:** $33.9B (+24% YoY)
- **Family of Apps Ad Revenue:** $33.6B (+24% YoY; +21% YoY on constant currency basis)
  - Online commerce was the largest contributor to YoY growth, followed by CPG and gaming
  - Online commerce and gaming benefited from strong spend among advertisers in China reaching customers in other markets
  - Acceleration and positive YoY growth in all verticals except politics (lapping 2022 U.S. midterms)
- **Ad Revenue by User Geography (YoY growth):**
  - Rest of World: +36%
  - Europe: +35%
  - Asia Pacific: +19%
  - North America: +17%
  - Foreign currency was a tailwind to ad revenue growth in all international regions
- **Regional Acceleration Details:**
  - North America accelerated by 7 points (strong demand from China advertisers; no currency tailwind)
  - Europe accelerated 21 points (broad-based acceleration, China advertiser demand, currency tailwind after Q2 headwinds)
  - Asia Pacific accelerated 9 points (stronger demand in Southeast Asian countries, currency tailwind vs. Q2 headwinds)
  - Rest of World accelerated 20 points (stronger pricing, same currency dynamic; Brazil strong contributor due to China advertiser demand)
- **Ad Impressions:** +31% YoY (mainly driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
- **Average Price Per Ad:** -6% YoY (decline driven by strong impression growth, especially from lower monetizing services and regions)
- **Family of Apps Other Revenue:** $293M (+53% YoY, driven by strong business messaging revenue growth from WhatsApp Business platform)
- **Family of Apps Expenses:** $16.4B (~81% of overall expenses; -9% YoY as infrastructure cost growth was more than offset by lower legal-related expenses, marketing, and headcount-related costs)
- **Family of Apps Operating Income:** $17.5B
- **Family of Apps Operating Margin:** 52%

---

## Q3 2023 Actuals — Reality Labs Segment

- **Reality Labs Revenue:** $210M (-26% YoY, due primarily to lower Quest 2 sales)
- **Reality Labs Expenses:** $4.0B (flat YoY; higher headcount-related expenses offset by lower non-headcount-related operating expenses)
- **Reality Labs Operating Loss:** $3.7B

## Engagement & Product Metrics

- **Reels Impact on Instagram Time Spent:** 40%+ increase in time spent on Instagram since Reels launch (with all ranking and product improvements)
- **Reels Monetization:** Now net neutral to overall company ad revenue (reached milestone earlier than expected); Meta will no longer quantify net revenue contribution from Reels going forward
- **AI-Driven Feed Recommendations (2023 YTD):**
  - Facebook time spent: +7% increase from recommendation improvements
  - Instagram time spent: +6% increase from recommendation improvements
- **Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns:** Reached $10B run rate
- **Advantage+ Creative Tools:** More than half of advertisers using Advantage+ creative tools to optimize images and text
- **Business Messaging:** 600M+ conversations between people and businesses per day across platforms
- **WhatsApp Business Messaging in India:** 60%+ of people on WhatsApp in India message a business app account every week
- **Click-to-Message Ads in India Revenue:** Doubled YoY
- **Click-to-WhatsApp Ad Revenue:** Growing very quickly; already at a multibillion-dollar annual run rate
- **Threads Monthly Actives:** Just under 100M (3 months post-launch)
- **Llama Downloads:** 30M+ downloads last month
- **Avatars Created:** 1B+ so far
- **Chinese Advertiser Spend:** Further accelerated in Q3; benefited from strong investments from larger clients and broader-based strength; tailwinds from lower shipping costs and easing regulations on gaming industry
- **Video Content Share:** Makes up more than half of time spent on Facebook and Instagram

## Q4 2023 Guidance — Revenue

- **Revenue:** $36.5B - $40.0B (midpoint: $38.25B)
- **FX Assumption:** ~2% tailwind to YoY total revenue growth in Q4 based on current exchange rates
- **Guidance Range Note:** Wider than typical range to capture uncertainty from geopolitical volatility (Middle East conflict); softer ad spend observed at beginning of Q4 correlating with start of conflict; no material direct revenue exposure to Israel and Middle East

---

## Full Year 2023 Guidance — Expenses & CapEx

- **Total Expenses:** $87B - $89B (lowered from prior range of $88B - $91B)
  - Includes ~$3.5B of restructuring costs related to facilities consolidation charges and severance and other personnel costs
- **Reality Labs Operating Losses:** Expected to increase YoY in 2023
- **Capital Expenditures:** $27B - $29B (updated from prior estimate of $27B - $30B)
- **Q4 2023 Tax Rate:** Expected to be similar to Q3 2023 (~17%)

---

## Full Year 2024 Preliminary Guidance — Expenses

- **Total Expenses:** $94B - $99B
- **Key Expense Growth Drivers:**
  1. Higher infrastructure-related costs: depreciation expenses in 2024 expected to increase by a larger amount than in 2023; higher operating costs from running a larger infrastructure footprint
  2. Payroll growth: spillover of hiring originally planned/budgeted for 2023 into 2024; FoA expected to be a larger source of payroll expense growth than Reality Labs; hiring concentrated in technical roles shifting workforce composition toward higher-cost basis
  3. Reality Labs operating losses: expected to increase meaningfully YoY due to ongoing AR/VR product development efforts and investments to further scale ecosystem
- **Expense Philosophy:** Management committed to earning the ability to invest by delivering consolidated operating income growth over time

---

## Full Year 2024 Preliminary Guidance — CapEx

- **Capital Expenditures:** $30B - $35B
- **Growth Drivers:** Investments in servers (both non-AI and AI hardware) and data centers (ramping up construction on sites with new data center architecture announced late 2022)

---

## Full Year 2024 Preliminary Guidance — Tax Rate

- **Tax Rate:** Expected to be similar to Q3 2023 (~17%)

---

## Full Year 2024 — Headcount Outlook

- **End-of-Year Headcount:** Expected to end 2024 meaningfully higher than current headcount (~66,100)
- **Growth Rate Beyond 2024:** Expected to grow at a slower rate
- **2023 Hiring Underrun:** Operating with significant underrun against 2023 budgeted headcount due to broad-based hiring freeze in late 2022 / early 2023 during restructuring; much of 2023-budgeted hiring expected to occur in 2024
- **2024 Incremental Headcount Priorities:** AI (largest area), infrastructure, Reality Labs, monetization, and regulatory/compliance needs
- **Efficiency Offsets:** Plan to reduce planned hiring in other areas across the company in 2024

---

## AI Strategy & Investment

- **2024 Investment Priority:** AI will be the biggest investment area in 2024, both in engineering and compute resources
- **Headcount Reallocation:** Continuing to deprioritize non-AI projects to shift people toward AI work; avoiding allocating a lot of new headcount
- **Llama 2:** Believed to be the leading open-source model; 30M+ downloads last month
- **Meta AI Assistant:** Rolled out across messaging experiences and smart glasses; answers questions, provides real-time information, generates photorealistic images
- **AI Studio Platform:** Enables people to create and interact with different AIs
- **Emu Image Model:** Produces high-quality images and stickers
- **Business AI:** Early alpha launched so businesses can have AI to interface with customers for sales and support
- **Creator AIs:** Planned launch in 2024 so creators can have AIs that fans can engage with
- **Gen AI Ad Tools (rolling out Q4 2023):**
  - Text variations: generating multiple ad text versions based on original copy
  - Image expansion: adjusting creative assets to fit different aspect ratios across Feed and Reels
  - Background generation: creating multiple backgrounds for product images via Advantage+ catalog ads
- **Open Source Strategy Benefits:** Increases adoption, creates industry standard, drives faster innovation, improves security/safety scrutiny, may reduce costs (similar to Open Compute), attracts top talent

---

## Reels & Video Strategy

- **Reels Status:** Graduated from early initiative to core part of apps; net neutral to overall company ad revenue
- **Future Reels Disclosure:** Will no longer quantify net revenue contribution from Reels going forward
- **2024 Reels Outlook:** Anticipated to be a "modest tailwind" to revenue in 2024
- **Ongoing Work:** Ranking improvements, making Reels ads increasingly interactive, growing ad supply
- **Broader Video Focus:** Going forward, focus on growing revenue and engagement on overall video and more holistically across product lines within Instagram and Facebook

---

## Business Messaging & Commerce

- **Daily Conversations:** 600M+ conversations between people and businesses per day
- **Click-to-WhatsApp Revenue:** Already at multibillion-dollar annual run rate, growing very quickly
- **India Metrics:** 60%+ of WhatsApp users in India message a business weekly; click-to-message ads revenue in India doubled YoY
- **Business AI Testing:** Recently started testing AI capabilities with a few partners for business messaging
- **Paid Messaging:** Serves as complement to click-to-message ads for ongoing customer relationships
- **Shops Ads:** Good early traction; expanded commerce integrations with third-party services in Q3
- **Conversions API:** Investing in easier advertiser adoption; added Google Cloud support (in addition to existing AWS support); introduced enhanced reporting; rolled out Conversions API for business messaging

---

## Metaverse & Reality Labs Hardware

- **Quest 3:** Launched as first mainstream mixed reality device; next-gen chipset with 2x graphics performance vs. Quest 2; best displays yet; 40% thinner form factor than Quest 2; competitively priced; early reviews "great"
- **Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses (next generation):** Upgraded in every way from first generation — better camera, clearer audio, lighter, more styles; can live-stream video; first smart glasses shipping with Meta AI built in
- **Horizon:** Growing faster as more worlds come online (e.g., Super Rumble, Citadel); started testing Horizon for phones, tablets, and PCs
- **Quest 3 Financials:** Not sharing explicit expectations for Q4 Reality Labs revenue or unit sales

---

## Regulatory & Legal Environment

- **FTC Consent Order:** FTC seeking to substantially modify existing consent order and impose additional restrictions on ability to operate; Meta contesting the matter; if unsuccessful, would have adverse impact on business
- **EU Legal Basis for Ads:** Intending to evolve legal basis for processing personal data for ads to a consent model in EU, EEA, and Switzerland; continuing to engage with DPC and other regulatory authorities
- **Increasing Regulatory Headwinds:** Monitoring active regulatory landscape including increasing legal and regulatory headwinds in EU and U.S.

---

## Meta Verified

- **Creators:** Rolled out to most markets globally; positive feedback from creators
- **Businesses:** Recently began testing Meta Verified for businesses on Facebook and Instagram in select countries; plans to expand to businesses on WhatsApp in the future
- **Status:** Very early; limited details to share

## Geopolitical Impact on Q4 Outlook

- **Middle East Conflict:** No material direct revenue exposure to Israel and Middle East; observed softer ad spend at beginning of Q4 correlating with start of conflict
- **Wider Guidance Range:** Reflects greater uncertainty and volatility
- **Historical Precedent:** Broader demand softness has followed other regional conflicts in the past (e.g., Ukraine war)
- **Positive Trends:** Coming into Q4, continued strong advertiser demand in key segments including online commerce and gaming

## Capital Allocation & Shareholder Returns

- **Q3 2023 Share Repurchases:** $3.7B of Class A common stock
- **Cash Position:** $61.1B in cash and marketable securities (end of Q3)
- **Debt:** $18.4B (end of Q3)

## Data Center Architecture

- **New Architecture:** Paused some existing data center construction to switch over to new data center architecture; expected to be more cost efficient; expected to provide greater fungibility in planning for CPU and GPU capacity; cost benefits and efficiencies expected to be realized in years to come
- **2023 CapEx Impact:** 2023 CapEx dynamics partly affected by the pause/transition to new architecture

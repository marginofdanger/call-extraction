# Meta Platforms Q3_2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 29, 2025 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2025 (July - September 2025)

---

## Q3 2025 Actuals

### Consolidated Results

- **Total Revenue:** $51.2B (+26% YoY; +25% on a constant currency basis)
- **Total Expenses:** $30.7B (+32% YoY)
  - YoY expense growth accelerated 20 percentage points from Q2 due to: (1) higher legal-related expense growth (charges recorded in Q3 plus lapping accrual reversals in Q3 prior year), (2) accelerated employee compensation growth driven by technical hires (particularly AI talent), (3) accelerated infrastructure cost growth (expanded data center fleet operating costs, depreciation on incremental CapEx, third-party cloud spend)
- **Operating Income:** $20.5B
- **Operating Margin:** 40%
- **Interest and Other Income:** $1.1B (driven primarily by unrealized gains on marketable equity securities)
- **Tax Rate:** 87% (GAAP; unfavorably impacted by a onetime noncash reduction in deferred tax assets under new U.S. tax law)
- **Tax Rate (Adjusted):** 14% (excluding the onetime tax charge)
- **Net Income:** $2.7B (GAAP)
- **Net Income (Adjusted):** $18.6B (excluding the onetime tax charge)
- **Diluted EPS:** $1.05 (GAAP)
- **Diluted EPS (Adjusted):** $7.25 (excluding the onetime tax charge)
- **Capital Expenditures (including principal payments on finance leases):** $19.4B (driven by investments in servers, data centers, and network infrastructure)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $10.6B
- **Share Repurchases:** $3.2B of Class A common stock
- **Dividends Paid:** $1.3B
- **Headcount:** 78,400+ employees (+8% YoY; driven by hiring in monetization, infrastructure, Reality Labs, Meta Superintelligence Labs, regulation and compliance)

### Balance Sheet Highlights

- **Cash and Marketable Securities:** $44.4B
- **Debt:** $28.8B

### Family of Apps Segment

- **Family of Apps Revenue:** $50.8B (+26% YoY)
- **Family of Apps Ad Revenue:** $50.1B (+26% YoY; +25% on a constant currency basis)
- **Family of Apps Other Revenue:** $690M (+59% YoY; driven by WhatsApp paid messaging revenue growth and Meta Verified subscriptions)
- **Ad Impressions Growth:** +14% YoY (healthy across all regions, driven by engagement and user growth, particularly on video surfaces)
- **Average Price Per Ad:** +10% YoY (benefiting from increased advertiser demand driven by improved ad performance; partially offset by impression growth from lower monetizing regions and surfaces)

### Reality Labs Segment

- **Reality Labs Revenue:** $470M (+74% YoY)
  - Significant YoY growth partly due to retail partners stocking up on Quest headsets ahead of holiday season (no similar Q3 benefit in prior year since Quest 3S launched in Q4 2024); strong AI glasses revenue also contributed

### Engagement & User Metrics

- **Family Daily Active People (DAP):** 3.5B+ (estimated for September 2025)
- **Instagram Monthly Active People:** 3B (milestone reached)
- **Threads Daily Actives:** 150M+ (recently passed; remains on track to become category leader)
- **Meta AI Monthly Active Users:** 1B+
- **Time Spent on Facebook:** +5% in Q3 (driven by AI recommendation systems)
- **Time Spent on Threads:** +10% in Q3 (driven by ranking optimizations in Q3 alone)
- **Video Time Spent on Instagram:** +30%+ YoY
- **U.S. Time Spent on Facebook and Instagram:** double-digit growth YoY (driven by continued video strength and healthy nonvideo time growth on Facebook)

### Ad Platform & Monetization Metrics

- **Reels Annual Run Rate:** $50B+
- **End-to-End AI-Powered Ad Tools Annual Run Rate:** $60B
- **Advantage+ Lead Campaigns:** advertisers seeing 14% lower cost per lead on average vs. non-Advantage+ users
- **Lattice Rollout to App Ads:** drove nearly 3% gain in conversions for that objective
- **Ads Ranking Model Consolidation:** ~100 models cut since introducing Lattice in 2023; expect to consolidate another 200 models over coming years
- **New Runtime Ads Ranking Model (Pilot):** >2% lift in conversions on Instagram in testing
- **Andromeda Improvement:** combining models across retrieval and early-stage ranking into a single model drove 14% increase in ads quality on Facebook surfaces
- **Advantage+ Creative Suite:** number of advertisers using at least 1 video generation feature up 20% vs. prior quarter
- **Click-to-WhatsApp Ads Revenue:** +60% YoY in Q3
- **Business AI Conversations:** millions of conversations between people and business AIs since July (in Philippines and Mexico test markets)
- **AI-Generated Images:** 20B+ images created using Meta products to date
- **Vibes (launched September):** media generation in the app increased more than tenfold since launch
- **Business Messaging Active Threads:** 1B+ active threads daily with business accounts across messaging platforms
- **Facebook Reels Freshness:** systems now surfacing 2x as many Reels published that day compared to start of year

## Q4 2025 Guidance (Next Quarter)

- **Total Revenue:** $56B - $59B
  - Assumes foreign currency is an approximately 1% tailwind to YoY total revenue growth based on current exchange rates
  - Reflects expectation for continued strong ad revenue growth, partially offset by lower YoY Reality Labs revenue in Q4
- **Reality Labs Revenue (Q4):** expected to be lower YoY due to lapping Quest 3S introduction in Q4 2024 and retail partners procuring Quest headsets during Q3 2025; significant YoY growth in AI glasses revenue expected but more than offset by Quest headwinds
- **Tax Rate (Q4 2025):** 12% - 15% (absent changes to tax landscape)

## Full Year 2025 Guidance

- **Total Expenses:** $116B - $118B (updated from prior outlook of $114B - $118B; representing +22% to +24% YoY growth)
- **Capital Expenditures (including principal payments on finance leases):** $70B - $72B (increased from prior outlook of $66B - $72B)

---

## 2026 Preliminary Outlook

- **CapEx Dollar Growth:** expected to be "notably larger" in 2026 than 2025; growth across all core areas (MSL, core AI, non-AI spend) with MSL AI needs growing the most
- **Total Expenses:** expected to grow at a "significantly faster percentage rate" in 2026 than 2025
  - Growth primarily driven by infrastructure costs (incremental cloud expenses and depreciation)
  - Employee compensation costs will be the second largest contributor (full year of compensation for 2025 hires, particularly AI talent, plus adding technical talent in priority areas)
- **Revenue Growth:** management expects investments in ads and organic engagement initiatives will enable continued "strong revenue growth" in 2026
- **Compute Needs:** have "continued to expand meaningfully, including versus our own expectations last quarter"; company plans to invest aggressively both by building own infrastructure and contracting with third-party cloud providers

---

## Long-Term Targets & Strategic Commentary

- **Onetime Tax Charge Impact:** although the transition to the new U.S. tax law resulted in a Q3 accounting charge, management continues to expect "significant cash tax savings for the remainder of the current year and future years" under the new law; the Q3 charge reflects the total expected impact from the transition
- **Infrastructure Strategy:** staging data center sites to "spring up capacity quickly in future years" and establishing strategic partnerships for option value on future compute needs
- **Blue Owl Joint Venture:** Meta will contribute 20% of remaining construction costs for the Louisiana data center (in line with ownership stake), recorded as other investing cash flows rather than CapEx; prior CapEx reflected a portion of build cost before the JV was established

---

## AI Strategy & Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL)

- **Meta Superintelligence Labs:** described as having "the highest talent density lab in the industry"; focused on training next-generation models and novel research
- **Superintelligence Timeline:** management acknowledges a range of timelines ("some people think a few years, others think 5-7 years or longer") and believes the right strategy is to "aggressively front-load building capacity" to be prepared for the most optimistic cases
- **Compute Stance:** Family of Apps and ads business described as operating in a "compute-starved state"; management sees significant profitable investment opportunities not being pursued due to compute constraints
- **Unified AI System Vision:** working on combining the 3 major AI systems (Facebook, Instagram, and ads recommendations) into a single unified AI system to improve trillions of daily recommendations
- **2026 Recommendation System Roadmap:**
  - Evolving Instagram systems to surface content across broader set of topics (following a similar approach implemented on Facebook)
  - Significant progress expected on longer-term foundational ranking model innovations; will "significantly scale up the amount of data and compute" used to train recommendation models
  - Leveraging LLMs to improve content understanding (more precise labeling of keywords and topics within videos and posts)

---

## Advertising Platform Innovation

- **Advantage+ Streamlined Campaign Creation:** completed global rollout for lead campaigns in Q3; now covers sales, app, and lead campaign objectives with end-to-end automation from the beginning
- **Advantage+ Adoption Opportunity:** many advertisers still only use end-to-end automated solutions for a portion of their campaigns; working to broaden adoption among advertisers using only single-step automated solutions
- **AI-Generated Music for Ads:** introduced in Q3 so advertisers can have music generated for their ads aligned with the tone and message of the creative
- **Ads Revenue Investment Pipeline (2026):** management sees a "similar list of revenue investments" for the 2026 budget process as the road map that paid off in 2025
- **Value-Weighted Conversion Rates:** growing strongly YoY; weighted conversions continue to grow faster than impressions

---

## Business Messaging & Business AI

- **Click-to-WhatsApp Ads:** revenue grew 60% YoY in Q3
- **Business AI Rollout:** expanded availability in October to all eligible businesses in Mexico (WhatsApp) and Philippines (Messenger)
- **U.S. Business AI:** starting to roll out ability for merchants to add Business AIs to their website to support the full sales funnel from ad to purchase
- **WhatsApp Status Ads:** continuing gradual introduction; expect to complete rollout next year
- **Threads Ads:** now running globally in feed; following typical monetization playbook of optimizing formats and performance before ramping supply
- **Threads Direct Messaging:** launched in Q3

---

## Wearables & Reality Labs Hardware

- **Ray-Ban Meta Glasses & Oakley Meta Vanguards:** both selling well; improved battery life, camera resolution, new AI capabilities
- **Meta Ray-Ban Display Glasses:** first glasses with high-resolution display; sold out in almost every store within 48 hours; demo slots fully booked through end of next month; company investing in increasing manufacturing
- **Meta Neural Band:** new interface device for display glasses
- **Orion (Full Field-of-View AR Prototype):** continuing investment; described as earlier in its curve towards becoming a sustaining business
- **Long-Term Hardware Goal:** management aims to reach "many hundreds of millions or billions of people" for wearables to become an "extremely profitable business"

---

## Legal & Regulatory Risks

- **EU Less Personalized Ads:** continuing to engage constructively with European Commission; cannot rule out Commission imposing further changes that "could have a significant negative impact on our European revenue as early as this quarter" (Q4 2025)
- **U.S. Youth-Related Trials:** a number of trials scheduled for 2026 that "may ultimately result in a material loss"
- **General:** management notes "increasing headwinds in the EU and the U.S. that could significantly impact our business and financial results"

---

## Capital Allocation

- **Blue Owl JV Structure:** codevelop data centers with external capital providers; Meta contributes 20% of remaining construction costs (in line with ownership stake); provides long-term optionality for future capacity needs given magnitude and uncertainty of capacity outlook
- **External Compute Demand:** management notes "almost every week, people come to us from outside the company asking us to stand up an API service or asking if we have different compute"; noted as potential option if infrastructure is overbuilt
- **Budget Process:** 2026 budget described as still in relatively early stages; capacity planning is "a particularly dynamic process"

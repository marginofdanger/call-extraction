# Meta Platforms Q3_2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** October 30, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q3 2024 (July-September 2024)

---

## Q3 2024 Actuals — Consolidated Results

- **Revenue:** $40.6B (+19% YoY; +20% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **Total Expenses:** $23.2B (+14% YoY)
- **Operating Income:** $17.4B
- **Operating Margin:** 43%
- **Tax Rate:** 12%
- **Net Income:** $15.7B
- **Diluted EPS:** $6.03
- **Capital Expenditures (including principal payments on finance leases):** $9.2B (impacted in part by timing of Q3 server deliveries, with cash payments pushed to Q4)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $15.5B
- **Headcount:** 72,404 employees (+9% YoY; growth driven by hiring in monetization, infrastructure, Reality Labs, generative AI, regulation and compliance)

### Expense Line Items (YoY)
- **Cost of Revenue:** +19% YoY (driven primarily by higher infrastructure costs)
- **R&D:** +21% YoY (driven by higher headcount-related expenses and infrastructure costs)
- **Marketing and Sales:** -2% YoY (driven primarily by lower restructuring costs)
- **G&A:** -10% YoY (driven primarily by lower legal-related expenses)

### Balance Sheet Highlights
- **Cash and Marketable Securities:** $70.9B
- **Debt:** $28.8B (completed $10.5B debt offering in Q3)

### Capital Return
- **Share Repurchases:** $8.9B of Class A common stock in Q3
- **Dividends Paid:** $1.3B in Q3

---

## Q3 2024 Actuals — Family of Apps Segment

- **Family Daily Active People (DAP):** 3.29B (more than 3.2B people using at least one app daily in September)
- **Family of Apps Revenue:** $40.3B (+19% YoY)
- **Family of Apps Ad Revenue:** $39.9B (+19% YoY; +20% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **Family of Apps Other Revenue:** $434M (+48% YoY; driven primarily by business messaging revenue growth from WhatsApp Business platform)
- **Family of Apps Expenses:** $18.5B (+13% YoY; ~80% of overall expenses; driven by higher infrastructure and headcount-related expenses, partially offset by lower legal-related expenses)
- **Family of Apps Operating Income:** $21.8B
- **Family of Apps Operating Margin:** 54%

### Ad Revenue by User Geography (YoY Growth)
- **Rest of World:** +23%
- **Europe:** +21%
- **Asia Pacific:** +18%
- **North America:** +16%

### Ad Revenue by Advertiser Geography (YoY Growth)
- **North America:** +21%
- **Europe:** +21%
- **Rest of World:** +17%
- **Asia Pacific:** +15% (decelerating from +28% in Q2 due to lapping a period of stronger demand from China-based advertisers)

### Ad Revenue Vertical Contribution (YoY Growth)
- **Online Commerce:** largest contributor to YoY growth
- **Health Care:** second largest contributor
- **Entertainment and Media:** third largest contributor

### Ad Monetization Metrics
- **Ad Impressions Growth:** +7% YoY (driven mainly by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
- **Average Price Per Ad:** +11% YoY (driven by increased advertiser demand and improved ad performance; partially offset by impression growth from lower monetizing regions and services; accelerated slightly from +10% in Q2, in part due to lower impression growth in Q3)

---

## Q3 2024 Actuals — Reality Labs Segment

- **Reality Labs Revenue:** $270M (+29% YoY; driven by hardware sales)
- **Reality Labs Expenses:** $4.7B (+19% YoY; driven primarily by higher headcount-related expenses and infrastructure costs)
- **Reality Labs Operating Loss:** $4.4B

---

## Q4 2024 Guidance

- **Revenue:** $45.0B to $48.0B (midpoint: $46.5B)
- **FX Assumption:** approximately neutral to YoY total revenue growth based on current exchange rates
- **Tax Rate:** expected in the low teens
- **Implied Q4 CapEx:** ~$15B to $17B (based on full year CapEx guidance of $38B-$40B less ~$23B spent in Q1-Q3; step-up driven by increases in server spend and, to a lesser extent, data center CapEx, plus timing of Q3 server deliveries with cash payments flowing in Q4)

---

## Full Year 2024 Guidance

- **Total Expenses:** $96.0B to $98.0B (narrowed from prior range of $96.0B to $99.0B)
- **Capital Expenditures:** $38.0B to $40.0B (updated from prior range of $37.0B to $40.0B; low end raised by $1B)
- **Reality Labs Operating Losses:** expected to increase meaningfully YoY in 2024 due to ongoing product development efforts and investments to further scale the ecosystem

---

## 2025 Directional Outlook

- **CapEx:** significant capital expenditure growth expected in 2025 (specific range not provided; budgeting process still in progress)
- **Infrastructure Expense Growth:** significant acceleration expected in 2025 given back end-weighted nature of 2024 CapEx; higher growth in depreciation and operating expenses of expanded infrastructure fleet
- **Headcount:** no specific guidance provided; budgeting process in progress; growth areas include monetization, infrastructure, Reality Labs, gen AI, regulation and compliance; efficiency efforts continue across the company
- **Threads Monetization:** not expected to be a meaningful driver of 2025 revenue

## Engagement and Product Metrics

### Family of Apps Engagement
- **Family DAP:** more than 3.2B daily (September 2024)
- **Facebook Time Spent:** +8% increase in 2024 (driven by AI-driven feed and video recommendations)
- **Instagram Time Spent:** +6% increase in 2024 (driven by AI-driven feed and video recommendations)
- **Facebook Video Player Time Spent:** +10% increase since introducing new unified video player experience and prediction systems (global rollout in June)
- **Instagram Original Content:** more than 60% of recommendations in the U.S. now coming from original posts

### Threads
- **Monthly Actives:** ~275M
- **Sign-ups:** more than 1M per day
- **Key Growth Markets in Q3:** U.S., Taiwan, Japan

### Meta AI
- **Monthly Actives:** more than 500M
- **Trajectory:** on track to be the most used AI assistant in the world by end of year
- **Key Use Cases:** information gathering, how-to tasks (largest use case), exploring interests, content search, image generation
- **Search Partners:** integrated with Bing and Google
- **Voice:** launched in English for U.S., Australia, Canada, New Zealand
- **Photo Upload:** available in the U.S. for learning about images, writing captions, editing images via text prompt
- **Foundation Model:** built on Llama 3.2 (first multimodal foundation model)

### WhatsApp
- **U.S. Growth:** one of the fastest-growing countries
- **Global Daily Calls:** 2B+ calls made per day (milestone)
- **Business Messaging:** strong growth in paid conversations; driven by both growth in number of businesses adopting paid messaging and conversational volume per business
- **Click-to-WhatsApp Ads:** particularly strong growth; scaling in markets like Brazil; early but growing in the U.S.

## AI Strategy and Llama Development

### Llama Ecosystem
- **Llama Token Usage:** grown "exponentially" in 2024
- **Llama 3.2 Release:** includes leading small models that run on device and open-source multimodal models
- **Enterprise and Government Adoption:** working with enterprises and U.S. government for Llama adoption
- **Ecosystem Benefits:** NVIDIA and AMD optimizing chips to run Llama specifically well; community contributions improve quality and efficiency

### Llama 4 Development
- **Training Cluster:** bigger than 100,000 H100s (described as bigger than anything reported by others)
- **Smaller Llama 4 Models:** expected to be ready early 2025
- **Capabilities:** new modalities, stronger reasoning, much faster

### Gen AI Advertising Tools
- **Advertiser Adoption:** more than 1M advertisers used gen AI tools in last month
- **Ad Volume:** more than 15M ads created with gen AI tools in last month
- **Conversion Uplift:** businesses using image generation seeing estimated 7% increase in conversions
- **Tools Available:** image expansion, background generation, text generation
- **Video Generation:** began testing video expansion and image animation; expected to be more broadly available by early 2025
- **New Ads Modeling:** 2% to 4% increase in conversions from new sequential action modeling techniques deployed in H1 2024

---

## Ads Platform Evolution

- **Advantage+ Solutions:** continued momentum with ad creative tools
- **Sequential Action Modeling:** new learning and modeling techniques enabling ad systems to consider the sequence of actions before and after seeing an ad (previously could only aggregate actions); deployed in H1 2024 with 2%-4% conversion increase in selected segments
- **Cross-Publisher Journey:** Q3 changes to ads ranking and optimization models to account for more of the cross-publisher journey, expected to increase Meta-attributed conversions visible in third-party analytics
- **Incremental Conversion Optimization:** testing new features allowing advertisers to optimize for incremental conversions rather than absolute conversions

---

## Reality Labs and Wearables

### Ray-Ban Meta Glasses
- **Demand:** "very strong"; clear edition sold out almost immediately and was trading online for over $1,000
- **AI Integration:** Meta AI integration with real-time multimodal AI, language translation, visual recognition
- **Partnership:** deepened partnership with EssilorLuxottica for future generations of smart eyewear

### Orion AR Glasses
- **Status:** first full holographic AR glasses shown at Connect; ~10 years in development
- **Timeline:** not far from delivering great-looking glasses to blend physical and digital worlds

### Quest 3S
- **Price Point:** $300 (more accessible than Quest 3)
- **Features:** high-quality color pass-through, new chipset
- **Reviews:** "great so far"
- **Timing:** launched ahead of holiday season

---

## Recommendations and Content Ranking Roadmap

- **New Ranking Architecture:** developed last year, inspired by scaling laws from large language models; capable of learning from significantly larger data sets
- **Facebook Video Ranking:** new architectures deployed; delivering more relevant recommendations and unlocking meaningful gains in watch time
- **Cross-Surface Data:** future phase will introduce cross-surface data so systems can learn from one app surface to improve recommendations on another
- **Engineering Efficiency Goal:** operate a smaller number of recommendation systems over time while improving relevance

---

## Capital Allocation Philosophy

- **Long-term View:** investing in a portfolio of opportunities expected to generate returns over different time periods
- **Near-term Investments:** continued healthy revenue growth in core business
- **Longer-term Investments:** infrastructure and talent to accelerate progress and maximize returns
- **Infrastructure Flexibility:** prioritizing fungibility of infrastructure capacity to respond to technology and market developments
- **Reality Labs Pivot:** evolved road map to respond to earlier-than-expected success of smart glasses

---

## Regulatory and Macro Environment

- **Regulatory Landscape:** monitoring increasing legal and regulatory headwinds in the EU and the U.S. that could significantly impact business and financial results
- **Olympics Impact:** historically have not seen meaningful incremental contribution from events like the Olympics; believed to be largely the case in 2024 as well
- **Macro Outlook:** growth expected to continue benefiting from healthy global advertising demand; wide range of possible macro backdrops reflected in revenue guidance range

## Q&A Highlights — Additional Quantitative and Qualitative Detail

### Internal AI Productivity (Justin Post, BofA)
- **Internal Coding Agent:** seeing "a lot of adoption" internally of Meta's internal assistant and coding agent
- **Content Moderation:** hope to deploy AI tools over time to make content moderation more efficient and effective
- **Other Areas:** relatively early in exploring LLM-based tools for various work streams across the company

### 2025 Headcount and Budgeting (Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley)
- **Budget Process:** still in progress; changed forward-looking guidance approach to give guidance on the next call
- **Investment Areas:** monetization, infrastructure, Reality Labs, gen AI, regulation and compliance
- **Evaluation Framework:** ROI-driven for core business opportunities; strategic opportunity assessment for longer-term areas

### CapEx Lumpiness (Doug Anmuth, JPMorgan)
- **Q4 Step-up:** driven by server spend and data center CapEx; timing dynamics from late Q3 server deliveries
- **Quarterly Extrapolation:** "quite a bit of lumpiness quarter-to-quarter" making it hard to extrapolate from any particular quarter

### AI-Generated Content in Feeds (Ken Gawrelski, Wells Fargo)
- **Testing:** starting to test AI-generated/AI-summarized content in feeds
- **2025 Impact:** not expected to be a big impact on the business in 2025
- **Long-term View:** high confidence that AI-generated content will be an important trend over the next several years

### Reality Labs Losses (Youssef Squali, Truist)
- **2023 Reality Labs Losses:** ~$16B (referenced by analyst)
- **2024 Reality Labs Losses:** expected north of $20B (analyst estimate; management confirmed expected to increase meaningfully YoY)
- **Peak Losses:** no guidance provided on timing of peak losses; Reality Labs remains a "strategic long-term priority" with significant investment expected

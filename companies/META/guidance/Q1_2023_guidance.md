- **Free Cash Flow:** $6.9B

- **Cash and Marketable Securities (end of quarter):** $37.4B

- **Cost of Revenue:** +2% YoY (driven by infrastructure-related costs, partially offset by lower Reality Labs COGS)

- **Family of Apps Revenue:** $28.3B (+4% YoY)
- **Family of Apps Ad Revenue:** $28.1B (+4% YoY, +7% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **Family of Apps Other Revenue:** $205M (-5% YoY; strong WhatsApp business messaging revenue growth offset by declines in other line items)

### Ad Revenue by User Geography (YoY Growth)

- **Rest of World:** +9% YoY (strongest growth)

## Long-Term Targets & Strategic Commentary

### Reels Revenue Trajectory

- **Reels Revenue Neutral Timeline:** Expected to become neutral to overall revenue by end of 2023 or early 2024
- **Reels Monetization Parity:** No line of sight to monetization parity per time with Feed or Stories "anytime soon" due to structural differences (longer viewing time per reel = fewer ad slots between posts); however, incremental engagement growth expected to drive positive revenue contribution beyond neutrality

### Capital Structure

- **Capital Structure Evolution:** Management expects to "modestly evolve" capital structure over time to improve overall cost of capital by periodically accessing debt markets to diversify funding sources while maintaining a positive or neutral net cash balance over time

### Headcount & Efficiency

- **Restructuring Waves:** 2 of 3 planned waves completed (recruiting and technical groups); third wave across business groups planned for May 2023
- **2024 Headcount Growth:** Expected in excess of 1% to 2% as hiring resumes after broad-based hiring freeze
- **Hiring Priority Areas:** Generative AI, ads, infrastructure, Reality Labs
- **Post-Restructuring Focus (rest of 2023):** Improving distributed work model, delivering AI tools for productivity, removing unnecessary processes

### CapEx Investment Framework (Three Buckets)

- **Bucket 1 - Non-AI Compute:** Ongoing general compute and storage needs; area of improving capital efficiency
- **Bucket 2 - Core AI:** Majority of current AI investment; supports discovery engine, ranking unconnected organic content, ranking ads; focused on measuring ROI
- **Bucket 3 - Generative AI:** Emerging investment area; earlier on the return curve; may represent "significant investment opportunity"; likely driver of incremental CapEx spend in the near future

## AI Strategy

### Recommendations & Ranking Infrastructure

- **Discovery Engine Impact:** 20%+ of Facebook and Instagram Feed content now AI-recommended from non-followed accounts; ~40% across all of Instagram
- **Instagram Engagement Lift:** +24% time spent since launching Reels (driven by AI ranking improvements)
- **Reels Monetization Efficiency Gains:** +30% on Instagram, +40% on Facebook (QoQ)

### Generative AI & Foundation Models

- **LLaMA Release:** 65B parameter model released to researchers in Q1; outperforms larger models
- **Open Source Visual Models Released:** Segment Anything, DINOv2, Animated Drawings (with training data and model weights)
- **Open Source Strategy:** Meta incentivized toward open ecosystem for foundational AI models/tools (contrasted with cloud providers); analogous to PyTorch and Open Compute Project approach
- **Product Applications:** AI agents for WhatsApp and Messenger chat, visual creation tools for Facebook/Instagram posts and ads, video and multimodal experiences planned

### Business Messaging & AI Agents

- **AI Agent Opportunity:** Enabling tens of millions of small businesses to deploy AI agents for customer engagement in chat; expected to unlock adoption for businesses that cannot afford human staffing for messaging
- **Click-to-Message Growth Vectors:** Investing in easier ad creation (revamped Ads Manager, native WhatsApp ad creation), cloud-hosted API across WhatsApp/Messenger/Instagram, ranking and optimization for better conversion, in-thread payments, and commerce tools
- **Geographic Expansion:** Core markets today are Southeast Asia and Latin America; strong growth emerging in U.S. and Canada

---

## Metaverse / Reality Labs Strategy

- **Commitment:** Management explicitly stated Meta is "not moving away" from metaverse vision; continuing to focus on both AI and metaverse
- **AI-Metaverse Convergence:** Computer vision enabled standalone VR; mixed reality built on AI technologies; procedural world generation important for experiences at scale; AR glasses vision involves AI-centric OS
- **Next-Generation Device:** New consumer virtual and mixed reality device planned for launch later in 2023 (successor to Quest 2, launched ~3 years prior); described as accessible price point
- **Reality Labs Losses:** Expected to increase YoY in 2023; management committed to continued investment

## Regulatory Environment

### EU Data Transfers (IDPC)

- **Expected Decision:** IDPC expected to issue decision in May relating to transatlantic data transfers of Facebook EU/EEA user data, including a suspension order and fine
- **Mitigation:** Ongoing consultations indicate proposed EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework expected to be fully implemented before suspension deadline, but cannot exclude possibility it will not be completed in time
- **Revenue at Risk:** ~10% of worldwide ad revenue comes from ads delivered to Facebook users in EU countries (additional advertiser impact variables would need to be assessed)

## Advertising Demand & Macro Commentary

- **Q1 Acceleration Drivers:** Stronger demand, lapping the Ukraine war onset (Q1 2022), acceleration from China-based advertisers targeting international users (due to dropping shipping costs and easing COVID lockdowns), FX dynamics
- **Q2 Tailwinds:** Easier comps from lapping weaker demand period; first full quarter without Russia revenue in comparison (vs. Q2 2022); expected reduced FX headwind
- **Macro Risks:** Volatile macro environment; inflation, higher interest rates, banking instability absorbed over the past year; hard to predict impacts on advertising market
- **Post-ATT Positioning:** Investing in Conversions API, on-site conversion objectives (click-to-message ads, lead ads, shops ads), and AI-powered ad systems to mitigate signal loss; company "quite well positioned" in current signals landscape

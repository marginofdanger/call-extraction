# Meta Platforms Q4_2025 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** January 28, 2026 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q4 2025 (Full Year 2025)

---

## Q4 2025 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $59.9B (+24% YoY; +23% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **Family of Apps Revenue:** $58.9B (+25% YoY)
- **Family of Apps Ad Revenue:** $58.1B (+24% YoY; +23% YoY on constant currency basis)
- **Family of Apps Other Revenue:** $801M (+54% YoY; driven by WhatsApp paid messaging revenue growth and Meta Verified subscriptions)
- **Reality Labs Revenue:** $955M (-12% YoY; decline due to lapping Quest 3S introduction in Q4 2024 and retail partners procuring Quest headsets in Q3 2025 ahead of holiday season)
- **Total Expenses:** $35.1B (+40% YoY; driven primarily by employee compensation, legal expenses and infrastructure costs)
- **Operating Income:** $24.7B
- **Operating Margin:** 41%
- **Interest and Other Income:** $609M (driven primarily by unrealized gains on equity investments)
- **Tax Rate:** 10% (lower than outlook of 12%--15% due to settlement of matters with tax authorities)
- **Net Income:** $22.8B
- **Diluted EPS:** $8.88
- **CapEx:** $22.1B (including principal payments on finance leases; driven by investments in data centers, servers and network infrastructure)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $14.1B
- **Cash and Marketable Securities:** $81.6B (end of quarter)
- **Debt:** $58.7B (end of quarter)
- **Headcount:** over 78,800 employees (+6% YoY; driven by hiring in monetization, infrastructure, Meta Superintelligence Labs, regulation and compliance)

### Q4 Engagement Metrics
- **Family DAP:** more than 3.5B people using at least one app daily (in December)
- **Facebook Daily Actives:** more than 2B
- **WhatsApp Daily Actives:** more than 2B
- **Instagram Daily Actives:** just shy of 2B
- **Ad Impressions:** +18% YoY (healthy across all regions; driven primarily by engagement and user growth and, to a lesser degree, ad load optimizations)
- **Average Price Per Ad:** +6% YoY (benefiting from increased advertiser demand, largely driven by improved ad performance)

### Q4 Ad Monetization & Product Metrics
- **Instagram Reels Watch Time:** +30% YoY in the U.S.
- **Facebook Video Time:** continued double-digit YoY growth in the U.S.
- **Facebook Organic Feed & Video Post Views:** +7% lift from Q4 ranking/product optimizations (largest quarterly revenue impact from Facebook product launches in past two years)
- **Facebook Reels Content Freshness:** systems surfacing over 25% more reels published that day vs. prior quarter
- **Instagram Original Content Prevalence (U.S.):** grew 10 percentage points in Q4; 75% of recommendations now from original posts
- **Threads Time Spent:** +20% lift from Q4 recommendation improvements
- **Video Generation Tools Combined Revenue Run Rate:** $10B in Q4 (QoQ growth outpacing overall ads revenue increase by nearly 3x)
- **Incremental Attribution Feature:** 24% increase in incremental conversions vs. standard attribution model; achieved multibillion-dollar annual run rate just 7 months since launching
- **GEM Model GPU Cluster:** doubled the number of GPUs used to train GEM model for ads ranking in Q4
- **GEM + Sequence Learning Improvements:** 3.5% lift in ad clicks on Facebook; more than 1% gain in conversions on Instagram in Q4
- **Instagram Runtime Model Launch:** 3% increase in conversion rates in Q4 (across Instagram feed, stories and reels)
- **Facebook Lattice Model Consolidation:** consolidated Facebook Stories and other services into overall Facebook model; drove 12% increase in ads quality
- **Click-to-Message Ads (U.S.):** revenue growth accelerated in Q4; U.S. up more than 50% YoY (driven by strong adoption of website-to-message ads)
- **WhatsApp Paid Messaging:** crossed $2B annual run rate in Q4
- **Business AIs:** over 1M weekly conversations between people and business AIs on messaging platforms (in Mexico and the Philippines)
- **Meta AI:** available in over 200 markets

### Q4 Advertiser Vertical Performance
- **Overall:** healthy YoY growth across all verticals except politics (lapping U.S. presidential election)
- **Online Commerce:** largest contributor to YoY growth; YoY growth relatively consistent with Q3 levels; broad-based across advertiser regions and sizes
- **Professional Services:** second-largest contributor; strong broad-based growth with nice contributions from lead generation ads (including Advantage+ lead campaigns fully rolled out at start of Q4)
- **Technology:** third-largest contributor; continued strength; broad-based across advertiser regions and sizes

### Q4 Internal AI Productivity
- **Engineer Output Growth:** +30% increase since beginning of 2025 (majority from adoption of agentic coding, which saw a big jump in Q4)
- **Power Users of AI Coding Tools:** output increased 80% YoY
- **AI Dubbing:** supporting 9 different languages with hundreds of millions of people watching AI-translated videos every day
- **Meta AI Edits App:** nearly 10% of reels people view each day created in Edits app (almost tripling from prior quarter)
- **Meta AI Media Generation DAUs:** tripled YoY in Q4

### Q4 Infrastructure & Silicon
- **Andromeda Ads Retrieval Engine:** extended to run on NVIDIA, AMD and MTIA; nearly tripled compute efficiency (along with model innovations)
- **GEM Coverage:** extended to cover Facebook Reels in Q4; now covers all major surfaces across Facebook and Instagram

## Full Year 2025 Actuals
- **Family DAP:** more than 3.5B (December figure)
- **Revenue:** $59.9B in Q4 (full year total not explicitly stated; Q4 was up 24% YoY)
- **Glasses Sales:** more than tripled YoY in 2025
- **Share Repurchases:** no stock repurchased in Q4 2025 (prioritizing AI investment; first quarter without buybacks in approximately one year)
- **Reality Labs Operating Losses:** disclosed as the baseline for 2026 guidance (expected similar in 2026; described as likely peak)

---

## Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2026)
- **Revenue:** $53.5B--$56.5B (midpoint: $55.0B)
- **FX Assumption:** approximately 4% tailwind to YoY total revenue growth (based on current exchange rates; 3 percentage points larger tailwind than Q4 2025)
- **Revenue Growth Drivers:** strong advertiser demand continuing from end of Q4 into start of 2026; ad performance improvements including advances to ads ranking and delivery systems, more effective redistribution of ad load, new features and ad products like Advantage+, and better measurement
- **Revenue Growth Context:** outlook reflects expectation for accelerated growth; range embeds outlook for accelerated growth underpinned by strong demand through end of Q4 and into start of 2026

---

## Full Year 2026 Guidance
- **Total Expenses:** $162B--$169B (majority of expense growth driven by infrastructure costs including third-party cloud spend, higher depreciation and higher infrastructure operating expenses; second-largest contributor is employee compensation driven by investments in technical talent including 2026 hires to support AI and full year of expenses from 2025 headcount levels)
- **CapEx:** $115B--$135B (including principal payments on finance leases; YoY growth driven by increased investment to support Meta Superintelligence Labs efforts and core business)
- **Operating Income:** expected to be above 2025 operating income in absolute dollars (not growth rate comparison; management clarified this compares absolute dollars, not YoY growth rates)
- **Tax Rate:** 13%--16% (absent any changes to tax landscape)
- **Segment Expense Growth:** driven by Family of Apps; Reality Labs operating losses expected to remain similar to 2025 levels
- **Reality Labs Losses:** expected similar to 2025; described by Zuckerberg as likely the peak, with gradual reduction expected going forward
- **Revenue Growth Context:** management expects both full year reported and constant currency revenue growth to be below Q1 2026 levels due to: (1) currency tailwinds expected to dissipate later in the year based on current rates, (2) lapping stronger periods of growth later in the year that benefited from 2025 ad performance investments and strong macro landscape, (3) potential headwinds from revised less personalized ads offering in the EU beginning to roll out later in Q1
- **Compute Capacity:** expect significantly more capacity in 2026 as cloud is added; likely still constrained through much of 2026 until additional capacity from own facilities comes online later in the year
- **Share Repurchases:** levels will vary; highest order priority is investing to position as a leader in AI; will continue to be opportunistic on buybacks
- **Financing:** will continue to look for opportunities to supplement operating cash flow with cost-efficient external financing; may eventually maintain a positive net debt balance

---

## Long-Term Targets & Strategic Direction
- **Operating Profit Growth Framework:** continue to use framework of growing consolidated operating profit over time to guide investments (shared several years ago)
- **Reality Labs Investment Direction:** directing most investment towards glasses and wearables; focusing on making Horizon a massive success on mobile; making VR a profitable ecosystem over coming years; losses expected to gradually reduce going forward from 2025 peak
- **Ads Revenue Dominance:** ads expected to be by far the most important driver of growth for the next couple of years
- **Beyond Ads:** focused on opportunities beyond ads including subscriptions, licensing; new products will take time to be meaningful at ads business scale

---

## AI Strategy & Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL)
- **MSL Progress:** approximately 6 months into building MSL; management pleased with quality of team; described as "most talent-dense research effort in the industry"; early indicators look positive
- **Frontier Model Path:** building multiple models over time across many different products; initial set of models and products to roll out over coming months; first models expected to be good but more importantly will show the rapid trajectory; expect to steadily push the frontier over the course of 2026
- **Personal Super Intelligence Vision:** building personal AI that understands personal context including history, interests, content and relationships; Meta believes it can provide a uniquely personal experience
- **LLM + Recommendation Systems:** working on merging LLMs with recommendation systems powering Facebook, Instagram, Threads and ad system; longer-term research project expected to yield dividends over a long period
- **Meta AI Personalization:** early testing shows personalized responses drive higher levels of engagement; expect to significantly advance personalization of Meta AI in 2026
- **Content Understanding:** investments will enable deeper understanding of each person's interests and preferences while identifying most relevant content to pull into responses

---

## Engagement & Recommendation Systems Roadmap (2026)
- **Model Scaling:** plan to continue scaling up models and increase amount of training data including longer history of content interactions
- **Ad Signal Validation:** starting to validate use of ad signals in organic content recommendations; working towards more shared platform for organic and ads recommendations over time
- **Real-Time Adaptation:** making recommendations more adaptive to what a person is engaging with during their session
- **LLM Integration:** more deeply incorporating LLMs into existing recommendation systems for better content understanding; particularly useful for recently posted content with less engagement data
- **GEM 2026 Plans:** meaningfully scale up GEM training to even larger cluster; increase model complexity; expand training data; leverage new sequence learning architecture; further improve transfer of learnings from GEM foundation models to runtime models
- **Lattice Model Consolidation:** in 2026 expect to consolidate more models than in prior two years; evolving towards running smaller number of highly capable models
- **First Scalable Recommendation Architecture:** described as first recommendation model architecture that can scale with similar efficiency as LLMs; expected to unlock ability to significantly scale up ranking models while preserving attractive ROI

---

## Ad Products & Business Messaging Roadmap (2026)
- **Meta AI Business Assistant:** started testing with advertisers in Q4; helps with campaign optimization and account support; to be made available to more advertisers in coming months with personalized recommendations
- **Threads Ads Expansion:** beginning to expand ads to all remaining countries this month (January 2026) including U.K., European Union and Brazil
- **WhatsApp Ads in Status:** expect to complete rollout throughout 2026; level of ads remaining low in near term while optimizing ad formats and performance before ramping inventory
- **Business AIs Expansion:** will expand availability to more markets; extending capabilities beyond answering questions to helping people get things done within WhatsApp
- **Agentic Shopping Tools:** allow people to find specific products from businesses in catalog; focused on making experiences work across both feeds and business messaging
- **Advantage+ Lead Campaigns:** fully rolled out at start of Q4 2025; driving strong contributions in professional services vertical

---

## Reality Labs & Glasses
- **Glasses Sales Growth:** more than tripled in 2025; described as "some of the fastest-growing consumer electronics in history"
- **Investment Focus:** directing most Reality Labs investment toward glasses and wearables going forward
- **Horizon Worlds on Mobile:** focused on making Horizon a massive success on mobile; AI combined with Horizon could open new areas in gaming and new forms of communication
- **VR Profitability:** focused on making VR a profitable ecosystem over coming years
- **Q4 Revenue Decline Context:** Reality Labs Q4 revenue of $955M (-12% YoY) due to lapping Quest 3S introduction in Q4 2024 and retail partners procuring Quest headsets in Q3 2025

---

## Infrastructure & Meta Compute
- **Meta Compute:** announced initiative with belief that being most efficient at engineering, investing and partnering to build infrastructure will become a strategic advantage
- **Leadership Hire:** Dina Powell McCormick joined as President and Vice Chairman to lead efforts to partner with governments, sovereigns and strategic capital partners to expand long-term capacity
- **Silicon Strategy:** deploying variety of chips that optimally support each different workload; extended Andromeda to run on NVIDIA, AMD and MTIA
- **MTIA Expansion:** in Q1 2026 will extend MTIA program to support core ranking and recommendation training workloads in addition to inference workloads
- **Long-Term Silicon & Energy:** making long-term investments; continuing to work with key partners while advancing own silicon program; architecting flexible systems; expect cost per gigawatt to decrease significantly over time
- **Capacity Flexibility:** changing how data center sites are developed; establishing strategic partnerships; contracting cloud capacity; establishing new ownership structures for some large data center sites
- **Compute Constraints:** continue to be capacity constrained; demands for compute increased even faster than supply through 2025; expect significantly more capacity in 2026 from cloud additions but likely still constrained through much of 2026

---

## Regulatory & Legal
- **EU Less Personalized Ads:** aligned with European Commission on further changes; will begin rolling out in Q1 2026; potential headwind to revenue later in the year
- **Legal and Regulatory Headwinds:** continue to monitor legal and regulatory headwinds in both EU and U.S. that could significantly impact business and financial results
- **Youth-Related Issues:** continued scrutiny; number of trials scheduled for 2026 in the U.S.; may ultimately result in a material loss
- **Q4 Legal Expenses:** growth driven by both lapping legal accrual reversals in Q4 2024 and charges recorded in Q4 2025

---

## Capital Allocation
- **Manus Acquisition:** noted by Zuckerberg as example of strategic acquisition; significant number of businesses already pay subscription to use Manus tool to accelerate business results; integrating into ads and business managers to offer more integrated solutions for millions of businesses on Meta platforms
- **Share Repurchases:** no stock repurchased in Q4 2025; highest-order priority for capital is investing to position as leader in AI; will continue to be opportunistic on buybacks vs. other uses of cash
- **External Financing:** will continue to look for opportunities to supplement operating cash flow with cost-efficient external financing; may eventually maintain a positive net debt balance
- **Net Cash Position:** strong net cash balance; expect business will continue to generate sufficient cash to fund 2026 infrastructure investments

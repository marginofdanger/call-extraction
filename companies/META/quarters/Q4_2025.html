What Changed vs Q3 2025

  • Raised FY Total Expenses: $162B - $169B (prior: $116B - $118B)
  • Changed Ad Impressions Growth: +18% YoY (prior: +14% YoY)
  • Changed Avg Price Per Ad: +6% YoY (prior: +10% YoY)
  • Changed Operating Margin: 41% (prior: 40%)

Quarterly Guidance (Q1 2026)

  • Revenue: $53.5B--$56.5B (midpoint: $55.0B)
  • FX Assumption: ~4% tailwind to YoY revenue growth (3pp larger than Q4 2025)
  • Revenue Growth Drivers: strong advertiser demand continuing into 2026; ad ranking/delivery advances, ad load optimization, Advantage+, better measurement
  • Revenue Growth Context: accelerated growth outlook underpinned by strong demand through Q4 and into 2026

Full Year 2026 Guidance

  • Total Expenses: $162B--$169B (driven by infrastructure costs + employee compensation for AI talent)
  • CapEx: $115B--$135B (incl. finance leases; growth driven by MSL + core business investment)
  • Operating Income: expected above 2025 in absolute dollars
  • Tax Rate: 13%--16% (absent any changes to tax landscape)
  • Segment Expense Growth: driven by FoA; Reality Labs losses expected similar to 2025
  • Reality Labs Losses: similar to 2025; likely peak, gradual reduction expected going forward
  • Revenue Growth Context: FY reported + constant currency growth expected below Q1 levels due to FX tailwind dissipation, tougher comps, and EU less personalized ads headwind
  • Compute Capacity: significantly more capacity in 2026 via cloud; likely still constrained until own facilities come online later in year
  • Share Repurchases: levels will vary; top priority is AI investment; opportunistic on buybacks
  • Financing: supplementing cash flow with cost-efficient external financing; may maintain positive net debt

Long-Term Targets & Strategic Direction

  • Operating Profit Growth Framework: continue using framework of growing consolidated operating profit over time
  • Reality Labs Investment Direction: most investment toward glasses/wearables; Horizon on mobile; VR profitability; losses reducing from 2025 peak
  • Ads Revenue Dominance: ads by far most important growth driver for next couple of years
  • Beyond Ads: focused on subscriptions, licensing; new products will take time to reach ads scale

Q4 Engagement Metrics

  • Family DAP: more than 3.5B people using at least one app daily (in December)
  • Facebook Daily Actives: more than 2B
  • WhatsApp Daily Actives: more than 2B
  • Instagram Daily Actives: just shy of 2B
  • Ad Impressions: +18% YoY (healthy across all regions; engagement/user growth + ad load optimizations)
  • Average Price Per Ad: +6% YoY (increased advertiser demand driven by improved ad performance)

Q4 Ad Monetization & Product Metrics

  • Instagram Reels Watch Time: +30% YoY in the U.S.
  • Facebook Video Time: continued double-digit YoY growth in the U.S.
  • Facebook Organic Feed & Video Post Views: +7% lift from Q4 optimizations (largest quarterly FB product revenue impact in 2 years)
  • Facebook Reels Content Freshness: +25% more same-day Reels surfaced vs. prior quarter
  • Instagram Original Content (U.S.): +10pp in Q4; 75% of recommendations now from original posts
  • Threads Time Spent: +20% lift from Q4 recommendation improvements
  • Video Gen Tools Revenue Run Rate: $10B in Q4 (QoQ growth ~3x faster than overall ads revenue)
  • Incremental Attribution: +24% incremental conversions vs. standard model; multibillion-dollar ARR in 7 months
  • GEM Model GPU Cluster: doubled the number of GPUs used to train GEM model for ads ranking in Q4
  • GEM + Sequence Learning: +3.5% ad clicks on FB; +1% conversions on IG in Q4
  • Instagram Runtime Model: +3% conversion rates in Q4 (feed, stories, reels)
  • Facebook Lattice Consolidation: consolidated FB Stories + other services into overall FB model; +12% ads quality
  • Click-to-Message Ads (U.S.): growth accelerated in Q4; +50% YoY (website-to-message ads adoption)
  • WhatsApp Paid Messaging: crossed $2B annual run rate in Q4
  • Business AIs: 1M+ weekly conversations on messaging platforms (Mexico and Philippines)
  • Meta AI: available in over 200 markets

Q4 Advertiser Vertical Performance

  • Overall: healthy YoY growth across all verticals except politics (lapping U.S. presidential election)
  • Online Commerce: largest contributor; growth consistent with Q3; broad-based across regions/sizes
  • Professional Services: second-largest; strong growth with Advantage+ lead campaign contributions
  • Technology: third-largest; continued strength; broad-based across regions/sizes

Q4 Internal AI Productivity

  • Engineer Output Growth: +30% since start of 2025 (mostly from agentic coding adoption, big jump in Q4)
  • Power Users of AI Coding Tools: output increased 80% YoY
  • AI Dubbing: 9 languages; hundreds of millions watching AI-translated videos daily
  • Meta AI Edits App: ~10% of daily Reels views created in Edits (nearly tripled QoQ)
  • Meta AI Media Generation DAUs: tripled YoY in Q4

Q4 Infrastructure & Silicon

  • Andromeda Ads Retrieval: extended to NVIDIA, AMD, MTIA; nearly tripled compute efficiency
  • GEM Coverage: added FB Reels in Q4; now covers all major FB + IG surfaces

AI Strategy & Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL)

  • MSL Progress: ~6 months in; "most talent-dense research effort in the industry"; early indicators positive
  • Frontier Model Path: multiple models across products rolling out over coming months; expect to push frontier steadily through 2026
  • Personal Super Intelligence Vision: building personal AI understanding context, history, interests, relationships
  • LLM + Recommendation Systems: merging LLMs with FB/IG/Threads/ads recommendation systems; longer-term research project
  • Meta AI Personalization: personalized responses drive higher engagement; significant advances expected in 2026
  • Content Understanding: deeper understanding of user interests/preferences; more relevant content in responses

Engagement & Recommendation Systems Roadmap (2026)

  • Model Scaling: continuing to scale models + increase training data with longer content interaction history
  • Ad Signal Validation: validating ad signals in organic recommendations; working toward shared organic/ads platform
  • Real-Time Adaptation: recommendations more adaptive to in-session engagement
  • LLM Integration: deeper LLM integration for content understanding; especially useful for new content with less engagement data
  • GEM 2026 Plans: scale to larger cluster; increase complexity; expand data; new sequence learning architecture
  • Lattice Consolidation: expect to consolidate more models in 2026 than prior two years; fewer, more capable models
  • Scalable Recommendation Architecture: first recommendation architecture scaling with LLM-like efficiency; unlocks significant ranking model scale-up

Ad Products & Business Messaging Roadmap (2026)

  • Meta AI Business Assistant: testing with advertisers in Q4; campaign optimization + account support; expanding availability
  • Threads Ads Expansion: expanding to all remaining countries (Jan 2026) including UK, EU, Brazil
  • WhatsApp Ads in Status: rollout throughout 2026; low ad levels initially while optimizing formats/performance
  • Business AIs Expansion: expanding to more markets; extending from Q&A to transactional capabilities in WhatsApp
  • Agentic Shopping Tools: product discovery from business catalogs; working across feeds + messaging
  • Advantage+ Lead Campaigns: fully rolled out at start of Q4 2025; driving strong contributions in professional services vertical

Reality Labs & Glasses

  • Glasses Sales Growth: 3x+ in 2025; "some of the fastest-growing consumer electronics in history"
  • Investment Focus: directing most Reality Labs investment toward glasses and wearables going forward
  • Horizon Worlds on Mobile: focused on mobile success; AI + Horizon could open new gaming/communication areas
  • VR Profitability: focused on making VR a profitable ecosystem over coming years
  • Q4 Revenue Decline: $955M (-12% YoY) due to lapping Quest 3S launch + Q3 retail pre-stocking

Infrastructure & Meta Compute

  • Meta Compute: new initiative; infrastructure efficiency as strategic advantage
  • Leadership Hire: Dina Powell McCormick joined as President/Vice Chairman for government + capital partnerships
  • Silicon Strategy: deploying NVIDIA, AMD, MTIA optimized per workload
  • MTIA Expansion: Q1 2026 extending to ranking/recommendation training workloads (beyond inference)
  • Long-Term Silicon & Energy: advancing own silicon; flexible systems; cost per GW expected to decrease significantly
  • Capacity Flexibility: new data center development approaches; strategic partnerships; cloud contracts; new ownership structures
  • Compute Constraints: still constrained; demand grew faster than supply in 2025; more capacity in 2026 via cloud but likely still constrained

Regulatory & Legal

  • EU Less Personalized Ads: aligned with EC on changes; rolling out Q1 2026; potential revenue headwind later in year
  • Legal/Regulatory Headwinds: monitoring EU + U.S. headwinds that could significantly impact results
  • Youth-Related Issues: trials scheduled for 2026 in U.S.; may result in material loss
  • Q4 Legal Expenses: growth from lapping Q4 2024 reversals + Q4 2025 charges

Capital Allocation

  • Manus Acquisition: strategic acquisition; integrating into ads/business managers for millions of businesses
  • Share Repurchases: no stock repurchased in Q4; top priority is AI investment; opportunistic on buybacks
  • External Financing: supplementing cash flow with cost-efficient external financing; may maintain positive net debt
  • Net Cash Position: strong; expects sufficient cash to fund 2026 infrastructure investments

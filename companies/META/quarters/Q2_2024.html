What Changed vs Q1 2024

  • Raised Revenue Guidance (Next Q) midpoint up 5.3% vs prior quarter ($39.8B vs $37.8B)
  • Raised FY CapEx midpoint up 2.7% vs prior quarter ($38.5B vs $37.5B)
  • Changed Reality Labs Op Loss: $4.5B (prior: $3.8B)

Q3 2024 Revenue Guidance

  • Revenue: $38.5B - $41.0B (midpoint: $39.75B)
  • FX Assumption: 2% headwind to YoY total revenue growth based on current exchange rates
  • Commentary: Expect YoY growth to slow in Q3 as Meta laps strong growth from China-based advertisers and strong Reels impression growth from prior year; expect modestly larger FX headwinds in Q3; continued healthy global advertising demand and ongoing ad performance improvements expected; broad-based strength across regions and verticals including smaller advertisers expected to continue

Full Year 2024 Guidance

Expense Outlook

  • Total Expenses: $96B - $99B (unchanged from prior outlook)
  • Reality Labs Operating Losses: expected to "increase meaningfully year-over-year" due to ongoing product development efforts and investments to further scale ecosystem

CapEx Outlook

  • CapEx: $37B - $40B (raised from prior range of $35B - $40B; low end raised by $2B)

2025 Preliminary Outlook

  • CapEx: "significant CapEx growth in 2025" expected to support AI research and product development efforts
  • Infrastructure Costs: expected to be "a significant driver of expense growth next year" as Meta recognizes depreciation and operating costs associated with expanded infrastructure footprint
  • Quantitative 2025 Guidance: not yet provided; expected on the Q4 2024 call
  • Headcount: no 2025 headcount growth expectations provided yet (budgeting process not started)

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $39.1B (+22% YoY, +23% on constant currency basis)
  • Total Expenses: $24.2B (+7% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $14.8B
  • Operating Margin: 38%
  • Tax Rate: 11%
  • Net Income: $13.5B
  • Diluted EPS: $5.16
  • Headcount: ~70,800 employees (+2% from Q1 2024)

Expense Line Items (YoY)

  • Cost of Revenue: +23% YoY (driven by higher infrastructure and Reality Labs inventory costs)
  • R&D: +13% YoY (driven by higher headcount-related expenses and infrastructure costs, partially offset by lower restructuring costs)
  • Marketing and Sales: -14% YoY (lower restructuring and headcount-related costs)
  • G&A: -12% YoY (lower legal-related expenses)

Capital Expenditures & Cash Flow

  • CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases): $8.5B (driven by investments in servers, data centers, and network infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $10.9B
  • Share Repurchases: $6.3B of Class A common stock
  • Dividends: $1.3B paid to shareholders

Balance Sheet Highlights

  • Cash and Marketable Securities: $58.1B
  • Debt: $18.4B

Family of Apps Segment

Engagement Metrics

  • Family DAP (Daily Active People): ~3.27B in June (also described as "more than 3.2 billion")
  • WhatsApp U.S. Monthly Actives: 100M+
  • Threads Monthly Actives: approaching 200M

Family of Apps Revenue

  • FoA Total Revenue: $38.7B (+22% YoY)
  • FoA Ads Revenue: $38.3B (+22% YoY, +23% on constant currency basis)
  • FoA Other Revenue: $389M (+73% YoY, driven primarily by WhatsApp business platform messaging revenue growth)

Ad Revenue by Vertical (YoY contribution to growth)

  • Largest Contributor: Online commerce
  • Second: Gaming
  • Third: Entertainment and media

Ad Revenue by User Geography (YoY growth)

  • Rest of World: +33%
  • Europe: +26%
  • Asia Pacific: +20%
  • North America: +17%

Ad Revenue by Advertiser Geography

  • Asia Pacific (advertiser basis): +28% (though below Q1 rate of +41% due to lapping stronger China-based advertiser demand)

Ad Impressions & Pricing

  • Ad Impressions: +10% YoY (mainly driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
  • Average Price Per Ad: +10% YoY (driven by increased advertiser demand and improved ad performance, partially offset by impression growth from lower monetizing regions and services)

Family of Apps Profitability

  • FoA Expenses: $19.4B (~80% of overall expenses, +4% YoY; driven by higher infrastructure and headcount-related expenses, partially offset by lower restructuring costs)
  • FoA Operating Income: $19.3B
  • FoA Operating Margin: 50%

Reality Labs Segment

  • RL Revenue: $353M (+28% YoY, driven primarily by Quest headset sales)
  • RL Expenses: $4.8B (+21% YoY, driven by higher headcount-related expenses and Reality Labs inventory costs)
  • RL Operating Loss: $4.5B

Tax Rate

  • Full Year 2024 Tax Rate: mid-teens (absent any changes to tax landscape)

Headcount

  • Year-End 2024 Headcount: expected to end "meaningfully higher" than year-end 2023 (working down a prior hiring underrun)

AI Strategy & Infrastructure Investment

Llama Foundation Models

  • Llama 3.1 Release: includes the "first frontier-level open source model" plus new small and medium-sized models
  • Llama 3.1 405B Parameter Model: better cost performance relative to leading closed models
  • Llama 3 Training: trained on 16,000 H100 GPUs
  • Llama 4 Compute Requirement: expected to require ~10x the compute used to train Llama 3 (~160,000 GPU equivalent)
  • GPU Fleet: 600,000 GPU equivalents expected by year-end 2024

Meta AI

  • Usage: on track to achieve goal of becoming the most used AI assistant by end of 2024
  • Queries: billions of queries since launch
  • Availability: 20+ countries and 8 languages
  • Largest Market for Meta AI Usage: India (on WhatsApp)
  • Monetization Timeline: not expected to be a meaningful driver of revenue in 2024; monetization expected to take "years"

AI Studio

  • Launch: launched the week of the earnings call; allows anyone to create AIs to interact with across Meta apps

Generative AI Advertising Tools

  • Advertiser Adoption: 1M+ advertisers used at least 1 generative AI ad feature (image expansion, background generation, text generation) in the past month
  • Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns: 22% higher return on ad spend for U.S. advertisers after adoption (per Meta study)
  • Full Image Generation: began rolling out into Advantage+ creative in May 2024

AI Investment Framework

  • Core AI: ROI-based approach; strong returns as engagement and ad performance improvements translate into revenue gains
  • GenAI: earlier stage; not expected to be a meaningful revenue driver in 2024; focused on 4 primary monetization opportunities: (1) enhanced core ads business, (2) business messaging, (3) Meta AI assistant, (4) organic content growth
  • Infrastructure Flexibility: CapEx built with fungibility in mind -- GenAI training capacity can be flexed to GenAI inference or core ranking/recommendation with modifications; data center sites staged at various phases of development

Ad Monetization & Engagement Drivers

Video & Reels

  • Facebook Unified Video Player: global rollout of unified video player and ranking systems in June 2024; brings Reels, longer videos, and live into a single experience
  • Unified AI Systems Impact: already increased engagement on Facebook Reels "more than our initial move from CPUs to GPUs did"
  • Instagram Reels: engagement continues to grow with ongoing recommendation system enhancements
  • Instagram Original Posts: more than half of recommendations in the U.S. now come from original posts
  • H2 2024 Impression Growth: video expected to remain a source of impression growth; Reels to continue driving Instagram growth; Facebook expected to grow overall video time while increasing short-form video mix

Advantage+ Suite

  • Advantage+ Shopping - Flexible Format: introduced in Q2, allows advertisers to upload multiple images/videos for automatic format optimization
  • Advantage+ Shopping - Conversion Types: expanded to include 10 additional conversion types (e.g., add to cart)

Business Messaging

  • WhatsApp Business Platform: primary driver of FoA other revenue growth (+73%)
  • Business AI Agents: in alpha testing; positive feedback; vision is every business will have an AI agent

Metaverse & Reality Labs

Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses

  • Demand: outpacing ability to build them; performing "better than expected"
  • Partnership: deepening long-term partnership with EssilorLuxottica for future generations of AI glasses

Quest 3

  • Sales: outpacing expectations
  • Use Cases: expanding beyond gaming into general computing (video watching, web browsing, virtual desktop PC extension)

Connect Conference

  • Date: September 25, 2024; planned updates on AI and metaverse work

Capital Return

  • Q2 Share Repurchases: $6.3B
  • Q2 Dividends: $1.3B

Regulatory Environment

  • Commentary: monitoring "increasing legal and regulatory headwinds in the EU and the U.S. that could significantly impact our business and our financial results"

Young Adult Engagement (Facebook)

  • U.S. & Canada Young Adults (18-29): healthy growth in app usage for the past several quarters
  • Key Products for Young Adults: Groups (posting to Groups in U.S. & Canada growing, boosted mainly by young adults) and Marketplace (strong demand for secondhand products)

Marketplace & Commerce

  • Shops Ads Revenue: growing at a strong YoY pace
  • Shops Ads: driving incremental performance for advertisers; working well with Advantage+ shopping

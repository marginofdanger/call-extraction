What Changed vs Q1 2025

  • Raised Revenue Guidance (Next Q) midpoint up 11.4% vs prior quarter ($49.0B vs $44.0B)
  • Raised FY CapEx midpoint up 1.5% vs prior quarter ($69.0B vs $68.0B)
  • Changed Family DAP: 3.4B+ (people using at least one Family of Apps daily in June) (prior: More than 3.4 billion people using at least one app daily (as of March))
  • Changed Ad Impressions Growth: +11% YoY (growth mainly driven by Asia Pacific; accelerated across all regions due to engagement tailwinds on Facebook and Instagram and ad load optimizations on Facebook) (prior: +5% YoY (growth mainly driven by Asia Pacific))
  • Changed Avg Price Per Ad: +9% YoY (increased advertiser demand driven by improved ad performance; pricing growth slowed modestly from Q1 due to accelerated impression growth in Q2) (prior: +10% YoY (benefited from increased advertiser demand driven by improved ad performance; partially offset by impression growth from lower monetizing regions and surfaces))
  • Changed Operating Margin: 43% (prior: 41%)
  • Changed Reality Labs Op Loss: $4.5B (prior: $4.2B)

Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2025)

  • Revenue: $47.5B--$50.5B (midpoint $49.0B)
  • FX Assumption: ~1% tailwind to YoY total revenue growth (based on current exchange rates)
  • Q4 2025 Commentary: Year-over-year growth rate in Q4 2025 expected to be slower than Q3 2025 as Meta laps a period of stronger growth in Q4 2024 (no formal Q4 range provided)

Full Year 2025 Guidance

  • Total Expenses: $114B--$118B (narrowed from prior outlook of $113B--$118B; represents +20% to +24% YoY growth)
  • CapEx: $66B--$72B including principal payments on finance leases (narrowed from prior outlook of $64B--$72B; up ~$30B YoY at midpoint)

2026 Preliminary Outlook

  • Total Expenses: 2026 YoY expense growth rate expected to be above the 2025 expense growth rate - Largest driver: Infrastructure costs, driven by sharp acceleration in depreciation expense growth and higher operating costs as infrastructure fleet scales - Depreciation growth driven by: (1) incremental depreciation from assets purchased and placed in service in 2026; (2) full year depreciation from infrastructure deployed in 2025 - Greater mix of CapEx in shorter-lived assets in 2025 and 2026 vs. prior years - Higher operating expenses including energy costs, leases, maintenance, and operational expenses - Increased spend on cloud services to meet capacity needs - Growth in network-related costs - Second largest driver: Employee compensation (adding technical talent in priority areas; recognizing full year of compensation for employees hired in 2025)
  • CapEx: Expect another year of similarly significant CapEx dollar growth in 2026 (analyst commentary implies potentially $100B+); driven by scaling genAI training capacity with higher spend across servers, networking, and data centers; continued significant core AI investment
  • Headcount: Talent additions across all priority areas expected to continue driving overall headcount growth through 2025 and 2026
  • Financing: Expect to finance some large share of CapEx internally; also exploring ways to work with financial partners to co-develop data centers (no finalized transactions announced)

Q2 Expense Line Item Detail

  • Cost of Revenue: +16% YoY (higher infrastructure costs and payments to partners, partially offset by server useful life extension benefit)
  • R&D: +23% YoY (higher employee compensation and infrastructure costs)
  • Marketing & Sales: +9% YoY (higher professional services for platform integrity and marketing costs, partially offset by lower employee compensation)
  • G&A: -27% YoY (lower legal-related costs)

Q2 Ad Revenue Detail

  • Family of Apps Ad Revenue: $46.6B (+21% YoY reported; +22% YoY constant currency)
  • Ad Impressions: +11% YoY (growth mainly driven by Asia Pacific; accelerated across all regions due to engagement tailwinds on Facebook and Instagram and ad load optimizations on Facebook)
  • Average Price Per Ad: +9% YoY (increased advertiser demand driven by improved ad performance; pricing growth slowed modestly from Q1 due to accelerated impression growth in Q2)
  • Largest contributor to YoY ad revenue growth: Online commerce vertical

Q2 Ad Revenue by User Geography

  • Europe: +24% YoY (strongest growth)
  • Rest of World: +23% YoY
  • North America: +21% YoY
  • Asia Pacific: +18% YoY

  • Meta AI Monthly Actives: 1B+
  • Instagram Video Time: +20%+ YoY globally
  • Facebook Video Time (U.S.): +20%+ YoY
  • Facebook Time Spent: +5% (driven by AI recommendation system advancements, this quarter)
  • Instagram Time Spent: +6% (driven by AI recommendation system advancements, this quarter)

  • FoA Ad Revenue: $46.6B (+21% YoY reported; +22% YoY constant currency)

Q2 Segment Results -- Reality Labs

Q2 Balance Sheet & Capital Return

  • Cash & Marketable Securities: $47.1B
  • Debt: $28.8B
  • Share Repurchases: $9.8B (Class A common stock)
  • Dividends Paid: $1.3B
  • Nonmarketable Equity Investments: $15.1B (includes minority investment in Scale AI and other investment activities)

2025 Tax Rate

  • Anticipated reduction in U.S. federal cash tax for remainder of year and future years due to new U.S. tax law enactment
  • 2025 full year tax rate expected to be higher than Q2 2025 tax rate of 11%, but magnitude cannot be quantified at this time
  • Multiple alternative implementation provisions under evaluation

AI Advertising System Improvements

  • AI-powered recommendation model for ads: Expanded to new surfaces with improved performance using more signals and longer context; drove ~5% more ad conversions on Instagram and ~3% on Facebook
  • GenAI ad creative adoption: Meaningful percent of ad revenue now coming from campaigns using at least one generative AI feature
  • Advantage+ Creative Suite: Nearly 2 million advertisers using video generation features (image animation and video expansion)
  • AI-powered ad translation: Testing automatic caption translation to 10 different languages; promising performance lifts in prelaunch tests
  • Andromeda model (ads retrieval): Enhancements drove nearly 4% higher conversions on Facebook Mobile Feed and Reels; expanded coverage to Facebook Reels
  • GEM (Generative Ads Recommendation, ranking stage): Doubled the length of event sequences used; increased ad conversions ~5% on Instagram and ~3% on Facebook Feed and Reels in Q2
  • Lattice model architecture: Deployed to earlier-stage ads ranking models in April 2025; drove nearly 4% increase in ad conversions across Facebook Feed and Reels in Q2
  • Advantage+ sales and app campaigns: Completed rollout of streamlined campaign creation flow; lifts in advertiser adoption since expanded availability; leads campaign rollout in coming months
  • Incremental attribution feature: Completed global rollout in Q2; only product on market that optimizes for and reports on incremental conversions
  • Omnichannel ads: Launched globally in Q2; median 15% reduction in total cost per purchase vs. website-only optimization in tests

Business Messaging

  • Click-to-Message Revenue (U.S.): +40%+ YoY in Q2
  • WhatsApp Daily Actives (Updates tab): 1.5B+
  • Business AI agents: Seeing product-market fit in a number of test countries; integrating into ads on Facebook and Instagram and directly into e-commerce websites
  • WhatsApp ads in status and channels: Rolling out gradually over course of 2025 and 2026; low levels of expected ad supply initially
  • WhatsApp ads pricing: Expected to earn lower average price than Facebook or Instagram ads for the foreseeable future (WhatsApp skews toward lower-monetizing markets with more limited targeting information)
  • WhatsApp ads revenue impact: Not expected to be a meaningful contributor to total impressions or revenue growth for the next few years

Threads Monetization

  • Threads feed ads: Began introducing ads in Feed on Threads in Q2; advertisers globally can run video and image ads to Threads users in most countries (including U.S.) as of May
  • Near-term outlook: Ad supply remains low; Threads not expected to be a meaningful contributor to overall impression growth in the near term
  • Long-term outlook: Optimistic as community, engagement, and monetization scale

Meta AI and Superintelligence Strategy

  • Meta AI reach: 1B+ monthly actives
  • Meta Superintelligence Labs: Established; includes foundations, product, and FAIR teams plus new lab focused on next-generation models
  • Key hires: Alexandr Wang (overall team lead), Nat Friedman (AI Products and Applied Research lead), Shengjia Zhao (Chief Scientist for new effort)
  • Model development: Good progress toward Llama 4.1 and 4.2; parallel work on next-generation frontier models expected in "the next year or so"
  • Infrastructure: Building several multi-gigawatt clusters - Prometheus cluster: Coming online next year; expected to be world's first gigawatt-plus cluster - Hyperion cluster: Can scale up to 5 gigawatts over several years - Multiple additional titan clusters in development
  • LLM in recommendations: LLMs now driving a meaningful share of ranking-related time spent gains on Threads; exploring extension to other apps
  • Bug report automation: Llama-powered actioning of bug reports reduced top-line bug reports in U.S. and Canada in Facebook Feed and notifications by ~30% over past 10 months
  • AI self-improvement: Beginning to see glimpses of AI systems improving themselves; improvement is "slow for now, but undeniable"
  • Open source strategy: Expect to continue producing and sharing leading open source models; will not open source everything (continuation of current approach); some models becoming too large for practical external use; heightened safety concerns as superintelligence is approached
  • GenAI revenue timeline: Not expected to be a meaningful driver of revenue in 2025 or 2026
  • ROI on core AI: Continues to see strong ROI with rigorous measurement

Reality Labs and AI Devices

  • Ray-Ban Meta glasses: Sales growth accelerated in Q2; demand still outstripping supply for most popular SKUs despite earlier production increases; working to ramp supply later this year
  • Oakley Meta HSTN: New performance AI glasses launching; longer battery life, higher resolution camera, designed for sports
  • Meta AI usage on glasses: Percent of people using Meta AI is growing; new user AI retention increasing
  • Quest ecosystem: People continuing to spend more time; community growing steadily
  • Quest 3S Xbox Edition: Launched last month; record interest in cloud gaming
  • Quest engagement beyond gaming: Media and web browsing contributing significant portion of engagement
  • Meta Connect: September 17, with more Reality Labs announcements planned

Engagement and Recommendations

  • Instagram recommended content (U.S.): Over 2/3 now comes from original posts
  • H2 2025 focus: Increasing freshness of original posts so right audiences discover original creator content soon after posting
  • Cross-surface foundation recommendation models: Research efforts continuing to progress
  • LLM in Threads recommendations: Now driving a meaningful share of ranking-related time spent gains
  • Meta AI on Facebook: Usage expanding as people ask about posts in Feed and find content via Search
  • Automatic translation/dubbing: Expected to help with content discovery by translating foreign language content into audience's local language; more details later in 2025
  • Recommendations road map: Making recommendations more adaptive to in-session engagement; helping smaller creator content break out by matching to right audiences sooner; improving discovery of diversified and niche interests through interest exploration and explicit user preferences; planning to scale up models further with more advanced techniques

EU Regulatory Risk

  • Less Personalized Ads (LPA): Introduced November 2024 based on European Commission DMA feedback; continuing engagement with EC
  • Risk: EC may seek to impose further modifications that would result in a materially worse user and advertiser experience
  • Potential revenue impact: Significant negative impact on European revenue possible as early as later in Q3 2025
  • Appeal: Meta has appealed the EC DMA decision, but modifications may be imposed during appeal process
  • Broader regulatory landscape: Increasing legal and regulatory headwinds in the EU that could significantly impact business and financial results

Capital Allocation

  • Priority: Investing capital back into the business; infrastructure and talent are top priorities
  • Share repurchases: Expect to continue repurchasing shares as part of buyback program to offset equity compensation
  • Dividends: Expect to continue quarterly cash dividend distributions
  • SBC/dilution management: Strong financial position expected to support investments while continuing buyback program and dividends

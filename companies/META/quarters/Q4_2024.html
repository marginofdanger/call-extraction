Q1 2025 Guidance

  • Revenue: $39.5B to $41.8B (midpoint: $40.65B)
  • Revenue Growth (YoY, reported): +8% to +15%
  • Revenue Growth (YoY, constant currency): +11% to +18%
  • FX Headwind: approximately 3% headwind to YoY total revenue growth (based on current exchange rates)
  • Leap Day Lapping Effect: Q1 2025 guidance reflects the effect of lapping leap day in Q1 2024

Full Year 2025 Guidance

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $48.4B (+21% YoY reported, +21% YoY constant currency)
  • Total Expenses: $25.0B (+5% YoY; expense growth rate reflects 13 percentage point favorable impact from legal accrual reductions in Q4 and lower YoY restructuring costs)
  • Operating Income: $23.4B
  • Operating Margin: 48%
  • Tax Rate: 12%
  • Net Income: $20.8B
  • Diluted EPS: $8.02
  • Capital Expenditures (incl. principal payments on finance leases): $14.8B (driven by investments in servers, data centers, and network infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $13.2B
  • Dividends Paid: $1.3B
  • Cash and Marketable Securities (end of year): $77.8B
  • Debt (end of year): $28.8B
  • Headcount (end of year): 74,000+ (+10% YoY; growth primarily in monetization, infrastructure, generative AI, Reality Labs, regulation and compliance)

Expense Line Items (Q4 YoY)

  • Cost of Revenue: +15% (driven mostly by higher infrastructure costs)
  • R&D: +16% (driven by higher employee compensation and infrastructure costs; partially offset by lower restructuring costs)
  • Marketing & Sales: approximately flat YoY
  • G&A: -67% (driven mostly by lower legal-related expenses due to $1.55B reduction in legal accruals related to certain legal proceedings)

Headcount Composition

  • R&D Share of YoY Headcount Growth: ~90% in Q4
  • Remaining Growth: primarily in cost of revenue (infrastructure headcount for data center operations)

Capital & Cash Flow (FY 2024 Q4 Run-Rate)

  • Q4 CapEx (incl. finance lease payments): $14.8B
  • Q4 Free Cash Flow: $13.2B
  • Q4 Dividends Paid: $1.3B

Server Useful Life Update

  • New Expected Useful Life (Non-AI and AI servers): approximately 5.5 years (extended from prior estimates; savings in annual CapEx and depreciation already included in guidance)

Revenue

  • Full Year Revenue Outlook: not providing a specific full year 2025 revenue range; management expects investments in core business will provide opportunity to "continue delivering strong revenue growth throughout 2025"

Total Expenses

  • Total Expenses: $114B to $119B (midpoint: $116.5B)
  • Largest Driver of Expense Growth: infrastructure costs (higher operating expenses and depreciation)
  • Second Largest Driver: employee compensation (adding technical talent in priority areas)

Capital Expenditures

  • CapEx: $60B to $65B (midpoint: $62.5B)
  • CapEx Growth Drivers: increased investment to support both generative AI efforts and core business
  • CapEx Allocation: majority directed toward core business
  • Server Component: largest portion of CapEx; biggest growth driver; growth in both AI and non-AI capacity
  • Data Center Component: higher spend driven by build-outs of large training clusters and higher power density data centers entering core construction phase; used primarily for core AI and non-AI use cases
  • Networking Component: growing in 2025 to accommodate growth in non-AI and core AI traffic plus large gen AI training clusters; investing in fiber for future cross-region training traffic
  • Infrastructure Breakdown: total infrastructure spend within each of gen AI, non-AI, and core AI all expected to increase in 2025

Tax Rate

  • Tax Rate: 12% to 15% (absent changes to tax landscape)

Headcount

  • Headcount Growth: expected to continue primarily in technical roles across infrastructure, monetization, Reality Labs, generative AI, regulation and compliance
  • Business Functions Headcount Growth: expected to remain "relatively limited"

Infrastructure Capacity

  • Data Center Capacity Coming Online (2025): almost 1 gigawatt
  • Large AI Data Center Under Construction: 2 gigawatt+ facility

AI Strategy and Product Roadmap

Meta AI

  • Target: reach 1B+ users in 2025; Meta AI already used by more people than any other assistant
  • Monthly Actives: 700M+
  • Strongest Engagement Platform: WhatsApp (information seeking, educational queries, emotional support)
  • Second Strongest Engagement: Facebook (feed deep dives integration)
  • Monetization Plans: initial focus on building great consumer experience; future opportunities include paid recommendations and a premium offering; not a meaningful revenue contributor in 2025
  • Personalization Roadmap: memory features (remembering details from prior queries), leveraging Facebook/Instagram engagement data for personalized responses

Llama 4

  • Llama 4 Mini: pretraining complete
  • Llama 4 Architecture: natively multimodal, omni model with agentic capabilities
  • Reasoning Models and Larger Model: "looking good" in training
  • Goal: lead (vs. Llama 3 goal of making open source competitive with closed models)
  • Release Cadence: multiple models at different dates (similar to Llama 3 rollout)

AI Engineering Agent

  • 2025 Target: possible to build an AI engineering agent with coding/problem-solving abilities of a "good mid-level engineer"
  • Timeline: primarily internal tool in 2025; more dramatic external impact expected in 2026+

Andromeda (Ads ML System)

  • Partnership: developed with NVIDIA
  • Capability: 10,000x increase in complexity of models used for ads retrieval
  • Result: 8% increase in quality of ads on objectives tested
  • Benefit: enables far more sophisticated prediction models for ad personalization; positions well for future as advertisers use generative AI tools to create more ads

Custom Silicon (MTIA)

  • 2024 Deployment: began deploying MTIA to ranking and recommendation inference workloads for ads and organic content
  • 2025 Plan: further ramp adoption for ranking/recommendation inference use cases; use for incremental capacity and to replace GPU-based servers at end of useful life
  • 2026 Plan: expand to core AI training workloads for ranking and recommendations; over time, some gen AI use cases

Advertising and Monetization Trends

Advantage+ Platform

  • Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns Annual Run Rate: $20B+ (growing 70% YoY in Q4)
  • Streamlined Campaign Creation: testing new flow where all campaigns optimizing for sales, app, or lead objectives will have Advantage+ turned on by default; plan to expand to more advertisers in coming months before full rollout later in 2025

Threads Monetization

  • Status: beginning to test ads in Q1 2025
  • Outlook: introduction of ads on Threads will be "gradual"; not anticipated to be a meaningful driver of overall impression or revenue growth in 2025

WhatsApp Business Messaging

  • Revenue Growth: +55% YoY (within FoA Other Revenue of $519M)
  • Status: continues as growth driver

Content and Engagement Strategy

Reels

  • Daily Re-shares: 4.5B+
  • New Feature (Q4 2024): allows creators to first share a reel with non-followers to test content before sharing with followers
  • U.S. Launch: new destination in Reels consisting of content friends have noted on or liked; "very positive early results"; plan to expand globally

Threads

  • Monthly Actives: 320M+
  • Growth Rate: 1M+ sign-ups per day
  • 2025 Focus: establishing Threads as the place people come to keep up with what they care about; updates to recommendation systems (prioritize recent posts, surface content from top creators, more followed-account content); improving custom feeds

Facebook

  • Focus: growing cultural influence; "OG Facebook" initiative; potential near-term trade-offs to focus on product areas over maximizing near-term business results

Creative Tools

  • Edits App: new standalone app launching in coming weeks providing a full suite of creative tools for creators to make Reels on their phones

Capital Allocation and Shareholder Returns

  • Primary Focus: investing capital back into the business (infrastructure and talent as top priorities)
  • Dividends Paid (Q4): $1.3B
  • Share Repurchases: management expects strong financial position will enable continued return of capital through repurchases and dividends
  • Cash Position (Year-End): $77.8B in cash and marketable securities
  • Debt (Year-End): $28.8B

Regulatory and Competitive Environment

  • EU and U.S. Regulatory Headwinds: monitoring active regulatory landscape including legal and regulatory headwinds that could "significantly impact" business and financial results
  • Content Moderation: transitioned to Community Notes system (replacing prior fact-checking approach)
  • Advertiser Impact from Content Policy Changes: no noticeable impact on advertiser spend observed
  • Government Relations: optimistic about new U.S. administration's stance on American technology companies
  • DeepSeek / China Competition: management views maintaining an American open-source AI standard as important for national advantage; novel techniques from competitors will be studied and potentially incorporated

Key Q&A Insights

Pricing vs. Impressions Growth Trajectory

  • Management expects continued opportunity to drive revenue growth across both pricing and impression growth (supply and demand dimensions)
  • Average price per ad is described as an "output metric" blending many factors (advertiser bids, cost per action, different objectives)
  • Healthy cost per action trends observed for advertisers
  • Expect continued improvement in driving conversions, which lifts CPMs over time (more conversions per impression = higher-value impressions)

Long-Run Capital Intensity

  • "Too early to determine" long-run capital intensity
  • Key variables: pace of model advancement, efficiency gains, gen AI product adoption, next-gen hardware innovation, monetization gains from AI investments
  • Compute mix shifting from pretraining toward inference (reasoning models), but total compute need not necessarily decline
  • Management continues to believe heavy CapEx/infrastructure investment will be a "strategic advantage over time"

Meta AI Business Opportunity Timing

  • Meta AI, AI Studio, business agents, and people interacting with AIs remain "outside of '25, for the most part" as revenue contributors
  • 2025 focus: scaling product to reach 1B+ users and establishing durable advantage
  • Business improvements in 2025 will come from applying AI methods to advertising, recommendations, and feeds
  • Typical Meta pattern: build product, scale to 1B+ users, then build business around it

GPU and Infrastructure Deployment

  • Planning to "significantly ramp up deployment of GPUs in 2025"
  • No updates on supply availability constraints
  • Continuing engagement with vendors and investing in own silicon

Performance-Based Headcount Reductions

  • Announced performance-related reductions for early 2025
  • No specific guidance on 2026/2027 headcount trajectory beyond continued focus on efficiency and investing in priority technical areas

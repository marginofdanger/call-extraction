What Changed vs Q4 2023

  • Raised Revenue Guidance (Next Q) midpoint up 5.6% vs prior quarter ($37.8B vs $35.8B)
  • Raised FY Total Expenses midpoint up 1.0% vs prior quarter ($97.5B vs $96.5B)
  • Raised FY CapEx midpoint up 11.9% vs prior quarter ($37.5B vs $33.5B)
  • Changed Operating Margin: 38% (prior: 41%)
  • Changed Reality Labs Op Loss: $3.8B (prior: $4.6B)

Q2 2024 Revenue Guidance

  • Revenue: $36.5B - $39.0B (midpoint: $37.75B)
  • FX Assumption: foreign currency is approximately 1% headwind to YoY total revenue growth based on current exchange rates

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $36.5B (+27% YoY reported; +27% YoY constant currency)
  • Total Expenses: $22.6B (+6% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $13.8B
  • Operating Margin: 38%
  • Tax Rate: 13%
  • Net Income: $12.4B
  • Diluted EPS: $4.71
  • Headcount: 69,300+ (end of Q1; +3% QoQ from Q4 2023)

Expense Line Item Detail

  • Cost of Revenue: increased 9% YoY (higher infrastructure-related costs, partially offset by lapping Reality Labs inventory-related valuation adjustments)
  • R&D: increased 6% YoY (higher headcount-related expenses and infrastructure costs, partially offset by lower restructuring costs)
  • Marketing & Sales: decreased 16% YoY (lower restructuring costs, professional services, and marketing spend)
  • G&A: increased 20% YoY (higher legal-related expenses, partially offset by lower restructuring costs; includes accruals related to ongoing legal matters)

Capital Expenditures & Cash Flow

  • CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases): $6.7B (driven by investments in servers, data centers, and network infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $12.5B

Balance Sheet Highlights

  • Cash and Marketable Securities: $58.1B (end of quarter)
  • Debt: $18.4B (end of quarter)

Capital Return

  • Share Repurchases: $14.6B of Class A common stock in Q1
  • Dividends: $1.3B paid to shareholders in Q1

Family of Apps — Engagement Metrics

  • Family Daily Active People (DAP): ~3.2B people using at least one Family app daily in March 2024
  • AI-Recommended Content on Facebook Feed: ~30% of posts delivered by AI recommendation system (up 2x over the last couple of years)
  • AI-Recommended Content on Instagram: 50%+ of content people see is now AI recommended (first time ever exceeding 50%)
  • Video as % of Time Spent: 60%+ of time on both Facebook and Instagram
  • Reels as % of Time on Instagram: 50% of time spent within the app
  • Threads Monthly Actives: 150M+

FoA Revenue

  • Family of Apps Revenue: $36.0B (+27% YoY)
  • Family of Apps Ad Revenue: $35.6B (+27% YoY reported; +26% YoY constant currency)
  • Family of Apps Other Revenue: $380M (+85% YoY, driven by business messaging revenue growth from WhatsApp business platform)

FoA Ad Revenue by Geography (YoY Growth)

  • Rest of World: +40% YoY (strongest growth)
  • Europe: +33% YoY
  • Asia Pacific: +25% YoY
  • North America: +22% YoY (6-point acceleration in total revenue growth from North America advertisers)
  • Asia Pacific Advertiser Segment: +41% YoY (fastest-growing advertiser region; driven in part by strong China-based advertiser spend in online commerce and gaming)

Ad Impressions & Pricing

  • Ad Impressions Growth: +20% YoY (mainly driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
  • Average Price Per Ad: +6% YoY (driven by advertiser demand, partially offset by strong impression growth from lower-monetizing regions and services)
  • Conversions Growth: grew at a faster rate than impressions growth in Q1 (indicating improving conversion rate)

FoA Expenses & Profitability

  • Family of Apps Expenses: $18.4B (+7% YoY; ~81% of overall expenses; higher legal and infrastructure costs partially offset by lower restructuring costs)
  • Family of Apps Operating Income: $17.7B
  • Family of Apps Operating Margin: 49%

Ad Platform Metrics

  • Advantage+ Shopping & Advantage+ App Campaigns Revenue: more than doubled since last year (combined)
  • Advantage+ Audience Targeting: campaigns saw on average 28% decrease in cost per click or per objective compared to regular targeting (based on tests)
  • Advantage+ Shopping Conversion Types: expanded from purchase events only to 10 additional conversion types in Q1

AI Recommendations Model Performance

  • Facebook Reels Watch Time from New Unified Model: 8%-10% increase in watch time after deploying new model architecture

Reality Labs (RL) Segment

  • Reality Labs Revenue: $440M (+30% YoY, driven by Quest headset sales)
  • Reality Labs Expenses: $4.3B (-1% YoY; higher headcount-related expenses more than offset by lapping inventory-related valuation adjustments and restructuring costs)
  • Reality Labs Operating Loss: $3.8B

Total Expenses

  • Total Expenses: $96B - $99B (raised from prior outlook of $94B - $99B; increase due to higher infrastructure and legal costs)

Capital Expenditures

  • CapEx: $35B - $40B (raised from prior range of $30B - $37B; increase driven by accelerating infrastructure investments to support AI road map)
  • 2025 CapEx Expectation: CapEx expected to continue to increase next year (no specific range given) as Meta invests aggressively to support ambitious AI research and product development efforts

Reality Labs

  • Reality Labs Operating Losses: expected to increase meaningfully YoY in 2024 due to ongoing product development efforts and investments to further scale ecosystem

Tax

  • Full Year 2024 Tax Rate: expected to be in the mid-teens (absent any changes to tax landscape)

AI Strategy — Meta AI & Llama 3

  • Meta AI Users: tens of millions of people have already tried Meta AI (as of call date)
  • Llama 3 Models Released: 8 billion and 70 billion parameter models (described as best-in-class for their scale)
  • Llama 3 Large Model: 400+ billion parameter model still training, on track to be industry leading on several benchmarks
  • Meta AI Availability: initially rolled out in some English-speaking countries; expanding to more languages and countries over coming months
  • Meta AI Features: creates animations from still images; generates high-quality images fast enough to create and update as user types
  • Meta AI with Vision on Glasses: launched in the week of the call, enabling users to ask questions about things they are looking at

AI Investment Framework (Susan Li)

  • Core AI Work: ROI-driven approach with strong returns; improvements to engagement and ad performance translating into revenue gains
  • Strategic Bets (Gen AI / Advanced Research): much earlier on the return curve; investing ahead of revenue; building systems with fungibility to flex capacity across different use cases

AI Monetization Pathways (Mark Zuckerberg)

  • Scaling business messaging (business AIs for sales, commerce, customer support)
  • Introducing ads or paid content into Meta AI interactions
  • Enabling people to pay for bigger AI models, more compute, premium features
  • AI improving app engagement (leading to more ads) and improving ad quality directly

Custom Silicon

  • Meta Training and Inference Accelerator Chip (MTIA): successfully enabled running some recommendations-related workloads on less expensive stack; plans to expand to more workloads as program matures over coming years

Advertising — Advantage+ Portfolio

  • Gen AI Ad Creative Features: image expansion, text variations, and background generation available; outsized adoption of image expansion with small businesses
  • Gen AI Image Expansion: rolled out across Facebook and Instagram Reels in Q1 (after introducing to Instagram feed in Q4 2023)
  • Meta Lattice: new ads ranking architecture consolidating smaller, specialized models into larger models that learn across multiple services (feed and Reels) and multiple ad types/objectives; deployed across Facebook and Instagram; plans to add support for web, app, and ROAS objectives over 2024

Reels Monetization

  • Reels Revenue: continued to grow across Instagram and Facebook in Q1 (driven by higher engagement and increased monetization efficiency)
  • Reels Ad Load: has increased over the last year but remains lower on a per-time basis than both Feed and Stories
  • Reels Monetization Strategy: continuing to look for opportunities to thoughtfully grow ad load; investing in higher density experiences/formats; increasingly personalizing ad loads

Business Messaging & Business AIs

  • WhatsApp Business Platform: driving other revenue growth (+85% YoY)
  • Business AI Testing: testing with a handful of businesses on Messenger and WhatsApp for shopping use cases (responding to product inquiries); businesses reporting significant time savings; consumers noting more timely response times; expanding tests over coming months
  • Businesses on Platform: 100M+ businesses

China-Based Advertisers

  • China Ad Spend: growth remained strong in Q1, driven by online commerce and gaming; company is lapping periods of increasingly strong China-based demand over the course of 2024 following 2023 recovery from pandemic-driven headwinds (no specific quantification provided)

Reality Labs — Ray-Ban Meta Glasses

  • Sales Status: sold out in many styles and colors; working to make more and release additional styles
  • New Design: Skyler cat-eye design released (more feminine option)
  • Strategy: starting with classics and expanding with increasing diversity of options over time; partnering with leading eyewear brands (Essilor Luxottica)

Meta Horizon OS

  • Announcement: opening up Meta Horizon OS (the operating system powering Quest) to third-party hardware partners
  • First-Party Expectation: Quest devices expected to continue to be the most popular headsets

Segment Reporting Commentary (Mark Zuckerberg)

  • Noted that Reality Labs work is increasingly serving AI efforts; current 2-segment financial reporting (FoA vs. RL) may not fully capture value generated across both segments; acknowledged need for better ways to articulate cross-segment value creation over time

Video Strategy

  • Unified Video Player on Facebook: launched updated full-screen video player in April bringing together Reels, longer videos, and live content into a single experience with unified recommendation system; rolled out in U.S. and Canada
  • Next-Generation Ranking Architecture: increasingly powering the unified video experience; expected to deliver more relevant video recommendations over time
  • Unified Model Architecture: being extended from Facebook Reels to also power Facebook's video tab; previously saw 8%-10% watch time gains on Facebook Reels; exploring use for other recommendation surfaces if successful

Regulatory & Legal

  • EU and U.S. Regulatory Environment: monitoring increasing legal and regulatory headwinds that could significantly impact business and financial results
  • Texas Facial Recognition Lawsuit: jury trial scheduled for June 2024 in suit brought by the state of Texas regarding use of facial recognition technology; could ultimately result in a material loss
  • Legal Accruals: recognized accruals in Q1 related to ongoing legal matters (contributing to G&A increase; details in 10-Q)
  • TikTok Ban/Sale: following events closely but too early to assess impact or what it would mean for Meta's business

Operating Efficiency & Headcount

  • Headcount: 69,300+ at end of Q1 (+3% QoQ)
  • Efficiency Discipline: management emphasized continuing the efficiency-oriented operating discipline built during 2023's "Year of Efficiency"; being very disciplined with allocation of new resources; carefully managing headcount and other expense growth
  • Resource Reallocation: broadly shifting resources from other areas (compute and otherwise) to advance AI efforts

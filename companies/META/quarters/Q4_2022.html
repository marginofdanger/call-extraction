Q1 2023 Guidance

  • Revenue: $26.0B to $28.5B (corresponds to -7% to +2% YoY growth)
  • Foreign Currency Headwind: ~2% headwind to YoY total revenue growth (less headwind than Q4)
  • Macro Assumptions: Wide range reflects continuation of uncertain and volatile macroeconomic environment; close monitoring of advertising demand

Full Year 2023 Guidance

Expense Outlook

  • Total Expenses: $89B to $95B (lowered from prior outlook of $94B to $100B)
  • Expense Reduction Drivers: (1) Slower payroll growth / broad hiring freeze in place / slower pace of hiring; (2) Slower cost of revenue growth due to extended useful lives of non-AI servers in Q4; (3) Estimated $1B in facilities consolidation restructuring charges (down from prior $2B estimate; portion already recognized in Q4 2022)
  • Hiring Target: No end-of-year hiring target to share; broad hiring freeze in place with ongoing evaluation of which roles to open
  • Additional Restructuring Charges: Possible as efficiency efforts progress further

CapEx Outlook

  • CapEx: $30B to $33B (lowered from prior estimate of $34B to $37B)
  • CapEx Reduction Drivers: Shift to new data center architecture that is more cost efficient, cheaper and faster to build, and can support both AI and non-AI workloads; phased approach with less initial capacity/capital outlay but ability to flex up quickly
  • CapEx Allocation: Substantially all capital expenditures continue to support the Family of Apps

Tax Outlook

  • Full Year 2023 Tax Rate: Expected in the low 20s percentage (absent changes to U.S. tax law)

Long-Term Targets and Strategic Direction

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $32.2B (-4% YoY; +2% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Foreign Currency Impact: ~$2B headwind (revenue would have been ~$2B higher at constant FX rates vs Q4 2021)
  • Total Expenses: $25.8B (+22% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $6.4B
  • Operating Margin: 20%
  • Tax Rate: 24%
  • Net Income: $4.7B
  • Diluted EPS: $1.76
  • CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases): $9.2B
  • Free Cash Flow: $5.3B
  • Headcount: ~86,400 employees (includes substantial majority of ~11,000 layoff-impacted employees still on payroll as of December 31 due to legal requirements; vast majority expected off headcount by end of Q1 2023)
  • Restructuring Costs: $4.2B in Q4 related to layoffs, office facilities, and data center asset write-downs

Expense Detail

  • Cost of Revenue: +31% YoY (driven by write-down of certain data center assets and growth in infrastructure-related costs)
  • R&D: +39% YoY
  • Marketing & Sales: +4% YoY (operating lease impairments and employee-related costs offset by lower marketing spend)
  • G&A: -7% YoY (decrease in legal-related expenses more than offset impairments and employee-related costs)

Ad Metrics

  • Ad Impressions: +23% YoY (driven primarily by Asia Pacific and Rest of World regions)
  • Average Price Per Ad: -22% YoY (driven by strong impression growth from lower monetizing surfaces/regions, lower advertiser demand, and foreign currency depreciation)
  • Advertiser Conversions: Over 20% more conversions YoY with decline in cost per acquisition, resulting in higher returns on ad spend

Revenue by User Geography

  • North America: Flat YoY
  • Asia Pacific: -3% YoY
  • Europe: -16% YoY
  • Rest of World: +5% YoY

Advertising Demand by Vertical

  • Largest negative contributors to YoY decline: Financial services and technology (relatively smaller revenue shares)
  • Online Commerce: Growth still negative YoY, but pace of decline slowed vs Q3
  • CPG: Growth remained negative YoY
  • Largest positive contributors to YoY growth: Travel and health care (relatively smaller verticals in absolute share)

Family of Apps Segment

  • Family of Apps Revenue: $31.4B (-4% YoY)
  • Family of Apps Ad Revenue: $31.3B (-4% YoY; +2% on constant currency basis)
  • Family of Apps Other Revenue: $184M (+19% YoY; strong business messaging revenue growth from WhatsApp Business Platform, partially offset by decline in other line items)
  • Family of Apps Expenses: $20.8B (+23% YoY; 81% of overall expenses; driven by restructuring-related expenses and infrastructure cost growth)
  • Family of Apps Operating Income: $10.7B
  • Family of Apps Operating Margin: 34%

Reality Labs Segment

  • Reality Labs Revenue: $727M (-17% YoY; due to lower Quest 2 sales)
  • Reality Labs Expenses: $5.0B (+20% YoY; driven by employee-related costs and restructuring-related expenses)
  • Reality Labs Operating Loss: $4.3B

Engagement Metrics

  • Family Daily Active People (DAP): ~2.96B (December 2022)
  • Family Monthly Active People (MAP): ~3.74B (December 2022; Mark Zuckerberg noted "more than 3.7 billion people monthly")
  • Facebook Daily Active Users (DAU): 2.0B (first time reaching 2B; +4% YoY or +71M)
  • Facebook Monthly Active Users (MAU): 2.96B (+51M or +2% YoY)
  • Facebook DAU/MAU Ratio: ~67%

Product Metrics

  • Click-to-Message Ads: $10B run rate
  • Reels Plays (Facebook + Instagram): More than doubled over the last year
  • Reels Social Resharing: More than doubled on both apps in the last 6 months alone
  • Reels Monetization Efficiency (Facebook): Doubled in the past 6 months
  • Reels Advertiser Adoption: Over 40% of advertisers using Reels ads across apps
  • Shop Ads Beta Revenue Run Rate: Hundreds of millions of dollars; triple-digit growth in both revenue and adoption across Q4
  • VR Apps >$1M Revenue: Over 200 apps on Meta VR devices
  • WhatsApp Avatars: Over 100M people created avatars; ~1 in 5 using avatar as WhatsApp profile photo

Balance Sheet / Capital Return

  • Cash and Marketable Securities (end of year): $40.7B
  • Share Repurchases (Q4): $6.9B
  • Share Repurchases (Full Year 2022): $27.9B
  • Remaining Repurchase Authorization (Dec 31): $10.9B on prior authorization
  • New Share Repurchase Authorization: $40B increase announced

Reality Labs

  • Reality Labs Losses: Expected to increase in 2023 vs 2022; continued meaningful investment given significant long-term opportunities

DSA Compliance

  • Digital Services Act (DSA) Costs: Meaningful but manageable; already factored into total expense guidance

Profitability Framework

  • Compounding Earnings Growth: Management committed to delivering compounding earnings growth while investing aggressively in future technology
  • CapEx as % of Revenue: Intention to bring CapEx as a percent of revenue down over the longer term; near-term capital intensity depends on revenue outlook and AI capacity needs

Reels Monetization

  • Reels Revenue Headwind Neutrality: On track to be roughly neutral by end of 2023 or early 2024 (through improving Reels monetization efficiency and growing incremental engagement)
  • Reels vs Feed Monetization Gap: Took several years to close Stories vs Feed gap; expected to take longer for Reels

Click-to-Message Ads

  • Click-to-Message Ads Incremental Demand: Over half of click-to-message advertisers exclusively use click-to-message ads on Meta's platform (indicating incremental demand)
  • Growth Levers: More businesses adopting via more entry points, simplified creation flows, partner integrations; better ROI and down-funnel optimization; growing business messaging ecosystem (e.g., WhatsApp business directory search)

Year of Efficiency -- Restructuring and Cost Management

  • 2023 Management Theme: "Year of Efficiency" -- sustained focus on increasing efficiency across execution of top priorities
  • Layoffs Completed: ~11,000 employees in late 2022; described as "beginning of our focus on efficiency and not the end"
  • Organizational Restructuring: Flattening org structure, removing layers of middle management to accelerate decision-making
  • AI Productivity Tools: Deploying AI tools to help engineers be more productive
  • Project Rationalization: More proactive about cutting underperforming or less crucial projects
  • Infrastructure Optimization: Working with infrastructure team to deliver road map while spending less on CapEx
  • Data Center Architecture: Streamlined to new architecture -- more cost efficient, more flexible, supports both AI and non-AI workloads; phased build approach with less initial capital outlay
  • Office Facilities: Consolidating office footprint; ~$1B estimated restructuring charges in 2023

AI Discovery Engine and Recommendation Systems

  • AI Recommendation Content Growth: Recommended content expected to grow as a percent of overall feeds in Facebook and Instagram; may reach ~30-40% by end of 2023 (Zuckerberg: "I don't know if it will be a majority by the end of this year, but maybe it will be 30-ish percent, 40%, something in that zone")
  • Model Evolution: Shifted from CPU-based to GPU-based models; using larger models requiring more computation with significant engagement gains
  • Discovery Engine (Non-Reels): Recommended photos, links, groups, and content on Facebook and Instagram driving incremental engagement; already profitable since existing content is already well-monetized

Generative AI Strategy

  • Key Focus Area for 2023: One of two biggest themes (alongside efficiency)
  • Applications: Large language models and diffusion models for generating images, videos, avatars, 3D assets across all products
  • Creator Empowerment: Long-term goal to empower creators to be more productive and creative
  • Scaling Challenge: Cost of generating images/videos/chat interactions is "cents or fraction of a cent" -- key challenge is making these workloads more efficient at scale for large user base
  • Timeline: Multiple launches expected in 2023

Metaverse and Reality Labs Strategy

  • Three Major Areas Within Reality Labs: (1) Augmented reality (largest area by budget, still a research problem, no shipped product yet); (2) VR (starting to ramp -- Quest 2, Quest Pro); (3) Metaverse software platform (smallest by budget but potentially most critical; software less capital-intensive)
  • Next-Generation Consumer Headset: Expected to launch later in 2023 with Meta Reality (mixed reality) technology; expected to establish MR as baseline for all headsets going forward and eventually AR glasses
  • Mixed Reality (MR): Quest Pro shipped as first mainstream mixed reality device; MR ecosystem relatively new but expected to grow significantly over next few years
  • Reality Labs Investment Commitment: No signals suggesting a shift in long-term Reality Labs strategy; efficiency principles will apply but investment will continue meaningfully

Advertising Technology and Signal Recovery

  • ATT (App Tracking Transparency) Impact: Still an absolute headwind to revenue; lapping its rollout and adoption; making progress mitigating impact through advertiser tools, on-site conversion formats, and AI investments in privacy-enhancing technologies
  • On-Site Conversion Formats: Click-to-message ads, lead ads, and shop ads bringing conversions on-platform to offset signal loss
  • CAPI Gateway: Strong adoption among advertisers as a signal recovery tool
  • Privacy-Enhancing Technologies: Longer-term AI investment to enable performance and privacy-safe ads
  • Android Privacy Changes: Too early to know outcome; Google taking collaborative approach with industry
  • EU DPC Ruling (Data for Ad Targeting): Meta disagrees with ruling; believes current approach is GDPR-compliant; appealing substance and fines; does not expect decisions to affect ability to provide personalized advertising in EU

Business Messaging Monetization

  • Third Revenue Pillar Vision: Facebook and Instagram are first two pillars; messaging expected to come online as third pillar over next few years
  • Click-to-WhatsApp Ads: Scaling as part of overall click-to-message growth
  • WhatsApp Business Platform: Continuing to onboard more businesses for customer questions, updates, and direct-in-chat sales (e.g., Air France using WhatsApp for boarding passes in 22 countries and 4 languages)
  • Business Messaging Revenue: Strong growth contributing to Family of Apps other revenue (+19% YoY in Q4)

Q4 2022 Actuals (Quarterly Results)

Full Year 2022 Actuals

  • Total Revenue: $116.6B (per S&P consensus table: $116,609M actual vs $116,289M consensus, +0.27% surprise)
  • Revenue Growth: Negative YoY (first time in company history)
  • Normalized EPS (FY): $8.59 (per S&P consensus table: vs $9.05 consensus, -5.08% surprise)
  • CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases): $9.2B in Q4 alone (quarterly figure; full year driven by servers, data centers, network infrastructure)
  • Share Repurchases (Full Year): $27.9B
  • Cash and Marketable Securities (year-end): $40.7B
  • Free Cash Flow (Q4): $5.3B
  • Q4 Restructuring Costs: $4.2B (related to layoffs, office facilities consolidation, data center asset write-downs)

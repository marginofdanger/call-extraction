Context

  • New First quarter in our tracking series

Q2 2022 Guidance

  • Revenue: $28B-$30B (midpoint: $29B)
  • FX Assumption: ~3% headwind to YoY growth based on current exchange rates
  • Guidance Assumptions: continuation of trends impacting Q1 revenue growth, including softness in back half of Q1 coinciding with war in Ukraine; ongoing ATT headwinds; incremental headwinds from iOS 15 and other regulatory changes

Full Year 2022 Guidance

  • Total Expenses: $87B-$92B (lowered from prior outlook of $90B-$95B)
  • Expense Growth Driver: primarily Family of Apps segment, followed by Reality Labs
  • Capital Expenditures (incl. principal payments on finance leases): $29B-$34B (unchanged from prior estimate)
  • Tax Rate: expected above Q1 rate of 16%, in the high teens (absent changes to U.S. tax law)
  • Hiring: adjusted plans for hiring and expense growth given revenue headwinds

Long-Term Financial Framework

  • Family of Apps Long-Term Goal: return to better revenue growth rates over time; sustain high operating margins
  • Reality Labs Investment Philosophy: generate sufficient FoA operating income growth to fund Reality Labs investment while still growing overall profitability (acknowledged this will not happen in 2022 given revenue headwinds)
  • Reality Labs Monetization Timeline: "later this decade"; primarily laying groundwork for "a very exciting 2030s" when Metaverse expected to be established as primary computing platform
  • Reality Labs Hardware Cycle: multiple product teams building 2-3 versions into the future in parallel (VR and AR); expenses from staffing these teams reflected in current results; growth rates in expenses expected to come down once teams are fully staffed

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $27.9B (+7% YoY; +10% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Foreign Currency Impact: Revenue would have been ~$893M higher had FX rates remained constant with Q1 2021
  • Total Expenses: $19.4B (+31% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $8.5B
  • Operating Margin: 31%
  • Tax Rate: 16%
  • Net Income: $7.5B
  • Diluted EPS: $2.72 (vs. $2.51 consensus; +8.37% surprise)
  • Capital Expenditures (incl. principal payments on finance leases): $5.5B
  • Free Cash Flow: $8.5B
  • Share Repurchases: $9.4B of Class A common stock in Q1
  • Cash and Marketable Securities (end of quarter): $43.9B
  • Headcount: 77,800+ full-time employees (+28% YoY; +5,800 net new hires in Q1, majority in technical functions)

Expense Line Item Detail

  • Cost of Revenue: +17% YoY (driven by core infrastructure investments, payments to partners, content-related costs)
  • R&D: +48% YoY (driven by hiring to support Family of Apps and Reality Labs)
  • Marketing & Sales: +16% YoY (driven by hiring and marketing spend)
  • G&A: +45% YoY (driven by legal-related costs and employee-related costs)

Ad Revenue Metrics

  • Total Ad Revenue: $27B (+6% YoY; +10% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Ad Impressions Growth: +15% YoY (driven primarily by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
  • Average Price Per Ad: -8% YoY (driven primarily by ongoing mix shift in ad impressions towards regions and services that monetize at lower rates)

Ad Revenue by Geography (YoY Growth)

  • Rest of World: +21% YoY
  • Asia Pacific: +20% YoY
  • North America: +1% YoY
  • Europe: flat YoY (disproportionately challenged by war in Ukraine)
  • North America Ad Impressions: down YoY in Q1 (rate improved from Q4; primarily demand-driven deceleration)

Family of Apps Segment

  • Family of Apps Revenue: $27.2B (+6% YoY)
  • Family of Apps Ad Revenue: $27B (+6% YoY; +10% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Family of Apps Other Revenue: $215M (+9% YoY; growth from WhatsApp Business API offset decline in payments-related revenue from Games)
  • Family of Apps Expenses: $15.7B (+27% YoY; driven by employee-related expenses, infrastructure costs, legal costs)
  • Family of Apps Operating Income: $11.5B
  • Family of Apps Operating Margin: 42%

Engagement Metrics

  • Family Daily Active People (DAP): ~2.9B (March 2022)
  • Family Monthly Active People (MAP): ~3.6B (March 2022)
  • Facebook Daily Active Users (DAU): 1.96B (+4% YoY; +82M YoY)
  • Facebook Monthly Active Users (MAU): 2.94B (+3% YoY; +83M YoY)
  • Facebook DAU/MAU Ratio: ~67%
  • Europe DAUs and MAUs: declined sequentially (negatively impacted by loss of users in Russia following government block)

Engagement Qualitative Highlights

  • Reels Share of Instagram Time: >20% of time spent on Instagram
  • Video Share of Facebook Time: 50% of time spent on Facebook
  • Click-to-Messaging Ads: already a multibillion-dollar business with healthy double-digit YoY growth in Q1

Reality Labs Segment

  • Reality Labs Revenue: $695M (+30% YoY; driven by Quest 2 sales)
  • Reality Labs Expenses: $3.7B (+55% YoY; driven by employee-related costs, R&D operating expenses, cost of goods sold)
  • Reality Labs Operating Loss: $3.0B

Apple iOS / Signal Loss Impact

  • Estimated ATT Impact: ~$10B order of magnitude (shared prior quarter; still believed to be that order of magnitude; not a precise point estimate; will not be updated)
  • ATT Lapping Context: impact existed in second half of 2021, so it is not entirely incremental in 2022
  • Additional Headwinds: incremental targeting and measurement headwinds expected from iOS 15 and other regulatory changes beyond ATT
  • Q3 Growth Rate Expectation: expected to be "modestly higher" than Q2 rate since Q3 will lap the first full quarter impact of ATT
  • Measurement Underreporting Gap: "good part" of underreporting gap has been closed and shared with advertisers; rest of the gap will take longer to close; particularly challenging for small businesses
  • iOS 16: no information to share at this time

Mitigation Strategy (Three Horizons)

  • Near Term: improvements to current solutions; privacy-enhanced technologies; Conversions API integration (including new Conversions API Gateway for SMBs); conversion modeling for measurement
  • Medium Term: move clients towards on-site conversions (Click-to-Messaging ads, lead gen ads, shop ads)
  • Long Term: rebuild ads stack to employ more machine learning and AI to be more effective with less data

Reels Monetization Strategy

  • Current State: Reels monetizes at a lower rate versus Feed and Stories; "very early days" in terms of ads in Reels
  • Revenue Headwind: Reels creates a near-term drag on revenue because Reels monetization is less than Feed/Stories; expected to shift from headwind to tailwind at some point
  • Stories Monetization Benchmark: Stories ramping began in 2018; by end of 2021, Stories in developed markets monetized on a per-time basis at a similar rate as Feed; still work to be done across the world
  • Reels Monetization Timeline: expected to be a "multiyear journey" similar to Stories
  • Early Advertiser Results: Prose (DTC hair care brand) A/B tested Reels ad placement: 23% lower cost per purchase, 52% higher unique audience reach, 3x higher video completion
  • Wendy's Campaign Example: used range of video formats across Feeds, Stories, Reels, and in-stream for March Madness; reached 52 million people; improved brand and message awareness

AI and Discovery Engine Strategy

  • Discovery Engine Vision: AI-based recommendations showing content from the full universe of content (not just from followed accounts) across all content types (text, links, photos, short-form video, long-form video)
  • Monetization of AI Recommendations: non-Reels AI-recommended content in Feed should "monetize quite naturally"; Reels-specific content has separate monetization ramp
  • AI Investment: significantly increasing investment in AI and machine learning across the company in 2022; large portion going towards ads
  • CapEx Intensity: AI/ML investments add to capital intensity of the business; additional CapEx on top of capacity growth

Business Messaging / Click-to-Messaging Ads

  • Revenue Scale: already a multibillion-dollar business
  • Growth Rate: healthy double-digit YoY growth in Q1
  • Regional Strength: APAC and Rest of World benefiting from strong demand in Click-to-Messaging ads
  • India: particular strength noted in APAC, benefiting from good supply tailwinds in impression growth

Metaverse / Hardware Roadmap

  • Quest 2: continues to be the leading VR headset
  • Project Cambria: higher-end headset launching later in 2022; focused on work use cases; features include improved ergonomics, full color pass-through mixed reality, eye tracking, face tracking
  • Horizon Web Version: planned for later in 2022 to enable Metaverse experiences from more platforms without a headset
  • Horizon Economy: building out Metaverse economy and helping creators make a living; expects to be "meaningfully better at monetization than others in the space"
  • Meta Avatar Integration: upcoming Quest release will embody users with Meta avatar and enable Horizon interaction directly from Quest home

Regulatory Environment

  • EU Digital Markets Act (DMA): expected to have "significant challenges" for the industry; working with European regulators; largely in range of expectations but final text not released; details will matter
  • Transatlantic Data Transfer: pleased with progress on a political agreement; monitoring developments regarding viability and potential impact on European operations
  • Overall Regulatory Assessment: "significantly challenging time" not just for Meta but across the whole industry

User Trends and Regional Commentary

  • Global MAU Outlook for Q2: expected to be flat to down sequentially due to loss of users in Russia (government restrictions implemented late February through early March; some Russian users still counted as active in Q1 MAU calculation)
  • Europe DAU/MAU: expected to decline sequentially into Q2
  • Brazil: macro conditions improved a bit after softness in second half of 2021
  • India: particular strength in APAC; benefiting from impression growth supply tailwinds
  • Engagement vs. Pre-Pandemic: both Facebook and Instagram engagement remain above pre-pandemic levels (globally and in the U.S.)

Workforce and Retention

  • Net New Hires in Q1: 5,800+ (majority in technical functions)
  • Total Headcount: 77,800+ full-time employees (+28% YoY)
  • Attrition: stepped up from pandemic lows but broadly consistent with pre-pandemic levels; "relatively in line with expectations"
  • Hiring Adjustment: plans adjusted for hiring and expense growth given revenue headwinds; shifting internal talent towards high-priority areas rather than solely relying on external hiring

Capital Allocation

  • Share Repurchases in Q1: $9.4B of Class A common stock
  • Cash Position: $43.9B in cash and marketable securities at end of Q1

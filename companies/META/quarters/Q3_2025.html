What Changed vs Q2 2025

  • Raised FY Total Expenses: $116B - $118B (prior: $114B - $118B)
  • Changed Ad Impressions Growth: +14% YoY (prior: +11% YoY)
  • Changed Avg Price Per Ad: +10% YoY (prior: +9% YoY)
  • Changed Operating Margin: 40% (prior: 43%)

Q4 2025 Guidance (Next Quarter)

  • Total Revenue: $56B - $59B (FX ~1% tailwind; strong ad growth, partially offset by lower RL revenue YoY)
  • Reality Labs Revenue (Q4): lower YoY due to lapping Quest 3S launch; AI glasses growth offset by Quest headwinds
  • Tax Rate (Q4 2025): 12% - 15%

Full Year 2025 Guidance

  • Total Expenses: $116B - $118B (raised from $114B - $118B; +22% to +24% YoY)
  • CapEx (incl. finance leases): $70B - $72B (raised from $66B - $72B)

2026 Preliminary Outlook

  • CapEx Dollar Growth: "notably larger" in 2026 vs. 2025; growth across all areas, MSL growing most
  • Total Expenses: "significantly faster percentage rate" in 2026 vs. 2025; driven by infrastructure costs and employee compensation (AI talent)
  • Revenue Growth: expects ads + organic engagement investments to enable continued strong growth in 2026
  • Compute Needs: expanding meaningfully beyond expectations; investing via own infrastructure + third-party cloud

Long-Term Targets & Strategic Commentary

  • Onetime Tax Charge Impact: Q3 charge from new U.S. tax law transition; expects significant cash tax savings going forward
  • Infrastructure Strategy: staging data center sites to scale capacity quickly; strategic partnerships for future compute optionality
  • Blue Owl JV: Meta contributes 20% of Louisiana data center costs (matching ownership stake); recorded as investing cash flows, not CapEx

Consolidated Results

  • Total Revenue: $51.2B (+26% YoY; +25% constant currency)
  • Total Expenses: $30.7B (+32% YoY) - Growth accelerated 20pp from Q2 driven by higher legal expenses, accelerated compensation growth (AI talent), and infrastructure cost growth (data center ops, depreciation, cloud spend)
  • Operating Income: $20.5B
  • Operating Margin: 40%
  • Interest and Other Income: $1.1B (unrealized gains on marketable equity securities)
  • Tax Rate: 87% (GAAP; impacted by onetime noncash deferred tax asset reduction under new U.S. tax law)
  • Tax Rate (Adjusted): 14% (excluding the onetime tax charge)
  • Net Income: $2.7B (GAAP)
  • Net Income (Adjusted): $18.6B (excluding the onetime tax charge)
  • Diluted EPS: $1.05 (GAAP)
  • Diluted EPS (Adjusted): $7.25 (excluding the onetime tax charge)
  • CapEx (incl. finance leases): $19.4B (servers, data centers, network infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $10.6B
  • Share Repurchases: $3.2B of Class A common stock
  • Dividends Paid: $1.3B
  • Headcount: 78,400+ employees (+8% YoY; hiring in monetization, infrastructure, RL, MSL, compliance)

Balance Sheet Highlights

  • Cash and Marketable Securities: $44.4B
  • Debt: $28.8B

Family of Apps Segment

  • Family of Apps Revenue: $50.8B (+26% YoY)
  • Family of Apps Ad Revenue: $50.1B (+26% YoY; +25% constant currency)
  • Family of Apps Other Revenue: $690M (+59% YoY; WhatsApp paid messaging + Meta Verified)
  • Ad Impressions Growth: +14% YoY (healthy across all regions; driven by engagement/user growth, especially video)
  • Average Price Per Ad: +10% YoY (improved ad performance; partially offset by lower-monetizing regions/surfaces)

Reality Labs Segment

  • Reality Labs Revenue: $470M (+74% YoY) - Growth from retail Quest stocking ahead of holidays + strong AI glasses revenue

Engagement & User Metrics

  • Family Daily Active People (DAP): 3.5B+ (estimated for September 2025)
  • Instagram Monthly Active People: 3B (milestone reached)
  • Threads Daily Actives: 150M+ (recently passed; remains on track to become category leader)
  • Meta AI Monthly Active Users: 1B+
  • Time Spent on Facebook: +5% in Q3 (driven by AI recommendation systems)
  • Time Spent on Threads: +10% in Q3 (driven by ranking optimizations in Q3 alone)
  • Video Time Spent on Instagram: +30%+ YoY
  • U.S. Time Spent on Facebook and Instagram: double-digit growth YoY (video strength + healthy nonvideo growth on FB)

Ad Platform & Monetization Metrics

  • Reels Annual Run Rate: $50B+
  • End-to-End AI-Powered Ad Tools Annual Run Rate: $60B
  • Advantage+ Lead Campaigns: advertisers seeing 14% lower cost per lead on average vs. non-Advantage+ users
  • Lattice Rollout to App Ads: drove nearly 3% gain in conversions for that objective
  • Ads Ranking Model Consolidation: ~100 models cut since Lattice (2023); expect to consolidate another 200 over coming years
  • New Runtime Ads Ranking Model (Pilot): >2% lift in conversions on Instagram in testing
  • Andromeda Improvement: combined retrieval + early-stage ranking models into one; +14% ads quality on Facebook
  • Advantage+ Creative Suite: advertisers using video generation features +20% QoQ
  • Click-to-WhatsApp Ads Revenue: +60% YoY in Q3
  • Business AI Conversations: millions of conversations since July (Philippines and Mexico test markets)
  • AI-Generated Images: 20B+ images created using Meta products to date
  • Vibes (launched September): media generation in the app increased more than tenfold since launch
  • Business Messaging Active Threads: 1B+ daily active threads with business accounts
  • Facebook Reels Freshness: 2x more same-day Reels surfaced vs. start of year

AI Strategy & Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL)

  • Meta Superintelligence Labs: described as having "the highest talent density lab in the industry"; focused on training next-generation models and novel research
  • Superintelligence Timeline: range of views (few years to 5-7+ years); strategy is to front-load capacity for most optimistic cases
  • Compute Stance: FoA and ads in "compute-starved state"; profitable investment opportunities not pursued due to constraints
  • Unified AI System Vision: combining 3 major AI systems (FB, IG, ads) into single unified system for trillions of daily recommendations
  • 2026 Recommendation Roadmap: Evolving IG to surface broader topics (like FB approach); scaling up data/compute for ranking models; leveraging LLMs for content understanding

Advertising Platform Innovation

  • Advantage+ Streamlined Campaigns: global rollout complete for lead campaigns in Q3; now covers sales, app, and lead objectives
  • Advantage+ Adoption Opportunity: many advertisers only use end-to-end automation for a portion of campaigns; broadening adoption
  • AI-Generated Music for Ads: introduced in Q3; music generated aligned with creative tone/message
  • Ads Revenue Investment Pipeline (2026): similar list of revenue investments as the roadmap that paid off in 2025
  • Value-Weighted Conversion Rates: growing strongly YoY; weighted conversions growing faster than impressions

Business Messaging & Business AI

  • Click-to-WhatsApp Ads: revenue grew 60% YoY in Q3
  • Business AI Rollout: expanded to all eligible businesses in Mexico (WhatsApp) and Philippines (Messenger) in Oct
  • U.S. Business AI: rolling out merchant website Business AIs to support full sales funnel
  • WhatsApp Status Ads: gradual introduction continuing; rollout expected complete next year
  • Threads Ads: running globally in feed; optimizing formats/performance before ramping supply
  • Threads Direct Messaging: launched in Q3

Wearables & Reality Labs Hardware

  • Ray-Ban Meta Glasses & Oakley Meta Vanguards: both selling well; improved battery life, camera resolution, new AI capabilities
  • Meta Ray-Ban Display Glasses: first with high-res display; sold out within 48hrs; investing in manufacturing scale-up
  • Meta Neural Band: new interface device for display glasses
  • Orion (AR Prototype): continuing investment; early in curve towards sustaining business
  • Long-Term Hardware Goal: aims for hundreds of millions/billions of users; wearables to become extremely profitable

Legal & Regulatory Risks

  • EU Less Personalized Ads: engaging with European Commission; risk of further changes with significant negative European revenue impact as early as Q4 2025
  • U.S. Youth-Related Trials: a number of trials scheduled for 2026 that "may ultimately result in a material loss"
  • General: increasing headwinds in EU and U.S. that could significantly impact business/financial results

Capital Allocation

  • Blue Owl JV Structure: codevelop data centers with external capital; Meta contributes 20% of costs; long-term capacity optionality
  • External Compute Demand: frequent external requests for API/compute services; potential option if infrastructure overbuilt
  • Budget Process: 2026 budget still in early stages; capacity planning is "particularly dynamic"

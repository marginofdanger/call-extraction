What Changed vs Q1 2022

  • Lowered FY Total Expenses: $85B - $88B (prior: $87B - $92B)
  • Changed Ad Impressions Growth: +15% YoY (prior: +15% YoY)
  • Changed Avg Price Per Ad: -14% YoY (prior: -8%)
  • Changed Operating Margin: 29% (prior: 31%)
  • Changed Reality Labs Op Loss: $2.8B (prior: $3.0B)

Q3 2022 Guidance

  • Total Revenue: $26B-$28.5B (implies midpoint of $27.25B; reflects continuation of weak advertising demand environment driven by broader macroeconomic uncertainty)
  • Reality Labs Revenue: Expected to be lower than Q2 2022 ($452M)
  • Foreign Currency Headwind: ~6% headwind to YoY total revenue growth in Q3 (based on current exchange rates)
  • Key Assumption - Macro: Continuation of the weak advertising demand environment experienced throughout Q2; broad-based weakness across most verticals
  • Key Assumption - Transatlantic Data Transfers: Continuing to monitor developments regarding viability of transatlantic data transfers and potential impact on European operations

Full Year 2022 Guidance

  • Total Expenses: $85B-$88B (lowered from prior outlook of $87B-$92B; reduced hiring and overall expense growth to account for more challenging operating environment)
  • CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases): $30B-$34B (narrowed from prior range of $29B-$34B)
  • Tax Rate: Expected above Q2 rate (18%) and in the high teens for full year 2022 (absent any changes to U.S. tax law)

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $28.8B (-1% YoY; +3% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Foreign Currency Impact: Revenue would have been ~$1.3B higher had FX rates remained constant with Q2 2021
  • Total Expenses: $20.5B (+22% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $8.4B
  • Operating Margin: 29%
  • Tax Rate: 18%
  • Interest and Other Expenses: Loss of $172M (driven mainly by unrealized losses on equity investments)
  • Net Income: $6.7B
  • Diluted EPS: $2.46

Expense Line Items

  • Cost of Revenue: Decreased 4% YoY (growth in core infrastructure and content-related costs more than offset by reduction in Reality Labs loss reserves due to Quest 2 price increase)
  • R&D: Increased 43% YoY (driven by hiring to support Family of Apps and Reality Labs)
  • Marketing and Sales: Increased 10% YoY (driven by hiring and marketing spend)
  • G&A: Increased 53% YoY (driven by legal-related and employee-related costs)

Headcount

  • Net New Hires (Q2): Over 5,700 (majority in technical functions)
  • Total Full-Time Employees: Over 83,500 (+32% YoY)
  • Outlook: Headcount growth expected to slow throughout the rest of 2022 due to reduction in hiring plans

Capital Expenditures and Cash Flow

  • CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases): $7.7B (driven by investments in servers, data centers and network infrastructure; big step-up YoY and sequentially related to server spend including AI infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $4.5B
  • Share Repurchases: $5.1B of Class A common stock in Q2
  • Cash and Marketable Securities: $40.5B (end of quarter)

Ad Monetization Metrics

  • Ad Impressions: Increased 15% YoY (driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
  • Average Price Per Ad: Decreased 14% YoY (driven by reduction in advertiser demand, mix shift toward lower monetizing services/regions, and foreign currency depreciation)

User / Engagement Metrics

  • Family Daily Active People (DAP): ~2.9B (June 2022)
  • Family Monthly Active People (MAP): ~3.6B (June 2022)
  • Facebook DAUs: 1.97B (+3% YoY, +60M YoY)
  • Facebook DAU/MAU Ratio: ~67%
  • Facebook MAUs: 2.93B (+39M YoY, +1% YoY)
  • Europe DAUs and MAUs: Declined sequentially (negatively impacted by loss of users in Russia and Ukraine)

AI-Recommended Content

  • Facebook Feed AI-Recommended Content: ~15% of content recommended by AI from people, groups, or accounts the user does not follow
  • Instagram Feed AI-Recommended Content: A little more than 15% from non-followed accounts
  • Target: These numbers expected to more than double by end of 2023

Reels Performance

  • Reels Time Spent Growth: More than 30% increase in time spent engaging with Reels across Facebook and Instagram (QoQ)
  • Reels Share of Instagram Time: Previously disclosed at 20% of time spent on Instagram (as of Q1 2022)
  • Reels Revenue Run Rate: Crossed $1B annual revenue run rate
  • Reels vs. Stories: Reels has a higher revenue run rate than Stories did at identical times post launch
  • Reels Watch Time Impact (Facebook): 15% increase in watch time in the Reels video player on Facebook alone after launching a new large AI model for recommendations
  • Reels Monetization Status: Does not yet monetize at the same rate as Feed or Stories; still a revenue headwind in near term as engagement shifts to Reels

Family of Apps Segment

  • Total Family of Apps Revenue: $28.4B (-1% YoY)
  • Family of Apps Ad Revenue: $28.2B (-1% YoY; +3% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Family of Apps Other Revenue: $218M (+14% YoY, driven by WhatsApp Business API)
  • Family of Apps Expenses: $17.2B (+23% YoY, driven by employee-related expenses, legal costs, and infrastructure costs)
  • Family of Apps Operating Income: $11.2B
  • Family of Apps Operating Margin: 39%

Ad Revenue by User Geography (YoY Growth)

  • Asia Pacific: +13% (benefited meaningfully from strong growth in click-to-messaging ads)
  • Rest of World: +11% (benefited meaningfully from strong growth in click-to-messaging ads)
  • North America: -4%
  • Europe: -12%
  • FX Impact: Headwind in all international regions; Europe and Asia Pacific experienced the largest impacts

Reality Labs Segment

  • Revenue: $452M (+48% YoY, driven primarily by Quest 2 sales)
  • Expenses: $3.3B (+19% YoY, due to growth in employee-related costs and R&D operating expenses, partially offset by reduction in loss reserves)
  • Operating Loss: $2.8B

Business Messaging and Click-to-Message Ads

  • Click-to-Message Advertiser Adoption: 40% of advertisers already using this format
  • Click-to-Message Revenue: Already a multibillion-dollar business, with strong double-digit YoY growth
  • Users Messaging Businesses: Estimated 1 billion users messaging with a business each week across WhatsApp, Messenger, and Instagram
  • Geographic Strength: Particularly popular with SMBs in emerging markets like Brazil and Mexico; many are new advertisers to Meta who come solely for this format
  • Performance Case Study (RoamHowl Creative): Click-to-Messenger ads resulted in 2.3x more qualified leads and 57% lower cost per lead compared to normal website conversion ads
  • Performance Case Study (Wild Alaskan Company): Adding Reels to campaign resulted in 36% increase in return on ad spend, similar uplift in new subscribers, and 26% lower cost per new subscriber

Reels Monetization Trajectory

  • Stories Monetization Timeline: Stories started rolling out in earnest in 2018; did not reach parity with Feed on a monetization-per-time-spent basis in developed markets until 2022 (multiyear journey)
  • Reels Status: Still very early in the multiyear monetization journey
  • Reels Revenue Headwind: Engagement shifting to Reels is an overall headwind on the business in 2022; expected to be a tailwind in the long run but not in 2022
  • Reels Net Impact on Engagement: Additive to total time spent (net positive), though some cannibalization of Feed and Stories engagement

Headcount and Cost Discipline

  • Hiring Plan: Steadily reducing headcount growth over the next year; many teams expected to shrink to shift energy to other areas
  • Reported Growth Lag: Headcount growth will still appear substantial for the next few quarters due to hires earlier in 2022, but should continue to decline over time
  • 2023 Headcount: No specific markers given for 2023; plan to maintain discipline on headcount growth going into 2023 budget cycle
  • Expense Focus: Reduced hiring and overall expense growth plans for 2022; directing resources toward company priorities

Metaverse / Reality Labs Strategy

  • Investment Posture: Reality Labs remains a key priority area with increasing investment; discipline applied across entire operating posture
  • Horizon (Social Metaverse Platform): Web version of Horizon to be launched later in 2022, accessible on all platforms, expected to dramatically increase number of users
  • Avatars Store: Launched with digital clothes from leading fashion houses; expanding selection and fidelity of Avatar system
  • Project Cambria: High-end VR device focused on professional users and work, with high-resolution color mixed reality, to be released later in 2022
  • Within Acquisition: FTC announced lawsuit to block acquisition of Within (VR fitness app); Meta believes acquisition would be good for competition and expand the VR ecosystem
  • Long-Term View: Management believes developing metaverse platforms will unlock hundreds of billions of dollars, if not trillions, over time

AI Strategy and Discovery Engine

  • AI Investment Focus: AI identified as the major technology wave driving the business today; heavy investment in AI compute CapEx
  • Discovery Engine: Social feeds transitioning from primarily people/accounts you follow to increasingly AI-recommended content from across Facebook and Instagram
  • Content Recommendation Scope: AI recommendations span all content types -- text, images, links, group content, short-form video (Reels), comments, and more (differentiated from competitors focused on single format)
  • AI for Ads: Investing in AI to improve ads ranking and measurement capabilities; AI-driven products include Advantage Detailed Targeting and Advantage Lookalikes
  • AI for Content Moderation: Most content moderation done through AI today; metrics for proactive identification of harmful content generally moving in the right direction
  • Privacy-Enhancing Technologies: Developing tools like Private Lift (beta testing with large advertisers) for measurement while minimizing personal information processed; investing in Conversions API for direct, reliable, privacy-safe advertiser data connection

Leadership Transitions

  • Sheryl Sandberg: Last earnings call as COO; transitioning to Meta's Board of Directors
  • Javier Olivan and Marne Levine: Taking on expanded roles to carry the business forward
  • Dave Wehner: Transitioning from CFO to Chief Strategy Officer (overseeing strategy and corporate development teams), effective November 2022
  • Susan Li: Promoted to CFO starting later in 2022 (currently runs financial planning process)

Capital Allocation

  • Share Repurchases: $5.1B in Q2 2022; substantial amount remaining in buyback program; expect to continue buybacks as part of capital allocation strategy
  • Cash Position: $40.5B in cash and marketable securities at end of Q2

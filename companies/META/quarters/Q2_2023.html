What Changed vs Q1 2023

  • Raised Revenue Guidance (Next Q) midpoint up 8.1% vs prior quarter ($33.2B vs $30.8B)
  • Raised FY Total Expenses midpoint up 1.7% vs prior quarter ($89.5B vs $88.0B)
  • Lowered FY CapEx midpoint down 9.5% vs prior quarter ($28.5B vs $31.5B)
  • Changed Operating Margin: 29% (prior: 25%)
  • Changed Reality Labs Op Loss: $3.7B (prior: $4.0B)
  • New NEW: New product launch discussed

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $32.0B (+11% YoY; +12% on constant currency basis)
  • Total Expenses: $22.6B (+10% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $9.4B
  • Operating Margin: 29%
  • Tax Rate: 16% (lower than prior full year outlook due to higher share price providing higher tax deduction)
  • Net Income: $7.8B
  • Diluted EPS: $2.98
  • Capital Expenditures (including principal payments on finance leases): $6.4B (driven by investments in data centers, servers, and network infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $11.0B (significantly benefiting from a deferral of income taxes expected to be paid in Q4)
  • Share Repurchases: $793M of Class A common stock in Q2
  • Cash and Marketable Securities: $53.4B (end of quarter)
  • Headcount: ~71,400 employees (down 7% from Q1)

Expense Line Item Detail

  • Cost of Revenue: increased 15% YoY (driven primarily by infrastructure-related costs)
  • R&D: increased 8% YoY (driven mainly by headcount-related costs from Reality Labs and Family of Apps segments, plus restructuring costs)
  • Marketing and Sales: decreased 12% YoY (due mostly to lower marketing spend and payroll-related costs)
  • G&A: increased 39% YoY (due primarily to increase in legal accruals, partially offset by lower payroll-related costs)

Headcount

  • Q2 Headcount: ~71,400 (down 7% QoQ from Q1)
  • Layoff Impact: Q2 headcount still included roughly half of the approximately 10,000 employees impacted by 2023 layoffs
  • Q3 Expectation: headcount will no longer include the vast majority of impacted employees

Family of Apps — Engagement Metrics

  • Family Daily Active People (DAP): ~3.07B in June
  • Family Monthly Active People (MAP): ~3.88B in June
  • Facebook Monthly Active Users (MAU): 3.03B in June (+3% YoY, or +96M); crossed 3B MAU for the first time
  • Facebook Daily Active Users (DAU): 2.06B (+5% YoY, or +96M)
  • Facebook DAU/MAU Ratio: ~68%
  • Monthly Active People Using at Least One App: 3.8B+

Family of Apps Revenue

  • FoA Total Revenue: $31.7B (+12% YoY)
  • FoA Ad Revenue: $31.5B (+12% YoY; +13% on constant currency basis)
  • FoA Other Revenue: $225M (+3% YoY; strong business messaging revenue growth from WhatsApp Business platform partially offset by decline in other line items)

Ad Revenue by Vertical (contribution to YoY growth, ranked)

  • 1. Online Commerce: largest contributor to YoY growth (benefited from strong spend among advertisers in China reaching customers in other markets) 2. Entertainment and Media: second largest contributor 3. CPG: third largest contributor

Ad Revenue by Geography (YoY growth)

  • Rest of World: +16%
  • Europe: +14%
  • North America: +11%
  • Asia Pacific: +10%
  • FX Impact: headwind to advertising revenue growth in all international regions

Ad Impressions and Pricing

  • Ad Impressions: +34% YoY (primarily driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
  • Average Price Per Ad: -16% YoY (driven by strong impression growth, especially from lower monetizing surfaces and regions)

Family of Apps Expenses and Profitability

  • FoA Expenses: $18.6B (~82% of overall expenses; +8% YoY due primarily to legal-related expenses and restructuring charges, partially offset by decrease in non-headcount-related operating expenses including marketing)
  • FoA Operating Income: $13.1B
  • FoA Operating Margin: 41%

Reels Metrics

  • Reels Plays: exceed 200B per day across Facebook and Instagram
  • Reels Annual Revenue Run Rate: exceeding $10B (up from $3B last fall)
  • Reels Advertiser Adoption: more than 3/4 of advertisers now using Reels ads

Business Messaging and Click-to-Message

  • WhatsApp Business App Monthly Users: 200M+
  • Businesses Using Paid Messaging Products: doubled YoY
  • Click-to-WhatsApp Ads Revenue: daily revenue growing over 80% YoY
  • Meta Advantage Adoption: almost all advertisers using at least one AI-driven ad product

Q3 2023 Revenue Guidance

  • Revenue: $32.0B-$34.5B (midpoint: $33.25B, implying ~20% YoY growth at midpoint)
  • FX Assumption: ~3% tailwind to YoY total revenue growth (based on current exchange rates)

Drivers of Q3 Acceleration

  • Lapping a much weaker demand period (Q3 2022 revenue declined 4.5% YoY)
  • FX flipping from a 1-point headwind in Q2 to a 3-point tailwind in Q3
  • Continued execution across core monetization work and engagement growth

Total Expenses

  • Total Expenses: $88B-$91B (raised from prior range of $86B-$90B due to legal-related expenses recorded in Q2)
  • Restructuring Costs Included: ~$4B (related to facilities consolidation charges and severance and other personnel costs)
  • Reality Labs Operating Losses: expected to increase YoY in 2023

Capital Expenditures

  • CapEx: $27B-$30B (lowered from prior estimate of $30B-$33B)
  • Drivers of Reduction: both cost savings (particularly on non-AI servers where there was underutilized capacity) and shifts in CapEx into 2024 from delays in data center projects and server/equipment deliveries (not a reduction in overall investment plans)

Tax Rate

  • Tax Rate: expected to be similar to Q2 2023 (~16%) for the rest of the year, absent changes to U.S. tax law

Expense Growth Drivers

  • Infrastructure-Related Costs: expected to be higher; depreciation expenses in 2024 expected to increase by a larger amount than in 2023 given increased capital investments in recent years; higher operating costs from running a larger infrastructure footprint
  • Payroll Expenses: anticipated growth as workforce composition evolves toward higher-cost technical roles
  • Reality Labs Operating Losses: expected to increase meaningfully YoY due to ongoing product development efforts in AR, VR, and investments to further scale the ecosystem; driven by combination of both headcount-related and operating costs
  • Headcount: newly budgeted headcount growth expected to be relatively low, but hiring in key areas will continue; many teams chose to let people go to hire different people with different skills, and much of that hiring will spill into 2024

Capital Expenditures

  • CapEx: expected to grow in 2024 vs. 2023
  • Drivers: investments across both data centers and servers, particularly in support of AI work
  • Data Center Spend: expected to grow in 2024 as Meta ramps up construction on sites with new data center architecture announced late 2022
  • Server Spend: expected to increase for both AI workloads (core AI ranking/recommendation + next-gen AI/gen AI efforts) and non-AI workloads (server refresh and capacity additions to support continued growth)
  • Capital Intensity: management mindful of intention to reduce capital intensity of investments over time
  • AI CapEx Uncertainty: exact level of AI CapEx unclear since it depends on how quickly new AI products scale; management wants capacity in place in case products scale very quickly

Threads Launch and Early Performance

  • Launch Timing: earlier in July 2023
  • Initial Growth: "unprecedented growth out of the gate"
  • Retention: daily return rates better than expected
  • Current Focus: retention and improving the basics; growth to follow, then monetization last
  • Development Approach: built by a relatively small team on a tight timeline; cited as biggest example of Year of Efficiency cultural changes enabling higher-quality products built faster
  • Long-Term Vision: potential to become "a fifth great app in the Family of Apps"
  • Monetization Timeline: not until the product reaches hundreds of millions of users and engagement is fully mature (described as years away)

AI Strategy and Llama 2

  • Llama 2: open-sourced in partnership with Microsoft for both research and commercial use
  • AI-Recommended Content: fastest-growing category of content on Facebook's feed; has driven a 7% increase in overall time spent on the platform since introduction
  • Llama 2 Licensing: open-source but includes a term that the largest companies (those with public cloud offerings) must negotiate a business arrangement with Meta to use it commercially
  • Revenue Expectation from Llama: not expected to result in a separate top-line enterprise revenue line; not expected to be a large amount of revenue in the near term
  • Three Categories of Generative AI Products: 1. AI agents (for consumers, creators, and businesses) 2. Generative AI-powered features (e.g., advertising creative tools via AI Sandbox: automatic text variation, background generation, image outcropping) 3. Internal productivity and efficiency tools (engineer code assistance, company knowledge base)
  • AI Agent Strategy: Meta envisions every business eventually having an AI agent that people can message and interact with; expected to help messaging monetization scale beyond low-labor-cost countries
  • Meta Lattice: new model architecture deployed that learns to predict ad performance across a variety of data sets and optimization goals
  • Open Source Benefits Expected: safety/security improvements from community scrutiny, efficiency gains (e.g., quantization), and ecosystem standardization that aids recruiting

Reels Monetization Progress

  • Annual Revenue Run Rate: exceeding $10B across apps (up from $3B last fall)
  • Advertiser Adoption: more than 3/4 of advertisers now using Reels ads
  • Monetization Gap: focused on further reducing the Reels revenue headwind and narrowing the monetization efficiency gap with more mature surfaces (Feed, Stories)
  • Structural Limitation: time on Reels expected to monetize at a lower rate than Stories and Feed "for the foreseeable future" since people scroll more slowly through video content

Business Messaging Monetization

  • WhatsApp Business App Users: 200M monthly users
  • Click-to-WhatsApp Ads: new capability for WhatsApp Business app users to create Click-to-WhatsApp ads for Facebook and Instagram without needing a Facebook account
  • Click-to-WhatsApp Revenue Growth: daily revenue growing over 80% YoY
  • Paid Messaging Adoption: number of businesses using paid messaging products doubled YoY
  • WhatsApp Channels: started rolling out in June 2023; expanding to more countries through rest of 2023
  • Future Focus: enabling businesses to optimize for conversions further down the funnel in messaging applications; investing in scaling other on-site objectives like lead generation and shops ads

Year of Efficiency and Cost Discipline

  • 2023 Layoff Status: major layoffs completed; rest of 2023 focused on creating stability, removing barriers, introducing AI-powered tools for productivity
  • 2024 Planning Philosophy: continue running the company "as lean as possible" for cultural reasons despite improved financial results
  • Investment Discipline: bringing Year of Efficiency discipline and habits forward into future planning; incremental investment targeted at areas with most significant opportunity (AI and metaverse)
  • Capital Structure: continue modestly evolving capital structure over time; executed second bond offering in May 2023; expect measured pace of future debt raises to improve overall cost of capital while maintaining positive or neutral net cash balance

Advantage+ and AI-Powered Advertising

  • Advantage+ Adoption: strong adoption, particularly with e-commerce and retail verticals; good traction with CPG (especially DTC brands)
  • Meta Advantage Overall: almost all advertisers using at least one AI-driven product
  • Product Pipeline: expanding the Advantage+ portfolio beyond Advantage+ Shopping campaigns to apply the same playbook to many different steps of the end-to-end ad buying experience
  • Conversion Growth: remained strong in Q2
  • AI Sandbox: testing playground for generative AI-powered ad tools (automatic text variation, background generation, image outcropping)

Reality Labs and Metaverse Strategy

  • Quest 3: launching at Connect conference on September 27, 2023 - Most powerful headset yet with better displays, resolution, next-gen Qualcomm chipset with 2x graphics performance - 40% thinner than Quest 2 - Mixed reality capabilities (blends physical and virtual worlds) - Expected to be "the first mainstream accessible device" for mixed reality, targeting many millions of users - Major near-term driver of Reality Labs expenses
  • Roblox on Quest: coming to Quest with open beta on App Lab
  • Horizon: team focused on retention, making good progress
  • Long-Term Thesis: 1-2 billion people have glasses today; in the future all will be smart glasses; all TV/computer time expected to shift toward more immersive VR experiences
  • Competitive Assessment: management does not view competitors as building things ahead of where Meta is; gives confidence in long-term thesis

Regulatory Environment

  • EU-U.S. Data Transfers: European Commission adopted final adequacy decision, allowing Meta to continue providing services in Europe (positive development)
  • Ongoing Headwinds: increasing legal and regulatory headwinds in the EU and U.S. that "could significantly impact our business and our financial results"

Q4 2023 Qualitative Commentary

  • Not providing Q4 revenue outlook at this point
  • Tailwinds: weaker prior year compare applies to Q4; at current FX rates, currency would be a larger tailwind in Q4 than in Q3
  • Uncertainty: sizable fluctuations in advertiser demand over the last year; while demand is strong now, harder to predict further forward; wide range of possible outcomes in Q4

Q2 2023 Revenue Acceleration Drivers (Q&A Detail)

  • Lapping weaker demand: first full quarter of war in Ukraine and suspension of services in Russia in prior year period
  • Improved supply and ad performance: including improved Reels monetization as the Reels revenue headwind was worked down
  • Lower FX headwinds: reduced currency impact in Q2 vs. prior quarters

Q2 2023 Actuals

  • RL Revenue: $276M (-39% YoY, due to lower Quest 2 sales)
  • RL Expenses: $4.0B (+23% YoY, due to lapping a reduction in Reality Labs loss reserves in Q2 of last year as well as growth in employee-related costs)
  • RL Operating Loss: $3.7B
  • Cumulative RL Losses: referenced as passing $40B (analyst comment during Q&A)

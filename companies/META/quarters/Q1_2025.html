What Changed vs Q4 2024

  • Raised Revenue Guidance (Next Q) midpoint up 8.2% vs prior quarter ($44.0B vs $40.6B)
  • Lowered FY Total Expenses midpoint down 0.9% vs prior quarter ($115.5B vs $116.5B)
  • Raised FY CapEx midpoint up 8.8% vs prior quarter ($68.0B vs $62.5B)
  • Changed Family DAP: 3.4B+ (prior: 3.35B+)
  • Changed Operating Margin: 41% (prior: 48%)
  • Changed Reality Labs Op Loss: $4.2B (prior: $5.0B)
  • New NEW: Trade/geopolitical impact discussed

Quarterly Guidance (Q2 2025)

  • Revenue: $42.5B-$45.5B ($3B range, wider than typical, reflecting potential for a wider range of outcomes given macro uncertainty)
  • FX assumption: ~1% tailwind to YoY total revenue growth (based on current exchange rates)

Full Year 2025 Guidance

  • Total Expenses: $113B-$118B (lowered from prior outlook of $114B-$119B; lower end reduced by $1B, upper end reduced by $1B; reflects more refined forecasts including updated employee compensation and non-headcount operating expenses, partially offset by higher infrastructure costs from increased CapEx outlook and higher Reality Labs COGS; $5B range maintained given dynamic operating environment)
  • CapEx (incl. principal payments on finance leases): $64B-$72B (raised from prior outlook of $60B-$65B; reflects additional data center investments to support AI efforts and increase in expected cost of infrastructure hardware; majority directed to core business)
  • Tax Rate: 12%-15% (absent changes to tax landscape)
  • 2026 CapEx: Too early to discuss plans beyond 2025

Q1 Expense Line Items

  • Cost of Revenue: +14% YoY (driven by higher infrastructure costs and payments to partners; partially offset by extension of server useful lives)
  • R&D: +22% YoY (higher employee compensation and infrastructure costs)
  • Marketing & Sales: +8% YoY (increase in professional services related to platform integrity efforts)
  • G&A: -34% YoY (lower legal-related costs)

Q1 Ad Revenue Metrics

  • Ad Impressions: +5% YoY (growth mainly driven by Asia Pacific)
  • Average Price Per Ad: +10% YoY (benefited from increased advertiser demand driven by improved ad performance; partially offset by impression growth from lower monetizing regions and surfaces)
  • Conversions: Grew at a faster rate than ad impressions in Q1, reflecting increased conversion rates

Q1 Ad Revenue by User Geography

  • Rest of World: +19% YoY (strongest growth)
  • North America: +18% YoY
  • Europe: +14% YoY
  • Asia Pacific: +12% YoY
  • Online commerce: Largest contributor to YoY ad revenue growth

Q1 Engagement Metrics

  • Family DAP: More than 3.4 billion people using at least one app daily (as of March)
  • WhatsApp MAU: More than 3 billion monthly actives
  • WhatsApp U.S. users: More than 100 million and growing quickly
  • Messenger MAU: More than 1 billion monthly users
  • Instagram DM volume: As many messages sent daily on Instagram as on Messenger
  • Threads MAU: More than 350 million monthly actives
  • Meta AI MAU: Almost 1 billion monthly actives across apps
  • Time Spent on Facebook: +7% (from AI recommendation improvements over last 6 months)
  • Time Spent on Instagram: +6% (from AI recommendation improvements over last 6 months)
  • Time Spent on Threads: +35% (from AI recommendation improvements over last 6 months)
  • Video consumption: Strong growth across Facebook and Instagram, particularly in the U.S., where video time spent grew double digits YoY
  • Llama in Threads recommendations: 4% lift in time spent from first launch (testing began end of last year)
  • Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses monthly actives: 4x+ vs. a year ago
  • Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses sales: Tripled in the last year

Q1 Family of Apps Segment

  • FoA Revenue: $41.9B (+16% YoY)
  • FoA Ad Revenue: $41.4B (+16% YoY; +20% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • FoA Other Revenue: $510M (+34% YoY; driven by WhatsApp business platform messaging revenue and Meta Verified subscriptions)
  • FoA Expenses: $20.1B (+10% YoY; 81% of overall expenses; growth driven by employee compensation and infrastructure costs, partially offset by lower legal-related expenses)
  • FoA Operating Income: $21.8B
  • FoA Operating Margin: 52%

Q1 Reality Labs Segment

  • RL Revenue: $412M (-6% YoY; lower Meta Quest sales partially offset by increased Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses sales)
  • RL Expenses: $4.6B (+8% YoY; driven by higher employee compensation)
  • RL Operating Loss: $4.2B

Q1 Cash Flow & Balance Sheet

  • Capital Expenditures (incl. principal payments on finance leases): $13.7B (driven by investments in servers, data centers, and network infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $10.3B
  • Share Repurchases: $13.4B of Class A common stock
  • Dividends Paid: $1.3B
  • Cash & Marketable Securities: $70.2B (end of quarter)
  • Debt: $28.8B (end of quarter)

AI Advertising Improvements

  • Reels ad conversion rates: +5% increase from new ads recommendation model testing
  • AI creative tools adoption: 30% more advertisers using AI creative tools QoQ
  • GEM (Generative Ads Recommendation Model): New architecture 2x as efficient at improving ad performance for a given amount of data and compute; trained on thousands of GPUs (largest cluster for ads training to date); up to 5% increase in ad conversions on Facebook Reels; rolling out to additional surfaces
  • Incremental attribution: Advertisers in tests seeing average 46% lift in incremental conversions vs. business-as-usual; expected to be available to all advertisers in coming weeks
  • Advantage+ Creative video expansion: Broadened access to Facebook Reels for all eligible advertisers
  • Advantage+ Image generation: Rolled out to all eligible advertisers
  • Advantage+ streamlined campaign creation: Rolled out to more advertisers in April; global rollout expected later this year

Threads Monetization

  • Opened to all eligible advertisers this month in 30+ markets including U.S.
  • Gradual ramp of ad supply expected as ad formats are optimized
  • Not expected to be a meaningful driver of overall impression or revenue growth in 2025

Business Messaging & WhatsApp

  • Testing business AIs with limited set of businesses in U.S. and a few additional countries on WhatsApp, Messenger, and on ads on Facebook and Instagram
  • Starting with small businesses, focusing on selling goods and services
  • Working on tools for full customer funnel: lead generation, order management, customer service
  • Launched new agent management experience and dashboard for business AI customization
  • Testing business AIs on Facebook and Instagram ads for product/return policy inquiries and purchase assistance

Meta AI & Llama

  • Llama 4: Released earlier in April; 17 billion parameters per expert; designed for Meta's infrastructure for low-latency voice-optimized experience; industry-leading context window length for personalization
  • Llama 4 Behemoth: Massive model still forthcoming; used for distillation to get 90%-95% of intelligence in lower-latency, more efficient form factors
  • Meta AI stand-alone app: Launched with personalization (leverages Reels browsing and cross-app content interests) and social feed; initial feedback positive
  • Meta AI top use cases: Information gathering (#1), social interactions/casual chatting (#2), writing assistance, visual content interaction
  • Meta AI by surface: WhatsApp strongest usage, Facebook second largest driver (feed deep dives integration)
  • Meta AI personalization: Testing began earlier this year with memory of prior queries and cross-app engagement data; already seeing deeper engagement; rolled out across Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, and Meta AI app in U.S. and Canada

AI Coding Agents

  • Prediction unchanged: mid-level engineer capability starting to become possible sometime in 2025, scaling into 2026
  • By middle to end of 2026, AI coding agents expected to be doing a substantial part of AI research and development

Reality Labs & AI Devices

  • Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses: Tripled sales YoY; 4x+ monthly actives YoY; voice command usage growing even faster
  • Live translations: Fully rolled out on Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses for English, French, Italian, Spanish across all markets
  • New launches: Exciting new launches with EssilorLuxottica expected later this year with new technological capabilities
  • Quest 3S: Making VR accessible to more people; seeing deeper engagement
  • Scaling ambition: Leading consumer electronics products by third generation often sell 10 million units; Meta focused on scaling toward and beyond that milestone

Macro & Trade Environment

  • China/Asia e-commerce impact: Some reduced spend in U.S. from Asia-based e-commerce exporters in anticipation of de minimis exemption ending May 2; portion redirected to other markets but overall spend below pre-April levels
  • Gaming vertical: YoY growth negative in Q1, lapping strong China-based advertiser spend promoting game titles in Q1 2024
  • Government & politics vertical: YoY growth dropped sharply as expected post-U.S. elections; remains a very small vertical
  • Infrastructure hardware costs: Higher costs from suppliers sourcing from countries around the world; significant uncertainty from ongoing trade discussions; Meta working on supply chain mitigations
  • Q2 outlook: Reflects trends seen in April, which have "generally been healthy"

EU Regulatory / DMA

  • European Commission decided Meta's "subscription for no ads" model is not compliant with DMA
  • Meta expects to need modifications that could result in "materially worse user experience for European users and a significant impact to our European business and revenue as early as Q3 2025"
  • Meta will appeal the decision, but modifications may be imposed before or during appeal
  • EU/EEA + Switzerland advertising revenue: 16% of worldwide total revenue in 2024
  • Continuing to engage actively with European Commission; hope to have more clarity by next quarter

Infrastructure & Compute Strategy

  • Majority of 2025 CapEx supporting the core business (not only gen AI)
  • Adjustments to build strategy to stand up capacity more quickly in 2025 and 2026
  • Internal compute demand exceeds supply across both gen AI and core AI ranking/recommendation teams
  • Meta is funding infrastructure for Llama training; no expectation that will change
  • Partners (AWS, Azure) investing alongside Meta to host Llama but not funding training infrastructure
  • Emphasis on efficiency: innovations in ranking work focused on increasing workload efficiency

Capital Allocation

  • Primary focus: investing capital back into business (infrastructure and talent as top priorities)
  • Hiring targeted at technical roles within company priorities
  • Share repurchases and dividends ongoing ($13.4B buybacks + $1.3B dividends in Q1)

Social Features & Content

  • Instagram "Friend Activity" feed: Launched in U.S.; feed of content friends have noted or liked; seeing good results
  • Blend: Opt-in DM experience blending Reels algorithms between friends to spark conversations
  • WhatsApp Status: Tens of billions of views of status posts daily; continued investment in updates tab
  • Edits app: Stand-alone video editing app launched; ultra-high resolution short-form video camera; includes gen AI tools (background removal, image animation)

Q1 2025 Actuals (Reported)

  • Revenue: $42.3B (+16% YoY; +19% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Total Expenses: $24.8B (+9% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $17.6B
  • Operating Margin: 41%
  • Tax Rate: 9% (benefited from excess tax benefits from share-based compensation due to increase in share price vs. prior periods)
  • Headcount: ~76,800 employees (+4% QoQ; ~2,800 employees added in Q1, significant majority in technical roles supporting monetization, infrastructure, generative AI, regulation/compliance, and Reality Labs)

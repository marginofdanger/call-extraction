What Changed vs Q3 2023

  • Lowered Revenue Guidance (Next Q) midpoint down 6.5% vs prior quarter ($35.8B vs $38.2B)
  • Raised FY Total Expenses midpoint up 9.7% vs prior quarter ($96.5B vs $88.0B)
  • Changed Family DAP: 3.1B+ (estimated daily active people across the family of apps) (prior: ~3.14B in September)
  • Changed Ad Impressions Growth: +21% YoY (mainly driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World) (prior: +31% YoY (mainly driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World))
  • Changed Avg Price Per Ad: +2% YoY (driven by advertiser demand and currency tailwinds, partially offset by strong impression growth from lower-monetizing services and regions) (prior: -6% YoY (decline driven by strong impression growth, especially from lower monetizing services and regions))
  • Changed Operating Margin: 41% (prior: 40% (highest operating margin in 2 years))
  • Changed Reality Labs Op Loss: $4.6B (prior: $3.7B)
  • New NEW: Efficiency/restructuring discussed

Q1 2024 Guidance

  • Revenue: $34.5B to $37.0B (midpoint: $35.75B)
  • FX Assumption: Foreign currency assumed to be neutral to YoY total revenue growth based on current exchange rates
  • Q1 includes Leap Day: February 29 benefit noted by management
  • Drivers: Strong broad-based advertising demand across verticals, particularly within online commerce and gaming; continued accrual of improvements from ad performance investments

Full Year 2024 Guidance

Long-Term Targets and Strategic Commentary

AI Investment Outlook

  • Management expects to continue investing aggressively in AI infrastructure
  • Training and operating future models expected to be even more compute-intensive
  • State-of-the-art LLMs have been trained on roughly 10x the amount of compute each year (trend cited by Zuckerberg)
  • CapEx expected to grow beyond 2024 to support long-term AI ambitions
  • GenAI products not expected to be a meaningful 2024 revenue driver, but expected to be meaningful contributors over time

Consolidated Results

  • Revenue: $40.1B (+25% YoY; +22% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Total Expenses: $23.7B (-8% YoY)
  • Operating Income: $16.4B
  • Operating Margin: 41%
  • Tax Rate: 17%
  • Net Income: $14.0B
  • Diluted EPS: $5.33
  • CapEx (including principal payments on finance leases): $7.9B (driven by investments in servers, data centers and network infrastructure)
  • Free Cash Flow: $11.5B (reflects a $1.6B payment of income taxes deferred from prior quarters of 2023)

Expense Line Item Detail

  • Cost of Revenue: decreased 8% YoY, driven mainly by lower restructuring costs, partially offset by higher infrastructure-related costs
  • R&D: increased 8% YoY, driven primarily by higher headcount-related costs from Family of Apps and Reality Labs, and higher non-headcount-related Reality Labs operating expenses, partially offset by lower restructuring costs
  • Marketing & Sales: decreased 29% YoY, due mainly to lower marketing spend and restructuring costs
  • G&A: decreased 26% YoY, due mainly to lower restructuring expenses

Headcount

  • Employees (end of Q4): 67,300+ (-22% YoY; +2% QoQ as hiring efforts resumed)

Balance Sheet

  • Cash and Marketable Securities: $65.4B (end of year)
  • Debt: $18.4B

Capital Return

  • Share Repurchases (Q4): $6.3B
  • Share Repurchases (Full Year 2023): $20.0B
  • Remaining Repurchase Authorization (as of Dec 31): $30.9B
  • New Repurchase Authorization Increase (announced Feb 1, 2024): $50.0B
  • Dividend: Announced initiation of a regular quarterly dividend (new; first-ever dividend for Meta)

Engagement Metrics

  • Family DAP: 3.1B+ (estimated daily active people across the family of apps)
  • Ad Impressions Growth (Q4): +21% YoY (mainly driven by Asia Pacific and Rest of World)
  • Average Price Per Ad Growth (Q4): +2% YoY (driven by advertiser demand and currency tailwinds, partially offset by strong impression growth from lower-monetizing services and regions)
  • Daily Video Watch Time: grew over 25% YoY in Q4 across all video types, driven by ongoing ranking improvements
  • Reels Daily Re-shares: 3.5B times per day
  • Threads Monthly Actives: 130M+
  • WhatsApp Channels: scaled to 500M+ monthly active users since global rollout in September

Ad Revenue by Geography (Q4)

  • Europe: +33% YoY
  • Rest of World: +32% YoY
  • Asia Pacific: +23% YoY
  • North America: +19% YoY
  • Foreign currency was a tailwind to advertising revenue growth in all international regions

Ad Revenue by Vertical (Q4)

  • Largest contributor to YoY growth: Online commerce
  • Followed by: CPG and gaming

Family of Apps Segment (Q4 2023)

  • Revenue: $39.0B (+24% YoY)
  • Ad Revenue: $38.7B (+24% YoY; +21% YoY on constant currency basis)
  • Other Revenue: $334M (+82% YoY, driven by business messaging revenue growth from WhatsApp Business platform)
  • Expenses: $18.0B (-13% YoY, due to lower restructuring expenses; ~76% of overall expenses)
  • Operating Income: $21.0B
  • Operating Margin: 54%

Shops Ads

  • Shops Ads Annual Run Rate: $2.0B+ (crossed in Q4; opened availability to all U.S. advertisers in Q2)

Reality Labs Segment (Q4 2023)

  • Revenue: $1.1B (+47% YoY, driven by Quest 3 sales during holiday season)
  • Expenses: $5.7B (+14% YoY, due primarily to higher headcount-related expenses and non-headcount-related R&D spend)
  • Operating Loss: $4.6B
  • Note: Reality Labs crossed $1B in quarterly revenue for the first time

Consolidated Results

  • Family of Apps Total Revenue: $133.0B (+16% YoY)
  • Family of Apps Ad Revenue: $131.9B (+16% YoY)
  • Share Repurchases: $20.0B
  • CapEx (implied from quarterly trajectory): driven by servers, data centers, and network infrastructure investments

Full Year Ad Revenue Drivers

  • Largest contributors to YoY ad revenue growth: Online commerce, CPG, entertainment and media, and gaming verticals
  • Online commerce and gaming verticals: benefited from strong demand by advertisers in China reaching people in other markets
  • Revenue from China-based advertisers: 10% of overall revenue; contributed 5 percentage points to total worldwide revenue growth

Total Expenses

  • Total Expenses: $94B to $99B (unchanged from prior outlook)
  • Key Expense Drivers: - Higher infrastructure-related costs (depreciation expenses in 2024 expected to increase by a larger amount than in 2023; higher operating costs from running a larger infrastructure footprint) - Growth in payroll expenses (working down current hiring underrun; adding incremental talent to support priority areas; workforce composition shifting toward higher-cost technical roles) - Reality Labs operating losses expected to increase meaningfully YoY due to ongoing product development efforts in AR/VR and investments to further scale the ecosystem

CapEx

  • CapEx: $30B to $37B (raised from prior range; $2B increase on the high end of prior range)
  • Drivers: Investments in servers (both AI and non-AI hardware) and data centers as construction ramps on sites with previously announced new data center architecture
  • Rationale for increase: Evolving understanding of AI capacity demands for next generations of foundational research and product development

Tax Rate

  • Tax Rate (Full Year 2024): mid-teens (absent any changes to U.S. tax law)

AI Infrastructure

  • H100 GPUs (by end of 2024): ~350,000
  • Total GPU Compute (by end of 2024): ~600,000 H100 equivalents (including other GPUs)
  • Management expects infrastructure investments to grow beyond 2024 to support ambitious long-term AI research and product development

Capital Return

  • Share repurchases expected to continue to be the primary way capital is returned to shareholders
  • Dividend introduced as a complement to the existing share repurchase program; does not change the total amount of capital returned
  • Dividend provides a more balanced capital return program and added flexibility

Headcount Philosophy

  • Management intends to keep new headcount additions "relatively minimal" compared to historical norms even beyond 2024
  • 2024 hiring focused on working down a recruiting backlog from restructuring (swapping talent profiles) rather than meaningful net new headcount
  • Company operates better as a leaner organization (permanent cultural shift from Year of Efficiency)

Efficiency and Cost Discipline

  • Year of Efficiency (2023) values to be carried forward permanently
  • Lean cost structure maintained to provide flexibility for surging investments when needed (e.g., training clusters)
  • Stability and flexibility prioritized to support long-term vision across volatile and unpredictable macro, geopolitical, and regulatory environment

AI Strategy and Products

Llama and Foundation Models

  • Llama 3: currently in training; described as "looking great so far"
  • Long-term roadmap extends to Llama 5, 6, and 7 with a goal of developing full general intelligence
  • FAIR research group moved from Reality Labs to Family of Apps to be closer to GenAI group
  • FAIR focused on foundational/longer-term research (couple of years to 10+ years out); GenAI group builds Llama launch vehicles and products

Meta AI Assistant

  • Fully rolled out in the U.S. at end of 2023 across WhatsApp, Messenger, and Instagram
  • Currently in "tuning phase"; not yet being pushed proactively
  • Expected to be rolled out much more prominently across apps over the course of 2024

GenAI Advertising Tools

  • Text Variations and Image Expansion: global rollout completed in Q4
  • Background Generation: broader availability planned for later in Q1 2024
  • Initial adoption strong with promising early performance gains
  • More than 20 GenAI features tested across Family of Apps

AI for Business Messaging

  • Testing AI chats on a very small scale with a few businesses
  • Good feedback from businesses testing them; expected to expand tests further in 2024
  • Described as a "compelling opportunity" for the longer term

Business Messaging Monetization

  • Click-to-Message Ads: continued strong revenue growth in Q4; broadening advertiser adoption
  • Purchase Optimization on Click-to-Messenger Ads: strong growth since rollout; planning to bring purchase optimization to click-to-WhatsApp campaigns in 2024
  • Paid Messaging: early stage but seeing good momentum, driven by marketing messages; updated pricing model introduced in June showing good results
  • Testing paid messaging purchases directly via Meta Ads Manager
  • Flows: launched globally in October; enables richer in-WhatsApp user experiences; seeing good early traction

Reality Labs and Metaverse Strategy

  • Quest 3: off to a strong start; expected to be the most popular mixed reality device
  • Quest app was the most downloaded app in the App Store on Christmas Day
  • Horizon: now in Top 10 Most Used Apps on Quest; growing quickly; 2024 focus on growing mobile version as well as VR
  • Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses: strong start in both sales and engagement; partner EssilorLuxottica planning to produce more than initially expected due to high demand; engagement and retention significantly higher than first version
  • Multimodal AI planned to be rolled out to smart glasses, followed by additional AI features later in the year
  • Smart glasses with AI assistance seen as a potential killer app even before full holographic displays

Regulatory Environment

  • FTC seeking to substantially modify existing consent order and impose additional restrictions on Meta's ability to operate; Meta contesting this matter; adverse impact on business if unsuccessful
  • Increasing legal and regulatory headwinds noted in both the EU and the U.S.
  • Apple's alternative App Store implementation in Europe described as "so onerous" and at odds with EU regulation intent; management does not expect it to have any meaningful impact on Meta

Metric Reporting Changes (Announced)

  • Transitioning away from reporting Facebook-specific metrics (Facebook DAU and MAU)
  • Will no longer report Family Monthly Active People (MAP)
  • Starting in Q1 2024: will report YoY changes in ad impressions and average price per ad at the regional level
  • Will continue to report Family Daily Active People (DAP)

Q4 2023 Actuals (Reported)

Revenue from China-Based Advertisers (Full Year 2023)

  • Share of Overall Revenue: 10%
  • Contribution to Total Worldwide Revenue Growth: 5 percentage points

# TSMC Q2 2024 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 18, 2024 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2024

---

## Q2 2024 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $20.8B (+10.3% QoQ in USD; +13.6% QoQ in NT), above guidance in USD terms
- **Gross Margin:** 53.2% (+10 bps QoQ; slightly ahead of high end of guidance, driven by higher-than-expected overall capacity utilization rate)
- **Operating Margin:** 42.5% (+0.5 pp QoQ)
- **Operating Expenses:** 10.5% of net revenue (down from 11.1% in Q1 due to operating leverage)
- **EPS:** TWD 9.56
- **ROE:** 26.7%
- **GM drivers:** Cost improvement and more favorable FX rate, partially offset by margin dilution from N3 ramp

### Q2 Technology Node Breakdown
- **3nm:** 15% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **5nm:** 35% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 17% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 67% of Q2 wafer revenue

### Q2 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 52% of Q2 revenue (+28% QoQ; surpassed 50% for the first time)
- **Smartphone:** 33% of Q2 revenue (-1% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 6% of Q2 revenue (+6% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 5% of Q2 revenue (+5% QoQ)
- **DCE:** 2% of Q2 revenue (+20% QoQ)

### Q2 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 2 trillion (USD 63 billion)
- **Current liabilities:** increased TWD 23B QoQ (mainly TWD 16B increase in accounts payable)
- **Long-term interest-bearing debt:** increased TWD 9B QoQ (raised TWD 12B in corporate bonds)
- **AR turnover days:** 28 days (-3 days QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 83 days (-7 days QoQ, primarily due to higher N3 wafer shipment)

### Q2 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 378 billion
- **CapEx:** TWD 206 billion (USD 6.36 billion)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 91 billion (Q3 2023 dividend)
- **Cash balance:** increased TWD 101B to TWD 1.8 trillion at end of Q2

## Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2024)
- **Revenue:** $22.4B-$23.2B (+9.5% QoQ at midpoint; +32% YoY at midpoint)
- **Gross Margin:** 53.5%-55.5% (midpoint 54.5%; +1.3 pp QoQ)
- **Operating Margin:** 42.5%-44.5%
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 32.5 TWD
- **GM drivers:** Higher overall capacity utilization rate and better cost improvement efforts including productivity gains, partially offset by continued dilution from N3 ramp-up, N5-to-N3 tool conversion costs, and higher electricity prices in Taiwan

## Full Year 2024 Guidance
- **Revenue growth:** Slightly above mid-20s% in USD terms (raised from prior guidance)
- **CapEx:** $30B-$32B (narrowed from $28B-$32B previously)
- **CapEx allocation:** 70%-80% for advanced process technologies; 10%-20% for specialty technologies; ~10% for advanced packaging, testing, mask-making and others
- **Semiconductor market (ex-memory):** ~10% growth YoY (unchanged from 3 months ago)

## Foundry 2.0 Industry Definition
- Expanded definition to include packaging, testing, mask-making and others plus all IDM excluding memory manufacturing
- Foundry 2.0 industry size was close to $250B in 2023 (vs $115B under previous definition)
- Foundry 2.0 industry growth forecast: close to 10% YoY in 2024
- TSMC share under new definition: 28% in 2023; expected to further increase in 2024

## Long-Term Targets
- **Gross Margin:** 53% and higher (excluding FX impact and factoring in global manufacturing footprint expansion); emphasis on "and higher" with suggestion of future upward revision
- **Overseas fab dilution:** 2-3 percentage points in 2025 and next several years

---

## N3 Technology
- **N3 GM dilution:** Reducing as yield improves and cost optimization continues; N3 is approaching greater maturity
- **Time to reach corporate average GM:** 10-12 quarters (vs 8-10 quarters for prior nodes)
- **N3 demand:** Very strong, driving high utilization across N3 and N5
- **N5-to-N3 conversion:** Not ruling out further converting more N5 tools to N3 due to strong N3 demand; conversion costs are a near-term GM headwind but beneficial in future years
- **N5/N3 tool commonality:** Over 90%; both nodes are adjacent in Tainan Science Park, making conversions easy
- N5 and N3 are NOT considered one big node family (unlike 10/7nm or 20/16nm), despite high tool commonality

## N2 Technology
- **Production:** On track for volume production in 2025
- **Ramp profile:** Similar to N3
- **Performance vs N3E:** 10-15% speed improvement at same power; 25-30% power improvement at same speed; >15% chip density increase
- **Tape-outs:** Number of new tape-outs in first 2 years expected to be higher than both N3 and N5 in their first 2 years
- **Demand:** Strong from both AI and smartphone applications; almost all AI innovators working with TSMC
- **Development:** Progressing well; device performance and yield on track, all ahead of plan
- **N2 revenue:** Will be bigger than N3 at a similar point in time of the ramp (per CFO)
- **N2 GM dilution:** Will be faster to reach corporate average than N3

## N2P
- Extension of N2 family
- **Performance vs N2:** Further 5% performance at same power; 5-10% power benefit at same speed (on top of N2)
- **Applications:** Supports both smartphone and HPC
- **Volume production:** Scheduled for H2 2026

## A16 (1.6nm)
- **Production:** Volume production scheduled for H2 2026
- Separate offering featuring Super Power Rail (SPR) -- TSMC's innovative backside power delivery solution
- First in industry to incorporate novel backside contact scheme preserving gate density and device width flexibility
- **Performance vs N2P:** 8-10% speed improvement at same power; 15-20% power improvement at same speed; 7-10% chip density gain
- Best suited for specific HPC products with complex signal routing and dense power delivery network

## Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint
- **Arizona Phase 1:** Beginning production in 2025 (one of two overseas fabs ramping next year)
- **Japan Kumamoto Phase 1:** Beginning production in 2025 (the other overseas fab ramping next year)
- **Europe:** Future expansion planned
- **Overseas GM dilution:** 2-3 percentage points in 2025 and next several years
- No change to overall overseas expansion strategy; no JV or joint investments with government or partners
- Expansion bottlenecks: land, electricity, talented people

## Government Subsidies
- **2023 total subsidies received:** Slightly higher than $1.5B equivalent, mainly received in Japan
- Subsidies received appear on cash flow statement, used to offset asset value on balance sheet; P&L impact comes when fab begins production

---

## Gross Margin Dynamics
- **Q2 2024:** 53.2%, slightly ahead of high end of guidance due to higher-than-expected utilization
- **Q3 2024 guidance midpoint:** 54.5% (+1.3 pp QoQ)
- **2H 2024:** Better than initially expected -- higher utilization, improved cost structure
- **Key headwinds:** N3 dilution (reducing), N5-to-N3 conversion costs, overseas ramp costs, higher electricity costs in Taiwan, cost inflation
- **Key tailwinds:** Higher utilization, cost improvement/productivity gains, favorable FX, value-selling
- **Long-term:** 53% and higher; not revising upward at this time but emphasizing "and higher"
- If high utilization rate maintained and everything else stays the same, high 50s/60% is possible (per CFO)

## Pricing / Value
- Strategic pricing approach, not opportunistic; selling value continues
- Pricing will differ between product sectors (e.g., HPC vs smartphone at the same node)
- Customer profitability improving in recent years, supporting TSMC's value case
- Rising cost challenges: increasing process complexity at leading node, higher electricity costs in Taiwan, global fab expansion in higher-cost regions, other cost inflation
- Continue working closely with customers to sell value and with suppliers on cost performance

---

## Advanced Packaging
- **CoWoS capacity 2024:** More than doubled vs 2023
- **CoWoS capacity 2025:** Plan to double again, or more than double
- **CoWoS 60% CAGR:** Referenced by analyst from TSMC symposium for next 4-5 years
- **CoWoS supply/demand:** Tight through 2025; hope to ease in 2026
- Multiple CoWoS variants (CoWoS-S, CoWoS-L, CoWoS-R) for different customer products; different versions require different tool sets with some common tools
- **Advanced packaging profitability:** Previously much lower than corporate average GM; now approaching corporate average due to scale economics and cost reduction efforts
- Working with OSAT partners to increase overall CoWoS supply to support customer growth
- **Fan-out panel-level packaging:** Under exploration; maturity not yet reached; expected ~3 years out; currently support customers up to 5-6x chip size

## Capacity Planning
- Disciplined framework: top-down and bottom-up approach to evaluate market demand
- 4 investment disciplines: technology leadership, flexible/responsive manufacturing, retaining customers' trust, earning sustainable/healthy return
- Not speculative; based on long-term market demand profile and customer commitments
- N5 and N3 capacity very tight; working hard to support customers through 2026
- Increasing overall capacity utilization rate for leading-edge 3nm and 5nm in H2 2024

---

## AI Demand
- AI-related demand assessed as real and structural; more real than 2021-2022 shortage-driven demand
- TSMC uses AI internally (machine learning for productivity improvement) to validate utility
- Top-down bottom-up approach with customers to ensure demand is real, not speculative
- Value of TSMC's technology position increasing as customers rely on TSMC for most advanced process and packaging at scale
- Structural demand underpinned by industry megatrends of AI, HPC, and 5G

## Edge AI / Silicon Content
- AI functionality in edge devices driving ~5-10% die size increase as a general rule
- Unit growth not yet visible; expected to stimulate shorter replacement cycles
- Meaningful unit growth in edge devices (smartphone, PC) expected ~2 years out
- Smartphone customers catching up on advanced packaging adoption (e.g., InFO); HPC customers moving faster due to bandwidth/latency/footprint requirements
- Migration to N2/A16 will drive chiplet approach, requiring advanced packaging technologies

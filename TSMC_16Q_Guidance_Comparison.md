# 21. KEY TAKEAWAYS — 16 QUARTER EVOLUTION

1. **AI transformed TSMC's trajectory.** From zero mentions in 2022 to the dominant growth driver by 2025, AI accelerator revenue went from nothing to high teens% of total revenue, with the CAGR raised from ~50% → mid-40s% → mid- to high 50s% as demand accelerated.

2. **Revenue nearly doubled in 4 years.** Quarterly guidance midpoints grew from ~$17.9B to ~$35.2B (Q2 '22 → Q1 '26), driven by AI, advanced technology leadership, and value-selling.

3. **The 2023 downcycle was sharp but short.** Revenue guidance troughed at ~$15.6B (Q2 '23) but recovered to $26.9B midpoint by Q4 '24 — a 72% recovery in just 6 quarters.

4. **N3 dilution was more painful than expected.** Initially guided at 2–3 pts, it expanded to 3–4 pts and took longer than historical nodes to recover. By Jan '26, N3 was expected to cross corporate average margin — a full recovery.

5. **Overseas expansion entered a new phase.** From a single Arizona fab in 2022, TSMC expanded to 4 Arizona fabs (+ advanced packaging), 2 Japan fabs, and 1 Germany fab by 2026 — building an independent giga-fab cluster in Arizona.

6. **Arizona Fab 1 achieved Taiwan yield parity** by Q4 2024 — dispelling skeptics. Fab 2 was brought forward from 2028 to H2 2027 due to strong demand.

7. **The 53% GM target was finally raised to 56%.** After maintaining 53%+ for 15 consecutive quarters despite actual GMs running 57–62%, management raised the LT GM target to 56% and higher in Jan '26, with an ROE target of high 20s%.

8. **CapEx surged to record levels:** From $36B (2022) → trough ~$30B (2024) → $40.9B actual (2025) → massive step-up to $52–56B (2026), driven by AI demand, N2 ramp, and accelerated Arizona buildout.

9. **Long-term CAGR raised twice:** From "15–20%" → "close to 20%" (Jan '25) → "approach 25%" (Jan '26) — the highest growth outlook in TSMC's history, reflecting AI-driven structural acceleration.

10. **CoWoS became the critical bottleneck.** Advanced packaging went from a minor business to the key constraint in AI chip production, with capacity doubled at least twice in 2 years. Advanced packaging revenue climbing to ~10%+ of total revenue by 2026.

11. **TSMC's pricing power increased.** The shift from "strategic pricing" to "value-selling" reflected TSMC's growing irreplaceability in the AI supply chain, enabling the 56% LT GM target.

12. **Technology roadmap executed flawlessly.** N2 maintained a 2H 2025 target for 16 consecutive quarters with zero delays, entering HVM at both Hsinchu and Kaohsiung in Q4 2025. A16 with backside power delivery advancing to volume production.

# TSMC Q2 2023 Earnings Call Guidance Extraction
**Call Date:** July 20, 2023 | **Reporting Quarter:** Q2 2023

---

## Q2 2023 Actuals (Reported)
- **Revenue:** $15.7B (-6.2% QoQ in USD; -5.5% QoQ in NT), impacted by global economic conditions dampening end market demand and customers' ongoing inventory adjustment
- **Gross Margin:** 54.1% (-220 bps QoQ; slightly exceeded high end of prior guidance due to more stringent cost control and slightly more favorable FX)
- **Operating Margin:** 42% (-3.5 pp QoQ), reflecting continued R&D investment in N3 and N2 despite cyclical downturn
- **EPS:** TWD 7.01
- **ROE:** 23.2%

### Q2 Technology Node Breakdown
- **5nm:** 30% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **7nm:** 23% of Q2 wafer revenue
- **Advanced tech (7nm and below):** 53% of Q2 wafer revenue

### Q2 Platform Revenue Breakdown
- **HPC:** 44% of Q2 revenue (-5% QoQ)
- **Smartphone:** 33% of Q2 revenue (-9% QoQ)
- **IoT:** 8% of Q2 revenue (-11% QoQ)
- **Automotive:** 8% of Q2 revenue (+3% QoQ)
- **DCE:** 3% of Q2 revenue (+25% QoQ)

### Q2 Balance Sheet
- **Cash & marketable securities:** TWD 1.5 trillion (USD 48 billion)
- **Current liabilities:** decreased TWD 62B QoQ, mainly due to net decrease of TWD 87B in income tax payable (paid TWD 120B for 2022 income tax, offset by TWD 33B accrued tax payables for Q2)
- **Long-term interest-bearing debt:** increased TWD 53B QoQ, mainly from TWD 41B in corporate bond issuances
- **AR turnover days:** 32 days (-2 days QoQ)
- **Inventory days:** 99 days (+3 days QoQ), primarily due to N3 ramp during the quarter

### Q2 Cash Flow
- **Cash from operations:** TWD 167 billion
- **CapEx:** TWD 251 billion (USD 8.17 billion)
- **Cash dividend distributed:** TWD 71 billion (Q3 2022 cash dividend)
- **Corporate bond issuances:** TWD 41 billion raised
- **Cash balance:** decreased TWD 109B to TWD 1.3 trillion at end of Q2
- **Free Cash Flow:** negative TWD 83 billion (operating cash flow more than offset by CapEx, partly due to TWD 120B income tax payment)

---

## Quarterly Guidance (Q3 2023)
- **Revenue:** $16.7B-$17.5B (+9.1% QoQ at midpoint), supported by strong ramp of N3 technologies partially offset by customers' continued inventory adjustment
- **Gross Margin:** 51.5%-53.5% (midpoint 52.5%; -1.6 pp QoQ at midpoint, as higher capacity utilization offset by 2-3 pp margin dilution from initial N3 ramp-up)
- **Operating Margin:** 38%-40%
- **FX assumption:** 1 USD = 30.8 TWD

## Full Year 2023 Guidance
- **Revenue:** Decline ~10% in USD terms (worsened from prior "low to mid-single-digit decline" due to weaker macro, slower China recovery, softer end market demand)
- **CapEx:** $32B-$36B (maintained; trending toward lower end due to tightened spending amid near-term uncertainties)
- **CapEx allocation:** 70%-80% leading-edge technology; 10%-20% specialty technology; remainder split between advanced packaging, EBO, and others
- **Depreciation:** Mid-20s% increase YoY, mainly as N3 technologies ramp
- **Semi ex-memory:** Decline mid-single-digit YoY in 2023 (maintained)
- **Foundry industry:** Decline mid-teens YoY in 2023
- **N3 revenue contribution:** Mid-single-digit percentage of total wafer revenue in 2023
- **2H 2023 vs 1H 2023:** Revenue up 10%-12% in 2H vs 1H (more mild than typical seasonality)

## 2H 2023 / Cycle Outlook
- Fabless semiconductor inventory expected to rebalance to healthier level exiting Q3 2023 (maintained from prior quarter)
- Customers more cautious and intend to further control inventory into Q4 2023 due to persistent weaker macro, slower China recovery, softer end market demand
- Fabless semiconductor inventory expected to exit Q4 2023 at healthier and lower level than previously expected 3 months ago
- Recovery more gradual than initially expected; other than AI, almost every segment seeing weakness in 2H
- Higher inflation and interest rates impacting end demand in all market segments in every region
- Management declined to comment on 2024 outlook at this time

## AI Demand (Major Theme)
- **AI server processor definition:** CPUs, GPUs, and AI accelerators performing training and inference functions
- **AI revenue share:** ~6% of TSMC total revenue in 2023
- **AI revenue CAGR:** Close to 50% over next 5 years
- **AI revenue target:** Low teens percent of total revenue (after 5-year CAGR period)
- **AI demand assessment:** Structural multiyear growth, not short-term frenzy; recent increase directionally positive for TSMC
- **AI demand model:** Based on data center structure, assuming certain percentage of data center processors will be AI processors
- **AI and CapEx:** Certain assumption for AI demand already embedded in long-term CapEx and growth forecast
- **Short-term AI cannibalization of CPUs:** Yes, when cloud service provider CapEx is fixed; long-term, CSP CapEx expected to increase as generative AI service revenue grows
- **ASIC demand:** Customers also have high demand on ASIC for AI applications
- **AI chip characteristics:** All AI applications (CPUs, GPUs, AI accelerators, ASICs) need leading-edge technologies and use very large die sizes -- TSMC's strength
- **TSMC positioning:** Able to address and capture major portion of semiconductor component market in AI
- **Edge/end device AI:** Insatiable need for energy-efficient computation starting from data centers, expected to proliferate to edge and end devices over time

## CoWoS / Advanced Packaging
- **CoWoS capacity:** Demand significantly exceeds supply; very tight, very hard to fulfill 100% of customer needs
- **CoWoS front-end vs back-end:** No problem supporting front-end wafer demand for AI; bottleneck is back-end advanced packaging (CoWoS)
- **2024 CoWoS capacity:** Roughly 2x (more than doubling from 2023)
- **CoWoS tightness timeline:** Expected to somewhat release toward end of 2024
- **CoWoS overcapacity risk:** Not a concern today; current concern is not enough capacity to support strong demand
- **CoWoS pricing:** Working with customers; supply assurance is most important priority for customers; sharing value at the same time

## Long-Term Targets
- **Revenue CAGR:** 15%-20% over next several years in USD terms (reiterated; communicated at January 2022 investor conference; CAGR period 2021-2026)
- **Gross Margin:** 53% and higher achievable (long-term target, NOT a 2023 FY guidance)
- **ROE:** Greater than 25% sustainable and achievable
- **Growth drivers:** Combination of underlying structural megatrends (5G, HPC) and TSMC technology leadership/differentiation; also market share gains including share of outsourcing
- **Semiconductor ex-memory long-term growth:** 4%-5% currently forecasted; may increase
- **HPC platform:** Expected to be main engine and largest incremental contributor to TSMC long-term growth

---

## N3 Technology
- **Status:** Already in volume production with good yield; most advanced semiconductor technology in both PPA and transistor technology
- **Ramp:** Strong ramp in 2H 2023 supported by both HPC and smartphone applications
- **Revenue contribution 2023:** Mid-single-digit percentage of total wafer revenue
- **N3 Q3 dilution:** 2-3 pp to corporate gross margin
- **N3 Q4 dilution:** 3-4 pp to corporate gross margin (continued steep ramp-up)
- **N3 2024 dilution:** ~3-4 pp (yield improving but revenue share growing; net-net still dilutive)
- **N3 profitability timeline:** Will take longer than historical 7-8 quarters to reach corporate average due to increasing process complexity and higher corporate baseline (used to be 50%, now 53%+)
- **N3E:** Passed qualification, achieved performance and yield targets; volume production starting Q4 2023; widely accepted by all customers; complete platform support for HPC and smartphone
- **N3P:** Customer engagement ongoing; some customers migrating from N3E to N3P
- **N3X:** For very high-speed, very high performance computing (CPU applications)
- **AI adoption of N3:** AI applications already adopting N3 technology
- **Long-term view:** Confident N3 family will be another large and long-lasting node for TSMC; dollar amount-wise much bigger node than predecessors (percentage share less relevant given much bigger corporate revenue)

## N2 Technology
- **Production:** 2H 2025 (on track)
- **Transistor structure:** Nanosheet (GAA); demonstrated excellent power efficiency
- **Performance:** Full node performance and power benefits; focus on power consumption reduction (critical for data centers/servers)
- **Node-to-node improvement:** Less improvement from 3nm to 2nm vs 5nm to 3nm in speed/density, but focus shifted to power efficiency which delivers full node performance
- **Customer engagement:** High level of customer interest from both HPC and smartphone applications
- **N2 with backside power rail:** 10%-12% additional speed gain and 10%-15% logic density boost on top of baseline N2; best suited for HPC applications; available to customers 2H 2025, production in 2026
- **Industry position:** Will be most advanced semiconductor technology in both density and energy efficiency when introduced

## Gross Margin Dynamics
- **Q2 2023 actual:** 54.1% (-220 bps QoQ); primarily due to lower capacity utilization and higher electricity cost, partially offset by stringent cost control and favorable FX
- **Q3 2023 guided:** 52.5% at midpoint (-1.6 pp QoQ); higher utilization offset by 2-3 pp N3 dilution
- **Q4 2023:** Not guiding specifically; flagging 3-4 pp N3 dilution plus higher electricity costs as challenges; cost reduction and value selling to continue
- **2023 full year challenges:** Lower capacity utilization (cyclicality), N3 ramp-up, overseas fab expansion, inflationary costs including higher utility costs in Taiwan
- **2023 full year actions:** Diligent internal cost improvement efforts, continuing to sell value
- **53% and higher:** Long-term gross margin target (NOT a 2023 FY guidance); management confirmed achievable even with overseas expansion
- **2024:** N3 dilution of 3-4 pp expected; overall GM guidance to be provided later

## Overseas Fabs / Global Manufacturing Footprint
- **Strategy:** Expand global footprint to increase customer trust, expand future growth potential, reach for more global talents; decisions based on customer needs and necessary government support; maximize shareholder value
- **Arizona Fab 1:** N4 process technology; construction started April 2021; now in critical phase of equipment installation; encountering challenges due to insufficient skilled workers for semiconductor-grade equipment installation; sending experienced technicians from Taiwan to train local workers; production pushed out to 2025
- **Japan (JASM Fab 1):** Specialty technology (12nm, 16nm, 22nm, 28nm); volume production on track for late 2024
- **Europe (Germany):** Evaluating specialty fab focusing on automotive-specific technologies; based on customer demand and level of government support; engaging with customers and partners
- **China (Nanjing):** Expanding 28nm as planned to support customers in China; continuing to follow all rules and regulations fully
- **Taiwan:** Continuing to invest and expand capacity to support customer growth
- **Cost gap:** ~50% cost premium for overseas fabs vs Taiwan (founder's statement confirmed by Chairman); due to smaller fab scale, higher supply chain costs, early-stage semiconductor ecosystem vs mature Taiwan ecosystem; labor costs somewhat higher than expected
- **Subsidy/cost gap strategy:** Cash subsidies and investment tax credits designed to cover the gap in approximately the first 5 years; after tools depreciate, ecosystem becomes more prominent (materials, chemicals, labor costs); U.S. administration also decided to subsidize TSMC's suppliers
- **Overseas GM dilution:** 2-3 pp initially when fabs start production; expected to improve over time
- **Pricing for overseas:** Strategic pricing to reflect value, including value of geographic flexibility
- **Cost management:** Leveraging manufacturing technology leadership, large volume, and economies of scale to continuously drive costs down; TSMC will absorb higher overseas costs while remaining most efficient and cost-effective manufacturer

## CapEx / Capital Intensity
- **2023 CapEx:** $32B-$36B, trending toward lower end
- **CapEx allocation:** 70%-80% leading-edge; 10%-20% specialty; remainder packaging, EBO, others
- **CapEx philosophy:** Every year CapEx spent in anticipation of future growth; disciplined planning based on long-term market demand profile
- **CapEx trajectory:** Dollar amount leveling off; past few years increased dramatically from $10B to $36B (2022); as TSMC starts to harvest investments, increase in CapEx will be slower than before; capital intensity expected to decline over next several years
- **Arizona delay impact on CapEx:** Push out of fab pushes out some CapEx, but does not affect a big part
- **2024 CapEx:** Too early to comment
- **Tool commonality:** Always build with commonality between nodes for flexibility; some N5 tools being repurposed for strong multiyear N3 demand

## Pricing
- Strategic, value-based approach continues
- Pricing to reflect value including geographic flexibility of overseas fabs
- Not providing ASP or volume/price breakdowns for AI CAGR forecast
- Supply assurance most important for customers in tight areas (CoWoS)

## Dividend
- **New minimum quarterly dividend:** TWD 3 per share (up from TWD 2.75); approved by Board in May 2023 for Q1 2023; to be distributed October 2023
- **2023 total dividend:** TWD 11.25 per share
- **2024 dividend:** At least TWD 12 per share
- **Policy shift:** As capital intensity begins to decline in next several years, focus of cash dividend policy expected to shift from sustainable to steadily increasing cash dividend per share
- **Capital management objectives:** Fund growth organically, generate good profitability, preserve financial flexibility, distribute sustainable and steadily increasing cash dividend

## Mature/Legacy Nodes
- **16nm and 28nm:** Down ~15%-20% QoQ in Q2; decline driven by companion chips for smartphone, PC, HPC -- as end unit volumes weaken, leading-edge and mature node demand drop together

## Inventory / Cycle
- Fabless inventory normalizing by end of Q3 2023; expected to exit Q4 2023 at healthier and lower level than expected 3 months ago
- Macroeconomic environment weaker than expected (inflation, interest rates, China recovery slower)
- Customer caution in 2H limiting recovery speed; other than AI, almost every segment seeing weakness in 2H
- Cycle taking longer than 2019 and 2015 downturns -- all about macro
- 2H seasonality more mild than previous years
- Management declined to comment on 2024 outlook at this time
